# Analysis of Bhagavad Gītā 4.34

## I. Introduction

### Sanskrit
तदेतत् विशिष्टं ज्ञानं तर्हि केन प्राप्यते इत्युच्यते —

### Transliteration
tadetat viśiṣṭaṃ jñānaṃ tarhi kena prāpyate ityucyate —

### Translation
How then is this special knowledge obtained? This is being stated —

## II. Main Verse

### Sanskrit
तद्विद्धि प्रणिपातेन परिप्रश्नेन सेवया ।
उपदेक्ष्यन्ति ते ज्ञानं ज्ञानिनस्तत्त्वदर्शिनः ॥ ३४ ॥

### Transliteration
tadviddhi praṇipātena paripraśnena sevayā |
upadekṣyanti te jñānaṃ jñāninastattvadarśinaḥ || 34 ||

### Translation
Know that through prostration, proper questioning, and service. The knowers, the seers of truth, will teach you that knowledge.

## III. Commentary

### Sanskrit
तत् विद्धि विजानीहि येन विधिना प्राप्यते इति । आचार्यान् अभिगम्य, प्रणिपातेन प्रकर्षेण नीचैः पतनं प्रणिपातः दीर्घनमस्कारः तेन, 'कथं बन्धः ? कथं मोक्षः ? का विद्या ? का चाविद्या ? ' इति परिप्रश्नेन, सेवया गुरुशुश्रूषया एवमादिना । प्रश्रयेण आवर्जिता आचार्या उपदेक्ष्यन्ति कथयिष्यन्ति ते ज्ञानं यथोक्तविशेषणं ज्ञानिनः । ज्ञानवन्तोऽपि केचित् यथावत् तत्त्वदर्शनशीलाः, अपरे न ; अतो विशिनष्टि तत्त्वदर्शिनः इति । ये सम्यग्दर्शिनः तैः उपदिष्टं ज्ञानं कार्यक्षमं भवति नेतरत् इति भगवतो मतम् ॥

### Transliteration
tat viddhi vijānīhi yena vidhinā prāpyate iti | ācāryān abhigamya, praṇipātena prakarṣeṇa nīcaiḥ patanaṃ praṇipātaḥ dīrghanamaskāraḥ tena, 'kathaṃ bandhaḥ? kathaṃ mokṣaḥ? kā vidyā? kā cāvidyā?' iti paripraśnena, sevayā guruśuśrūṣayā evamādinā...

### Translation
Know, understand by what method it is obtained. Approaching teachers, through prostration - prostration means falling down low with intensity, long salutation - through proper questioning like "What is bondage? What is liberation? What is knowledge? What is ignorance?" through service - attending to the guru and so forth. Teachers pleased by humility will teach, tell you that knowledge with the characteristics mentioned - the knowers. Though some may be knowledgeable, only some have the nature of seeing truth properly, others do not; therefore specifies "seers of truth". Knowledge taught by those who are seers of truth becomes effective, not otherwise - this is the Lord's view.
