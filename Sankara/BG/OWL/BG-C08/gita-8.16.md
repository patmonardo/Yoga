# Technical Analysis: Absolute Distinction between Return and Transcendence

## I. Mūla Verse

### Context
kiṁ punaḥ tvattaḥ anyat prāptāḥ punarāvartante iti, ucyate —

### Sanskrit
आ ब्रह्मभुवनाल्लोकाः पुनरावर्तिनोऽर्जुन ।
मामुपेत्य तु कौन्तेय पुनर्जन्म न विद्यते ॥ १६ ॥

### Transliteration
ā brahmabhuvanāllokāḥ punarāvartino'rjuna |
māmupetya tu kaunteya punarjanma na vidyate || 16 ||

### Translation
Up to brahma's world all realms are subject to return, Arjuna;
But having attained Me, O son of Kunti, rebirth is not found.

## II. Śaṅkara's Commentary

### Sanskrit
आ ब्रह्मभुवनात् भवन्ति अस्मिन् भूतानि इति भुवनम् , ब्रह्मणो भुवनं ब्रह्मभुवनम् , ब्रह्मलोक इत्यर्थः, आ ब्रह्मभुवनात् सह ब्रह्मभुवनेन लोकाः सर्वे पुनरावर्तिनः पुनरावर्तनस्वभावाः हे अर्जुन । माम् एकम् उपेत्य तु कौन्तेय पुनर्जन्म पुनरुत्पत्तिः न विद्यते ॥

### Key Sections

1. Realm of Return:
   - brahmabhuvana as highest world
   - All realms included
   - Complete cycle

2. Nature of Return:
   - punarāvartin as essence
   - Inherent nature
   - Universal law

3. Absolute Distinction:
   - mām ekam (Me alone)
   - Complete transcendence
   - No return

## III. Term Analysis

### A. Primary Terms

1. brahmabhuvana (ब्रह्मभुवन):
   - Compound: brahma + bhuvana
   - Technical meaning: highest realm
   - Complete world-system

2. punarāvartin (पुनरावर्तिन्):
   - Compound: punar + āvartin
   - Technical meaning: subject to return
   - Essential nature

3. upetya (उपेत्य):
   - Root: i with upa
   - Technical meaning: perfect attainment
   - Complete reaching

### B. Distinction Terms

1. āvartin (आवर्तिन्):
   - Root: vṛt with ā
   - Technical meaning: returning
   - Cyclic nature

2. na vidyate (न विद्यते):
   - Negative existence
   - Technical meaning: complete absence
   - Absolute transcendence

3. ekam (एकम्):
   - Numeral: one
   - Technical meaning: alone/unique
   - Absolute uniqueness
