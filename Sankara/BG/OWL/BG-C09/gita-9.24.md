# Bhagavad Gītā 9.24 - Technical Analysis

## I. Mūla Verse

### Sanskrit
अहं हि सर्वयज्ञानां भोक्ता च प्रभुरेव च ।
न तु मामभिजानन्ति तत्त्वेनातश्च्यवन्ति ते ॥ २४ ॥

### Transliteration
ahaṃ hi sarvayajñānāṃ bhoktā ca prabhur eva ca |
na tu mām abhijānanti tattvenātaś cyavanti te || 24 ||

### Translation
For I am indeed the enjoyer and lord of all sacrifices. But they do not know Me in truth, and therefore they fall.

## II. Śaṅkara Bhāṣya

### Sanskrit
अहं हि सर्वयज्ञानां श्रौतानां स्मार्तानां च सर्वेषां यज्ञानां देवतात्मत्वेन भोक्ता च प्रभुः एव च । मत्स्वामिको हि यज्ञः, 'अधियज्ञोऽहमेवात्र' (भ. गी. ८ । ४) इति हि उक्तम् । तथा न तु माम् अभिजानन्ति तत्त्वेन यथावत् । अतश्च अविधिपूर्वकम् इष्ट्वा यागफलात् च्यवन्ति प्रच्यवन्ते ते ॥

### Transliteration
ahaṃ hi sarvayajñānāṃ śrautānāṃ smārtānāṃ ca sarveṣāṃ yajñānāṃ devatātmatvena bhoktā ca prabhuḥ eva ca | matsvāmiko hi yajñaḥ, 'adhiyajño'ham evātra' (BG 8.4) iti hi uktam | tathā na tu mām abhijānanti tattvena yathāvat | ataśca avidhipūrvakam iṣṭvā yāgaphalāt cyavanti pracyavante te ||

### Translation
For I alone am the enjoyer and lord of all sacrifices, both Vedic and traditional, through being the Self of all deities. Indeed sacrifice belongs to Me as its master, for it has been said "I am indeed the Adhiyajña here" (BG 8.4). Yet they do not know Me in truth, as I am. Therefore, having worshipped without proper understanding, they fall from the fruit of sacrifice.

## III. Term Analysis

### Key Terms from Mūla:

सर्वयज्ञानाम् (sarvayajñānām): of all sacrifices
  - सर्व (sarva): all
  - यज्ञ (yajña): sacrifice
  - Shows complete sovereignty

भोक्ता (bhoktā): enjoyer
  - From भुज् (bhuj): to enjoy
  - Shows sovereign reception

प्रभु (prabhu): lord
  - प्र (pra): forth
  - भू (bhū): to be
  - Shows sovereign mastery

### Key Terms from Bhāṣya:

देवतात्मत्वेन (devatātmatvena): through being Self of deities
  - Shows ultimate identity
  - Perfect sovereignty

यथावत् (yathāvat): as it is
  - यथा (yathā): as
  - वत् (vat): like
  - Shows true reality

यागफलात् (yāgaphalāt): from fruit of sacrifice
  - याग (yāga): sacrifice
  - फल (phala): fruit
  - Shows limitation

## IV. Structuring Points

1. Sovereign Status:
   - Enjoyer of all
   - Lord of all
   - Self of deities

2. Knowledge Lack:
   - Not knowing truth
   - Missing reality
   - Incomplete vision

3. Result Pattern:
   - Limited worship
   - Temporary fruit
   - Inevitable fall
