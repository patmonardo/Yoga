# Bhagavad Gītā Chapter 4, Verse 13

## I. Contextual Introduction

### A. Sanskrit Text
मानुषे एव लोके वर्णाश्रमादिकर्माधिकारः, न अन्येषु लोकेषु इति नियमः किंनिमित्त इति ? अथवा वर्णाश्रमादिप्रविभागोपेताः मनुष्याः मम वर्त्म अनुवर्तन्ते सर्वशः इत्युक्तम् । कस्मात्पुनः कारणात् नियमेन तवैव वर्त्म अनुवर्तन्ते न अन्यस्य इति ? उच्यते —

### B. Transliteration
mānuṣe eva loke varṇāśramādiakarmādhikāraḥ, na anyeṣu lokeṣu iti niyamaḥ kiṃnimitta iti? athavā varṇāśramādipravibhāgopetāḥ manuṣyāḥ mama vartma anuvartante sarvaśaḥ ityuktam | kasmātpunaḥ kāraṇāt niyamena tavaiva vartma anuvartante na anyasya iti? ucyate —

### C. Translation
"Why is there this rule that eligibility for actions of varṇas and āśramas exists only in the human world, not in other worlds? Or it was said that humans endowed with the divisions of varṇas and āśramas follow My path in all ways. For what reason do they necessarily follow Your path alone and not another's? It is said—"

## II. Mūla Verse

### A. Sanskrit Text
चातुर्वर्ण्यं मया सृष्टं गुणकर्मविभागशः ।
तस्य कर्तारमपि मां विद्ध्यकर्तारमव्ययम् ॥ १३ ॥

### B. Transliteration
cāturvarṇyaṃ mayā sṛṣṭaṃ guṇakarmavibhāgaśaḥ |
tasya kartāramapi māṃ viddhyakartāramavyayam || 13 ||

### C. Translation
"The system of four varṇas has been created by Me according to the distribution of guṇas and karma. Though I am its creator, know Me to be the non-doer and immutable."

## III. Bhāṣya

### A. Key Analysis
Śaṅkara's commentary elaborates three crucial points:

1. Distribution of Guṇas:
   - Brāhmaṇa: sattva-predominant
   - Kṣatriya: rajas with subordinate sattva
   - Vaiśya: rajas with subordinate tamas
   - Śūdra: tamas with subordinate rajas

2. Corresponding Karmas:
   - Brāhmaṇa: śama (control), dama (restraint), tapas (austerity)
   - Kṣatriya: valor and power
   - Vaiśya: agriculture and trade
   - Śūdra: service

3. Divine Non-Agency:
   - Creation through māyā
   - Ultimate non-doership
   - Immutable nature

## IV. Technical Analysis

### A. Key Terms and Compounds
1. System Structure:
   - चातुर्वर्ण्य (cāturvarṇya): system of four varṇas
   - गुणकर्मविभाग (guṇakarmavibhāga): distribution by guṇas and karma

2. Divine Nature:
   - कर्तृ/अकर्तृ (kartṛ/akartṛ): doer/non-doer
   - अव्यय (avyaya): immutable

### B. Structural Elements
1. Creation Framework:
   - Through guṇas
   - Through karma
   - Through māyā

2. Paradoxical Teaching:
   - Creator yet non-creator
   - Active yet unchanging
   - Present yet transcendent

## V. Philosophical Analysis

### A. Systematic Position
1. Social-Metaphysical Structure:
   - Based on guṇas
   - Expressed through karma
   - Divinely ordained

2. Divine Relationship:
   - Creation through māyā
   - Ultimate non-involvement
   - Transcendent nature

### B. Development Function
1. Integration of Orders:
   - Social and cosmic
   - Action and being
   - Divine and human

2. Resolution of Paradox:
   - Agency vs non-agency
   - Creation vs immutability
   - Presence vs transcendence
