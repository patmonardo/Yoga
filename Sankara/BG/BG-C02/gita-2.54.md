# BG 2.54 Technical Analysis

## Introduction
### Sanskrit
प्रश्नबीजं प्रतिलभ्य अर्जुन उवाच लब्धसमाधिप्रज्ञस्य लक्षणबुभुत्सया

### Transliteration
praśnabījaṃ pratilabhya arjuna uvāca labdhasamādhiprajñasya lakṣaṇabubhutsayā

### Translation
Having obtained the seed of the question, Arjuna spoke with a desire to know the characteristics of one who has attained samādhi-prajñā.

## Mūla Verse

### Sanskrit
अर्जुन उवाच —
स्थितप्रज्ञस्य का भाषा समाधिस्थस्य केशव ।
स्थितधीः किं प्रभाषेत किमासीत व्रजेत किम् ॥ ५४ ॥

### Transliteration
arjuna uvāca —
sthitaprajñasya kā bhāṣā samādhisthasya keśava |
sthitadhīḥ kiṃ prabhāṣeta kimāsīta vrajeta kim || 54 ||

### Translation
Arjuna said:
What is the description of one established in wisdom, one established in samādhi, O Keśava?
How does one established in understanding speak? How does he sit? How does he move?

## Śaṅkara's Commentary

### Sanskrit
स्थिता प्रतिष्ठिता 'अहमस्मि परं ब्रह्म' इति प्रज्ञा यस्य सः स्थितप्रज्ञः तस्य स्थितप्रज्ञस्य का भाषा किं भाषणं वचनं कथमसौ परैर्भाष्यते समाधिस्थस्य समाधौ स्थितस्य हे केशव । स्थितधीः स्थितप्रज्ञः स्वयं वा किं प्रभाषेत । किम् आसीत व्रजेत किम् आसनं व्रजनं वा तस्य कथमित्यर्थः । स्थितप्रज्ञस्य लक्षणमनेन श्लोकेन पृच्छ्यते ॥

### Transliteration
sthitā pratiṣṭhitā 'ahamasmi paraṃ brahma' iti prajñā yasya saḥ sthitaprajñaḥ tasya sthitaprajñasya kā bhāṣā kiṃ bhāṣaṇaṃ vacanaṃ kathamasau parairbhāṣyate samādhisthasya samādhau sthitasya he keśava | sthitadhīḥ sthitaprajñaḥ svayaṃ vā kiṃ prabhāṣeta | kim āsīta vrajeta kim āsanaṃ vrajanaṃ vā tasya kathamityarthaḥ | sthitaprajñasya lakṣaṇamanena ślokena pṛcchyate ||

### Translation
One whose wisdom (prajñā) is established (sthitā) and grounded (pratiṣṭhitā) in "I am the supreme Brahman" - he is sthitaprajña. What is his description (bhāṣā)? What is his speech (bhāṣaṇa), his utterance (vacana)? How is he described by others (parair bhāṣyate), one who is established in samādhi (samādhistha), O Keśava? How does the one established in understanding (sthitadhī), the sthitaprajña himself speak (prabhāṣeta)? How does he sit (āsīta)? How does he move (vrajeta)? What is his sitting (āsana) and moving (vrajana)? That is the meaning. The characteristics (lakṣaṇa) of the sthitaprajña are being asked about in this verse.

## Technical Analysis

### Key Compound Terms
1. स्थितप्रज्ञ (sthitaprajña):
   - स्थित (sthita) - established, firm
   - प्रज्ञा (prajñā) - wisdom, understanding
   - Karmadhāraya compound: "one whose wisdom is established"

2. समाधिस्थ (samādhistha):
   - समाधि (samādhi) - complete absorption
   - स्थ (stha) - established in
   - Tatpuruṣa compound: "established in samādhi"

3. स्थितधी (sthitadhī):
   - स्थित (sthita) - established
   - धी (dhī) - understanding
   - Parallel formation to स्थितप्रज्ञ

### Structural Elements
1. Question Pattern:
   - का भाषा (kā bhāṣā) - external description
   - किं प्रभाषेत (kiṃ prabhāṣeta) - self-expression
   - किम् आसीत (kim āsīta) - mode of being
   - किं व्रजेत (kiṃ vrajeta) - mode of movement

2. Commentary Structure:
   - Definition of स्थितप्रज्ञ
   - Analysis of भाषा/भाषण/वचन
   - Exposition of आसन/व्रजन
   - Culmination in लक्षण inquiry

### Philosophical Terminology
1. प्रज्ञा (prajñā):
   - Technical term for discriminative wisdom
   - Distinguished from mere बुद्धि or धी
   - Related to समाधि achievement

2. लक्षण (lakṣaṇa):
   - Characteristic mark
   - Defining feature
   - Means of recognition

3. समाधि (samādhi):
   - Complete absorption
   - State of unified consciousness
   - Beyond subject-object division

1. External Description:
   - भाषा (bhāṣā) as characteristic speech
   - परैर्-भाषणम् (parair-bhāṣaṇam) - description by others
   - Objective manifestation

2. Self-Expression:
   - प्र-भाषण (pra-bhāṣaṇa) as authentic speech
   - स्व-वचन (sva-vacana) - self-utterance
   - Direct manifestation

3. Concrete Being:
   - आसन (āsana) as mode of stability
   - व्रजन (vrajana) as mode of movement
   - Complete manifestation

### B. Philosophical Integration
1. Beyond Subject-Object:

   - Original synthetic unity

2. Complete Integration:
