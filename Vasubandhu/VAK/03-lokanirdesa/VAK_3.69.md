# VAK_3.69 — Deva-World Ascent: Six Kāma-Devas and Modes of Desire

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
तत ऊर्ध्वं विमानेषु देवाः कामभुजस्तु षट् ।
द्वंद्वालिङ्गनपाण्याप्तिहसितेक्षितमैथुनाः ॥ VAkK_3.69 ॥
```

## 3. Source Caution

The source reading is:

```iast
tata ūrdhva vimāneṣu devāḥ kāmabhujastu ṣaṭ /
dvaṃdvāliṃṅganapāṇyāptivasitekṣitamaithunāḥ // VAkK_3.69 //
```

The source appears to contain orthographic corruption in the second pāda: `dvaṃdvāliṃṅgana...` and likely `vasita` for `hasita`. The first-pass normalized reading is:

```iast
tataḥ ūrdhvam vimāneṣu devāḥ kāma-bhujaḥ tu ṣaṭ /
dvandva-āliṅgana-pāṇi-āpti-hasita-īkṣita-maithunāḥ //
```

The sequence is provisionally read as modes of sexual/desire contact: pairing, embrace, hand-contact, smiling, and looking. This should be checked carefully against the bhāṣya and printed editions.

## 6. Literal Translation

Above that, in aerial mansions, there are six gods who enjoy desire. Their sexual modes are pairing, embracing, hand-contact, smiling, and looking.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

Beyond the Meru summit complex, six classes of desire-enjoying gods dwell in aerial mansions. Their enjoyment of desire becomes progressively more subtle: from bodily union and embrace to hand-contact, smile, and glance.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.63–3.68 built the Meru summit deva-world: terraces, lower devas, Trāyastriṃśas, Sudarśana, Vaijayanta, gardens, Pārijāta, and Sudharmā. VAkK_3.69 now moves upward beyond that summit-field.

```text
Meru summit deva-world
    → above, in vimānas
    → six kāma-devas
    → differentiated modes of desire-contact
```

The key point is that ascent does not yet mean release from desire. These gods are explicitly `kāma-bhujaḥ`: enjoyers of desire.

## 10. Logical Determination

This verse introduces graded refinement of desire.

```text
gross contact
    → embrace
    → hand-contact
    → smile
    → glance
```

The ascent is therefore not yet negation of kāma. It is a refinement of kāma’s mode of appearance and satisfaction.

## 11. Interpretive Note

This is one of the most useful verses for the VAK_3d thread. It shows exactly why the deva-world must be included in the Path/not-Path discrimination. The heavenly realm can be elevated, subtle, radiant, and ordered, yet still structured by desire-enjoyment.

```text
deva ascent
    ≠ liberation
    = refined kāmadhātu
```

The progression from bodily union to glance also gives a useful bridge into later dhyāna-path analysis: refinement of object-relation is not the same as release from object-relation.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

Light contact point:

```text
The divine ascent refines desire without abolishing desire.
```

This is an important rational-empirical marker. The field rises from city and garden to aerial mansion, but the defining predicate remains `kāma-bhuj`: desire-enjoyer. The form is elevated; the ground remains kāma.
