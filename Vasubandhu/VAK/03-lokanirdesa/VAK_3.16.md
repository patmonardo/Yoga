## 5. Syntax / Construction

This verse classifies womb-entry and related stages by degrees of awareness or confusion.

First:

```iast
saṃprajānan viśaty ekaḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
One enters knowingly / with clear awareness.
```

The action is **viśati**, entering. In context this means entry into the womb or birth-matrix.

Second:

```iast
tiṣṭhaty apy aparaḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
Another also remains [with awareness].
```

The awareness implied from **saṃprajānan** carries forward: another type not only enters with awareness, but also remains with awareness.

Third:

```iast
aparaḥ niṣkrāmaty api
```

Meaning:

```text
A further one also exits [with awareness].
```

So the scale is cumulative:

```text
Type 1:
    aware at entry

Type 2:
    aware at entry and remaining

Type 3:
    aware at entry, remaining, and exit
```

Fourth:

```iast
sarvāṇi mūḍho 'nyaḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
Another is confused in all [these stages].
```

The ordinary being is deluded through entry, residence, and exit.

Fifth:

```iast
nityam aṇḍajaḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
The egg-born is always [confused].
```

The phrase is compressed; the sense is that the egg-born is invariably mūḍha with respect to these stages.

Formal operation:

```text
Womb-entry process:
    entry
    staying
    exit

Awareness profile:
    clear at entry
    clear through residence
    clear through exit
    deluded throughout

Special rule:
    egg-born = always deluded
```

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
One enters knowing clearly; another also remains [knowing]; another also exits [knowing]. Another is deluded in all [stages]; the egg-born is always [deluded].
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
One enters with clear awareness; another also remains with awareness; a further one also exits with awareness. Another is confused in all these stages, and the egg-born is always confused.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
Entry into birth is not uniform. Some beings enter with clear awareness; some maintain awareness while remaining; some even exit with awareness. Others are deluded through the whole process, and egg-born beings are always so. The bhava-cakra therefore contains graded awareness even within rebirth-entry.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The transition into embodied existence is not merely biological. It is a knowledge-conditioned process. The Loka-field records not only birth-mode and destination, but also the clarity or obscuration of knowing during entry, residence, and exit.
```

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK 3.16 gives:

```text
World as graded awareness in birth-entry.
```

The previous verses established the intermediate being and its transition toward a destination. This verse now turns to the actual entry into birth.

The transition has phases:

```text
entry
residence
exit
```

And each phase may be accompanied by:

```text
clarity
or
confusion
```

So birth is not merely:

```text
biological generation
```

It is:

```text
knowledge-conditioned embodiment
```

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This verse is crucial for modeling the bhava-cakra as Yoga-relevant machinery.

Yoga is not merely concerned with stopping gross rebirth. It is concerned with the conditions of awareness, confusion, and transition through the whole cycle of becoming.

```text
Bhava-cakra technical features:
    birth-mode
    destination
    intermediate transition
    desire-direction
    awareness-profile
```

For the Semantic Dataset:

```text
BirthEntryProcess:
    hasPhase Entry
    hasPhase Residence
    hasPhase Exit
    hasAwarenessProfile Clear | Deluded
```

This is still not an ātman entering a womb.

```text
No soul enters.
A conditioned sentient continuity enters a birth-matrix.
```

The kriyā of the bhava-cakra now includes epistemic state:

```text
Kriyā:
    lawful transition-activity
    plus awareness/obscuration profile
```

Karma remains Loka-level here:

```text
KarmaPrincipleFeature
    conditions birth-entry
    but full Agent-level responsibility is still deferred
```

The stronger Yoga formula:

```text
To stop the bhava-cakra,
one must understand not only desire and action,
but also the obscuration of knowing through entry, residence, and exit.
```
