# VAK_3.102 — End-Times: The Sequence of Fire, Water, and Wind Destructions

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
सप्ताग्निना अद्भिरेका एवं गतेऽद्भिः सप्तके पुनः ।
तेजसा सप्तकः पश्चाद्वायुसंवर्तनी ततः ॥ VAkK_3.102 ॥
```

## 3. Source Caution

Source reading:

```iast
saptāgninā adbhirekā evaṃ gate 'dbhiḥ saptake punaḥ /
tejasā saptakaḥ paścādvāyusaṃvartanī tataḥ // VAkK_3.102 //
```

Light separation:

```iast
sapta agninā adbhir ekā evaṃ gate adbhiḥ saptake punaḥ /
tejasā saptakaḥ paścāt vāyu-saṃvartanī tataḥ //
```

The verse gives the order of cosmic destructions. The standard sense is: seven destructions by fire are followed by one destruction by water; this pattern repeats until a set involving seven water destructions is completed; after that comes a further set of seven by fire, and then the wind-destruction. The transmitted line is compressed, so the exact counting should be checked against the bhāṣya.

This is the final kārikā of the Loka-nirdeśa in this source.

## 6. Literal Translation

Seven [destructions] by fire, one by water. When it has gone thus, and again when the sevenfold [set] by water [has occurred], afterwards there is a sevenfold [set] by fire; then the wind-destruction.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

The cosmic destructions are ordered in a vast sequence. Seven fire-destructions are followed by one water-destruction. This pattern continues until the water-destructions form their completed set; after a final sevenfold fire sequence, the wind-destruction occurs.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.100 established three cosmic destructions:

```text
fire
    → water
        → wind
```

VAkK_3.102 gives their long sequence:

```text
7 fire destructions
    → 1 water destruction
        → repeated until the water-set is complete
            → 7 fire destructions
                → wind destruction
```

This closes the cosmic end-times machinery.

## 10. Logical Determination

```text
cosmic destruction
    → not random
    → ordered by counted recurrence
    → culminates in wind-contraction
```

The end of the world-wheel is itself a wheel: repeated fire sequences, water interruptions, and final wind.

## 11. Interpretive Note

The important point is not apocalyptic drama but numerical order.

```text
fire
    → repeated many times
water
    → appears after fire-sets
wind
    → final and rare contraction
```

So even destruction is lawful. The Kośa closes Loka-nirdeśa by placing world-dissolution inside a measured recurrence schema.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

```text
Even the end of the world is governed by sequence, count, and measure.
```

The chapter began with beings and world-domains and ends with the counted destruction of the world-field. Loka is therefore not merely “space.” It is the lawful field of beings, worlds, lifespans, karmic decline, and cosmic contraction.
