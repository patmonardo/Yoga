# VAK_3.100 — End-Times: Three Cosmic Destructions by Fire, Water, and Wind

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
संवर्तन्यः पुनस्तिस्रो भवन्त्यग्न्यम्बुवायुभिः ।
ध्यानत्रयं द्वितीयादि शीर्ष तासां यथाक्रमम् ॥ VAkK_3.100 ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
saṃvartanyaḥ punas tisro bhavanty agny-ambu-vāyubhiḥ /
dhyānatrayaṃ dvitīyādi śīrṣa tāsāṃ yathākramam // VAkK_3.100 //
```

## 3. Source Caution

Source reading:

```iast
saṃvartanyaḥ punastistro bhavantyagnyambuvāyubhiḥ /
dhyānatrayaṃ dvitīyādi śīrṣa tāsāṃ yathākramam // VAkK_3.100 //
```

Light separation:

```iast
saṃvartanyaḥ punas tisraḥ bhavanti agni-ambu-vāyubhiḥ /
dhyāna-trayam dvitīya-ādi śīrṣa tāsām yathā-kramam //
```

The verse moves from the human terminal calamities of VAkK_3.99 to larger cosmic destructions. There are three saṃvartanīs, destructions or contractions, by fire, water, and wind. Their upper limits are the three dhyānas beginning with the second, respectively.

## 6. Literal Translation

Moreover, there are three destructions by fire, water, and wind. The three dhyānas beginning with the second are their upper limits respectively.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

Beyond the human terminal crises, the world-system is subject to three cosmic contractions: destruction by fire, destruction by water, and destruction by wind. Each reaches a different meditative-world limit: up to the second, third, and fourth dhyāna respectively.

```text
    → human-social scale
        → weapon / disease / famine
    → cosmic-environmental scale

```

The end of a world-field is stratified by scale and by upper boundary.

## 11. Interpretive Note

The important term is `saṃvartanī`: not merely a disaster, but a contraction of the world-system. The three destructive media are mapped in order to dhyāna-level limits.

```text
fire
    → burns up to the second dhyāna limit

water
    → reaches the third dhyāna limit

wind
    → reaches the fourth dhyāna limit
```

This is cosmological lingo, not modern prophecy.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

```text
The world-wheel ends through graded contractions of the world-field.
```

The Kośa distinguishes terminal human decline from full cosmic contraction.
