# VAK_3.70 — Deva-World Ascent: Birth-Form of the Kāma-Devas

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
पञ्चवर्षोपमो यावद्दशवर्षोपमः शिशुः ।
संभवत्येषु संपूर्णाः सवस्त्राश्चैव रूपिणः ॥ VAkK_3.70 ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
pañcavarṣopamo yāvad daśavarṣopamaḥ śiśuḥ /
saṃbhavaty eṣu saṃpūrṇāḥ savastrāś caiva rūpiṇaḥ // VAkK_3.70 //
```

## 3. Source Caution

The source reading is:

```iast
pañcavarṣopamo yāvat daśavarṣopamaḥ śiśuḥ /
saṃbhavatyeṣu saṃpūrṇāḥ savastrāścaiva rūpiṇaḥ // VAkK_3.70 //
```

Sandhi has been lightly separated:

```iast
pañca-varṣa-upamaḥ yāvat daśa-varṣa-upamaḥ śiśuḥ /
saṃbhavati eṣu saṃpūrṇāḥ sa-vastrāḥ ca eva rūpiṇaḥ //
```

The first pāda is provisionally read as a range: among these gods, the newborn child appears like a five-year-old up to a ten-year-old. The precise distribution by deva class should be checked against the bhāṣya.

## 6. Literal Translation

Among these, a child is born resembling a five-year-old up to one resembling a ten-year-old. They are complete, clothed, and possessed of form.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

In these kāma-deva realms, birth does not appear as helpless infancy. The newborn divine being appears already childlike within a five-to-ten-year range, fully formed, clothed, and visibly embodied.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.69 described the six desire-enjoying gods and their graded modes of desire-contact. VAkK_3.70 describes how beings arise among them.

```text
six kāma-devas
    → birth in deva class
    → already childlike
    → complete
    → clothed
    → possessed of form
```

This continues the theme of refined kāmadhātu. Deva birth is more complete and aesthetically formed than ordinary human birth, but it remains birth within the world-field.

## 10. Logical Determination

This verse distinguishes deva birth from ordinary biological emergence.

```text
human birth
    → helpless infancy / gradual completion

deva birth
    → immediate formed appearance
```

The deva field refines embodiment by making birth appear already formed, clothed, and visibly complete.

## 11. Interpretive Note

This is important for the observer-territory control. The deva-world is a territory of appearance in which bodies arise already as aesthetic-complete forms. But this does not break the saṃsāric structure.

```text
complete appearance
    ≠ freedom from arising
```

The `rūpiṇaḥ` marker is especially useful: these devas remain possessed of form. Their refinement is still within a field observable by the draṣṭṛ.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

Light contact point:

```text
The divine field presents birth as immediate formed appearance.
```

This is not the abolition of birth, but the aesthetic completion of birth within the kāma-world. The territory observed by Purusha here is elevated and luminous, yet still a territory of arising, form, and enjoyment.
