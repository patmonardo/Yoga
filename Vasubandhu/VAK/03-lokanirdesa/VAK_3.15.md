## 5. Syntax / Construction

This verse explains how the intermediate being moves toward its destination.

First clause:

```iast
viparyasta-matir yāti gatideśaṃ riraṃsayā
```

Meaning:

```text
With inverted cognition, it goes to the destination-place of its gati through longing/desire.
```

The subject is the intermediate being / gandharva from the previous verses.

The key structure is:

```text
viparyasta-matiḥ
    distorted / inverted cognition

riraṃsā
    desire, longing, wish to enjoy/enter

gati-deśa
    destination-place of rebirth
```

So movement toward rebirth is not neutral. It is affectively and cognitively conditioned.

Second clause:

```iast
gandhasthānābhikāmo 'nyaḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
Another [intermediate being] desires the scent-place.
```

This continues the scent-feeding theme from 3.14. The gandharva is drawn toward a scent-location or scent-support.

Third clause:

```iast
ūrdhvapādas tu nārakaḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
But the hell-being is feet-upward.
```

The nāraka-destined intermediate being is described as inverted, with feet upward. This gives a special orientation for descent into hell-birth.

Formal operation:

```text
Intermediate being:
    moves toward gati-destination
    through desire
    under inverted cognition

Another type:
    is drawn toward scent-location

Hell-destined type:
    inverted, feet upward
```

---

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
With inverted mind, it goes to the destination-place of the gati through desire. Another, desiring the scent-place, [goes]; but the hell-being is feet-upward.
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
Driven by longing, and with distorted cognition, the intermediate being goes to the place of its destiny. Another is drawn to the place of scent; but one destined for hell goes inverted, with feet upward.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The transition to rebirth is governed by desire and distortion. The intermediate being moves toward its destined place under inverted cognition, drawn by longing and by scent-support. For hell-birth, the trajectory is marked by inversion itself: the being goes feet upward, as if the whole direction of its transition has been overturned.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The bhava-cakra now reveals transition as affectively directed kriyā. The intermediate state does not merely drift toward the next world-position. It is drawn by desire, misrecognition, scent-support, and destiny-orientation. Becoming is lawful, but it is also distorted by viparyaya.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### viparyasta-mati

```text
viparyasta-mati
    inverted cognition
    distorted mind
    mind turned the wrong way
```

This continues the earlier importance of **viparyaya**.

Working rule:

```text
viparyaya
    structural reversal/error in the appearance-field
```

Here, the reversal is no longer only a static perceptual condition. It directs transition.

```text
viparyasta-mati
    distorted cognition guiding rebirth-movement
```

### yāti

```text
yāti
    goes
    proceeds
    moves toward
```

The verse describes transition as movement toward a destination.

### gati-deśa

```text
gati-deśa
    place of destiny
    destination-place of the gati
```

This is the target of transition. It is not just spatial location; it is a destiny-place within the world-system.

### riraṃsā

```text
riraṃsā
    desire to enjoy
    desire to enter
    longing
```

This is not yet full Agent-level intention. At the Loka level it functions as affective pull in the transition-system.

```text
riraṃsā
    affective orientation toward rebirth
```

### gandha-sthāna

```text
gandha-sthāna
    scent-place
    place/support of scent
```

This connects with **gandha-bhuk** in 3.14. The scent-feeding intermediate being is drawn toward the scent-location.

### abhikāma

```text
abhikāma
    desiring
    longing for
    directed toward by desire
```

The transition-being is not merely mechanically pushed; it is also drawn by desire.

### ūrdhva-pāda

```text
ūrdhva-pāda
    feet-upward
    inverted orientation
```

This marks the special orientation of the hell-destined being.

### nāraka

```text
nāraka
    hell-being
    being destined for hell
```

Here **nāraka** names the hell-destined transition-being, not necessarily one already born in hell.

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK 3.15 gives:

```text
World as desire-directed and inverted transition.
```

Previous verses established:

```text
antarābhava
    exists between death and rebirth
    bears the form of the future existence
    has technical transition-properties
```

This verse adds the dynamic orientation:

```text
transition is directed by:
    desire
    inverted cognition
    scent-location
    destiny-place
```

The bhava-cakra is therefore not only serial continuity. It is affective-cognitive transition.

```text
viparyaya + riraṃsā
    → movement toward gati-deśa
```

The hell-bound case intensifies the logic:

```text
nāraka
    → inverted trajectory
```

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This verse is very important for the kriyā model.

Kriyā is not mere movement. It is lawful, directed, conditioned activity.

```text
Kriyā in VAkK 3.15:
    transition-motion
    shaped by desire
    distorted by viparyaya
    oriented toward destiny-place
```

For the Semantic Dataset, this gives transition features:

```text
TransitionState:
    hasCognitiveCondition: ViparyastaMati
    hasAffectiveCondition: Riramsa
    hasDestination: GatiDesa
    mayHaveSupportAttractor: GandhaSthana
    mayHaveOrientation: UrdhvaPada
```

This also clarifies why the bhava-cakra matters to Yoga.

Yoga is concerned with reversing the machinery of becoming. Here the machinery is described technically:

```text
misrecognition
    → desire
    → destination-orientation
    → rebirth-transition
```

So the Yoga problem is not merely ethical. It is mechanical, cognitive, and ontological:

```text
How does one stop the kriyā of the bhava-cakra?
```

At this stage, however, we remain in Lokanirdeśa:

```text
Karma:
    Loka-level transition-feature

Viparyaya + desire:
    transition-orientation features

Agent-level responsibility:
    deferred
```

The key Organon formula:

```text
The next world-position is not selected by a sovereign self.
It is approached through distorted cognition and desire-conditioned transition.
```
