# VAK_3b — VAkK_3.31

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

विद्याविद्येतरस्पर्शाः अमलक्लिष्टशेषिताः ।
व्यापादानुनयस्पर्शौ सुखवेद्यादयस्त्रयः ॥ VAkK_3.31 ॥

## Sanskrit — IAST

vidyāvidyetarasparśāḥ amalakliṣṭaśeṣitāḥ /
vyāpādānunayasparśau sukhavedyādayas trayaḥ // VAkK_3.31 //

## Source Caution

The source reads:

```text
vidyāvidyetarasparśāḥ amalakliṣṭaśeṣitāḥ /
vyāpādānunayasparśau sukhavedyādayastrayaḥ // VAkK_3.31 //
```

The compound `vidyāvidyetara-sparśāḥ` is resolved cautiously as a threefold classification: contact associated with knowledge, contact associated with ignorance, and other contact. The second pāda correlates these with pure, defiled, and remaining contact.

## Literal Translation

Contacts are of knowledge, ignorance, and the other: pure, defiled, and remaining. The two contacts are aversion and attraction; the three are those beginning with what is to be felt as pleasant.

## Philosophical Translation

Contact is further classified in three ways: as contact associated with knowledge, with ignorance, or with what remains; correspondingly, it may be pure, defiled, or residual. Contact is also classified affectively as aversive or attractive, and as threefold according to what is felt as pleasant, painful, or neither.

## Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.30 classified contact according to its mode:

```text
five resistant contacts
one designation-contact
```

VAkK_3.31 now classifies contact according to its ethical-cognitive and affective quality.

First classification:

```text
vidyā-sparśa
    = pure / stainless contact

avidyā-sparśa
    = defiled contact

itara-sparśa
    = remaining contact
```

Second classification:

```text
vyāpāda-sparśa
    = contact of aversion

anunaya-sparśa
    = contact of attraction / inclination
```

Third classification:

```text
sukha-vedya
    = to be felt as pleasant

duḥkha-vedya
    = to be felt as painful

aduḥkhāsukha-vedya
    = to be felt as neither painful nor pleasant
```

Thus sparśa is not merely the fact of contact. It is classifiable by knowledge/ignorance, purity/defilement, attraction/aversion, and feeling-tone.

## Logical Determination

This verse shows that contact is already internally differentiated.

```text
contact
    is not a bare event

contact
    has cognitive-ethical quality
    has affective direction
    has feeling-tone
```

The logical structure is:

```text
contact as event
    ↓
contact as qualified by vidyā / avidyā / remainder
    ↓
contact as oriented by aversion / attraction
    ↓
contact as felt as pleasant / painful / neutral
```

This helps explain how the wheel reproduces itself. Contact is already qualified before craving explicitly appears.

## Interpretive Note

This verse should make us cautious. Contact is not simply the meeting of subject and object. It is already classified in a dense Abhidharma system.

The verse gives contact-points for later interpretation, but the primary vocabulary must remain:

```text
vidyā / avidyā / itara
amala / kliṣṭa / śeṣita
vyāpāda / anunaya
sukha-vedya / duḥkha-vedya / neutral
```

So the system does not wait until tṛṣṇā to introduce affective direction. Contact itself may already carry the orientation that allows craving, aversion, or neutral feeling to develop.
