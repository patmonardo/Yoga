# VAK_3.94 — Kalpa Machinery: Buddhas and Pratyekabuddhas in the Declining Age

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
बुद्धत्वमपकर्षे हि शताद्यावत्तदुद्भवः ।
द्वयोः प्रत्येकबुद्धानां खड्गः कल्पशतान्वयः ॥ VAkK_3.94 ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
buddhatvam apakarṣe hi śatād yāvat tadudbhavaḥ /
dvayoḥ pratyekabuddhānāṃ khaḍgaḥ kalpaśatānvayaḥ // VAkK_3.94 //
```

## 3. Source Caution

The source reading is:

```iast
buddhatvam apakarṣe hi śatādyāvattadudbhavaḥ /
dvayoḥ pratyekabuddhānāṃ khaḍgaḥ kalpaśatānvayaḥ // VAkK_3.94 //
```

Sandhi has been lightly separated:

```iast
buddhatvam apakarṣe hi śatāt yāvat tad-udbhavaḥ /
dvayoḥ pratyeka-buddhānām khaḍgaḥ kalpa-śata-anvayaḥ //
```

This verse is difficult in compression. The main doctrinal points are provisionally read as follows: buddhahood arises in the declining phase, `apakarṣa`, when lifespan has reached the humanly teachable range marked by `śata` / one hundred years; pratyekabuddhas arise in the two phases, and the rhinoceros-like solitary pratyekabuddha, `khaḍga`, is connected with a hundred kalpas. The exact force of `śatād yāvat tadudbhavaḥ` should be checked against the bhāṣya.

## 6. Literal Translation

Buddhahood indeed [arises] in the decline, from the hundred [year marker] as far as that arising. For pratyekabuddhas, [there is arising] in the two [phases]; the rhinoceros-like one is connected with a hundred kalpas.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

The kalpa-wheel now becomes a field for awakened appearance. Complete buddhahood arises in the declining phase of the lifespan-cycle, in the humanly teachable range marked by the hundred-year lifespan. Pratyekabuddhas are associated with both phases, and the solitary rhinoceros-like pratyekabuddha is linked to a preparation of a hundred kalpas.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.93 defined the mahākalpa as the four-phase wheel of eighty intermediate kalpas. VAkK_3.94 now locates awakened emergence within that wheel.

```text
Buddha
    → arises in apakarṣa
    → declining lifespan phase
    → humanly teachable range marked by śata

Pratyekabuddhas
    → associated with the two phases
    → khaḍga type linked with a hundred kalpas
```

The temporal schema is therefore not cosmology alone. It becomes a condition for the historical arising of awakened beings.

## 10. Logical Determination

This verse gives path-appearance as phase-conditioned.

```text
kalpa-wheel
    → phase of decline
        → Buddha-arising

kalpa-wheel
    → increase/decrease phases
        → pratyekabuddha-arising
```

Awakening is not outside the wheel as an event of appearance. It appears within a determinate temporal condition, even though its truth is not reducible to that condition.

## 11. Interpretive Note

This is important for the phrase “wheel turning.” The wheel is not merely physical cosmology. It conditions when the Dharma can appear as historical teaching.

```text
apakarṣa
    → decline
    → the world becomes teachable
    → Buddha appears
```

The Buddha does not simply appear anywhere in the kalpa. Buddhahood has a temporal placement within the world-cycle. That is precise lingo: `buddhatvam apakarṣe`.

The `khaḍga` pratyekabuddha is also important. `Khaḍga` literally means rhinoceros; here it evokes the solitary, single-going type of pratyekabuddha.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

Light contact point:

```text
The Dharma-wheel appears within a determinate world-wheel phase.
```

For this pass, we stay close to the Kośa. The kalpa machinery has now become a temporal condition for Buddhahood and pratyekabuddhahood. This is not just Indian vast-time imagination; it is a technical doctrine of when awakened forms can arise.
