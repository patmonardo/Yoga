# VAK_3.80 — Time-Measure: Higher Kāma-Devas and Rūpa-Devas

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
द्विगुणोत्तरमूर्ध्वानामुभयं रूपिणां पुनः ।
नास्त्यहोरात्रमायुस्तु कल्पैः स्वाश्रयसंमितैः ॥ VAkK_3.80 ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
dviguṇottaram ūrdhvānām ubhayaṃ rūpiṇāṃ punaḥ /
nāsty ahorātram āyus tu kalpaiḥ svāśrayasaṃmitaiḥ // VAkK_3.80 //
```

## 3. Source Caution

The source reading is:

```iast
dviguṇottaramurdhvānāmubhayaṃ rūpiṇāṃ punaḥ /
nāstyahorātramāyustu kalpaiḥ svāśrayasaṃmitaiḥ // VAkK_3.80 //
```

Sandhi has been lightly separated:

```iast
dvi-guṇa-uttaram ūrdhvānām ubhayam rūpiṇām punaḥ /
na asti ahorātram āyuḥ tu kalpaiḥ sva-āśraya-saṃmitaiḥ //
```

`ubhayam` is provisionally read as referring to both the day-and-night measure and the lifespan measure for the higher kāma-devas: both increase successively by doubling. For the rūpa-devas, however, there is no day-and-night; lifespan is measured in kalpas corresponding to their own bodily support or level. This should be checked against the bhāṣya.

## 6. Literal Translation

For the higher ones, both [measures] are successively doubled. But for the form-possessing beings there is no day-and-night; lifespan is by kalpas measured according to their own support.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

Among the higher kāma-devas, both the divine day-and-night and lifespan increase by successive doubling. But for the rūpa-devas, the ordinary day-and-night structure no longer applies. Their lifespan is measured in kalpas, proportioned to their own level of embodied support.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.79 established that fifty human years equal one day-and-night of the lower kāma-devas, and that their lifespan is five hundred divine years. VAkK_3.80 extends the kāma-deva pattern upward and then distinguishes rūpa-deva temporality.

```text
lower kāma-devas
    → 50 human years = 1 divine day-and-night
    → lifespan: 500 divine years

higher kāma-devas
    → both measures successively double

rūpa-devas
    → no day-and-night
    → lifespan measured in kalpas
    → kalpas proportioned to their own support
```

This is a major refinement of the time block: higher realms do not simply extend ordinary time; they alter the very unit by which duration is counted.

## 10. Logical Determination

This verse distinguishes two temporal regimes.

```text
kāma-deva time
    → day-and-night remains
    → measures increase by doubling

rūpa-deva time
    → no day-and-night
    → lifespan measured by kalpas
```

The shift from kāma to rūpa is therefore not merely an increase in lifespan. It is a change in temporal structure.

## 11. Interpretive Note

The absence of `ahorātra` for the rūpa-devas is significant. Day-and-night belongs to a world ordered by celestial alternation, appearance, and sensibility. In the form realm, duration is no longer articulated by that daily alternation.

```text
kāma-world
    → relative day-and-night

rūpa-world
    → kalpa-duration
    → no daily alternation
```

For the present pass, the important point is textual: the Kośa distinguishes temporal regimes by realm.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

Light contact point:

```text
Higher worlds do not merely last longer; they may have different temporal forms.
```

This continues the movement from spatial measure to time measure. A world-field determines its own clock; at the rūpa level, the clock is no longer day-and-night, but kalpa-scale duration proportioned to the being's own support.
