# VAkK_3.46 — Bhājanaloka: the Water-Maṇḍala and Golden Remainder

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
अपामेकादशोद्वेधं सहस्राणि च विंशतिः ।
अष्टलक्षौच्छ्रयं पश्चाच्छेषं भवति काञ्चनम् ॥ VAkK_3.46 ॥
```

## 3. Source Caution

The source reading is:

```iast
apāmekādaśodvedhaṃ sahasrāṇi ca viṃśatiḥ /
aṣṭalakṣaucchrayaṃ paścāccheṣaṃ bhavati kāñcanam // VAkK_3.46 //
```

Sandhi has been lightly separated for analysis:

```iast
apām ekādaśa-udvedham sahasrāṇi ca viṃśatiḥ /
aṣṭa-lakṣa-ucchrayaṃ paścāt śeṣaṃ bhavati kāñcanam //
```

The numerical expression should be checked against Pradhan and the bhāṣya in Pass 2. The first-pass interpretation takes the verse to describe the water-maṇḍala as having an elevation of eleven lakṣas plus twenty thousand, followed by the formation of a golden remainder eight lakṣas high.

## 6. Literal Translation

Of the waters, the elevation is eleven and twenty thousand; afterwards, the remainder, having a height of eight lakṣas, becomes gold.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

After the wind-maṇḍala, the Kośa gives the water-support. This water-mass is measured vertically, and from it a remaining portion becomes the golden support-field. The cosmological foundation therefore proceeds by ordered support: wind, water, and then gold.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.46 continues the exposition of the container-world foundations. VAkK_3.45 established the `vāyumaṇḍala`, the wind-support beneath the world-container. This verse now introduces the water-mass and the golden remainder.

The sequence is important:

```text
vāyu-maṇḍala
    → ap-maṇḍala
        → kāñcana
```

The world-container is not yet the visible inhabited world. It is still being built as a layered support-field. The text first determines the underlying supports before presenting Meru, mountains, seas, continents, heavens, and hells.

## 10. Logical Determination

```text
support as force
    → support as fluid mass
        → support as golden solidity
```

The logical movement is from invisible dynamism to fluid support to stable base. The cosmological field becomes increasingly determinate. Wind supports; water gathers; gold stabilizes.

This remains first-pass structural description. The verse does not yet require a symbolic or yogic reading. Its immediate function is to continue the determinate construction of `bhājanaloka`.

## 11. Interpretive Note

The verse should be read as part of the Kośa's attempt to determine a complete world-field. The point is not merely that the cosmos contains wind, water, and gold. Rather, the world of beings presupposes a structured receptacle. That receptacle is layered, measured, and ordered.

For Organon purposes, this suggests that the empirical field is not introduced as an unstructured expanse. It is generated through support-determinations:

```text
beneath
    measure
        remainder
            stable support
```

The `śeṣa` is especially important. A remainder becomes a new foundational determination. This may become significant in Pass 2 and Pass 3 when the bhāṣya is examined for the exact process by which water gives rise to gold.
