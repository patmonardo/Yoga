## 5. Syntax / Construction

The verse has two major operations.

First operation:

```iast
ārūpyadhātur asthānaḥ upapattyā caturvidhaḥ
```

This defines the ārūpyadhātu through a paradoxical structure:

```text
formless-domain:
    without station / non-spatial
    yet fourfold by rebirth
```

Feature extraction:

```text
Domain identification:
    ārūpya-dhātuḥ

Negation of station:
    a-sthānaḥ

Fourfold determination:
    caturvidhaḥ

Instrumental ground of the fourfoldness:
    upapattyā
```

Second operation:

```iast
nikāyaṃ jīvitaṃ cātra niśritā cittasantatiḥ
```

This gives the support-basis for continuity in the formless domain:

```text
Here, the mind-continuity is dependent on nikāya and jīvita.
```

Feature extraction:

```text
Subject:
    citta-santatiḥ

Predicate:
    niśritā

Supports:
    nikāyam
    jīvitam

Domain-locator:
    atra
```

So the verse performs a shift:

```text
spatial station
    negated

rebirth-type
    retained

continuity
    supported by class and life
```

---

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
The formless-domain is without station, fourfold by rebirth. Here, the continuity of mind is dependent on class and life.
```

A slightly fuller literal rendering:

```text
The formless-domain has no station; it is fourfold through rebirth. In this domain, the continuity of mind rests upon class and life.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The formless-domain is not organized by place or station. Nevertheless, it is fourfold according to modes of rebirth. In it, the stream of mind continues by dependence upon class-continuity and life-continuity.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The ārūpya-domain is World at the limit of spatial placement. It is not stationed like the form-domain, yet it remains within World because rebirth, class, life, and mind-continuity still obtain.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### ārūpya-dhātu

```text
ārūpya-dhātu
    formless-domain
    domain of non-form
    domain beyond rūpa but not beyond conditioned existence
```

We should not render this as “immaterial heaven” in the main vocabulary. The direct structure is:

```text
a-rūpya
    not-form / formlessness

dhātu
    domain
```

So:

```text
ārūpyadhātu
    formless-domain
```

### asthāna

This is one of the verse’s first real technical challenges.

```text
a-sthāna
    without station
    non-stationed
    without place
```

Since verse 3.2 used **sthāna** for the seventeen stations of rūpadhātu, this negation is deliberate:

```text
rūpadhātu:
    saptadaśa-sthānaḥ
    having seventeen stations

ārūpyadhātu:
    asthānaḥ
    without station
```

So **asthāna** should not be flattened to “placeless” only. It directly negates the prior **sthāna** structure. Best working translation:

```text
asthānaḥ
    non-stationed
```

Then in explanation:

```text
not organized by spatial or station-like loci
```

### upapatti

```text
upapatti
    rebirth
    arising-into-birth
    birth-occurrence
```

The instrumental **upapattyā** is crucial:

```text
upapattyā caturvidhaḥ
    fourfold by rebirth
    fourfold according to mode of rebirth
```

So the formless domain is not differentiated by place but by **mode of arising**.

### caturvidha

```text
caturvidha
    fourfold
    of four kinds
```

The verse itself does not list the four here. For the primary verse analysis, we say:

```text
fourfold according to rebirth
```

and leave the enumeration for later unless the bhāṣya demands it.

### nikāya

This is a serious vocabulary challenge.

```text
nikāya
    class
    group
    collection
    continuity-class
```

Here it is in the accusative as something upon which **citta-santati** depends.

The problem:

```text
nikāya
    cannot simply mean “body” here
    because ārūpyadhātu is formless.

But:
    it also cannot be merely abstract “category”
    because it functions as a support for continuity.
```

Working translation:

```text
Literal layer:
    class

Technical note:
    class-continuity
```

### jīvita

```text
jīvita
    life
    life-faculty
    life-continuity
```

Given Chapter 2’s indriya analysis, **jīvitendriya** is already a technical life-faculty. Here **jīvita** supports the continuity of mind in the absence of form-station.

Working translation:

```text
jīvita
    life-continuity
```

### citta-santati

```text
citta
    mind

santati
    continuity
    stream
    succession
```

So:

```text
citta-santati
    mind-continuity
    stream of mind
```

Since the Sanskrit is **citta**, not **vijñāna**, “mind-continuity” is safe here.

### niśrita

```text
niśrita
    dependent on
    resting on
    supported by
```

This is a support relation, not identity. The mind-continuity is not the same as nikāya and jīvita; it rests upon them.

### dharmadhātu exclusion note

The opening domain-list gives:

```text
kāmadhātu
rūpadhātu
ārūpyadhātu
```

It does not give **dharmadhātu** as a fourth world-domain. This matters.

```text
ārūpyadhātu
    ≠ dharmadhātu
    ≠ Absolute Reason
    ≠ liberation

ārūpyadhātu
    = the highest abstraction of World
    = conditioned formless continuity
```

Formlessness is not Absolute because it stands in relation to Form:

```text
rūpa ↔ ārūpya
form ↔ formless
station ↔ non-station
```

Thus:

```text
Formless is not Absolute.
Formless is the negative partner of Form.
Dharma begins where this relation is grasped as law.
```

This note belongs to the seed pass as a warning; a fuller development belongs to the later bhāṣya pass.

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK_3.1:

```text
World as counted desire-domain.
```

VAkK_3.2:

```text
World as vertical hierarchy of refined form.
```

VAkK_3.3:

```text
World as non-spatial continuity.
```

The sequence is systematic:

```text
kāmadhātu
    differentiated by destiny / inhabitant / desire-placement

rūpadhātu
    differentiated by station / dhyāna / form-level

ārūpyadhātu
    not differentiated by station
    but still differentiated by rebirth
    and sustained by mind-continuity
```

So World is not reducible to physical space.

The formless-domain forces a higher abstraction:

```text
World can persist even when station is negated.

What remains?
    rebirth
    class
    life
    mind-continuity
```

## 10. Organon Interpretation

The opening triad of domains becomes visible:

```text
kāmadhātu
    world as desire-placement

rūpadhātu
    world as form-station

ārūpyadhātu
    world as non-spatial continuity
```

This is a rationalized ascending map of conditioned manifestation:

```text
desire
    ↓
form
    ↓
formlessness
```

But the crucial point is:

```text
formlessness is still World.
```

It is not liberation, because:

```text
upapatti remains
jīvita remains
nikāya remains
citta-santati remains
```

That is, rebirth, life-continuity, class-belonging, and mind-continuity remain. So the formless domain is not the Absolute. It is the subtlest world-domain.

Hegel-clean formula:

```text
The negation of form is not yet freedom from conditioned existence.

It is only the world’s abstraction from form.
```

Technical warning:

```text
Do not mistake abstraction for liberation.
Do not mistake formlessness for the Absolute.
Do not mistake non-stationed being for freedom from bhava.
```

Organon formula:

```text
VAkK_3.3 gives World at the limit of spatiality.

The domain is non-stationed,
but not unconditioned.

It remains a world-domain because continuity still depends on birth, class, life, and mind-stream.
```

## Working Summary

```text
VAkK_3.3 establishes the ārūpyadhātu as the formless-domain.

It is:
    non-stationed,
    fourfold by rebirth,
    and sustained by mind-continuity dependent on class and life.

This completes the opening domain triad:
    kāmadhātu      = desire-placement
    rūpadhātu      = form-station
    ārūpyadhātu    = non-spatial continuity

The result:
    formlessness is still World.
    abstraction from form is not liberation.
```
