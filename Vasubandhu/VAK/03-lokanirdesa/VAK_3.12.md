## 5. Syntax / Construction

This verse gives a chain of reasons for accepting **antarābhava**.

The implied predicate is:

```iast
asti
```

Meaning:

```text
there is [antarābhava]
```

The verse gathers several supporting reasons:

```text
1. saha ekatra dvaya-abhāvāt
    because two do not exist simultaneously in one locus

2. asantānāt
    because otherwise there would be no continuity

3. dvaya-udayāt
    because of the arising of two / twofold arising

4. kaṇṭhokteḥ
    because of explicit statement

5. gandharvāt
    because of the gandharva

6. pañcokteḥ
    because of the mention of five

7. gati-sūtrataḥ
    because of the gati-sūtra / discourse on destinies
```

The verse is compressed and argumentative. It does not describe the intermediate existence directly; it defends its doctrinal necessity.

Formal operation:

```text
Antarābhava exists
    because continuity requires an intermediate transition
    and because doctrinal statements support it.
```

The proof has two sides:

```text
logical / continuity reasons:
    two cannot occupy one locus together
    without antarābhava there is no continuity
    the arising of the two requires mediation

scriptural / doctrinal reasons:
    explicit statement
    gandharva
    mention of five
    gati-sūtra
```

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
Because two are absent together in one place, because otherwise there is no continuity, because of the arising of two; and because of explicit statement, there is [intermediate existence] from the gandharva, from the mention of five, and from the gati-sūtra.
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
There is intermediate existence: because two do not occur together in one locus, because without it there would be no continuity, and because of twofold arising; also because it is explicitly stated, because of the gandharva, because of the mention of five, and because of the discourse on destinies.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The intermediate existence is not introduced mythologically but argued for. The system requires it because continuity between death and rebirth cannot be secured by placing two existences in one locus, nor by leaving a gap in the series. Scriptural/doctrinal evidence also supports it: explicit statement, the gandharva, the mention of five, and the gati-sūtra.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The Loka-field requires a transition-operator. Without such an operator, the bhava-cakra would either collapse two states into one locus or break continuity altogether. Antarābhava therefore functions as the transition-phase through which lawful becoming remains serial without becoming self-substantial.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### saha

```text
saha
    together
    simultaneously
```

The verse denies simultaneous placement of two relevant states in one locus.

### ekatra

```text
ekatra
    in one place
    in one locus
```

The problem is not merely temporal. It is locative/logical: two states cannot simply be collapsed into one locus.

### dvaya-abhāva

```text
dvaya-abhāva
    absence of two
    non-presence of two together
```

This supports the need for mediation. Death-existence and rebirth-existence are not simply two simultaneous states occupying one and the same locus.

### asantāna

```text
asantāna
    non-continuity
    absence of series
    break in continuity
```

This is the negative danger. Without antarābhava, there would be a break in the continuity-series.

Working rule:

```text
antarābhava protects santāna from interruption
without positing ātman.
```

### dvaya-udaya

```text
dvaya-udaya
    arising of two
    twofold arising
```

This phrase is compact and should be marked for bhāṣya-pass review. At seed-pass level, it indicates that the two poles of the transition require explanation: the ending of one existence and the arising of another cannot be reduced to one unmediated state.

### kaṇṭhokti

```text
kaṇṭhokti
    explicit statement
    direct utterance
    clear doctrinal saying
```

This is doctrinal evidence. The text appeals not only to logical necessity but also to explicit teaching.

### gandharva

```text
gandharva
    intermediate transition-being
    gandharva
```

In this context, **gandharva** is technical. It should not be reduced to mythological ornament.

Working rule:

```text
gandharva
    names the intermediate being in the doctrine of rebirth-transition
```

It does not imply a permanent soul-substance.

### pañcokti

```text
pañca-ukti
    mention of five
```

This likely refers to a doctrinal enumeration in which a fifth member requires acknowledgment of intermediate existence. The exact reference belongs to the bhāṣya-pass.

Seed-pass restraint:

```text
Mark the proof-structure.
Do not over-specify the citation until bhāṣya review.
```

### gati-sūtra

```text
gati-sūtra
    discourse on destinies
    sūtra concerning gati
```

This again provides doctrinal support. The verse invokes a scriptural/discursive source tied to destinies.

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK 3.12 gives:

```text
World as transition-continuity requiring doctrinal proof.
```

The previous verse gave:

```text
continuity without identity-substance
```

This verse now argues:

```text
antarābhava must be accepted
because otherwise the transition-system fails
```

Two failures are avoided:

```text
collapse:
    two states in one locus

break:
    no continuity between states
```

Antarābhava mediates between these extremes:

```text
not collapse
not break
but mediated transition
```

So the logical form is:

```text
Death-state
    ↓
Intermediate transition-state
    ↓
Rebirth-state
```

This is **kriyā** at the Loka level: lawful activity/transition, not yet full Agent-level Karma.

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This is where the companion to Hegel's Logic becomes visible.

Hegel gives the pure logical movement of determinations. Here, the Abhidharma system gives the **kriyā** of Loka: the lawful activity by which becoming transitions without self-substance.

```text
Logic:
    determination

Kriyā:
    lawful transition/activity of determination

Loka:
    the appearance-field where that transition is systematized
```

For Organon, this means:

```text
The Semantic Dataset cannot merely classify states.
It must encode lawful transition between states.
```

Thus:

```text
State_t
    cannot simply equal State_t+1
    and cannot be disconnected from State_t+1

There must be:
    TransitionPhase
    ContinuityRule
    DestinationOrientation
```

At the Loka level, Karma remains only a dataset feature:

```text
KarmaPrincipleFeature
    transition-principle
    lawful orientation of continuity
    not yet full agential/juridical karma
```

The bhava-cakra is therefore not just a diagram. It is a transition-system:

```text
bhava-cakra
    wheel of becoming
    generated through lawful serial transition
    without ātman
```

The key rule:

```text
Antarābhava prevents both collapse and rupture.
```
