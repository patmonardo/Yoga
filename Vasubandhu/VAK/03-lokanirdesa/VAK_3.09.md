## 5. Syntax / Construction

This verse distributes the four birth-modes introduced in VAkK 3.8 across classes of beings.

First:

```iast
caturdhā narāḥ tiryañcaḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
Humans and animals are fourfold.
```

This means humans and animals can be classified across the four yonis:

```text
aṇḍaja
    egg-born

jarāyuja
    womb-born

saṃsvedaja
    moisture-born

upapāduka
    spontaneously arisen
```

Second:

```iast
nārakā upapādukāḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
Hell-beings are spontaneously arisen.
```

Third:

```iast
antarābhavadevāś ca
```

Meaning:

```text
Intermediate-existence beings and gods are also spontaneously arisen.
```

The predicate **upapādukāḥ** carries forward from the previous pāda:

```text
nārakāḥ
antarābhavāḥ
devāḥ
    → upapādukāḥ
```

Fourth:

```iast
pretā api jarāyujāḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
Pretas also are womb-born.
```

The **api** is important. It does not necessarily restrict all pretas to womb-birth; rather, it marks that pretas may also be jarāyuja in the relevant classification.

Formal operation:

```text
Humans + animals:
    fourfold by yoni

Hell-beings + intermediate beings + gods:
    spontaneously arisen

Pretas:
    also womb-born
```

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
Humans and animals are fourfold. Hell-beings are spontaneously arisen; intermediate-existence beings and gods also [are spontaneously arisen]. Pretas too are womb-born.
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
Humans and animals occur in all four birth-modes. Hell-beings, intermediate-existence beings, and gods are spontaneously arisen; pretas also may be womb-born.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The four birth-modes are not distributed uniformly. Humans and animals span the full fourfold range. Hell-beings, intermediate beings, and gods arise spontaneously. Pretas are also included among the womb-born. The world-field therefore assigns birth-mode as a lawful feature according to class of sentient continuity.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The Loka-field now becomes a typed generation-system. Sentient continuities are not merely placed in domains; they are instantiated through permitted birth-modes. Birth-mode is a lawful dataset feature, not an arbitrary biological accident.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### caturdhā

```text
caturdhā
    fourfold
    in four ways
```

This carries forward the four **yoni** classes from VAkK 3.8.

### nara

```text
nara
    human
    man
```

Here **nara** names the human class. Humans are said to be **caturdhā**, fourfold with respect to yoni.

### tiryañc

```text
tiryañc
    animal
    horizontal-going being
```

**Tiryañc** is the animal destiny/class. Like humans, animals are here said to occur in all four birth-modes.

### nāraka

```text
nāraka
    hell-being
    inhabitant of hell
```

Hell-beings are classified as **upapāduka**, spontaneously arisen.

### upapāduka

```text
upapāduka
    spontaneously arisen
    apparition-like birth
    birth without ordinary womb/egg/moisture process
```

This is one of the four yonis. In this verse it applies to:

```text
nārakāḥ
antarābhavāḥ
devāḥ
```

This is very important for Block C because **antarābhava** first appears here as a spontaneously arisen transition-being.

### antarābhava

```text
antarābhava
    intermediate existence
    between-state existence
    transition-phase between death and rebirth
```

This term will dominate the next mini-block. Here it is only introduced by placement in the yoni classification.

The restraint:

```text
antarābhava is not a soul traveling between lives.
It is a conditioned transition-phase of sentient continuity.
```

### deva

```text
deva
    god
    luminous/celestial being
```

In this verse, devas are included among the spontaneously arisen.

This resonates with the Loka-level indriya/deity apparatus, but we must not overdevelop that here. At this level, deva is a class within the birth-mode distribution.

### preta

```text
preta
    departed being
    preta-class being
    hungry-ghost class
```

The verse says:

```iast
pretā api jarāyujāḥ
```

So pretas are also womb-born. The **api** leaves room for a broader classification; it marks inclusion rather than simple exclusivity.

### jarāyuja

```text
jarāyuja
    womb-born
    placenta-born
```

This is another of the four yonis.

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This verse is a clean Semantic Dataset verse.

It gives a mapping table:

```text
SattvaClass
    hasAllowedBirthMode
        BirthMode
```

The key point is that birth is not treated as an untyped event. It is governed by lawful compatibility between class and mode.

```text
Class of being
    constrains
Mode of arising
```

At the Loka level, Karma remains only a transition-feature of the dataset:

```text
Loka-level Karma:
    built into the apparatus of appearance
    built into indriya and world-function
    built into birth-mode and transition
```

But this is still not full Agent-level Karma:

```text
Agent-level Karma:
    inward Dharma-dhātu
    responsibility-space
    reflective/juridical access to Law
```

So for this verse, the disciplined statement is:

```text
The birth-mode distribution is karmically structured at the Loka level,
but the full doctrine of agential karma is deferred to Karmanirdeśa.
```

The verse also introduces **antarābhava** without yet explaining it. In Organon terms, this is the first explicit sign that the world-field must include a transition-object:

```text
Current life-state
    ↓
Intermediate transition-state
    ↓
Next birth-mode
```

But no self-substance is implied. The transition belongs to conditioned continuity.
