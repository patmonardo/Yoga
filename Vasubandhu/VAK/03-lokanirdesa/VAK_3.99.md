# VAK_3.99 — End-Times: Three Calamities at the Exit of a Kalpa

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
कल्पस्य शस्त्ररोगाभ्यां दुर्भिक्षेण च निर्गमः ।
दिवसान् सप्त मासांश्च वर्षाणि च यथाक्रमम् ॥ VAkK_3.99 ॥
```

## 3. Source Caution

Source reading:

```iast
kalpasya śastrarogābhyāṃ durbhikṣeṇa ca nirgamaḥ /
divasān sapta māsāṃśca varṣāṇi ca yathākramam // VAkK_3.99 //
```

Light separation:

```iast
kalpasya śastra-rogābhyām durbhikṣeṇa ca nirgamaḥ /
divasān sapta māsān ca varṣāṇi ca yathā-kramam //
```

The verse lists three end-time calamities: weapon, disease, and famine. Their durations are seven days, seven months, and seven years respectively, in order.

## 6. Literal Translation

The exit of the kalpa is by weapon and disease, and by famine: seven days, seven months, and seven years respectively.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

The kalpa’s terminal human crisis has three forms: a weapon-calamity, a disease-calamity, and a famine-calamity. They last respectively seven days, seven months, and seven years.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

```text
kalpa-exit / nirgama
    → weapon-calamity
        → 7 days
    → disease-calamity
        → 7 months
    → famine-calamity
        → 7 years
```

This continues the decline sequence from karmapatha predominance and ten-year lifespan.

## 10. Logical Determination

```text
ethical decline
    → lifespan contraction
        → terminal social calamity
            → kalpa-exit
```

The end-time report is not random catastrophe; it is the crisis-form of a declining world-age.

## 11. Interpretive Note

The three calamities are socially and bodily concrete:

```text
weapon
    → mutual violence

disease
    → bodily collapse

famine
    → material deprivation
```

The durations are not identical; the verse gives a precise ordered mapping.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

```text
The world-age exits through determinate crisis-forms.
```

The end of a human kalpa-phase is measured by the kind of crisis and by its duration.
