## 5. Syntax / Construction

This verse gives the formal status of **antarābhava**.

First clause:

```iast
ekākṣepād asāv aiṣyat-pūrvakāla-bhavākṛtiḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
Because of a single ākṣepa, this one has the form of the future existence before its time.
```

The subject is **asau**, “this one,” referring back to the intermediate being / antarābhava.

The key term is:

```text
eka-ākṣepa
    one projection
    one impulsion
    one throwing-forward
```

At seed-pass level, this should be read as the single karmic projection/impulsion that shapes the intermediate being toward the next existence. But the full doctrine of **ākṣepa** belongs to the bhāṣya-pass and to the later karma analysis.

The compound **aiṣyat-pūrva-kāla-bhava-ākṛtiḥ** is dense:

```text
aiṣyat
    future / about-to-come

pūrva-kāla
    prior time / before its time

bhava-ākṛti
    form of existence
```

So:

```text
antarābhava has the form of the coming existence
before the actual moment of rebirth.
```

Second clause:

```iast
sa punar maraṇāt pūrva upapattikṣaṇāt paraḥ
```

The intended temporal relation is:

```text
maraṇāt paraḥ
    after death

upapatti-kṣaṇāt pūrvaḥ
    before the moment of rebirth
```

The compact verse order places **pūrva** and **para** in a difficult sequence. Analytically:

```text
saḥ
    that intermediate being

punaḥ
    further

maraṇāt paraḥ
    after death

upapatti-kṣaṇāt pūrvaḥ
    before the moment of rebirth
```

Thus the formal definition is:

```text
Antarābhava:
    after death
    before the moment of rebirth
    bearing the form of the future existence
```

---

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
Because of a single projection, this [intermediate being] has the form of the future prior-time existence. Further, it is after death and before the moment of rebirth.
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
By one karmic projection, this intermediate being assumes the form of the coming existence before that existence has actually arisen. It is after death and before the moment of rebirth.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The intermediate being is not a neutral ghost between lives. It is already shaped by a single projection toward the next birth. It bears the form of the future existence before rebirth has occurred, and its temporal position is precisely between death and the moment of new arising.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The bhava-cakra is not a vague cycle. It has a transition-phase whose form is already oriented toward the next state. The intermediate being is after the prior termination and before the next instantiation, yet already shaped by the future birth-form.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### eka-ākṣepa

```text
eka-ākṣepa
    single projection
    single impulsion
    single throwing-forward
```

This is a key transition term. It indicates that the intermediate being is shaped by one orienting projection toward the future birth.

Seed-pass restraint:

```text
ākṣepa here is treated as Loka-level transition-projection.
The full karmic mechanics of projection are deferred to Karmanirdeśa and the bhāṣya-pass.
```

### asau / saḥ

```text
asau / saḥ
    this one / that one
```

Both pronouns refer to the **antarābhava**, the intermediate being discussed in the previous verses.

### aiṣyat

```text
aiṣyat
    future
    coming
    about-to-come
```

The intermediate being bears the form of the future existence, not merely a residue of the past one.

### pūrva-kāla

```text
pūrva-kāla
    prior time
    before the time
```

This means the future birth-form is present in advance, before the actual moment of rebirth.

### bhava-ākṛti

```text
bhava-ākṛti
    form of existence
    shape of becoming
    configuration of a coming life-state
```

This is the crucial ontological marker:

```text
antarābhava bears the ākṛti of the coming bhava.
```

### maraṇa

```text
maraṇa
    death
    death-event
```

The intermediate being is **after death**, not identical with the previous life-state.

### upapatti-kṣaṇa

```text
upapatti-kṣaṇa
    moment of rebirth
    instant of arising-into-birth
```

The intermediate being is **before the moment of rebirth**, not yet the new born state.

### para / pūrva

```text
para
    after
    subsequent

pūrva
    before
    prior
```

The verse defines the interval through these temporal relations:

```text
after death
before rebirth
```

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK 3.13 gives:

```text
World as future-oriented transition-form.
```

The previous verses established:

```text
antarābhava exists
    between death and rebirth
    preserving continuity without ātman
```

This verse adds:

```text
antarābhava has form
    but its form is future-oriented
```

The formal structure:

```text
Death-state
    ↓
Intermediate-state bearing future-birth form
    ↓
Moment of rebirth
```

The transition is therefore not empty. It is already oriented and shaped.

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This verse is central for the technical manual of **bhava-cakra**.

The wheel of becoming is not just repetition. It is lawful transition through future-oriented form.

```text
bhava-cakra
    not merely circular recurrence
    but transition through projected future-form
```

In Organon terms:

```text
CurrentState terminates.
TransitionState appears.
TransitionState bears FutureStateForm.
FutureState instantiates at upapatti-kṣaṇa.
```

This is precisely the kriyā companion to Logic:

```text
Logic:
    determines the form

Kriyā:
    carries the form through transition

Bhava-cakra:
    cycles the form through becoming
```

At the Loka level, this is still not full Agent-level Karma. It is Karma as dataset feature:

```text
KarmaPrincipleFeature
    appears as ākṣepa
    projects the future birth-form
    orients transition toward upapatti
```

But we defer the full catastrophe of agential responsibility to Karmanirdeśa.

The key modeling rule:

```text
The intermediate state is not past residue only.
It is already future-shaped.
```
