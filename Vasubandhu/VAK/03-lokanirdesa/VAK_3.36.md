## Grammar

```text

    = another matter has been said and will be said

atra tu kleśaḥ / kleśāḥ iṣyate
    = but here affliction / afflictions are intended

bīja-vat ... mūla-vat ... vṛkṣa-vat ... tuṣa-vat tathā
    = like seed, like root, like tree, and like husk, etc.
```

The verse uses simile-language to return from the manopavicāra classification to the kleśa / karma / vastu analysis.

## Literal Translation

Something else has been said and will be said; but here affliction is intended: like seed, like nāga, like root, like tree, and likewise like husk.

## Philosophical Translation

Although other analyses have been and will be given elsewhere, here the concern is with affliction. Kleśa is presented through a sequence of generative similes: seed, root, tree, husk, and related images. The point is that affliction functions as a generative and sustaining condition within the wheel.

## Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.36 begins a new micro-section after the analysis of contact, feeling, and mental approaches.

It returns to the compressed structure from 3.26–3.27:

```text
kleśa
karma
vastu / phala
```

Here the first focus is `kleśa`.

The similes suggest that kleśa is not merely an accidental disturbance. It is a generative condition in the wheel:

```text
kleśa as seed
kleśa as root
kleśa as developed growth
kleśa as covering / husk
```

## Logical Determination

The logical role of kleśa is generative and reproductive.

```text
kleśa
    is seed-like
    root-like
    growth-like
    covering-like
```

This connects directly back to 3.27:

```text
kleśa
    → kriyā
        → vastu
            → kleśa again
```

VAkK_3.36 now begins to explain why that recurrence works: kleśa functions as a seed/root structure within conditioned experience.

## Interpretive Note

This verse should be kept modest until the Bhāṣya controls each simile. The safe result is:

```text
kleśa is treated as a generative condition of the wheel.
```

This belongs to the first part of Lokanirdeśa as the world of conditioned human experience. The wheel is not merely described externally; its generative root is being identified.

## Organon Interpretation

Light contact point:

This is not yet a full speculative mapping. It is a marker for the structure of recurrence.
