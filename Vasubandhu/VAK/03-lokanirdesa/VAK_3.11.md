## 5. Syntax / Construction

This verse argues for uninterrupted continuity of becoming and rejects an inadequate analogy.

First:

```iast
vrīhisantānasādharmyād avicchinnabhavodbhavaḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
Because of similarity to a rice-seed continuity, the arising of existence is uninterrupted.
```

The logic is:

```text
rice-seed continuity
    seed → sprout → plant → seed

bhava-continuity
    prior existence → intermediate transition → next existence
```

The point is not identity of substance, but continuity of series.

```text
santāna
    serial continuity
    not a permanent self
```

Second:

```iast
pratibimbam asiddhatvād asāmyāc cānidarśanam
```

Meaning:

```text
A reflection is not a valid example, because it is not established and because it is dissimilar.
```

The verse accepts the rice-continuity analogy but rejects the reflection analogy.

Formal operation:

```text
Accept:
    vrīhi-santāna
    because it shows serial arising without identity-substance

Reject:
    pratibimba
    because it is not established and not sufficiently similar
```

---

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
Because of similarity to a rice-seed continuity, the arising of existence is uninterrupted. A reflection is not an example, because it is unestablished and because of dissimilarity.
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
The arising of existence is uninterrupted, like the continuity of rice-seed. A reflected image is not a valid illustration, since it is not established and is dissimilar.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The transition between lives is to be understood as a continuity-series, not as the passage of a self-substance. Like the series of rice-seed and sprout, existence arises without interruption. The analogy of a reflection is rejected, because it does not adequately establish the kind of continuity required here.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The Loka-field requires continuity without identity. The next aggregate-state arises in an uninterrupted series, but no ātman travels through the series. The proper model is generative continuity, not mirror-image reflection.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### vrīhi

```text
vrīhi
    rice
    rice-seed
```

Here **vrīhi** functions as the accepted analogy. A rice-seed series shows continuity through generative transformation.

### santāna

```text
santāna
    stream
    continuity
    serial continuum
    succession-series
```

This is one of the most important terms in Block C.

Working rule:

```text
santāna carries continuity without ātman.
```

The continuity is real enough to explain transition, but not substantialized into a permanent self.

### sādharmya

```text
sādharmya
    similarity
    common character
    analogy by shared dharma
```

The argument depends on similarity, not identity. The rice-seed series is not the same as bhava-continuity; it is similar in the relevant respect.

### avicchinna

```text
avicchinna
    uninterrupted
    unbroken
    not cut off
```

This describes the continuity of **bhava-udbhava**. The sequence of becoming is not broken by a gap requiring a self-substance to bridge it.

### bhava-udbhava

```text
bhava-udbhava
    arising of existence
    arising of becoming
```

The verse concerns the arising of **bhava**, not the transmigration of a self.

### pratibimba

```text
pratibimba
    reflection
    reflected image
```

This analogy is rejected. It is not the right model for antarābhava and continuity.

### asiddhatva

```text
asiddhatva
    not-establishedness
    lack of proof
    unestablished status
```

The reflection analogy fails because it is **asiddha**, not established in the required sense.

### asāmya

```text
asāmya
    dissimilarity
    lack of sufficient similarity
```

Even if reflection were considered, it is not similar enough to the continuity being explained.

### anidarśana

```text
anidarśana
    not an example
    not a valid illustration
    invalid analogy
```

The verse is doing argument-analysis. It distinguishes an acceptable analogy from an unacceptable one.

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK 3.11 gives:

```text
World as uninterrupted continuity without identity-substance.
```

VAkK 3.10 defined:

```text
antarābhava
    the transition-state between death and rebirth
```

VAkK 3.11 explains how such transition can be intelligible:

```text
not by self-substance
not by mere reflection
but by santāna-continuity
```

The formal distinction:

```text
continuity
    yes

identity-substance
    no
```

The accepted analogy:

```text
seed-series
    generative continuity
```

The rejected analogy:

```text
reflection
    not established
    dissimilar
```

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This is a crucial Semantic Dataset verse.

The dataset must model transition without inserting a hidden self-object.

```text
Do not model:
    Self travels from state A to state B

Model instead:
    Aggregate-continuity generates next aggregate-state
```

So the operative structure is:

```text
SkandhaState_t
    ↓ continuity / santāna
SkandhaState_t+1
```

But because we are still in Lokanirdeśa, karma remains a Loka-level transition-feature:

```text
KarmaPrincipleFeature
    conditions transition
    does not yet unfold full Agent-level responsibility
```

The bhava-cakra is beginning to become visible:

```text
bhava-cakra
    wheel of becoming
    continuity of existence-series
    not transmigration of a permanent self
```

This verse therefore supplies one of the central rules for the Organon model:

```text
Continuity is serial, not substantial.
Transition is lawful, not self-identical.
```
