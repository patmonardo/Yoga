## 5. Syntax / Construction

The verse has two major operations.

First operation:

```iast
vijñānasthitayaḥ sapta
```

This names the set constructed in VAkK 3.5:

```text
The knowledge-stations are seven.
```

Feature extraction:

```text
Class named:
    vijñāna-sthiti

Count:
    seven
```

Second operation:

```iast
bhavāgrāsaṃjñisattvāś ca sattvāvāsā nava smṛtāḥ
```

This gives the broader class of abodes of beings:

```text
The seven knowledge-stations,
plus bhavāgra,
plus the non-percipient beings,
are remembered as nine abodes of beings.
```

Feature extraction:

```text
Base set:
    seven vijñāna-sthiti

Additional members:
    bhavāgra
    asaṃjñi-sattvāḥ

Broader class:
    sattvāvāsa

Total count:
    nine
```

The middle expression:

```iast
śeṣaṃ tatparibhedavat
```

indicates that the remaining classification proceeds according to their distinctions/subdivisions. The verse is distinguishing a narrower set and a broader set:

```text
vijñāna-sthiti
    seven stations of conditioned knowing

sattvāvāsa
    nine abodes of beings
```

The key formal operation is:

```text
Seven knowledge-stations
    + peak of existence
    + non-percipient beings
        = nine being-abodes
```

---

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
The knowledge-stations are seven; the remainder is according to their subdivisions. Together with the peak of existence and the non-percipient beings, nine abodes of beings are remembered.
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
There are seven knowledge-stations. With the remaining distinctions, and together with the peak of existence and the non-percipient beings, nine abodes of beings are traditionally stated.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The classes constructed above are now identified as the seven stations of conditioned knowing. A wider classification is also given: when the peak of existence and the non-percipient beings are included, the system remembers nine abodes of beings.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The world-field is not only a field of bodies and perceptions; it is also a field in which conditioned knowing can be stationed. Yet not every abode of beings is a knowledge-station. The abode is wider than the station of knowing.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### vijñāna-sthiti

```text
vijñāna-sthiti
    knowledge-station
    station of conditioned knowing
    station where knowledge-continuity can be established
```

Inherited translation often gives “station of consciousness.” For this project, **vijñāna = knowledge**, not consciousness. But this does not mean absolute knowledge, prajñā, or buddhi. It means conditioned knowing within a domain.

Working rule:

```text
vijñāna-sthiti
    not consciousness-station
    not buddhi-station
    not prajñā-station
    but station of conditioned knowing
```

This preserves the Abhidharma specificity. There is no puruṣa behind the station, and no Sāṃkhya buddhi-substance above it. There is a station of conditioned knowing within the world-field.

### sthiti

```text
sthiti
    standing
    establishment
    station
    stable placement
```

This is not the same as **sthāna** in VAkK 3.2, though they are related. **Sthāna** there marked a station/place in the rūpadhātu. **Sthiti** here marks establishment or standing of knowing.

```text
sthāna
    station/place/locus

sthiti
    standing/establishment/stationing
```

### sapta

```text
sapta
    seven
```

The seven are generated by the previous verse:

```text
four body/perception configurations
    +
three formless classes
        = seven knowledge-stations
```

### śeṣa

```text
śeṣa
    remainder
    residue
    what is left
```

The verse indicates that the remaining classification is handled by distinction/subdivision. This is not mere leftover material; it marks a controlled extension from one classificatory set to another.

### paribheda

```text
paribheda
    subdivision
    distinction
    differentiation
```

This continues the **bheda** logic already seen in VAkK 3.1. The world-field is intelligible because it is differentiated by lawful distinctions.

### bhavāgra

```text
bhava-agra
    peak of existence
    summit of becoming
```

This is a major warning term.

```text
bhavāgra
    highest bhava
    not beyond bhava
```

It is not liberation and not Absolute Reason. It is the summit of conditioned existence.

### asaṃjñi-sattva

```text
asaṃjñi-sattva
    non-percipient being
    being without active perception
```

These are included among the nine **sattvāvāsa**, but they are not included among the seven **vijñāna-sthiti**. This distinction is essential:

```text
A being can have an abode
without that abode being a station of knowing.
```

### sattvāvāsa

```text
sattva-āvāsa
    abode of beings
    dwelling-place of sentient continuities
```

This is wider than **vijñāna-sthiti**.

```text
vijñāna-sthiti
    where conditioned knowing is stationed

sattvāvāsa
    where beings abide
```

### smṛta

```text
smṛta
    remembered
    traditionally stated
    handed down
```

The verse says these nine are **smṛtāḥ**. This does not merely mean someone remembers casually. It marks the classification as received in the doctrinal memory of the system.

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK 3.6 gives:

```text
World as knowledge-station and being-abode.
```

The movement from 3.5 to 3.6 is:

```text
body/perception configuration
    ↓
knowledge-station
    ↓
being-abode
```

The verse distinguishes two scopes:

```text
narrower:
    seven vijñāna-sthiti

broader:
    nine sattvāvāsa
```

This is a crucial logical distinction:

```text
Not every abode is a knowledge-station.
```

The world is therefore not merely a set of places. It is structured by whether conditioned knowing can be established there.

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This verse should carry the reflections on the Semantic Dataset, but without forcing them into the translation.

The **Semantic Dataset** has the Principle. The Agent has the Result. Here, VAkK 3.6 shows the dataset-level structure for conditioned knowing:

```text
A sentient continuity may:
    abide somewhere
    but not necessarily be established there as knowing.
```

That is why **sattvāvāsa** is broader than **vijñāna-sthiti**.

```text
Being-abode
    where a sentient continuity abides

Knowledge-station
    where conditioned knowing stands or is established
```

This makes the Law of Appearance more precise. It is not a speed-limit sign. It is the lawful semantic field in which beings abide, knowing is stationed, karma is carried, and the next aggregate-state becomes possible.

The strong Organon formula:

```text
The Semantic Dataset carries the Principle.
The Agent carries the Result.
Karma carries the transition.
Vijñāna-sthiti marks where conditioned knowing can stand within the world-field.
```

The verse also protects the hierarchy:

```text
vijñāna
    conditioned knowing

prajñā
    principle / higher discrimination

jñāna
    stabilized knowing
```

So **vijñāna-sthiti** is not liberation, but it is also not mere perception. It is the stationing of conditioned knowing within Loka.
