# VAK_3b — VAkK_3.35

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

चत्वारोऽरूपिसामन्ते रूपगाः एक ऊर्ध्वगः ।
एको मौले स्वविषयः सर्वेऽष्टादश सास्रवाः ॥ VAkK_3.35 ॥

## Sanskrit — IAST

catvāro 'rūpisāmante rūpagāḥ eka ūrdhvagaḥ /
eko maule svaviṣayaḥ sarve 'ṣṭādaśa sāsravāḥ // VAkK_3.35 //

## Source Caution

This verse completes the technical distribution of the eighteen `manopavicāras` begun in VAkK_3.33–3.34. The exact enumeration should remain provisional until checked against the Bhāṣya.

## Grammar

```text

    = four, at the border of the formless, are form-going

    = one is upward-going

    = all eighteen are with outflows
```

## Literal Translation

Four, at the border of the formless, are form-going; one is upward-going. One, in the root, has its own object-field. All eighteen are with outflows.

## Philosophical Translation

The technical distribution of the eighteen mental approaches is completed. Some are directed toward the form field at the threshold of the formless, one is directed upward, and one at the root or base level has its own field. Yet all eighteen remain with outflows: they belong to conditioned experience.

## Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.35 completes the distribution of the eighteen mental approaches.

```text
arūpi-sāmanta
    → four are rūpa-going

one
    → ūrdhva-going

one at the root/base
    → has its own object-field

all eighteen
    → sāsrava
```

The decisive statement is:

```text
sarve aṣṭādaśa sāsravāḥ
```

All eighteen mental approaches are with outflows. Even when experience is refined through higher fields and subtle supports, it still belongs to the conditioned wheel.

## Logical Determination

The preceding verses show that mental approach is:

```text
field-bound
support-bound
range-limited
```

VAkK_3.35 adds:

```text
sāsrava
```

So the world of human conditioned experience is structured and subtle, but still contaminated.

## Interpretive Note

This is an important restraint on interpretation. A refined mental field is not automatically freedom. A higher or subtler mental approach may still belong to the wheel if it is `sāsrava`.

Thus the first part of Lokanirdeśa is not only ordinary experience. It is the structured world of conditioned experience, including subtle meditative fields, insofar as they remain with outflows.
