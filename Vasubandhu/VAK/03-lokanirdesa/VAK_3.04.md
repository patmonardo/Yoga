## 5. Syntax / Construction

The verse has three operations.

### First operation: definition of the five destinies

```iast
narakādisvanāmoktā gatayaḥ pañca
```

Feature extraction:

```text
Subject:
    gatayaḥ

Numerical determination:
    pañca

Naming rule:
    naraka-ādi-sva-nāma-uktāḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
The five destinies are those called by their own names beginning with naraka.
```

This refers back to the list of beings/destinies already introduced in VAkK_3.1.

### Second operation: location within the domains

```iast
teṣu tāḥ
```

Feature extraction:

```text
Locator:
    teṣu
    among those domains / in those

Resumptive pronoun:
    tāḥ
    those five destinies
```

Meaning:

```text
Among those domains are these five destinies.
```

This places the five **gatayaḥ** within the already established domain-field.

### Third operation: restriction of their ontological / ethical status

```iast
akliṣṭāvyākṛtā eva sattvākhyā nāntarābhavaḥ
```

Feature extraction:

```text
Predicate:
    akliṣṭa-avyākṛtāḥ eva

Designation:
    sattva-ākhyāḥ

Exclusion:
    na antarābhavaḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
They are only unafflicted-indeterminate, called beings; intermediate existence is not [a gati].
```

The formal operation is:

```text
Define five destinies
    ↓
locate them within the domain-field
    ↓
restrict their status
    ↓
exclude antarābhava
```

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
The five destinies, called by their own names beginning with naraka, are among those. They are only unafflicted-indeterminate and called beings; intermediate existence is not [one of them].
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
Among those [domains], the five destinies are named by their own names, beginning with naraka. They are only unafflicted and indeterminate, called beings; intermediate existence is not [a destiny].
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
Within the world-domains, the five destinies are the named courses of beings beginning with hell-existence. These destinies are classified as unafflicted and indeterminate, and they are called “beings.” Intermediate existence is excluded from the set of destinies.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
After establishing the three domains, the text now determines the five destinies within them. A destiny is a named course of being, not a moral act in itself. It is an indeterminate world-position of a being. Intermediate existence, though real for the system, is not counted as one of these destinies.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### gati

```text
gati
    going
    course
    path
    destiny
    destination-state
```

Here **destiny** is probably best, but we should hear the verbal force of √gam, “to go.”

```text
gati
    not merely a place
    not merely a species
    but a course of rebirth-placement
```

The five gatayaḥ are:

```text
naraka
preta
tiryañc
manuṣya
deva
```

The verse says “beginning with naraka” because it uses the already-given names rather than relisting them fully.

### narakādi-svanāmoktāḥ

This compound is important because it gives a naming-rule.

```text
naraka-ādi
    beginning with naraka

sva-nāma
    own name

uktāḥ
    called / stated
```

So:

```text
called by their own names beginning with naraka
```

This indicates that the list is not being newly derived here. It is being formally identified as the five **gatayaḥ**.

### akliṣṭa

```text
a-kliṣṭa
    unafflicted
    not defiled
    not afflicted by kleśa in itself
```

This is subtle. A hell-destiny is painful and karmically produced, but the **gati as classificatory result** is not itself a kleśa.

So:

```text
gati as world-position
    akliṣṭa

karmic causes leading there
    may be kliṣṭa / akuśala
```

The destiny is the field or result-position, not the afflicted volition itself.

### avyākṛta

```text
avyākṛta
    indeterminate
    undeclared
    ethically indeterminate
    not karmically classified as wholesome or unwholesome in itself
```

This is one of the first real challenges.

We should avoid translating **avyākṛta** merely as “neutral” if that sounds psychologically bland. It is a technical Abhidharma category: not morally determinable as wholesome/unwholesome in the relevant sense.

Working translation:

```text
avyākṛta
    indeterminate
```

With explanation:

```text
not ethically determinative as kuśala or akuśala in itself
```

### akliṣṭāvyākṛta

Together:

```text
akliṣṭa-avyākṛta
    unafflicted-indeterminate
```

The destinies are:

```text
not afflictive mental factors
not wholesome/unwholesome actions
but indeterminate world-positions of beings
```

This supports the developing Loka–Karma distinction:

```text
Loka:
    gati as result-position

Karma:
    ground of placement, disclosed later
```

### sattvākhyāḥ

```text
sattva-ākhyāḥ
    called sattvas
    named beings
```

The destinies are “called beings” because they classify sentient continuities, not the external container-world.

This prepares the distinction later between:

```text
sattvaloka
    world of beings

bhājanaloka
    container-world
```

We should mark this now as a seed.

### antarābhava

```text
antarā-bhava
    intermediate existence
    in-between becoming
    existence between death and rebirth
```

The verse says:

```text
na antarābhavaḥ
    intermediate existence is not [a gati]
```

This does not necessarily deny intermediate existence. It excludes it from the five destinies.

That distinction matters because the next verses will discuss **antarābhava** more directly.

Working rule:

```text
antarābhava
    may be a bhava
    but is not a gati
```

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK_3.4 moves from **domain** to **destiny**.

The first three verses established:

```text
kāmadhātu
rūpadhātu
ārūpyadhātu
```

Now 3.4 determines:

```text
gatayaḥ pañca
    the five destinies
```

The logical operation:

```text
Domain-field
    ↓
courses of beings within that field
    ↓
classification as unafflicted-indeterminate
    ↓
exclusion of intermediate existence
```

This is important because the Kośa is distinguishing:

```text
domain
    dhātu

destiny / course
    gati

intermediate becoming
    antarābhava
```

They are not the same.

So the logical determination:

```text
World appears not only as domain,
but as named courses of sentient placement.
```

Or shorter:

```text
World as destiny-structure.
```

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This verse gives the first strong internal differentiation of **sattvaloka**.

The first three verses gave the world-domains:

```text
desire-domain
form-domain
formless-domain
```

Verse 3.4 now says:

```text
Within these domains,
there are five destinies,
named from hell onward.
```

So the worldling is not merely “in a domain.” The worldling is on a **course**.

```text
dhātu
    field of possible arising

gati
    course or destiny of a being within the field
```

This deepens the waking-worldling structure:

```text
I of consciousness
    bare enclosure

I of worldling
    not merely “here”
    but on a course
    placed in a destiny-structure
```

But the verse immediately prevents a moral confusion:

```text
The destiny itself is akliṣṭa-avyākṛta.
```

That means:

```text
A world-position is not itself the moral act.

It is the result-field in which a sentient continuity appears.
```

This protects the Loka–Karma distinction:

```text
Loka:
    structured field and destiny-position

Karma:
    grounding act / intention / formation that explains placement
```

And then:

```text
antarābhava is excluded.
```

Why is that important?

Because **antarābhava** is a transitional becoming, not a full destiny. It belongs to the mechanics of passage, not to the fivefold destination-list.

```text
gati
    settled destiny-course

antarābhava
    interval-becoming between death and rebirth
```

So the logical edge of the verse is:

```text
Not every bhava is a gati.
```
