# VAK_3.101 — End-Times: Why the Fourth Dhyāna Is Not Destroyed

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
तदपक्षालसाधर्म्यात् न चतुर्थेऽस्त्यनिञ्जनात् ।
न नित्यं सह सत्त्वेन तद्विमानोदयव्ययात् ॥ VAkK_3.101 ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
tadapakṣālasādharmyāt na caturthe 'sty aniñjanāt /
na nityaṃ saha sattvena tadvimānodayavyayāt // VAkK_3.101 //
```

## 3. Source Caution

Source reading:

```iast
tadapakṣālasādharmyāt na caturthe 'styaniñjanāt /
na nityaṃ saha sattvena tadvimānodayavyayāt // VAkK_3.101 //
```

Light separation:

```iast
tad-apakṣāla-sādharmyāt na caturthe asti aniñjanāt /
na nityam saha sattvena tad-vimāna-udaya-vyayāt //
```

The first compound `tad-apakṣāla-sādharmyāt` is difficult and should be checked against the bhāṣya. The doctrinal sense is that lower destructions correspond to defect-like conditions in lower dhyānas, while the fourth dhyāna is not subject to such destruction because it is `aniñjana`, unmoving or without disturbance. Still, the fourth-dhyāna abodes are not eternal, since their mansions arise and perish together with beings.

## 6. Literal Translation

Because of similarity with those defects, [destruction occurs]; but in the fourth [dhyāna] there is none, because it is unmoving. It is not eternal together with beings, because those mansions arise and perish.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

The fourth dhyāna is not destroyed by fire, water, or wind because it lacks the disturbance corresponding to those destructive conditions. Yet this does not make it eternal. Its celestial mansions arise and perish together with the beings inhabiting them.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.100:

```text
fire destruction
    → reaches second dhyāna
water destruction
    → reaches third dhyāna
wind destruction
    → reaches fourth dhyāna limit
```

VAkK_3.101 clarifies:

```text
fourth dhyāna
    → not destroyed by the three destructions
    → because it is aniñjana, undisturbed
```

But:

```text
fourth-dhyāna abodes
    → not eternal
    → arise and perish with beings
```

## 10. Logical Determination

```text
non-destruction
    ≠ eternity
```

A realm may be exempt from a specific destruction without being unconditioned. The fourth dhyāna is not destroyed by the three saṃvartanīs, but its abodes still arise and perish.

## 11. Interpretive Note

This is a precise Abhidharma distinction. The fourth dhyāna is stable relative to fire, water, and wind destruction, but it is still conditioned.

```text
not destroyed by these calamities
    → relative stability

arises and perishes with beings
    → not eternal
```

## 12. Organon Interpretation

```text
Relative immunity is not absolute permanence.
```

The Kośa keeps the conditioned field closed: even the highest stable rūpa-world is not unconditioned.
