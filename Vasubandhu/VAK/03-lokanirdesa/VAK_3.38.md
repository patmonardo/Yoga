त्रिधान्ये त्रय आरूप्ये आहारस्थितिकं जगत् ॥ VAkK_3.38 ॥

## Literal Translation

Birth-existence is afflicted by all afflictions of its own level. In the source-form `tridhānye`, three in the formless; the world is established through nutriment.

## Philosophical Translation

Rebirth-existence is defiled by all the afflictions proper to its own level. The verse then turns to a wider principle: the world persists through nutriment. The exact technical distribution in the middle phrase remains provisional pending Bhāṣya control.

## Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.36–3.37 explained kleśa, karma, and vastu through functional similes.

VAkK_3.38 now states:

```text
upapatti-bhava
    is kliṣṭa
    by all same-level kleśas
```

This is important because becoming is not afflicted in the abstract. It is afflicted according to its own field or level:

```text
bhava
    is bound to bhūmi

kleśa
    operates according to that bhūmi
```

The verse then states the next general principle:

```text
jagat
    is āhāra-sthitika
    sustained by nutriment
```

## Interpretive Note

This verse marks the transition from the simile-analysis to the analysis of nutriment.

The key phrase is:

```text
āhāra-sthitikaṃ jagat
```

The world is maintained by nutriment. This prepares VAkK_3.39 onward, where āhāra is analyzed.
