# VAK_3.78 — Time-Measure: Human Lifespans Across the Four Continents

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
सहस्रामायुः कुरुषु द्वयोरर्धार्धवर्जितम् ।
इहानियतमान्ते तु दशाब्दा आदितोऽमितम् ॥ VAkK_3.78 ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
sahasrām āyuḥ kuruṣu dvayor ardhārdhavarjitam /
```

## 3. Source Caution

The source reading is:

```iast
sahasrāmāyuḥ kuruṣu dvayorardhārdhavarjitam /
ihāniyatam ante tu daśābdāḥ ādito 'mitam // VAkK_3.78 //
```

Sandhi has been lightly separated:

```iast
sahasram āyuḥ kuruṣu dvayoḥ ardha-ardha-varjitam /
iha aniyatam ante tu daśa-abdāḥ āditaḥ amitam //
```

The first pāda is read as: in the Kurus, lifespan is one thousand years. `dvayoḥ ardha-ardha-varjitam` is provisionally read as the two other continents having successively reduced lifespans by halves. `iha` refers to this Jambūdvīpa human world, where lifespan is indeterminate: at the end, ten years; at the beginning, immeasurable. This distribution should be checked against the bhāṣya.

## 6. Literal Translation

Among the Kurus, lifespan is one thousand. In the two [other continents] it is reduced by half and half. Here it is indeterminate: at the end, ten years; at the beginning, immeasurable.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

The text now shifts from bodily magnitude to lifespan. The northern Kurus have a fixed lifespan of one thousand years; the two other continents have lifespans successively reduced by halves. Here in Jambūdvīpa, however, lifespan is not fixed: at the lowest end it becomes ten years, while at the beginning it is immeasurable.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.75–3.77 measured bodies: human bodies by continent, kāma-deva bodies, and rūpa-deva bodily supports. VAkK_3.78 begins the lifespan block.

```text
body-measure
    → spatial magnitude of embodiment

lifespan-measure
    → temporal duration of embodiment
```

The four-continent human world is now differentiated not by body-size, but by lifespan.

```text
Uttarakuru
    → fixed lifespan: 1000 years

Two other continents
    → successively reduced by halves

Jambūdvīpa / here
    → indeterminate lifespan
    → terminal minimum: 10 years
    → beginning: immeasurable
```

## 10. Logical Determination

This verse shifts the determining measure from space to time.

```text
loka-position
    → body-scale
    → lifespan-scale
```

Jambūdvīpa is especially important because its lifespan is `aniyata`, unfixed. It is the human field of variability, decline, and therefore historical-temporal determination.

## 11. Interpretive Note

This is the clean opening of the time-focus. The Kośa does not abruptly abandon spatial cosmology; rather, it carries measure from bodies into duration.

```text
measure of body
    → measure of life

space of embodiment
    → time of embodiment
```

The contrast is important: other continents have fixed or ordered lifespans, while `iha`, here, lifespan is indeterminate. This makes Jambūdvīpa the unstable human field where lifespan can decline to ten years or extend back toward immeasurability.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

Light contact point:

```text
Temporal measure begins where embodied measure becomes lifespan.
```

For this pass, we simply observe the Kośa’s presentation. Loka is not only the place where beings arise; it is also the temporal horizon within which their embodied existence lasts.
