## 5. Syntax / Construction

This verse completes the womb-entry discussion opened in VAkK 3.16.

First clause:

```iast
garbhāvakrāntayas tisraś cakravartti-svayaṃbhuvām
```

Meaning:

```text
There are three womb-entries of cakravartins and svayaṃbhūs.
```

The key term is **garbhāvakrānti**:

```text
garbha
    womb / embryo / birth-matrix

avakrānti
    descent, entry, coming-down-into
```

So:

```text
garbhāvakrānti
    entry into the womb / descent into the birth-matrix
```

Second clause:

```iast
karma-jñāna-ubhayeṣāṃ vā viśadatvād yathākramam
```

Meaning:

```text
Respectively, because of the clarity of karma, knowledge, or both.
```

The verse gives a threefold classification:

```text
1. karma-viśadatva
    clarity of karma

2. jñāna-viśadatva
    clarity of knowledge

3. ubhaya-viśadatva
    clarity of both
```

The exact assignment between cakravartin and svayaṃbhū belongs to the bhāṣya-pass, but the verse clearly gives the formal rule:

```text
three womb-entries
    according to clarity
    of karma, knowledge, or both
```

Formal operation:

```text
Birth-entry is classified not only by awareness/confusion,
but also by clarity of the conditioning principle:
    karma
    jñāna
    both
```

Adhyāsa-control:

```text
Do not raise the lower principle to the higher location.

jñāna here:
    local clarity-feature in birth-entry

not:
    Prajñā as Principle
    Buddhi/Mahat
    Puruṣa
    Absolute Knowledge
```

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
There are three womb-entries of cakravartins and svayaṃbhūs, respectively, according to the clarity of karma, knowledge, or both.
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
For cakravartins and svayaṃbhūs, womb-entry is threefold: in order, because of the lucidity of karma, of knowledge, or of both.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The entry into birth is not merely obscure or confused. In exceptional cases it is differentiated by clarity: clarity of karmic force, clarity of knowledge, or clarity of both. Thus the bhava-cakra includes special birth-entries in which the transition into embodiment is more lucidly conditioned, without thereby becoming a higher metaphysical principle.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The world-field now admits refined cases of birth-entry. The transition into embodiment may be governed by clear karma, clear local knowledge, or their conjunction. Even the wheel of becoming contains degrees of lucidity within its own machinery, but these degrees remain within the Loka-register.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### garbhāvakrānti

```text
garbhāvakrānti
    womb-entry
    descent into the womb
    entry into the birth-matrix
```

This does not mean entry of a soul-substance. It means the conditioned sentient continuity entering a new birth-matrix.

Working rule:

```text
garbhāvakrānti
    birth-matrix entry of skandha-santati
    not ātman entering a body
```

### tisraḥ

```text
tisraḥ
    three
```

The verse gives a threefold classification.

### cakravartin

```text
cakravartin
    wheel-turning ruler
    universal monarch
```

The cakravartin belongs to the exceptional class whose birth-entry may be marked by special clarity.

### svayaṃbhū

```text
svayaṃbhū
    self-arisen one
    self-become one
```

Here **svayaṃbhū** must be treated cautiously. In this Abhidharma context it marks an exceptional being in relation to special womb-entry. It must not be raised into Vedāntic Brahman, Īśvara, Puruṣa, or any higher theological/metaphysical location.

Adhyāsa warning:

```text
svayaṃbhū in this verse
    = exceptional Loka-level being in the womb-entry classification

not:
    Absolute Self
    Brahman
    Puruṣa
    Īśvara
```

### karma

```text
karma
    action
    karmic conditioning
    Loka-level transition-principle in this chapter
```

Here karma appears more explicitly than in the previous verses. But it is still not yet the full doctrine of agential karma.

Seed-pass rule:

```text
karma in VAkK 3.17
    clarity of karmic conditioning in birth-entry

full karma-doctrine
    deferred to Karmanirdeśa
```

### jñāna

```text
jñāna
    knowledge
    knowing
    lucid cognition
```

This is a knowledge-factor associated with exceptional clarity in womb-entry. It must not be inflated into a higher principle.

Register-control:

```text
jñāna here
    = local lucidity in garbhāvakrānti

not:
    vijñāna-sthiti
    Prajñā as Principle
    Buddhi/Mahat
    Puruṣa
    Absolute Knowledge
```

To raise this local jñāna into a higher metaphysical principle would be **adhyāsa**: superimposition of a higher location upon a lower determination.

### ubhaya

```text
ubhaya
    both
```

The third case involves both karma and jñāna.

### viśadatva

```text
viśadatva
    clarity
    lucidity
    distinctness
```

This is the central term of the verse. The distinction is not only causal but luminous or clear:

```text
karma-viśadatva
jñāna-viśadatva
ubhaya-viśadatva
```

The clarity remains a feature of the birth-entry classification. It is not liberation and not the final Principle.

### yathākramam

```text
yathākramam
    in order
    respectively
```

This marks an ordered correlation between the three womb-entries and the three bases of clarity.

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK 3.17 gives:

```text
World as clarified womb-entry.
```

VAkK 3.16 showed:

```text
birth-entry can occur with clear awareness or with delusion
```

VAkK 3.17 now refines this:

```text
exceptional womb-entry can be clear through:
    karma
    local jñāna
    both
```

So the bhava-cakra is not one flat mechanism. It contains graded modes of clarity.

```text
ordinary entry:
    often mūḍha, confused

exceptional entry:
    marked by viśadatva, clarity
```

But the logical register must be guarded:

```text
clarity within Loka
    does not equal liberation

local jñāna
    does not equal Prajñā as Principle

svayaṃbhū as exceptional being
    does not equal Absolute Self
```

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This verse gives an important bridge between Loka-level Karma and local clarity. It does not authorize raising Loka-level clarity into a higher metaphysical principle.

In Block C, karma has mostly been treated as transition-feature. Here it becomes explicitly paired with **jñāna**, but the pairing remains inside the birth-entry machinery.

```text
karma
    transition-conditioning

jñāna
    local lucidity / knowledge-feature

ubhaya
    their conjunction within garbhāvakrānti
```

This matters for the Organon because birth-entry is not only mechanical. It can be knowledge-graded.

```text
BirthEntryProcess:
    hasConditioningMode:
        KarmaClarity
        JnanaClarity
        KarmaJnanaClarity
```

Adhyāsa-control for the Dataset:

```text
Do not lift:
    BirthEntry.JnanaClarity
into:
    Prajñā.Principle

Do not lift:
    Svayambhu.ExceptionalBeing
into:
    AbsoluteSelf

Do not lift:
    Loka.KarmaClarity
into:
    Agent-level Karmanirdeśa
```

This confirms the Yoga importance of the bhava-cakra, but in a disciplined way. Yoga is not merely escaping a wheel of blind causation. It is learning how obscuration and clarity function inside the machinery of becoming.

```text
Question:
    How is birth governed by obscuration?

Further question:
    How can clarity appear within the machinery of becoming without being mistaken for the final Principle?
```

Still, we preserve the chapter boundary:

```text
Lokanirdeśa:
    identifies karma and jñāna as features of birth-entry clarity

Karmanirdeśa:
    will unfold karma as full action-system
```

The no-self rule remains:

```text
Even clarified womb-entry is not ātman-entry.
It is a lucidly conditioned skandha-santati entering birth.
```
