# VAK_3.85 — Time-Measure: Intermediate Death and the Limits of Form, Name, and Time

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
कुरुबाह्योऽन्तरामृत्युः परमाण्वक्षरक्षणाः ।
रूपनामाध्वपर्यन्ताः परमाणुरणुस्तथा ॥ VAkK_3.85 ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
kurubāhyo 'ntarāmṛtyuḥ paramāṇv-akṣara-kṣaṇāḥ /
rūpa-nāmādhva-paryantāḥ paramāṇur aṇus tathā // VAkK_3.85 //
```

## 3. Source Caution

The source reading is:

```iast
kurubāhyo 'ntarāmṛtyuḥ paramāṇvakṣarakṣaṇāḥ /
rūpanāmādhvaparyantāḥ paramāṇuraṇustathā // VAkK_3.85 //
```

Sandhi has been lightly separated:

```iast
kuru-bāhyaḥ antarā-mṛtyuḥ paramāṇu-akṣara-kṣaṇāḥ /
rūpa-nāma-adhva-paryantāḥ paramāṇuḥ aṇuḥ tathā //
```

The second half begins a new technical sequence: `paramāṇu`, `akṣara`, and `kṣaṇa` are limits respectively of rūpa, nāma, and adhvan/time. The closing `paramāṇur aṇus tathā` begins the atomic-size scale continued in VAkK_3.86. This should be read with the following verse.

## 6. Literal Translation

Intermediate death exists outside the Kurus. Atom, letter, and moment are the limits of form, name, and time. The ultimate atom is also an atom, and so [the sequence continues].

## 7. Philosophical Translation

The text now shifts from the length of life to the possibility of life being interrupted. Except among the Kurus, there is intermediate death. Then the Kośa introduces the minimal limits of determination: the atom is the limit of form, the letter or syllable is the limit of name, and the moment is the limit of time. From the ultimate atom, the scale of material measure begins.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.78–3.84 presented lifespans across human, deva, arūpya, naraka, animal, preta, and cold naraka realms. VAkK_3.85 now shifts from lifespan as duration to two new determinations:

```text
lifespan
    → may be interrupted
    → antarā-mṛtyu outside the Kurus

measure
    → minimal limits
        → rūpa: paramāṇu
        → nāma: akṣara
        → adhvan/time: kṣaṇa
```

This is an important hinge from macro-duration to micro-measure.

## 10. Logical Determination

The verse gives three terminal minima.

```text
rūpa
    → paramāṇu

nāma
    → akṣara

adhvan / time
    → kṣaṇa
```

The previous verses stretched time into immense durations; this verse contracts measure down to the limit of a moment. Loka is measured both cosmically and atomically.

## 11. Interpretive Note

This verse is especially important for the user’s note on arūpya and nāma. The Kośa explicitly aligns three limits:

```text
form-limit
    → atom

name-limit
    → letter / syllable

time-limit
    → moment
```

So `arūpya` is not a vague nothingness. It is a technical negation of rūpa, and the present verse helps expose why nāma becomes decisive where form is no longer the operative register.

The world-analysis therefore contains both:

```text
macro-cosmic duration
    → kalpas and mahākalpas

micro-analytic limit
    → atom / letter / moment
```

## 12. Organon Interpretation

Light contact point:

```text
Every domain has a minimal unit of determination.
```

For this pass, we stay close to the Kośa. The text moves from the vastness of lifespan to the finitude of measure. Form, name, and time each have a limiting term. This is exactly the kind of analytic machinery that will matter when moving toward Karma-nirdeśa.
