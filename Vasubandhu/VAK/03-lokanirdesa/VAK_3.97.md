# VAK_3.97 — Loka Genealogy: The Muni’s Marks and the First Decline of Beings

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
देशस्थोत्तप्तपूर्णत्वैर्लक्षणातिशयो मुनेः ।
प्रागासन् रूपिवत् सत्त्वा रसरागात्ततः शनैः ॥ VAkK_3.97 ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
deśasthottaptapūrṇatvair lakṣaṇātiśayo muneḥ /

```

## 3. Source Caution

The source reading is:

```iast
deśasthottaptapūrṇatvairlakṣaṇātiśayo muneḥ /
prāgāsan rūpivat sattvāḥ rasarāgāttataḥ śanaiḥ // VAkK_3.97 //
```

Sandhi has been lightly separated:

```iast
deśastha-uttapta-pūrṇatvaiḥ lakṣaṇa-atiśayaḥ muneḥ /
prāk āsan rūpi-vat sattvāḥ rasa-rāgāt tataḥ śanaiḥ //
```

The first pāda likely completes the comparison between Buddha and cakravartin: the Muni’s marks are superior because they are in the proper places, sharply manifest, and complete. The second pāda begins a new genealogy of beings at the beginning of the world-cycle: beings were formerly like rūpa-realm beings, then gradually declined through attachment to taste. The precise interpretation of `uttapta` should be checked against the bhāṣya.

## 6. Literal Translation

The Muni has superiority of marks by their being properly placed, clear, and complete. Formerly, beings were like rūpa-beings; then, because of attachment to taste, gradually [they declined].

## 7. Philosophical Translation

The Buddha’s bodily marks surpass those of the wheel-turning king by proper placement, clarity, and completeness. The text then turns to the genealogy of the present human world: at first beings were like beings of the rūpa realm, but through attachment to taste, decline began gradually.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.95–3.96 treated cakravartins. VAkK_3.97 first distinguishes the Buddha from the cakravartin by the excellence of marks, then begins the origin-story of human decline.

```text
Buddha’s marks
    → properly placed
    → clear / well-manifest
    → complete
    → superior to cakravartin marks
```

Then:

```text
first beings
    → rūpivat
    → like rūpa-realm beings

attachment to rasa
    → gradual decline
```

## 10. Logical Determination

This verse moves from superior form to decline through taste.

```text
perfected Buddha-body
    → complete lakṣaṇa

early beings
    → rūpa-like condition
        → rasa-rāga
            → gradual descent
```

The world-genealogy begins with attachment as the differentiating cause.

## 11. Interpretive Note

The turn is elegant. After royal and Buddha wheel-turners, the text begins to explain how the present degraded human order arises.

```text
rūpa-like beings
    → taste appears
        → attachment to taste
            → gradual decline
```

This connects directly to the earlier discussion of arūpya/rūpa/nāma. Beings begin in a more subtle, rūpa-like condition; decline begins when rasa becomes an object of rāga.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

Light contact point:

```text
World-history begins its decline when savor becomes attachment.
```

For this pass, we stay close to the Kośa. The present social world is not simply given; it has a genealogy beginning with subtle beings and gradual degradation through appropriation of taste.
