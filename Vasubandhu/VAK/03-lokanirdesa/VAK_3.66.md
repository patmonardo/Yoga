# VAK_3.66 — Deva-World Summit: Sudarśana City

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
मध्ये सार्धद्विसाहस्रपार्श्वमध्यर्धयोजनम् ।
पुरं सुदर्शनं नाम हैमं चित्रतलं मृदु ॥ VAkK_3.66 ॥
```

## 3. Source Caution

The source reading is:

```iast

puraṃ sudarśanaṃ nāma haimaṃ citratalaṃ mṛdu // VAkK_3.66 //
```

Sandhi has been lightly separated:

```iast
madhye sārdha-dvi-sāhasra-pārśvam adhy-ardha-yojanam /
puram sudarśanam nāma haimam citra-talam mṛdu //
```

The measure compound is dense. It is provisionally read as a city whose side is two-and-a-half thousand, with an additional one-and-a-half yojanas in the relevant measure. The exact measurement should be checked against the bhāṣya.

## 6. Literal Translation

In the middle is a city named Sudarśana, with sides of two-and-a-half thousand and a measure of one-and-a-half yojanas; it is golden, variegated in floor, and soft.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

At the center of the Trāyastriṃśa summit-field stands the city called Sudarśana. It is measured, golden, beautifully surfaced, and soft: a divine civic form in which measure, brilliance, appearance, and tactile pleasantness are united.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.65 located the Trāyastriṃśa gods on the summit of Meru. VAkK_3.66 specifies the central city within that summit-world.

```text
Meru summit
    → Trāyastriṃśa habitation
    → central city Sudarśana
```

The deva-world is now no longer merely a habitation-zone. It has civic form: a measured, named, materially brilliant, sensuously pleasant city.

## 10. Logical Determination

This verse converts summit habitation into civic organization.

```text
summit region
    → center
    → city
    → measured and sensuous form
```

The city is not simply a location. It is an organized center of divine life, determined by measure, name, material, appearance, and tactile quality.

## 11. Interpretive Note

`Sudarśana` marks the deva-world as a world of beautiful appearance. The name itself carries the appearance-determination: “well-seen,” “beautiful to behold.” This follows naturally after the sun/moon verses, where appearance was already shown as conditioned by relation.

```text
celestial appearance
    → divine visible city
```

The world of the gods is still kāmadhātu. Its refinement appears through beauty, measure, gold, color-pattern, and softness, not through liberation.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

Light contact point:

```text
The divine summit becomes concrete as an aesthetic civic form.
```

Sudarśana is a determinate object-world of divine enjoyment. Its logic is not freedom from appearance, but perfected appearance within the world-field.
