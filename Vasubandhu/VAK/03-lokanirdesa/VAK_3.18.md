## 5. Syntax / Construction

This verse gives the no-self payoff of the antarābhava sequence.

First:

```iast
na ātmā asti
```

Meaning:

```text
There is no ātman.
```

This is the controlling negation. The text explicitly forbids inserting a self-substance into the transition.

Second:

```iast
skandha-mātraṃ tu
```

Meaning:

```text
but only the aggregates
```

The **tu** gives the replacement:

```text
not ātman
but skandha-only
```

Third:

```iast
kleśa-karma-abhisaṃskṛtam
```

Meaning:

```text
conditioned / formed by affliction and karma
```

This describes the skandha-complex. The aggregates are not neutral. They are shaped by:

```text
kleśa
    affliction

karma
    action / karmic conditioning
```

Fourth:

```iast
antarābhava-saṃtatyā kukṣim eti pradīpavat
```

Meaning:

```text
through the continuity of intermediate existence, it enters the womb like a flame.
```

The subject is the skandha-mātra, the aggregate-only continuity conditioned by kleśa and karma.

Formal operation:

```text
No ātman exists.
Only skandha-mātra exists.
This skandha-mātra is conditioned by kleśa and karma.
Through antarābhava-continuity it enters the womb.
The analogy is pradīpa-vat, like a flame.
```

---

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
There is no self. Rather, there is only the aggregate-complex, conditioned by affliction and karma. By the continuity of intermediate existence, it enters the womb like a flame.
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
No ātman exists; only the aggregates, conditioned by kleśa and karma, enter the womb through the continuity of intermediate existence, like a flame.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
Nothing like an ātman travels into rebirth. What continues is only the aggregate-stream, formed by afflictions and karma. Through the continuity of the intermediate existence, this conditioned aggregate-series reaches the womb, like flame passing on without a permanent substance moving underneath it.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The bhava-cakra is now explicitly de-substantialized. The transition into birth is not self-transmigration. It is skandha-continuity, formed by kleśa and karma, moving through antarābhava into a new birth-matrix. The flame analogy marks continuity without identity-substance.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### na ātmā asti

```text
na ātmā asti
    there is no self
    no ātman exists
```

This is the decisive guardrail.

Adhyāsa-control:

```text
Do not place ātman at the skandha locus.
Do not place ātman in antarābhava.
Do not place ātman in garbhāvakrānti.
```

To put ātman where the text says **skandha-mātra** is precisely the error of raising or projecting a higher/substantial principle into a lower conditioned locus.

### skandha-mātra

```text
skandha-mātra
    aggregate-only
    mere aggregate-complex
    only the skandhas
```

This is the replacement term for ātman.

```text
not:
    ātman

but:
    skandha-mātra
```

The skandhas are the conditioned aggregate-complex of sentient continuity.

### tu

```text
tu
    but
    rather
```

The **tu** is doctrinally important. It marks the substitution:

```text
No self,
but only aggregates.
```

### kleśa

```text
kleśa
    affliction
    defilement
    disturbing condition
```

Kleśa is one of the two formative conditions named here.

### karma

```text
karma
    action
    karmic conditioning
    formative transition-principle
```

Here karma is now explicitly paired with kleśa as a conditioning force of the skandha-complex.

Still, the full analysis of karma as agential action-system belongs to Karmanirdeśa.

### abhisaṃskṛta

```text
abhisaṃskṛta
    conditioned
    formed
    constructed
    thoroughly prepared/shaped
```

The skandha-mātra is not a raw entity. It is shaped by kleśa and karma.

### antarābhava-saṃtati

```text
antarābhava-saṃtati
    continuity of intermediate existence
    intermediate-existence stream
```

This names the continuity mechanism introduced in the preceding verses.

```text
antarābhava
    transition-state

saṃtati
    continuity-stream
```

### kukṣi

```text
kukṣi
    womb
    belly
    uterine cavity
```

The aggregate-continuity enters the womb, not a self-substance.

### pradīpavat

```text
pradīpavat
    like a lamp
    like a flame
```

The flame analogy indicates continuity without numerical identity of a permanent substance.

Working rule:

```text
flame-continuity
    neither total identity
    nor total discontinuity
```

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK 3.18 gives:

```text
World as no-self aggregate-continuity.
```

The prior verses built the transition machinery:

```text
yoni
    birth-mode

antarābhava
    intermediate transition

santāna / saṃtati
    continuity

gandharva
    transition-being

garbhāvakrānti
    womb-entry
```

This verse now prevents the wrong conclusion:

```text
Since there is continuity,
there must be a self.
```

The text says the opposite:

```text
No ātman.
Only skandha-mātra.
```

Thus:

```text
continuity
    yes

self-substance
    no
```

The logical structure:

```text
skandha-mātra
    conditioned by kleśa + karma
    carried through antarābhava-saṃtati
    enters kukṣi
    like flame-continuity
```

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This is the major payoff of Block C.

The dataset must not contain a hidden Self entity at the transition locus.

```text
Wrong model:
    Atman travels through birth-death transition.

Correct model:
    SkandhaContinuity is conditioned by Kleśa and Karma
    and transitions through AntarabhavaContinuity into BirthMatrix.
```

The category mistake is adhyāsa:

```text
placing ātman at the skandha locus
placing Absolute Self at the continuity-function
placing higher Principle where the text gives conditioned aggregate-process
```

For the Organon:

```text
Bhava-cakra
    = conditioned skandha-continuity
      through kleśa-karma formation
      via antarābhava into birth

not:
    transmigration of a self-substance
```

The flame analogy is ideal for the computational model:

```text
State_t
    conditions
State_t+1

but:
    State_t is not numerically identical with State_t+1
    and there is no extra self-object carried across
```

This supports the Next() formulation:

```text
skandha_{t+1}
    = Next(skandha_t, kleśa, karma, antarābhava-saṃtati)
```

But the warning remains:

```text
Next() does not carry ātman.
Next() carries conditioned continuity.
```
