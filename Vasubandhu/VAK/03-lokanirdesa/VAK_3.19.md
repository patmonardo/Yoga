## 5. Syntax / Construction

This verse completes the Block C analysis of the bhava-cakra.

First:

```iast
yathākṣepaṃ kramād vṛddhaḥ santānaḥ kleśakarmabhiḥ
```

Meaning:

```text
According to projection, the continuity-stream grows gradually through kleśa and karma.
```

The subject is **santānaḥ**:

```text
santānaḥ
    the continuity-stream
```

It is described as:

```text
vṛddhaḥ
    grown / developed

kramāt
    gradually, in sequence

yathākṣepam
    according to projection / according to the throwing-forward

kleśa-karmabhiḥ
    by afflictions and karmas
```

Second:

```iast
paralokaṃ punar yāti
```

Meaning:

```text
it goes again to another world.
```

The continuity does not terminate with one birth. It returns into another world-position.

Third:

```iast
ity anādibhavacakrakam
```

Meaning:

```text
thus, the beginningless wheel of becoming.
```

The **iti** marks the conclusion of the whole sequence:

```text
This is the beginningless bhava-cakra.
```

Formal operation:

```text
Projection
    → gradual growth of santāna
    → conditioning by kleśa and karma
    → re-entry into another world
    → beginningless wheel of becoming
```

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
According to projection, the continuity-stream, gradually grown by afflictions and karmas, goes again to another world. Thus, the beginningless wheel of becoming.
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
The continuum, developing in sequence according to its projection and through afflictions and karmas, goes again to another world. This is the beginningless wheel of becoming.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The stream of aggregates develops according to its karmic projection, shaped by afflictions and actions. Having grown through this ordered process, it proceeds again to another world. This is the beginningless bhava-cakra: continuity without self, transition without first beginning, recurrence through kleśa and karma.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The wheel is now complete. The bhava-cakra is not the journey of a self but the lawful recurrence of conditioned aggregate-continuity. Projection, sequence, growth, affliction, karma, and re-worlding together form the beginningless cycle of becoming.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### yathākṣepam

```text
yathākṣepam
    according to projection
    according to the throwing-forward
    in accordance with karmic impulsion
```

This links back to **ekākṣepa** in VAkK 3.13.

Here ākṣepa is not an eternal decree and not an ātman-project. It is the transition-projection governing how the santāna develops into the next world-position.

Seed-pass rule:

```text
ākṣepa
    Loka-level projection / transition-impulsion

not yet:
    full Agent-level karmic responsibility
```

### krama

```text
krama
    sequence
    order
    gradual process
```

The santāna does not arise chaotically. It grows by order.

### vṛddha

```text
vṛddha
    grown
    developed
    increased
```

This marks the development of the continuity-stream through the conditions named in the verse.

### santāna

```text
santāna
    continuity-stream
    serial continuum
    aggregate-series
```

This is the continuity-carrier of the verse.

Working rule:

```text
santāna carries continuity without ātman.
```

### kleśa

```text
kleśa
    affliction
    disturbing condition
    obscuring factor
```

Kleśa is one of the engines of the wheel.

### karma

```text
karma
    action
    karmic conditioning
    transition-producing action-complex
```

Here karma is an explicit engine of santāna-growth. But full analysis of karma as action-system is deferred to Karmanirdeśa.

### paraloka

```text
paraloka
    another world
    next world
    other world-position
```

The continuity-stream goes again to another world. This is not self-travel. It is conditioned re-worlding.

### punaḥ

```text
punaḥ
    again
```

The recurrence is explicit. The process repeats.

### anādi-bhava-cakraka

```text
anādi-bhava-cakraka
    beginningless wheel of becoming
    beginningless wheel of existence
```

This is the name of the completed structure.

```text
anādi
    without beginning

bhava
    becoming / existence

cakraka
    wheel / cycle
```

The wheel has no first temporal starting-point within the system.

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This verse completes the technical manual of the bhava-cakra for Block C.

The system must model becoming as a lawful cycle, not as an isolated event.

```text
BhavaCakra:
    hasProjection Aksepa
    hasSequence Krama
    hasContinuity Santana
    conditionedBy Klesha
    conditionedBy Karma
    returnsTo Paraloka
    recursAgain Punar
    hasNoBeginning Anadi
```

The computational formula now becomes explicit:

```text
skandha_{t+1}
    = Next(skandha_t, kleśa_t, karma_t, ākṣepa_t)
```

But this must be guarded:

```text
Next() does not carry ātman.
Next() carries conditioned santāna.
```

The bhava-cakra is therefore:

```text
not:
    soul-transmigration

not:
    arbitrary rebirth

not:
    a merely symbolic wheel

but:
    beginningless lawful re-worlding of skandha-continuity
    through kleśa, karma, and projection
```

This is why it is so important for Yoga. Yoga must stop not a thing, but a process:

```text
Stop the wheel
    = interrupt the kleśa-karma projection-cycle
    = end the kriyā of conditioned becoming
```

In Hegel/Organon terms:

```text
Logic gives determinate form.
Kriyā gives lawful transition.
Bhava-cakra gives the world-process as recurring transition.
Yoga gives the discipline of stopping that recurrence.
```

Adhyāsa-control remains decisive:

```text
Do not raise santāna into ātman.
Do not raise ākṣepa into divine decree.
Do not raise local karma into the whole Agent-level doctrine.
Do not place the Absolute at the skandha locus.
```
