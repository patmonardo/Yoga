# VAK_3a.05 — VAkK_3.5

## 1. Sanskrit

```sanskrit
नानात्वकायसंज्ञाश्च नानाकायैकसंज्ञिनः ।
विपर्ययाच्चैककायसंज्ञाश्चारूपिणस्त्रयः ॥
```

## 2. IAST

```iast
nānātvakāyasaṃjñāś ca nānākāyaikasaṃjñinaḥ /
viparyayāc caikakāyasaṃjñāś cārūpiṇas trayaḥ //
```

Source note: the working source reads the final compound as `cārūpiṇasrayaḥ`. For analysis, this is read as `ca ārūpiṇaḥ trayaḥ`, since VAkK 3.6 immediately counts seven vijñāna-sthiti. This is most likely a textual/typing compression around `trayaḥ`.

## 5. Syntax / Construction

The verse constructs a classificatory matrix.

First, it states:

```iast
nānātvakāyasaṃjñāś ca
```

Feature extraction:

```text
Body-configuration:
    diverse

Perception-configuration:
    diverse
```

Second:

```iast
nānākāyaikasaṃjñinaḥ
```

Feature extraction:

```text
Body-configuration:
    diverse

Perception-configuration:
    one / common
```

Third:

```iast
viparyayāt ca
```

This tells us to generate inverse cases:

```text
By reversal:
    one body + diverse perception
    one body + one perception
```

Fourth:

```iast
cārūpiṇas trayaḥ
```

This adds the three formless classes.

So the formal operation is:

```text
body-difference
    ×
perception-difference
        ↓
four embodied classes

plus:
    three formless classes

result:
    seven classes to be named in VAkK 3.6 as vijñāna-sthiti
```

The verse itself does not yet say `vijñāna-sthitayaḥ sapta`; that comes in the next verse. But 3.5 constructs the list that 3.6 names.

---

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
Those with diversity of body and perception, those with diverse bodies and one perception, and by reversal those with one body and perception, and the three formless ones [are the seven].
```

A smoother literal rendering:

```text
Those with diverse bodies and diverse perceptions, those with diverse bodies and one perception, and by reversal those with one body and [diverse or one] perception, together with the three formless classes [make seven].
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The classification now proceeds by body and perception. There are beings whose bodies and perceptions are both diverse; beings whose bodies are diverse but whose perception is one; by reversal, beings whose body is one while perception is either diverse or one; and finally the three formless classes.
```

Organon-clean:

```text
The world-field is now analyzed as a semantic matrix of embodiment and perception. Beings are not merely located in domains; they are configured by body-structure and perception-structure, and this matrix is extended by the three formless modes.
```

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### nānā

```text
nānā
    diverse
    manifold
    differentiated
```

In this verse, **nānā** functions as a feature value.

```text
nānā-kāya
    diverse body

nānā-saṃjñā
    diverse perception
```

### eka

```text
eka
    one
    single
    common
    unified
```

Here **eka** is also a feature value.

```text
eka-kāya
    one body

eka-saṃjñā
    one perception
```

### kāya

```text
kāya
    body
    body-complex
    embodiment-configuration
```

Here **kāya** should not be reduced to gross physical body only. Since the verse operates across cosmological classes, **body-configuration** is a better technical note.

### saṃjñā

```text
saṃjñā
    perception
    recognition
    designation-perception
```

This is not yet conceptual thinking. In this verse it is the perceptual/recognitional configuration of a class of beings.

### viparyaya

```text
viparyaya
    reversal
    inversion
    contrary arrangement
```

Here it is not error or false cognition in the usual Yoga sense. It is a combinatorial instruction: reverse the relation of body/perception to generate the other cases.

### ārūpin

```text
ārūpin
    formless one
    belonging to the formless domain
```

This continues the ārūpya analysis from VAkK 3.3, but now at the level of classificatory placement.

### trayaḥ

```text
trayaḥ
    three
```

The three formless classes are added to the four body/perception classes to yield the seven knowledge-stations named in VAkK 3.6.

## 9. Logical Determination

VAkK 3.5 gives:

```text
World as body/perception configuration.
```

The previous verses gave:

```text
domain
    dhātu

destiny
    gati
```

This verse introduces a new layer:

```text
configuration
    kāya / saṃjñā
```

The logical operation is a feature matrix:

```text
Body:
    diverse / one

Perception:
    diverse / one

Formlessness:
    three further classes
```

So the world is not only a location or destiny. It is also a structured configuration of embodiment and perception.

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This is the first explicit “semantic dataset” verse in the strong sense.

```text
A being is classified by:
    domain
    destiny
    body-configuration
    perception-configuration
```

That is why this is compatible with both Hegel and Yoga.

For Hegel:

```text
Appearance has law because appearance has determinate relations.
```

For Yoga:

```text
Experience appears through structured configurations of embodiment and perception.
```

For Organon:

```text
The Agent does not merely occupy a place.
The Agent appears with feature-bearing configuration:
    kāya-state
    saṃjñā-state
    rūpa / ārūpa relation
```

This is not a moral command. It is the Law of Appearance as a structured feature-space.
