# VAkK_3.49 — Bhājanaloka: Remaining Mountain Rings and the Outer Cakravāḍa

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
अश्वकर्णो विनितको निमिन्धरगिरिः ततः ।
द्वीपाः बहिश्चक्रवाडः सप्त हैमाः स आयसः ॥ VAkK_3.49 ॥
```

## 3. Source Caution

The source reading is:

```iast
aśvakarṇo vinitako nimindharagiriḥ tataḥ /
dvīpāḥ bahiścakravāḍaḥ sapta haimāḥ sa āyasaḥ // VAkK_3.49 //
```

Sandhi has been lightly separated:

```iast
aśvakarṇaḥ vinitakaḥ nimindhara-giriḥ tataḥ /
dvīpāḥ bahiḥ cakravāḍaḥ sapta haimāḥ saḥ āyasaḥ //
```

The source form `vinitakaḥ` should be checked against Pradhan and the bhāṣya in Pass 2. Some inherited lists of the seven mountains use forms that vary across editions and traditions. First-pass work preserves the source reading.

## 6. Literal Translation

Aśvakarṇa, Vinitaka, and then Nimindhara mountain; the islands; outside is Cakravāḍa. The seven are golden; that one is iron.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

The mountain-ring system continues with Aśvakarṇa, Vinitaka, and Nimindhara. Beyond these appear the island-regions, and outside them stands the encircling Cakravāḍa. The seven inner mountains are golden; the outer boundary is iron.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.49 completes the list begun in VAkK_3.48. Together, 3.48 and 3.49 give the seven golden mountains surrounding Meru and the outer iron Cakravāḍa.

The seven golden mountains are:

```text
Yugandhara
Īśādhāra
Khadiraka
Sudarśana
Aśvakarṇa
Vinitaka
Nimindhara
```

Outside them is:

```text
Cakravāḍa
    = iron outer boundary
```

The `dvīpāḥ` are mentioned here but not yet fully described. Their detailed measures begin later, especially from VAkK_3.53.

## 10. Logical Determination

```text
central mountain
    → seven golden enclosing mountains
        → island-regions
            → outer iron boundary
```

The verse completes the spatial logic of enclosure. The container-world now has:

```text
center
    rings
        regions
            boundary
```

The distinction between `haima` and `āyasa` gives material differentiation to the structure. The inner enclosing order is golden; the terminal limit is iron. The world-container is therefore not merely spatially bounded but materially differentiated.

## 11. Interpretive Note

The important first-pass result is that the cosmology has moved from support-fields into an articulated bounded world.

```text
vāyu / jala / kāñcana
    → Meru
        → seven golden mountains
            → dvīpas
                → iron Cakravāḍa
```

This is not yet a doctrine of lived geography. It is the placement of a bounded world-field. The inhabited or region-bearing `dvīpas` are only signaled here; the text will return to them with explicit measures in the following block.

For Organon purposes, VAkK_3.49 is the emergence of limit:

```text
ordered interior
    → exterior boundary
```

The `cakravāḍa` is the world-ring as determinate limit of the container-world.
