Sandhi has been lightly separated:

```iast
pariṣaṇḍāḥ catasraḥ asya daśa-sāhasrika-antarāḥ /
```

`pariṣaṇḍa` is provisionally translated as “terrace” or “ledge.” The numerical series appears to give their outward projection: sixteen thousand, eight thousand, four thousand, and two thousand. The exact spatial interpretation should be checked against the bhāṣya.

## 6. Literal Translation

There are four terraces of this Meru, separated by intervals of ten thousand. They project outward sixteen thousand, eight thousand, four thousand, and two thousand.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

Around Meru are four surrounding terraces, each separated by a ten-thousand interval. Their outward extensions diminish in ordered sequence: sixteen thousand, eight thousand, four thousand, and two thousand.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAkK_3.60–3.62 treated sun and moon as celestial markers of time, appearance, and devic ascent. VAkK_3.63 now begins the explicit divine approach-field around Meru.

```text
Meru-axis
    → celestial measure
    → surrounding terraces
    → deva-world approach
```

The terraces prepare the habitation-field named in VAkK_3.64, where the classes of gods associated with these slopes and mountains begin to appear.

## 10. Logical Determination

This verse introduces graded projection from a central axis.

```text
center
    → surrounding levels
    → measured outward extension
    → descending proportional series
```

The deva-world does not appear as an abstract heaven outside the container-world. It is articulated as a measured projection around Meru.

## 11. Interpretive Note

This is the proper threshold of the “gods” block. The text does not first name gods, but establishes their structural approach-zone. The divine field is prepared by architecture: terraces, intervals, projections, and proportional measure.

```text

```

This supports the VAK_3d thread decision to include the sun and moon: celestial measure and divine habitation belong to one vertical ascent-field.
