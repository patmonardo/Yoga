## 5. Textual Caution

This verse is extremely compressed. It belongs to the Abhidharma classification of faculties by **vipāka** and **savipāka** status. The exact mapping of every counted group should be checked against the Bhāṣya layer.

This file therefore preserves the kārikā structure and marks the classification as provisional until the commentary is integrated.

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
Life is result. Twelve are twofold, except the final eight;
and except distress. That, however, is one. Ten are with result; twofold.
```

A smoother provisional rendering:

```text
The life faculty is a result. Twelve are twofold, excluding the final eight and distress.
That one is otherwise classified. Ten are with result, and are twofold.
```

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The faculties are further classified by their relation to karmic maturation. The life faculty
is treated as result. A set of twelve is classified as twofold, with exclusions including the
final eight and distress. Another set of ten is classified as having result and as twofold.
This verse shifts the indriya doctrine from functional governance into karmic-result analysis.
```

## 9. Classification Note

VAK_2.10 continues the classification begun in VAK_2.09. The topic is now **vipāka**.

```text
vipāka
    =
karmic maturation / result

savipāka
    =

```

The verse states at least these points:

```text
jīvita
    -> vipāka

    -> dvedhā

    -> excluded from that count

    -> savipāka and dvidhā

```

The exact set-membership should be finalized only after Bhāṣya integration.

## 10. Logical Determination

VAK_2.09 classified faculties by purity and outflow-status. VAK_2.10 classifies them by result-status.

```text
purity classification
    -> amala / sāsrava / dvidhā

result classification
    -> vipāka / savipāka / dvidhā
```

The indriya doctrine is therefore being folded into karmic law.

```text
faculty
    =
governing efficacy
    + path-status
    + outflow-status
    + result-status
```

This confirms that faculties are not isolated powers. They are functions within a lawful karmic field.

## 11. Transcendental Placement

The Logic of Experience now includes karmic maturation.

```text
experience is governed
experience is contaminated or purified
experience matures as result
```

The life faculty is especially important because it is placed directly under **vipāka**. This links persistence of the series to lawful maturation.

```text
jīvita
    =
life-series persistence

vipāka
    =
result / maturation

jīvita as vipāka
    =
persistence as matured lawful result
```

This continues the VAK_2.03 insight:

```text
jīvita-indriya
    =
synthetic persistence-driver
```

Here that persistence is viewed under the aspect of result.

## 12. Organon Interpretation

Organon reading:

```text
vipāka-status
    =
classification of a faculty by matured lawful result

savipāka-status
    =
classification of a faculty by relation to result-production or result-possession

dvidhā-status
    =
twofold operating mode
```

The same indriya-system can therefore be viewed under multiple model dimensions:

```text
function
path
purity
outflow
result
```

This is a move from simple ontology to multidimensional classification.
