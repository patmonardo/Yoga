```text
     successive attainment, nirvāṇa, and so forth”
```

The verse continues the governing formula of the chapter:

```text
ādhipatyataḥ indriyam
```

That is:

```text
A determination is called an indriya because it exercises governing efficacy.
```

Here the governing efficacy is no longer ordinary cognition, embodied differentiation, persistence, affective entanglement, or purification in general. It is the governing efficacy of progressive realization.

## 5. Classification Note

VAK_2.04 explains faculties 20–22 in the twenty-twofold list.

```text
20. ājñāsyāmīndriya
    the faculty named “I shall know”

21. ājñendriya
    the faculty of knowing / knowledge

22. ājñātāvīndriya
    the faculty of one who has known
```

These three are the faculties of realization. They complete the twenty-two faculties introduced across VAK_2.01–2.04.

The sequence is:

```text
not-yet-known, to-be-known
    -> ājñāsyāmīndriya

knowing in progress
    -> ājñendriya

known, completed knowing
    -> ājñātāvīndriya
```

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
Likewise, the faculty called “I shall know,” the faculty called “knowing,”
and the faculty of one who has known are regarded as faculties because of
governing efficacy with respect to successive attainment, nirvāṇa, and so forth.
```

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The three realization-faculties are counted as faculties because they govern the
progressive movement of awakening: the resolve toward knowing what is not yet
known, the operative knowing that advances through the path, and the completed
standpoint of one who has known. Their governing efficacy concerns successive
attainment, nirvāṇa, and the higher fruits of realization.
```

## 9. Logical Determination

VAK_2.04 completes the enumeration by introducing the three faculties of realization.

Their structure is temporal-logical:

```text
future knowing
    -> “I shall know”

operative knowing
    -> knowing / knowledge

completed knowing
    -> one who has known
```

This is not merely chronological. It is the inner logic of realization:

```text
orientation
    -> operation
    -> completion
```

The three faculties govern the transition from purification to attainment.

```text
śraddhā, vīrya, smṛti, samādhi, prajñā
    =
purificatory faculties

ājñāsyāmīndriya, ājñendriya, ājñātāvīndriya
    =
realization faculties
```

Thus VAK_2.03 and VAK_2.04 form a pair:

```text
VAK_2.03
    purification-drivers

VAK_2.04
    realization-drivers
```

## 10. Transcendental Placement

The three realization-faculties belong to the transcendental structure of the path. They are not empirical mental states. They are governing powers of knowing as it passes through its own stages.

```text
ājñāsyāmīndriya
    =
prospective realization
    =
the faculty of knowing as to-be-accomplished

ājñendriya
    =
operative realization
    =
the faculty of knowing as path-activity

ājñātāvīndriya
    =
completed realization
    =
the faculty of knowing as accomplished standpoint
```

The verse therefore places knowing itself under the doctrine of *ādhipatya*.

Knowing is not merely a passive registration of fact. It has governing efficacy. It moves the series through successive attainment toward nirvāṇa.

```text
knowing as resolve
    -> to know

knowing as path
    -> knowing

knowing as fruit
    -> having known
```

This is a Kant-clean point as well: the path is not a heap of experiences. It is a lawful progression governed by principles of synthesis, orientation, and completion.

## 11. Organon Interpretation

VAK_2.04 gives the realization-architecture of the Indriya chapter.

The earlier faculties govern access, embodiment, persistence, affect, and purification. The final three govern the explicit transition into attainment.

```text
cognitive faculties
    -> access to object-domains

embodied-formative faculties
    -> sexed embodiment

jīvita
    -> synthetic persistence of the series

vedanā
    -> affective entanglement

śraddhā-prajñā
    -> purification and clarification

ājñā-faculties
    -> realization and attainment
```

Organon mapping:

```text
ājñāsyāmīndriya
    =
prospective realization-driver

ājñendriya
    =
operative realization-driver

ājñātāvīndriya
    =
completed realization-driver
```

The three realization-faculties are therefore the path-version of the synthetic act:

```text
Future
    I shall know

Present
    knowing

Perfect
    one who has known
```

This recalls the Sāṃkhya-Yoga insight that the inner instrument operates across the three times. Here, however, the kārikā gives a specifically Abhidharma structure: realization itself is triply governed as prospective, operative, and completed knowing.

## 12. Compact Formula

```text
VAK_2.04 completes the twenty-two faculties.

They govern the path as future knowing, operative knowing, and completed knowing.

```

```text

```
