# VAK_2.15 — Cessation of Faculties at Death or Withdrawal

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```text
निरोधयत्युपरमान्नारूप्ये जीवितं मनः ।
उपेक्षां चैव रूपेऽष्टौ कामे दश नवाष्टौ वा ॥ VAkK_2.15 ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
nirodhayaty uparamān nārūpye jīvitaṃ manaḥ /
upekṣāṃ caiva rūpe 'ṣṭau kāme daśa navāṣṭau vā // VAkK_2.15 //
```

Source form:


## 3. Textual Caution

This verse concerns cessation or loss of faculties at death/withdrawal in different realms. It is count-sensitive and should be checked against the Bhāṣya later.

## 4. Literal Translation

```text
At cessation through withdrawal, in the formless realm, life, mind, and equanimity cease.
In the form-realm, eight; in the desire-realm, ten, nine, or eight.
```

## 5. Working Interpretation

The verse mirrors VAK_2.14. There the question was acquisition; here it is cessation.

```text
nirodha
    = cessation

uparama
    = withdrawal / stopping / death
```

The faculties cease differently according to realm:

```text
formless-realm
    life, mind, equanimity

form-realm
    eight

desire-realm
    ten, nine, or eight
```

## 7. Logical Determination

VAK_2.15 classifies faculties by cessation-condition.

```text
faculty
    = governing efficacy
    + acquisition-condition
    + cessation-condition
```

The faculty-system has both an installation profile and a shutdown profile.
