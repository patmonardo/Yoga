```text

    nominal expression
    “the six faculties”

    ablative singular compound

    nominative / accusative dual neuter
    “the female and male faculties”
```

The verse continues the logic of VAK_2.01:

```text
ādhipatyāt indriyam
```

That is:

```text
A dharma is counted as an indriya where it has governing efficacy.
```

## 5. Classification Note

VAK_2.02 explains the first eight faculties from the list introduced structurally in VAK_2.01.

```text
1–6. Six faculties:
    cakṣus-indriya
    śrotra-indriya
    ghrāṇa-indriya
    jihvā-indriya
    kāya-indriya
    manas-indriya

7–8. Two sexual faculties:
    strī-indriya
    puruṣa-indriya
```

The first five faculties govern the apprehension of their own objects:

```text
cakṣus  -> rūpa
śrotra  -> śabda
ghrāṇa  -> gandha
jihvā   -> rasa
kāya    -> spraṣṭavya
```

The sixth faculty, **manas**, is marked by the phrase **sarvasya ca**: it has governing efficacy with respect to all object-domains.

The two sexual faculties are distinguished from the body-faculty because their governing efficacy is not merely tactile or bodily apprehension, but the determination of female-being and male-being.

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
Because of governing efficacy in the apprehension of their own objects,
and of all, there are the six faculties.
But because of governing efficacy over female-being and male-being,
the female and male faculties are distinguished from the body.
```

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The six faculties are established through their governing efficacy in cognition:
the first five govern the apprehension of their own object-domains,
and mind governs the apprehension of all. The female and male faculties,
though grounded in the body, are counted separately because they govern the
specific determinations of female-being and male-being.
```

## 9. Logical Determination

The first six faculties are established by cognitive governing efficacy:

```text
indriya
    =
a faculty insofar as it governs apprehension
```

The five sensory faculties are domain-specific:

```text
cakṣus, śrotra, ghrāṇa, jihvā, kāya
    =
faculties of proper-object apprehension
```

The mind-faculty is universal relative to the sensory field:

```text
manas
    =
faculty of all-object apprehension
```

The female and male faculties are established by formative governing efficacy:

```text
strī-indriya / puruṣa-indriya
    =
faculties governing the determinations of sexed embodiment
```

Thus VAK_2.02 distinguishes two kinds of governing efficacy:

```text
cognitive ādhipatya
    -> six faculties

embodied-formative ādhipatya
    -> female and male faculties
```

## 10. Organon Interpretation

VAK_2.02 clarifies that an *indriya* is not a passive organ. It is a model-driver of experience.

The first five faculties are specialized SDSL drivers:

```text
visual model      -> visible form
sonic model       -> sound
olfactory model   -> smell
gustatory model   -> taste
tactile model     -> tangible contact
```

The mind-faculty is a higher coordinator:

```text
manas
    =
general representation-coordinator
    =
faculty with governing efficacy over all object-domains
```

The female and male faculties show that *indriya* is broader than sense-cognition. A faculty is any determinate model that exercises governing efficacy over a domain of conditioned experience.

So the structure is:

```text
Indriya as cognitive model:
    apprehends object-domains

Indriya as embodied model:
    governs sexed embodiment
```

This confirms the working thesis from VAK_2.01:

```text
indriya
    =
relative prajñā-dharma formation
    =
conditioned model of experience
    =
governing efficacy within a domain
```
