## 3. Textual Caution

This verse continues the configuration-count analysis begun in VAK_2.17. It lists minimal associated counts when particular faculties are present. Exact scenario mapping should be checked later against the Bhāṣya.

## 4. Literal Translation

```text
With pleasure and body, there are four.
One possessing eye and the rest has five.
One with gladness, and one with pain, has seven;
likewise one possessing the female faculty and so on.
```

## 5. Working Interpretation

The verse gives further minimum co-presence counts.

```text
sukha + kāya
    -> four

cakṣus-ādi
    -> five

saumanasya / duḥkha
    -> seven

strī-indriya-ādi
    -> seven
```

The point is not a free-standing psychology but the lawful co-configuration of faculties.

The verse expands the minimum configuration doctrine.


A faculty is therefore not isolated. It belongs to a rule-governed network of co-presence.
