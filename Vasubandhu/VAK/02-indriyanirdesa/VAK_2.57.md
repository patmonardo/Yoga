# VAK_2.57 — Definitions of Vipāka, Niṣyanda, and Visaṃyoga

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```text
विपाकोऽव्याकृतो धर्मः सत्त्वाख्यः व्याकृतोद्भवः ।
निःष्यन्दो हेतुसदृशः विसंयोगः क्षयो धिया ॥ VAkK_2.57 ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
vipāko 'vyākṛto dharmaḥ sattvākhyaḥ vyākṛtodbhavaḥ /
niḥṣyando hetusadṛśaḥ visaṃyogaḥ kṣayo dhiyā // VAkK_2.57 //
```

## 3. Literal Translation

```text
Vipāka is an indeterminate dharma, called connected with beings, arisen from determinate karma.
Niṣyanda is similar to its ground. Visaṃyoga is destruction by insight.
```

## 4. Philosophical Translation

```text
The result-types are defined.
Maturation-result is neutral in itself, connected with the being-series, and arises from a determinate prior action.
Homogeneous outflow resembles its ground.
Disconnection-result is the destruction of the relevant bond through knowing.
```
