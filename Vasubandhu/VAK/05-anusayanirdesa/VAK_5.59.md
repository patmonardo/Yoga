# VAK 5.59

## Sanskrit

कामे निवारणान्येकविपक्षाहारकृत्यतः ।
द्वयकैताः पञ्चता स्कन्धविघातविचिकित्सनात् ॥ ५.५९ ॥

## IAST

kāme nivaraṇāny ekavipakṣāhārakṛtyataḥ /
dvayakaitāḥ pañcatā skandhavighātavicikitsanāt // 5.59 //

> **Textual note:** The transmitted continuous reading `dvayaketā` is best understood through sandhi as **dvayakā etāḥ**, “these are constituted by pairs.” The verse concerns the two paired hindrances: **styāna-middha** and **auddhatya-kaukṛtya**.

## Padaccheda

kāme | nivaraṇāni | eka-vipakṣa-āhāra-kṛtyataḥ | dvayakāḥ | etāḥ | pañcatā | skandha-vighāta-vicikitsanāt

## Grammar

- **kāme** — locative singular of **kāma**, “in the desire-domain.” Here it abbreviates **kāmadhātu**.
- **nivaraṇāni** — nominative plural neuter of **nivaraṇa**, “hindrances,” literally factors that obstruct, cover, or prevent passage.
- **eka-vipakṣa-āhāra-kṛtyataḥ** — ablative singular compound, “because of having one counteragent, nourishment, and function.”
  - **eka-vipakṣa** — having the same or a single opposing factor;
  - **āhāra** — nourishment, the conditions that feed and sustain the hindrance;
  - **kṛtya** — function, task, or operative effect.
- **dvayakāḥ** — nominative plural, “consisting of two,” “formed as pairs.”
- **etāḥ** — nominative plural feminine, “these,” referring to the paired hindrance-groups.
- **pañcatā** — abstract noun, “fivefoldness,” “the state of being five.”
- **skandha-vighāta-vicikitsanāt** — ablative compound giving the reason for the fivefold classification:
  - **skandha-vighāta** — destruction or obstruction of the aggregates or functional groups required for the Path;
  - **vicikitsā** — doubt, indecision, inability to settle upon the truth.

## Literal Translation

The hindrances are in the desire-domain. These [two groups] are constituted by pairs because they have one counteragent, one nourishment, and one function. Their fivefoldness is due to the obstruction of the aggregates and to doubt.

## Philosophical Translation

The hindrances are practical obstructions operating within the desire-domain. Two of them are not single factors but paired structures, because each pair is sustained by the same conditions, opposed by the same corrective force, and performs one obstructive task. The hindrances are counted as five because together they disable the functional aggregates of the Path, while doubt suspends the transition from obstruction to determinate knowledge.

## Structural Analysis

VAK 5.47–5.58 analyzed affliction through multiple coordinates:

```text
root
manifest derivative
ethical quality
domain
cognitive support
feeling-conjunction
```

VAK 5.59 now performs a synthesis.

It asks:

```text
Which afflictive structures
have one practical obstructive function?
```

The answer is the five **nivaraṇas**:

```text
1. kāmacchanda
   sensual appropriation

2. vyāpāda
   hostile rejection

3. styāna-middha
   mental rigidity + sleepiness

4. auddhatya-kaukṛtya
   agitation + regret

5. vicikitsā
   doubt or suspended determination
```

Two of these are paired:

```text
styāna + middha
    → one hindrance

auddhatya + kaukṛtya
    → one hindrance
```

They count as single hindrances because each pair has:

```text
one nourishment
one counteragent
one function
```

Thus the classification is not based on the number of lexical items. It is based on functional unity.

## Technical Vocabulary

### Nivaraṇa

**Nivaraṇa** derives from the sense of covering, obstructing, enclosing, or preventing passage.

It is therefore stronger than merely “an unpleasant mental state.”

```text
upakleśa
    derivative affliction

nivaraṇa
    derivative structure considered
    in its Path-obstructing function
```

The same factor can be analyzed differently depending on the question:

```text
What is its root?
    → genealogy of affliction

What feeling accompanies it?
    → affective compatibility

What does it prevent?
    → nivaraṇa classification
```

### Āhāra

**Āhāra** means nourishment: the conditions that sustain and renew a hindrance.

The doctrine therefore refuses to treat an obstruction as an isolated object.

```text
hindrance
    persists because
it is fed
```

A Path-marker is incomplete until it records what repeatedly nourishes the obstruction.

### Vipakṣa

**Vipakṣa** is the opposed factor or counteragent.

The pairing rule is exact:

```text
same nourishment
same counteragent
same obstructive effect
    ↓
one practical hindrance
```

The counteragent is not an externally imposed punishment. It is the determinate contrary that disables the hindrance’s function.

### Kṛtya

**Kṛtya** is the operative task or effect performed by the obstruction.

For example:

```text
styāna-middha
    makes the cognitive field unworkable

uaddhatya-kaukṛtya
    makes the cognitive field unstable
```

The first disables through collapse; the second through dispersal.

## The Five Hindrances as a Path-System

The five can be arranged as a practical architecture:

```text
kāmacchanda
    pulls toward the desired object

vyāpāda
    pushes against the unwanted object

styāna-middha
    collapses the power to investigate

auddhatya-kaukṛtya
    disperses the power to remain

vicikitsā
    suspends determinate commitment to truth
```

This yields three broad failures:

```text
object distortion
    kāmacchanda / vyāpāda

instrumental incapacity
    styāna-middha / auddhatya-kaukṛtya

judgmental suspension
    vicikitsā
```

The hindrances therefore obstruct the Path at different levels:

```text
relation to object
capacity of cognition
power of determination
```

## Why Two Hindrances Are Pairs

### Styāna–Middha

```text
styāna
    rigidity, heaviness, non-plasticity of mind

middha
    sleepiness, collapse of active cognition
```

Together they perform one function:

```text
cognitive contraction
    ↓
inability to investigate
```

Their difference remains real, but their practical effect is unified.

### Auddhatya–Kaukṛtya

```text
auddhatya
    agitation, elevation, scattering

kaukṛtya
    regret, anxious return to what was or was not done
```

Together they perform one function:

```text
cognitive dispersion
    ↓
inability to remain collected
```

The first moves outward and away; the second recursively returns to the past. Both prevent stable presence.

Thus the two pairs are opposed distortions of the middle:

```text
collapse
    styāna-middha

collection
    required Path-function

dispersion
    auddhatya-kaukṛtya
```

## Doctrinal Determination

The hindrances operate in **kāmadhātu** because their standard formation presupposes the desire-domain’s affective and object-relational structure.

Yet the verse’s main concern is not cosmological location alone. It is why these many factors are gathered into five practical units.

The criterion is functional:

```text
species-count
    names distinct mental factors

genus-count
    groups factors by one Path-obstructing operation
```

This repeats the logic already seen in the fetter-classifications:

```text
many determinations
    may instantiate
one obstructive genus
```

### The skandha-obstruction explanation

The Bhāṣya explains the fivefoldness through the disabling of Path-functions:

```text
kāmacchanda + vyāpāda
    damage the ethical aggregate

styāna-middha
    damage the discriminative aggregate

auddhatya-kaukṛtya
    damage the concentration aggregate

vicikitsā
    arises where discrimination and concentration fail,
    leaving uncertainty concerning the Truths
```

Here **skandha** should not be read merely as a static inventory of personal components. It is the organized functional mass through which the Path becomes operative.

```text
ethical formation
concentration
discriminative knowing
    ↓
functional Path-complex
```

The hindrances are five because they attack this complex in determinately different ways.

## Logical Determination

VAK 5.59 introduces a principle of **functional quotienting**.

Distinct elements may be treated as one class when they share:

```text
same sustaining conditions
same determinate contrary
same output function
```

Formally:

```text
x ~ y
iff
Nutrition(x) = Nutrition(y)
and Counteragent(x) = Counteragent(y)
and Function(x) = Function(y)
```

Then:

```text
{x, y} / ~
    → one hindrance-class
```

This is not arbitrary grouping. It is a rigorous equivalence relation grounded in Path-function.

The verse therefore distinguishes:

```text
numerical plurality of factors
    from
logical unity of obstruction
```

### Fivefoldness as systemic completeness

The number five expresses a complete obstruction-system:

```text
appropriation
rejection
collapse
dispersion
indecision
```

These cover the principal ways in which the cognitive-practical field fails to advance from ignorance toward knowledge.

## Path Determination

This verse confirms your point that one maps wrong behavior and places markers along the way.

The hindrance-map is:

```text
What is occurring?
    identify the factor

What feeds it?
    identify its āhāra

What does it prevent?
    identify its kṛtya

What directly opposes it?
    identify its vipakṣa
```

The Path thus appears within the middle of experience as a discriminative review of the conditions already at work.

But it must remain centered on Origin:

```text
manifest obstruction
    ↓
its nourishment
    ↓
its root orientation
    ↓
its determinate counteragent
```

Without the movement back to nourishment and root, practice degenerates into suppression of symptoms.

Without the movement forward to a counteragent, insight remains descriptive and does not become Path.

### Practical markers

```text
repeated attraction
    → inspect kāmacchanda

repeated rejection
    → inspect vyāpāda

loss of cognitive energy
    → inspect styāna-middha

inability to remain gathered
    → inspect auddhatya-kaukṛtya

inability to decide or proceed
    → inspect vicikitsā
```

Each marker points not merely to a state but to a functional break in the Path.

## The Path Encroaching Upon Itself

The five hindrances explain how the Path can “encroach upon itself.”

The practitioner may begin to practice and then encounter:

```text
desire for a result
aversion toward difficulty
collapse of energy
agitation about progress
uncertainty about the teaching
```

These are not external accidents. They are the old afflictive structures appearing inside the practice-process itself.

```text
Path-practice
    becomes a new object
for old affliction
```

Therefore the Path requires reflexive discrimination:

```text
not only:
Am I practicing?

but:
What relation is determining
my practice right now?
```

The hindrance doctrine is thus a practical logic of self-review.

## Bhāṣya Questions

1. What exact **āhāra**, **vipakṣa**, and **kṛtya** does the Bhāṣya assign to **styāna-middha**?
2. What exact nourishment and counter-nourishment sustain and remove **auddhatya-kaukṛtya**?
3. Does **skandhavighāta** refer directly to the five aggregates, or to the Path’s aggregates of morality, concentration, and principle?
4. Why are sensual desire and hostility treated separately rather than as one object-relational pair?
5. Why does doubt count as a distinct hindrance rather than merely the result of impaired discrimination?
6. How does the Bhāṣya reconcile the canonical order of the five hindrances with its explanation of which Path-aggregate each one obstructs?
7. Does the fivefold classification represent an exhaustive logic of practical obstruction, or only the principal obstacles to meditative collection?

## Closing Determination

VAK 5.59 converts the preceding taxonomy into a logic of intervention.

```text
many afflictive factors
    ↓
classified by shared operation
    ↓
five hindrance-functions
    ↓
precise practical counteraction
```

The Path is therefore not a straight line externally imposed upon experience. It is the discriminative reconstruction of experience according to:

```text
what obstructs
what nourishes the obstruction
what function is disabled
what contrary restores that function
```

This is why the hindrances are a practical means of Seeing. They reveal, within the middle of the Path, the determinate structures by which the movement from ignorance to knowledge is interrupted.
