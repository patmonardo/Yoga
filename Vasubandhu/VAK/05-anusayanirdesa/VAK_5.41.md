# VAK 5.41

## Sanskrit

संयोजनादिभेदेन पुनस्ते पञ्चधोदिताः ।
द्रव्यामर्शनसामान्याद् दृष्टी संयोजनान्तरम् ॥ ५.४१ ॥

## IAST

saṃyojanādi-bhedena punas te pañcadhoditāḥ /
dravya-āmarśana-sāmānyād dṛṣṭī saṃyojanāntaram // 5.41 //

## Padaccheda

saṃyojana-ādi-bhedena | punaḥ | te | pañcadhā | uditāḥ | dravya-āmarśana-sāmānyāt | dṛṣṭī | saṃyojana-antaram

## Grammar

- **saṃyojana-ādi-bhedena** — instrumental singular masculine of a compound: “by the division beginning with fetter,” or “according to the differentiation into fetters and the remaining classes.”
- **punaḥ** — indeclinable, “again,” marking a renewed classification of the same anuśayas from another standpoint.
- **te** — nominative plural masculine, “they,” referring to the anuśayas or afflictive dharmas previously classified as āsrava, ogha, yoga, upādāna, and anuśaya.
- **pañcadhā** — indeclinable, “fivefold,” “in five ways.”
- **uditāḥ** — nominative plural masculine, past passive participle from ud-√vac, “stated,” “declared,” “taught.”
- **dravya-āmarśana-sāmānyāt** — ablative singular neuter compound, “because of commonality of substance and grasping.” The compound gives the ground for grouping several views under two fetter-headings.
- **dṛṣṭī** — nominative dual feminine of **dṛṣṭi**, “the two [classes of] views.” The dual does not mean merely two individual views; it indicates the two grouped view-fetters.
- **saṃyojana-antaram** — nominative/accusative singular neuter, “a distinct fetter,” “another fetter-class.” In sense, each of the two view-groups is counted as a distinct saṃyojana.

## Literal Translation

Again, by the division beginning with fetters, they are stated fivefold. Because of commonality of substance and grasping, the two [classes of] views are distinct fetters.

## Philosophical Translation

The same afflictive determinations are classified once more, now according to five modes beginning with bondage. Within the fetter classification, the views are gathered into two distinct bonds because the members of each group share both one cognitive substance and one mode of appropriative grasping.

## Structural Analysis

VAK 5.35–5.40 classified the afflictive field through five operative names:

```text
āsrava
ogha
yoga
upādāna
anuśaya
```

VAK 5.41 now begins a second fivefold classification:

```text
saṃyojana
bandhana
anuśaya
upakleśa
paryavasthāna
```

The transition is marked by **punaḥ**:

```text
same afflictive field
    ↓
classified again
    ↓
according to another principle
```

The preceding fivefoldness was chiefly functional:

```text
flowing
carrying away
binding
appropriating
lying latent
```

The new fivefoldness concerns modes in which affliction structures and occupies the continuum:

```text
fettering
binding
persisting as tendency
secondary contamination
overrunning manifestation
```

Thus the Kośa does not merely add more names. It changes the principle of division.

## The Two View-Fetters

The standard fetter-list does not count all five views separately. They are gathered under two headings:

```text
dṛṣṭi-saṃyojana
parāmarśa-saṃyojana
```

A working distribution is:

```text
dṛṣṭi-saṃyojana
    satkāya-dṛṣṭi
    antagrāha-dṛṣṭi
    mithyā-dṛṣṭi

parāmarśa-saṃyojana
    dṛṣṭi-parāmarśa
    śīlavrata-parāmarśa
```

The verse gives two grounds for this grouping:

1. **dravya-sāmānya** — commonality of substance;
2. **āmarśana-sāmānya** — commonality of grasping or estimative appropriation.

All five are modes of afflicted **prajñā**. They therefore share one cognitive substance while differing in object and determination.

They also share the character of **āmarśana**: cognition does not merely encounter an object but takes it up under a fixed, appropriative determination.

```text
seeing
    becomes
holding-as
```

The two fetters are therefore not arbitrary lexical bundles. They are genera formed from shared cognitive constitution and shared operation.

## Technical Vocabulary

### Saṃyojana

From **sam-√yuj**, “to yoke together, connect, fasten.” A saṃyojana is an afflictive determination insofar as it binds the continuum to a domain, object, view, or mode of existence.

Canonical working translation:

```text
saṃyojana = fetter / binding connection
```

The translation “connection” is formally accurate but too neutral when the doctrinal force is bondage. “Fetter” preserves the path-context.

### Bheda

A division according to a determinate principle. The same material may be divided repeatedly when a different ground of classification is introduced.

```text
identity of field
    ≠
identity of division
```

### Dravya

Here **dravya** indicates the determinate cognitive substance or real factor underlying the classified views. The views are not unified merely because their names resemble one another; they share the same type of cognitive dharma, afflicted prajñā.

### Āmarśana

From ā-√mṛś, “to touch, consider, grasp, take hold of.” In this context it is not neutral reflection. It is the cognitive act of taking something as fixed, superior, pure, self, permanent, causally adequate, or otherwise determinately valid.

Working determination:

```text
āmarśana
    = appropriative grasping
    = estimative holding-as
```

### Sāmānya

Commonality or universality under which several determinations are gathered. The present grouping is explicitly grounded in a shared character rather than accidental association.

### Dṛṣṭi

A view is afflicted prajñā functioning as a fixed determination of its object. It is not passive sight and not mere opinion.

```text
dṛṣṭi
    = cognitive determination
      hardened into grasped validity
```

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse establishes that the anuśayas admit multiple legitimate classifications because each classification follows a distinct doctrinal question.

The same affliction can be considered:

```text
as latent tendency
as flowing contamination
as overwhelming force
as binding connection
as appropriative grasping
as derivative disturbance
as manifest occupation
```

No single classification exhausts the afflictive dharma.

This prevents two opposite errors:

```text
nominal proliferation
    — treating every name as a separate substance

reductive flattening
    — treating every name as an interchangeable synonym
```

The Kośa instead presents:

```text
one real field
    with
multiple grounded divisions
```

The treatment of the views is especially important. Five doctrinal views are not counted as five separate fetters because fetter-enumeration is not simply item-enumeration. The enumeration follows common substance and common mode of grasping.

Thus:

```text
five species of view
    ↓ grouped by ground
 two fetter-genera
```

## Logical Determination

VAK 5.41 gives a clear theory of classification.

A valid class is grounded by:

```text
shared substrate
    +
shared operation
```

Formally:

```text
sameDravya(x, y)
and
sameGraspingMode(x, y)
    →
classifiableUnderSharedFetter(x, y)
```

The verse therefore distinguishes:

```text
species-count
    from
genus-count
```

Five view-species can yield two fetter-genera because the counting principle has changed.

This is a movement from extensional enumeration to intensional organization:

```text
What items are present?
    ↓
What common determination makes them one class?
```

The logical result is not an abstract universal imposed from outside. The universal is generated from the real commonality of substance and operation.

```text
particular views
    ↓
common dravya and āmarśana
    ↓
determinate universal: fetter-class
```



VAK 5.41 therefore begins the movement from latent afflictive ground toward the articulated forms in which that ground binds and appears.

## Bhāṣya Questions

1. How exactly does the Bhāṣya enumerate the five divisions beginning with **saṃyojana**?
2. Does **dṛṣṭī** explicitly denote **dṛṣṭi-saṃyojana** and **parāmarśa-saṃyojana**?
3. Does **dravya-sāmānya** refer simply to all five views having prajñā as their substance?
4. How does the Bhāṣya define **āmarśana-sāmānya**: grasping, esteeming, adherence, or another more precise operation?
5. Is the division of the five views into groups of three and two explicitly stated here or supplied from the inherited fetter-list?
6. Why is commonality sufficient to form two fetters rather than one universal view-fetter?
7. Does Vasubandhu accept this classification without qualification, or is he reporting a Vaibhāṣika enumeration to be examined later?
