# VAK 5.65

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

अन्या अवरभागीयरूपसर्वास्रवक्षयाः ।
तिस्रः परिज्ञाः षट् क्षान्तिफलं ज्ञानस्य शेषिताः ॥ ५.६५ ॥

### IAST

anyā avarabhāgīya-rūpa-sarvāsrava-kṣayāḥ |
tisraḥ parijñāḥ ṣaṭ kṣānti-phalaṃ jñānasya śeṣitāḥ || 5.65 ||

## Padaccheda

anyāḥ | avarabhāgīya-kṣayaḥ | rūpa-kṣayaḥ | sarva-āsrava-kṣayaḥ | tisraḥ | parijñāḥ | ṣaṭ | kṣānti-phalam | jñānasya | śeṣitāḥ

## Grammar

- **anyāḥ** — nominative plural feminine: the others, the remaining ones; referring to the three parijñās not included among the six enumerated in VAK 5.64.
- **avarabhāgīya-kṣayaḥ** — nominative singular masculine compound: destruction of what belongs to the lower portion; doctrinally, destruction of the lower fetters.
- **rūpa-kṣayaḥ** — nominative singular masculine compound: destruction connected with form; the exact doctrinal expansion is to be fixed from the Bhāṣya, most plausibly the destruction of attachment or influx belonging to the form-domain.
- **sarva-āsrava-kṣayaḥ** — nominative singular masculine compound: destruction of all influxes.
- **tisraḥ parijñāḥ** — nominative plural feminine: three complete discriminations.
- **ṣaṭ** — numeral: six.
- **kṣānti-phalam** — nominative singular neuter: fruit of patience; applied distributively to the six parijñās.
- **jñānasya śeṣitāḥ** — literally, the remainder belongs to knowledge; **śeṣitāḥ** indicates the residual set after the six have been assigned to patience.

The compact first pāda is elliptical. It names three abandonments by their objects or ranges:

```text
lower-part destruction
form-related destruction
complete destruction of all influxes
```

These constitute the remaining three parijñās.

## Literal Translation

The other three complete discriminations are the destruction of the lower-part [fetters], the destruction connected with form, and the destruction of all influxes. Six are the fruit of patience; the remaining [three] are of knowledge.

## Philosophical Translation

Beyond the six complete discriminations produced through seeing, three further completions arise through cultivation: the lower binding structure is destroyed, the remaining form-related attachment is destroyed, and finally all influxes are exhausted. Thus the ninefold series divides into six completions whose immediate result belongs to patience and three whose completion belongs to knowledge.

## Structural Analysis

VAK 5.64 gave six parijñās belonging to abandonment through seeing:

```text
three in the desire-domain
three in the higher domains
```

VAK 5.65 now adds the remaining three:

```text
7. avarabhāgīya-kṣaya
   destruction of the lower fetters

8. rūpa-kṣaya
   destruction of the remaining form-related bond

9. sarvāsrava-kṣaya
   destruction of all influxes
```

The full ninefold structure is therefore:

```text
seeing
    → six parijñās

cultivation
    → three parijñās
```

The verse then overlays a second classification:

```text
six
    → fruit of kṣānti

three
    → fruit of jñāna
```

The verse is thus not merely completing a list. It is coordinating two orders:

```text
order of abandonment
    seeing / cultivation

order of cognitive completion
    patience / knowledge
```

## Technical Vocabulary

### Avarabhāgīya

**Avarabhāgīya** means belonging to the lower portion.

In the path doctrine it refers to the fetters that hold the practitioner to the lower field, especially the desire-domain.

```text
lower binding structure
    ↓
continued return to the lower domain
```

Its destruction therefore marks more than the removal of isolated afflictions. It marks the collapse of a whole lower-range dependency.

### Rūpa-kṣaya

The compound is textually compact and requires Bhāṣya confirmation for its full doctrinal expansion.

The safest structural determination is:

```text
form-related residual bond
    ↓
destruction
```

This is later than the destruction of the lower fetters and earlier than the destruction of all influxes.

Therefore it marks an intermediate completion:

```text
freedom from lower-domain bondage
    but not yet
complete exhaustion of every influx
```

### Sarvāsrava-kṣaya

**Sarvāsrava-kṣaya** is the destruction of all influxes.

```text
āsrava
    inflowing or outflowing afflictive continuity

sarvāsrava-kṣaya
    complete exhaustion of that continuity
```

This is the terminal parijñā of the series.

### Kṣānti

**Kṣānti** here is not mere passive tolerance.

It is the receptive certainty by which truth is admitted without reversal.

```text
truth encountered
    ↓
non-rejection
    ↓
irreversible acceptance
```

The six parijñās connected with seeing are called its fruit because patience immediately breaks resistance to the truths that are seen.

### Jñāna

**Jñāna** is determinate knowledge.

The final three are assigned to knowledge because cultivation brings the earlier seeing into completed and stabilized destruction.

```text
kṣānti
    admits the truth

jñāna
    determines and completes it
```

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse makes clear that parijñā is indexed not simply by what is known, but by what has been irreversibly abandoned.

The nine parijñās therefore do not form a merely epistemic list:

```text
nine propositions known
```

They form a path-structure:

```text
nine completed abandonments
```

The first six belong to the seeing of the truths because false orientations are cut off at the level of principle.

The final three belong to cultivation because the remaining afflictive continuity must be exhausted through repeated development.

```text
seeing
    removes error in principle

cultivation
    removes residual continuity
```

The final sequence is progressive:

```text
lower fetters destroyed
    ↓
form-related residual bond destroyed
    ↓
all influxes destroyed
```

This is not merely increasing quantity. Each step changes the range of possible bondage.

## Logical Determination

VAK 5.65 coordinates a three-stage completion with a twofold cognitive source.

The three-stage completion is:

```text
partial range destruction
    ↓
higher residual destruction
    ↓
total destruction
```

The twofold cognitive source is:

```text
kṣānti
    immediate acceptance of truth

jñāna
    determinate completion of truth
```

Thus:

```text
six = kṣānti-phala
three = jñāna-phala
```

The relation is not competitive.

```text
patience
    prepares irreversible seeing

knowledge
    completes irreversible abandonment
```

The verse therefore distinguishes:

```text
truth admitted
truth determined
bond destroyed
```

These are related but non-identical determinations.

## Path Determination

The Path does not end when the practitioner first sees correctly.

Correct seeing establishes the principle of liberation, but residual bondage remains to be cultivated away.

```text
seeing
    reveals the structure

cultivation
    exhausts its persistence
```

This gives a practical diagnostic map:

```text
Has the false view been cut?

Has the lower dependency been destroyed?

Has the higher residual attachment been destroyed?

Have all influxes ceased?
```

The practitioner must therefore distinguish insight from completion.

```text
I understand
    ≠
this tendency is exhausted
```

VAK 5.65 gives the markers by which that difference can be seen.

## Organon Note

The verse presents a movement from immediate truth-recognition to completed rational possession.

```text
kṣānti
    truth received

jñāna
    truth determined

parijñā
    truth embodied as completed abandonment
```

This suggests a threefold logical movement:

```text
receptivity
    ↓
determination
    ↓
realized disconnection
```

The last three parijñās are especially important for the Organon because they are not new abstract truths. They are successive transformations of the subject's actual range of bondage.

```text
lower range negated
    ↓
higher residual range negated
    ↓
total afflictive range negated
```

The sequence therefore moves from limited negation to complete universality of freedom.

## Bhāṣya Questions

1. How does the Bhāṣya expand **avarabhāgīya-kṣaya**?
2. Does **rūpa-kṣaya** mean destruction of attachment to form, destruction of influxes belonging to the form-domain, or another technically fixed category?
3. Why are the first six called fruits of **kṣānti** rather than of the subsequent knowledge-moments?
4. Why are the final three assigned specifically to **jñāna**?
5. Are the three cultivation-stage parijñās mapped directly to stream-enterer, non-returner, and arhat stages, or is the relation more technical?
6. How does this ninefold classification coordinate with the repeated acquisition of disconnection described in VAK 5.63?
