# VAK 5.37

## Sanskrit

तथौघयोगा दृष्टीनां पृथग्भावस्तु पाटवात् ।
नास्रवेष्वसहायानां न किलास्यानुकूलता ॥ ५.३७ ॥

## IAST

tathaughayogā dṛṣṭīnāṃ pṛthagbhāvas tu pāṭavāt |
nāsraveṣv asahāyānāṃ na kilāsyānukūlatā || 5.37 ||

## Padaccheda

tathā | ogha-yogāḥ | dṛṣṭīnām | pṛthak-bhāvaḥ | tu | pāṭavāt |
na | āsraveṣu | asahāyānām | na | kila | asya | anukūlatā |

## Grammar

- **tathā** — “likewise”; extends the preceding classification to further afflictive groupings.
- **ogha-yogāḥ** — nominative plural; “the floods and the yokes.”
- **dṛṣṭīnām pṛthagbhāvaḥ** — “the separate status of the views.”
- **pāṭavāt** — ablative singular; “because of sharpness, acuteness, force.”
- **āsraveṣu** — locative plural; “among the influxes.”
- **asahāyānām** — genitive plural; “of those without assistance/support.”
- **asya anukūlatā** — “its conduciveness, suitability, conformity.”
- The second half is compressed and depends on the Bhāṣya for its exact scholastic force. Pass 1 therefore preserves the structural determination without pretending that every elliptical relation is already settled.

## Literal Translation

“Likewise are the floods and the yokes; but the views have separate status because of their sharpness. Among the influxes, when unsupported, there is said not to be such conduciveness.”

## Philosophical Translation

The same afflictive field is classified as floods and as yokes. In those classifications, views are distinguished as an independent current because their acuteness gives them a special power to carry consciousness away and fasten it. Yet this same independence is not assigned in exactly the same way within the influx classification, where their operation depends on a supporting afflictive field.

## Analysis

Verse 5.37 introduces a decisive methodological principle: one and the same dharma-complex may be reorganized according to distinct operative questions.

The verse does not merely add new names to an existing list. It asks what the afflictions do when considered under different practical relations:

- as **āsrava**, they flow, leak, or sustain the current of saṃsāric continuity;
- as **ogha**, they overwhelm and carry the stream of consciousness away;
- as **yoga**, they fasten and bind the stream to its object and domain.

The ontology remains the same, but the classificatory standpoint changes.

### The Separate Power of Views

Views are given a distinct place among floods and yokes because of **pāṭava**, their sharpness or acuteness.

A craving may attract. A latent tendency may dispose. But a view can organize the entire field of experience under one explicit determination. It does not merely incline consciousness; it supplies a principle according to which consciousness interprets itself, its world, its practice, and its alternatives.

Thus a view has a special practical force:

```text
view
    → organizes objectivity
        → directs the stream
            → fastens the agent
```

This explains why the genealogy of 5.32–5.33 culminated in attachment to one’s own view, pride, and hostility toward another. Once a view becomes sharp enough to order the whole field, it functions both as flood and as yoke:

```text
flood
    carries consciousness away

yoke
    fixes consciousness to a determination
```

### Same Dharmas, Different Functional Partitions

The crucial logical distinction is:

```text
same extension
    ≠
same intension
```

The same afflictive dharmas can enter several classifications because each classification tracks a different power.

```text
classifiedAsInflux
classifiedAsFlood
classifiedAsYoke
```

are therefore not redundant labels. They encode distinct practical predicates.

This is important for the Dialectical Copula. A determination cannot be exhausted by membership in one static class. The same state may:

```text
sustain a stream
carry a stream away
bind a stream to an object
```

The Copula must therefore be able to relate one determination to several operative roles without multiplying the underlying event into several substances.

## Technical Vocabulary

- **āsrava** — influx, outflow, sustaining current of afflictive continuity.
- **ogha** — flood; that which overwhelms and carries away.
- **yoga** — yoke, bond; that which fastens consciousness.
- **dṛṣṭi** — view; a cognitive determination capable of organizing an entire field.
- **pāṭava** — sharpness, acuteness, practical force.
- **pṛthagbhāva** — separate status or distinct classification.
- **anukūlatā** — conduciveness, conformity, suitability to an operation.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse establishes that afflictive classification is functional rather than merely extensional.

The same kleśas are redescribed according to whether they:

```text
flow
flood
bind
```

Views receive independent status in the latter two because their explicit cognitive form grants them exceptional power over the stream of experience.

The compressed second half warns, however, that this independence is not absolute. A view does not operate in a vacuum. Its practical efficacy belongs to a supporting afflictive field. The exact scholastic relation between unsupported views and the influx classification should be confirmed in Pass 2 from the Bhāṣya and its commentarial witnesses.

## Logical Determination

VAK 5.37 requires a multi-axial ontology:

```text
DharmaIdentity
    ≠
FunctionalClassification
```

One dharma may bear several role-predicates:

```text
x sustainsStream
x overwhelmsStream
x bindsStream
```

The sharpness of a view is then not another substance but a reason for assigning an independent functional role.

This yields:

```text
functional difference
    does not require
substantial difference
```

That is exactly the path from Essence to Essence: the same underlying determination appears differently according to the operative relation through which it is grasped.

## Organon Note

For the Dialectical Copula, this verse implies that relation-types must be first-class.

The system should not infer:

```text
same node set
    → same relation
```

Instead, it should represent:

```text
Affliction --sustains--> EventStream
Affliction --overwhelms--> EventStream
Affliction --binds--> AgentOrStream
View --organizes--> ObjectField
```

A view is especially important because it can become a high-level organizing determination whose effects propagate across the entire graph.

This suggests a practical rule:

```text
sharp cognitive determination
    → enlarged causal scope
```

The system should therefore track not merely whether a proposition is held, but whether it has become architectonic for the agent’s event-stream.

## Bhāṣya Questions for Pass 2

1. How precisely does the Bhāṣya explain **asahāyānām**?
2. What is the exact referent of **asya** in the final pāda?
3. Why are views separated among floods and yokes but not identically among influxes?
4. Does **pāṭava** denote cognitive acuteness, practical violence, rapid penetration, or all three?
5. How do the major commentaries distinguish the operative senses of **āsrava**, **ogha**, and **yoga**?
6. Can the second half be read as denying independent influx-status to views when they lack the assistance of ignorance, or is a different relation intended?
