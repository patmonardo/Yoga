# VAK_5.09 — The Four Inversions

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
दृष्टित्रयाद्विपर्यासचतुष्कं विपरीततः ।
नितीरणात् समारोपात् संज्ञाचित्ते तु तद्वशात् ॥ ५.९ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
dṛṣṭi-trayād viparyāsa-catuṣkaṃ viparītataḥ |
nitīraṇāt samāropāt saṃjñā-citte tu tad-vaśāt || 5.9 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
dṛṣṭitrayādviparyāsacatuṣkaṃ viparītataḥ /
nitīraṇāt samāropāt saṃjñācitte tu tadvaśāt // VAkK_5.9 //
```

The reading `nitīraṇāt` is retained provisionally from the local source. Its exact form and interpretation should be verified in the Pradhan text and Bhāṣya pass.

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `dṛṣṭi-trayāt` | ablative singular compound | from three views |
| `viparyāsa-catuṣkam` | nominative/accusative neuter singular | the fourfold inversion |
| `viparītataḥ` | adverb/ablatival form | through inversion; from being contrary |
| `nitīraṇāt` | ablative singular, provisional reading | from determination or decisive fixing |
| `samāropāt` | ablative singular | from superimposition |
| `saṃjñā-citte` | locative dual or compressed conjunctive expression | in perception/designation and mind |
| `tu` | adversative particle | but, however |
| `tad-vaśāt` | ablative singular compound | through dependence upon that; under its influence |

The verse is highly compressed. Its secure structure is causal:

```text
three views
    generate
four inversions

through
    contrary determination
    and superimposition

perception/designation and mind
    proceed under their influence
```

---

## 5. Literal translation

> From three views comes the fourfold inversion, through contrary determination and superimposition; perception and mind, however, are under its influence.

Because the transmitted second pāda is compressed and textually uncertain, this translation remains provisional.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> Three determinate false views give rise to four fundamental inversions. These inversions do not remain isolated propositions: through decisive misdetermination and superimposition, they govern perception and mind themselves. The field is therefore not first neutrally perceived and only afterward judged falsely. Rather, cognition is already organized under the power of the inversion.

The four classical inversions are:

```text
what is impermanent
    taken as permanent

what is painful
    taken as pleasurable

what is non-self
    taken as self

what is impure
    taken as pure
```

The verse therefore advances from false view to a deeper account of cognitive formation:

```text
false view
    → inversion
    → superimposition
    → perception and mind governed by the inversion
```

---

# 7. `Dṛṣṭi-traya`

```text
dṛṣṭi-traya
    three views
```

The kārikā presupposes a determinate subset of the five views previously defined. The exact identification of the three should be established from the Bhāṣya rather than reconstructed dogmatically from the compressed verse alone.

The secure point is structural:

```text
not every false view
    contributes identically

a determinate triad
    grounds the four inversions
```

This already shows that the Kośa is not merely listing errors. It is tracing their genesis.

---

# 8. `Viparyāsa-catuṣkam`

```text
viparyāsa
    inversion
    reversal
    taking the contrary as true
```

The inversion is stronger than simple ignorance.

```text
ignorance
    fails to know adequately

inversion
    positively determines the contrary
```

Thus the fourfold inversion is a system of malformed positive judgments:

```text
impermanent  → permanent
painful      → pleasurable
non-self     → self
impure       → pure
```

This is why the sequence is dialectically rich. The error possesses a positive content and therefore requires a positively adequate counter-determination.

---

# 9. `Viparītataḥ`

```text
viparīta
    reversed
    contrary
    inverted
```

The object is not merely absent from cognition. It appears under a contrary predicate.

```text
what the field is
    and
what cognition determines it to be

stand in contradiction
```

The inversion therefore has the form:

```text
A is not B

but cognition posits

A is B
```

This is a genuine failure of judgment.

---

# 10. `Samāropa`

```text
samāropa
    superimposition
    attribution
    placing upon
```

Superimposition supplies the positive mechanism of inversion.

```text
conditioned field
    receives an alien determination

impermanent
    receives permanence

non-self
    receives selfhood
```

The error is therefore productive:

```text
it does not merely overlook
it adds
it projects
it stabilizes
```

This helps explain why simple informational correction is insufficient. The projected predicate has become part of the operative form through which the object appears.

---

# 11. `Saṃjñā` and `citta`

The verse then places:

```text
saṃjñā
    recognition
    designation
    perceptual-conceptual marking

citta
    mind
    the operative mental continuum
```

under the influence of the inversion:

```text
tad-vaśāt
    under its power
    dependent upon it
```

This is decisive.

The inversion does not remain only at the level of explicit theory:

```text
doctrinal view
    becomes
cognitive orientation

cognitive orientation
    governs
recognition and mental operation
```

Thus the false view becomes world-forming for the agent.

---

# 12. Darśana as primary with respect to duḥkha

The preceding verses showed that distorted causal doctrines rooted in a permanent self are abandoned through seeing suffering.

VAkK_5.09 explains why `darśana` is primary:

```text
suffering is not merely one object among others

it is the field in which
    permanence
    pleasure
    selfhood
    and purity
are decisively contradicted
```

To see suffering adequately is therefore to expose the positive inversion structuring experience.

```text
duḥkha-darśana
    does not merely add a fact

it overturns
    the governing predicates
    through which the field was misrecognized
```

This is a positive dialectic:

```text
false positive determination
    → contradiction disclosed in experience
    → adequate positive determination
```

---

# 13. Logical structure

The verse gives a genetic sequence:

```text
three false views
    ↓
four inversions
    ↓
contrary determination
    +
superimposition
    ↓
saṃjñā and citta governed by them
```

The critical point is recursive:

```text
view structures perception
perception confirms the view
mind reproduces both
```

The inversion therefore becomes self-reinforcing until the relevant path-cognition interrupts the circuit.

---

# 14. Restrained Organon interpretation

For the Organon, the verse suggests a distinction between:

```text
incorrect output

and

incorrect formative schema
```

An isolated false judgment may be corrected locally. An inversion is deeper:

```text
it alters the Feature through which the field is recognized
```

Then:

```text
wrong Feature
    → wrong Model determination
    → recurrent wrong judgment
```

The corrective process must therefore reconstruct the formative relation, not merely replace one proposition.

This remains a provisional architectural analogy and should not override the Abhidharma classification.

---

# 15. Bhāṣya openings

The later VAKB pass should establish:

```text
1. Which exact three views constitute dṛṣṭi-traya?
2. How are the four inversions distributed among those views?
3. What is the preferred reading and sense of nitīraṇāt?
4. How does the Bhāṣya distinguish dṛṣṭi-viparyāsa,
   saṃjñā-viparyāsa, and citta-viparyāsa?
5. Why are perception and mind said to proceed under the power
   of the primary inversion?
```

The kārikā is independently sufficient to establish the genetic structure, but the Bhāṣya is required to type its members precisely.

# 17. Determinate result

VAkK_5.09 establishes:

```text
false views do not remain abstract doctrines

through inversion and superimposition
    they govern recognition and mind
```

The resulting movement is:

```text
view
    → inversion
    → superimposition
    → lived cognitive world
```

This is why `darśana` is primary in relation to `duḥkha`: adequate seeing must reach the formative determination under which suffering was falsely presented as permanence, pleasure, selfhood, or purity.
