# VAK 5.54

## Sanskrit

समानसिद्धा दृग्घेया मनोविज्ञानभूमिकाः ।
उपक्लेशाः स्वतन्त्राश्च षड्विज्ञानाश्रयाः परे ॥ ५.५४ ॥

## IAST

samāna-siddhā dṛg-gheyā manovijñāna-bhūmikāḥ /
upakleśāḥ svatantrāś ca ṣaḍ-vijñāna-āśrayāḥ pare // 5.54 //

## Padaccheda

samāna-siddhāḥ | dṛk-heyāḥ | manas-vijñāna-bhūmikāḥ | upakleśāḥ | svatantrāḥ | ca | ṣaṭ-vijñāna-āśrayāḥ | pare

## Grammar

- **samāna-siddhāḥ** — nominative plural masculine, literally “established in the same way” or “similarly established.” The Bhāṣya determines the intended extension; the phrase gathers additional cases whose support is likewise mental rather than sensory.
- **dṛk-heyāḥ** — nominative plural masculine, “those to be abandoned by seeing,” i.e. by seeing the truths.
- **manovijñāna-bhūmikāḥ** — nominative plural masculine, “belonging to the level of mental knowledge,” “situated on the plane of manovijñāna.”
- **upakleśāḥ svatantrāḥ** — nominative plural masculine, “the independent derivative afflictions.” These are not merely concomitant with another affliction but arise as distinct determinations.
- **ca** — “and,” adding the autonomous upakleśas to the same mental-only class.
- **ṣaḍ-vijñāna-āśrayāḥ** — nominative plural masculine, “having the six knowledges as support,” meaning capable of arising with visual, auditory, olfactory, gustatory, bodily, or mental knowledge.
- **pare** — nominative plural masculine, “the others,” contrasting the remaining afflictions with the mental-only group.

## Literal Translation

Those abandoned by seeing, together with those similarly established, belong to the level of mental knowledge; so also do the independent derivative afflictions. The others have the six knowledges as their support.

## Philosophical Translation

Afflictions whose correction requires seeing the truths arise only on the plane of reflective mental knowledge. The same is true of the additional determinations established in that manner and of the autonomous derivative afflictions. The remaining afflictions can occupy the entire cognitive field, taking any of the six forms of knowledge as their support.

## Structural Analysis

VAK 5.52–5.53 mapped the derivative afflictions according to:

```text
mode of abandonment
domain
ethical value
range of persistence
```

VAK 5.54 adds another coordinate:

```text
cognitive support
```

The question is no longer only:

```text
Where does this affliction exist?
```

It is now:

```text
Through which mode of knowledge can it become actual?
```

The verse distinguishes two ranges.

### Mental-only afflictions

```text
darśana-heya afflictions
additional similarly established cases
independent upakleśas
    ↓
manovijñāna only
```

### Sixfold-supported afflictions

```text
remaining afflictions
    ↓
visual knowledge
auditory knowledge
olfactory knowledge
gustatory knowledge
bodily knowledge
mental knowledge
```

This is a major doctrinal refinement. An affliction may belong to a domain and still differ from another affliction in the cognitive channel through which it operates.

## Technical Vocabulary

### Manovijñāna

Here **manovijñāna** is not generic “consciousness.” It is the reflective mental knowledge capable of taking dharmas, propositions, universals, past and future determinations, and doctrinal objects as its support.

Canonical working translation:

```text
manovijñāna
    =
mental knowledge
```

The afflictions abandoned by seeing are mental-only because their objects are not merely sensed things. They involve determinations such as:

```text
self
truth
cause
path
existence
nonexistence
false universal judgment
```

Such objects require mental construction and judgment.

### Bhūmi

A **bhūmi** is a plane or operative level on which a determination can arise.

In this verse:

```text
manovijñāna-bhūmi
    =
the cognitive plane proper to reflective mental knowledge
```

### Āśraya

**Āśraya** means support or basis of occurrence.

The six knowledges do not cause an affliction merely by existing. They provide the cognitive support in dependence upon which the affliction becomes manifest.

```text
knowledge-event
    + afflictive condition
    = afflicted cognition
```

### Svatantra Upakleśa

An independent upakleśa is not simply the coloring of another active affliction. It has enough functional distinctness to arise as its own manifest determination.

Yet “independent” does not mean unconditioned:

```text
functional autonomy
    ≠
absolute independence
```

### Dṛg-heya

That which is abandoned through seeing the truths.

Its defect lies in a false determination of the object, not merely in a habituated emotional force.

```text
false view
    ↓ corrected by
seeing what is the case
```

## Doctrinal Determination

Afflictions abandoned through seeing are confined to **manovijñāna** because they require conceptual or doctrinal objects.

A visual knowledge may disclose a visible form, but it does not by itself assert:

```text
this is a permanent self
this discipline is the absolute path
causality does not exist
liberation is impossible
```

Those determinations belong to mental knowledge.

Thus:

```text
sensory presentation
    ≠
false view as judgment
```

The same mental-only status applies, according to the Bhāṣya, to further cases such as conceit and languor insofar as their full range is established on the mental plane, and to the autonomous upakleśas such as jealousy and avarice.

The remaining afflictions can operate with all six knowledges. Desire, hostility, ignorance, and their dependent derivatives can arise directly around:

```text
visible form
sound
odor
taste
tangible object
mental object
```

This means the Path must discriminate not only the doctrinal root but the **cognitive doorway** through which the affliction enters.

## Logical Determination

VAK 5.54 distinguishes two levels of determination:

```text
judgmental affliction
    versus
field-pervasive affliction
```

A judgmental affliction requires mental mediation:

```text
presentation
    ↓
reflection
    ↓
false determination
```

A field-pervasive affliction can adhere immediately to any cognitive presentation:

```text
object appears
    ↓
attraction / resistance / obscuration
```

This gives a precise distinction between:

```text
error in the form of judgment
and
error in the manner of relation
```

The former is necessarily mental. The latter may occupy the whole sixfold cognitive field.

The Path therefore cannot use one undifferentiated corrective procedure.

```text
false judgment
    → seeing

sensory-affective occupation
    → cultivation and restraint
```

## Relation to the Truth of Origin

The Path appears here in the middle of the afflictive analysis, but its rationale remains grounded in **samudaya**, the Truth of Origin.

The manifest cognitive support tells us where the affliction appears. It does not yet tell us why it arises.

```text
support
    ≠
origin
```

For example:

```text
visible form
    may support desire

but visible form
    is not the root of desire
```

The root lies in the conditioned orientation of appropriation.

Thus the practical map must preserve two directions:

```text
from manifestation
    back to origin

from origin
    toward the path of removal
```

This prevents the Path from mistaking sensory avoidance for knowledge of origin.

## Organon Note

VAK 5.54 gives a strong architectural distinction between:

```text
Domain
Support
Ground
Mode of Removal
```

These must not be collapsed.

A determination may be represented as:

```text
Affliction
    hasDomain Bhūmi
    hasCognitiveSupport Vijñāna
    hasGround Kleśa
    hasAbandonmentMode DarśanaOrBhāvanā
```

The verse also distinguishes two propagation patterns:

```text
MentalOnlyPropagation
SixfoldCognitivePropagation
```

This matters for speculative reasoning because a false universal judgment and an affective reaction may have the same object while differing completely in logical form.

```text
same object-support
    ≠
same cognitive operation
```

## Bhāṣya Questions

1. What precise items are included under **samāna-siddhāḥ**?
2. Does the Bhāṣya explicitly add **māna** and **middha** to the manovijñāna-only class?
3. Why are autonomous upakleśas such as jealousy and avarice restricted to mental knowledge?
4. Which “remaining” afflictions are said to rest on all six knowledges?
5. Does **ṣaḍ-vijñāna-āśraya** mean that each may arise with every one of the six knowledges, or only that the class as a whole is distributed among them?
6. How does Vasubandhu distinguish a sensory support from the doctrinal or karmic origin of an affliction?
7. Does the verse imply a different practical countermeasure for mental-only and sixfold-supported afflictions?
