# VAK_5.13 — Range and Co-Arising of the Universal Anuśayas

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
न वोर्ध्वालम्बना एषां दृष्टिद्वयविवर्जिताः ।
प्राप्तिवर्ज्याः सहभुवो येऽप्येभिस्तेऽपि सर्वगाः ॥ ५.१३ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
na vordhvālambanā eṣāṃ dṛṣṭidvayavivarjitāḥ |
prāptivarjyāḥ sahabhuvo ye 'py ebhis te 'pi sarvagāḥ || 5.13 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
navordhvālambanā eṣāṃ dṛṣṭidvayavivarjitāḥ /
prāptivarjyāḥ sahabhuvo ye 'pyebhiste 'pi sarvagāḥ // VAkK_5.13 //
```

The verse continues the determination of the `sarvatraga` anuśayas introduced in VAK_5.12. It now specifies:

```text
how far their object-range extends
and
which co-arisen dharmas share their universal status
```

---

## 3. Padaccheda

```text
na |
vā |
ūrdhva-ālambanāḥ |
eṣām |
dṛṣṭi-dvaya-vivarjitāḥ |

prāpti-varjyāḥ |
saha-bhuvaḥ |
ye |
api |
ebhiḥ |
te |
api |
sarvagāḥ
```

A syntactically useful reconstruction is:

```text
eṣāṃ dṛṣṭi-dvaya-vivarjitāḥ
na ūrdhvālambanāḥ.

ye api ebhiḥ sahabhuvaḥ,
prāpti-varjyāḥ,
te api sarvagāḥ.
```

> Among these, those other than the two views do not take higher levels as their object. Whatever dharmas arise together with these—except acquisition—are also universal.

The precise identity of the two views is doctrinally important and should be verified in the Bhāṣya. The standard classification points toward the two views capable of taking a superior sphere as object, but the kārikā itself leaves them unnamed.

---

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `na` | negative particle | not |
| `vā` | particle | or; alternatively; sometimes metrically connective |
| `ūrdhva-ālambanāḥ` | nominative plural compound | having a higher level or superior sphere as object |
| `eṣām` | genitive plural pronoun | among these; of these |
| `dṛṣṭi-dvaya-vivarjitāḥ` | nominative plural compound | excluding the two views; apart from the two views |
| `prāpti-varjyāḥ` | nominative plural compound | excluding acquisition |
| `saha-bhuvaḥ` | nominative plural | co-arisen, arising together |
| `ye ... te` | correlative pronouns | whatever ... those |
| `ebhiḥ` | instrumental plural pronoun | with these |
| `sarvagāḥ` | nominative plural | universal; ranging everywhere within the relevant field |

The first half states a restriction upon object-range.

The second half extends the designation `sarvatraga` beyond the anuśaya itself to associated and co-arisen dharmas, while explicitly excluding `prāpti`.

---

## 5. Literal translation

> Among these, those excluding the two views do not have a higher level as their object. Whatever dharmas arise together with these, except acquisition, are also universal.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> Universal affliction is not unlimited in an undifferentiated sense. Its range is governed by the level of existence to which it belongs: except for two determinate forms of view, the universal anuśayas do not direct themselves toward a superior sphere. Yet when a universal anuśaya actually arises, the mental determinations that arise inseparably with it participate in its universal operation. Acquisition alone is excluded. Thus universality belongs not only to an isolated afflictive factor, but to a structured event of cognition whose associated moments share the same extensive reach.

The verse adds two constraints:

```text
universality
    is not unrestricted transcendence of level

universality
    is not isolated possession by one dharma
```

Instead:

```text
object-range is bounded
co-arisen operation is shared
```

---

# 7. `Ūrdhvālambana`

```text
ūrdhva
    higher
    superior
    above

ālambana
    object-support
    cognitive object
```

The compound therefore means:

```text
having a superior level as object
```

This introduces a vertical structure into the taxonomy.

An anuśaya may be universal relative to its own field without therefore taking every superior field as its object.

```text
universal within a sphere
    ≠
unrestricted across all spheres
```

The distinction is crucial. `Sarvatraga` cannot be interpreted as absolute omnidirectionality.

It is a technical universality:

```text
range across the relevant field
under determinate restrictions
```

---

# 8. The exception of two views

The verse states:

```text
dṛṣṭi-dvaya-vivarjitāḥ
    excluding two views
```

This means that two views are exceptional with respect to the prohibition against taking a higher level as object.

The kārikā does not name them here.

The likely doctrinal reconstruction is that two among the universal views can range upward toward a superior sphere, whereas the remaining universal anuśayas cannot.

The exact identification should remain a Bhāṣya question:

```text
Which two views?
Why can they take a higher sphere as object?
What distinguishes their object-structure from the others?
```

The independent result of the kārikā is secure:

```text
most universal anuśayas
    do not take higher levels as object

two views
    constitute an exception
```

---

# 9. Universality as determinate range

The chapter is refining the meaning of `sarvatraga`.

VAK_5.12 identified the universal anuśayas.

VAK_5.13 now prevents a false inference:

```text
sarvatraga
    does not mean
        without any boundary
```

Rather:

```text
sarvatraga
    means extensive causal and cognitive operation
        within a technically determined domain
```

This is a dialectical correction of abstract universality.

```text
abstract universality
    everything without qualification

concrete universality
    the whole field under its constitutive limits
```

The Kośa consistently prefers the second.

---

# 10. `Sahabhu`

```text
saha-bhu
    arising together
    co-existent in the same event
    co-produced
```

The universal anuśaya does not arise alone.

It occurs within a structured mental event containing associated determinations.

```text
anuśaya
    +
co-arisen mental factors
    =
a single afflicted cognitive event
```

The verse extends universality to these co-arisen factors:

```text
they arise with a universal anuśaya
therefore
they participate in its universal character
```

This is not identity of essence.

It is shared operation through conjunction.

```text
same event
same object-field
shared afflicted reach
```

---

# 11. The exclusion of `prāpti`

```text
prāpti-varjyāḥ
    excluding acquisition
```

`Prāpti` is omitted from the extension of universal status.

This is philosophically important because the verse distinguishes:

```text
co-arisen participation in the cognitive event
from
possession or acquisition of a dharma
```

The associated mental determinations share the universal operation.

`Prāpti` does not.

The Bhāṣya must explain the exact ontological and causal reason for this exclusion.

But the first-pass distinction is clear:

```text
association
    can transmit functional classification

acquisition
    does not automatically inherit that classification
```

---

# 12. A structured field, not an isolated factor

The verse shifts the unit of analysis.

Earlier the chapter classified individual anuśayas.

Now it shows that the operative reality is a complex:

```text
object
anuśaya
associated mental factors
co-arisen determinations
acquisition relation
```

The afflicted event is therefore relational.

```text
one factor does not operate in isolation
```

Its character spreads through the conjunction, but only according to determinate rules.

This is why the chapter requires both:

```text
classification of dharmas
and
classification of their relations
```

---

# 13. Positive dialectic

The verse again displays the positive dialectical method already visible in the preceding kārikās.

It first asserts universality.

Then it determines that universality by internal limits:

```text
universal
    but not upward-ranging in every case

shared by co-arisen factors
    but not by acquisition
```

Thus the truth is not the first abstract predicate.

The truth is the predicate after its limits have been articulated.

```text
universality
    → restriction of object-range
    → extension through conjunction
    → exclusion of acquisition
    → concrete universal structure
```

This is exactly why the chapter is so valuable dialectically.

---

# 14. Organon restraint

A restrained architectural analogy is possible:

```text
universal rule
    does not necessarily apply outside its level

co-arising components
    may inherit the rule's operational scope

possession metadata
    need not inherit the rule's semantic scope
```

But this remains only an analogy.

The kārikā is primarily establishing the Abhidharma structure of object-range, association, and acquisition.

The Organon translation should wait for the Bhāṣya and later reporting pass.

---

# 15. Bhāṣya openings

The later VAKB study should determine:

```text
1. the exact identity of the two exceptional views
2. why those two can take a superior level as object
3. the precise range implied by ūrdhva
4. which dharmas count as sahabhu here
5. why prāpti is excluded
6. whether shared universality is based on object, association, causality, or all three
7. how this classification varies across the three realms
```

These are genuine presuppositions of the compressed kārikā.

---

# 16. Verse movement

```text
VAK_5.12
    identifies the universal anuśayas

VAK_5.13
    limits their vertical object-range
    and extends universality to their co-arisen complex

VAK_5.14
    turns to the anuśayas whose objects are uncontaminated:
        cessation and path
```

The movement is therefore:

```text
which anuśayas are universal
    → how their universality is bounded and shared
    → how affliction can take uncontaminated dharmas as object
```

---

## Working determination

```text
Sarvatraga is concrete universality:

bounded by level,
exceptionally capable of upward reference in two views,
and shared by the co-arisen cognitive complex,
though not by acquisition.
```
