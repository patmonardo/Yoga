# VAK 5.69

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

नैकया पञ्चभिर्यावद्दर्शनस्थः समन्वितः ।
भावनास्थः पुनः षड्भिरे्कया वा द्वयेन वा ॥ ५.६९ ॥

### IAST

naikayā pañcabhiryāvad darśanasthaḥ samanvitaḥ |
bhāvanāsthaḥ punaḥ ṣaḍbhir ekayā vā dvayena vā || 5.69 ||

## Padaccheda

na | ekayā | pañcabhiḥ | yāvat | darśana-sthaḥ | samanvitaḥ | bhāvanā-sthaḥ | punaḥ | ṣaḍbhiḥ | ekayā | vā | dvayena | vā

## Grammar

- **na** — negation: not, none.
- **ekayā** — instrumental singular feminine: with one; understood with **parijñayā**.
- **pañcabhiḥ** — instrumental plural: with five.
- **yāvat** — up to, as far as; here marking a progressive range.
- **darśana-sthaḥ** — nominative singular masculine compound: one standing in the path of seeing.
- **samanvitaḥ** — nominative singular masculine past passive participle from **sam-anu-√i**: endowed with, possessed of, conjoined with.
- **bhāvanā-sthaḥ** — nominative singular masculine compound: one standing in the path of cultivation.
- **punaḥ** — again, but, in turn; marks the contrast with the path of seeing.
- **ṣaḍbhiḥ** — instrumental plural: with six.
- **ekayā vā** — or with one.
- **dvayena vā** — or with two.

The implied noun throughout is **parijñā**:

```text
no parijñā
one to five parijñās
six parijñās
one parijñā
or two parijñās
```

## Literal Translation

One who stands in the path of seeing is endowed with none, or with as many as five. One who stands in the path of cultivation, however, is endowed with six, or with one, or with two.

## Philosophical Translation

During the path of seeing, completed abandonments accumulate progressively from none to five. Once the practitioner has entered the path of cultivation, the configuration changes: the practitioner may possess six completed discriminations, or—after higher integrations—only one or two, because earlier attainments are gathered into more comprehensive completions.

## Structural Analysis

VAK 5.64–5.68 established:

```text
what the nine parijñās are
what produces them
what makes an abandonment complete
```

VAK 5.69 now asks:

```text
How many parijñās
is a practitioner actually endowed with
at each Path-stage?
```

The answer is stage-dependent.

### Path of seeing

```text
darśanamārga
    → 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, or 5 parijñās
```

The number rises progressively as the truth-sequence advances.

The Bhāṣya locates the sequence by the successive moments of seeing:

```text
before the relevant completion
    → none

as seeing proceeds
    → one
    → two
    → three
    → four
    → five
```

Thus the path of seeing has an accumulative form.

### Path of cultivation

```text
bhāvanāmārga
    → six
    → or one
    → or two
```

The apparently smaller numbers do not indicate regression in knowledge. They indicate **integration**.

Earlier distinct parijñās are no longer counted separately once a more comprehensive abandonment has been acquired.

## Technical Vocabulary

### Darśanastha

**Darśanastha** is one established in the path of seeing.

This is the irreversible cognitive penetration of the truths in their ordered sequence.

Its parijñā-count is progressive because the abandonments arise serially:

```text
successive seeing
    → successive completed abandonments
```

### Bhāvanāstha

**Bhāvanāstha** is one established in the path of cultivation.

Here the task is no longer the first seeing of the truths, but the exhaustion of what remains through repeated cultivation.

The resulting parijñās are organized differently because later completions subsume earlier ones.

### Samanvita

**Samanvita** means endowed with or presently possessed of.

The verse is therefore not recounting every parijñā ever produced in the practitioner's history. It counts those with which the practitioner is currently associated.

```text
historically produced
    ≠
currently counted as possessed
```

### Saṃkalana as implicit principle

Although **saṃkalana**, consolidation, is stated explicitly only in VAK 5.70, it already governs VAK 5.69.

The transition from six to one or two is possible because several earlier completions are gathered into a later, more comprehensive one.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse distinguishes two modes of Path-order.

### Accumulation in seeing

During seeing, each newly completed abandonment adds to the prior set.

```text
0 → 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5
```

This is a serial structure.

Each stage retains the prior completions while adding a new one.

### Consolidation in cultivation

During cultivation, the count does not simply continue upward from six to seven, eight, and nine.

Instead:

```text
six distinct completions
    ↓
consolidated by a higher abandonment
    ↓
one or two comprehensive completions
```

Thus the ninefold taxonomy is not a cumulative inventory that must always be simultaneously possessed as nine separate items.

The same liberative history can be represented under a smaller current count because the attainments have been structurally integrated.

### Why one?

According to the Bhāṣya, one may be endowed with one parijñā in cases such as:

```text
destruction of the lower fetters
or
complete exhaustion of all fetters
```

The single parijñā is not minimal. It is maximally comprehensive relative to its field.

### Why two?

One may be endowed with two when both:

```text
lower-fetter destruction
and
form-attachment destruction
```

remain the operative completed abandonments.

The two correspond to two distinct domain-completions not yet gathered into the final exhaustion of all influxes.

## Logical Determination

The verse distinguishes **cardinality** from **scope**.

```text
more items
    does not necessarily mean
more complete liberation
```

During the path of seeing:

```text
higher count
    = more completed stages accumulated
```

During the path of cultivation:

```text
lower count
    may equal greater integration
```

The governing relation is therefore:

```text
count of attainments
    ≠
degree of comprehensiveness
```

A later parijñā may subsume several earlier parijñās.

This is a logic of **compression by completion**:

```text
many partial completions
    → one comprehensive completion
```

The verse thus prevents a crude numerical reading of the Path.

## Path Determination

VAK 5.69 gives the practitioner a state-accounting rule.

The question is not merely:

```text
How many insights have occurred?
```

It is:

```text
Which completed abandonments
remain structurally operative now?
```

This matters because the Path has both:

```text
serial development
and
integrative consolidation
```

The path of seeing advances by adding determinate completions.

The path of cultivation advances by transforming their relation into broader domain-completions.

Thus a practitioner may move from six to one without losing liberation:

```text
reduction in count
    may be
increase in unity
```

## Organon Note

The verse requires a distinction between an event history and a current normalized state.

Suggested representation:

```text

    produces Parijna

PractitionerState
    currentlyPossesses ParijnaSet

ConsolidationRule
    maps several Parijna instances
    to one comprehensive Parijna
```

The model must therefore support:

```text
historical multiplicity
    +
current canonical compression
```

A naïve monotonic counter would be incorrect.

The relevant operation is:

```text
normalize(current attainments)
    according to
Path-stage and domain-completion
```

## Bhāṣya Questions

1. At precisely which darśanamārga moments does the count become one, two, three, four, and five?
2. Why is the first cultivation-stage count six rather than continuing directly from five to seven?
3. Under what exact conditions is the cultivator endowed with one parijñā?
4. Under what exact conditions is the cultivator endowed with two?
5. Does **samanvita** count present possession through **prāpti**, rather than historical production?
6. How does the consolidation of earlier parijñās operate ontologically within the Sarvāstivāda theory of acquisition?
7. Why are the lower-fetter and form-attachment completions retained as two, while the final destruction of all influxes is counted as one?

## Core Determination

```text
In the path of seeing,
completed abandonments accumulate
from none to five.

In the path of cultivation,
they are possessed as six,
or consolidated as one or two.

Liberative progress is therefore measured
not by number alone,
but by the scope and integration
of completed abandonment.
```
