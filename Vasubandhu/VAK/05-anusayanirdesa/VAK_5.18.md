# VAK 5.18

## Sanskrit

नानास्रवोर्ध्वविषयाः अस्वीकाराद् विपक्षतः ।
येन यः संप्रयुक्तस्तु स तस्मिन् संप्रयोगतः ॥ ५.१८ ॥

## IAST

nānāsravordhvaviṣayāḥ asvīkārād vipakṣataḥ |
yena yaḥ saṃprayuktas tu sa tasmin saṃprayogataḥ || 5.18 ||

## Translation

Those whose objects are different, uncontaminated, or higher do not adhere there, because those objects are not appropriated and because they are opposed. But whichever anuśaya is conjoined with a given dharma adheres to that dharma through conjunction.

## Analysis

Verse 5.17 defined adherence through the object (*ālambanataḥ*). Verse 5.18 now both restricts that mode and adds a second mode:

```text
anuśayana
    through object
    through conjunction
```

The first half denies unrestricted objective adherence. An anuśaya does not simply adhere to anything it can cognitively take as object. Three boundaries are compressed into the compound `nānāsravordhvaviṣayāḥ`:

```text
different domain or class
uncontaminated object
higher-level object
```

The kārikā gives two reasons:

```text
asvīkāra
    the object is not appropriated as one's own basis

vipakṣa
    the object stands in opposition to the anuśaya
```

Thus mere object-directedness is insufficient for adherence. The relation must be one in which the affliction can take possession of, settle into, or reproduce itself through its field.

The second half introduces adherence by conjunction:

```text
yena yaḥ saṃprayuktaḥ
    whichever anuśaya is associated with a dharma

sa tasmin saṃprayogataḥ
    adheres to that dharma through association
```

This shifts the analysis from intentional range to internal composition. An anuśaya can belong to a cognitive event not only because that event is its object, but because it arises together with the event's mental factors.

## Dialectical determination

The verse distinguishes two fundamentally different relations:

```text
objective relation
    what the anuśaya takes as its field

constitutive relation
    what the anuśaya enters into by co-arising association
```

This prevents a major confusion. The object of an affliction and the dharmas structurally joined to it are not the same relation.

The full movement of 5.17–5.18 is therefore:

```text
breadth of objective range
    → limits of objective adherence
    → internal adherence through conjunction
```

The strongest determination is:

```text
mere cognition of an object
    does not entail appropriation by affliction

but conjunction
    makes the affliction immanent to the cognitive event
```

So *anuśaya* is not only a tendency directed toward something. It is also a contaminating principle capable of inhering in a complex through association.

## Organon note

The verse separates two forms of relation:

```text
reference
    relation to an object

inherence
    relation within a constituted complex
```

That is a decisive scientific distinction. A determination may refer beyond itself without becoming constitutively identical with its object; by contrast, conjunction makes it an internal moment of the event.

## Bhāṣya questions

1. How exactly does the Bhāṣya divide the compound `nānāsravordhvaviṣayāḥ`?
2. Does `nānā` mean a different nikāya, a different realm, or a more general heterogeneity of object?
3. What precise force does `asvīkāra` have: non-appropriation, non-identification, or inability to treat the object as one's own support?
4. Which uncontaminated and higher objects are counted as `vipakṣa` in each case?
5. Does adherence through conjunction extend to every co-arisen caitta, and how is it distinguished from the exclusion of `prāpti` in 5.13?
