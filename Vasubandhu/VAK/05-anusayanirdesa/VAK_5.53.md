# VAK 5.53

## Sanskrit

माया शाठ्यं च कामाद्यध्यानयोर्ब्रह्मवञ्चनात् ।
स्त्यानौद्धत्यमदा धातुत्रयेऽन्ये कामधातुजाः ॥ ५.५३ ॥

## IAST

māyā śāṭhyaṃ ca kāmādya-dhyānayor brahma-vañcanāt /
styānauddhatya-madā dhātu-traye 'nye kāma-dhātujāḥ // 5.53 //

## Padaccheda

māyā | śāṭhyam | ca | kāma-ādya-dhyānayoḥ | brahma-vañcanāt | styāna-auddhatya-madāḥ | dhātu-traye | anye | kāma-dhātujāḥ

## Grammar

- **māyā** — nominative singular feminine, “deceit,” “falsifying appearance.”
- **śāṭhyam** — nominative singular neuter, “crookedness,” “duplicity.”
- **ca** — “and.”
- **kāma-ādya-dhyānayoḥ** — locative dual: “in the desire-domain and in the first meditation.” The compound joins **kāma** with **ādya-dhyāna**.
- **brahma-vañcanāt** — ablative singular: “because of the deceiving of Brahmā,” giving the doctrinal reason why deceit and crookedness are admitted in the first dhyāna.
- **styāna-auddhatya-madāḥ** — nominative plural: torpor, restlessness, and intoxicated self-elevation.
- **dhātu-traye** — locative singular, “in the three domains.”
- **anye** — nominative plural, “the others.”
- **kāma-dhātujāḥ** — nominative plural, “born in” or “belonging to the desire-domain.”

## Literal Translation

Deceit and crookedness occur in the desire-domain and in the first meditation, because Brahmā is deceived. Torpor, restlessness, and intoxicated self-elevation occur in the three domains; the others arise in the desire-domain.

## Philosophical Translation

The derivative afflictions are distributed according to the range within which their function remains possible. Deceit and crookedness extend beyond the desire-domain into the first meditation, since deceptive practical presentation can occur even there. Torpor, restlessness, and self-intoxication persist throughout all three domains. The remaining derivative afflictions are restricted to the desire-domain.

## Structural Analysis

VAK 5.52 classified derivative afflictions by mode of abandonment and ethical value. VAK 5.53 adds a third coordinate:

```text
mode of abandonment
ethical quality
domain of occurrence
```

This creates a genuine map of the afflicted field.

The verse divides the upakleśas into three ranges:

```text
limited extension
    māyā
    śāṭhya
    → kāmadhātu + first dhyāna

universal extension across conditioned existence
    styāna
    auddhatya
    mada
    → all three domains

restricted extension
    remaining upakleśas
    → kāmadhātu only
```

The classification is not based merely on intensity. It follows the functional conditions under which each determination can operate.

## Technical Vocabulary

### Māyā

A falsification of appearance through which the subject presents what is not the case as though it were the case.

```text
māyā
    = distortion of appearance
```

### Śāṭhya

Crookedness or duplicity in practical intention. It is not simply false speech; it is the oblique organization of conduct so that the subject’s actual orientation is concealed.

```text
śāṭhya
    = distortion of intention
```

### Brahma-vañcana

The allusion establishes that deceptive presentation is still possible in the first dhyāna. The higher meditative domain does not by itself eliminate strategic falsification.

The doctrinal point is therefore:

```text
higher state
    ≠
complete transparency
```

### Styāna

Mental heaviness or torpor. Its presence in all three domains means that diminished functional responsiveness is not confined to sensual existence.

### Auddhatya

Restlessness or elevation of mind. It persists in all three domains because agitation can accompany even refined forms of existence.

### Mada

Intoxicated self-elevation based on one’s condition, attainments, or qualities. Since any conditioned domain can be appropriated as “my superior state,” mada extends through all three domains.

### Dhātu-traya

The three domains:

```text
kāmadhātu
rūpadhātu
ārūpyadhātu
```

The verse therefore places derivative afflictions inside the cosmological architecture of conditioned existence.

## Doctrinal Determination

The decisive teaching is that ascent does not remove every affliction uniformly.

Some afflictions disappear when the conditions distinctive of the desire-domain disappear. Others survive because their function does not depend on sensual objects.

Thus:

```text
sensual dependence
    → domain-restricted affliction

formal or reflexive dependence
    → higher-domain persistence
```

Deceit and crookedness extend only into the first dhyāna because social and practical presentation remain possible there in the doctrinal example of deceiving Brahmā.

Torpor, restlessness, and intoxication persist through all three domains because they concern the internal mode of the continuum itself:

```text
styāna
    failure of responsiveness

auddhatya
    failure of composure

mada
    appropriation of one’s state
```

The remaining derivative afflictions require the relational and affective conditions of the desire-domain.

## Logical Determination

The verse introduces classification by **range of validity**.

A determination is not fully known merely by defining its content. One must also determine the field across which it can operate.

```text
what the determination is
    +
where the determination can occur
    =
complete doctrinal specification
```

The logical form is:

```text
function
    determines
range
```

The higher domains do not negate affliction abstractly. They remove only those afflictions whose conditions have been removed.

This yields an important rule:

```text
transcendence of a field
    removes only
field-dependent contradictions
```

Contradictions rooted in appropriation, agitation, or incapacity may reappear at a higher level.

## Path Determination

The Path must therefore mark not only the appearance and root of an affliction, but also its possible range.

```text
recognize the affliction
    ↓
identify its root
    ↓
determine its domain
    ↓
ask whether ascent alone removes it
```

VAK 5.53 warns against treating meditative elevation as universal purification.

```text
leaving kāmadhātu
    does not entail
leaving all upakleśas
```

The practitioner must test higher states for:

```text
crooked presentation
mental heaviness
restlessness
self-intoxication
```

The map prevents a higher attainment from being mistaken for the completion of the Path.

## Organon Note

For the Kośa Speculative Reason model, an afflictive determination needs at least four predicates:

```text
hasRoot
hasManifestFunction
hasAbandonmentMode
hasDomainRange
```

A domain transition should not delete every affliction attached to the lower state. It should remove only those whose enabling conditions are no longer satisfied.

```text
DomainTransition
    evaluates
Affliction.enablingConditions
```

This permits a precise representation of refined bondage:

```text
higher state
    + persistent reflexive affliction
    = higher-domain bondage
```

4. What is the precise doctrinal reason that styāna occurs in the formless domain?

6. Is mada in the higher domains based on attainment, existence, purity, longevity, or all of these?
