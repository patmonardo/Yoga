# VAK_5.05 — The Ninety-Eight Anuśayas

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
चत्वारो भावनाहेयास्त एवाप्रतिघाः पुनः ।
रूपधातौ तथारूप्ये इत्यष्टानवतिर् मताः ॥ ५.५ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
catvāro bhāvanāheyās ta evāpratighāḥ punaḥ |
rūpadhātau tathārūpye ity aṣṭānavatir matāḥ || 5.5 ||
```

The local source gives:


This verse completes the realm-by-realm classification begun in VAK_5.04 and establishes the canonical total of ninety-eight anuśayas.

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `catvāraḥ` | nominative masculine plural | four |
| `bhāvanā-heyāḥ` | nominative masculine plural compound | to be abandoned by cultivation |
| `te eva` | pronoun + emphatic particle | those very same |
| `apratighāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | without resistance / hostility |
| `punaḥ` | adverb | again, in turn |
| `rūpa-dhātau` | locative singular | in the form domain |
| `tathā` | adverb | likewise |
| `ārūpye` | locative singular | in the formless domain |
| `iti` | quotative / concluding particle | thus |
| `aṣṭānavatiḥ` | feminine numeral | ninety-eight |
| `matāḥ` | past passive participle, nominative plural by attraction to the classified items | held, regarded, accepted |

The expression:

```text
te eva apratighāḥ
```

means that the same classificatory pattern recurs in the higher domains, but without `pratigha`.

---

## 5. Literal translation

> Four are to be abandoned by cultivation. Those very same, again without resistance, occur in the form domain and likewise in the formless domain. Thus ninety-eight are held.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> In the desire domain, four anuśayas remain to be abandoned not by the initial seeing of the truths but through cultivation. In the form and formless domains, the same overall system recurs with hostility excluded. By combining the truth-specific classes abandoned by seeing with the residual classes abandoned by cultivation across the three domains, the system recognizes ninety-eight anuśayas.

---

# 7. The four abandoned by cultivation

The verse states:

```text
catvāraḥ bhāvanā-heyāḥ
    four are to be abandoned by cultivation
```

On the basis of the preceding tenfold system, the four are:

```text
rāga
pratigha
māna
avidyā
```

These are the non-view anuśayas that can continue after the truth-specific view-complex has been broken by seeing.

Their persistence is practical and dispositional rather than merely doctrinal:

```text
wrong view may be destroyed
    while attachment persists

truth may be seen
    while conceit persists

false doctrine may be removed
    while non-knowing persists as habit
```

Thus:

```text
darśana
    breaks the truth-specific cognitive structure

bhāvanā
    repeatedly transforms the remaining disposition
```

The Bhāṣya pass must determine the precise sense of `bhāvanā` here and how it differs from the direct abandonment produced by seeing.

---

# 8. Why `pratigha` is absent above the desire domain

The verse states:

```text
apratighāḥ
    without pratigha
```

`Pratigha` belongs to the desire domain and is absent from the form and formless domains.

A cautious first-pass determination is:

```text
pratigha
    resistance
    hostility
    aversive collision with an object
```

The higher domains remain conditioned and afflicted, but their afflictions do not include this gross aversive determination.

Therefore:

```text
desire domain
    rāga
    pratigha
    māna
    avidyā

form and formless domains
    rāga
    māna
    avidyā
```

This does not make the higher domains liberated.

```text
absence of hostility
    ≠
absence of attachment

subtle affliction
    ≠
freedom from affliction
```

That distinction continues the warning already made in VAK_5.02 concerning inward-directed attachment.

# 9. The arithmetic of ninety-eight

## 9.1 Desire domain

From VAK_5.04:

```text
seeing suffering       10
seeing origination      7
seeing cessation        7
seeing the path         8
                       --
                       32
```

Add the four abandoned by cultivation:

```text
32 + 4 = 36
```

Thus:

```text
kāmadhātu
    36 anuśayas
```

## 9.2 Form domain

Remove `pratigha` from each relevant class:

```text
seeing suffering        9
seeing origination      6
seeing cessation        6
seeing the path         7
                       --
                       28
```

Then add the three abandoned by cultivation:

```text
rāga
māna
avidyā
```

Therefore:

```text
28 + 3 = 31
```

## 9.3 Formless domain

The same calculation applies:

```text
28 + 3 = 31
```

## 9.4 Total

```text
36 + 31 + 31 = 98
```

Hence:

```text
aṣṭānavatiḥ anuśayāḥ
    ninety-eight anuśayas
```

# 10. Scientific character of the classification

The number ninety-eight does not indicate ninety-eight wholly unrelated psychological entities.

It is produced by a classificatory matrix:

```text
anuśaya species
    ×
truth-object
    ×
mode of abandonment
    ×
realm
```

The same underlying kind is counted distinctly when its:

```text
object differs
realm differs
mode of abandonment differs
```

Thus the classification is relational and systematic.

```text
one abstract affliction
    becomes many determinate classes
    through its position in the total field
```

This is why the Kośa can be encyclopedic without merely listing disconnected items.

# 11. Doctrinal determination

VAK_5.04–5.05 jointly establish:

```text
seeing-abandonment
    truth-specific
    cognitively decisive

cultivation-abandonment
    dispositional
    progressive
```

Across the three domains:

```text
kāmadhātu       36
rūpadhātu       31
ārūpyadhātu     31
                --
                98
```

The chapter has now moved from the generic six anuśayas to a complete technical map of their determinate occurrence.

# 12. Logical determination

The verse expresses differentiation by systematic position:

```text
genus
    anuśaya

species
    rāga, pratigha, māna, avidyā, dṛṣṭi, vicikitsā

further determination
    truth-object
    realm
    mode of abandonment
```

The result is:

```text
abstract unity
    → articulated multiplicity
    → systematic total
```

The ninety-eight are therefore not an accidental aggregate. They are a generated total.

# 15. Openings reserved for the Bhāṣya pass

The Bhāṣya should clarify:

1. the exact identity and rationale of the four `bhāvanā-heya` anuśayas;
2. why `pratigha` is impossible in the form and formless domains;
3. whether `te eva` refers to the complete prior classificatory pattern or only to specific classes;
4. the precise derivation of the ninety-eight;
5. the difference between abandonment by seeing and abandonment by cultivation;
6. whether the higher-domain forms of rāga, māna, and avidyā differ qualitatively from their desire-domain counterparts.
