# VAK_5.06 — Kṣānti, Seeing, and Cultivation

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
भवाग्रजाः क्षान्तिवध्या दृग्घेया एव शेषजाः ।
दृग्भावनाभ्यामक्षान्तिवध्या भावनयैव तु ॥ ५.६ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
bhavāgrajāḥ kṣāntivadhyā dṛggheyā eva śeṣajāḥ |
dṛgbhāvanābhyām akṣāntivadhyā bhāvanayaiva tu || 5.6 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
bhavāgrajāḥ kṣāntivadhya dṛggheyā eva śeṣajāḥ /
dṛgbhāvanābhyām akṣāntivadhyā bhāvanayaiva tu // VAkK_5.6 //
```

The verse is highly compressed. It introduces a relation among:

```text
bhavāgra
kṣānti
seeing
cultivation
and destruction or abandonment
```

Its exact distribution of predicates should remain explicitly open for the Bhāṣya pass.

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `bhavāgra-jāḥ` | nominative masculine plural compound | born in or belonging to the summit of existence |
| `kṣānti-vadhyāḥ` | nominative masculine plural compound | destroyable by kṣānti |
| `dṛk-heyāḥ` | nominative masculine plural compound | to be abandoned by seeing |
| `eva` | restrictive particle | precisely, only |
| `śeṣa-jāḥ` | nominative masculine plural compound | those born elsewhere; those belonging to the remaining levels |
| `dṛk-bhāvanābhyām` | instrumental dual | by seeing and cultivation |
| `a-kṣānti-vadhyāḥ` | nominative masculine plural compound | not destroyable by kṣānti |
| `bhāvanayā` | instrumental singular | by cultivation |
| `tu` | contrastive particle | but, however |

### Key compounds

```text
bhava-agra
    summit of existence
    highest level of conditioned existence

kṣānti-vadhya
    that which is to be destroyed by kṣānti

dṛk-heya
    that which is to be abandoned by seeing

bhāvanā-heya
    that which is to be abandoned by cultivation
```

`Kṣānti` is retained untranslated at this stage. It is not merely ordinary patience. In the path structure it names a determinate cognitive-path function whose exact technical force will be established in the Bhāṣya and Chapter 6.

---

## 5. Literal translation

> Those belonging to the summit of existence are destroyable by kṣānti; those belonging to the remaining levels are precisely to be abandoned by seeing. Those not destroyable by kṣānti are [abandoned] by seeing and cultivation, but [others] by cultivation alone.

This literal rendering is provisional because the abbreviated syntax permits more than one distribution of the final predicates.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> The anuśayas are not removed through one undifferentiated act. Their abandonment depends upon their level, their classificatory status, and the path-function capable of opposing them. The dispositions associated with the summit of conditioned existence stand in a distinctive relation to `kṣānti`; other dispositions are assigned to seeing, to the combined scope of seeing and cultivation, or to cultivation alone. The scientific point is that the counteragent must be adequate to the determination that is to be removed.

The verse adds a new axis to the ninety-eightfold matrix:

```text
realm and level
    × class of anuśaya
    × path-function
    = mode of abandonment
```

---

# 7. `Bhavāgra`

```text
bhava
    existence
    conditioned becoming

agra
    summit
    highest point
```

`Bhavāgra` names the summit of conditioned existence, conventionally the highest formless level.

Its importance here is practical rather than merely cosmological:

```text
highest conditioned level
    is still conditioned

subtle existence
    is still existence

refinement
    is not release
```

The finest state within the field cannot be treated as standing beyond the field.

---

# 8. `Kṣānti-vadhya`

```text
kṣānti
    a technical path determination

vadhya
    to be struck down
    destroyable
    liable to elimination
```

The compound does not describe accidental disappearance. It identifies a determinate opposition:

```text
specific afflictive determination
    ↔
specific path capacity
```

The Bhāṣya must clarify:

```text
which kṣānti is intended
why bhavāgra is singled out
whether kṣānti directly destroys the anuśaya
or establishes the decisive abandonment relation
```

---

# 9. Seeing and cultivation

The verse continues the distinction established in VAK_5.04–5.05:

```text
darśana
    seeing the truth
    decisive disclosure of an error in determination

bhāvanā
    cultivation
    repeated stabilization and development
```

These are not interchangeable procedures.

```text
seeing
    overturns what depends upon radical misdetermination

cultivation
    removes what persists as habituated or graded disposition
```

The kārikā now indicates that the path must be differentiated according to the structure of what is abandoned.

---

# 10. The meaning of `vadhya` and `heya`

The verse uses two distinct verbal ideas:

```text
vadhya
    to be destroyed

heya
    to be abandoned
```

They should not be collapsed without examination.

A possible distinction is:

```text
destruction
    the counteragent's decisive operation

abandonment
    the resulting disjunction from the afflicted determination
```

This remains a working hypothesis for the Bhāṣya pass.

---

# 11. Logical determination

VAK_5.06 transforms the static classification of anuśayas into a theory of adequate negation.

```text
not every defect
    is removed by the same operation
```

The negating process must correspond to the form of the defect:

```text
object-error
    → seeing

habitual persistence
    → cultivation

highest subtle attachment
    → technically determined kṣānti relation
```

Thus abandonment is not simple deletion. It is a scientifically specified transformation.

---

# 12. Restrained Organon interpretation

For the later Organon Report, the verse suggests a typed counteragent system:

## 14. Kārikā–Bhāṣya opening

The independent kārikā establishes:

```text
abandonment is differentiated
by level and path-function
```

The Bhāṣya must recover:

```text
1. the precise referent of each predicate
2. the technical definition of kṣānti-vadhya
3. why bhavāgra receives special treatment
4. the scope of dṛgbhāvanābhyām
5. the subject governed by bhāvanayā eva
6. the distinction between vadha and hāna/prahāṇa
```

This is a clear example of encyclopedic compression presupposing commentary while remaining a foundational textual determination.
