# VAK 5.61

## Sanskrit

प्रहाणाधारभूतत्त्वदूषणाख्यश् चतुर्विधः ।
प्रतिपक्षः प्रहातव्यः क्लेश आलम्बनान् मतः ॥ ५.६१ ॥

## IAST

prahāṇādhārabhūtatva-dūṣaṇākhyaś caturvidhaḥ |

pratipakṣaḥ prahātavyaḥ kleśa ālambanān mataḥ || 5.61 ||

## Textual Note

The transmitted GRETIL reading is compressed and appears textually uncertain in the compound:

```text
prahāṇādhārabhūtatva-dūṣaṇākhyaḥ
```

The kārikā explicitly states `caturvidhaḥ`, “fourfold,” but the transmitted compound does not yield four securely separable names without emendation or commentary. This first-pass file therefore preserves the received text and does not manufacture a four-member list. The critical reading and doctrinal enumeration must be established in the Bhāṣya pass.

## Grammar

The first sentence is nominal:

```text
pratipakṣaḥ caturvidhaḥ
    the counteragent is fourfold
```

The compound ending in `-ākhyaḥ` predicates the names or designations of that fourfold counteragent, but the transmitted reading is not sufficiently secure to divide the list with confidence.

The second sentence is also nominal:

```text
prahātavyaḥ kleśaḥ ālambanāt mataḥ
```

The gerundive `prahātavyaḥ` agrees with `kleśaḥ`:

```text
the affliction is to be abandoned
```

The ablative `ālambanāt` gives the standpoint or ground from which it is so considered:

```text
from the object-support
through the object-support
with respect to the object-support
```

The exact doctrinal force of this ablative remains to be fixed from the Bhāṣya.

## Literal Translation

The counteragent is fourfold, bearing the compressed designation concerning abandonment, support, actuality, and discrediting. The affliction is held to be something to be abandoned from the standpoint of its object-support.

## Philosophical Translation

Counteraction is fourfold. An affliction is not treated as an isolated item; it is determined as something to be abandoned in relation to the object-support through which it operates.

## Technical Vocabulary

### pratipakṣa

```text
pratipakṣa
    counteragent
    opposing determination
```

Verse 5.60 named the arising of a counteragent as one ground of exhaustion. Verse 5.61 now states that this counteragent is itself fourfold.

### prahātavya

```text
prahātavya
    that which is to be abandoned
```

The gerundive gives a normative-path determination: the kleśa is not merely destructible; it is specifically an object of abandonment.

### kleśa

```text
kleśa
    affliction
```

Here the kleśa is determined through its relation to an `ālambana`.

### ālambana

```text
ālambana
    object-support
```

The verse preserves the relational constitution of affliction:

```text
kleśa
    operates with an object-support
```

### ākhya

```text
ākhya
    called
    designated
```

It marks a technical classification, though the transmitted names require textual control.

## Logical Determination

The secure kārikā structure is:

```text
pratipakṣa
    is fourfold

kleśa
    is to be abandoned
    in relation to object-support
```

The verse therefore adds two determinations to 5.60:

```text
counteraction has internal differentiation

abandonment is object-relational
```

The first-pass conclusion must remain modest. We can securely distinguish:

```text
kṣaya
    exhaustion

pratipakṣa
    counteragent

prahātavya kleśa
    affliction as that which is to be abandoned

ālambana
    the object-support relative to which abandonment is determined
```

We cannot yet securely identify the four counteragents from the corrupt or compressed compound alone.

## Organon Interpretation

Restrained Organon formulation:

```text
The negative determination is not isolated.

It is a relation to an object-support,
and its counteraction is internally differentiated.
```

This preserves the scientific structure without prematurely supplying a speculative sequence.
