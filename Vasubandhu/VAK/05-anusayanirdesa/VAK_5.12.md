# VAK_5.12 — The Universal Anuśayas

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
सर्वत्रगा दुःखहेतुदृग्घेया दृष्टयस्तथा ।
विमतिः सह ताभिश्च याऽविद्याऽवैणिकी च या ॥ ५.१२ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
sarvatragā duḥkha-hetu-dṛg-gheyā dṛṣṭayas tathā |
vimatiḥ saha tābhiś ca yāvidyā 'veṇikī ca yā || 5.12 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
sarvatragā duḥkhahetudṛggheyā dṛṣṭayastathā /
vimatiḥ saha tābhiśca yāvidyāveṇikī ca yā // VAkK_5.12 //
```

This verse begins the classification of the `sarvatraga` anuśayas: the universally operative afflictive determinations.

---

## 3. Padaccheda

```text
sarvatragāḥ |
duḥkha-hetu-dṛk-gheyāḥ |
dṛṣṭayaḥ |
tathā |
vimatiḥ |
saha |
tābhiḥ |
ca |
yā |
avidyā |
āveṇikī |
ca |
yā
```

A working syntactical reconstruction is:

```text
duḥkha-hetu-dṛk-gheyāḥ dṛṣṭayaḥ sarvatragāḥ |
tathā vimatiḥ |
tābhiḥ saha yā avidyā |
yā ca āveṇikī avidyā |
[sarvatragāḥ]
```

That is:

```text
The views abandoned through seeing suffering and origin are universal;
so too is doubt,
the ignorance associated with those views,
and the ignorance that is independent.
```

---

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `sarvatragāḥ` | nominative plural | going everywhere, universally operative |
| `duḥkha-hetu-dṛk-gheyāḥ` | compound, nominative plural | to be abandoned through seeing suffering and its origin |
| `dṛṣṭayaḥ` | nominative feminine plural | views |
| `vimatiḥ` | nominative feminine singular | doubt, divided judgment |
| `tābhiḥ saha` | instrumental plural + indeclinable | together with those |
| `avidyā` | nominative feminine singular | ignorance, non-knowing |
| `āveṇikī` | nominative feminine singular | independent, unassociated, exclusive |

The compound:

```text
duḥkha-hetu-dṛk-gheyāḥ
```

contains two truth-fields:

```text
duḥkha
    suffering

hetu
    origin or cause
```

and one mode of abandonment:

```text
dṛk-gheya
    to be abandoned through seeing
```

---

## 5. Literal translation

> The views to be abandoned through seeing suffering and its origin are universal. So too are doubt, the ignorance associated with those views, and the ignorance that is independent.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> Certain afflictive determinations are called universal because they do not remain confined to one narrow object or occasion. The views removed through seeing suffering and its causal origin operate across the conditioned field. Doubt shares this universal reach. So does ignorance when conjoined with those views, and even ignorance when it arises independently rather than merely accompanying another affliction.

The verse therefore distinguishes two modes of ignorance:

```text
associated ignorance
    ignorance operating with false view

independent ignorance
    ignorance operating on its own
```

Both can have universal range.

---

# 7. `Sarvatraga`

```text
sarvatra-gā
    going everywhere
    ranging throughout
    universally operative
```

The term does not mean that an anuśaya is literally active at every moment.

It means that its field of possible operation is broad and structurally pervasive.

```text
particular affliction
    binds through a restricted object

universal affliction
    can structure the entire conditioned field
```

The universal anuśaya is therefore not merely one more item in the list.

It has a different logical reach.

```text
local distortion
    affects one determinate relation

universal distortion
    governs the form under which many relations are understood
```

---

# 8. Why suffering and origin are primary

The universal views are specifically those abandoned through seeing:

```text
duḥkha
    the conditioned field as suffering

samudaya / hetu
    the causal production of that field
```

This is significant.

The most pervasive false determinations concern:

```text
what conditioned existence is
and
how conditioned existence arises
```

A false determination at either level propagates widely:

```text
false determination of the field
    → false judgment concerning its parts

false determination of causality
    → false explanation of arising and continuation
```

Thus the universal anuśayas are rooted in errors about both:

```text
being-thus of the conditioned field
and
becoming of that field
```

---

# 9. `Vimati`

```text
vi-mati
    divided thought
    uncertainty
    doubt
```

Doubt is universal because it can suspend or destabilize the whole path-relevant determination of the field.

```text
Is this suffering?
Is this its cause?
Is cessation possible?
Is this the path?
```

The verse, however, places universal doubt here with the views abandoned through seeing suffering and origin.

The exact scope of that placement should be confirmed in the Bhāṣya.

The secure first-pass point is:

```text
doubt is not merely absence of a conclusion

it is an afflicted mode of divided determination
```

It can prevent the field from being stably known under its adequate predicates.

---

# 10. Associated ignorance

```text
tābhiḥ saha yā avidyā
    the ignorance that is together with those [views]
```

Ignorance may operate as the obscuring condition within a false view.

```text
false view
    is the determinate distortion

associated ignorance
    is the non-knowing sustaining that distortion
```

The two are not identical.

```text
avidyā
    obscures

dṛṣṭi
    positively determines falsely
```

Together they form a stronger structure:

```text
non-knowing
    +
false determination
    =
self-reinforcing error
```

This is one reason such anuśayas can be universal.

---

# 11. Independent ignorance

```text
āveṇikī avidyā
    independent ignorance
    ignorance not merely associated with another affliction
```

This is philosophically important.

Ignorance is not reducible to a hidden component of false opinion.

It can operate independently:

```text
without articulated doctrine
without explicit wrong view
without conscious denial
```

The continuum may simply fail to apprehend the field adequately.

```text
absence of adequate determination
    can itself govern cognition and activity
```

Thus the Kośa recognizes two failures:

```text
positive falsity
    taking what is not the case as the case

independent obscuration
    failing to apprehend what is the case
```

Both are causally productive.

---

# 12. Dialectical structure

The verse gives a precise hierarchy of defective cognition:

```text
avidyā
    non-knowing

vimati
    divided determination

dṛṣṭi
    false positive determination
```

These are not interchangeable.

They express increasingly determinate forms of failure:

```text
obscuration
    → hesitation
    → fixed distortion
```

Yet the causal order need not always be linear.

A false view may reinforce ignorance, while ignorance sustains the false view.

```text
avidyā sustains dṛṣṭi

dṛṣṭi deepens avidyā
```

This reciprocal reinforcement explains their pervasive range.

---

# 13. Positive dialectic

The classification of universal affliction implies a corresponding universal correction.

```text
universal false determination
    requires
truth-directed seeing
```

The counter-operation is not arbitrary suppression.

```text
error concerning suffering
    is removed by seeing suffering

error concerning origin
    is removed by seeing origin
```

The path is therefore positive and determinate:

```text
adequate object
    +
adequate seeing
    =
removal of the corresponding universal affliction
```

This is the same positive dialectic already emerging in 5.06–5.09.

---

# 14. Restrained Organon interpretation

The verse suggests a useful distinction between local and global error conditions.

```text
local defect
    corrupts one judgment or operation

universal defect
    corrupts the governing model of the field
```

In an Agent architecture:

```text
local error
    one Feature misclassifies one object

universal error
    the Model or Frame distributes a false determination
    across many Features and Plans
```

The analogy should remain restrained.

The kārikā is classifying afflictive dharmas, not software defects.

But its scientific lesson is exact:

```text
the reach of an error
    depends upon the structural level
    at which it operates
```

---

# 15. Kārikā–Bhāṣya tension

The kārikā securely establishes:

```text
views abandoned through seeing suffering and origin are universal

doubt is universal

ignorance associated with those views is universal

independent ignorance is universal
```

The Bhāṣya must still clarify:

```text
which exact views are included

how many universal anuśayas are counted

whether universality is restricted to one's own realm or level

the exact definition of āveṇikī avidyā

why doubt is grouped in this way

how universal anuśayas take their objects
```

The next verse continues precisely this inquiry by specifying their upward object-range and the dharmas that share their universality.

---

# 16. Summary

```text
universal anuśayas
    views abandoned by seeing suffering and origin
    doubt
    ignorance associated with those views
    independent ignorance
```

Their common feature is not mere intensity.

It is range:

```text
sarvatraga
    a distortion capable of governing the conditioned field broadly
```

The verse therefore introduces a new axis of classification:

```text
not only
    what an affliction is

but also
    how widely it operates
```
