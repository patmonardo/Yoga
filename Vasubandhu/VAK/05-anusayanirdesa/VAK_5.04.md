# VAK_5.04 — Abandonment by Seeing the Four Truths in the Desire Domain

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
दशैते सप्त सप्ताष्टौ त्रिद्विदृष्टिविवर्जिताः ।
यथाक्रमं प्रहीयन्ते कामे दुःखादिदर्शनैः ॥ ५.४ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
daśaite sapta saptāṣṭau tri-dvi-dṛṣṭi-vivarjitāḥ |
yathākramaṃ prahīyante kāme duḥkhādi-darśanaiḥ || 5.4 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
daśaite saptāsaptāṣṭau tridvidṛṣṭivivarjitāḥ /
yathākramaṃ prahīyante kāme duḥkhādidarśanaiḥ // VAkK_5.4 //
```

The verse applies the tenfold classification of VAK_5.03 to the four truths within the desire domain.

## 5. Literal translation

> These ten, [then] seven, seven, and eight, deprived respectively of three and two views, are abandoned in the desire domain through seeing suffering and the remaining truths in order.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> Within the desire domain, the ten anuśayas are distributed according to the four truths by which they are overcome. Seeing suffering abandons all ten. Seeing origination abandons seven; seeing cessation likewise abandons seven; and seeing the path abandons eight. The reduced groups arise because certain views do not take every truth as their determining object. Abandonment is therefore not an undifferentiated suppression of affliction, but a truth-specific transformation in which each class of underlying disposition is removed through the disclosure of the field it misconstrues.

The verse introduces a decisive scientific structure:

```text
anuśaya
    is classified
        by the truth whose seeing abandons it
```

---

# 7. The four counts

```text
duḥkha-darśana
    10

samudaya-darśana
    7

nirodha-darśana
    7

mārga-darśana
    8
```

The sequence is governed by:

```text
duḥkha-ādi
    suffering
    origination
    cessation
    path
```

Thus the verse does not merely count afflictions. It maps them onto the fourfold structure of liberating cognition.

---

# 8. `Darśana`

```text
darśana
    seeing
    direct doctrinal cognition
    truth-disclosing determination
```

Here `darśana` is not ordinary sensory seeing.

It is the cognition through which a truth becomes determinative for the continuum:

```text
truth is seen
    ↓
corresponding false determination loses its ground
    ↓
anuśaya is abandoned
```

The verse therefore links epistemic disclosure and practical transformation.

```text
seeing
    is itself
        an operation of abandonment
```

---

# 9. `Prahīyante`

```text
pra-hā
    to abandon
    to relinquish
    to remove
```

The passive form:

```text
prahīyante
    they are abandoned
```

places emphasis on the classes of anuśaya as what is removed through the corresponding seeing.

This is not mere behavioral restraint.

```text
restraint
    prevents an expression

abandonment
    removes the afflictive determination at its classified ground
```

The later Bhāṣya must clarify the precise sense in which the anuśaya is abandoned and how this relates to acquisition of disjunction.

---

# 10. Why the counts differ

The ten anuśayas are not uniformly related to all four truths.

Some views are structurally tied to suffering as appropriated existence and therefore do not appear in every later truth-specific group.

The verse marks this by subtraction:

```text
full field
    ten

reduced fields
    seven
    seven
    eight
```

The classification is relational:

```text
anuśaya
    + object-field
    + mode of seeing
    = determinate abandonment class
```

This prepares the later discussion of:

```text
ālambana
    object-support

sarvatraga
    universally operative anuśaya

prahāṇa
    abandonment
```

---

# 11. Doctrinal determination

The verse establishes four sets of anuśayas to be abandoned by seeing in the desire domain:

| Truth seen | Number abandoned |
|---|---:|
| suffering | 10 |
| origination | 7 |
| cessation | 7 |
| path | 8 |

The likely exclusions are:

```text
for origination and cessation
    three views excluded

for path
    two views excluded
```

The kārikā gives the subtraction rule but not its full doctrinal explanation. That explanation properly belongs to the Bhāṣya pass.

---

# 12. Logical determination

VAK_5.04 changes the classification from a simple list into a matrix.

```text
anuśaya species
    ×
truth-object
    ×
mode of abandonment
```

The resulting determination is no longer merely:

```text
what afflictions exist?
```

It becomes:

```text
which affliction
is removed
by which disclosure?
```

This is the beginning of a genuine path-science.

The class is defined by its counter-determination:

```text
misdetermination of suffering
    ↔ seeing suffering

misdetermination of origination
    ↔ seeing origination

misdetermination of cessation
    ↔ seeing cessation

misdetermination of path
    ↔ seeing path
```

# 13. Light Organon interpretation

The verse presents a compact rule system rather than a flat taxonomy.

```text
AfflictionClass {
    domain
    truthObject
    abandonmentMode
}
```

A preliminary scientific representation would therefore require more than an `Anusaya` class. It would require a relation among:

```text
Anuśaya
Truth
Domain
Seeing
Abandonment
```

The practical meaning is:

```text
correct classification
    identifies
        the exact cognition required for removal
```

This interpretation remains provisional until the Bhāṣya explains the excluded views and the technical mechanics of abandonment.

# 15. Kārikā–Bhāṣya opening

The kārikā independently establishes:

```text
four truth-specific counts
location in the desire domain
abandonment through seeing
subtraction of three and two views
```

The Bhāṣya must recover:

```text
which views are excluded from each group
why those views do not belong to those truth-fields
how seeing effects abandonment
whether the count concerns anuśayas as substances, types, or acquisitions
```

The first-pass result is therefore determinate but intentionally incomplete.
