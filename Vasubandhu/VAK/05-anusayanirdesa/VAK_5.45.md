# VAK 5.45

## Sanskrit

पञ्चधैवोर्ध्वभागीयं द्वौ रागौ रूप्यरूपिजौ ।
औद्धत्यमानमोहाश्च विद्वशाद् बन्धनत्रयम् ॥ ५.४५ ॥

## IAST

pañcadhaivordhvabhāgīyaṃ dvau rāgau rūpyarūpijau /
auddhatyamānamohāś ca vidvaśād bandhanatrayam // 5.45 //

## Source Caution

The transmitted source used for this Kārikā pass reads **vidvaśād bandhanatrayam** in the second half. The five named higher fetters are clear, but the exact segmentation and explanatory force of **vidvaśād** require confirmation in the Pradhan text and Bhāṣya during the second pass. The primary structural analysis therefore rests on the unambiguous enumeration rather than on a forced interpretation of this phrase.

## Padaccheda

pañcadhā | eva | ūrdhva-bhāgīyam | dvau | rāgau | rūpi | arūpi-jau | auddhatya | māna | mohāḥ | ca | vidvaśāt | bandhana-trayam

## Grammar

- **pañcadhā eva** — “precisely fivefold,” emphasizing the fixed five-member classification.
- **ūrdhva-bhāgīyam** — accusative/nominative singular neuter used collectively: “belonging to the higher division,” hence “the higher-group [fetters].”
- **dvau rāgau** — nominative dual masculine, “two attachments” or “two passions.”
- **rūpi-arūpi-jau** — nominative dual masculine: “arising in relation to the form and formless [domains].” The first is attachment to form-existence; the second attachment to formless existence.
- **auddhatya** — restlessness, excitation, or elevation of mind.
- **māna** — conceit, self-estimation, comparative elevation.
- **mohāḥ** — here singular in sense despite the transmitted form: delusion or ignorance.
- **ca** — “and.”
- **bandhana-trayam** — “a triad of bonds.” The phrase appears to gather auddhatya, māna, and moha as three further modes of bondage.

## Literal Translation

The higher-group [fetters] are precisely fivefold: the two attachments arising with respect to form and the formless, and the triad of bonds—restlessness, conceit, and delusion.

## Philosophical Translation

Even after the lower field has been transcended, bondage persists in five subtler forms: attachment to existence in the form-domain, attachment to existence in the formless domain, restless elevation, comparative self-assertion, and ignorance. The higher standpoint is therefore not yet liberation; it can itself become an object of appropriation and self-maintenance.

## Structural Analysis

VAK 5.43–5.44 analyzed the five lower fetters according to two failures:

```text
failure to leave the desire-domain
    +
failure to secure the path of departure
```

VAK 5.45 now completes the fetter classification by showing that successful ascent does not yet abolish bondage.

```text
lower fetters
    bind through the lower domain

higher fetters
    bind through elevated existence itself
```

The five higher fetters are:

| Fetter | Domain or operation | Determination |
|---|---|---|
| **rūpa-rāga** | form-domain | attachment to refined determinate existence |
| **arūpa-rāga** | formless domain | attachment to indeterminate or immaterial existence |
| **auddhatya** | mental movement | failure of complete composure |
| **māna** | self-relation | comparative or reflexive elevation of self |
| **moha / avidyā** | cognition | non-discrimination of the true character of existence |

The important transition is:

```text
bondage to lower objects
    ↓
bondage to higher modes of being
```

Thus transcendence can remain finite. One may negate coarse attachment while preserving attachment to the elevated result of that negation.

## Technical Vocabulary

### Ūrdhvabhāgīya

Literally “belonging to the higher division.” These fetters bind beings to the higher domains of form and formless existence. “Higher” is therefore a cosmological and meditative determination, not an assertion that the fetters themselves are superior.

### Rūparāga

Attachment to existence associated with form. This is subtler than ordinary sensual attachment because its object is refined meditative or form-domain existence.

### Arūparāga

Attachment to formless existence. Even the abstraction from form can be appropriated as a desirable state and retained as an end.

### Auddhatya

Restlessness, excitation, or the mind’s inability to become completely settled. Here it is not merely gross distraction; it survives at advanced stages and prevents final release.

### Māna

Conceit or self-measurement. It is a reflexive comparison through which the continuum continues to posit itself as higher, equal, or lower.

### Moha

Delusion or ignorance. It is the remaining non-discrimination that allows elevated existence, restlessness, and self-measurement to retain their binding force.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse denies that ascent through the domains is identical with liberation.

```text
higher rebirth
    ≠
release

subtle meditative existence
    ≠
cessation of appropriation
```

The first two fetters bind through objects of existence:

```text
form-existence
formless existence
```

The remaining three bind through the structure of the subject:

```text
movement
self-estimation
non-knowing
```

This yields a twofold architecture:

```text
objective retention
    = the two rāgas

subjective retention
    = auddhatya + māna + moha
```

The practitioner may therefore relinquish the lower world while still preserving both a preferred mode of being and the reflexive subject that prefers it.

## Logical Determination

VAK 5.45 reveals a characteristic form of finite transcendence:

```text
negation of the lower
    becomes
affirmation of the higher
    which becomes
new bondage
```

The higher determination remains finite whenever it is held as a fixed result.

```text
ascent
    without self-sublation
        = elevated attachment
```

The verse therefore distinguishes:

```text
transition to a higher sphere
    from
freedom from the form of attachment
```

This is a decisive speculative distinction. A determination is not liberated merely because its content is more universal, subtle, or abstract. Liberation requires that the very act of holding the determination be overcome.

## Organon Note

For the Kośa Speculative Reason work, VAK 5.45 supplies a model of **recursive elevation**:

```text
Stage A is negated.
Stage B emerges as its higher truth.
Stage B is appropriated as a fixed possession.
Stage B therefore reproduces bondage at a higher level.
```

A reasoning system must consequently distinguish:

```text
higherOrder(state)
    from
liberated(state)
```

and:

```text
abstractedFrom(x, lowerDomain)
    does not entail
freeFromAttachment(x)
```

This may become an important action-plan rule:

> Every successful transcendence must be tested for re-appropriation of its result.

The Kingdom of Ends remains finite when the agent treats even the highest available end as something possessed by a persisting self.

## Bhāṣya Questions

1. How does the Bhāṣya explain the transmitted phrase **vidvaśād bandhanatrayam**?
2. Why are the two rāgas separated from the final triad in the syntax of the verse?
3. Is **auddhatya** understood as a universal subtle instability or specifically as instability remaining in higher-domain beings?
4. How does **māna** function after explicit self-view has already been abandoned?
5. Is **moha** here equivalent to the remaining bhāvanā-heya avidyā?
6. Does the Bhāṣya explicitly distinguish attachment to meditative attainment from attachment to rebirth in the corresponding domain?
7. In what precise sense do these five bind the practitioner to the “higher division”?
