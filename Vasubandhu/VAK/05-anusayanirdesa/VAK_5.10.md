# VAK_5.10 — Seven Conceits and Their Modes of Abandonment

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
सप्त मानाः नवविधास्त्रिभ्यो दृग्भावनाक्षयाः ।
वधादिपर्यवस्थानं हेयं भावनया तथा ॥ ५.१० ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
sapta mānāḥ navavidhās tribhyo dṛg-bhāvanā-kṣayāḥ |
vadhādi-paryavasthānaṃ heyaṃ bhāvanayā tathā || 5.10 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
sapta mānāḥ navavidhāstribhyaḥ dṛgbhāvanākṣayāḥ /
vadhādiparyavasthānaṃ heyaṃ bhāvanayā tathā // VAkK_5.10 //
```

The verse is highly compressed. It brings together:

```text
seven forms of conceit
nine forms derived from three
abandonment through seeing
abandonment through cultivation
and a further manifestation beginning with vadhā
```

The precise allocation of the seven and nine forms to the two modes of exhaustion must remain open for the Bhāṣya pass.

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `sapta` | numeral | seven |
| `mānāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | conceits, comparative self-estimations |
| `navavidhāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | ninefold, of nine kinds |
| `tribhyaḥ` | ablative plural of `tri` | from three |
| `dṛk-bhāvanā-kṣayāḥ` | compound, nominative masculine plural | exhausted through seeing and cultivation |
| `vadhādi-paryavasthānam` | nominative/accusative neuter singular compound | a manifest fixation beginning with killing or injury |
| `heyam` | gerundive, nominative/accusative neuter singular | to be abandoned |
| `bhāvanayā` | instrumental singular | through cultivation |
| `tathā` | adverb | likewise, in the same way |

### `Māna`

```text
māna
    measuring
    estimation
    conceit
    comparative self-positioning
```

The term is connected with the verbal root √`mā`, “to measure.” Conceit is therefore not merely vanity or an emotion of pride. It is a distorted act of measurement in which the continuum places itself as:

```text
superior
 equal
 inferior
```

relative to another.

---

## 5. Literal translation

> The conceits are seven; they are ninefold from three. They are exhausted through seeing and cultivation. Likewise, the manifest fixation beginning with killing is to be abandoned through cultivation.

Because the verse compresses several technical distributions into one statement, this translation preserves the sequence without pretending to resolve every doctrinal allocation independently of the Bhāṣya.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> Conceit is not a single undifferentiated affliction. It is articulated into seven determinate forms and, from three fundamental comparative positions, into a ninefold system. Some of these false measurements are extinguished when their supporting view is broken by seeing; others persist as habitual dispositions and therefore require cultivation. A manifest afflictive fixation beginning with injury or killing is likewise removed not merely by conceptual correction, but through the sustained transformation of cultivation.

The verse moves from false view to false self-measurement:

```text
5.7–5.9
    the field is falsely determined

5.10
    the agent measures itself falsely within that field
```

Thus `māna` is a relational failure:

```text
self
    measured against
other
    under an afflicted standard
```

---

# 7. The seven forms of `māna`

The standard Abhidharma enumeration associated with the phrase `sapta mānāḥ` is:

```text
1. māna
2. atimāna
3. mānātimāna
4. asmimāna
5. abhimāna
6. ūnamāna
7. mithyāmāna
```

These names should be verified and fully defined through the Bhāṣya, but they provide the likely classificatory field presupposed by the kārikā.

## 7.1 `Māna`

Ordinary conceit: measuring oneself against another through a distorted comparative judgment.

## 7.2 `Atimāna`

Excessive conceit: taking oneself as superior to one who is equal, or as equal to one who is superior.

## 7.3 `Mānātimāna`

Conceit beyond conceit: taking oneself as superior even to one who is actually superior.

## 7.4 `Asmimāna`

The conceit “I am”:

```text
asmi
    I am

māna
    self-measurement
```

This is more fundamental than ordinary social vanity. It is the afflicted reflex by which the aggregate continuum is appropriated under the determination “I am.”

## 7.5 `Abhimāna`

Presumptive conceit: claiming an attainment or quality not actually possessed.

## 7.6 `Ūnamāna`

Conceit of inferiority: measuring oneself as only slightly inferior, or maintaining self-reference through the judgment of inferiority.

This is especially important because conceit does not disappear merely when the agent judges itself lower than another.

```text
superiority can be conceit
 equality can be conceit
 inferiority can be conceit
```

The affliction lies in the appropriative measurement itself.

## 7.7 `Mithyāmāna`

False conceit: claiming excellence or attainment in relation to a basis that does not support the judgment.

The exact boundaries among `abhimāna` and `mithyāmāna` require Bhāṣya clarification.

---

# 8. The ninefold system from three

The phrase:

```text
navavidhāḥ tribhyaḥ
    ninefold from three
```

indicates a combinatorial production from three fundamental comparative positions:

```text
superior
 equal
 inferior
```

A likely matrix is produced by relating:

```text
what one is
    superior / equal / inferior

to how one takes oneself
    superior / equal / inferior
```

This yields nine possibilities:

| Actual or posited relation | Taken as superior | Taken as equal | Taken as inferior |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| superior | 1 | 2 | 3 |
| equal | 4 | 5 | 6 |
| inferior | 7 | 8 | 9 |

The Bhāṣya must determine whether this is the exact intended generation and how these nine are assigned to named forms of conceit.

Nevertheless, the kārikā securely establishes the principle:

```text
three elementary comparative positions
    generate
nine determinate forms of self-measurement
```

This is an explicitly systematic production, not an accidental list.

---

# 9. Conceit as afflicted measurement

`Māna` is one of the clearest examples of an affliction whose structure is already logical.

```text
subject
    measures itself
against an object
    according to a standard
and produces
    a comparative judgment
```

The failure can occur in each moment:

```text
subject appropriated as self
object reduced to comparison
standard distorted by attachment or view
judgment converted into superiority, equality, or inferiority
```

Thus conceit is not merely an additional emotion following cognition. It is a malformed relational judgment.

---

# 10. `Dṛk-bhāvanā-kṣayāḥ`

The compound establishes a twofold exhaustion:

```text
dṛk-kṣaya
    exhaustion through seeing

bhāvanā-kṣaya
    exhaustion through cultivation
```

This continues the positive dialectic of 5.4–5.6.

Some forms of conceit depend directly upon a false view. When the view is contradicted by seeing, the conceit loses its determining ground.

Other forms survive the correction of view as dispositions embedded in the continuum:

```text
view corrected
    yet comparative reaction recurs

therefore
    cultivation is required
```

The difference may be represented as:

```text
false principle
    removed by seeing

habitual operation
    removed by cultivation
```

This is a major Abhidharma distinction. Correct insight does not automatically erase every sedimented mode of reaction.

---

# 11. Seeing and cultivation as positive dialectic

The verse does not say merely that conceit is negated.

It distinguishes the positive operation adequate to each form:

```text
seeing
    overturns the false determination

cultivation
    restructures the recurrent disposition
```

Thus abandonment is not external suppression:

```text
not
    force the affliction away

but
    establish the cognition and practice
    under which it can no longer reproduce itself
```

The dialectic is positive because the counteragent is not an empty negation. It is a determinate realization of truth.

---

# 12. `Vadhādi-paryavasthānam`

The compound is difficult and should remain textually open.

```text
vadha
    killing
    injury
    striking down

ādi
    beginning with

paryavasthāna
    fixation
    enveloping manifestation
    active afflictive occupation
```

A cautious rendering is:

```text
a manifest afflictive fixation beginning with injury or killing
```

The verse states that it is:

```text
bhāvanayā heyam
    to be abandoned through cultivation
```

This suggests that even when a doctrinal error has been corrected, violent or harmful reactive patterns can remain as cultivated tendencies requiring sustained counter-cultivation.

The Bhāṣya must clarify:

```text
which manifestations are included under vadhādi
why they are classed as paryavasthāna
why cultivation alone is specified
how this clause relates to the seven and nine conceits
```

---

# 13. From view to measurement to action

Verses 5.7–5.10 now form a strong sequence:

```text
false view
    determines the field incorrectly

inversion
    predicates the opposite determination

conceit
    measures the self within that inverted field

manifest fixation
    carries the malformed relation into action
```

Or:

```text
ontology
    → predication
    → self-relation
    → conduct
```

This is why the chapter is a dialectical treasury. It traces affliction as an articulated process rather than treating it as a merely private feeling.

---

# 14. Restricted Organon reading

Without prematurely importing the verse into Organon architecture, its general logical lesson is clear:

```text
measurement presupposes
    a subject
    a comparator
    a standard
    a judgment rule
```

A corrupt standard can generate a formally valid but materially false comparison.

```text
correct computation
    under a false measure
    = afflicted determination
```

The remedy therefore requires more than changing an output. It requires correcting the principle of measurement and then retraining its recurrent application.

This remains an interpretive bridge, not yet an Organon implementation claim.

---

# 15. Kārikā–Bhāṣya tension

The kārikā independently establishes:

```text
seven conceits
ninefold differentiation from three
seeing and cultivation as modes of exhaustion
cultivation as the remedy for a further manifest fixation
```

But it does not independently provide:

```text
the names and exact definitions of all seven
how the nine are generated from the three
which conceits are seeing-abandoned
which are cultivation-abandoned
the precise meaning of vadhādi-paryavasthāna
```

This is a paradigmatic instance of the Kośa's encyclopedic compression.

```text
Kārikā
    supplies the classificatory skeleton

Bhāṣya
    supplies the joints, derivations, disputes, and examples
```

The first-pass study therefore preserves the verse as foundational while marking exactly where doctrinal completion is still required.

---

# 16. Structural summary

```text
māna
    afflicted measurement

seven named forms
    qualitative differentiation

three comparative positions
    superior
    equal
    inferior

ninefold production
    combinatorial differentiation

seeing
    removes view-dependent conceit

cultivation
    removes residual disposition
    and manifest harmful fixation
```

The central result is:

```text
false self-measurement
    is not removed by one uniform negation

its principle must be seen through
and its recurrence must be cultivated away
```
