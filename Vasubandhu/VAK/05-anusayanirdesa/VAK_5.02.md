# VAK_5.02 — Six Spoken as Seven

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
षड्रागभेदात् सप्तोक्ताः भवरागो द्विधातुजः ।
अन्तर्मुखत्वात् तन्मोक्षसंज्ञाव्यावृत्तये कृतः ॥ ५.२ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
ṣaḍrāgabhedāt saptoktāḥ bhavarāgo dvidhātujaḥ |
antarmukhatvāt tanmokṣasaṃjñāvyāvṛttaye kṛtaḥ || 5.2 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
ṣaḍrāgabhedātsaptoktāḥ bhavarāgo dvidhātujaḥ /
antarmukhatvāttanmokṣasaṃjñāvyāvṛttaye kṛtaḥ // VAkK_5.2 //
```

The first pāda resumes the six anuśayas named in VAK_5.01:

```text
six
    → spoken as seven

because rāga is divided
```

The verse then identifies one side of that division:

```text
bhavarāga
    attachment to existence
    belonging to two realms
```

The final pāda gives a reason for marking this attachment separately, but its compressed syntax requires caution. The independent kārikā pass can establish the grammatical structure and doctrinal direction without pretending that the full rationale is self-evident apart from the Bhāṣya.

## 3. Padaccheda

```text
ṣaṭ |
rāga-bhedāt |
sapta |
uktāḥ |

bhava-rāgaḥ |
dvi-dhātu-jaḥ |

antar-mukhatvāt |
tat-mokṣa-saṃjñā-vyāvṛttaye |

```

Possible syntactical reconstruction:

```text
ṣaṭ [anuśayāḥ]
rāgasya bhedāt
sapta uktāḥ.

bhavarāgaḥ
dvidhātujaḥ [asti].

antarmukhatvāt
[tasya pṛthagvyavasthā]
tanmokṣasaṃjñāvyāvṛttaye
kṛtā.
```

The exact referent of `kṛtaḥ` is compressed. It most naturally points to the doctrinal making or establishment of `bhavarāga` as a distinct category.

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `ṣaṭ` | numeral | six |
| `rāga-bhedāt` | ablative singular compound | because of the division of attachment |
| `sapta` | numeral | seven |
| `uktāḥ` | past passive participle, nominative masculine plural | spoken, stated, taught |
| `bhava-rāgaḥ` | nominative masculine singular | attachment to existence |
| `dvi-dhātu-jaḥ` | nominative masculine singular compound | arisen in, or belonging to, two domains |
| `antar-mukhatvāt` | ablative singular abstract noun | because of inward-directedness |
| `tat-mokṣa-saṃjñā-vyāvṛttaye` | dative singular compound | for excluding the conception of that as liberation |
| `kṛtaḥ` | past passive participle, nominative masculine singular | made, established, set forth |

The grammatical movement is:

```text
enumerative correction
    six become seven

causal explanation
    because rāga is divided

specific classification
    bhavarāga belongs to two domains

purpose clause
    the distinction is established
    to block a mistaken conception
```

---

## 5. Literal translation

> Because attachment is divided, the six are stated as seven. Attachment to existence belongs to two domains. Because it is inward-directed, [this distinction] is made in order to exclude the conception of that as liberation.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> The six underlying dispositions are counted as seven because attachment must be divided into distinct forms. One of these is attachment to existence, which operates in two domains beyond the ordinary field of sensual attachment. Precisely because this attachment turns inward toward refined modes of existence and may therefore resemble detachment from external objects, it must be distinguished explicitly so that such inwardness is not mistaken for liberation.

This translation preserves the verse’s doctrinal direction while marking where the Bhāṣya must later supply the full argumentative mediation.

---

## 7. From six to seven

VAK_5.01 gave the basic sixfold list:

```text
rāga
pratigha
māna
avidyā
dṛṣṭi
vicikitsā
```

VAK_5.02 immediately modifies the count:

```text
rāga
    → divided

six anuśayas
    → seven anuśayas
```

The verse therefore shows that the initial list is not yet the final classificatory form.

The count changes because one apparently simple genus contains a doctrinally decisive internal difference.

```text
one name
    rāga

contains
    more than one operative mode
```

This is the first sign that the chapter will not merely enumerate afflictions. It will determine their exact range, field, and mode of operation.

---

## 8. `Rāga-bheda`

```text
rāga
    attachment
    attraction
    adhesive appropriation

bheda
    division
    distinction
    differentiation
```

The division is not introduced for numerical convenience.

It indicates that attachment changes its structure according to its object and domain.

```text
attachment to sensuous objects
    is not identical with

attachment to refined existence
```

Both bind, but they bind through different appearances.

```text
external attraction
    obvious appropriation

refined inward attachment
    apparent transcendence of coarse appropriation
```

The latter is more difficult to identify because it can present itself as spiritual advancement.

---

## 9. `Bhavarāga`

```text
bhava
    existence
    becoming
    conditioned mode of being

rāga
    attachment
```

`Bhavarāga` is not merely a wish to remain alive in an ordinary biological sense.

It is attachment to a mode of conditioned existence as such.

The verse locates it in two domains:

```text
rūpadhātu
ārūpyadhātu
```

Thus:

```text
bhavarāga
    = attachment associated with the form and formless domains
```

This gives the initial sevenfold structure:

```text
kāmarāga
bhavarāga
pratigha
māna
avidyā
dṛṣṭi
vicikitsā
```

The kārikā itself presupposes this division more than it explains it. That is exactly the point at which the later Bhāṣya pass must recover the formal rationale in full.

---

## 10. `Dvidhātuja`

```text
dvi
    two

dhātu
    domain

ja
    born from
    arising in
    belonging to
```

The compound does not mean that attachment is produced by two material elements.

Here `dhātu` retains the project’s canonical sense:

```text
dhātu
    = domain
```

So `dvidhātuja` means that bhavarāga belongs to, or arises within, two domains.

These are the two higher domains of conditioned existence:

```text
form-domain
formless-domain
```

The classification is therefore topological as well as psychological.

```text
an anuśaya
    is determined partly by
    the domain in which it operates
```

---

## 11. `Antarmukhatva`

```text
antar
    inward

mukha
    facing
    oriented

tva
    state or quality
```

`Antarmukhatva` names inward-directedness.

This is a subtle determination because inwardness can appear superior to outward desire.

```text
withdrawal from coarse external objects
    may look like
    freedom from attachment
```

But the verse warns that inward orientation is not yet liberation.

An attachment may become more refined without ceasing to be attachment.

```text
coarse object is abandoned
    but

conditioned existence itself
    remains appropriated
```

Thus the higher domains do not automatically transcend rāga. They can become its more interior object.

---

## 12. Excluding a false conception of liberation

The compound:

```text
tat-mokṣa-saṃjñā-vyāvṛtti
```

may be unfolded as:

```text
tat
    that

mokṣa-saṃjñā
    conception or designation of liberation

vyāvṛtti
    exclusion
    removal
    blocking
```

The purpose of the distinction is therefore corrective.

```text
refined inward existence
    must not be conceived as
    liberation
```

This is a central path-discrimination principle:

```text
subtle bondage
    can resemble
    release
```

The classification exists to prevent that confusion.

---

## 13. Doctrinal determination

The verse makes four determinations:

```text
1. The sixfold list is not numerically final.

2. Rāga contains a doctrinally necessary division.

3. Bhavarāga operates in two higher domains.

4. Its inward character creates a danger of mistaking refined existence for liberation.
```

The chapter therefore moves immediately from simple enumeration to discriminatory classification.

```text
count
    depends upon
    real functional difference
```

---

## 14. Logical determination

VAK_5.02 performs a division of a genus.

```text
rāga
    universal determination

is divided according to
    domain and mode of orientation
```

The resulting species are not merely externally juxtaposed.

They express two forms of the same binding relation:

```text
attachment to desired objects
attachment to conditioned existence
```

The second form negates the first only incompletely.

```text
external desire is reduced
    but

attachment is preserved inwardly
```

This gives a determinate appearance of liberation that is not liberation.

```text
relative inwardness
    ≠
absolute release
```

The verse is therefore an exercise in preventing a false identification based on superficial resemblance.

---

## 15. Light Organon interpretation

For the Agent, this distinction warns against classifying every reduction of external dependence as genuine release.

```text
external object-dependence decreases
    but

attachment to a refined state persists
```

A system may become more inward, abstract, or self-contained while still preserving the same underlying appropriation.

The Feature determination must therefore distinguish:

```text
change of object
    from

change of underlying relation
```

That is the practical force of `rāgabheda`.

The Agent must not infer:

```text
less externality
    therefore
    freedom
```

It must ask whether the binding structure itself has been removed.

---

## 16. Kārikā–Bhāṣya tension

The kārikā independently establishes:

```text
six become seven
bhavarāga belongs to two domains
its inwardness motivates a corrective distinction
```

But it leaves several questions compressed:

```text
What exactly are the two species of rāga?
Why are the two higher domains grouped together?
How precisely does inwardness generate a false conception of liberation?
What is the exact referent of kṛtaḥ?
```

These questions should remain explicitly open for the VAKB pass.

The first-pass fiction of independence is preserved by determining everything the verse itself supports, while refusing to fabricate the reflective argument that belongs to the Bhāṣya.
