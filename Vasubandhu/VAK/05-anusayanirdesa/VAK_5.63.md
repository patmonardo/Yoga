# VAK 5.63

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

सकृत् क्षयो विसंयोगलाभस्तेषां पुनः पुनः ।
प्रतिपक्षोदयफलप्राप्तीन्द्रियविवृद्धिषु ॥ ५.६३ ॥

### IAST

sakṛt kṣayo visaṃyoga-lābhas teṣāṃ punaḥ punaḥ |
pratipakṣodaya-phala-prāptīndriya-vivṛddhiṣu || 5.63 ||

## Padaccheda

sakṛt | kṣayaḥ | visaṃyoga-lābhaḥ | teṣām | punaḥ punaḥ | pratipakṣa-udaya | phala-prāpti | indriya-vivṛddhiṣu

## Grammar

- **sakṛt** — indeclinable adverb: once, on one occasion.
- **kṣayaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: destruction, exhaustion, cessation.
- **visaṃyoga-lābhaḥ** — nominative singular masculine compound: acquisition of disconnection or separation.
- **teṣām** — genitive plural: of those; its exact referent depends on the preceding discussion and must be fixed from the Bhāṣya.
- **punaḥ punaḥ** — repeated adverbial expression: again and again, repeatedly.
- **pratipakṣa-udaya** — arising of the counteragent.
- **phala-prāpti** — attainment of a fruit.
- **indriya-vivṛddhiṣu** — locative plural: upon or in the increases, developments, or refinements of the faculties.

The final compound is best read as a locative series giving the occasions on which **visaṃyoga-lābha** is obtained repeatedly:

```text
pratipakṣodaya
phala-prāpti
indriya-vivṛddhi
```

## Literal Translation

Destruction occurs once; but the acquisition of disconnection from those occurs repeatedly, upon the arising of a counteragent, the attainment of a fruit, and the development of the faculties.

## Philosophical Translation

The affliction itself is destroyed only once. Yet the attained state of separation from it can be newly acquired or renewed on successive occasions: when a counteragent arises, when a path-fruit is attained, and when the spiritual faculties are further developed.

## Technical Vocabulary

### kṣaya

**Kṣaya** is the destruction or exhaustion of the relevant affliction. The verse marks it as **sakṛt**, occurring once.

```text
kṣaya
    once
```

The secure point is numerical and structural: the same affliction is not destroyed repeatedly after it has already been destroyed.

### visaṃyoga

**Visaṃyoga** is disconnection, separation, or dissociation from the affliction.

It must not be collapsed into **kṣaya**:

```text
kṣaya
    destruction of the affliction

visaṃyoga
    separation from the affliction
```

### lābha

**Lābha** is acquisition or attainment. The compound **visaṃyoga-lābha** therefore denotes the acquisition of the state of disconnection.

The verse says that this acquisition may occur **punaḥ punaḥ**, repeatedly.

### pratipakṣodaya

The arising of a counteragent is one occasion for a renewed acquisition of disconnection.

This connects 5.63 directly with the doctrine of **pratipakṣa** introduced in 5.60–5.61.

### phala-prāpti

The attainment of a fruit constitutes another occasion. The kārikā does not itself enumerate the fruits here; that classification belongs to the Bhāṣya and the larger path doctrine.

### indriya-vivṛddhi

The development or refinement of the faculties is the third named occasion.

The exact faculties and the stages of their development require Bhāṣya confirmation.

## Analysis

The verse establishes a sharp distinction between two events:

```text
kṣaya
    once

visaṃyoga-lābha
    repeatedly
```

This prevents the repeated acquisition of liberation from being mistaken for repeated destruction of the same affliction.

The affliction is destroyed once. What can recur is the acquisition of a new or strengthened relation of disconnection.

The verse then specifies three occasions for that repeated acquisition:

```text
arising of a counteragent
attainment of a fruit
development of the faculties
```

The kārikā therefore distinguishes:

```text
objective change
    destruction of the affliction

attainment change
    acquisition of disconnection
```

The first concerns what is destroyed. The second concerns what is acquired by the practitioner.

## Logical Determination

The principal determination is:

```text
destruction
    is non-repeatable with respect to the same affliction

acquisition of disconnection
    is repeatable under new path conditions
```

This yields a precise distinction among:

```text
cessation
attainment
renewed qualification
```

The destruction of an affliction and the subject's successive acquisitions of freedom from it are not the same determination.

A thing may cease once, while the attained relation to that cessation is repeatedly re-established under newly developed conditions.

## Organon Note

A restrained logical formulation is:

```text
negation of the defective determination
    occurs once

appropriation of the achieved freedom
    may recur at higher stages
```

The verse thus distinguishes the completed negation of an affliction from the repeated development of the subject's attained relation to that negation.

This is not yet a claim that freedom itself is lost and regained. It is a distinction between:

```text
what has been destroyed
and
what is newly acquired at successive stages
```

## Bhāṣya Questions

1. What is the exact referent of **teṣām**?
2. Does **visaṃyoga-lābha** denote repeated acquisition of distinct disconnections, repeated acquisition of the same disconnection under different path states, or repeated acquisition of associated non-material dharmas?
3. Which counteragent-arising events are intended?
4. Which fruits are included under **phala-prāpti**?
5. Which faculties develop under **indriya-vivṛddhi**, and how does their development generate a fresh acquisition of disconnection?
6. How is this doctrine coordinated with the Sarvāstivāda theory of **prāpti** and **aprāpti**?

## Core Determination

```text
The affliction is destroyed once.
The acquisition of separation from it
can occur repeatedly
as the path, its fruits,
and the faculties develop.
```
