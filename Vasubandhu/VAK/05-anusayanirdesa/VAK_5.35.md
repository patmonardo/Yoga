# VAK 5.35

## Sanskrit

कामे सपर्यवस्थानाः क्लेशाः कामास्रवो विना ।
मोहेनानुशया एव रूपारूप्ये भवास्रवः ॥ ५.३५ ॥

## IAST

kāme saparyavasthānāḥ kleśāḥ kāmāsravo vinā |
mohenānuśayā eva rūpārūpye bhavāsravaḥ || 5.35 ||

## Padaccheda

kāme | sa-paryavasthānāḥ | kleśāḥ | kāma-āsravaḥ | vinā | mohena | anuśayāḥ | eva | rūpa-arūpye | bhava-āsravaḥ |

## Grammar

- **kāme** — locative singular: “in the desire-domain.”
- **sa-paryavasthānāḥ kleśāḥ** — nominative plural: “the afflictions together with the envelopments / secondary afflictive eruptions.”
- **kāmāsravaḥ** — nominative singular: “the influx of desire.”
- **vinā mohena** — “apart from delusion”; *vinā* governs the instrumental.
- **anuśayāḥ eva** — “the latent tendencies alone.”
- **rūpārūpye** — locative dual: “in the form and formless domains.”
- **bhavāsravaḥ** — nominative singular: “the influx of existence.”

## Literal Translation

In the desire-domain, the afflictions together with the envelopments, excluding delusion, constitute the influx of desire. In the form and formless domains, the latent tendencies alone, excluding delusion, constitute the influx of existence.

## Philosophical Translation

The stream called desire-influx is formed by the active afflictive field of the desire-domain, including its secondary eruptions, but with ignorance reserved for separate treatment. In the higher domains, the stream called existence-influx consists only of the corresponding latent tendencies, again apart from ignorance.

## Analysis

Verse 5.34 described the complete causal conditions for a manifest affliction. Verse 5.35 now widens the unit of analysis from an individual event to a **stream**: an **āsrava**.

An āsrava is not merely one kleśa. It is an organized continuity through which afflictive powers sustain a domain of experience.

The verse distinguishes two such currents:

1. **kāmāsrava** — the influx of desire;
2. **bhavāsrava** — the influx of continued existence.

In the desire-domain, the current includes both the principal afflictions and the **paryavasthānas**, their more manifest or enveloping expressions. This is a comparatively outward, eruptive field.

In the form and formless domains, the current is subtler. Only the latent tendencies are counted here. The higher-domain afflictions do not require the same coarse paryavasthāna structure characteristic of desire-domain existence.

Ignorance (*moha / avidyā*) is excluded from both classifications because the following verse will identify it as a distinct influx. This indicates that ignorance does not merely belong beside the other afflictions. It operates as their more universal ground.

## Technical Vocabulary

- **āsrava** — influx, outflow, contaminating current; a sustained stream through which affliction maintains conditioned existence.
- **kāmāsrava** — the influx associated with the desire-domain.
- **bhavāsrava** — the influx associated with existence in the form and formless domains.
- **paryavasthāna** — an afflictive enveloping, eruption, or manifest domination of mind.
- **anuśaya** — latent tendency; an afflictive power capable of reactivation.
- **moha / avidyā** — delusion or ignorance, here withheld for separate classification.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse distinguishes an affliction from the stream it helps constitute.

```text
kleśa
    = determinate afflictive event or power

āsrava
    = organized continuity of afflictive powers
      sustaining a mode of existence
```

Thus:

```text
hasAffliction
    ≠
participatesInAfflictiveStream
```

A domain is not reproduced by one isolated event. It persists through recurrent and mutually supporting powers.

The difference between the desire-domain and the higher domains is also operational:

```text
desire-domain influx
    = latent tendency + manifest enveloping activity

higher-domain influx
    = subtler latent continuity
```

## Logical Determination

The logical unit is no longer a single predicate attached to a subject. It is a **continuity relation** that gathers many events into one operative current.

```text
Disposition
    + repeated manifestation
    + domain-specific reinforcement
        → Stream
```

This gives the Dialectical Copula a trans-event form:

```text
CopulaEvent
    generates a determination

CopulaStream
    preserves and reproduces
    a field of determinations
```

An āsrava is therefore a practical universal: not an abstract class imposed from outside, but a universal existing through the recurrence of its instances.

## Bhāṣya Questions

1. How precisely does the Bhāṣya distinguish *paryavasthāna* from *kleśa* and *anuśaya* here?
2. Does *anuśayā eva* exclude all manifest higher-domain afflictions, or only the separate paryavasthāna class?
3. Why is ignorance structurally capable of forming a separate āsrava rather than remaining within the two domain-based influxes?
4. Does Vasubandhu accept this classification straightforwardly, or prepare a critique of its numerical and conceptual divisions?
