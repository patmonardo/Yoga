# VAK_5.08 — Causal Fixation and the View of Suffering

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
ईश्वरादिषु नित्यात्मविपर्यासात् प्रवर्तते ।
कारणाभिनिवेशोऽतो दुःखदृग्घेय एव सः ॥ ५.८ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
īśvarādiṣu nityātma-viparyāsāt pravartate |
kāraṇābhiniveśo 'to duḥkha-dṛg-gheya eva saḥ || 5.8 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
īśvarādiṣu nityātmaviparyāsāt pravartate /
kāraṇābhiniveśo 'to dūḥkhadṛggheya eva saḥ // VAkK_5.8 //
```

The form `dūḥkha` is normalized here to `duḥkha`. The compound `duḥkha-dṛg-gheya` is analyzed as “to be abandoned by seeing suffering.”

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `īśvarādiṣu` | locative masculine plural | in Īśvara and similar supposed causes |
| `nitya-ātma-viparyāsāt` | ablative singular compound | from the inversion concerning a permanent self |
| `pravartate` | 3rd person singular, present middle | arises, proceeds, becomes active |
| `kāraṇa-abhiniveśaḥ` | nominative masculine singular | fixation upon a cause |
| `ataḥ` | ablative adverb | therefore, from this |
| `duḥkha-dṛg-gheyaḥ` | gerundive compound, nominative masculine singular | to be abandoned by seeing suffering |
| `eva` | emphatic particle | precisely, only |
| `saḥ` | nominative masculine singular pronoun | that |

`abhiniveśa` here is not a neutral thesis. It indicates a forceful adherence or fixation.

```text
abhi-ni-viś
    to enter into
    to settle firmly upon
    to adhere intensely
```

---

## 5. Literal translation

> Fixation upon a cause such as Īśvara arises from the inversion concerning a permanent self. Therefore that [fixation] is to be abandoned precisely by seeing suffering.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> The determination of an eternal personal cause—Īśvara or an analogous first agent—does not arise independently. It proceeds from a prior inversion in which permanence and selfhood are projected upon conditioned existence. Because this causal fixation is rooted in that misconstrual of the existent aggregate-complex, it is removed through the direct seeing of suffering rather than through an abstract debate about cosmological causation.

The movement is:

```text
conditioned existence
    ↓ misconstrued
permanent self
    ↓ projected outward
eternal personal cause
    ↓ fixed as explanation
kāraṇābhiniveśa
```

The corrective movement is:

```text
seeing suffering
    → seeing conditioned formation as conditioned
    → dissolving the permanent-self projection
    → dissolving the supposed eternal cause
```

---

# 7. `Nityātma-viparyāsa`

```text
nitya
    permanent

ātman
    self

viparyāsa
    inversion
    reversal
    taking what is otherwise to be what it is not
```

The verse does not describe a simple absence of information. It describes a positive reversal:

```text
impermanent
    taken as permanent

non-self
    taken as self
```

This inversion then becomes productive. It generates a further explanatory construction.

```text
false determination of the subject
    → false determination of the cause
```

The mistake is therefore dialectical in structure: one distorted determination produces another.

---

# 8. `Īśvarādiṣu`

The plural expression is significant:

```text
īśvara-ādiṣu
    in Īśvara and similar cases
```

The verse is not limited to one proper name. It identifies a class of explanatory posits:

```text
an eternal lord
an eternal self
an unconditioned personal maker
an absolute agent standing behind conditioned existence
```

The shared structure is more important than the individual doctrine:

```text
conditioned field
    explained by
permanent personal cause
```

The Bhāṣya must determine the exact opponents and examples intended by `ādi`.

---

# 9. `Kāraṇābhiniveśa`

```text
kāraṇa
    cause

abhiniveśa
    fixation
    entrenched adherence
```

The problem is not causal inquiry as such. Abhidharma is itself an extensive science of causes and conditions.

The defective form is:

```text
cause
    falsely absolutized

conditional relation
    replaced by
permanent agent
```

Thus:

```text
causal analysis
    is not rejected

causal hypostatization
    is rejected
```

This is a particularly important distinction for the Kośa as a Treasury of scientific method.

---

# 10. Why Seeing Suffering?

The concluding determination is:

```text
duḥkha-dṛg-gheya
    abandoned by seeing suffering
```

This initially appears surprising. A causal theory might seem to belong under the truth of origination.

But the kārikā locates the root elsewhere:

```text
not first
    false theory of origination

but first
    false determination of conditioned existence itself
```

The supposed eternal cause arises because the field of suffering has already been misread as containing or requiring a permanent self.

Hence the counter-operation is:

```text
see the conditioned field correctly
    before
correcting the derived causal thesis
```

The Bhāṣya should clarify why this view is classified exclusively under abandonment by seeing suffering.

---

# 11. Positive Dialectic

This verse makes the positive dialectical structure especially clear.

```text
first determination
    nityātman
    permanent self

second determination
    īśvara-kāraṇa
    eternal personal cause

counter-determination
    duḥkha-darśana
    seeing conditioned existence as suffering

result
    abandonment of causal fixation
```

The correction does not merely say:

```text
there is no Īśvara
```

It reconstructs the genesis of the view:

```text
why the view arises
what prior inversion sustains it
which cognition removes it
```

That is why this chapter is functioning as a dialectical treasury rather than a list of dogmatic denials.

---

# 12. Relation to VAK_5.07

VAK_5.07 defined `śīlavrata-parāmarśa` through fixation upon a non-cause as cause and a non-path as path.

VAK_5.08 now isolates one important causal fixation:

```text
permanent self inversion
    → eternal personal cause
```

It therefore moves from definition to genesis:

```text
5.07
    What is the malformed determination?

5.08
    From what prior inversion does it arise?
```

---

# 13. Kārikā–Bhāṣya Tension

The kārikā independently establishes four secure points:

```text
1. fixation upon causes such as Īśvara occurs
2. it arises from permanent-self inversion
3. it belongs to a determinate abandonment class
4. it is abandoned by seeing suffering
```

The Bhāṣya remains necessary to determine:

```text
which doctrines are included under īśvarādi
whether the target is śīlavrata-parāmarśa specifically
why duḥkha-darśana alone is adequate
how the causal fixation is generated in detail
```

The foundational reading therefore remains independent but open.

---

# 14. Restrained Organon Reading

The verse provides a general diagnostic schema:

```text
misdetermined subject-model
    → misdetermined causal model
    → entrenched explanatory feature
```

A scientific correction cannot begin only at the final proposition. It must identify the prior model deformation that generates it.

```text
false ontology
    produces
false causality
```

This belongs first to the Yoga-based Kośa study. A later Organon report may translate it into a general account of model error and causal hypostatization.

# 16. Final Determination

```text
permanent-self inversion
    generates
fixation upon an eternal cause

seeing suffering
    removes
both the derived causal fixation
and its underlying misconstrual
```

The verse is therefore not an isolated polemic against a creator doctrine. It is a compact genetic critique of how an absolute causal agent is produced from a prior inversion of the conditioned field.
