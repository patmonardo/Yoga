# VAK 5.43

## Sanskrit

पञ्चधावरभागीयं द्वाभ्यां कामानतिक्रमः ।
त्रिभिस्तु पुनरावृत्तिर्मुखमूलग्रहात्त्रयम् ॥ ५.४३ ॥

## IAST

pañcadhāvarabhāgīyaṃ dvābhyāṃ kāmānatikramaḥ /
tribhis tu punarāvṛttir mukha-mūla-grahāt trayam // 5.43 //

## Padaccheda

pañcadhā | avara-bhāgīyam | dvābhyām | kāma-anatikramaḥ | tribhiḥ | tu | punaḥ-āvṛttiḥ | mukha-mūla-grahāt | trayam

## Grammar

- **pañcadhā** — indeclinable, “fivefold,” “in five ways.”
- **avara-bhāgīyam** — nominative/accusative singular neuter, “belonging to the lower portion,” “connected with the lower region”; here the collective designation for the five lower fetters.
- **dvābhyām** — instrumental dual, “by the two.” These are desire and resistance, the two affective bonds specifically fastening one to the desire-domain.
- **kāma-anatikramaḥ** — nominative singular masculine compound, “non-transcendence of desire,” more precisely non-passage beyond the desire-domain.
- **tribhiḥ** — instrumental plural, “by the three.”
- **tu** — “but,” distinguishing the function of the remaining three fetters from that of the first two.
- **punar-āvṛttiḥ** — nominative singular feminine, “return again,” “reversion,” “renewed coming back.”
- **mukha-mūla-grahāt** — ablative singular, “because of grasping the entrance and the root.” The compound gives the functional ground for treating the three as one group.
- **trayam** — nominative/accusative singular neuter, “the triad,” “the threefold group.”

## Literal Translation

The lower-part group is fivefold. Through two there is non-transcendence of desire; through three, however, there is return again. The triad [is so grouped] because it grasps the entrance and the root.

## Philosophical Translation

The fetters belonging to the lower sphere form a fivefold structure. Two directly prevent passage beyond the desire-domain; the remaining three reproduce the conditions of return. These three constitute one functional triad because they seize both the entrance into the path and the root from which bondage is regenerated.

## Structural Analysis

VAK 5.41 established that the anuśayas are reclassified according to five forms beginning with **saṃyojana**. VAK 5.42 then showed why jealousy and avarice count separately: each is independently unwholesome and exercises an autonomous binding operation.

VAK 5.43 now presents the first major saṃyojana-system:

```text
five lower fetters
    =
2 + 3
```

The division is functional rather than merely numerical:

```text
two affective fetters
    → prevent transcendence of the desire-domain

three cognitive-normative fetters
    → generate renewed return
```

The standard five are:

```text
satkāya-dṛṣṭi
śīla-vrata-parāmarśa
vicikitsā
kāma-rāga
pratigha
```

The two are:

```text
kāma-rāga
pratigha
```

They fasten the stream directly to the desire-domain through attraction and resistance.

The three are:

```text
satkāya-dṛṣṭi
śīla-vrata-parāmarśa
vicikitsā
```

They do not merely attach the practitioner to particular desirable objects. They deform the very orientation by which a path is entered, recognized, and maintained.

## Technical Vocabulary

### Avara-bhāgīya

Literally, “belonging to the lower portion.” The term indicates those fetters whose operation retains the stream within the lower field, especially the desire-domain.

This should not be flattened into a merely spatial image. “Lower” is a path-relative determination:

```text
that which obstructs ascent
that which produces return
```

### Anatikrama

“Non-transcendence,” “failure to pass beyond.” The two affective fetters do not simply occur within the desire-domain; they actively prevent transition beyond it.

### Punarāvṛtti

“Return again.” The term indicates more than repetition of a mental event. It names the reconstitution of the lower existential position after an apparent or partial movement beyond it.

### Mukha

“Entrance,” “opening,” “mouth.” In the path-context it may indicate the point of entry into a liberating course, or the opening through which bondage gains access.

### Mūla

“Root.” The root is the generative ground from which the bound structure can arise again.

### Graha

“Grasping,” “seizing,” “taking hold.” The three fetters operate by taking possession of both orientation and ground.

## Doctrinal Determination

The five lower fetters are not homogeneous.

Their unity contains a functional opposition:

```text
affective fixation
    versus
cognitive-path distortion
```

The two affective fetters produce:

```text
non-departure
```

The three cognitive-normative fetters produce:

```text
renewed return
```

This distinction is crucial. A being may fail to transcend because it remains positively attached to the desire-domain. But even where gross attachment is weakened, distorted view, misplaced discipline, and doubt can restore the entire lower structure.

Thus bondage has at least two modes:

```text
retention
regeneration
```

The first holds the stream where it is. The second reconstructs what had begun to be left behind.

## Logical Determination

VAK 5.43 gives a determinate architecture of obstruction:

```text
Obstruction
    ├── Retentive Obstruction
    └── Recursive Obstruction
```

Retentive obstruction blocks transition:

```text
state A
    ↛
state B
```

Recursive obstruction produces return:

```text
state A
    → partial transition
    → restoration of A
```

This is stronger than a simple list of five defects. The verse distinguishes two different logical failures:

1. inability to negate the present domain,
2. inability to preserve the negation once initiated.

The threefold group is especially important because it seizes both:

```text
mukha
    = the opening of transformation

mūla
    = the generative basis of recurrence
```

Hence the triad governs both entry and reproduction.

## Organon Note

For the Kosa Speculative Reason work, this verse suggests a decisive distinction between two forms of conflict with the Path:

```text
adhesion to a determinate field
    ≠
misdetermination of the transition itself
```

The first belongs to attraction and resistance. The second belongs to a corruption of discrimination:

```text
wrong determination of the self-field
wrong determination of the means
suspension before the truth
```

This clarifies the relation between Prajñā and Dharma without making Pure Prajñā one competing dharma among others.

The conflict occurs among discriminated determinations and their path-functions. Pure Prajñā is not one side within that field; rather, the lower fetters distort the finite operation by which the field is discriminated and traversed.

A speculative reconstruction may therefore distinguish:

```text
Root Discriminator
    ↓
path-discrimination
    ↓
possible distortion of entry, means, and commitment
```

The triad of views and doubt does not oppose Pure Prajñā directly. It captures the finite mouth and root through which discriminative knowing becomes effective in a conditioned stream.

## Bhāṣya Questions

1. Which two fetters does the Bhāṣya explicitly identify under **dvābhyām**?
2. How does Vasubandhu explain **kāmānatikramaḥ**: non-transcendence of the desire-domain, non-attainment of the non-returner state, or both?
3. What exactly do **mukha** and **mūla** designate in the commentary?
4. Does **mukha** refer to entry into the desire-domain, entry into wrong practice, or the entrance to liberation that the three obstruct?
5. Does **mūla** refer specifically to satkāya-dṛṣṭi, to the root of all lower bondage, or to the causal basis of renewed birth?
6. Are the three grouped because each grasps both entrance and root, or because different members grasp different moments within one structure?
7. How does this fivefold lower-fetter system relate to the earlier division into views, doubt, desire, and resistance?
