# VAK_5.03 — The Five Views and the Ten Anuśayas

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
दृष्टयः पञ्च सत्कायमिथ्यान्तग्रहदृष्टयः ।
दृष्टिशीलव्रतपरामर्शाविति पुनर्दश ॥ ५.३ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
dṛṣṭayaḥ pañca satkāya-mithyā-antagraha-dṛṣṭayaḥ |
dṛṣṭi-śīlavrata-parāmarśāv iti punar daśa || 5.3 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
dṛṣṭayaḥ pañca satkāyamithyāntagrahadṛṣṭayaḥ /
dṛṣṭiśīlavrataparāmarśāviti punardaśaḥ // VAkK_5.3 //
```

The continuous compounds must be analytically separated. The verse enumerates five forms of `dṛṣṭi` and then recalculates the anuśayas as ten.

---

## 3. Padaccheda

```text
dṛṣṭayaḥ |
pañca |
satkāya-dṛṣṭiḥ |
mithyā-dṛṣṭiḥ |
antagraha-dṛṣṭiḥ |
dṛṣṭi-parāmarśaḥ |
śīlavrata-parāmarśaḥ |
iti |
punaḥ |
daśa
```

The five views are:

```text
1. satkāya-dṛṣṭi
2. mithyā-dṛṣṭi
3. antagraha-dṛṣṭi
4. dṛṣṭi-parāmarśa
5. śīlavrata-parāmarśa
```

The kārikā compresses the final two as a dual:

```text
dṛṣṭi-śīlavrata-parāmarśau
```

that is:

```text
dṛṣṭi-parāmarśaḥ
and
śīlavrata-parāmarśaḥ
```

---

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `dṛṣṭayaḥ` | nominative feminine plural | views |
| `pañca` | indeclinable numeral | five |
| `satkāya-dṛṣṭayaḥ` | compound within the enumeration | views concerning the existing body or personal aggregate-complex |
| `mithyā-dṛṣṭayaḥ` | compound within the enumeration | false views |
| `antagraha-dṛṣṭayaḥ` | compound within the enumeration | views grasping an extreme |
| `dṛṣṭi-parāmarśau` | first member of a dual compound | adherence to or overvaluation of views |
| `śīlavrata-parāmarśau` | second member of the dual | adherence to or overvaluation of discipline and observance |
| `iti` | quotative particle | thus |
| `punaḥ` / `punar` | adverb | again, in turn |
| `daśa` | numeral | ten |

The transmitted form `daśaḥ` is metrically and syntactically awkward if treated as an ordinary nominative singular adjective. The evident force is numerical:

```text
iti punaḥ daśa
    thus, again, [they are] ten
```

The Bhāṣya pass should verify the preferred reading and any editorial discussion.

---

## 5. Literal translation

> The views are five: the view of the existing body, false view, the view grasping an extreme, adherence to views, and adherence to discipline and observances. Thus, again, [the anuśayas are] ten.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> The single anuśaya called `view` is internally differentiated into five determinate modes: appropriation of the aggregate-complex as self or belonging to self; false denial or distortion; fixation upon an extreme; attachment to a view precisely as superior or authoritative; and attachment to disciplines or observances as intrinsically sufficient. Once `dṛṣṭi` is unfolded into these five species, the previously stated six anuśayas are recalculated as ten.

The verse therefore performs a second internal differentiation:

```text
5.1
    six anuśayas

5.2
    rāga divided
        six → seven

5.3
    dṛṣṭi divided into five
        seven → ten
```

The arithmetic is:

```text
seven
    minus the undivided member dṛṣṭi
    plus its five species
    = eleven
```

But the chapter's conventional count at this point is stated as ten. This means the exact relation between the sixfold, sevenfold, and tenfold enumerations must be handled carefully rather than reconstructed casually from arithmetic alone. The kārikā clearly states the five views and the renewed count of ten; the Bhāṣya must supply the precise classificatory reconciliation.

This is a deliberate point of openness for the later VAKB pass.

---

# 7. `Dṛṣṭi`

```text
dṛṣṭi
    view
    doctrinal seeing
    determinate cognitive stance
```

Here `dṛṣṭi` is not mere visual perception and not simply any opinion.

It is a settled cognitive determination through which the field is interpreted and appropriated.

```text
object appears
    through a fixed determination

that determination
    governs judgment
    orientation
    and conduct
```

A view becomes an anuśaya when it is not merely entertained but functions as an underlying disposition capable of repeatedly structuring experience.

---

# 8. The five views

## 8.1 `Satkāya-dṛṣṭi`

```text
sat-kāya-dṛṣṭi
```

The inherited doctrinal sense concerns taking the aggregate-complex as self or as belonging to self.

A cautious first-pass rendering is:

```text
view of the existing body
or
view concerning the personal aggregate-complex
```

The term should not yet be reduced to a polished conventional translation without the Bhāṣya's analysis of `sat` and `kāya`.

Its logical character is appropriation:

```text
conditioned aggregate-complex
    → posited as self
    or
    → posited as mine
```

## 8.2 `Mithyā-dṛṣṭi`

```text
mithyā-dṛṣṭi
    false view
    wrong determination
```

The specific doctrinal range will be clarified later in the chapter. At minimum, it names cognition whose determination of its object is false in a path-relevant way.

## 8.3 `Antagraha-dṛṣṭi`

```text
anta-graha-dṛṣṭi
    view that grasps an extreme
```

The compound gives a clear structure:

```text
anta
    extreme, limit

graha
    grasping, taking hold
```

The view does not merely reach a conclusion. It fixes the field at an extreme determination.

The Bhāṣya will need to specify which extremes are intended and how they arise from `satkāya-dṛṣṭi`.

## 8.4 `Dṛṣṭi-parāmarśa`

```text
dṛṣṭi-parāmarśa
    adherence to views
    overvaluation of a view
```

`Parāmarśa` indicates more than possessing a view. It suggests taking hold of it, clinging to it, or assigning it a privileged status.

The error is reflexive:

```text
not only a false determination
but
attachment to that determination as authoritative
```

## 8.5 `Śīlavrata-parāmarśa`

```text
śīla-vrata-parāmarśa
    adherence to discipline and observance
```

The problem is not discipline or observance as such.

The afflictive determination lies in grasping them falsely, especially where a relative practice is taken as intrinsically sufficient, absolutely purifying, or itself the final path.

The Bhāṣya must determine the precise doctrinal scope.

# 9. Classification by internal differentiation

The opening verses do not merely append items to a list. They reveal that one genus can contain multiple operative species.

```text
rāga
    is divided according to domain

dṛṣṭi
    is divided according to cognitive determination
```

This is an encyclopedic procedure:

```text
state the principal class
    ↓
identify internal difference
    ↓
recalculate the system
```

The kārikā is therefore functioning as a compressed taxonomic schema.

---

# 10. Doctrinal determination

VAkK_5.03 establishes:

```text
1. dṛṣṭi is not doctrinally simple.
2. five distinct afflicted views are recognized.
3. the classification of anuśayas must be recalculated accordingly.
4. false cognition is differentiated by the precise way it determines and appropriates its object.
```

The five are not interchangeable errors.

They distinguish:

```text
self-appropriation
false denial or distortion
extreme fixation
attachment to views
attachment to observances
```

This supports the larger Chapter 5 movement from latent disposition toward a scientific classification of the forms by which affliction structures cognition.

---

# 11. Logical determination

The verse moves from genus to species:

```text
dṛṣṭi
    universal afflicted determination

        ↓ differentiation

satkāya-dṛṣṭi
mithyā-dṛṣṭi
antagraha-dṛṣṭi
dṛṣṭi-parāmarśa
śīlavrata-parāmarśa
```

The key point is that error has form.

```text
false cognition
    is not indeterminate confusion

it has
    object
    mode of grasping
    orientation
    and practical consequence
```

That makes it available for path-discrimination. A counteragent can be specified only when the afflicted determination itself has been specified.

---

# 12. Restrained Organon interpretation

For the later Organon Report, this verse offers a useful distinction between:

```text
incorrect model content
and
attachment to the model as authoritative
```

Approximately:

```text
mithyā-dṛṣṭi
    false determination within the Model

dṛṣṭi-parāmarśa
    reflexive overcommitment to that determination

śīlavrata-parāmarśa
    overcommitment to a procedure or protocol
    as though procedural conformity guaranteed truth or release
```

This remains an interpretive contact point, not part of the primary translation.

The immediate textual lesson is sufficient:

```text
an Agent may fail
    by representing the field falsely

or
    by absolutizing the representation or procedure
    through which it approaches the field
```

---

# 13. Kārikā–Bhāṣya opening

The kārikā independently gives a stable fivefold enumeration, but leaves several questions compressed:

```text
What precisely does satkāya mean?
Which denials define mithyā-dṛṣṭi?
Which extremes define antagraha-dṛṣṭi?
What is the grammatical and doctrinal force of parāmarśa?
How exactly does the fivefold division yield the renewed count of ten?
```

These are not defects in the first-pass study. They mark the point where the encyclopedic kārikā presupposes its Bhāṣya.

The useful methodological fiction is maintained:

```text
analyze the kārikā as foundational
without pretending it is exhaustively self-explanatory
```
