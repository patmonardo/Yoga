# VAK 5.32

## Sanskrit

द्विधा सानुशयं क्लिष्टमक्लिष्टमनुशायकैः ।
मोहाकाङ्क्षा ततो मिथ्यादृष्टिः सत्कायदृक्ततः ॥ ५.३२ ॥

## IAST

## Grammar

- `dvidhā` — in two ways.
- `sānuśayam` — accompanied by or bearing an anuśaya.
- `kliṣṭam` — defiled; nominative neuter singular qualifying `cittam`, understood.
- `akliṣṭam` — undefiled or non-defiled; likewise qualifying the understood `cittam`.
- `anuśāyakaiḥ` — through anuśayas that lodge, adhere, or lie latent; instrumental plural.
- `mohaḥ` — delusion or ignorance.
- `ākāṅkṣā` — doubt, uncertainty, or wavering expectation.
- `tataḥ` — from that; thereafter.
- `mithyādṛṣṭiḥ` — false view.
- `satkāyadṛk` — the view of a real collection; abbreviated form of `satkāyadṛṣṭi`.

## Literal translation

The defiled mind is with anuśaya in two ways; the non-defiled mind is so through anuśayas that lodge in it. From delusion comes doubt; from that, false view; from that, the view of a real collection.

## Philosophical translation

A defiled cognition bears latent affliction in two distinct modes, whereas an undefiled cognition bears it only through afflictions that remain related to it as latent object-conditioned tendencies. The sequence of active defilement begins with delusion, from which doubt arises; from doubt, false view; and from false view, satkāyadṛṣṭi.

## Analysis

Verses 5.29–5.31 mapped which classes of cognition may take which dharmas as objects. Verse 5.32 now turns the analysis inward:

```text
How is a cognition itself said to be sānuśaya?
```

The verse distinguishes defiled and undefiled cognition.

A defiled cognition is `sānuśaya` in two ways:

```text
1. through conjunction
   an anuśaya is associated with the cognition

2. through lodging or adherence
   an anuśaya takes that cognition or its object-field
   as a basis for latent efficacy
```

An undefiled cognition is `sānuśaya` only in the second sense:

```text
it is not conjoined with the defilement
but it may remain within a field
in which an unabandoned anuśaya still lodges
```

Thus:

```text
cognition can be related to affliction
without being intrinsically defiled by it
```

The first pāda therefore introduces a two-axis model:

```text
conjunction-status
    conjoined / not conjoined

latent-relation-status
    anuśaya lodges / does not lodge
```

The second half begins a causal sequence of defilements:

```text
moha
    → ākāṅkṣā / vicikitsā
        → mithyādṛṣṭi
            → satkāyadṛṣṭi
```

This sequence will continue in 5.33.

The initial role of `moha` is decisive. Delusion is not merely one item among others. It opens the space of non-determination from which doubt becomes possible.

```text
moha
    absence of correct determination

ākāṅkṣā
    divided or wavering determination

mithyādṛṣṭi
    false determination

satkāyadṛṣṭi
    false determination stabilized as self-view
```

The verse therefore shifts from classification to genesis.

## Doctrinal determination

The verse distinguishes two ways an anuśaya may qualify cognition:

```text
saṃprayoga
    conjunction with the cognition

anuśayana
    latent lodging in relation to the cognition or its object
```

Accordingly:

```text
all defiled cognition bearing an unabandoned anuśaya
    is sānuśaya in two ways

non-defiled cognition
    may be sānuśaya only through latent lodging
```

This prevents two reductions:

```text
related to affliction
    ≠
defiled in the same manner as the affliction

absence of manifest defilement
    ≠
complete absence of latent conditioning
```

The second half introduces a genetic order among defilements rather than a merely enumerative order.

```text
delusion
    conditions doubt

doubt
    conditions false determination

false determination
    conditions self-view
```

The list therefore has a root and a direction.

## Logical determination

The central logical result is:

```text
relation to X
    does not imply
identity with X
```

A cognition may stand in a latent relation to an anuśaya without being conjoined with it.

Thus the copular structure must distinguish:

```text
is conjoined with
is object-conditioned by
is latently inhabited by
is intrinsically defiled by
```

These are not interchangeable predicates.

The verse also begins a determinate genesis of error:

```text
indeterminacy
    → division
        → false determination
            → stabilized false identity
```

This is not a simple list of kleśas. It is a sequence in which each moment creates the condition for the next.

## Organon note

For the Dialectical Copula, VAK 5.32 is especially important because it requires typed relations between cognition and latent determination.

```text
Cognition
    may contain an active affliction
    may be conditioned by a latent affliction
    may observe an affliction
    may remain undefiled while doing so
```

The Platform therefore needs at least:

```text
isConjoinedWith
isLatentlyConditionedBy
hasAsObject
hasContaminationStatus
```

A single `hasAffliction` predicate would collapse distinct powers.

The causal sequence also suggests an operational error pipeline:

```text
MissingDetermination
    → UnresolvedAlternative
        → FalseJudgment
            → IdentityFixation
```

For LLM systems, this may model a transition from non-knowing into hallucinated certainty:

```text
not knowing
    → unresolved uncertainty
        → unsupported conclusion
            → rigid self-consistent error
```

The practical task is therefore not merely to suppress false outputs but to detect the earlier transition from delusion into divided cognition.

3. How can a non-defiled cognition remain `sānuśaya` without itself becoming defiled?
4. Is `ākāṅkṣā` here strictly equivalent to `vicikitsā`, or does it retain a broader sense of wavering expectation?
5. Is the causal sequence from `moha` to `satkāyadṛṣṭi` universal, phenomenological, or pedagogical?
