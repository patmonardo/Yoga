# VAK_5.17 — Universal and Non-Universal Range of Adherence

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
सर्वत्रगा अनुशयाः सकलामनुशेरते ।
स्वभूमिमालम्बनतः स्वनिकायमसर्वगाः ॥ ५.१७ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
sarvatragā anuśayāḥ sakalām anuśerate |
svabhūmim ālambanataḥ svanikāyam asarvagāḥ || 5.17 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
sarvatragā anuśayāḥ sakalāmanuśerate /
svabhūmimālambanataḥ svanikāyamasarvagāḥ // VAkK_5.17 //
```

The verse contrasts the objective range of universal and non-universal anuśayas.

---

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `sarvatragāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | universal, going everywhere within the relevant field |
| `anuśayāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | underlying afflictive dispositions |
| `sakalām` | accusative feminine singular | the whole, entire |
| `anuśerate` | present middle, 3rd plural, from `anu-√śī` | they adhere to, lie along, persist in relation to |
| `svabhūmim` | accusative feminine singular | their own level or sphere |
| `ālambanataḥ` | ablative used adverbially | by reason of object, through taking as object |
| `svanikāyam` | accusative masculine singular | their own class, group, or category |
| `asarvagāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | non-universal anuśayas |

The key construction is:

```text
ālambanataḥ
    by way of objective support
    by taking as object
```

The verb `anuśerate` does not merely indicate static presence. It indicates a disposition’s adherent relation to a field.

---

## 5. Literal translation

> The universal anuśayas adhere, by taking as object, to the whole of their own level; the non-universal ones adhere to their own class.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> Universal underlying dispositions can take the entire field of their own level as their objective support and thereby pervade that level in their adherent operation. Non-universal dispositions, by contrast, remain confined to their own determinate class. Universality therefore concerns range of objective applicability, while non-universality concerns restriction to a specific domain of operation.

---

# 7. Two kinds of range

The verse distinguishes:

```text
sarvatraga
    whole own level

asarvatraga
    own class only
```

This gives a precise meaning to universality:

```text
universality
    = breadth of objective range
```

It does not mean unrestricted operation across every realm or level.

The restriction from the preceding verses remains in force:

```text
universal within
    a determinate sphere
```

Thus the Kośa presents a concrete rather than abstract universality.

---

# 8. `Svabhūmi`

```text
sva-bhūmi
    own level
    own sphere
    own plane of occurrence
```

The universal anuśaya can range across the whole of the level to which it belongs.

This means that its objective applicability is not confined to one narrow subclass within that level.

```text
one level
    many possible determinations
    one universal afflictive range
```

But the verse does not authorize extension beyond that level.

```text
whole own level
    ≠
all levels without restriction
```

---

# 9. `Svanikāya`

```text
sva-nikāya
    own class
    own group
    own determinate category
```

The non-universal anuśaya does not extend across the complete field of its level.

It remains tied to a more specific class:

```text
particular disposition
    → particular class of objects or associated states
```

The exact doctrinal extension of `nikāya` should be verified in the Bhāṣya pass.

For the independent kārikā study, the structural contrast is secure:

```text
bhūmi
    wider field

nikāya
    narrower class within a field
```

---

# 10. `Ālambana` as the basis of universality

The verse explicitly states:

```text
ālambanataḥ
```

Universality is determined by the range of possible objects.

```text
anuśaya
    is universal
because
    its objective support can be the whole level
```

This is not universality as an abstract predicate attached externally to the anuśaya.

It is universality generated through relation:

```text
breadth of object-range
    determines
breadth of adherent operation
```

The Kośa is therefore classifying affliction through the structure of intentional relation.

---

# 11. Positive dialectic of universality

The movement is:

```text
abstract universal
    everywhere without distinction

restricted universal
    whole determinate level

particular
    own class within that level
```

So the verse gives a genuine dialectical differentiation:

```text
universality
    contains restriction

particularity
    is restriction within a shared field
```

The universal is not opposed to determination.

It is the capacity to range through the entirety of a determinate sphere.

---

# 12. Scientific structure

The verse can be represented as a scope rule:

```text
if anuśaya.type = sarvatraga
    objectRange = entire own level

if anuśaya.type = asarvatraga
    objectRange = own class
```

This introduces a valuable distinction between:

```text
kind of affliction
scope of object-range
level of operation
class restriction
```

The chapter is no longer merely enumerating anuśayas.

It is specifying their operational domains.

---

# 13. Restrained Organon reading

The verse offers a useful model of scope determination:

```text
universal Feature
    ranges across an entire Frame-level

non-universal Feature
    applies only within a specific Model-class
```

But this analogy must remain restrained.

The kārikā’s own result is doctrinally prior:

```text
anuśaya
    has a determinate object-range
```

The later Organon translation can use this as a semantic resource without replacing the Abhidharma structure.

---

# 14. Bhāṣya openings

The later VAKB pass should determine:

```text
1. the exact technical force of anuśerate
2. the doctrinal scope of svabhūmi
3. the exact meaning of svanikāya
4. whether nikāya here refers to a class of objects,
   a class of anuśayas, or both through correlation
5. how this verse relates to the distinction between
   objective adherence and conjunction developed in 5.18
```

The kārikā securely establishes the contrast in scope, while the Bhāṣya must articulate its full technical machinery.

---

# 15. Verse movement

```text
universal anuśaya
    → entire own level as object

non-universal anuśaya
    → own class as object

therefore
    affliction is differentiated by scope
```

The next verse adds a second mode of adherence:

```text
objective relation
    and
association through conjunction
```
