# VAK 5.62

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

वैलक्षण्याद्विपक्षत्वाद्देशविच्छेदकालतः ।
भूतशीलप्रदेशाध्वद्वयानामिव दूरताः ॥ ५.६२ ॥

### IAST

vailakṣaṇyād vipakṣatvād deśa-viccheda-kālataḥ |
bhūta-śīla-pradeśādhva-dvayānām iva dūratāḥ || 5.62 ||

## Padaccheda

vailakṣaṇyāt | vipakṣatvāt | deśa-vicchedāt | kālataḥ | bhūta-śīla-pradeśa-adhva-dvayānām | iva | dūratāḥ

## Grammar

- **vailakṣaṇyāt** — ablative singular of *vailakṣaṇya*, “because of difference in characteristic” or “because of dissimilarity.”
- **vipakṣatvāt** — ablative singular of *vipakṣatva*, “because of being opposed” or “because of standing as a counter-side.”
- **deśa-vicchedāt** — ablative singular, “because of separation in place.”
- **kālataḥ** — ablatival adverb, “with respect to time” or “because of temporal separation.”
- **bhūta-śīla-pradeśa-adhva-dvayānām** — genitive plural compound; the exact internal division and referents require the Bhāṣya. The verse compares the relevant kinds of distance to paired cases involving *bhūta*, *śīla*, *pradeśa*, and *adhvan*.
- **iva** — “like,” “as in the case of.”
- **dūratāḥ** — nominative plural of *dūratā*, “distances,” “states of remoteness.”

The four ablative expressions give four grounds or modes of remoteness. The nominative plural **dūratāḥ** is the predicate to be supplied with them.

## Literal Translation

There are remotenesses because of dissimilarity, because of opposition, because of separation in place, and with respect to time—like those of the two pairs involving element and conduct, and region and temporal course.

## Philosophical Translation

Remoteness is not a single relation. Something may be remote from something else through difference of nature, through direct opposition, through spatial separation, or through temporal separation. The verse illustrates these distinct modes through conventional paired examples.

## Technical Vocabulary

### dūratā

**Dūratā** means distance or remoteness. Here it is classified according to the ground on which two terms are said to be distant.

### vailakṣaṇya

**Vailakṣaṇya** is difference in defining characteristic. Two dharmas may be remote because their intrinsic marks are unlike.

### vipakṣatva

**Vipakṣatva** is opposition. The relation is stronger than mere difference: one term stands as contrary to the other.

### deśa-viccheda

**Deśa-viccheda** is separation by place or locus.

### kāla / adhvan

**Kāla** and **adhvan** concern temporal differentiation. The verse distinguishes temporal remoteness from spatial remoteness.

## Analysis

Verse 5.61 introduced a fourfold counteragent and connected abandonment with the object-relation. Verse 5.62 now classifies **dūratā**, remoteness, into four distinct forms:

```text
remoteness through characteristic difference
remoteness through opposition
remoteness through spatial separation
remoteness through temporal separation
```

The verse therefore prevents the word “distance” from remaining equivocal. Not every separation is spatial, and not every opposition is merely a difference of kind.

The secure kārikā-level determination is classificatory:

```text
dūratā
    is fourfold according to its ground
```

The four grounds are not presented as a temporal sequence. They are alternative determinations of one general relation.

## Logical Determination

The verse distinguishes:

```text
difference
    two terms have unlike characteristics

opposition
    one term stands contrary to the other

spatial separation
    the terms are divided by place

temporal separation
    the terms are divided by time
```

Thus:

```text
mere non-identity
    ≠
contrariety
    ≠
spatial distance
    ≠
temporal distance
```

This is the principal reference-work value of the verse: it supplies a precise fourfold division of remoteness.

## Restrained Organon Interpretation

The verse separates four forms of externality:

```text
qualitative externality
contrary relation
spatial externality
temporal externality
```

The useful point is not to force these into a speculative progression, but to preserve the distinctions. “Otherness” can be grounded in character, contradiction, place, or time, and these relations should not be conflated.

## Bhāṣya Questions

1. How exactly does the Bhāṣya divide **bhūta-śīla-pradeśādhva-dvayānām**?
2. What are the two specific paired examples intended by **dvayānām**?
3. Which example illustrates **vailakṣaṇya**, which **vipakṣatva**, which **deśa-viccheda**, and which temporal remoteness?
4. How does this classification of **dūratā** connect to the fourfold **pratipakṣa** of 5.61?
5. Does **vipakṣatva** here denote a technical counteragent relation or opposition in a broader sense?

## Core Determination

```text
Remoteness is fourfold:
by difference of characteristic,
by opposition,
by spatial separation,
and by temporal separation.
```
