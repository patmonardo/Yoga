# VAK_5.07 — Definitions of the Five Views

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
आत्मात्मीयध्रुवोच्छेदनास्तिहीनाग्रदृष्टयः ।
अहेत्वमार्गे तद्दृष्टिरेतास्ताः पञ्च दृष्टयः ॥ ५.७ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
ātmātmīya-dhruvoccheda-nāsti-hīnāgra-dṛṣṭayaḥ |
ahetvamārge tad-dṛṣṭir etās tāḥ pañca dṛṣṭayaḥ || 5.7 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
ātmātmīyadhruvocchedanāstihīnā gradṝṣṭayaḥ /
ahetvamārge taddṛṣṭiretāstāḥ pañca dṛṣṭayaḥ // VAkK_5.7 //
```

The source line is typographically compressed and appears to contain spacing and transliteration noise around `hīnāgra-dṛṣṭayaḥ`. The doctrinal sequence is nevertheless clear: the verse defines the five views named in VAK_5.03 by means of five characteristic determinations.

---

## 3. Padaccheda

```text
ātma-ātmīya-dṛṣṭiḥ |
dhruva-uccheda-dṛṣṭiḥ |
nāsti-dṛṣṭiḥ |
hīna-agra-dṛṣṭiḥ |
ahetu-amārga-dṛṣṭiḥ |
etāḥ |
tāḥ |
pañca |
dṛṣṭayaḥ
```

The five definitions map as follows:

```text
satkāya-dṛṣṭi
    ātma-ātmīya-dṛṣṭi

antagraha-dṛṣṭi
    dhruva-uccheda-dṛṣṭi

mithyā-dṛṣṭi
    nāsti-dṛṣṭi

dṛṣṭi-parāmarśa
    hīna-agra-dṛṣṭi

śīlavrata-parāmarśa
    ahetu-amārga-dṛṣṭi
```

The order of the defining expressions is not identical to the order used in VAK_5.03. The verse therefore must be read as a doctrinal mapping, not as a mechanically parallel list.

---

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `ātma` | nominal stem | self |
| `ātmīya` | adjective or substantive | belonging to self, mine |
| `dhruva` | adjective | permanent, enduring |
| `uccheda` | masculine noun | annihilation, complete cutting off |
| `nāsti` | negative existential expression | there is not, does not exist |
| `hīna` | adjective | inferior, lower |
| `agra` | adjective or noun | foremost, highest, supreme |
| `ahetu` | compound | non-cause, what is not a cause |
| `amārga` | compound | non-path, what is not a path |
| `tad-dṛṣṭiḥ` | feminine nominative singular | the view concerning that |
| `etāḥ tāḥ pañca dṛṣṭayaḥ` | feminine nominative plural | these are those five views |

The first pāda is an extreme example of nominal compression. It gives a chain of predicative contents rather than five fully inflected independent definitions.

The second pāda makes the classificatory identity explicit:

```text
etāḥ tāḥ pañca dṛṣṭayaḥ
    these are those five views
```

---

## 5. Literal translation

> The views are those concerning self and what belongs to self; permanence and annihilation; nonexistence; what is inferior as highest; and what is not a cause or path as cause or path. These are those five views.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> The five views are determinate distortions of relation. One appropriates the conditioned aggregate-complex as self or as belonging to self. Another fixes that appropriated complex at the extremes of permanence or annihilation. A third denies what is operative or real in the relevant causal and ethical field. A fourth elevates an inferior view as supreme. A fifth mistakes what is not a cause for a cause and what is not a path for a path. The five views are therefore not arbitrary opinions but structured failures of determination.

---

# 7. The five views as five distortions

The verse moves beyond enumeration to definition.

```text
VAK_5.03
    names the five views

VAK_5.07
    determines what each view does
```

The five can be arranged as:

```text
appropriation
    self / mine

extremification
    permanence / annihilation

deniation
    nonexistence

inversion of rank
    inferior as highest

inversion of means
    non-cause as cause
    non-path as path
```

This is already a positive dialectical structure because each error is defined through the determinate relation it misconstructs.

---

# 8. `Ātma-ātmīya-dṛṣṭi`

```text
ātman
    self

ātmīya
    belonging to self
    one's own
```

This defines `satkāya-dṛṣṭi` through a twofold appropriation:

```text
aggregate-complex
    posited as self

or

aggregate-complex
    posited as belonging to self
```

The error is not merely that a wrong object is selected. A possessive structure is imposed upon the conditioned field.

```text
this is I
or
this is mine
```

The view therefore constitutes the basic subjective fixation from which the remaining distortions can develop.

---

# 9. `Dhruva-uccheda-dṛṣṭi`

```text
dhruva
    permanent
    fixed

uccheda
    annihilation
    complete cessation
```

This defines `antagraha-dṛṣṭi` by its two extremes:

```text
permanent persistence
    or
complete annihilation
```

Once a self or possession has been posited, it can be projected as either:

```text
absolutely enduring
```

or:

```text
absolutely cut off
```

The contradiction between the two does not make them unrelated. Both arise from fixing conditioned becoming into an extreme determination.

---

# 10. `Nāsti-dṛṣṭi`

```text
na asti
    it does not exist
```

This is the defining mark of `mithyā-dṛṣṭi` in the verse.

The first-pass text does not yet specify the entire doctrinal range of what is denied. The Bhāṣya must clarify whether the immediate targets include:

```text
cause and effect
ethical consequence
past and future
path and attainment
or other accepted determinations
```

The secure result is that false view operates here as negation of what should be recognized as operative.

```text
real relation
    denied

causal efficacy
    denied

practical consequence
    potentially denied
```

This is not disciplined negation. It is a false cancellation of the field.

---

# 11. `Hīnāgra-dṛṣṭi`

```text
hīna
    inferior

agra
    highest
    foremost
```

This defines `dṛṣṭi-parāmarśa`:

```text
what is inferior
    is taken as supreme
```

The error presupposes ranking, but reverses it.

```text
partial determination
    elevated as final

conditioned standpoint
    elevated as absolute

false view
    elevated as highest truth
```

This is more than holding a false proposition. It is the reflexive attachment to a view because that view is judged superior.

---

# 12. `Ahetu-amārga-dṛṣṭi`

```text
ahetu
    not a cause

amārga
    not a path
```

This defines `śīlavrata-parāmarśa` by inversion of efficacy:

```text
non-cause
    taken as cause

non-path
    taken as path
```

The error concerns practical mediation.

```text
wrong means
    selected for a desired end

ineffective observance
    treated as liberative

external discipline
    absolutized apart from causal adequacy
```

The fault is therefore not discipline itself. It is the false determination of a practice as causally sufficient or path-forming.

---

# 13. A positive dialectic of correction

The verse defines each false view by a determinate opposition that also suggests its correction:

```text
self / mine
    corrected by analysis of conditioned aggregates

permanence / annihilation
    corrected by conditioned continuity without self-substance

nonexistence
    corrected by recognition of causal efficacy

inferior as highest
    corrected by valid ordering of determinations

non-cause / non-path as cause / path
    corrected by adequate mediation
```

The dialectic is positive because the false determination is not merely rejected.

It is replaced by the relation adequate to the field.

```text
negation of error
    becomes
positive determination of structure
```

---

# 14. Restrained Organon interpretation

The five views can be treated as five malformed operations of reflective determination:

```text
appropriation error
extreme fixation
false negation
rank inversion
means-end inversion
```

A scientific system cannot merely label these outputs as false. It must identify the failed relation in each case.

```text
wrong subject relation
wrong temporal determination
wrong existential judgment
wrong hierarchy
wrong causal mediation
```

That is why the Kośa classification is potentially valuable to the Organon. It behaves less like a list of beliefs and more like a taxonomy of systematic failure modes.

This remains a preliminary translation layer. The Bhāṣya must establish the exact doctrinal definitions before any stronger architectural import.

# 16. Kārikā–Bhāṣya opening

The kārikā independently establishes:

```text
five views
    = five determinate distortions
```

The Bhāṣya is still needed to clarify:

```text
the exact derivation of each definition
what `nāsti` specifically denies
how satkāya-dṛṣṭi produces the two extremes
why dṛṣṭi-parāmarśa is expressed as hīnāgra
which disciplines and observances count as ahetu or amārga
```

The verse is therefore foundational but deliberately compressed.
