# VAK_5.15 — Cessation, Path, and the Range of Their Objects

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
स्वभूम्युपरमो मार्गः षड्भूमिनवभूमिकः ।
तद्गोचराणां विषयो मार्गो ह्यन्योऽन्यहेतुकः ॥ ५.१५ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
svabhūmy-uparamo mārgaḥ ṣaḍbhūmi-navabhūmikaḥ |
tad-gocarāṇāṃ viṣayo mārgo hy anyo 'nya-hetukaḥ || 5.15 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
svabhūmyuparamo mārgaḥ ṣaḍbhūminavabhūmikaḥ /
tadgocarāṇāṃ viṣayo mārgo hyanyo 'nyahetukaḥ // VAkK_5.15 //
```

The verse continues the discussion begun in VAK_5.14. There, six anuśayas were said to take uncontaminated objects: cessation and path. Here the kārikā differentiates the range and level-structure of those two objects.

---

## 3. Padaccheda

```text
sva-bhūmi-uparamaḥ |
mārgaḥ |
ṣaḍ-bhūmiḥ |
nava-bhūmikaḥ |
tad-gocarāṇām |
viṣayaḥ |
mārgaḥ |
hi |
anyaḥ |
anya-hetukaḥ
```

A cautious syntactical reconstruction is:

```text
uparamaḥ
    sva-bhūmiḥ

mārgaḥ
    ṣaḍ-bhūmiḥ
    nava-bhūmikaḥ

mārgaḥ
    tad-gocarāṇāṃ viṣayaḥ

hi
    anyaḥ anyasya hetuḥ
```

The exact distribution of `ṣaḍbhūmi` and `navabhūmika` is highly compressed and should be checked against the Bhāṣya before being fixed more narrowly.

---

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Working meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `sva-bhūmi` | compound | its own level or sphere |
| `uparamaḥ` | nominative masculine singular | cessation, subsiding, coming to rest |
| `mārgaḥ` | nominative masculine singular | path |
| `ṣaḍ-bhūmi` | compound adjective | belonging to six levels |
| `nava-bhūmikaḥ` | nominative masculine singular adjective | belonging to nine levels |
| `tad-gocarāṇām` | genitive plural | of those whose object-range is that |
| `viṣayaḥ` | nominative masculine singular | object, field |
| `hi` | explanatory particle | for, indeed |
| `anyaḥ` | nominative masculine singular | another, one distinct from another |
| `anya-hetukaḥ` | compound adjective | having another as cause; causally related to another |

The verse uses both `gocara` and `viṣaya`:

```text
gocara
    operative range
    field traversed by cognition

viṣaya
    determinate object
```

The distinction should not be overdrawn in the first pass, but the pairing emphasizes that the question concerns not merely the existence of cessation and path, but their admissible object-range.

---

## 5. Literal translation

> Cessation belongs to its own level. The path is of six levels and of nine levels. The path is the object of those whose range is that, for one [path] is causally connected with another.

This translation preserves the verse's compressed structure without pretending to settle the exact doctrinal allocation of all levels.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> Cessation is determined relative to the level whose afflictive process has ceased. The path, however, possesses a broader and internally articulated range: it is classified across multiple levels and may become the object of afflictions whose cognitive field extends to it. This broader range is possible because paths are not isolated events; one path can stand in causal relation to another. Thus the path is both a liberative determination and an internally mediated series.

---

# 7. `Svabhūmy-uparama`

```text
sva-bhūmi
    own level
    own sphere

uparama
    cessation
    subsiding
    coming to rest
```

The phrase indicates that cessation is not treated as an abstract undifferentiated nothing.

It is cessation of a determinate conditioned domain:

```text
this level of affliction
    ceases

therefore
    the cessation is indexed
    to that level
```

So:

```text
cessation
    is not bare absence

cessation
    is determinate non-continuation
    of a specific conditioned structure
```

This makes `uparama` a positive relational determination.

---

# 8. Cessation as a determinate result

The kārikā's expression allows a crucial distinction:

```text
mere absence
    no positive relation to what is absent

cessation
    determinate relation
    to what has been brought to an end
```

The path does not produce an indeterminate void. It brings about the ending of a particular afflictive structure at a particular level.

Thus:

```text
conditioned process
    → counteraction
    → cessation of that process
```

The result remains intelligible through the determination it terminates.

---

# 9. `Mārga`

```text
mārga
    path
    way
    operative course of liberation
```

In VAK_5.14, the path was an uncontaminated object that could nevertheless be misconceived by contaminated cognition.

VAK_5.15 adds that the path itself has internal level-structure:

```text
mārga
    not one featureless act

mārga
    articulated across bhūmis
```

The path is therefore a system of determinate operations rather than a single instantaneous negation.

---

# 10. `Ṣaḍbhūmi` and `Navabhūmika`

The verse gives two level counts:

```text
six levels
nine levels
```

What is secure from the kārikā is:

```text
path
    has a differentiated level-range
```

What remains open for the Bhāṣya is:

```text
which path is six-level
which is nine-level
how the two counts are generated
whether they concern origin, object, support, or operation
```

The independent kārikā pass should preserve the numerical architecture without inventing the missing explanatory machinery.

---

# 11. `Tad-gocarāṇāṃ viṣayaḥ`

```text
tad-gocarāṇām
    of those whose operative range is that

viṣayaḥ
    object
```

The verse is concerned with admissible object-relations.

An affliction is not classified only by its intrinsic type. It is also classified by what it can take as object:

```text
anuśaya-type
    +
object-range
    =
concrete afflictive determination
```

VAK_5.14 showed:

```text
contaminated cognition
    may take an uncontaminated object
```

VAK_5.15 now specifies that the path's own internal range helps explain how such object-relations are distributed.

---

# 12. `Anyo 'nyahetukaḥ`

```text
anyaḥ
    one distinct path

anya-hetukaḥ
    caused by another
    causally related to another
```

The verse's most important philosophical claim is that paths are mutually or serially mediated:

```text
earlier path-determination
    conditions
later path-determination
```

or, more cautiously:

```text
one path
    can serve as cause
    for another path
```

The path is therefore not an externally imposed correction. It develops through an internally connected causal sequence.

---

# 13. Positive dialectic of the path

The positive dialectic becomes explicit:

```text
false determination
    is opposed by
appropriate seeing

appropriate seeing
    conditions
further path-development

further path-development
    produces
cessation at a determinate level
```

Thus the path is not merely destructive.

It is productive of its own successor determinations:

```text
path₁
    → path₂
    → path₃
    → determinate cessation
```

The negative moment is contained within a positive causal articulation.

---

# 14. Cessation and path are structurally different

The verse distinguishes:

```text
cessation
    indexed to its own level

path
    capable of broader causal and objectual range
```

So:

```text
cessation
    determinate terminus

path
    mediated process
```

This is a crucial difference.

Cessation is the ending of a specific binding structure. The path is the articulated means through which such endings become possible.

---

# 15. Dialectical determination

The movement can be expressed as:

```text
abstract cessation
    ↓
cessation of a determinate level

abstract path
    ↓
path distributed across levels

isolated path
    ↓
causally mediated sequence of paths
```

This is another instance of concrete universality:

```text
path is universal
    not because it is featureless

but because
    its distinct moments mediate one another
```

---

# 16. Relation to VAK_5.14

```text
VAK_5.14
    contaminated cognition can take
    cessation and path as objects

VAK_5.15
    cessation and path have
    distinct level-structures and object-ranges
```

Together:

```text
uncontaminated object
    does not mean
    indeterminate or unstructured object
```

The path and cessation are themselves scientifically differentiated.

---

# 17. Bhāṣya openings

The following questions should remain open for the VAKB pass:

```text
1. What exactly does `svabhūmy-uparama` qualify?

2. How are the six and nine bhūmis counted?

3. Which forms of path belong to each count?

4. What is the precise scope of `tad-gocarāṇām`?

5. Does `anyo 'nyahetukaḥ` indicate serial causation,
   mutual causation, or a more restricted technical relation?

6. How does the level of an anuśaya relate to the level
   of the path or cessation it takes as object?
```

The kārikā independently establishes the architecture:

```text
cessation
    own-level determination

path
    multi-level determination
    causally mediated determination
    object of cognition
```

The Bhāṣya must supply the full map.

# 19. Compact result

```text
cessation
    = determinate ending
      of a structure at its own level

path
    = multi-level liberative process
      whose moments causally mediate one another
```

The verse therefore turns the uncontaminated field into a positive architecture:

```text
not bare purity
but structured cessation and mediated path
```
