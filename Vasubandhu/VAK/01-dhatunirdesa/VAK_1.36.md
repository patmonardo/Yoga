# VAK_1.36

## 1. Sanskrit (Devanāgarī)

> छिनत्ति छिद्यते चैव बाह्यं धातुचतुष्टयम् ।
>
> दह्यते तुलयत्येवं विवादो दग्धृतुल्ययोः ॥ १.३६ ॥

## 4. Grammar

### bāhyaṃ dhātu-catuṣṭayam

The subject is the **external tetrad of domains**. In context this means the four external material object-domains capable of such physical operation:

```text
rūpa-dhātu
 gandha-dhātu
 rasa-dhātu
 spraṣṭavya-dhātu
```

Sound is excluded. Sound was already treated as always unappropriated in VAK_1.34, and it is not a cuttable, burnable, or weighable body in this sense.

### chinatti / chidyate

The external tetrad both **cuts** and **is cut**.

This is not merely a casual empirical observation. It expresses reciprocal material operation:

```text
active capacity
    chinatti = cuts

passive susceptibility
    chidyate = is cut
```

The same external material domain can be both agent and patient of division.

### dahyate

The external tetrad **is burned**. The passive form matters: the verse does not simply say that it burns, but that it is burnable. Burnability is a susceptibility of material form.

### tulayati

The external tetrad **weighs** or **is weighed/balanced** according to the interpretive frame. The active form **tulayati** literally means it weighs, balances, or measures. It indicates weight as a determination of material relation.

### vivādo dagdhṛtulyayoḥ

There is a dispute concerning the cases of burning and weighing. The verse itself records a scholastic controversy. Cutting and being cut are straightforwardly assigned. Burning and weighing involve disagreement concerning how these operations are to be attributed.

The important point for this analysis is not to erase the dispute, but to preserve it as part of the logical texture of the verse.

## 5. Literal Translation

> The external tetrad of domains cuts and is cut. It is burned and weighs. Thus there is dispute concerning the burner/burned and the weigher/weighed cases.

Alternative literal rendering:

> The four external domains both cut and are cut; they are burned and they weigh. In this way there is controversy concerning burning and weighing.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The four external material domains are not inert objects. They enter reciprocal material operation: they can divide and be divided, undergo burning, and stand in relations of weight or measure. Yet the attribution of burning and weighing is disputed, showing that material operation is not a simple predicate but a relational determination.

Hegel-clean working rendering:

> External materiality is operative reciprocity. It is not merely there; it acts and is acted upon. In cutting, materiality appears as active division and passive divisibility. In burning and weighing, its determinations become more controversial, because material process and material measure are not simple qualities but relations of operation.

## 8. Logical Determination

VAK_1.35 established material constitution:

```text
Elemental basis
    ↓
Derivative materiality
    ↓
Accumulated form-bearing structure
```

VAK_1.36 now gives material operation:

```text
External material tetrad
    ↓
active operation: cuts
    ↓
passive susceptibility: is cut
    ↓
processual susceptibility: is burned
    ↓
measure-relation: weighs / is weighed
```

The decisive logical distinction is:

```text
materiality
    ≠ inert objecthood

materiality
    = reciprocal operative determinacy
```

The external domains are not merely bearers of qualities. They are capable of acting, undergoing, transforming, and being measured.

## 9. Interpretive Note

This verse gives life to the material domains.

After VAK_1.35, one might think of rūpa as accumulated material structure. VAK_1.36 immediately adds that accumulated materiality is operational. It cuts and is cut. It undergoes burning. It enters weight-relation.

The pairing **chinatti / chidyate** is especially important. The same domain can be understood under active and passive determinations:

```text
as active
    it cuts

as passive
    it is cut
```

This is not accidental. It is a logic of material reciprocity.

### Cutting and divisibility

Cutting reveals external materiality as divisive determination. Matter is not just extended; it is dividable and dividing. The external domain can impose limit and receive limit.

### Burning and transformation

Burning introduces process. A material domain is not merely bounded but transformable. Fire is not just an element in a list; combustion is a real alteration of form-bearing materiality.

### Weighing and measure

Weighing introduces measure. Materiality is not only qualitative but enters quantitative determination. This prepares a movement toward measure-like thinking inside the domain-analysis itself.

### The dispute

The kārikā does not hide controversy. It says there is **vivāda** concerning burning and weighing. That matters. The system is not merely dogmatic enumeration; it records where the predication of material operation becomes dialectically unstable.

Organon reading:

```text
cutting
    = limit imposed and received

burning
    = alteration of material constitution

weighing
    = materiality entering measure

vivāda
    = instability in assigning operation to substrate
```

Thus VAK_1.36 moves from material constitution to material actuality.
