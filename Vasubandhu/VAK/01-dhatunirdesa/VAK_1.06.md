# VAK_1.6

## 1. Sanskrit (Devanāgarī)

> प्रतिसंख्यानिरोधो यो विसंयोगः पृथक् पृथक् ।
>
> उत्पादात्यन्तविघ्नोऽन्यो निरोधोऽप्रतिसंख्यया ॥

## 2. Sanskrit (IAST)

> pratisaṃkhyānirodho yo visaṃyogaḥ pṛthak pṛthak /
>
> utpādātyantavighno 'nyo nirodho 'pratisaṃkhyayā //

## 3. Padaccheda

| Pada | Form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| pratisaṃkhyā-nirodhaḥ | nom.sg.m | cessation through discriminative cognition |
| yaḥ | pronoun | which |
| visaṃyogaḥ | nom.sg.m | disjunction, dissociation |
| pṛthak pṛthak | indeclinable | individually, separately |
| utpāda-atyanta-vighnaḥ | nom.sg.m compound | complete obstruction of arising |
| anyaḥ | nom.sg.m | the other |
| nirodhaḥ | nom.sg.m | cessation |
| apratisaṃkhyayā | instr.sg.f | through non-discriminative cessation |

## 4. Literal Translation

> The cessation through discriminative cognition is the dissociation occurring separately in each case. The other cessation is the complete obstruction of arising through non-discriminative cessation.

## 5. Philosophical Translation

> The two unconstructed cessations are distinguished according to whether cessation proceeds through discriminative determination or through the mere non-occurrence of production. The first is achieved through analytic separation; the second is the simple absence of further arising.

## 6. Technical Vocabulary

| Sanskrit | Rendering | Note |
|---|---|---|

| nirodha | cessation | suspension or elimination |

| apratisaṃkhyā | non-discriminative | absence of analytical determination |
| utpāda | arising | production of a dharma |
| vighna | obstruction | prevention of occurrence |
