# VAK_1.1

## 1. Sanskrit (Devanāgarī)

> यः सर्वथासर्वहतान्धकारः संसारपङ्काज्जगदुज्जहार ।
>
> तस्मै नमस्कृत्य यथार्थशास्त्रे शास्त्रं प्रवक्ष्याम्यभिधर्मकोशम् ॥ १.१ ॥

## 4. Grammar

The verse opens with a relative-correlative structure:

```text
yaḥ ... tasmai
    he who ... to him
```

The Buddha is described by two acts:

```text
1. sarvathā sarva-hata-andhakāraḥ
   darkness destroyed in every way

2. saṃsāra-paṅkāt jagat ujjahāra
   he raised the world from the mire of saṃsāra
```

After bowing to him, Vasubandhu announces the work:

```text
śāstraṃ pravakṣyāmi abhidharmakośam
    I shall proclaim the treatise, the Abhidharmakośa
```

## 5. Literal Translation

> Having bowed to him who, with darkness destroyed in every way, raised the world from the mire of saṃsāra, I shall proclaim the treatise, the Abhidharmakośa, in accordance with the teaching of reality.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The work begins by bowing to the one whose ignorance has been wholly extinguished and who raises beings out of saṃsāric entanglement. The Abhidharmakośa is then announced as a śāstra ordered to yathārtha: reality as it is.

Organon rendering:

> Abhidharma begins from the overcoming of darkness. The Buddha is not invoked as ornament but as the actual disclosure of Dharma: one who has destroyed obscuration and lifted the world from the mud of appearance. The treatise is therefore a treasury of determinate reality.

## 8. Logical Determination

The opening structure is:

```text
obscuration
    ↓
removal of darkness
    ↓
raising from saṃsāra
    ↓
proclamation of the Dharma-treatise
```

This is not a neutral preface. It establishes the condition of Abhidharma:

```text
Dharma-analysis begins where darkness has been overcome.
```

## 9. Interpretive Note

The first verse already sets the chapter's whole orientation. Abhidharma is not mere scholastic inventory. It is grounded in liberation from darkness and in the disclosure of reality.

For the later dhātu analysis, this matters because the domains will not be treated as dead categories. They are the lawful determinations through which the world of saṃsāra can be understood, judged, cultivated, and transcended.

Organon note:

```text
Buddha
    = one in whom obscuration is destroyed

Abhidharma
    = analysis of Dharma according to reality

Kośa
    = treasury of determinate truth
```
