# VAK_1.33

## 1. Sanskrit (Devanāgarī)

> निरूपणानुस्मरणविकल्पेनाविकल्पकाः ।
>
> तौ प्रज्ञा मानसी व्यग्रा स्मृतिः सर्वैव मानसी ॥ १.३३ ॥

## 4. Grammar

### nirūpaṇānusmaraṇavikalpena

```text
nirūpaṇa-anusmaraṇa-vikalpena
```

This is an instrumental singular compound. It does not say that the five sensory knowledge-domains are absolutely without all cognitive articulation. VAK_1.32 has just said that they are **sa-vitarka-vicāra**, accompanied by vitarka and vicāra. Therefore **avikalpakāḥ** in VAK_1.33 must be restricted by this instrumental compound.

The sense is:

```text
avikalpakāḥ
        not with respect to every possible articulation,
        but with respect to nirūpaṇa-anusmaraṇa-vikalpa.
```

They are without the vikalpa that consists in determination and reproductive recollection.

### avikalpakāḥ

```text
avikalpa-kaḥ = having no vikalpa / not operating through vikalpa
```

The nominative plural masculine refers back to the five **vijñānadhātavaḥ** from VAK_1.32. These five sensory knowledge-domains are not blank; they are already with vitarka and vicāra. But they do not yet operate through the higher mental functions named in this verse.

### tau

```text
tau = those two
```

The dual points back to the two named differentiating functions within the compound:

```text
nirūpaṇa
anusmaraṇa
```

The verse then identifies their cognitive bases:

```text
nirūpaṇa → prajñā, when mental and dispersed/engaged
anusmaraṇa → smṛti, which is entirely mental
```

### prajñā mānasī vyagrā

```text
prajñā = principle / discriminative knowing
mānasī = mental
vyagrā = dispersed, occupied, engaged
```

Here **nirūpaṇa** is grounded in **prajñā**, but not prajñā as pure principle in itself. It is **mental prajñā**, and specifically **vyagrā**: prajñā as engaged in differentiating specification.

This is not yet pure contemplative principle. It is discriminative knowing functioning in the mental domain.

### smṛtiḥ sarvaiva mānasī

```text
smṛtiḥ = recollection
sarvā eva = entirely, wholly
mānasī = mental
```

Reproductive recollection is not sensory. It belongs wholly to manas. Sense-knowledge may occasion it, but the operation of recollection as such is mental.

## 5. Literal Translation

> They are without vikalpa with respect to the vikalpa of determination and reproductive recollection. Those two are: mental, engaged prajñā; and recollection, which is entirely mental.

Expanded literal rendering:

> The five [knowledge-domains] are avikalpaka with respect to determination-and-recollection-vikalpa. Of those two, [determination] is mental, engaged prajñā; [recollection] is smṛti, which is wholly mental.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The five sensory knowledge-domains, though accompanied by vitarka and vicāra, are non-vikalpic in the stricter sense: they do not operate through the vikalpa of determination and reproductive recollection. Determination is discriminative knowing as mentally engaged; reproductive recollection is recollection itself, and it belongs wholly to the mental domain.

Hegel-clean working rendering:

> Sensory knowledge is already logically touched by immediate Reason and essential Reflection, but it is not yet the free differentiating activity of mental determination and recollective reproduction. Determination is prajñā as engaged mental discrimination; recollection is wholly manasic. Thus vikalpa begins only where knowledge is inwardly mediated by manas.

## 8. Logical Determination

VAK_1.33 resolves the apparent tension created by VAK_1.32.

VAK_1.32 says:

```text
five sensory knowledge-domains
        = with vitarka and vicāra
```

VAK_1.33 now says:

```text
five sensory knowledge-domains
        = avikalpaka
        = without vikalpa of nirūpaṇa and anusmaraṇa
```

Therefore:

```text
vitarka / vicāra
        ≠ vikalpa in the strict sense
```

They are not yet the higher activity of imaginative differentiation. They are logical immediacy and reflective articulation within sensory knowledge. Vikalpa, by contrast, requires manas.

The internal structure is:

```text
Sensory knowledge-domain
        ↓
with vitarka and vicāra
        ↓
without nirūpaṇa-anusmaraṇa-vikalpa
        ↓
not yet mental imaginative differentiation
```

Then the verse assigns the two higher functions:

```text
nirūpaṇa
        ↓
mental, engaged prajñā

anusmaraṇa
        ↓
smṛti, wholly mental
```

So VAK_1.33 establishes a boundary:

```text
sensory articulation
        ≠
mental differentiation
```

This boundary is essential for keeping vijñāna, prajñā, smṛti, and vikalpa distinct.

## 9. Interpretive Note

This verse is crucial because it prevents a crude reading of VAK_1.32.

If the five sensory knowledge-domains are with vitarka and vicāra, one might think they already possess full conceptual or imaginative differentiation. VAK_1.33 says no. They are **avikalpaka** with respect to the more advanced vikalpa of determination and reproductive recollection.

That means there are levels of articulation:

```text
1. vitarka / vicāra
   immediate logical articulation of sensory knowledge

2. nirūpaṇa
   determination as mental, engaged prajñā

3. anusmaraṇa
   reproductive recollection as smṛti

4. vikalpa
   imaginative differentiation grounded in mental operation

5. saṅkalpa
   conceptual positing, to be kept distinct from vikalpa
```

The inherited translation habit of making **vikalpa** into "conceptualization" is too blunt. It collapses the whole sequence into a modern psychological category. Here **vikalpa** is better preserved as imaginative differentiation: the power by which manas differentiates through determination and recollection.

### Organon note

VAK_1.33 gives the first clear transition from the externally classified domains to the internal genetic sequence of cognition.

```text
vitarka
        = Reason as immediate determination
        = immediate determinations of Being

vicāra
        = essential reflection
        = determination of Being through reflective articulation

nirūpaṇa
        = determinate specification
        = prajñā functioning in manas

anusmaraṇa
        = reproductive recollection
        = smṛti as wholly mental

vikalpa
        = imaginative differentiation
        = not yet Concept
```

This is the exact bridge into a Kant-clean and Hegel-clean architecture. The sensory knowledge-domains are not empiricist givens. They already have immediate logical articulation. But the higher power of differentiation requires the mental domain, because determination and recollection are not merely received; they are inwardly mediated.

Thus VAK_1.33 marks the beginning of the **inner genesis of knowing**:

```text
knowledge-domain
        ↓
logical immediacy and reflection
        ↓
mental determination
        ↓
reproductive recollection
        ↓
imaginative differentiation
        ↓
conceptual positing
```
