## 4. Grammar

### tridhā anye

The verse begins with a continuation from VAK_1.37. Some domains were classified as **vipākaja**, **aupacayika**, and **naiḥṣyandika**. Now the text says that the others are **tridhā**, threefold.

The likely reference is to domains that may be determined through the three causal modes:

```text
vipākaja
    born of maturation

aupacayika
    accumulative / growth-produced

naiḥṣyandika
    born of outflow-continuity
```

Thus **tridhā anye** does not introduce a new transcendental triad. It extends the causal-genetic classification.

### dravyavān ekaḥ

```text
dravya-vān ekaḥ
    one is substance-bearing / one has real entity-status
```

This is a delicate phrase and should remain marked for bhāṣya review. In the working Organon analysis, it indicates that one domain has a special status as possessing real determinate entityhood in a way not reducible to merely grouped classification.

The most plausible candidate in context is **dharmadhātu**, because the dharma-domain contains determinations not reducible to the gross material domains or the sensory knowledge-domains. It is the domain in which dharmas as such are gathered.

This should not yet be over-read as metaphysical substance in the Western sense. **Dravya** here means real entity, real determinate thing, or substantial datum in the Abhidharma sense.

### kṣaṇikāḥ paścimās trayaḥ

```text
paścimāḥ trayaḥ
    the last three

kṣaṇikāḥ
    momentary
```

The last three domains in the eighteen-domain sequence are:

```text
manodhātu
dharmadhātu
manovijñānadhātu
```

They are called **kṣaṇika**, momentary. This does not mean they are unreal. It means their mode of occurrence is momentary arising.

This is crucial for the Organon: momentariness is not nullity. It is the mode of actuality of these determinations.

### cakṣurvijñānadhātvoḥ

```text
cakṣus-dhātu
    eye-domain

cakṣurvijñāna-dhātu
    eye-knowledge-domain
```

The compound refers to the two domains together in the genitive/locative dual: eye and eye-knowledge.

### pṛthag lābhaḥ sahāpi ca

```text
pṛthak lābhaḥ
    separate acquisition

saha api ca
    and also together
```

The acquisition of eye-domain and eye-knowledge-domain may occur separately or together. This is not a minor doctrinal point. It shows that faculty and knowledge are correlated but not simply identical, and not necessarily acquired in a single undifferentiated event.

## 5. Literal Translation

> The others are threefold. One is substance-bearing. The last three are momentary. Of the eye-domain and eye-knowledge-domain, acquisition may be separate and also together.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The remaining domains admit a threefold causal determination. One domain bears real entity-status in a special sense. The final three domains are momentary. The eye-domain and eye-knowledge-domain may be acquired separately or together, showing that faculty and knowledge are correlated without being simply identical.

Hegel-clean working rendering:

> The classification now passes from causal type to modal status. Some domains stand under the threefold causal genesis; one domain bears determinate substantial status; the final triad occurs momentarily. Eye and eye-knowledge may be obtained apart or together, so the condition of knowing and the event of knowing are internally related but not collapsed.

## 8. Logical Determination

VAK_1.37 gave causal genesis:

```text
vipākaja
aupacayika
naiḥṣyandika
```

VAK_1.38 now adds modal and acquisition determinations:

```text
threefold causal status
    ↓
real-entity status
    ↓
momentariness
    ↓
separate/together acquisition
```

The structure is:

```text
Others
    = threefold by causal mode

One
    = dravyavān, real-entity-bearing

Last three
    = momentary

Eye-domain and eye-knowledge-domain
    = acquired separately or together
```

The final clause is especially important:

```text
faculty
    ≠ knowledge-event

faculty
    may be acquired apart from knowledge

knowledge
    may arise with faculty

but the two are not simply identical
```

This preserves the difference between condition and act.

## 9. Interpretive Note

VAK_1.38 is a compact but powerful verse. It continues the causal analysis of VAK_1.37, but it also opens a new problem: the relation between domain-possession and domain-operation.

The eye-domain is not the same as eye-knowledge-domain. The former is the visual faculty-domain; the latter is the event or domain of visual knowing. Their acquisition may be separate or together.

That means the text refuses both crude identity and crude separation:

```text
not identity:
    eye ≠ eye-knowledge

not external separation:
    eye and eye-knowledge may be acquired together
```

This is a very fine logical point. The condition of knowing and the event of knowing are distinct, but internally coordinated.

### Momentariness

The last three domains — mind-domain, dharma-domain, and mental-knowledge-domain — are called **kṣaṇika**. This does not reduce them to nothing. Rather, their actuality is evental. They are real as momentary determinations.

```text
momentary
    ≠ unreal

momentary
    = actual as arising determination
```

### Dravya

The phrase **dravyavān ekaḥ** must remain open for later review. It is too important to settle prematurely. But the working insight is clear: the verse is distinguishing mere classificatory grouping from real determinate entity-status.

For the Organon, this means:

```text
domain
    can be classificatory
    can be causal-genetic
    can be momentary
    can be real-entity-bearing
    can be acquired
```

The dhātu-system is now no longer a table. It is a logic of possible domain-status.

### Organon note

The movement from 1.34 to 1.38 is now visible:

```text
1.34 object-support / appropriation
1.35 material constitution
1.36 material operation
1.37 causal genesis
1.38 modal status and acquisition
```

This is foundational for a Kant-Fichte-Hegel reconstruction. A domain is not just an item. It is a position in a generated system, determined by:

```text
object-relation
embodiment
material constitution
operation
causal genesis
momentary actuality
acquisition
```

The system is becoming genetic.
