## 4. Grammar

The verse opens with a relative-correlative structure:

```text
yaŠł• ... tasmai
    he who ... to him
```

The Buddha is described by two acts:

```text
1. sarvathńĀ sarva-hata-andhakńĀraŠł•
   darkness destroyed in every way

2. saŠĻÉsńĀra-paŠĻÖkńĀt jagat ujjahńĀra
   he raised the world from the mire of saŠĻÉsńĀra
```

After bowing to him, Vasubandhu announces the work:

## 5. Literal Translation

> Having bowed to him who, with darkness destroyed in every way, raised the world from the mire of saŠĻÉsńĀra, I shall proclaim the treatise, the AbhidharmakoŇõa, in accordance with the teaching of reality.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The work begins by bowing to the one whose ignorance has been wholly extinguished and who raises beings out of saŠĻÉsńĀric entanglement. The AbhidharmakoŇõa is then announced as a ŇõńĀstra ordered to yathńĀrtha: reality as it is.

Organon rendering:

> Abhidharma begins from the overcoming of darkness. The Buddha is not invoked as ornament but as the actual disclosure of Dharma: one who has destroyed obscuration and lifted the world from the mud of appearance. The treatise is therefore a treasury of determinate reality.

## 9. Interpretive Note

The first verse already sets the chapter's whole orientation. Abhidharma is not mere scholastic inventory. It is grounded in liberation from darkness and in the disclosure of reality.

For the later dhńĀtu analysis, this matters because the domains will not be treated as dead categories. They are the lawful determinations through which the world of saŠĻÉsńĀra can be understood, judged, cultivated, and transcended.

Organon note:

```text
Buddha
    = one in whom obscuration is destroyed

Abhidharma
    = analysis of Dharma according to reality

KoŇõa
    = treasury of determinate truth
```
