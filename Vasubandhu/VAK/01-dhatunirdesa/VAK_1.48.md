## 4. Grammar

The verse closes by gathering several remaining classifications.

### pañca bāhyā dvijñeyāḥ

The five external object-domains are knowable by two knowledges. These are:

```text
rūpa
śabda
gandha
rasa
spraṣṭavya
```

They are external object-domains and may be known by their corresponding sensory knowledge and by mental knowledge.

### nityā dharmā asaṃskṛtāḥ

The permanent dharmas are the unconditioned dharmas.

```text
nitya
    = permanent

asaṃskṛta
    = unconditioned / uncompounded
```

This is a crucial closing point: not all dharmas are conditioned events. The dharma-domain includes unconditioned dharmas.

### dharmārdham indriyam

A part of the dharma-domain is **indriya**, faculty. The dharma-domain is therefore internally differentiated: part of it is faculty-status, part of it is not.

### ye ca dvādaśādhyātmikāḥ smṛtāḥ

The twelve internal domains are recalled from VAK_1.39:

```text
six faculty-domains
six knowledge-domains
```

These are remembered as internal.

## 5. Literal Translation

> The five external [domains] are knowable by two. The permanent dharmas are unconditioned. A part of the dharma-domain is faculty; and those twelve are remembered as internal.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The five external object-domains are knowable both by their corresponding sensory knowledge and by mental knowledge. Permanent dharmas are the unconditioned. A portion of the dharma-domain has faculty-status, and the twelve faculty-and-knowledge domains are remembered as internal.

Organon rendering:

> The chapter closes by sealing the domain-system. External objects are not trapped in one disclosure; they may be known by sensory knowledge and by mind. The unconditioned dharmas are permanent. The dharma-domain contains a faculty-part. The twelve internal domains are retained as the internal side of the whole architecture.

## 8. Logical Determination

VAK_1.48 seals the chapter with four closures:

```text
1. five external domains
    = knowable by two

2. permanent dharmas
    = unconditioned

3. part of dharmadhātu
    = faculty

4. twelve domains
    = remembered as internal
```

The key logical distinction:

```text
conditioned domain
    ≠
unconditioned dharma
```

And:

```text
external object
    is not restricted to one mode of knowing

it may be disclosed sensorially
and recollected/determined mentally
```

## 9. Interpretive Note

This is an extraordinary closing verse for the first pass.

The chapter began by enumerating aggregates, spheres, and domains. It now ends by distinguishing external knowability, permanence, unconditioned dharmas, faculty-status within dharmadhātu, and the remembered internal twelve.

The dharma-domain remains the decisive hinge. It includes unconditioned dharmas. It includes a faculty-part. It can function as the sixth external object-domain. And in the higher Organon reading, it is the real-domain, the Island of Truth.

### Absolute Philosophy note

This closing verse permits the great overlay without forcing it into the primary translation:

```text
dharmadhātu
    = domain of dharmas as determinations
    = includes unconditioned permanence
    = contains faculty-status
    = object-domain for manas
    = real-domain in the higher theological-logical sense
```

Kant-Fichte-Hegel-Śaṅkara-Kṛṣṇa-Yoga-Sāṃkhya note:

```text
The real is not the sum of appearances.
The real is the lawful domain in which appearance is determinable.

The five external domains are knowable.
The internal twelve are remembered.
The unconditioned dharmas are permanent.
Dharmadhātu remains the hinge between appearance and truth.
```

Thus Dhātunirdeśa closes not as a list, but as a principial map of knowability, embodiment, causality, path, and truth-status.
