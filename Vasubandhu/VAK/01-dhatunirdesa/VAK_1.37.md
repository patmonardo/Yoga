## 4. Grammar

### vipākajaupacayikāḥ pañcādhyātmam

The five internal domains are **vipākaja** and **aupacayika**.

```text
pañca adhyātmam
    = the five internal domains
    = the five sense-faculty domains
```

These are:

```text
cakṣus-dhātu
śrotra-dhātu
ghrāṇa-dhātu
jihvā-dhātu
kāya-dhātu
```

They are **vipākaja** because they arise as maturation-results of karma. They are also **aupacayika** because they are maintained, increased, nourished, or built up through accumulation and growth.

### vipākajaḥ na śabdaḥ

```text
na śabdaḥ vipākajaḥ
    sound is not born of maturation
```

Sound is excluded from karmic-resultant materiality. Sound may arise dependently, but it is not a karmic maturation-result in the way the living sense-faculties are.

This continues the special treatment of sound from VAK_1.34, where sound was marked as unappropriated.

### apratighā aṣṭau

The eight **apratigha** domains are the non-resistant or non-obstructive domains:

```text
seven citta-dhātus
    five sensory knowledge-domains
    manovijñānadhātu
    manodhātu

plus dharmadhātu
```

They do not resist or obstruct like material form. They are not spatially impeding bodies.

### naiḥṣyandika-vipākajāḥ

The eight non-resistant domains are classified as:

```text
naiḥṣyandika
    born of outflow-continuity / homogeneous continuation

vipākaja
    born of karmic maturation
```

This means that the non-resistant domains are not material accumulations, but they nevertheless have causal genesis. They arise through continuity and through karmic-resultant maturation.

## 5. Literal Translation

> The five internal [domains] are born of maturation and accumulation. Sound is not born of maturation. The eight non-resistant [domains] are born of outflow-continuity and maturation.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The five internal sense-faculty domains arise both as karmic maturation and as organic accumulation. Sound, though a material object-domain, is not a maturation-result. The eight non-resistant domains — the seven knowing-domains together with the dharma-domain — arise through continuity and karmic maturation rather than through obstructive material accumulation.

Hegel-clean working rendering:

> The living faculties are not mere matter: they are organic accumulations shaped by karmic result. Sound is excluded from this maturation-structure. The non-resistant domains are not spatial bodies, yet they are not causally empty; they arise through continuity and maturation. Thus the chapter now separates material growth, karmic result, and non-obstructive causal flow.

## 8. Logical Determination

VAK_1.36 gave material operation:

```text
cuts / is cut / is burned / weighs
```

VAK_1.37 now gives causal genesis:

```text
vipākaja
    karmic-resultant genesis

aupacayika
    organic accumulation / growth

naiḥṣyandika
    continuity / outflow

apratigha
    non-obstructive mode of domain-being
```

The structure is:

```text
Five internal sense faculties
    = vipākaja + aupacayika

Sound
    = not vipākaja

Eight non-resistant domains
    = naiḥṣyandika + vipākaja
```

The chapter is therefore distinguishing three kinds of causal determination:

```text
1. maturation-result
2. accumulative embodiment
3. continuity-flow
```

## 9. Interpretive Note

This verse is not a mere causal footnote. It tells us that domains are not only classified by object, faculty, materiality, and operation. They are also classified by genesis.

The five internal faculties are living bases. They are not simply external material objects. They are karmically matured and organically accumulated.

```text
sense faculty
    = living internal domain
    = maturation-result
    = accumulated organic basis
```

Sound is the counter-case. It is material, but not appropriated as organic faculty and not born as karmic maturation-result. This preserves the special status of sound already marked in VAK_1.34.

The eight non-resistant domains are subtler. They do not obstruct like material bodies, but they are still causally structured. They arise through continuity and maturation. That prevents a false opposition between material causality and mental freedom.

### Organon note

VAK_1.37 adds a genetic-causal layer to the sequence:

```text

    object-support and appropriation

VAK_1.35
    material constitution

VAK_1.36
    material operation

VAK_1.37
    causal genesis
```

This is foundationally strong because it distinguishes:

```text
embodiment
    = appropriation and accumulation

material operation
    = reciprocal activity and susceptibility

causal genesis
    = maturation, growth, and continuity
```

Hegel-clean:

```text
A domain is not known merely by what it is.
A domain is known by how it arises,
how it operates,
and whether it obstructs.
```

The verse therefore deepens dhātu from static domain into generated domain.
