# VAK_1.14

## 1. Sanskrit (Devanāgarī)

> इन्द्रियार्थास्त एवेष्टा दशायतनधातवः ।
>
> वेदनानुभवः संज्ञा निमित्तोद्ग्रहणात्मिका ॥

## 2. Sanskrit (IAST)

> indriyārthās ta eveṣṭā daśāyatanadhātavaḥ /
>
> vedanānubhavaḥ saṃjñā nimittodgrahaṇātmikā //

## 3. Padaccheda

| Pada | Form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| indriya-arthāḥ | nom.pl.m compound | the sense faculties and their objects |
| te eva | pronoun expression | these very ones |
| iṣṭāḥ | nom.pl.m | are accepted, designated |
| daśa-āyatana-dhātavaḥ | nom.pl.m compound | as the ten āyatanas and dhātus |
| vedanā | nom.sg.f | feeling, undergoing, receptivity |
| anubhavaḥ | nom.sg.m | experience, lived undergoing |
| saṃjñā | nom.sg.f | recognition, determination |
| nimitta-udgrahaṇa-ātmikā | nom.sg.f compound | whose essence is the grasping of marks |

## 4. Literal Translation

> These very sense faculties and their objects are accepted as the ten āyatanas and dhātus. Vedanā is experience, and saṃjñā is that whose nature is the apprehension of marks.

## 5. Philosophical Translation

> The sensory field constitutes the determinate domain of cognition. Vedanā is the receptive undergoing of appearance, while saṃjñā is the grasping of the determinate marks through which cognition becomes possible.
