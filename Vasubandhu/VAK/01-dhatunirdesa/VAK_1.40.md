## 4. Grammar

### daśa bhāvanayā heyāḥ

The ten are **heyāḥ**, to be abandoned, by **bhāvanā**, cultivation. These are the ten material/form-bearing domains established in VAK_1.35:

```text
five sense-faculty domains:
    cakṣus, śrotra, ghrāṇa, jihvā, kāya

five object-domains:
    rūpa, śabda, gandha, rasa, spraṣṭavya
```

They are not abandoned by seeing. Their transformation belongs to the gradual path of cultivation.

### pañca ca

The five sensory knowledge-domains are also abandoned by cultivation:

```text
cakṣurvijñāna-dhātu
śrotravijñāna-dhātu
ghrāṇavijñāna-dhātu
jihvāvijñāna-dhātu
kāyavijñāna-dhātu
```

This fits the earlier structure: the five sensory knowledge-domains are with vitarka/vicāra, but avikalpaka with respect to the higher mental vikalpa. Their defilement-structure is not primarily cut by seeing but transformed through cultivation.

### antyāḥ trayaḥ tridhā

The last three are:

```text
manodhātu
dharmadhātu
manovijñānadhātu
```

These are **tridhā**, threefold, with respect to abandonability:

```text
1. dṛṣṭiheya
   to be abandoned by seeing

2. bhāvanāheya
   to be abandoned by cultivation

3. aheya
   not to be abandoned
```

This is where the dharmadhātu becomes decisive. The last triad can include what is defiled, what is transformed through cultivation, and what is not to be abandoned at all.

### na dṛṣṭiheyam akliṣṭam

The undefiled is not to be abandoned by seeing. Seeing abandons false view and defiled construction; it does not abandon what is unafflicted.

### na rūpam

Form or materiality is not to be abandoned by seeing. Rūpa is transformed by cultivation, not cut by the direct seeing of truth.

### na api aṣaṣṭhajam

The phrase **a-ṣaṣṭha-jam** means what is not born from the sixth. The sixth is the mental faculty / manas. Thus the five sensory knowledges and non-mental formations are not dṛṣṭiheya. Abandonment by seeing applies especially to defiled mental constructions arising from the sixth domain.

## 5. Literal Translation

> Ten are to be abandoned by cultivation, and five also. The last three are threefold. The undefiled is not to be abandoned by seeing, nor form, nor what is not born from the sixth.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The ten material domains, and also the five sensory knowledge-domains, are abandoned through cultivation. The final triad — mind-domain, dharma-domain, and mental-knowledge-domain — is threefold: some is abandoned by seeing, some by cultivation, and some is not to be abandoned. What is undefiled, what is material, and what does not arise from the sixth domain are not abandoned by seeing.

Organon working rendering:

> The system now distinguishes modes of negation. Material and sensory structures are not abolished by insight alone; they are transformed through cultivation. The final mental-dharma triad alone has the full threefold structure: error to be cut by seeing, residue to be transformed by cultivation, and truth not to be abandoned. Seeing is not destruction of the real; it is the removal of defiled construction.

## 8. Logical Determination

VAK_1.39 added functional actuality. VAK_1.40 now adds soteriological negation:

```text
abandoned by cultivation
abandoned by seeing
not to be abandoned
```

The structure is:

```text
Ten material domains
    = bhāvanāheya

Five sensory knowledge-domains
    = bhāvanāheya

Last three domains
    = threefold:
        dṛṣṭiheya
        bhāvanāheya
        aheya
```

The decisive rule is negative:

```text
not dṛṣṭiheya:
    akliṣṭa
    rūpa
    aṣaṣṭhaja
```

Therefore **seeing** does not abolish materiality, undefiled dharma, or non-mental sensory arising. Seeing cuts defiled mental construction.

## 9. Interpretive Note

This verse is foundational for the path-logic of the chapter.

The dhātu-system is no longer merely descriptive. It is now ordered by the path:

```text
What must be abandoned by seeing?
What must be abandoned by cultivation?
What is not to be abandoned?
```

This is where the earlier Organon note on dharmadhātu becomes especially important. If dharmadhātu alone has real-domain status, then the path cannot mean the destruction of dharma. It means the removal of what obscures real determination.

### Seeing is not destruction

**Dṛṣṭi** here is not a destructive force applied to everything. Seeing abandons what is false, afflicted, and mentally constructed. It does not abandon the undefiled. It does not abandon material form merely as form. It does not abandon what is not born from the sixth domain.

### Cultivation is transformation

The ten material domains and five sensory knowledge-domains are bhāvanāheya. This means they are transformed through development, discipline, habituation, and path-practice. They are not eliminated by a single act of insight.

### The last triad is the full path-field

The last three — manas, dharmadhātu, manovijñāna — are threefold. They are where false view, cultivated transformation, and non-abandonable truth meet.

Organon sequence:

```text
1.34 object-support / appropriation
1.35 material constitution
1.36 material operation
1.37 causal genesis
1.38 modal status and acquisition
1.39 internality and functional actuality
1.40 path-negation / abandonability
```

Kant-Fichte-Hegel note:

```text
seeing
    = direct insight into principle

cultivation
    = genetic transformation of the formed system

not-to-be-abandoned
    = truth-status, the real-domain that must be preserved
```

The path is therefore not nihilistic. It is discriminative: it cuts illusion, cultivates transformation, and preserves truth.
