## 4. Grammar

### sapta sālambanāś cittadhātavaḥ

```text
sapta = seven
sa-ālambanāḥ = with object-support
citta-dhātavaḥ = mind-domains / knowing-domains
```

The seven **citta-dhātavaḥ** are the seven knowing or mind domains:

```text
cakṣurvijñānadhātu
śrotravijñānadhātu
ghrāṇavijñānadhātu
jihvāvijñānadhātu
kāyavijñānadhātu
manovijñānadhātu
manodhātu
```

These are **sālambana**: they have object-support. Their very operation is not sealed within themselves; they arise as knowing in relation to an object-support.

This continues VAK_1.32–1.33. The five sensory knowledge-domains are with vitarka/vicāra but are avikalpaka with respect to nirūpaṇa-anusmaraṇa-vikalpa. VAK_1.34 now adds that all seven mind/knowing domains are object-supported.

### ardhaṃ ca dharmataḥ

```text
ardhaṃ ca dharmataḥ
```

Literally: "and half from the dharma [domain]" or "and a part with respect to the dharma-domain."

The **dharmadhātu** is not homogeneous. A portion of the dharma-domain is also **sālambana**, because some dharmas are mental factors or cognitive determinations that take object-support. But not the whole dharma-domain is object-supported, since the dharma-domain also includes non-object-taking determinations such as unconditioned dharmas and other non-cognitive factors.

Thus the verse distinguishes:

```text
seven citta-dhātus
        = wholly sālambana

part of dharmadhātu
        = sālambana

not all of dharmadhātu
        = sālambana
```

### navānupāttāḥ

```text
nava anupāttāḥ = nine are unappropriated
```

**Upātta** means appropriated, taken up, organically possessed, incorporated into a living psycho-physical continuum. **Anupātta** means not so appropriated.

This is not a moral term. It is a structural term: whether a domain is taken up into the living body-stream as organic basis.

### te cāṣṭau śabdaś ca

```text
te ca aṣṭau śabdaḥ ca
```

The nine unappropriated domains are:

```text
the eight just indicated as not organically appropriated
        +
sound
```

The "eight" are best understood as the seven citta-dhātus together with the dharmadhātu. They are not material organic bases in the way sense-faculties can be. Sound is added because it is not appropriated as part of the living organism, even though it is a material object-domain.

### anye nava dvidhā

```text
anye nava = the remaining nine
dvidhā = twofold
```

The remaining nine domains are twofold: they may be appropriated or unappropriated depending on their occurrence.

These are the five sense-faculty domains plus the four non-sound object-domains:

```text
cakṣur-dhātu
śrotra-dhātu
ghrāṇa-dhātu
jihvā-dhātu
kāya-dhātu
rūpa-dhātu
gandha-dhātu
rasa-dhātu
spraṣṭavya-dhātu
```

The five faculties can be appropriated as living organic faculties. The object-domains, except sound, can be either appropriated or unappropriated according to whether they are part of the living body-stream or external material objects.

## 5. Literal Translation

> The seven mind-domains are with object-support, and so is a part of the dharma-domain. Nine are unappropriated: those eight and sound. The remaining nine are twofold.

Expanded literal rendering:

> The seven citta-domains possess object-support; and a portion of the dharma-domain does also. Nine domains are unappropriated — those eight together with sound. The other nine are of two kinds.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The seven domains of knowing are object-supported, and a portion of the dharma-domain also has object-support. Nine domains are not organically appropriated: the seven citta-domains together with the dharma-domain, plus sound. The remaining nine domains admit a twofold status, according as they are appropriated into a living continuum or remain unappropriated.

Hegel-clean working rendering:

> Knowing is not self-enclosed immediacy: the seven mind/knowing domains are essentially object-supported. The dharma-domain partly shares this object-relation. But object-support is not the same as organic appropriation. The verse therefore separates intentional support from living embodiment: some domains are object-related without being organically taken up, while the remaining domains stand in a twofold relation to appropriation.

## 8. Logical Determination

VAK_1.34 introduces two distinct axes of determination:

```text
1. Object-support
   sālambana / without object-support

2. Appropriation
   upātta / anupātta
```

These two axes must not be collapsed.

A domain can be object-supported without being appropriated. This is the crucial point. The seven citta-dhātus are **sālambana**, but they are also **anupātta**. They know by object-support, but they are not themselves material organic bases.

The structure is:

```text
Seven citta-dhātus
        ↓
object-supported
        ↓
unappropriated

Part of dharmadhātu
        ↓
object-supported
        ↓
counted with the unappropriated eight

Sound
        ↓
unappropriated object-domain

Remaining nine
        ↓
twofold: appropriated / unappropriated
```

This verse therefore moves from the internal logic of knowledge in VAK_1.32–1.33 to the relation between knowing, object-support, and embodiment.

## 9. Interpretive Note

This verse is easily mistaken for a dry scholastic classification, but it is doing something profound.

VAK_1.32 established that the five sensory knowledge-domains are accompanied by vitarka and vicāra. VAK_1.33 clarified that they are nevertheless avikalpaka with respect to the higher mental operations of nirūpaṇa and anusmaraṇa. VAK_1.34 now adds a further determination: the seven mind/knowing domains are **sālambana**, object-supported.

So the system is saying:

```text
knowing is articulated
        but not yet necessarily vikalpic

knowing is object-supported
        but not therefore organically appropriated

embodiment is a further determination
        not identical with object-relation
```

This is a very clean logical distinction.

### Object-support is not objecthood

**Ālambana** should not be flattened into "object" in the ordinary empirical sense. It is the support or basis upon which a knowing takes place. A cognition is not a private self-enclosed state; it is structurally supported by what it is directed toward.

Thus:

```text
citta-dhātu
        = not enclosed subjectivity
        = object-supported knowing-domain
```

This fits the developing Abhidharma logic: the knowing-domain is always relationally determined, but that relation is not yet the full conceptual mediation of vikalpa.

### Appropriation is embodiment

**Upātta** marks whether a domain is taken up into the living psycho-physical stream. The sense-faculties can be appropriated as organic bases. Sound cannot be appropriated in this way. The citta-dhātus and dharmadhātu are not material organic bases, and so are counted as unappropriated.

The remaining nine are twofold because they can appear either as part of the living organism or as external/objective material domains.

### Organon note

VAK_1.34 gives a new architectonic layer:

```text
VAK_1.32
        inner logical articulation:
        vitarka / vicāra

VAK_1.33
        mental differentiation:
        nirūpaṇa / anusmaraṇa / vikalpa

VAK_1.34
        relational embodiment:
        ālambana / upātta / anupātta
```

This is the beginning of a very precise distinction between:

```text
relation-to-object
        and
incorporation-into-body
```

For the Organon, that matters enormously. Object-support is not yet embodiment. Embodiment is a further appropriation-determination.

Hegel-clean:

```text
Knowing
        is not abstract self-identity.

Knowing
        is object-supported relation.

But object-supported relation
        is not yet organic possession.

Organic possession
        is appropriation into the living continuum.
```

So VAK_1.34 does not merely classify dhātus. It separates **intentional relation** from **organic embodiment**.
