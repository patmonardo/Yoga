# VAK_4.96

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

आनन्तर्याणि कर्माणि तीव्रक्लेशोऽथ दुर्गतिः ।
कौरवासंज्ञित्वं च मतमावरणत्रयम् ॥ ४.९६ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**ānantaryāṇi karmāṇi tīvrakleśo ’tha durgatiḥ |**  
**kauravāsaṃjñitvaṃ ca matam āvaraṇatrayam || 4.96 ||**

## 3. Padaccheda

`ānantaryāṇi | karmāṇi | tīvra-kleśaḥ | atha | durgatiḥ | kaurava | asaṃjñitvam | ca | matam | āvaraṇa-trayam`

Expanded classification:

`ānantaryāṇi karmāṇi, tīvrakleśaḥ, atha durgatiḥ kauravam asaṃjñitvaṃ ca — āvaraṇatrayaṃ matam.`

## 4. Grammar

- **ānantaryāṇi karmāṇi** — karmic activities whose result follows without an intervening birth.
- **tīvra-kleśaḥ** — intense affliction.
- **durgatiḥ** — bad destiny.
- **kaurava** — existence among the Kurus, especially Uttarakuru.
- **asaṃjñitvam** — the state of being non-percipient.
- **āvaraṇa-trayam** — the threefold obstruction.
- **matam** — held, regarded.

The source reading around `kauravāsaṃjñitva` is orthographically compressed; the intended classification is Kuru existence plus non-percipient existence under resultant obstruction.

## 5. Literal Translation

> The karmic activities of immediate consequence, intense affliction, and then bad destiny, existence among the Kurus, and non-percipient existence are held to constitute the threefold obstruction.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> Three obstructions are recognized: karmic obstruction, consisting in acts of immediate consequence; afflictive obstruction, consisting in intense affliction; and resultant obstruction, consisting in a bad destiny, birth among the Kurus, or non-percipient existence.

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

- **ānantarya-karma** — karma whose result follows immediately after death without another birth intervening.
- **karmāvaraṇa** — obstruction through karma.
- **kleśāvaraṇa** — obstruction through affliction.
- **vipākāvaraṇa** — obstruction through karmic result.
- **tīvra-kleśa** — affliction intensified enough to block Path-arising.
- **durgati** — bad destiny.
- **kaurava** — Kuru existence, especially Uttarakuru birth.
- **asaṃjñitva** — non-percipient existence.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

The three obstructions are located in three moments of one karmic continuum:

- **karma** — what the continuum has done;
- **kleśa** — what presently governs it;
- **vipāka** — what it has consequently become.

Bad destinies, Uttarakuru birth, and non-percipient existence belong together not because all are painful, but because each is structurally incompatible with the arising of the Path.

## 9. Logical Determination

The obstruction may lie:

- behind the present state as determining activity;
- within the present state as dominant affliction;
- as the present state itself, as resultant existence.

Thus `āvaraṇa` is not an external barrier. It is an internal determination of the continuum as past activity, present disposition, or resultant actuality.

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

VAK_4.95 distinguished projecting from completing karma. VAK_4.96 now introduces karma as closure of a path rather than merely production of a world.

The decisive criterion is functional:

`Can this continuum generate the Path?`

Pleasure and pain are secondary. Even a pleasant resultant existence may obstruct discriminative transformation.
