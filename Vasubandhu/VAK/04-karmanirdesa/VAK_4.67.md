# VAK_4d — VAkK_4.67

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

अशुभाः षडविज्ञप्तिर्द्विधैकस्तेऽपि कुर्वतः ।
द्विविधाः सप्त कुशलाः अविज्ञप्तिः समाधिजाः ॥ VAkK_4.67 ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

aśubhāḥ ṣaḍ avijñaptir dvidhaikaḥ te ’pi kurvataḥ /
dvividhāḥ sapta kuśalāḥ avijñaptiḥ samādhijāḥ // VAkK_4.67 //

Source text:

```text
aśubhāḥ ṣaḍavijñaptiḥ dvidhaikaḥ te 'pi kurvataḥ /
dvividhāḥ sapta kuśalāḥ avijñaptiḥ samādhijāḥ //
```

The kārikā classifies the ten karmapathas according to vijñapti and avijñapti. The compact syntax requires Bhāṣya confirmation, especially the precise reference of ṣaṭ, ekaḥ, and te.


It then distinguishes avijñapti produced from concentration.

## 5. Literal Translation

Of the unwholesome courses, six are non-manifest; one is twofold; they also belong to one who is performing them. The seven wholesome courses are twofold, while those born from concentration are non-manifest.

A more interpretive first-pass rendering:

The bodily and verbal courses of unwholesome action are differentiated according to whether they are manifest acts, retained non-manifest determinations, or both. The corresponding seven wholesome courses can possess both forms, whereas those produced through meditative concentration exist only as non-manifest karmic determinations.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

The verse shifts from asking which courses are wholesome or unwholesome to asking how a karmic course exists.

```text
vijñapti
    manifest action

avijñapti
    non-manifest karmic determination
```

Thus karmapatha is now classified not only by content but also by mode of realization.

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

### vijñapti

Manifestation, making-known, expressive action. In the karmic context it is bodily or verbal action insofar as it becomes outwardly expressed.

### avijñapti

Non-manifest determination. It is a real karmic determination that may persist as restraint, non-restraint, ethical continuity, or meditatively generated discipline even when no outward act is presently occurring.

### kurvat

One doing or presently performing. The expression distinguishes the agent actively carrying out a karmic course from retained avijñapti continuing beyond manifest performance.

### dvividha

Twofold, likely referring to vijñapti and avijñapti.

### samādhija

Born from concentration or generated by meditative integration. This refers to non-manifest restraint associated with concentration-born discipline.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

Only the first seven karmapathas can directly possess bodily or verbal manifestation:

```text
3 bodily
4 verbal
    =
7 potentially manifest courses
```

The three mental courses—abhidhyā, vyāpāda, and mithyādṛṣṭi—are mental bad conduct but were already stated in 4.65 not to be karma in the strict sense.

The verse therefore concentrates on seven unwholesome and seven wholesome bodily-verbal courses and examines them through:

```text
manifest action
non-manifest determination
```

The exact counting mechanism on the unwholesome side remains provisional until the Bhāṣya pass.

Wholesome abstention can exist as outwardly enacted discipline and/or retained non-manifest restraint. Concentration-born restraint is avijñapti only.

## 9. Logical Determination

The verse distinguishes content from mode of being.

```text
ethical quality
    wholesome / unwholesome

mode of realization
    manifest / non-manifest
```

The same karmic course can therefore be considered from two perspectives:

```text
what determination it is

how that determination exists
```

```text
vijñapti
    determination externalized

avijñapti
    determination retained beyond outward expression
```

Moral determination therefore exceeds visible behavior.

The samādhija avijñapti represents internalized ethical form:

```text
external discipline
    ↓
internalized stable determination
```

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

A moral-processing architecture must distinguish:

```text
1. orientation
2. intention
3. manifest execution
4. retained disposition
5. stable internalized restraint
```

The verse focuses on the final three.

```text
vijñapti
    = event

avijñapti
    = persistent ethical state

samādhija avijñapti
    = stabilized state generated by disciplined integration
```

The system must represent not only current output but latent permissions, prohibitions, commitments, restraints, and acquired non-restraints.

```text
manifest behavior
    = current output

avijñapti
    = persistent policy-state

samādhija restraint
    = policy made immanent through training
```
