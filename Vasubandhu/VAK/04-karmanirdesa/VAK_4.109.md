# VAK_4.109

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

जम्बूद्वीपे पुमानेव सम्मुखं बुद्धचेतनः ।
चिन्तामयं कल्पशते शेषमाक्षिपते हि तत् ॥ ४.१०९ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**jambūdvīpe pumān eva saṃmukhaṃ buddhacetanaḥ |**  
**cintāmayaṃ kalpaśate śeṣam ākṣipate hi tat || 4.109 ||**

## 3. Padaccheda

`jambū-dvīpe | pumān | eva | saṃmukham | buddha-cetanaḥ | cintā-mayam | kalpa-śate | śeṣam | ākṣipate | hi | tat`

The verse continues the account of `lakṣaṇa-karma` introduced in VAK_4.108.

## 4. Literal Translation

> Only in Jambūdvīpa, as a male, face-to-face with Buddhas, and with volition directed toward Buddhahood, does he perform that karma formed through reflection; for during a hundred kalpas he projects the remainder.

## 5. Philosophical Translation

> The karma producing the marks of a Buddha is undertaken only in Jambūdvīpa, in a specifically qualified human embodiment, in the direct presence of Buddhas, and through deliberate Buddha-oriented volition. Over one hundred kalpas, this reflected activity projects the remaining causal accumulation required for Buddha-form.

## 6. Technical Vocabulary

- **jambūdvīpa** — the privileged human locus of Buddha-directed karma.
- **saṃmukha** — face-to-face, in the direct presence of a Buddha.
- **buddha-cetanā** — volition teleologically directed toward Buddhahood.
- **cintā-maya** — formed through deliberate reflection.
- **kalpaśata** — one hundred kalpas.
- **ākṣipate** — projects or casts forward a future result.

## 7. Doctrinal Determination

Mark-producing karma is locally determined, embodiment-dependent, relationally mediated, intentionally specified, reflectively produced, temporally accumulated, and future-projecting.

The Buddha marks are not adventitious additions. They are the matured appearance of an immense, consciously organized karmic history.

## 8. Logical Determination

`MarkProducingKarma(agent)` requires:

- Jambūdvīpa as locus;
- qualified embodiment;
- direct presence of a Buddha;
- Buddha-directed volition;
- reflective formation.

Repeated over one hundred kalpas, it projects the remaining conditions of Buddha-form.

## 9. Light Organon Interpretation

The actual Buddha serves as exemplar, reflective Bodhisattva volition as mediating activity, and the future Buddha as projected result. The end exists first objectively, then subjectively as volition, and finally objectively again as Buddha-form.
