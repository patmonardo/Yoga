# VAK_4.98

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

सङ्घभेदस्त्वसामग्रीस्वभावो विप्रयुक्तकः ।
अक्लिष्टाव्याकृतो धर्मः सङ्घस्तेन समन्वितः ॥ ४.९८ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**saṃghabhedas tv asāmagrīsvabhāvo viprayuktakaḥ |**  
**akliṣṭāvyākṛto dharmaḥ saṃghas tena samanvitaḥ || 4.98 ||**

## 3. Padaccheda

`saṃgha-bhedaḥ | tu | asāmagrī-svabhāvaḥ | viprayuktakaḥ | akliṣṭa-avyākṛtaḥ | dharmaḥ | saṃghaḥ | tena | samanvitaḥ`

## 4. Grammar

- **saṃghabhedaḥ** — division of the Saṃgha.
- **asāmagrī-svabhāvaḥ** — having non-concord as its constitutive determination.
- **viprayuktakaḥ** — dissociated from mind and mental factors.
- **akliṣṭa-avyākṛtaḥ** — non-afflicted and ethically indeterminate.
- **dharmaḥ** — conditioned determination.
- **saṃghaḥ tena samanvitaḥ** — the Saṃgha is endowed with that state.

## 5. Literal Translation

> Division of the Saṃgha has non-concord as its self-nature. It is a mind-dissociated, non-afflicted, indeterminate determination. The Saṃgha is endowed with it.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> Saṃgha-division is constituted by the objective non-concord of the community. This divided state is a conditioned relational determination dissociated from any single mind, neither itself afflicted nor karmically determinate; it belongs to the Saṃgha that has become divided.

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

- **saṃghabheda** — division or rupture of the Saṃgha.
- **asāmagrī** — absence of concord or unified communal operation.
- **viprayuktaka** — a conditioned determination dissociated from mind.
- **akliṣṭa** — non-afflicted.
- **avyākṛta** — ethically indeterminate.
- **samanvita** — endowed with or connected with.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

The verse distinguishes:

- the afflicted, unwholesome activity of the divider;
- the objective divided condition produced in the Saṃgha.

The division itself is not the divider’s intention or false speech. It is a real collective determination borne by the Saṃgha. Because it is not greed, hatred, delusion, or intentional activity, it is classified as non-afflicted and indeterminate.

## 9. Logical Determination

Saṃgha-division is a relational whole that cannot be reduced to a private mental episode. The members remain, but their concordant unity has been negated.

Thus:

- causal agency belongs to the divider;
- the resultant relational state belongs to the collective;
- an unwholesome cause may produce an ethically indeterminate conditioned determination.

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

The Saṃgha exists as organized concord, not merely as a numerical aggregate. Division transforms the relations constituting the collective while leaving its physical members intact.

This gives a clean model of collective state:

```text
before: coordinated plurality functioning as one Saṃgha
after: opposed plurality functioning as divided communities
```
