# VAK_4.18

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
अनागम्ये प्रहाणाख्यौ तावानन्तर्यमार्गजौ ।
सम्प्रज्ञानस्मृती द्वे तु मनइन्द्रियसंवरौ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
anāgamye prahāṇākhyau tāv ānantaryamārgajau |
saṃprajñānasmṛtī dve tu manaindriyasaṃvarau ||
```

## 3. Source reference and caution

Source: `VAkK_4.18`.

The opening duals most naturally refer to the two mind-following restraints of VAK_4.17: meditation-born restraint and uncontaminated restraint. The exact scope of `anāgamye` and the doctrinal force of `prahāṇākhya` should be checked in the Bhāṣya.

## 5. Literal translation

> In the preliminary concentration, the two are called abandonment restraints; those two are born from the uninterrupted path. Clear comprehension and mindfulness are the two restraints of mind and of the faculties.

## 6. Cautious philosophical translation

> When associated with the preliminary concentration and the uninterrupted path by which defilements are abandoned, meditation-born and uncontaminated restraint are designated as restraints of abandonment. In another twofold classification, clear comprehension restrains the mind, while mindfulness restrains the sense faculties.

## 8. Two restraint systems

### Restraint arising from path-attainment

```text
meditation-born restraint
uncontaminated restraint

when functioning in abandonment:
    prahāṇa-saṃvara
```

### Restraint as cognitive discipline

```text
clear comprehension
    restraint of mind

mindfulness
    restraint of faculties
```

The verse therefore gives two distinct dual classifications rather than one undifferentiated list.

## 9. Doctrinal determination

The verse adds three determinations:

```text
1. the two mind-following restraints
   can be called abandonment restraints

2. they arise from the uninterrupted path

3. clear comprehension and mindfulness
   constitute mind-restraint and faculty-restraint
```

The chapter is thus extending restraint beyond disciplinary status into the structure of meditative cognition.

## 10. Logical determination

The same general term, `saṃvara`, is differentiated by both origin and function.

```text
By origin:
    born from formal undertaking
    born from meditation
    born from uncontaminated path

By function:
    abandonment of defilements
    restraint of mind
    restraint of sensory faculties
```

## 11. Light Organon interpretation

```text
restraint may be determined by:

    the path from which it arises
    the domain it regulates
    the function it performs
```

Here:

```text
uninterrupted path
    → abandonment

clear comprehension
    → regulation of mind

mindfulness
    → regulation of faculties
```
