# VAK_4.09 — Associated and Originative Ethical Determination

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
संप्रयोगेण तद्युक्ताः समुत्थानात् क्रियादयः ।
विपर्ययेणाकुशलं परमाव्याकृते ध्रुवे ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
saṃprayogeṇa tadyuktāḥ samutthānāt kriyādayaḥ |
viparyayeṇākuśalaṃ paramāvyākṛte dhruve ||
```

## 3. Source Caution

External source siglum: VAkK_4.9.

The verse continues the classification of ethical predicates begun in VAK_4.08. The likely structure is:

```text
wholesome in the ultimate sense
    liberation

wholesome intrinsically
    wholesome roots, hrī, apatrāpya

wholesome through association
    dharmas associated with those

wholesome through origination
    bodily/verbal action and related products
```

The final dual `paramāvyākṛte dhruve` most likely refers to the two permanent unconditioned dharmas other than liberation: ākāśa and apratisaṃkhyā-nirodha. This should be verified in the Bhāṣya pass.

## 5. Grammar

### `saṃprayogeṇa tadyuktāḥ`

Those associated with the intrinsically wholesome dharmas of VAK_4.08 are wholesome through association.

### `samutthānāt kriyādayaḥ`

Actions and related products are wholesome through the wholesome originating condition from which they arise.

### `viparyayeṇākuśalam`

The unwholesome is constituted conversely: intrinsically unwholesome factors, factors associated with them, and actions generated by them.

### `paramāvyākṛte dhruve`

The two permanent dharmas are indeterminate in the ultimate sense.

## 6. Literal Translation

Those associated with them are wholesome through association; actions and the rest are wholesome through their originating condition. The unwholesome is constituted conversely. The two permanent dharmas are indeterminate in the ultimate sense.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

Mental factors conjoined with intrinsically wholesome dharmas acquire goodness through association. Bodily and verbal actions and their related products acquire goodness from the wholesome condition that gives rise to them. Unwholesomeness is structured in the contrary manner. The two remaining permanent unconditioned dharmas are ethically indeterminate in the ultimate sense.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAK_4.08–4.09 establish four modes of wholesome determination:

```text
1. ultimate goodness
    mokṣa

2. intrinsic goodness
    wholesome roots, hrī, apatrāpya

3. associative goodness
    dharmas associated with intrinsically wholesome factors

4. originative goodness
    bodily/verbal actions generated from wholesome mental conditions
```

The unwholesome is arranged conversely.

## 10. Logical Determination

The verse differentiates ethical predication according to:

```text
what something is
what it is associated with
what it arises from
what ultimate status it possesses
```

Thus the same predicate, “wholesome,” belongs in distinct ways.

## 11. Relation to Karma

The verse shows how ethical determination passes from consciousness into activity:

```text
intrinsically wholesome mental factors
    → associated cognitive event
    → originating condition
    → bodily/verbal action
```

This is the precise role of `samutthāna`.

## 12. Interpretive Note

The kārikā creates a grammar of ethical predication. “Wholesome” is not one homogeneous quality but is assigned according to different modes of belonging.

## 13. Light Organon Interpretation

```text
good in itself
good through conjunction
good through production
good in the ultimate sense
```

The first-pass result is simply that ethical determination is systematically differentiated according to its mode of belonging.
