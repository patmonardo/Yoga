# VAkK 4.63

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```text
अन्ये नरकवेद्यान्यकामवेद्यं द्वयं विदुः ।
दृग्धेयं कृष्णमन्येऽन्यत्कृष्णशुक्लं तु कामजम् ॥ ४.६३ ॥
```

## Literal translation

> Other authorities understand the pair as, respectively, that which is to be experienced in hell and that which is to be experienced elsewhere in the desire-domain. Others call that which is abandoned by seeing “black,” while the remaining desire-domain karma is black-and-white.

## First alternative interpretation

The “pair” is:

```text
black karma
black-and-white karma
```

Some authorities distinguish them by the destination of their maturation:

```text
black
    → experienced in hell

black-and-white
    → experienced elsewhere in the desire-domain
```

Thus the classification is based on the type of result rather than simply on the ethical quality of the action.

## Second alternative interpretation

Other authorities distinguish the two according to how the corresponding karma is abandoned:

```text
desire-domain karma abandoned by seeing
    → black

the remaining desire-domain karma
    → black-and-white
```

### Dṛgdheya

```text
dṛk-dheya
    to be abandoned by seeing
```

This refers to what is abandoned through the Path of Seeing.

The remainder is desire-domain karma associated with what is abandoned through cultivation rather than seeing.

## Doctrinal significance

VAkK 4.60 gave the principal classification:

```text
unwholesome karma
    = black

wholesome form-domain karma
    = white

wholesome desire-domain karma
    = black-and-white
```

VAkK 4.63 records two alternative explanations of the first and third categories:

1. classification by **place of maturation**;
2. classification by **mode of abandonment**.

The verse therefore shows that the same karmic terminology could be interpreted according to different grounds of division.

## Technical vocabulary

- **narakavedya** — to be experienced in hell
- **anyakāmavedya** — to be experienced elsewhere in the desire-domain
- **dṛgdheya** — to be abandoned by seeing
- **kāmaja** — arising in or belonging to the desire-domain
- **kṛṣṇaśukla** — black-and-white

## Determinate result

VAkK 4.63 preserves two alternative classifications:

```text
by maturation:
    hell-result karma = black
    other desire-domain result karma = black-and-white

by abandonment:
    desire-domain karma abandoned by seeing = black
    the remaining desire-domain karma = black-and-white
```
