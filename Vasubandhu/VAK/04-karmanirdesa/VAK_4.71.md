## 5. Literal Translation

But false view is completed through delusion; the remaining courses are held to be completed through all three roots. Their bases are living beings and possessions, name-and-form, and name.

A slightly more explicit rendering:

False view reaches completion through delusion. The remaining unwholesome courses may be completed through greed, hatred, or delusion. Living beings, objects of possession, name-and-form, and name constitute the bases upon which these karmic courses operate.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

False view culminates specifically in delusion, since its defining defect is a false determination of moral reality. The remaining verbal courses may be completed from greed, hatred, or delusion. More generally, the ten courses act upon distinct objective supports: living beings, appropriable goods, embodied name-and-form, and linguistic name.

The verse completes a threefold architecture of failure:

```text
greed
    improper appropriation

hatred
    hostile negation

delusion
    false determination
```

It then adds the objective side:

```text
root
    why the course is generated

course
    what determinate action occurs

basis
    that upon which the course operates
```

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

### mithyādṛṣṭi

`mithyādṛṣṭi` means false view or wrong principial determination. It is not merely a mistaken opinion. In this context it is a morally effective denial or distortion of the structure of action and result.

### moha

`moha` is delusion, obscuration, or confused determination.

```text
moha
    general obscuring root

mithyādṛṣṭi
    determinate doctrinal or principial expression
```

### śeṣa

`śeṣa` means the remainder or what is left over. Here it denotes false speech, divisive speech, and idle or disconnected speech.

### adhiṣṭhāna

`adhiṣṭhāna` means standing upon, basis, objective support, or locus. A karmapatha is not only rooted in an affliction; it is directed toward or established upon some objective basis.

Provisional canonical translation:

```text
adhiṣṭhāna
    = objective basis
```

### sattva

`sattva` means living being or sentient being. Courses involving injury, deception, division, or hostility presuppose another living being as their basis.

### bhoga

`bhoga` means enjoyment, possession, usable good, or property. In this verse, possession or object of enjoyment is likely more useful than the abstract enjoyment.

### nāmarūpa

`nāmarūpa` means name-and-form. Here it likely indicates the embodied psycho-physical complex as an objective basis. The exact allocation remains for the Bhāṣya.

### nāma

`nāma` means name or linguistic designation. The verbal karmapathas operate through names, words, and linguistic determination.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

VAK_4.70–4.71 together provide the full provisional completion-root mapping.

### Completed through hatred

```text
killing
ill will
harsh speech
```

### Completed through greed

```text
taking what is not given
sexual misconduct
covetousness
```

### Completed through delusion

```text
false view
```

### Completed through any of the three roots

```text
false speech
divisive speech
idle or disconnected speech
```

The complete structure is therefore:

| Completion root | Karmapathas |
|---|---|
| Hatred | killing, ill will, harsh speech |
| Greed | theft, sexual misconduct, covetousness |
| Delusion | false view |
| Any of the three | false speech, divisive speech, idle speech |

False speech may arise from greed to acquire advantage, from hatred to injure another, or from delusion through confused conviction. Divisive speech and idle speech are similarly multi-rooted.

The second half introduces four supports:

```text
sattva
    living being

bhoga
    possession or object of enjoyment

nāmarūpa
    embodied name-and-form

nāma
    linguistic name
```

A provisional functional distribution is:

```text
living beings
    basis of harmful and interpersonal courses

possessions
    basis of appropriative courses

name-and-form
    basis of embodied or psycho-physical engagement

name
    basis or medium of verbal courses
```

The important doctrinal point is that a karmapatha requires not only an internal root but also an objective field upon which the course is completed.

## 9. Logical Determination

VAK_4.71 completes the action structure as a relation between three moments:

```text
subjective root
determinate course
objective basis
```

Or:

```text
Ground
    ↓
Activity
    ↓
Object-support
```

The root is the internal generative basis. The course is the determinate form taken by that root. The basis is that upon which the course operates.

```text
Root
    does not remain inward

it becomes determinate
as a course

the course reaches completion
upon an objective basis
```

False view is the purest expression of `moha` among the karmapathas because its defect lies in determination itself.

```text
greed
    corrupts appropriation

hatred
    corrupts negation

delusion
    corrupts knowing
```

The three remaining verbal courses can be completed by all three roots because speech is a mediating instrument.

```text
speech
    can serve appropriation
    can serve hostility
    can express delusion
```

The introduction of `adhiṣṭhāna` prevents the analysis from remaining purely psychological.

```text
root alone
    is not yet the completed act

root + course + objective basis
    constitutes determinate moral activity
```

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

VAK_4.71 yields a useful moral-processing tuple:

```text
MoralAct
    =
Root
    × Course
    × ObjectiveBasis
```

This indicates that all three dimensions are required for adequate classification.

```text
Root dimension
    Why is the process occurring?

Course dimension
    What type of action is being realized?

Basis dimension
    Upon what object or relation is it operating?
```

An agent model should therefore avoid classifications such as `action = lie` without also asking:

```text
root:
    greed, hatred, or delusion?

basis:
    which person, possession, embodied relation,
    or linguistic designation?

phase:
    preparation, completion, or aftermath?
```

The emerging architecture is:

```text
MoralProcess
    hasRoot
    hasPreparation
    realizesCourse
    hasObjectiveBasis
    hasCompletionRoot
    producesAftermath
    establishesPersistentState
```

VAK_4.70–4.71 together define three general failure modes:

```text
ImproperAppropriation
    lobha

HostileNegation
    dveṣa

FalseDetermination
    moha
```

The inclusion of `nāma` is especially important for agent architecture. Language is not merely descriptive; it is a medium in which karmic activity executes.

```text
name
    can represent

but name can also
    deceive
    divide
    injure
    disorder
```

Thus semantic production itself belongs within moral processing.
