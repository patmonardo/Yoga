# VAkK 4.61

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```text
धर्मक्षान्तिषु वैराग्ये चानन्तर्यपथाष्टके ।
या चेतना द्वादशधा कर्म कृष्णक्षयाय तत् ॥ ४.६१ ॥
```

## Literal translation

> The volition occurring in the dharma-patiences and in the eight uninterrupted paths of detachment is twelvefold; that karma serves for the destruction of black karma.

## The twelve volitions

The number twelve is obtained from:

```text
four dharma-patiences
+
eight uninterrupted paths of detachment
=
twelve
```

The four dharma-patiences are the dharma-knowledge patiences associated with the four truths. The eight uninterrupted paths are the first eight stages in the cultivation of detachment from the desire-domain.

## Cetanā as karma

The relevant karma is **cetanā**, volition. These supramundane volitions are without influxes and function toward the destruction of black karma.

## Doctrinal determination

The four dharma-patiences remove afflictions abandoned through seeing. The first eight uninterrupted paths progressively remove afflictions abandoned through cultivation. Their associated volitions are therefore said to operate:

```text
kṛṣṇa-kṣayāya
    for the destruction of black karma
```

This does not mean that every past unwholesome action is mechanically erased. The path eliminates the afflictive conditions that sustain the corresponding unwholesome karmic process.

## Technical vocabulary

- **dharmakṣānti** — dharma-knowledge patience on the Path of Seeing
- **vairāgya** — detachment
- **ānantarya-mārga** — uninterrupted path
- **cetanā** — volition
- **kṛṣṇa-kṣaya** — destruction of black karma

## Determinate result

VAkK 4.61 identifies twelve supramundane volitions:

```text
four dharma-patiences
eight uninterrupted paths of detachment
```

Their function is the destruction of black karma.
