# VAkK 4.60

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

अशुभं रूपकामाप्तं शुभं चैव यथाक्रमम् ।
कृष्णशुक्लोभयं कर्म तत्क्षयाय निरास्रवम् ॥ ४.६० ॥

## Sanskrit — IAST

*aśubhaṃ rūpakāmāptaṃ śubhaṃ caiva yathākramam /*
*kṛṣṇaśuklobhayaṃ karma tatkṣayāya nirāsravam // VAkK_4.60 //* 

## Padaccheda

- aśubham
- rūpa-āptam śubham
- kāma-āptam śubham
- ca eva
- yathākramam
- kṛṣṇam
- śuklam
- ubhayam
- karma
- tat-kṣayāya
- nirāsravam

The compressed middle is understood as three corresponding classes:

- aśubha karma
- rūpāpta śubha karma
- kāmāpta śubha karma

## Literal translation

> Unwholesome karma, wholesome karma belonging respectively to the form-domain and the desire-domain, are, in order, black, white, and both. Karma without influxes is for their destruction.

## The four kinds

### 1. Black karma

Unwholesome karma is called **black** because both its causal character and its maturation are unfavorable.

### 2. White karma

Wholesome karma belonging to the form-domain is called **white**. It is unmixed with the unwholesome karma characteristic of the desire-domain.

### 3. Black-and-white karma

Wholesome karma belonging to the desire-domain is called **both black and white**. This does not mean that one single mental act is simultaneously wholesome and unwholesome. Rather, desire-domain karmic existence contains mixed wholesome and unwholesome courses whose results occur within the same continuum.

### 4. Neither black nor white

Karma without influxes is not classified as black or white because it does not generate a further samsaric maturation. Its function is **tat-kṣayāya**, “for the destruction of those karmas,” where **tat** refers to the preceding three classes.

## Technical vocabulary

- **aśubha** — unwholesome
- **rūpāpta** — belonging to the form-domain
- **kāmāpta** — belonging to the desire-domain
- **kṛṣṇa** — black
- **śukla** — white
- **ubhaya** — both
- **nirāsrava** — without influxes
- **kṣaya** — destruction, exhaustion

## Doctrinal determination

The first three karmas remain within conditioned karmic production:

- black
- white
- black-and-white

The fourth has a different function:

- neither black nor white
- destroys the preceding karmic classes

It is therefore not merely a fourth moral color. It belongs to the path that terminates karmic accumulation.

## Determinate result

VAkK 4.60 completes the fourfold classification:

- unwholesome karma = black
- form-domain wholesome karma = white
- desire-domain wholesome karma = black-and-white
- karma without influxes = neither black nor white and destroys the preceding three
