# VAK_4.106

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

दूषणं मातुरर्हन्त्या नियतिस्थस्य मारणम् ।
बोधिसत्त्वस्य शैक्षस्य सङ्घायद्वारहारिका ॥ ४.१०६ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**dūṣaṇaṃ mātur arhantyā niyatisthasya māraṇam |**  
**bodhisattvasya śaikṣasya saṃghāyadvārahārikā || 4.106 ||**

## 3. Padaccheda

`dūṣaṇam | mātuḥ | arhantyāḥ | niyati-sthasya | māraṇam | bodhisattvasya | śaikṣasya | saṃghāya | dvāra-hārikā`

## 4. Literal Translation

> Violation of one’s mother when she is an arhat; killing a bodhisattva established in certainty; killing a trainee; and depriving the Saṃgha of its means of support—

The list continues in VAK_4.107, where these are identified as acts analogous to immediate-consequence karma and destruction of a stūpa is named as the fifth.

## 5. Philosophical Translation

> Certain acts reproduce the structure and gravity of the five immediate-consequence karmas without technically being those acts: violating a mother who is also an arhat, killing a bodhisattva whose awakening is assured, killing a noble trainee, and cutting off the Saṃgha’s material means of communal life.

## 6. Technical Vocabulary

- **dūṣaṇa** — violation, defilement, grave dishonoring.
- **niyatistha** — established in certainty; fixed in an assured trajectory.
- **bodhisattva** — a being progressing toward Buddhahood.
- **śaikṣa** — a noble trainee whose Path remains incomplete.
- **dvārahārikā** — depriving of access, means, or support.
- **ānantarya-sabhāga** — an act analogous to immediate-consequence karma.

## 7. Doctrinal Determination

The verse extends karmic gravity beyond the strict five ānantarya acts. These analogues attack equivalent exceptional fields:

- an arhat mother combines benefactor- and quality-fields;
- a fixed bodhisattva bears assured future Buddhahood;
- a śaikṣa embodies the Path in active development;
- the Saṃgha depends upon material conditions for communal operation.

Thus technical identity and structural analogy must be distinguished.

## 8. Logical Determination

A karmic field may be determined through:

- prior benefit;
- actual qualities;
- assured future;
- communal function.

The field is therefore not exhausted by its present external attributes.

## 9. Light Organon Interpretation

The bodhisattva and śaikṣa show that a state may carry a determinate future as one of its present logical properties. Karma responds not merely to actuality but to assured becoming and active development.
