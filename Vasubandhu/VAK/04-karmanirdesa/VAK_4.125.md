# VAK_4.125

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
धर्मदानं यथाभूतसूत्राद्यक्लिष्टदेशना ।
पुण्यनिर्वाणनिर्वेधभागीयं कुशलं त्रिधा ॥ ४.१२५ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
dharmadānaṃ yathābhūtasūtrādyakliṣṭadeśanā |
puṇyanirvāṇanirvedhabhāgīyaṃ kuśalaṃ tridhā || 4.125 ||
```

This verse first defines:

```text
dharmadāna
    the gift of Dharma
```

It then divides wholesome activity according to three distinct orientations:

```text
1. puṇya-bhāgīya
    participating in merit

2. nirvāṇa-bhāgīya
    participating in or conducive to nirvāṇa

3. nirvedha-bhāgīya
    participating in penetrative realization
```

This is an important transition from the **instrument of activity** to the **direction in which wholesome activity develops the instrument**.

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `dharmadānam` | nominative neuter singular | the gift of Dharma |
| `yathābhūta` | compound member | according to what has become; as it really is |
| `sūtrādi` | compound | sūtras and the other teachings |
| `akliṣṭa` | adjective | unafflicted, uncontaminated |
| `deśanā` | nominative feminine singular | exposition, instruction |
| `puṇya` | compound member | merit |
| `nirvāṇa` | compound member | nirvāṇa |
| `nirvedha` | compound member | penetration, breakthrough |
| `bhāgīyam` | adjective | sharing in, conducive to, belonging to the side of |
| `kuśalam` | nominative neuter singular | the wholesome |
| `tridhā` | indeclinable | threefold |

The definition is nominal:

```text
yathābhūta-sūtrādy-akliṣṭa-deśanā
    =
dharmadānam
```

The verse does not define Dharma-giving as merely speaking about Buddhism. It requires three determinations:

```text
content
    sūtra and the other teachings

accuracy
    yathābhūta

purity
    akliṣṭa
```

---

## 5. Literal translation

> The unafflicted exposition of the sūtras and the other teachings as they really are is the gift of Dharma. The wholesome is threefold: belonging to merit, belonging to nirvāṇa, and belonging to penetration.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> The gift of Dharma consists in communicating the teachings—sūtra and the other legitimate bodies of instruction—without distortion and without the exposition being governed by affliction. Teaching becomes a true gift only when the meaning is presented according to reality and the act of presentation is itself uncontaminated. Wholesome activity may then be distinguished according to its developmental direction: some improves conditioned existence through merit, some turns the continuum toward nirvāṇa, and some belongs directly to the breakthrough by which reality is penetrated.

The verse therefore distinguishes:

```text
correct representation of Dharma

pure transmission of Dharma

transformative destination of wholesome activity
```

---

# 7. `Dharmadāna`

```text
dharma
    determination
    teaching
    liberating Law

dāna
    giving
    making available
```

A material gift transfers an object that supports life.

The gift of Dharma makes available a determination capable of transforming understanding and conduct.

```text
material gift
    supports an existing continuum

Dharma gift
    can reorient the continuum itself
```

This is why teaching belongs within the analysis of giving.

Something is relinquished and made available:

```text
knowledge
interpretation
method
discernment
path-direction
```

The teacher does not lose Dharma by communicating it, but does relinquish exclusive possession of its use and meaning.

---

# 8. Dharma as an instrument given

The gift is not merely a collection of propositions.

The hearer receives an instrument for:

```text
recognizing suffering

discriminating its grounds

restraining activity

cultivating antidotes

penetrating reality
```

Thus Dharma-giving provides a **higher-order instrument**.

```text
ordinary gift
    gives an object for use

Dharma gift
    gives a means of determining use,
action, and transformation
```

It supplies not merely content inside the hearer’s existing ToolChain, but material from which the ToolChain itself can be reconstructed.

---

# 9. `Sūtrādi`

```text
sūtra
    discourse
    scriptural teaching

ādi
    and the others
```

The `ādi` indicates that Dharma transmission is not restricted to one textual class.

It may include, depending upon the scholastic context:

```text
sūtra
vinaya
abhidharma
explanation
interpretive instruction
practical guidance
```

The kārikā’s immediate point is inclusive:

> The gift concerns the legitimate teaching in its articulated forms, not merely one isolated sentence or genre.

The exact scope of `ādi` should be confirmed from the Bhāṣya.

---

# 10. `Yathābhūta`

```text
yathā
    according as

bhūta
    become
    actual
    real

yathābhūta
    according to actuality
    as it really is
```

This is the first criterion of Dharma-giving.

The teaching must not merely be eloquent, persuasive, or emotionally attractive.

It must correspond to the determination taught.

```text
representation
    must track
the actual structure of its object
```

A distorted presentation may transmit words associated with Dharma without transmitting Dharma properly.

---

# 11. Truthful transmission

`Yathābhūta` excludes such distortions as:

```text
changing the teaching to gain approval

suppressing essential qualifications

presenting a provisional statement as ultimate

confusing one doctrinal category with another

inventing certainty where the source is compressed
```

This last point is especially relevant to our own method.

When the kārikā is underdetermined, fidelity requires:

```text
state what the verse securely says

identify the ambiguity

reserve its resolution for the Bhāṣya
```

That methodological restraint is itself part of transmitting the Dharma `yathābhūtam`.

---

# 12. Representation and reality

The teacher necessarily uses representations:

```text
words
sentences
examples
classifications
schemas
```

But the representation must point beyond itself toward the determination.

```text
text
    is not identical with
the meaning

yet the text must be organized
so that the meaning can appear
```

The Dharma gift therefore has the same problem we identified for GDSL.

A determinate language is necessary, but its fixed expressions must not be mistaken for the complete Art or actuality they express.

---

# 13. `Deśanā`

```text
diś
    to point out
    indicate
    show a direction

deśanā
    exposition
    instruction
    showing
```

Teaching is literally a pointing-out.

The teacher cannot directly insert realization into another continuum.

The teacher can:

```text
identify distinctions

exhibit relations

remove misunderstandings

indicate a path of examination

provide practices
```

The hearer must then perform the corresponding cognition and cultivation.

Thus deśanā is an instrument that enables another instrument to operate.

---

# 14. Teaching does not transfer realization

This distinguishes Dharma-giving from physical transfer.

```text
material gift:
    object passes into another’s possession

Dharma gift:
    conditions are supplied
    for another’s own understanding
```

One cannot hand over:

```text
insight
concentration
disjunction
```

as completed objects.

One can give the teaching through which another agent may produce them.

```text
teacher
    provides the program and orientation

hearer
    must execute, test, and cultivate
```

The gift preserves the agency of the recipient.

---

# 15. `Akliṣṭa`

```text
a-
    not

kliṣṭa
    afflicted
    contaminated
    governed by kleśa

akliṣṭa
    unafflicted
```

Correct content is not sufficient.

The mode and motive of exposition must also be uncontaminated.

One may teach accurate doctrine while being governed by:

```text
desire for fame

financial calculation

rivalry

conceit

desire to dominate

hostility toward an opponent
```

The words may be correct while the teaching-act remains afflicted.

The Kośa therefore examines both:

```text
semantic correctness

agential purity
```

---

# 16. Truth can be used untruthfully

An accurate statement can be inserted into a corrupt practical relation.

For example, it can be used to:

```text
humiliate a listener

establish superiority

create dependency

win a factional contest

conceal one’s own confusion
```

The proposition remains accurate in abstraction, but the deed of teaching has acquired an afflicted form.

Thus Dharma-giving requires:

```text
true content
    +
unafflicted execution
```

This repeats the chapter’s fundamental doctrine:

> Karma is not determined by content alone but by the complete instrument through which the content is enacted.

---

# 17. The teacher as ToolChain

A Dharma exposition involves:

```text
āśaya
    underlying purpose

cetanā
    intention to teach

prajñā
    discrimination of meaning

smṛti
    retention of teaching

vāc
    verbal formulation

kṣetra
    the hearer or audience

deśanā
    executed presentation
```

The teacher is therefore not a passive conduit through which a fixed text passes.

The teacher must actively align:

```text
meaning
representation
audience
occasion
purpose
```

Dharma-giving is a complete agential Pipeline.

---

# 18. The Shell of teaching

Every exposition requires a Shell:

```text
a determinate vocabulary

an order of presentation

a selected example

a level of abstraction

a finite beginning and ending
```

This introduces necessary fixity.

But good teaching does not absolutize that Shell.

The presentation must remain responsive to:

```text
the meaning

the recipient’s capacity

the question being asked

the distinction between secure doctrine and interpretation
```

Thus higher pedagogical Art governs the determinate instructional program.

---

# 19. `Kuśalaṃ tridhā`

The second half changes from the definition of Dharma-giving to a classification of the wholesome:

```text
kuśala
    wholesome
    skillful
    causally healthy

tridhā
    in three ways
```

The division is not principally by external deed-type.

It is by **direction of development**.

The same general form of activity—giving, discipline, cultivation, or teaching—can occupy different places according to what it conditions.

```text
What world does this wholesome activity construct?

What freedom does it prepare?

What realization does it immediately support?
```

---

# 20. `Bhāgīya`

```text
bhāga
    part
    share
    side

bhāgīya
    belonging to a share
    participating in
    conducive to
```

The term does not mean that the wholesome deed is numerically a fragment of nirvāṇa.

It means that it belongs to the causal side or developmental stream leading toward a specified result.

```text
activity
    participates in
a larger teleological sequence
```

Thus the classification concerns orientation and causal membership.

---

# 21. `Puṇya-bhāgīya`

```text
puṇya
    merit
    fortunate karmic efficacy

puṇyabhāgīya
    conducive to merit
    participating in meritorious result
```

This wholesome activity principally produces favorable conditioned maturation:

```text
human prosperity

heavenly rebirth

beauty

honor

pleasurable experience

supportive circumstances
```

It improves the configuration of saṃsāric existence.

```text
bad world-generation
    →
better world-generation
```

But it does not necessarily challenge the fundamental appropriation and ignorance sustaining the cycle.

---

# 22. Merit-directed wholesomeness

Puṇya-bhāgīya activity can be excellent and indispensable.

It establishes:

```text
trust

stability

resources

ethical habit

freedom from gross remorse

conditions favorable to Dharma
```

Yet its objective may remain:

```text
future happiness

fortunate birth

social harmony

celestial enjoyment
```

The Agent becomes better configured while remaining an Agent operating within conditioned projection.

---

# 23. `Nirvāṇa-bhāgīya`

```text
nirvāṇa
    extinction
    cessation
    release

nirvāṇabhāgīya
    belonging to the side of nirvāṇa
    conducive to release
```

This category turns beyond improvement of conditioned existence.

Its activity contributes to weakening the structures that reproduce saṃsāra.

```text
not merely:
    produce a better result

but:
    reduce the need for karmic result-production
```

It includes wholesome roots and practices oriented toward eventual liberation rather than merely favorable rebirth.

The exact technical extension of this category should be recovered from the Bhāṣya.

---

# 24. From merit to liberation

The distinction is qualitative:

```text
puṇyabhāgīya
    reorganizes conditioned experience favorably

nirvāṇabhāgīya
    reorients the continuum toward cessation
```

The first may improve the Agent’s world.

The second begins to question the binding architecture through which that world continually has to be projected.

```text
better projection
    versus
release from compelled projection
```

---

# 25. `Nirvedha`

```text
nir-√vyadh
    to pierce through
    penetrate
    break open

nirvedha
    penetration
    breakthrough
```

This should not be confused with `nirveda`, disenchantment, though the concepts can be related in broader Buddhist usage.

Here `nirvedha` indicates penetrative realization: cognition breaking through the ordinary conceptual enclosure and entering direct comprehension of the truths.

```text
representation of reality
    →
preparation for insight
    →
penetration of reality
```

---

# 26. `Nirvedha-bhāgīya`

```text
nirvedhabhāgīya
    belonging to penetration
    conducive to breakthrough
```

This is the most technically advanced of the three classes.

It concerns wholesome determinations belonging closely to the transition into direct realization.

Traditionally, the exact stages and members of this class are unfolded in the Path chapter; the kārikā here only establishes the category.

At the present level we can safely say:

```text
puṇya-bhāgīya
    favorable conditioned maturation

nirvāṇa-bhāgīya
    general orientation toward release

nirvedha-bhāgīya
    proximate preparation for penetrative realization
```

---

# 27. Why distinguish nirvāṇa and penetration?

One may orient life toward liberation long before possessing the concentrated discriminative capacity immediately preceding breakthrough.

For example:

```text
faith in liberation

hearing Dharma

ethical commitment

renunciation

initial cultivation
```

may belong to the nirvāṇa-directed stream.

Nirvedha-bhāgīya wholesomeness is more specifically organized toward penetration.

```text
release as objective
    ≠
immediate cognitive breakthrough
```

The first establishes the destination.

The second prepares the decisive act of passage.

---

# 28. A threefold developmental architecture

The three classes can be represented:

## 1. World improvement

```text
puṇyabhāgīya

constructs a favorable conditioned world
```

## 2. World-transcending orientation

```text
nirvāṇabhāgīya

turns the continuum toward release
```

## 3. Penetrative transition

```text
nirvedhabhāgīya

prepares cognition to break through
the structure of deluded appearance
```

This is not simply a ranking of good deeds.

It is a classification of three functional destinations of wholesome formation.

---

# 29. Program, Pipeline, and Feature

This verse illuminates our architecture sharply.

## Feature

```text
recognizes the actual determination:
    yathābhūta
```

## Program

```text
organizes the teaching:
    sūtrādi-deśanā
```

## Pipeline

```text
moves meaning through
teacher, representation, and hearer
```

## Agent-quality

```text
ensures the process is:
    akliṣṭa
```

And the resulting wholesome execution can be tagged by developmental destination:

```text
MeritPipeline

NirvanaPipeline

PenetrationPipeline
```

But these are functional representations. The higher Art remains the power to determine which Pipeline is appropriate and how its finite representation must be revised.

# 30. GDSL can represent the teaching, not replace knowing

GDSL might encode:

```text
teach(
    source = Sutra,
    interpretation = Yathabhuta,
    contamination = None,
    target = Recipient
)
```

This is useful.

It can record constraints, provenance, transformations, and expected effects.

But the representation cannot by itself guarantee:

```text
that the meaning has been understood

that the interpretation is true

that the teacher is unafflicted

that the hearer has penetrated the determination
```

Those require actual discriminative and agential operations.

```text
representation of knowing
    is not
the knowing represented
```

The higher Art appears precisely in maintaining this distinction.

---

# 31. Dharma-giving as transfer of executable possibility

The teacher does not transfer a completed mental state.

The teacher transfers an executable possibility:

```text
a distinction that can be understood

a practice that can be undertaken

a contradiction that can be recognized

a path that can be traversed
```

The hearer receives something analogous to a Program, but must instantiate it within a distinct continuum.

```text
same teaching

different recipient history

different features

different execution

different resulting formation
```

Dharma transmission is therefore reproducible without being mechanically identical.

---

# 32. The technical manual reveals the Agent again

The text appears to say simply:

```text
teach the scriptures correctly and purely

wholesome action has three classes
```

But underneath it has specified:

```text
a truth-sensitive evaluator

a representation generator

a contamination monitor

a recipient-sensitive interface

a developmental classifier
```

That is already an Agent architecture.

The “manual” can state the constraints, but only an Agent can integrate them into a living act of exposition.

---

# 33. Doctrinal determination

The verse establishes:

1. Dharma-giving is a genuine form of giving;
2. it consists in exposition of sūtra and the other teachings;
3. the teaching must correspond to actuality;
4. the teaching-act must be unafflicted;
5. semantic correctness and purity of motivation are distinct requirements;
6. wholesome activity is divided threefold by developmental orientation;
7. merit-conducive wholesomeness produces favorable conditioned maturation;
8. nirvāṇa-conducive wholesomeness turns the continuum toward release;
9. penetration-conducive wholesomeness belongs closely to breakthrough;
10. the technical boundaries of the latter two categories remain for Bhāṣya and Path-chapter refinement.

# 35. Organon interpretation

The verse separates three powers of the higher Art:

```text
represent actuality truthfully

transmit representation without corrupt purpose

orient execution toward a determinate transformation
```

The Dharma Shell is a finite exposition.

The Dharma Pipeline carries meaning through teacher and hearer.

The Dharma Program supplies repeatable distinctions and practices.

But the truth of the operation lies in whether these determinations become actual cognition.

```text
Shell
    exposes the teaching

Pipeline
    mediates the teaching

Program
    organizes the teaching

Agent
    understands, evaluates,
    transmits, and transforms through it
```

And the threefold wholesome classification tells us that not every successful execution has the same objective:

```text
some improve the operating environment

some reorient the system toward emancipation

some prepare the decisive breakthrough
```
