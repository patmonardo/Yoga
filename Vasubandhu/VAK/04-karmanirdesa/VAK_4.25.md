# VAK_4.25

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
विज्ञप्त्यैवान्वितः कुर्वन् मध्यस्थो मृदुचेतनः ।
त्यक्तानुत्पन्नविज्ञप्तिरविज्ञप्त्यार्यपुद्गलः ॥
```

## Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
vijñaptyaivānvitaḥ kurvan madhyastho mṛducetanaḥ |
tyaktānutpannavijñaptir avijñapty āryapudgalaḥ ||
```

## Source reference and caution

Source: `VAkK_4.25`.

The verse distinguishes the manifest act from the persistent unmanifest karmic form. It presents two opposed cases:

```text
madhyastha with weak intention
    vijñapti without avijñapti

āryapudgala
    avijñapti without present vijñapti
```


## Literal translation

> An intermediate person who acts with weak intention is endowed only with manifest action. A noble person whose manifest action has ceased or has not arisen is endowed with unmanifest action.

## Philosophical translation

> When an agent standing between restraint and non-restraint acts through an intention lacking sufficient force, the act remains merely manifest and produces no enduring unmanifest formation. Conversely, a noble person may possess the unmanifest restraint of the path even when its corresponding manifest action has ceased or has never arisen.

## Technical analysis

### `madhyastha`

The `madhyastha` is the person established in neither restraint nor non-restraint.

```text
saṃvarastha
    established in restraint

asaṃvarastha
    established in non-restraint

madhyastha
    established in neither
```

The intermediate person may perform wholesome or unwholesome acts without acquiring either standing practical form.

### `mṛducetana`

```text
mṛdu
    weak; mild; lacking force

cetanā
    intention; volitional determination
```

Weak intention is insufficient to generate persistent `avijñapti`.

```text
weak intention
    → manifest act
    → no persistent unmanifest formation
```

Thus occasional action does not by itself constitute the agent as restrained or established in non-restraint.

### `vijñaptyaiva anvitaḥ`

The restrictive `eva` gives the exact conclusion:

```text
vijñapti only
    no avijñapti
```

Manifest performance and persistent karmic form are therefore distinguishable.

### `tyaktānutpannavijñapti`

The compound indicates two possible conditions:

```text
tyakta-vijñapti
    manifest action has ceased or been abandoned

anutpanna-vijñapti
    manifest action has not arisen
```

In either case, the noble person may still possess `avijñapti`.

### `āryapudgala`

The noble person possesses the unmanifest restraint generated by the noble path. This possession does not depend on a simultaneous bodily or verbal manifestation.

```text
noble path
    → uncontaminated avijñapti

present outward act
    not required
```

## Crossed relation

| Agent | Vijñapti | Avijñapti |
|---|---:|---:|
| intermediate, weakly intending | present | absent |
| noble person | ceased or unarisen | present |

Therefore:

```text
vijñapti
    does not necessarily generate avijñapti

avijñapti
    does not require continuing vijñapti
```

## Doctrinal determination

1. A weakly intended act by an intermediate person remains `vijñapti` alone.
2. The noble person can possess `avijñapti` without a present corresponding `vijñapti`.
3. Manifest action and persistent karmic form are distinct.
4. `Cetanā` mediates whether a manifest act establishes a continuing karmic form.

## Relation to VAK_4.24

VAK_4.24 established that `asaṃvara` is a positive determinate non-restraint rather than the simple absence of restraint.

VAK_4.25 proves the distinction by introducing the intermediate person:

```text
not-saṃvara
    divides into

asaṃvara
    determinate non-restraint

madhyastha
    non-possession of either standing form
```

Thus `asaṃvara` is not the contradictory negation of `saṃvara` in the broadest sense. It is one determinate species within the field outside restraint.

## Logical determination

The verse distinguishes three moments:

```text
cetanā
    determining activity

vijñapti
    manifest occurrence

avijñapti
    persistent formal determination
```

Cetanā determines whether the manifest event remains transient or becomes a continuing practical form.

```text
weak activity
    event without standing form

noble-path activity
    standing form without present event
```

## Light Organon interpretation

The practical truth of action is not exhausted by sensible manifestation.

```text
vijñapti
    appearance of action

avijñapti
    persistence of action as form

cetanā
    ground of their unity or separation
```

The noble person demonstrates that practical form may persist without a corresponding outward act, while the intermediate person demonstrates that an outward act need not establish a persistent form.
