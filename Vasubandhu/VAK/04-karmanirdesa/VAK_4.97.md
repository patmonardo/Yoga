# VAK_4.97

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

त्रिषु द्वीपेष्वानन्तर्यं षण्ढादीनां तु नेष्यते ।
अल्पोपकारालज्जित्वाच्छेषे गतिषु पञ्चसु ॥ ४.९७ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**triṣu dvīpeṣv ānantaryaṃ śaṇḍhādīnāṃ tu neṣyate |**  
**alpopakārālajjitvāc cheṣe gatiṣu pañcasu || 4.97 ||**

## 3. Padaccheda

`triṣu | dvīpeṣu | ānantaryam | śaṇḍha-ādīnām | tu | na | iṣyate | alpa-upakārāt | alajjitvāt | śeṣe | gatiṣu | pañcasu`

## 4. Grammar

- **triṣu dvīpeṣu** — in the three continents.
- **ānantaryam** — immediate-consequence karma.
- **śaṇḍhādīnām** — of eunuchs and related cases.
- **na iṣyate** — is not accepted.
- **alpopakārāt** — because of little benefit.
- **alajjitvāt** — because of absence of moral inhibition.
- **śeṣe** — in the remainder.
- **gatiṣu pañcasu** — throughout the five destinies.

## 5. Literal Translation

> Immediate-consequence karma occurs in the three continents, but it is not accepted for eunuchs and the like, because of little benefit and shamelessness. The remaining acts occur throughout the five destinies.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The five immediate-consequence karmas arise only among human beings of the three continents and are not technically constituted in eunuchs and comparable cases. Their full determination requires a sufficiently developed relation of benefit and moral responsibility. Other grave karmic activities may occur throughout all five destinies.

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

- **tri-dvīpa** — Jambūdvīpa, Pūrvavideha, and Aparagodānīya.
- **ānantarya** — karma of immediate consequence.
- **śaṇḍha** — a traditional embodied category excluded from the complete technical classification.
- **upakāra** — benefit, assistance, or constitutive service.
- **lajjā** — moral inhibition or ethical shame.
- **pañca-gati** — the five destinies.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

The verse specifies the conditions under which karmic obstruction is instantiated:

- a qualified locus: human existence in the three continents;
- a qualified agent;
- a qualified field and relation;
- completed intentional activity.

The visible deed alone does not determine the complete karmic status. The same external act may differ according to agent, field, prior benefit, intention, and locus.

## 9. Logical Determination

Karma is not an isolated predicate attached to an event. It is activity determined through an agent–field–relation whole.

For complete classification one must ask:

- who acts;
- against whom;
- what relation binds them;
- what benefit has been received;
- what intention governs the act;
- what world supports its occurrence.

Thus `visible act ≠ complete karma`.

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

The three human continents support both Path-entry and the most radical karmic obstruction. Capacity for liberation and capacity for ānantarya belong to the same intensified form of moral agency.

In parental killing, the produced being negates the beneficent ground of its own produced existence. The enormity is therefore reflexive, not merely physical.
