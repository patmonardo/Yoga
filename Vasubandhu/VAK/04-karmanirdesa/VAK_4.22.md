# VAK_4.22

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
असंवरस्थः शुभयाशुभया संवरे स्थितः ।
अविज्ञप्त्यान्वितो यावत् प्रसादक्लेशवेगवान् ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
asaṃvarasthaḥ śubhayāśubhayā saṃvare sthitaḥ |
avijñaptyānvito yāvat prasādakleśavegavān ||
```

## 3. Source reference and caution

Source: `VAkK_4.22`.

The compressed parallel construction is:

```text
one established in non-restraint
    may be endowed with wholesome avijñapti

one established in restraint
    may be endowed with unwholesome avijñapti
```

The duration is governed by:

```iast
yāvat prasāda-kleśa-vega-vān
```

> for as long as the force of faith or defilement remains powerful.

The respective correspondence is strongly suggested by word order:

```text
asaṃvarasthaḥ
    śubhayā avijñaptyā anvitaḥ

saṃvare sthitaḥ
    aśubhayā avijñaptyā anvitaḥ
```

## 5. Literal translation

> One established in non-restraint may be endowed with wholesome avijñapti, and one established in restraint with unwholesome avijñapti, for as long as he possesses the force of faith or defilement.

## 6. Philosophical translation

> A stable ethical orientation does not prevent a contrary occasional determination from arising. One established in non-restraint may temporarily generate wholesome non-manifest action through a powerful movement of faith, while one established in restraint may generate unwholesome non-manifest action through a powerful defilement. The contrary determination lasts only while that motivating force remains active.

## 7. Stable position and occasional determination

The verse distinguishes:

```text
established ethical system
    saṃvara or asaṃvara

from

occasional contrary avijñapti
```

Thus:

```text
asaṃvara-system
    does not exclude every wholesome determination

saṃvara-system
    does not exclude every unwholesome determination
```

The stable system remains one thing; the occasional action-trace is another.

## 8. One established in non-restraint

```iast
asaṃvarasthaḥ śubhayā avijñaptyā anvitaḥ
```

> One established in non-restraint is endowed with wholesome avijñapti.

This person has a stable unwholesome orientation, but may undergo a strong wholesome impulse.

The verse does not say that this immediately destroys the non-restraint system.

Rather:

```text
stable non-restraint
    +
temporary wholesome avijñapti
```

can coexist.

## 9. One established in restraint

```iast
aśubhayā saṃvare sthitaḥ avijñaptyā anvitaḥ
```

> One established in restraint is endowed with unwholesome avijñapti.

Likewise, a person possessing restraint may undergo a sufficiently forceful defilement.

Again, the verse distinguishes:

```text
possession of restraint

from

temporary contrary karmic determination
```

Possessing a vow-system does not entail that every subsequent mental event is wholesome.

## 10. `prasāda`

```text
prasāda
    clarity
    serene confidence
    faith
```

Here it is the wholesome motivating force.

A strong movement of `prasāda` can generate wholesome avijñapti even in one established in non-restraint.

The term should not be reduced merely to assent to a proposition. It indicates an affectively and cognitively powerful wholesome orientation.

## 11. `kleśa`

```text
kleśa
    affliction
    defilement
```

Here it is the unwholesome motivating force.

A sufficiently powerful defilement can generate unwholesome avijñapti even in one established in restraint.

## 12. `vega`

```text
vega
    force
    momentum
    intensity
    powerful impulse
```

The avijñapti continues:

```iast
yāvat ... vegavān
```

> only as long as the motivating force remains powerful.

This is not the indefinite persistence of an established restraint-system.

It is a temporary determination dependent upon continuing motivational intensity.

## 13. Two layers of feature structure

The verse gives a particularly clear typed structure:

```text
[ ESTABLISHED-STATUS
    restraint | non-restraint ]

[ OCCASIONAL-AVIJNAPTI
    wholesome | unwholesome ]

[ MOTIVATING-FORCE
    faith | defilement ]

[ DURATION
    while-force-persists ]
```

The values are constrained but not collapsed into one another.

For example:

```text
[ STATUS       non-restraint
  AVIJNAPTI    wholesome
  CAUSE        strong-faith
  DURATION     force-dependent ]
```

and:

```text
[ STATUS       restraint
  AVIJNAPTI    unwholesome
  CAUSE        strong-defilement
  DURATION     force-dependent ]
```

This is exactly why “typed feature logic for karmic continua” is a useful architectural analogy.

## 14. Non-identity of person and state

The verse prevents a simplistic classification:

```text
person in restraint
    = wholly wholesome

person in non-restraint
    = wholly unwholesome
```

Instead, the continuum supports multiple determinations with distinct types and persistence conditions.

```text
standing disposition
    ≠
every occurring determination
```

The bearer remains classified by an established system while temporarily carrying a contrary avijñapti.

## 15. Relation to VAK_4.21

VAK_4.21 examined the `madhyastha`, the person established in neither system.

VAK_4.22 now examines persons already established in one of the two opposed systems:

```text
VAK_4.21
    neither restraint nor non-restraint
    may acquire occasional avijñapti

VAK_4.22
    restraint or non-restraint bearer
    may acquire contrary occasional avijñapti
```

Thus occasional avijñapti is not confined to the middle person.

## 16. Persistence condition

Established restraint and non-restraint persist according to their own acquisition and abandonment conditions.

The contrary occasional avijñapti persists according to another rule:

```text
motivating force remains
    → avijñapti continues

motivating force ceases
    → occasional avijñapti ceases
```

So the verse introduces a distinct duration constraint.

## 17. Doctrinal determination

The verse establishes:

```text
1. one established in non-restraint
   may possess wholesome avijñapti

2. one established in restraint
   may possess unwholesome avijñapti

3. the contrary avijñapti
   does not necessarily replace
   the established ethical system

4. it continues only while
   strong faith or strong defilement persists
```

## 18. Light Organon interpretation

A stable determination does not exhaust the possible states of its bearer.

```text
underlying system
    may remain constant

while

a contrary local determination
    temporarily arises
```

The local determination has its own cause and duration rule.

So:

```text
identity of ethical position
    contains
the possibility of contrary episodic determination
```

No heavier reconstruction is needed here.
