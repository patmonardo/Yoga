# VAK_4.17

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
प्रातिमोक्षान्विता अष्टौ ध्यानजेन तदन्वितः ।
अनास्रवेणार्यसत्त्वाः अन्त्यौ चित्तानुवर्तिनौ ॥
```

A sandhied reading of the opening may be written:

```sanskrit
प्रातिमोक्षान्वितावष्टौ
```

but the electronic source separates:

```iast
prātimokṣānvitā aṣṭau
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
prātimokṣānvitā aṣṭau dhyānajena tadanvitaḥ |
anāsraveṇāryasattvāḥ antyau cittānuvartinau ||
```

## 3. Source reference and caution

Source: `VAkK_4.17`.

The verse distributes the three restraints identified in VAK_4.13 among their possessors:

```text
prātimokṣa restraint
meditation-born restraint
uncontaminated restraint
```

The syntax is compressed, especially `dhyānajena tadanvitaḥ`. The precise antecedent and force of `tad-anvitaḥ` should be confirmed in the Bhāṣya.

## 5. Literal translation

> The eight are endowed with prātimokṣa restraint. One possessing meditation-born restraint is endowed with that. Noble beings are endowed with uncontaminated restraint. The last two follow the mind.

## 6. Cautious philosophical translation

> The eight disciplinary classes possess prātimokṣa restraint. Meditation-born restraint belongs to one established in the corresponding meditative condition, while uncontaminated restraint belongs to noble beings. The latter two restraints arise and persist in dependence upon the corresponding state of mind.

## 7. `prātimokṣānvitā aṣṭau`

The “eight” refers back to the nominal eightfold division of prātimokṣa restraint introduced in VAK_4.14.

```text
eight named disciplinary classes
    → possess prātimokṣa restraint
```

## 8. `anvita`

```text
anu + ita
    accompanied by
    connected with
    endowed with
```

The restraint is not identical with the person. It is a dharma with which the person’s continuum is associated.

## 9. Meditation-born restraint

`dhyānajena` means “through or with meditation-born restraint.”

```text
one possessing the relevant meditation
    → possesses meditation-born restraint
```

The full range of meditative attainments should not yet be specified from this verse alone.

## 10. Uncontaminated restraint

```iast
anāsraveṇa āryasattvāḥ
```

> Noble beings are endowed with uncontaminated restraint.

Thus:

```text
uncontaminated restraint
    belongs to noble beings
```

## 11. `antyau`

`antyau`, “the last two,” refers to:

```text
meditation-born restraint
uncontaminated restraint
```

These are said to be `cittānuvartinau`, “following the mind.”

## 12. `cittānuvartin`

```text
citta
    mind

anuvartin
    following
    accompanying
    conforming to
```

A restrained interpretation is:

```text
when the corresponding mind is present,
the restraint is present
```

The exact temporal and possessive details are developed in the following verses.

## 13. Contrast with prātimokṣa restraint

```text
prātimokṣa restraint
    grounded in formal undertaking

meditation-born and uncontaminated restraint
    follow the corresponding mental state
```

The first line is inferred from VAK_4.15; VAK_4.17 explicitly applies `cittānuvartinau` only to the latter two.

## 14. Three restraint–bearer relations

```text
prātimokṣa restraint
    → eight disciplinary classes

meditation-born restraint
    → possessor of meditation

uncontaminated restraint
    → noble beings
```

And:

```text
meditation-born restraint
uncontaminated restraint
    → follow mind
```

## 15. Relation to `anuvartaka`

Earlier:

```text
anuvartaka
    continuing consciousness sustaining action
```

Here:

```text
cittānuvartin
    restraint following consciousness
```

The direction of dependence differs and the two terms should not be collapsed.

## 16. Empirical sequence

```text
VAK_4.13
    three kinds of restraint

VAK_4.14–15
    internal classification of prātimokṣa

VAK_4.16
    technical names of restraint

VAK_4.17
    bearers of the three restraints
    and mind-dependence of the last two
```

## 17. Light Organon interpretation

The safe logical result is:

```text
a determination may persist
in different ways
according to its ground
```

```text
formal undertaking
    → prātimokṣa restraint

meditative consciousness
    → meditation-born restraint

uncontaminated consciousness
    → uncontaminated restraint
```

No stronger identification of citta with transcendental subjectivity is required here.
