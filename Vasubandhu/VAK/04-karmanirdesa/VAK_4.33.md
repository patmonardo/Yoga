# VAK_4.33

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
मिथ्याचारातिगर्ह्यत्वात्सौकर्यादक्रियाप्तितः ।
यथाभ्युपगमं लाभः संवरस्य न संतते ॥
```

## Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
mithyācārātigarhyatvāt saukaryād akriyāptitaḥ |
yathābhyupagamaṃ lābhaḥ saṃvarasya na saṃtate ||
```

## Source reference and caution

Source: `VAkK_4.33`.

The first pāda is compressed and should be checked against the Bhāṣya. The secure conclusion is that restraint is acquired according to the precise content of the undertaking and does not automatically extend beyond it.

## Literal translation

> Because sexual misconduct is especially blameworthy, because of practicability, and because acquisition occurs through abstention, the acquisition of restraint is according to the undertaking; it does not extend beyond it.

The first half remains provisional pending the Bhāṣya.

## Philosophical translation

> A restraint is not generated by status alone, nor does one accepted prohibition automatically produce every other restraint. Since ethical restraint consists in intentionally assumed abstention, its karmic extent corresponds precisely to the content of the undertaking. What is accepted becomes a standing restraint; what is not accepted is not silently included.

## Technical analysis

### `yathābhyupagamaṃ lābhaḥ saṃvarasya`

The acquisition of restraint follows the undertaking itself:

```text
undertakes one precept
    → acquires that restraint

undertakes several precepts
    → acquires those restraints

undertakes the complete set
    → acquires the complete restraint
```

### `na saṃtate`

The restraint does not automatically spread from one accepted rule to every other possible rule.

```text
undertaking:
    determinate

acquisition:
    correspondingly determinate
```

### `abhyupagama`

`abhyupagama` is deliberate acceptance or undertaking. The extent of karmic acquisition is fixed by what is actually accepted, not merely by title, social status, or community membership.

### Restraint and mere inactivity

Three cases must be distinguished:

```text
1. no harmful act occurs because no occasion arose
2. a harmful act is desired but temporarily blocked
3. a harmful act is intentionally renounced through undertaking
```

Only the third constitutes vow-restraint in the strict sense.

```text
absence of action
    is a negative fact

undertaken abstention
    is a positive karmic form
```

### Relation to VAK 4.31

VAK 4.31 reports the view that all upāsakas acquire the whole lay restraint and are called partial practitioners according to how much they preserve. VAK 4.33 presents the stricter alternative:

```text
partial undertaking
    → partial acquisition
```

This suggests a critical correction of the received explanation.

## Doctrinal determination

1. Restraint is acquired through intentional abstention.
2. Its content is fixed by the actual undertaking.
3. It does not automatically extend to unaccepted rules.
4. Practical status alone does not substitute for determinate karmic acquisition.

## Light Organon interpretation

The verse establishes an identity of measure between the content of intention and the content of acquired form:

```text
extent of undertaking
    =
extent of restraint acquired
```

A vow binds because it is determinate. Its limit is internal to its content.
