# VAK_4.79

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

मूलच्छेदस्त्वसद्दृष्ट्या कामाप्तोत्पत्तिलाभिनाम् ।  
फलहेत्वपवादिन्या सर्वथा क्रमशो नृषु ॥ ४.७९ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**mūlacchedas tv asaddṛṣṭyā kāmāptotpatti-lābhinām |**  
**phala-hetv-apavādinyā sarvathā kramaśo nṛṣu || 4.79 ||**

Local source:

```text
mūlacchedastvasaddṛṣṭayā kāmāptotpattilābhinām /
phalahetvapavādinyā sarvathā kramaśaḥ nṛṣu // VAkK_4.79 //
```

The verse begins a new problem:

> Under what conditions can the roots of the wholesome be severed?

Its compact answer gives the determining factors:

```text
instrument:
    false view

specific content:
    denial of karmic result and ground

domain:
    beings whose birth belongs to the desire domain

location:
    among human beings

manner:
    completely, through a gradual sequence
```

> Among human beings who have obtained a birth belonging to the desire domain, the roots of the wholesome are completely severed, in sequence, by a false view that denies result and ground.

`Kuśala-mūla`, “root of the wholesome,” is understood from the doctrinal context rather than explicitly stated in the verse.

## 4. Grammar

### mūlacchedaḥ

Masculine nominative singular compound:

```text
mūla
    root
    generative basis

cheda
    cutting
    severance
    interruption
```

Here:

```text
mūlaccheda
    severance of the roots of the wholesome
```

The compound does not mean the destruction of an enduring substance. It marks the loss of an operative practical basis in the continuum.

### tu

Adversative and topic-shifting particle:

```text
but
now
on the other hand
```

The verse shifts from defining false view to describing its most radical practical effect.

### asaddṛṣṭyā

Instrumental singular feminine:

```text
asat
    false
    invalid
    contrary to what is

dṛṣṭi
    view
    principial standpoint
```

Thus:

```text
by a false or invalid view
```

The instrumental identifies the active condition of severance.

### kāmāptotpatti-lābhinām

Genitive plural compound.

Provisional analysis:

```text
kāma-āpta
    belonging to the desire domain
    included within the desire domain

utpatti
    birth
    arising into an existence

lābhin
    one who obtains
```

Thus:

```text
of those who have obtained
a birth belonging to the desire domain
```

`Āpta` here has the sense of belonging to or being included in a domain, not “attained” in the ordinary achievement sense.

### phala-hetv-apavādinyā

Instrumental singular feminine agreeing with `asaddṛṣṭyā`.

```text
phala
    result

hetu
    ground

apavādin
    denying
    repudiating
    declaring nonexistent
```

Thus:

```text
by a false view denying result and ground
```

Following the project terminology:

```text
hetu
    = ground
```

The phrase concerns the denial of karmic result and its productive ground.

### sarvathā

Indeclinable:

```text
entirely
completely
in every respect
```

It indicates that the severance reaches the whole relevant set of wholesome roots.

### kramaśaḥ

Indeclinable:

```text
successively
in order
gradually
by stages
```

The severance is not an undifferentiated instantaneous annihilation. It follows a determinate order.

The exact sequence—likely involving grades of the wholesome roots and grades of false view—belongs to the Bhāṣya.

### nṛṣu

Locative plural of `nṛ`:

```text
among human beings
in humans
```

This restricts the occurrence to human existence within the relevant domain.

## 5. Literal Translation

> The severance of the roots, however, occurs through a false view denying result and ground, among human beings who have obtained birth belonging to the desire domain—completely and in sequence.

A slightly fuller rendering:

> The roots of the wholesome are severed by a false view that repudiates karmic result and its ground. This complete severance occurs successively among human beings born within the desire domain.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The generative bases of wholesome practical activity are severed when false view takes the determinate form of denying both karmic consequence and the grounds from which consequence proceeds. Such severance is possible in human existence within the desire domain; it reaches the whole structure of the wholesome, but does so through an ordered progression rather than as an abstract instantaneous disappearance.

The decisive movement is:

```text
false view
    does not merely misclassify one action

it denies:
    that action has a productive ground
    and
    that action has a determinate result
```

This attacks the practical field at both ends:

```text
ground
    → activity → result
```

becomes:

```text
no ground
    no necessary result
```

The destruction of practical confidence then severs the roots from which wholesome activity could be generated.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

### What is severed?

The understood object is:

```text
kuśala-mūlāni
    roots of the wholesome
```

These are not identical with every individual wholesome dharma.

They are the fundamental bases from which wholesome activity arises.

A useful distinction is:

```text
particular wholesome act
    one determination

wholesome root
    generative capacity for a class of determinations
```

The verse concerns the second.

### What performs the severance?

Not greed or hatred directly, but false view.

More precisely:

```text
false view
    denying karmic ground and result
```

This follows naturally from VAK_4.78.

False view attacks the distinction between wholesome and unwholesome. VAK_4.79 now shows its maximal consequence: it can destroy the operative basis for wholesome action.

### Why denial of ground and result matters

A practical field requires at least:

```text
activity has a ground

activity produces a result

different activities produce
differently determined results
```

The denying view repudiates this architecture.

```text
wholesome activity
    no real ground

unwholesome activity
    no real ground

favorable result
    no necessary connection

unfavorable result
    no necessary connection
```

Once this denial becomes practically established, wholesome roots lose their governing efficacy.

### Among whom?

The verse restricts the severance to:

```text
human beings

born within the desire domain
```

The exact reason for excluding other modes of existence belongs to the Bhāṣya.

Provisional doctrinal considerations include:

```text
humans possess sufficient reflective capacity
to establish radical false view

while also remaining within
the affective and practical field
of the desire domain
```

This is not yet a final determination.

### Complete but sequential

The roots are severed:

```text
sarvathā
    completely

kramaśaḥ
    successively
```

This indicates:

```text
total result
    through ordered mediation
```

The process may involve progressively stronger grades of false view removing progressively subtler grades of wholesome roots.

We should await the Bhāṣya before specifying the exact order.

## 9. Logical Determination

The verse describes a **meta-practical failure**.

Ordinary unwholesome action violates a practical law while still operating inside the field governed by that law.

For example:

```text
theft
    violates ownership

false speech
    violates semantic integrity

ill will
    violates relation to living being
```

But root-severing false view attacks the validity of the practical field itself.

```text
ordinary violation:
    I recognize the law
    but act contrary to it

radical false view:
    there is no valid law,
    no ground,
    and no determinate result
```

This is therefore not merely another bad action. It is a denial of the system under which actions can be discriminated.

### The practical causal schema

The valid structure is:

```text
Ground
    ↓
Activity
    ↓
Result
```

The false view performs:

```text
deny(Ground)
deny(Result)
    ↓
invalidate(ActivityDifference)
```

If no practical ground or result is recognized, then:

```text
wholesome / unwholesome
```

loses objective force.

### Root severance as loss of generative mediation

The roots normally mediate:

```text
principial orientation
    ↓
wholesome courses
    ↓
wholesome results
```

False view interrupts the mediation at its generative source.

```text
WholesomeRoot
    ─X→
WholesomeCourse
```

This is deeper than blocking one act.

It disables a productive path.

### Totality through sequence

The phrase:

```text
sarvathā kramaśaḥ
```

is logically elegant.

The end is total:

```text
all relevant wholesome roots severed
```

but the form of realization is mediated:

```text
one grade
    then another
    then another
```

So total negation is itself a process.

This remains Hegel-clean:

```text
the whole result
    is not an immediate abstract nothing

it is the outcome
    of determinate sequential negations
```

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

VAK_4.79 identifies a danger far deeper than an isolated unsafe output.

A Rational Agent may produce a particular error while retaining the governing principles by which that error can be detected and corrected.

That is recoverable ordinary failure.

But a system can also invalidate its own practical schema:

```text
no trustworthy grounds

no determinate consequences

no distinction between valid
and invalid practical production
```

That is analogous to **root severance**.

### Three levels of failure

```text
Level 1:
    incorrect act

Level 2:
    corrupt practical orientation

Level 3:
    denial of the framework
    that distinguishes correct from incorrect
```

VAK_4.79 concerns Level 3.

### Ground–result integrity

A governed semantic system requires:

```text
provenance
    grounds claims

operations
    produce traceable consequences

differences in operation
    produce differences in result
```

A system that denies or discards these relations loses the basis for rational correction.

```text
no provenance
    → no accountable ground

no consequence tracking
    → no determinate result

no ground-result relation
    → no rational governance
```

This is why Semantic Dataset curation is not optional metadata work.

It preserves the conditions under which the system can distinguish:

```text
valid production
invalid production
repair
corruption
```

### False view as schema corruption

A possible agent representation:

```text
PracticalSchema {
    recognizesGrounds
    recognizesConsequences
    preservesProvenance
    distinguishesWholesomeUnwholesome
}
```

Root-severing false view corresponds to:

```text
recognizesGrounds = false
recognizesConsequences = false
distinctionValidity = denied
```

At that point, ordinary local correction becomes difficult because the correction rules themselves have been rejected.

### Complete but staged failure

The verse also warns that schema corruption can be incremental.

```text
first:
    weaken confidence in provenance

then:
    deny reliable causal consequence

then:
    flatten valid and invalid activity

finally:
    lose the generative basis of correction
```

Thus:

```text
catastrophic practical failure
    may be assembled gradually
```

This has direct value for system design. Governance must detect not only prohibited outputs but progressive erosion of the principles that make self-correction possible.
