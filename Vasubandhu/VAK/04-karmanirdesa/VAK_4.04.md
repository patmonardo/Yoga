# VAK_4.04 — Grounds and Arising of Non-Manifest Action

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
त्रिविधामलरूपोक्तिवृद्ध्यकुर्वत्पथादिभिः ।
क्षणादूर्ध्वमविज्ञप्तिः कामाप्तातीतभूतजा ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
trividhāmala-rūpokti-vṛddhy-akurvat-pathādibhiḥ |
kṣaṇād ūrdhvam avijñaptiḥ kāmāptātīta-bhūtajā ||
```

## 3. Source Caution

External source siglum: VAkK_4.4.

The transmitted first pāda is highly compressed:

```iast
trividhāmalarūpoktivṛddhayakurvatpathādibhiḥ
```

A provisional separation is:

```iast
trividha-amala-rūpa-ukti-vṛddhi-akurvat-patha-ādibhiḥ
```

It appears to collect several grounds adduced for non-manifest action:

```text
statement of threefold pure form
increase or accumulation
an action-path while one is not presently acting
and related grounds
```

This segmentation is suitable for the initial kārikā pass but must be checked against the Bhāṣya before becoming canonical.

## 5. Grammar

### `trividhāmala-rūpokti-vṛddhy-akurvat-pathādibhiḥ`

The compound terminates in instrumental plural `-bhiḥ`, giving the force:

```text
by ...
through ...
on the grounds of ...
```

The verse appears to enumerate doctrinal or scriptural grounds for admitting `avijñapti`.

`ukti` means statement or declaration. `vṛddhi` means increase or growth. `akurvat` is the present active participle of √kṛ with privative `a-`: one not doing or not presently acting. `patha` is most probably connected with `karmapatha`, a course or path of action.

### `kṣaṇād ūrdhvam avijñaptiḥ`

```text
kṣaṇāt
    after the moment

ūrdhvam
    subsequently / beyond

avijñaptiḥ
    non-manifest action
```

The phrase indicates that avijñapti continues or arises beyond the initiating manifest moment. It is therefore not identical with the momentary bodily or verbal manifestation itself.

### `kāmāptātīta-bhūtajā`

This feminine nominative singular compound qualifies `avijñaptiḥ`:

```text
kāma-āpta
    belonging to the desire domain

atīta-bhūta-jā
    born from past great material determinations
```

The precise scope of the compound should be checked in the commentary, but its general determination is clear: desire-domain avijñapti is generated in dependence upon material determinants that are already past.

## 6. Literal Translation

Through the statement concerning threefold pure form, through increase, through an action-path while one is not presently acting, and through related grounds, [non-manifest action is established]. Beyond the moment, non-manifest action belonging to the desire domain is born from past great material determinations.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

Non-manifest action is admitted on several grounds: scriptural classification of pure form, the continued increase of karmic efficacy, and the persistence of an action-path even when no manifest deed is presently being performed. After the initiating moment, desire-domain non-manifest action arises in dependence upon material determinants that have already passed.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAK_4.02 introduced two modes:

```text
vijñapti
    manifest action

avijñapti
    non-manifest action
```

VAK_4.04 now gives the initial reason why the second category is needed.

```text
manifest action
    occurs in a moment
    and perishes

karmic determination
    may continue
    may increase
    may constitute an action-path
    even without a present manifest deed
```

Therefore:

```text
karma cannot be exhausted
by the manifest moment alone
```

The verse also begins the material genealogy of avijñapti:

```text
past great material determinations
    → subsequent avijñapti
```

## 10. Logical Determination

The argument has the structure:

```text
present manifestation
    is momentary

but:
    efficacy persists or increases
    an action-path remains attributable
    no current deed need be visible

therefore:
    a non-manifest determination is posited
```

This is not yet a final explanation of karmic continuity. It is the first formal statement of the problem that avijñapti is intended to solve.

## 11. Interpretive Note

The decisive phrase is:

```text
kṣaṇād ūrdhvam
    beyond the moment
```

The category of avijñapti opens precisely where manifest action ceases to be present. The theory therefore distinguishes:

```text
the event of action
    from

the continuing determination established by action
```

This should not yet be interpreted as an enduring substance. The verse instead describes a subsequent conditioned determination generated from prior material conditions.

## 12. Light Organon Interpretation

At the first-pass level, the structure is:

```text
vijñapti
    manifest event

avijñapti
    subsequent non-manifest determination

relation
    the latter is generated from conditions established by the former
```

This begins a theory of how a momentary act can establish a determination extending beyond its own appearance. Deeper identification with Principle, Ground, or Path-Knowledge should wait until the full sequence of kārikās has been traversed.
