# VAkK 4.54

```text
तीव्रक्लेशप्रसादेन सातत्येन च यत्कृतम् ।
```


The compound `tīvrakleśaprasādena` compresses two alternatives:

```text

    through intense affliction

tīvreṇa prasādena
    through intense confidence or serene faith
```

## Literal translation

> Whatever action is performed through intense affliction or intense confidence, and continuously, and in a field of qualities, is determinate; and so is that which involves the killing of either of one’s parents.

## Philosophical translation

> Karma becomes fixed in its maturation when it is generated with exceptional force: through powerful affliction, through powerful confidence, through sustained repetition, through action directed toward an exceptionally qualified field, or through the grave act of killing one’s mother or father.

The verse identifies the conditions under which an action ceases to be merely one causal possibility among others and becomes `niyata`, karmically determinate.

## Relation to VAkK 4.50–4.53

The preceding verses asked:

```text
What is determinate karma?
When will it mature?
Who can produce it?
What continuum does its result belong to?
```

VAkK 4.54 asks:

```text
What makes karma determinate?
```

The answer is not one single factor. Determinacy can arise from:

```text
intensity of motivation
continuity of performance
excellence of the field
exceptional gravity of the act
```

Thus karmic fixity is produced by the total causal structure of action.

## Intense affliction — tīvra-kleśa

```text
tīvra kleśa
    powerful greed
    powerful hatred
    powerful delusion
```

An action rooted in a very intense affliction possesses exceptional causal force.

```text
unwholesome quality
    + exceptional intensity
        → determinate maturation
```

The force of the volitional ground strengthens the karmic trajectory.

## Intense confidence — tīvra-prasāda

`Prasāda` includes clarity, serene confidence, faith, devotional trust, and lucid receptivity.

An action performed through exceptionally strong confidence or devotion may likewise become determinate.

```text
intense affliction
    can fix unwholesome karma

intense confidence
    can fix wholesome karma
```

The ethical direction differs, but the principle of causal intensification is shared.

## Continuity — sātatya

```text
sātatya
    uninterruptedness
    continuity
    repeated cultivation
```

An action repeatedly performed becomes karmically stabilized.

```text
single performance
    may remain weak or defeasible

continuous performance
    reinforces disposition
    consolidates the causal pattern
```

Repeated action transforms the continuum that acts:

```text
act
    → disposition

repeated act
    → stabilized character

stabilized character
    → determinate karmic production
```

## Field of qualities — guṇa-kṣetra

```text
guṇa
    quality
    virtue
    excellence

kṣetra
    field
```

Canonical rendering:

> field of qualities

This refers to an exceptionally qualified recipient or object of action, especially one possessing spiritual attainment or eminent virtue.

```text
same apparent gift or injury
    + different recipient-field
        → different karmic weight
```

Karmic efficacy is relational. Agent, action, intention, recipient, and recipient’s qualities all contribute to the result.

## Killing one’s parents

```text
pitror ghātakam
```

Grammatically, `pitṛ` in the dual refers to both parents. The intended rule is that killing either parent constitutes determinate karma.

These acts are exceptionally grave because the parents are a uniquely weighty field: they are the causal condition of the present embodied life and have rendered foundational benefit.

## Structural organization

The verse gives:

```text
1. intense affliction
2. intense confidence
3. continuity
4. field of qualities
5. killing a parent
```

Structurally:

```text
A. force of intention
    intense affliction
    intense confidence

B. force of repetition
    continuity

C. force of the object-field
    field of qualities

D. intrinsic gravity of a privileged relation
    killing a parent
```

## Doctrinal determination

Karma does not become determinate merely because it has been performed. Its fixity depends upon causal amplification.

```text
karma
    + weak motivation
    + isolated performance
    + ordinary field
        → may remain indeterminate

karma
    + intense motivation
    or repeated performance
    or exceptional field
    or grave privileged act
        → determinate
```

The karmic result is not calculated from the external act alone.

## Action is relationally weighted

```text
KARMIC WEIGHT =
    intensity of motive
    × continuity
    × quality of field
    × gravity of relation
```

Not every dimension must be present simultaneously. Exceptional strength in one can be sufficient to produce determinacy.

The act is an organized relation among:

```text
agent-ground
intention
performance-pattern
recipient-field
social and existential relation
```

## Practical reason and saintly path

The verse teaches how actions become causally decisive.

```text
What one repeatedly does
becomes increasingly difficult to reverse.

What one does with total conviction
acquires greater causal determination.
```

This applies both negatively and positively:

```text
repeated hatred
    stabilizes an unwholesome trajectory

repeated generosity or contemplative confidence
    stabilizes a wholesome trajectory
```

The saintly path deliberately reorganizes these determinants:

```text
weaken affliction
strengthen lucid confidence
cultivate wholesome continuity
act toward superior fields
avoid irreversible grave actions
```

## Logic of Experience

```text
[ KARMA
  MOTIVE
      [ QUALITY wholesome | unwholesome
        INTENSITY weak | moderate | intense ]
  PERFORMANCE
      [ CONTINUITY isolated | repeated | continuous ]
  FIELD
      [ QUALITY ordinary | eminent ]
  RELATIONAL-GRAVITY
      ordinary | exceptional
  MATURATION-FIXITY
      determinate | indeterminate ]
```

This allows a reasoner to distinguish actions that are externally similar but causally different.

The threshold can be crossed through:

```text

    repetition
```

## Determinate result

VAkK 4.54 establishes that karma becomes determinate through exceptional causal force:

```text
intense affliction
intense confidence
continuous performance
an eminent field
or killing one’s mother or father
```

Its central principle is:

```text
the karmic force of an act
depends not merely on what is done

but on the intensity,
continuity,
field,
and relational gravity
through which it is done
```
