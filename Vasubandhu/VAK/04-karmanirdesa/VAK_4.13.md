# VAK_4.13 — Threefold Avijñapti and Restraint

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
अविज्ञप्तिस्त्रिधा ज्ञेया संवरासंवरेतरा ।
संवरः प्रातिमोक्षाख्यो ध्यानजोऽनास्रवस्तथा ॥
```

## 4. Grammar

### `avijñaptis tridhā jñeyā`

```text
avijñaptiḥ
    non-manifest action

tridhā
    in three ways

jñeyā
    is to be known
```

Avijñapti is to be known as threefold.

### `saṃvarāsaṃvaretarā`

This is a compact three-member expression:

```text
saṃvara
asaṃvara
itarā
```

Restraint, non-restraint, and the other.

Thus:

```text
avijñapti
├── saṃvara
├── asaṃvara
└── itarā
```

### `saṃvaraḥ prātimokṣākhyaḥ`

```text
saṃvaraḥ
    restraint

prātimokṣa-ākhyaḥ
    called prātimokṣa
```

Restraint is that called prātimokṣa, but the sentence continues with two further kinds.

### `dhyānajo ’nāsravas tathā`

```text
dhyāna-jaḥ
    born from meditation

anāsravaḥ
    uncontaminated

tathā
    likewise
```

Thus restraint is itself threefold.

## 5. Literal Translation

Non-manifest action is to be known as threefold: restraint, non-restraint, and the other. Restraint is that called prātimokṣa, that born from meditation, and likewise the uncontaminated.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

Non-manifest action falls into three classes: restraint, non-restraint, and a neutral remainder belonging to neither. Restraint itself has three forms: restraint established through prātimokṣa discipline, restraint generated through meditation, and uncontaminated restraint.

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

### `saṃvara`

```text
saṃ-√vṛ
    to cover
    contain
    restrain
```

Working translation: restraint or ethical containment.

This is not merely a momentary decision not to act. It is a continuing non-manifest determination.

### `asaṃvara`

```text
a-saṃvara
    non-restraint
```

This is not simply the absence of restraint. It is a positively established contrary disposition or action-continuum.

```text
lack of restraint
    ≠
asaṃvara
```

### `itarā`

The third class is neither restraint nor non-restraint. Its specific scope will be developed later.

### `prātimokṣa`

Retain `prātimokṣa` untranslated as a technical term. A cautious working determination is restraint established through formal discipline or vows.

### `dhyānaja`

```text
dhyāna
    meditation

-ja
    born from
```

This restraint arises from meditative attainment rather than merely from a formal vow.

### `anāsrava`

Without influx; uncontaminated. This restraint belongs to the uncontaminated Path, though its full relation to Path-Knowledge should remain deferred.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

The prior verses classified avijñapti by material support, causal mode, ethical quality, domain, and originating consciousness. VAK_4.13 now introduces a practical classification:

```text
avijñapti
├── restraint
├── non-restraint
└── neither
```

This is a major transition. Avijñapti is now organized according to stable ethical orientation.

## 9. Three Kinds of Restraint

```text
saṃvara
├── prātimokṣa restraint
├── meditation-born restraint
└── uncontaminated restraint
```

These differ by origin:

```text
formal discipline
    → prātimokṣa restraint

meditative attainment
    → meditation-born restraint

uncontaminated Path
    → uncontaminated restraint
```

## 10. Why This Belongs Under Avijñapti

A vow or meditative restraint remains effective even when no visible act of restraint is occurring.

```text
no visible abstention is occurring

but:
    a continuing ethical determination remains
```

That is precisely the function of avijñapti.

## 11. Restraint Is Not Mere Non-Action

```text
not performing an act
    may be accidental

saṃvara
    is an established determination
    against the act
```

Thus absence of killing is not identical with restraint from killing.

## 12. Non-Restraint Is Also Positive

`Asaṃvara` is not merely failure to possess a vow. It is a determinate contrary orientation.

```text
restraint
active non-restraint
neither class
```

The Bhāṣya will be essential for the exact definition.

## 13. Logical Determination

The verse distinguishes three positions:

```text
positive determination
    saṃvara

contrary positive determination
    asaṃvara

undetermined remainder
    itarā
```

The third term prevents the opposition from exhausting the field.

## 14. Relation to the Preceding Sequence

```text
VAK_4.10
```

## 15. Interpretive Note

Avijñapti can carry a stable practical orientation across moments in which no corresponding manifest act occurs.

The verse does not yet require a speculative account of how such continuity is ultimately possible.

## 16. Light Organon Interpretation

```text
ethical determination
    becomes stable

stability
    persists beyond individual acts

persistence
    is classified as:
        restraint
        contrary restraint
        neither
```

The stronger relation to habit, disposition, or Ground should remain deferred to the Bhāṣya pass.
