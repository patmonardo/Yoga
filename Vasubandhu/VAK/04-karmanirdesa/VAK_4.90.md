# VAK_4.90

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

सर्वेऽतीतस्य चत्वारि मध्यमस्याप्यनागताः ।  
मध्यमा द्वे अजातस्य फलानि त्रीण्यनागताः ॥ ४.९० ॥

Local source:

```text
sarve 'tītasya catvāri madhyamasyāpyanāgatāḥ /
madhyamā dve ajātasya phalāni trīṇyanāgatāḥ // VAkK_4.90 //
```

Normalized cautiously:

```text
sarve ’tītasya catvāri madhyamasyāpy anāgatāḥ |
madhyamā dve ’jātasya phalāni trīṇy anāgatāḥ ||
```

The verse introduces another classification of result-relations—this time according to the temporal determination of karma and result.

Because the compressed syntax is unusually difficult, the exact allocation of every numeral should remain provisional until the Bhāṣya.

The verse therefore adds time as a further axis to the result system developed in VAK_4.87–4.89.

This suggests:

> Past karma stands in all the result-relations; present karma in four; for not-yet-arisen karma, two are “middle” and three are future results.

However, `madhyamā dve` may instead mean the middle two result-types within the standard fivefold list. That question must be settled by the Bhāṣya.

## 4. Grammar

### sarve

Masculine nominative plural:

```text
all
all of them
```

The understood referent is most naturally the five technical results.

### atītasya

Genitive singular:

```text
of the past
of past karma
```

The understood noun is `karmaṇaḥ`.

### catvāri

Neuter plural:

```text
four
```

The understood noun is `phalāni`.

### madhyamasya

Genitive singular:

```text
of the middle one
of the present
```

Within the temporal triad:

```text
past — present — future
```

`madhyama`, “middle,” can designate the present.

### api

```text
also
even
```

It connects the present classification with that of past karma.

### anāgatāḥ / anāgatāni

From `an-āgata`:

```text
not yet come
future
```

The transmitted continuous text compresses grammatical agreement. The term clearly introduces future temporal status.

### madhyamāḥ dve

Literally:

```text
the middle two
```

This expression is ambiguous at the kārikā level.

It may mean:

1. two results belonging to the present temporal position; or
2. the two middle members of the five-result enumeration.

The Bhāṣya is required to decide between these readings.

### ajātasya

Genitive singular:

```text
of what has not arisen
of future or not-yet-produced karma
```

`Ajāta` emphasizes non-arising rather than merely chronological futurity.

### phalāni trīṇi

```text
three results
```

### anāgatāḥ

```text
future
not yet arisen
```

It qualifies a set of results whose actuality lies ahead.

## 5. Literal Translation

A deliberately provisional rendering:

> All the results belong to past karma, and four to present karma. For karma that has not yet arisen, there are two middle results and three future results.

A more cautious formulation:

> Past karma is related to all five results, while present karma is related to four. With respect to not-yet-arisen karma, the verse distinguishes two “middle” results and three future results.

The meaning of “middle two” remains to be resolved.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> A karmic determination does not possess the same range of result-relations in every temporal condition. Once activity has become past, it may stand in the full set of recognized relations to result. Activity presently operative has a more restricted range. Activity not yet arisen is related only to results compatible with its unfinished temporal status. Causal relation is therefore mediated not only by quality and result-type, but also by the temporal position of both terms.

The developing system now has several axes:

```text
karma quality
contamination status
path status
result-type
result quality
temporal status
```

The result relation is progressively determined through their conjunction.

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

### atīta

```text
atīta
    past
    that whose present activity has passed
```

The term does not necessarily mean nonexistent. Within the Sarvāstivāda framework, pastness is a temporal determination of a dharma.

### madhyama

```text
madhyama
    middle
```

In the temporal sequence, it can indicate the present:

```text
past
present
future
```

But in `madhyamā dve`, it may instead indicate the middle two members of an ordered fivefold series.

### anāgata

```text
anāgata
    future
    not yet come
```

### ajāta

```text
ajāta
    not arisen
    not yet produced
```

`Ajāta` and `anāgata` are close but not simply redundant:

```text
ajāta
    emphasizes non-arising

anāgata
    emphasizes future temporal position
```

### Temporal result-relation

The verse concerns not merely whether karma has a result, but whether a specific result-relation is compatible with:

```text
past karma
present karma
future karma

and

past result
present result
future result
```

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

### Past karma

The verse begins:

```text
sarve ’tītasya
```

The natural first-pass reading is:

```text
past karma:
    all five results
```

Past karma has already completed its own present activity and can therefore stand in the broadest range of result-relations.

The exact doctrinal explanation belongs to the Bhāṣya.

### Present karma

```text
catvāri madhyamasya
```

Provisional determination:

```text
present karma:
    four results
```

One of the five result-relations is unavailable to karma while it is presently occurring.

Which result is excluded should not be guessed.

### Not-yet-arisen karma

The second half introduces:

```text
ajātasya
    of the not-yet-arisen
```

and distinguishes:

```text
two middle results

three future results
```

The kārikā clearly imposes temporal constraints, but its compressed syntax does not safely establish the complete matrix without commentary.

### Time changes relational availability

The doctrinal point that can be securely retained is:

```text
same fivefold result taxonomy

but

different admissible relations
according to temporal status
```

Thus a dharma’s causal role cannot be inferred merely from its universal type.

Its temporal mode also determines what relations it may presently bear.

## 9. Logical Determination

Let:

```text
K
    karma

R
    result-type

Tₖ
    temporal status of karma

Tᵣ
    temporal status of result
```

The result relation must be represented as:

```text
ResultRelation(K, R, Tₖ, Tᵣ)
```

not simply:

```text
Produces(K, R)
```

The provisional cardinalities are:

```text
TemporalStatus(K) = Past
    →
ResultCardinality(K) = 5
```

```text
TemporalStatus(K) = Present
    →
ResultCardinality(K) = 4
```

For future or not-yet-arisen karma, the verse supplies a further division involving two and three, but the precise predicate structure remains pending.

### Temporal status is relationally active

The verse denies:

```text
SameKarmaType(x, y)
    →
SameAvailableResultRelations(x, y)
```

when:

```text
TemporalStatus(x) ≠ TemporalStatus(y)
```

Temporal determination changes the admissible relation-set.

### A deeper matrix

The previous matrix was:

```text
SourceQuality × ResultType × ResultQuality
```

VAK_4.90 adds temporal indexing:

```text
SourceTime
× SourceQuality
× ResultType
× ResultQuality
× ResultTime
```

The kārikā supplies only one projection of this larger structure.

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

At the Kernel level, the result relation now requires temporal qualification.

A minimal record is:

```text
TemporalResultRelation {
    sourceKarma
    sourceTemporalStatus
    resultType
    resultTemporalStatus
    admissible
}
```

The Kernel must not treat a result relation as timelessly available merely because its source and target types are compatible.

It must ask:

```text
Has the source already occurred?

Is it presently active?

Has it not yet arisen?

Is the proposed result past,
present, or future?
```

This is a direct extension of the earlier endowment–manifestation distinction:

```text
type relation
    is not enough

temporal mode
    determines present applicability
```

No stronger architectural interpretation is needed before the Bhāṣya resolves the two-versus-three construction.
