# VAK_4.20

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

तथैवासंवरस्थोऽपि ध्यानसंवरवान् सदा ।
अतीताजातया आर्यस्तु प्रथमे नाभ्यतीतया ॥

## Sanskrit — IAST

tathaivāsaṃvarastho ’pi dhyānasaṃvaravān sadā |
atītājātayā āryas tu prathame nābhyatītayā ||

## Source reference and caution

Source: `VAkK_4.20`.

The verse continues the temporal analysis of possession begun in VAK_4.19. The first pāda indicates that one established in non-restraint follows the same basic temporal pattern as one established in prātimokṣa restraint. One possessing meditation-born restraint is related to past and future restraint as well as the present restraint that follows the corresponding mind.

The second pāda concerns the noble being at the first moment of the Path. A cautious working interpretation is that present and future uncontaminated restraint are possessed, but no past uncontaminated restraint exists yet. The compressed phrase `prathame nābhyatītayā` requires Bhāṣya verification.

## Literal translation

> One established in non-restraint is likewise. One possessing meditation-born restraint is always endowed with past and future restraint; but the noble being, at the first moment, is not yet endowed with past restraint.

## Doctrinal determination

1. Non-restraint has a temporal pattern analogous to prātimokṣa restraint: present non-restraint while it is possessed, and past non-restraint after the first moment.
2. Meditation-born restraint is possessed in past, present, and future modes.
3. At the first noble-path moment, uncontaminated restraint has present and future instances, but no past instance yet.

## Working temporal table

|---|---:|---:|---:|

| non-restraint bearer | analogous to prātimokṣa | analogous | not stated |
| meditation-born restraint bearer | yes | yes | yes |
| noble being, first Path moment | no | yes | yes |

## Light Organon interpretation

The temporal extension of a determination depends upon the ground from which it arises.

- undertaken restraint or non-restraint: present succession plus accumulated past
- meditation-born restraint: past, present, and future
- first noble-path moment: present and future, but no past yet

Different determinations therefore possess different temporal ranges.

## Verse summary

VAK_4.20 extends the temporal analysis from prātimokṣa restraint to non-restraint, meditation-born restraint, and uncontaminated restraint. Non-restraint follows the prātimokṣa pattern; meditation-born restraint spans the three times; and the first noble-path moment has no past uncontaminated restraint yet.
