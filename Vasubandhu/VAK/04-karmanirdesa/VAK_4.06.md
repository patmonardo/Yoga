# VAK_4.06 — Causal Modes of Non-Manifest Action

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
नैःष्यन्दिकी च सत्त्वाख्या निष्यन्दोपात्तभूतजा ।
समाधिजौपचयिकानुपात्ताभिन्नभूतजा ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
naiḥṣyandikī ca sattvākhyā niṣyandopātta-bhūtajā |
```

A clearer syntactic separation is:

## 3. Source Caution

External source siglum: VAkK_4.6.

The second line requires sandhi restoration as `samādhijā aupacayikā`. The exact scope of `abhinna` remains provisional pending the Bhāṣya pass.

## 5. Grammar

### `naiḥṣyandikī ca sattvākhyā`

Both adjectives qualify the understood subject `avijñaptiḥ`:

> Non-manifest action is outflow-derived and designated as belonging to a sentient being.

### `niṣyandopātta-bhūtajā`

The compound qualifies avijñapti:

> It is born from outflow-derived, appropriated great material determinations.

The avijñapti itself is non-appropriated, while its material support may be appropriated.

### `samādhijā aupacayikā`

> The avijñapti born from concentration is development-produced.

### `anupāttā abhinna-bhūtajā`

> It is non-appropriated and born from non-distinct great material determinations.

The exact doctrinal sense of `abhinna` must be checked against the commentary.

## 6. Literal Translation

> It is outflow-derived and designated as belonging to a sentient being, born from outflow-derived appropriated great material determinations. That which is born from concentration is development-produced, non-appropriated, and born from non-distinct great material determinations.

## 7. Philosophical Translation

> Ordinary non-manifest action belongs to a sentient continuum and arises as a homogeneous continuation grounded in appropriated material determinations. Non-manifest action produced through concentration has a different causal status: it is developed through cultivation, remains non-appropriated, and arises from a non-distinct material basis.

## 9. Doctrinal Determination

VAK_4.06 differentiates at least two causal modes of avijñapti:

```text
ordinary avijñapti
    → outflow-derived
    → sentient-continuum related
    → based on appropriated great material determinations

concentration-born avijñapti
    → development-produced
    → non-appropriated
    → based on non-distinct great material determinations
```

Avijñapti is therefore not one undifferentiated remainder of action.

## 10. Logical Determination

The verse distinguishes:

```text
niṣyanda
    homogeneous causal continuation

upacaya
    development through accumulation or cultivation
```

These are distinct modes of karmic continuation.

## 11. Relation to VAK_4.05

VAK_4.05 established that avijñapti is non-appropriated. VAK_4.06 qualifies this:

```text
non-appropriated
    does not mean
    unrelated to appropriated material support
```

## 12. Interpretive Note

The verse develops a theory of grounded continuation. Avijñapti is neither a persisting mental intention nor the visible physical act. It is a subsequent determination whose causal mode may be homogeneous outflow or cultivated development.

## 13. Light Organon Interpretation

```text
manifest event
    perishes

causal series
    continues through niṣyanda

cultivated determination
    develops through upacaya
```

The deeper identification with Absolute Ground remains deferred.
