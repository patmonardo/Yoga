# VAK_4.104

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

बुद्धे न ताडनेच्छस्य प्रहारान्नोर्ध्वमर्हति ।
नानन्तर्यप्रयुक्तस्य वैराग्यफलसम्भवः ॥ ४.१०४ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**buddhe na tāḍanecchasya prahārān nordhvam arhati |**  
**nānantaryaprayuktasya vairāgyaphalasaṃbhavaḥ || 4.104 ||**

## 3. Padaccheda

`buddhe | na | tāḍana-icchasya | prahārāt | na | ūrdhvam | arhati | na | ānantarya-prayuktasya | vairāgya-phala-saṃbhavaḥ`

## 4. Grammar

- **buddhe** — with respect to a Buddha.
- **tāḍanecchasya** — of one wishing to strike or injure.
- **prahārān nordhvam** — not beyond the blow.
- **ānantaryaprayuktasya** — of one engaged in an immediate-consequence act.
- **vairāgya** — liberating dispassion.
- **phala** — fruit of the noble Path.
- **saṃbhavaḥ** — possibility or arising.

## 5. Literal Translation

> With respect to a Buddha, the act of one wishing to strike him cannot proceed beyond a blow. For one engaged in an immediate-consequence act, there is no possibility of dispassion or a fruit.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> A Buddha cannot be killed by another; hostile aggression can culminate only in wounding him. The relevant immediate-consequence karma is therefore maliciously causing the Buddha’s blood to flow. While a person remains engaged in such immediate-consequence activity, neither liberating dispassion nor a fruit of the Path can arise.

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

- **tāḍana** — striking or bodily injury.
- **prahāra** — blow or wound.
- **ānantarya-prayoga** — operative engagement in an immediate-consequence act.
- **vairāgya** — liberation through dispassion.
- **phala** — noble fruit.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

The Buddha is the supreme quality-field. His qualities determine both the magnitude of the offense and the limit of hostile agency: an aggressor may wound him but cannot complete his killing.

Immediate-consequence karma is also a present obstruction. It not only necessitates painful maturation after death; while operative, it blocks the arising of liberating dispassion and noble fruit.

## 9. Logical Determination

The complete karmic event distinguishes:

- intended result;
- result possible relative to the field;
- completed result;
- karmic classification.

Thus karma is neither intention alone nor physical result alone. It is activity determined through intention, qualified field, possible completion, and actual result.

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

The agent projects an end, but the object-field conditions whether and how that end can become actual. In attacking the supreme field of liberation, the agent reflexively obstructs liberation within his own continuum.
