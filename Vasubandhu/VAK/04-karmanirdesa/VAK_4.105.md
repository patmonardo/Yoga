# VAK_4.105

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

सङ्घभेदे मृषावादो महावद्यतमो मतः ।
भवाग्रचेतना लोके महाफलतमा शुभे ॥ ४.१०५ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**saṃghabhede mṛṣāvādo mahāvadyatamo mataḥ |**  
**bhavāgracetanā loke mahāphalatamā śubhe || 4.105 ||**

## 3. Padaccheda

`saṃgha-bhede | mṛṣā-vādaḥ | mahā-avadya-tamaḥ | mataḥ | bhava-agra-cetanā | loke | mahā-phala-tamā | śubhe`

Expanded:

`saṃghabhede mṛṣāvādaḥ mahāvadyatamaḥ mataḥ; śubhe loke bhavāgracetanā mahāphalatamā matā.`

## 4. Grammar

- **saṃghabhede** — in the division of the Saṃgha.
- **mṛṣāvādaḥ** — false speech.
- **mahāvadyatamaḥ** — most greatly blameworthy.
- **bhavāgra-cetanā** — volition associated with the summit of existence.
- **loke** — within the world, conditioned existence.
- **mahāphalatamā** — producing the greatest result.
- **śubhe** — among wholesome karmas.

## 5. Literal Translation

> False speech employed in dividing the Saṃgha is regarded as the most greatly blameworthy. Among wholesome worldly karma, volition at the summit of existence bears the greatest result.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> Saṃgha-dividing false speech is supreme in culpability because it destroys the communal field of Dharma. Conversely, among wholesome karmic activities that remain within conditioned existence, the volition belonging to the summit of becoming produces the greatest worldly result.

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

- **mahāvadyatama** — maximally culpable.
- **bhavāgra** — summit of existence or becoming; the highest conditioned domain.
- **bhavāgra-cetanā** — volition associated with the highest worldly meditative attainment.
- **mahāphalatama** — maximally fruitful.
- **laukika-śubha** — mundane wholesome karma.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

The verse establishes two extrema within worldly karma:

- maximum fault: false speech that divides the Saṃgha;
- maximum wholesome worldly result: volition associated with bhavāgra.

Bhavāgra is the apex of conditioned becoming, not liberation beyond becoming. Thus greatest worldly fruit and noble Path-fruit must remain distinct.

## 9. Logical Determination

Karmic evaluation requires separate axes:

- ethical valence;
- quality of the field;
- relational gravity;
- magnitude of result;
- compatibility with the Path.

A karma may produce the greatest conditioned result without transcending conditioned existence.

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

Bhavāgra is the summit internal to the sphere of becoming: the highest possible abode still governed by bhava. It is therefore structurally the ultimate training-ground or “dojo” within the Abode of God, but not yet the transcendence of that abode.

The verse opposes:

- maximal fragmentation: divisive false speech;
- maximal elevation within becoming: bhavāgra volition.

The maximum within a sphere does not cross the boundary of that sphere.
