# VAK_4.91

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

स्वभूमिधर्माश्चत्वारि त्रीणि द्वे वान्यभूमिकाः ।
शैक्षस्य त्रीणि शैक्षाद्याः अशैक्षस्य तु कर्मणः ॥ ४.९१ ॥

Local source:

```text
svabhūmidharmāścatvāri trīṇi dve vānyabhūmikāḥ /
śaikṣasya trīṇi śaikṣādyāḥ aśaikṣasya tu karmaṇaḥ // VAkK_4.91 //
```

Normalized:

```text
sva-bhūmi-dharmāś catvāri trīṇi dve vānya-bhūmikāḥ |
śaikṣasya trīṇi śaikṣādyā aśaikṣasya tu karmaṇaḥ ||
```

This verse continues the classification of karma and results through two new relational axes:

```text
1. same domain-level versus another domain-level

2. trainee, beyond-training, and neither
```

The second classification continues grammatically into VAK_4.92 and should not be completed from this verse alone.

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**sva-bhūmi-dharmāś catvāri trīṇi dve vānya-bhūmikāḥ |**  
**śaikṣasya trīṇi śaikṣādyā aśaikṣasya tu karmaṇaḥ || 4.91 ||**

The first half states a result-count according to whether the result-dharmas belong to:

```text

```

The second half begins a table whose ordered classes are:

```text
śaikṣa
    trainee

aśaikṣa
    beyond training

naiva-śaikṣa-nāśaikṣa
    neither trainee nor beyond-training
```

VAK_4.92 supplies the continuation required to complete this table.

## 4. Grammar

### sva-bhūmi

Compound:

```text
sva
    own
    same

bhūmi
    level
    stage
    domain-level
```

Thus:

```text
sva-bhūmi
    belonging to the same level
    as the karma under consideration
```

`Bhūmi` should not be flattened into physical location. It marks a classified level or field to which a dharma belongs.

### dharmāḥ

Masculine nominative plural:

```text
determinations
dharmas
```

Here these are dharmas functioning as results.

### catvāri

Neuter plural:

```text
four
```

The understood noun is `phalāni`, results.

There is compressed agreement between the plural `dharmāḥ` and the neuter numeral referring to result-types.

### trīṇi dve vā

```text
trīṇi
    three

dve
    two

vā
    or
```

Thus other-level dharmas may enter three or two of the technical result-relations, depending upon a distinction not yet explicit in the kārikā alone.

### anya-bhūmikāḥ

Masculine nominative plural:

```text
anya
    other

bhūmika
    belonging to a level
```

Thus:

```text
dharmas belonging to another domain-level
```

### śaikṣasya

Genitive singular:

```text
of a trainee
of that which belongs to training
```

The understood phrase is:

```text
śaikṣasya karmaṇaḥ
    of the karma of a trainee
```

### śaikṣa

From `√śikṣ`, to train or learn:

```text
śaikṣa
    one still undergoing training
    trainee
```

This is a technical path-status, not merely an ordinary student.

### śaikṣādyāḥ

Compound:

```text
śaikṣa
    trainee

ādi
    beginning with
```

Thus:

```text
those beginning with the trainee class
```

It introduces an ordered series, likely:

```text
trainee
beyond-training
neither
```

### aśaikṣasya

Genitive singular:

```text
of one beyond training
```

`Aśaikṣa` does not mean untrained. It means that the required training has been completed.

### tu

Contrastive particle:

```text
but
whereas
```

It moves from trainee karma to beyond-training karma.

### karmaṇaḥ

Genitive singular:

```text
of karma
of activity
```

It governs both the trainee and beyond-training classifications.

## 5. Literal Translation

> Dharmas belonging to the same level constitute four results; those belonging to another level constitute three or two. For the karma of a trainee, the result-dharmas beginning with those of a trainee are three; but for the karma of one beyond training—

The final dash is deliberate: the grammatical and numerical construction continues into VAK_4.92.

A slightly smoother rendering:

> Four result-relations involve dharmas of the karma’s own level, while three or two involve dharmas belonging to another level. For trainee karma, three results belong to the ordered classes beginning with the trainee class; as for the karma of one beyond training, the account continues in the following verse.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The causal relation between karma and result is further restricted by the classified level to which each term belongs. A result belonging to the same level as its causal activity can enter four of the recognized result-relations; a result belonging to another level enters only three or two. The same result system must also be indexed by status within the path: trainee, beyond-training, or neither. Karma therefore does not pass immediately into an abstract result; its efficacy is mediated through the respective level and path-status of source and result.

The verse adds two more determinations to the result relation:

```text
level relation:
    same / other

path-status relation:
    trainee / beyond-training / neither
```

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

### bhūmi

```text
bhūmi
    ground
    level
    stage
    classified domain-level
```

Here it is relational:

```text
same bhūmi as the source karma

or

a different bhūmi from the source karma
```

It therefore cannot be assigned to a dharma without specifying the relevant comparison.

### sva-bhūmika

```text
sva-bhūmika
    belonging to its own or the same level
```

This means:

```text
Level(result) = Level(karma)
```

### anya-bhūmika

```text
anya-bhūmika
    belonging to another level
```

Thus:

```text
Level(result) ≠ Level(karma)
```

### śaikṣa

```text
śaikṣa
    trainee
```

A person or dharma belonging to the path in which further training remains.

### aśaikṣa

```text
aśaikṣa
    beyond training
    one for whom the training is complete
```

This is a positive completed status, not a privative lack of discipline.

### naiva-śaikṣa-nāśaikṣa

Not stated fully in VAK_4.91, but implied by `śaikṣādyāḥ` and supplied by the continuing classification:

```text
neither trainee
nor beyond-training
```

This third class covers what does not belong to either of the two noble path-statuses.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

### Same-level result-dharmas

The verse assigns:

```text
same-level dharmas:
    four result-types
```

This means that four of the five technical result-relations may connect karma with a dharma belonging to the same `bhūmi`.

The excluded relation should be identified from the Bhāṣya rather than guessed.

### Other-level result-dharmas

For dharmas belonging to another level:

```text
three or two result-types
```

The alternative indicates that “other level” is not itself a sufficient final condition.

A further distinction determines whether the count is three or two.

The kārikā compresses that distinction; the Bhāṣya must unfold it.

### Path-status as another axis

The second half begins a classification by:

```text
source karma status

and

result-dharma status
```

This resembles the quality matrix of 4.88–4.89, but the new classes are not ethical qualities. They are path-statuses:

```text
trainee
beyond-training
neither
```

### The verse is structurally incomplete by design

The line:

```text
aśaikṣasya tu karmaṇaḥ
```

ends with:

> but of the karma of one beyond training …

Its predicate and numerical distribution occur in VAK_4.92.

Therefore no complete table should be fabricated from VAK_4.91 alone.

## 9. Logical Determination

Let:

```text
K
    source karma

R
    result dharma

B(K)
    bhūmi of the karma

B(R)
    bhūmi of the result
```

Then the first relation is indexed by equality:

```text
B(K) = B(R)
    →
QualifiedResultCount(K, R) = 4
```

For inequality:

```text
B(K) ≠ B(R)
    →
QualifiedResultCount(K, R) ∈ {3, 2}
```

A further condition must select between `3` and `2`.

### Bhūmi is role-relative

The same dharma may be:

```text
sva-bhūmika
```

relative to one source and:

```text
anya-bhūmika
```

relative to another.

Therefore these are not simple intrinsic unary properties.

The correct predicates are:

```text
SameBhūmi(K, R)

DifferentBhūmi(K, R)
```

### Path-status matrix

Let:

```text
Sₖ
    path-status of source karma

Sᵣ
    path-status of result dharma
```

The continuing table has the form:

```text
QualifiedResultCount(Sₖ, Sᵣ)
```

where:

```text
S ∈ {
    Trainee,
    BeyondTraining,
    Neither
}
```

VAK_4.91 provides only the opening cells; VAK_4.92 completes the matrix.

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

At the Kernel level, this verse requires **relational comparison**, not merely independent metadata fields.

A weak representation would store:

```text
karma.level
result.level
```

A stronger rule derives:

```text
LevelRelation {
    source
    result
    relation: Same | Different
}
```

The result-count is then conditioned by that derived relation.

Likewise, training status forms a second matrix:

```text
PathStatusRelation {
    sourceStatus
    resultStatus
    permittedResultTypes[]
}
```

The key structural lesson is:

```text
“same” and “other”
are not properties of isolated dharmas

they are determinations
of a relation between dharmas
```

That is the verse’s mediation-focused simplicity.
