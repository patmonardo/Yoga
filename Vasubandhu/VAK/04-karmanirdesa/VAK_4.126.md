# VAK_4.126

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
योगप्रवर्तितं कर्म ससमुत्थापकं त्रिधा ।
लिपिमुद्रे सगणनं काव्यं संख्या यथाक्रमम् ॥ ४.१२६ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
yogapravartitaṃ karma sasamutthāpakaṃ tridhā |
lipimudre sagaṇanaṃ kāvyaṃ saṃkhyā yathākramam || 4.126 ||
```

The local Sanskrit source reads:

```text
yogapravartitaṃ karma sasamutthāpakaṃ tridhā /
lipimudre sagaṇanaṃ kāvyaṃ saṃkhyā yathākramam //
```

This is a compressed technical verse. Its general architecture is clear, although the precise assignment of the examples should remain open for confirmation by the Bhāṣya.

The verse appears to classify activities produced through trained or technical application according to the three channels:

```text
body
speech
mind
```

It then gives examples—writing, sealing, calculation, poetry, and enumeration—assigned `yathākramam`, “in the corresponding order.”

## 3. Padaccheda

```text
yoga-pravartitam |
karma |
sa-samutthāpakam |
tridhā |

lipi-mudre |
sa-gaṇanam |
kāvyam |
saṃkhyā |
yathā-kramam
```

A cautious prose arrangement is:

```text
yogena pravartitaṃ karma,
samutthāpakena saha,
tridhā [bhavati].

lipi-mudre,
gaṇanena saha kāvyam,
saṃkhyā ca
yathākramam.
```

Provisionally:

> Activity set in motion through applied skill, together with that which gives rise to it, is threefold. Writing and sealing, poetry together with calculation, and enumeration correspond respectively [to those three forms].

The precise internal construction of `lipimudre sagaṇanaṃ kāvyaṃ saṃkhyā` is unusually compressed. The Bhāṣya must confirm exactly which examples are grouped together and assigned to bodily, verbal, and mental activity.

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `yoga` | compound member | application, disciplined employment, technical practice |
| `pravartitam` | past passive participle | set in motion, caused to operate |
| `karma` | nominative neuter singular | activity |
| `sa-samutthāpakam` | nominative neuter singular | together with that which produces or initiates it |
| `tridhā` | indeclinable | threefold |
| `lipi` | compound member | writing, written characters |
| `mudre` | nominative dual or compound form | seals, stamping, marking |
| `gaṇanam` | neuter singular | calculation, reckoning |
| `kāvyam` | neuter singular | poetry, poetic composition |
| `saṃkhyā` | feminine singular | enumeration, counting, numerical determination |
| `yathākramam` | indeclinable | respectively, according to order |

The governing statement is:

```text
yogapravartitaṃ karma
    sasamutthāpakaṃ
    tridhā
```

> Technically applied activity, together with its initiating factor, is threefold.

## 5. Literal translation

> Activity set in motion by application, together with its producer, is threefold: writing and sealing, poetry accompanied by calculation, and enumeration, respectively.

Because the second half is compressed, a less committal translation is preferable:

> Activity initiated through disciplined application, together with the factor that sets it in motion, is threefold. Writing, sealing, calculation, poetry, and enumeration are assigned to these in their respective order.

## 6. Philosophical translation

> Skilled activity does not consist merely in an outward product. It includes both the bodily, verbal, or mental operation and the cognition that initiates and organizes it. Writing, inscription or sealing, calculation, poetic composition, and enumeration exemplify activities in which a learned procedure is applied through one or more of the three channels of action. A technical art is therefore a compound event: a formative cognition sets a structured operation in motion, and that operation becomes objectively manifest through body, speech, or mind.

The verse is astonishingly close to our present question:

```text
How does a represented procedure
become an actual technical operation?
```

Its answer begins:

```text
Program
    is not enough

there must also be
an initiating and organizing cognition
```

# 7. `Yoga`

```text
yoga
    joining
    application
    employment
    disciplined practical exercise
```

Here `yoga` does not primarily mean contemplative union. It indicates an applied capacity through which an acquired method is brought into operation.

```text
knowledge of a procedure
    +
capacity to apply it
    =
yoga
```

The term points toward skill in execution. One may know what writing, computation, or poetry is without presently performing it. `Yoga` is the practical conjunction through which the relevant capacities are assembled and activated.

# 8. `Pravartita`

```text
pra-√vṛt
    to set in motion
    cause to operate
    bring into activity
```

The technical activity does not simply exist statically. It must be initiated.

```text
available capacity
    →
application
    →
actual operation
```

`Pravartita` marks the passage from potential skill to running process.

```text
Program available
    ≠
Program executing
```

The verse concerns the activity once its execution has been set in motion.

# 9. `Yogapravartitaṃ karma`

The compound may be rendered:

```text
activity produced through application
activity set running by trained exercise
technically initiated operation
```

This is different from a spontaneous reflex or accidental movement. The operation is mediated through an acquired practical form.

Examples such as writing and calculation involve learned signs, rules of transformation, selection of instruments, ordered execution, and error discrimination. The activity is therefore already shaped by a technique.

# 10. `Sasamutthāpaka`

```text
sa-
    together with

samutthāpaka
    that which causes to arise
    initiating factor
    motivating cognition
```

The technical operation must be considered together with the factor that gives rise to it. The outward operation is not self-explanatory.

```text
written mark
    presupposes
a cognition that intends and forms the mark
```

Likewise, a spoken poem presupposes selection, ordering, meter, and intended meaning. The `samutthāpaka` is the initiating mental activity that organizes the embodied or verbal execution.

# 11. Operation and initiator

The verse therefore refuses to identify karma with the observable output alone.

```text
output
    bodily or verbal operation

initiator
    cognition setting it in motion
```

These belong together:

```text
samutthāpaka
    →
pravartita karma
```

Yet they should not be collapsed. The cognition and the executed operation are distinct moments of one practical event. This precisely matches our distinction between Agent determination and ToolChain execution.

# 12. `Tridhā`

```text
tridhā
    threefold
```

The natural reference is to the established threefold classification:

```text
kāya-karma
    bodily activity

vāk-karma
    verbal activity

mano-karma
    mental activity
```

Technical arts can therefore be examined according to the principal channel through which they become actual. The same art may involve all three, but the classification identifies its predominant manifest operation.

# 13. Writing

```text
lipi
    written signs
    inscription
    writing
```

Writing involves a mental determination becoming an enduring visible configuration.

```text
meaning intended
    →
sign selected
    →
bodily movement
    →
visible inscription
```

It is therefore an exemplary ToolChain operation. The thought is not merely expressed and immediately gone. It becomes fixed as an objective sign that can be encountered by another cognition.

# 14. Writing as embodied representation

Writing requires symbol system, motor execution, spatial ordering, material support, and semantic intention. The bodily movement is controlled by a representation of which sign, where, in what sequence, and with what intended meaning.

```text
mental rule
    →
bodily inscription
```

The body executes a symbolic determination.

# 15. `Mudrā`

```text
mudrā
    seal
    stamp
    impression
    identifying mark
```

Sealing or stamping also produces an objective mark through bodily operation. But unlike free writing, the instrument already contains a fixed form.

```text
seal matrix
    →
pressure or application
    →
repeated impression
```

This makes `mudrā` an especially clear example of technical mediation. The agent does not redraw the entire sign each time. A stabilized instrument reproduces a form.

# 16. The seal as compiled form

Writing can form signs dynamically. A seal carries a sign in preconfigured form.

```text
writing
    executes sign construction

sealing
    executes sign reproduction
```

The seal is almost a compiled representation:

```text
complex form
    stabilized in instrument

single operation
    reproduces the form
```

But the artifact still requires selection, application, appropriate field, and interpretation. It cannot initiate or understand itself.

# 17. `Gaṇana`

```text
gaṇana
    calculation
    reckoning
    counting procedure
```

Calculation is an ordered manipulation of determinate units. It requires rules such as identifying operands, selecting an operation, preserving place or order, deriving a result, and verifying consistency.

Depending upon the traditional classification, calculation may be manifested verbally, manually, or mentally. The kārikā’s exact assignment must be confirmed through the Bhāṣya. The important secure point is that calculation is a paradigmatic rule-governed practical cognition.

# 18. Calculation as Pipeline

Calculation is not merely possession of numbers. It is a sequence:

```text
input
    →
representation
    →
operation
    →
intermediate states
    →
result
```

This is a genuine Pipeline. Each step is constrained by a rule, and the result depends upon maintaining the relevant determinations through the sequence.

```text
correct operation
    requires
state continuity
```

The calculating agent must retain what has been done and what remains to be done.

# 19. `Kāvya`

```text
kāvya
    poetry
    poetic composition
```

Poetry involves verbal activity at a high level of organization. It integrates meaning, sound, meter, syntax, imagery, audience, and purpose.

The poem is not merely speech with ornament added. It is language organized according to a determinate Art.

```text
semantic objective
    +
formal constraints
    +
verbal execution
```

This makes poetry a powerful example of activity that cannot be captured by its surface output alone.

# 20. Poetry and higher Art

A metrical representation of poetry can specify syllable counts, permitted patterns, rhyme or cadence, lexical constraints, and figures of speech. But such specifications do not exhaust poetic Art.

The poet must determine what is worth saying, which form embodies that meaning, where rules should govern strictly, and how multiple constraints form one expression.

Thus the verse’s inclusion of `kāvya` is almost a warning against reducing skill to procedure. A procedure is necessary, but poetic unity arises through an Agent capable of evaluating the whole.

# 21. `Saṃkhyā`

```text
saṃkhyā
    enumeration
    numerical determination
    ordered counting
```

`Saṃkhyā` can indicate the mental apprehension and ordering of units. If `gaṇana` emphasizes the executed procedure of calculation, `saṃkhyā` may emphasize the determination of number itself.

A useful provisional distinction is:

```text
gaṇana
    the act or procedure of calculating

saṃkhyā
    the numerical determination established
through counting or enumeration
```

The Bhāṣya must tell us whether this is the intended distinction here.

# 22. Calculation and enumeration

The presence of both terms is significant. It suggests a difference between performing numerical operations and forming or retaining numerical determination.

In our architecture:

```text
gaṇana
    Pipeline execution

saṃkhyā
    resulting structured representation
```

Or perhaps:

```text
gaṇana
    externally expressed reckoning

saṃkhyā
    mental counting
```

Both readings are plausible at the kārikā level; neither should yet be hardened into doctrine.

# 23. `Yathākramam`

```text
yathā
    according to

krama
    sequence
    order

yathākramam
    correspondingly
    respectively
```

This tells us that the examples are assigned in sequence to the three kinds of karma. But the compressed compound structure leaves the exact grouping uncertain.

A plausible provisional distribution is:

```text
bodily:
    writing and sealing

verbal:
    calculation and poetry

mental:
    numerical enumeration
```

This would explain the progression from visible inscription, through articulated verbal operation, to inward numerical determination. But we should mark this mapping as provisional until Bhāṣya confirmation.

# 24. Technical activity is mixed

Even if one channel is predominant, the operations are rarely pure. Writing involves mental meaning, bodily execution, and often subvocal or verbal organization. Poetry involves mental composition, verbal expression, and sometimes bodily inscription. Calculation involves mental rules, verbal symbols, and bodily marks or counters.

Therefore `tridhā` should not be read as though each craft occurs through only one isolated channel. The classification identifies the activity’s principal operative manifestation.

# 25. The complete technical act

```text
Initiating cognition
    samutthāpaka

Applied capacity
    yoga

Execution
    pravartita karma

Channel
    body / speech / mind

Artifact or result
    writing / seal / calculation / poem / number
```

This is nearly a complete ToolChain schema. The technical artifact is the final appearance of a mediated process.

# 26. Shell, Program, and execution

Writing or calculation may be represented by a formal Shell:

```text
write(text, target)
stamp(seal, medium)
calculate(operation, operands)
compose(form, meaning)
```

But the Shell only defines an executable interface. The actual activity still requires an Agent to determine which operation is appropriate, what its arguments mean, whether the result is correct, and how failure should alter the procedure.

The Shell makes the Art finitely addressable. It does not generate the higher Art by which the operation is understood and judged.

# 27. `Yoga` as ToolChain assembly

We can now give `yoga` a precise architectural meaning:

```text
the active joining of capacities,
rules, instruments, and objective support
into one executable operation
```

For writing:

```text
meaning
+ alphabet
+ hand
+ stylus
+ surface
+ sequence
```

For computation:

```text
problem
+ numerical representation
+ operation
+ memory
+ verification
```

For poetry:

```text
meaning
+ language
+ meter
+ aesthetic judgment
+ audience
```

Yoga is the act of bringing these into operative unity.

# 28. Program Feature

The verse also clarifies the Program Feature. A Program does not run on “reality in general.” It runs because some feature has been discriminated:

```text
this is a sign to be written
this object requires a seal
this quantity must be calculated
this meaning calls for poetic form
```

Feature-selection determines which technical capacity becomes relevant.

```text
Feature
    selects the practical universal

Program
    articulates its operation

Yoga
    assembles execution

Karma
    is the actual activity
```

The Agent is the unity that performs this movement.

# 29. The Agent cannot be reduced to the program

A fixed program can reproduce a known operation. But the Agent must also determine whether the program applies, whether its representation is adequate, whether an exception has occurred, whether the intended result was achieved, and whether the program itself needs revision.

This is why the Agent emerged from behind the technical manual. Every technical rule presupposes a capacity to recognize its domain and limits.

```text
Program
    is determinate

Agent
    determines the determinacy
and its appropriate application
```

# 30. Art and artifact

The five examples reveal several forms of artifact:

```text
lipi
    constructed sign

mudrā
    reproduced fixed sign

gaṇana
    calculated result

kāvya
    articulated verbal whole

saṃkhyā
    numerical determination
```

Each artifact preserves something of the activity that produced it. But none is identical with the productive Art.

```text
artifact
    is the deposited form of activity

Art
    is the capacity to produce,
evaluate, and transform such forms
```

This is the same distinction between GDSL representation and the higher Art that generates and revises GDSL.

# 31. Representation becomes causal

Writing and sealing introduce fixity into thought. Once produced, the sign can act back upon future cognition:

```text
instruction is written

another agent reads it

the written representation
organizes later action
```

Thus the technical artifact becomes a new causal support.

```text
Agent activity
    →
representation
    →
future Agent activity
```

The ToolChain externalizes itself and then encounters its own externalization as an instrument.

# 32. A recursive technical world

```text
Agent forms Program

Program directs ToolChain

ToolChain produces artifact

artifact enters shared field

artifact conditions another Agent

new Agent revises or extends Program
```

Writing, calculation, and poetry are not merely outputs inside a world. They participate in constructing the world of finite cognizers. The technical environment is accumulated agency made objective.

# 33. Karma as execution trace

```text
Program
    stabilized practical universal

yoga
    assembly and application

karma
    actual execution

artifact
    objective result

vāsanā
    update to future Agent disposition
```

Karma is therefore not equivalent to the Program. It is the Program’s determinate execution through an Agent in a concrete field. The same Program may generate different karmic events according to intention, field, object, execution, accompaniment, and result.

# 34. The role of the `samutthāpaka`

The initiating cognition is especially important because it joins meaning to operation. Without it, the outward form may be reproduced mechanically.

```text
mark appears

but no meaning was intended
```

The complete technical activity includes representation of an end, selection of operation, activation of instrument, and monitoring of execution.

The `samutthāpaka` is therefore close to the Agent’s command-forming activity. It does not merely start the process temporally. It determines what process it is.

# 35. A limit of GDSL

GDSL may accurately encode operations, types, arguments, constraints, and execution order. But the verse forces us to retain the factor that sets the encoded operation into meaningful motion.

A GDSL command becomes an activity only within an actual context of purpose, interpretation, field, selection, and evaluation.

```text
syntax can describe executable form

but syntax does not supply
its own purposive samutthāpaka
```

The higher Art is not another hidden command inside the language. It is the living capacity by which commands acquire determinate relevance.

# 36. Doctrinal determination

The verse securely establishes:

1. there are activities set in motion through disciplined application;
2. such activity must be considered together with its initiating factor;
3. technically applied activity is classified threefold;
4. the likely basis is bodily, verbal, and mental activity;
5. writing, sealing, calculation, poetry, and enumeration are its examples;
6. the examples are assigned correspondingly by `yathākramam`;
7. the exact grouping of the compressed examples requires Bhāṣya confirmation;
8. technical output is inseparable from the cognition organizing its execution.

# 38. Organon interpretation

VAK_4.126 is almost a compact philosophy of the ToolChain.

```text
samutthāpaka
    initiating Agent determination

yoga
    assembly of capacities and instruments

pravartita karma
    running operation

tridhā
    execution through body, speech, or mind

lipi, mudrā, gaṇana, kāvya, saṃkhyā
    objective and cognitive technical products
```

The Shell exposes a fixed operation. The Program gives that operation repeatable form. The Feature determines its field of application. The Pipeline unfolds its execution. But the Agent supplies the purposive and discriminative initiation through which the whole becomes an actual deed.

The technical manual can describe the chain. It cannot replace the Art that sets the chain into meaningful motion.
