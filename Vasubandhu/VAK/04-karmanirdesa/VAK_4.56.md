# VAkK 4.56

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```text
ये निरोधारणामैत्रीदर्शनार्हत्फलोत्थिताः ।
तेषु कारापकारस्य फलं सद्योऽनुभूयते ॥ ४.५६ ॥
```

## Literal translation

> For those who have emerged from cessation, non-contention, loving-kindness, the Path of Seeing, or the fruit of arhatship, the result of benefit or injury done toward them is experienced immediately.

## Doctrinal determination

The verse specifies five exceptional fields:

```text
emergence from cessation
emergence from non-contention
emergence from loving-kindness
emergence from the Path of Seeing
emergence from the fruit of arhatship
```

Toward such persons:

```text
benefit
    → immediate favorable result

injury
    → immediate unfavorable result
```

The repeated force of **utthita**, “having emerged,” is important. The verse concerns persons freshly emerged from an exceptional attainment or path transformation.

## Logical determination

```text
exceptional recipient-field
    + benefit or injury
        → immediate maturation
```

The ethical character of the act determines the polarity of the result; the exceptional field determines its accelerated maturation.

## Determinate result

VAkK 4.56 concretizes the **kṣetra-viśeṣa** of VAkK 4.55: acts of benefit or injury toward persons emerging from these five exceptional states bear fruit immediately.
