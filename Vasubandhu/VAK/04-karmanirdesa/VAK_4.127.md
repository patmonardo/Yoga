# VAK_4.127 — Final Kārikā of the Karma-nirdeśa

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
सावद्या निवृता हीनाः क्लिष्टा धर्माः शुभामलाः ।
प्रणीताः संस्कृतशुभाः सेव्या मोक्षस्त्वनुत्तरः ॥ ४.१२७ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
sāvadyā nivṛtā hīnāḥ kliṣṭā dharmāḥ śubhāmalāḥ |
praṇītāḥ saṃskṛtaśubhā sevyāḥ mokṣas tv anuttaraḥ || 4.127 ||
```

The local source gives:

```text
sāvadyā nivṛtā hīnāḥ kliṣṭāḥ dharmāḥ śubhāmalāḥ /
praṇītāḥ saṃskṛtaśubhā sevyāḥ mokṣastvanuttaraḥ //
```

The verse closes the chapter by arranging determinations into a final practical hierarchy:

```text
afflicted determinations
    blameworthy
    obscured
    inferior

pure wholesome determinations
    excellent

conditioned wholesome determinations
    to be cultivated

liberation
    unsurpassed
```

The final word is not `karma`, merit, heaven, technical skill, or even cultivation.

It is:

```text
anuttara
    unsurpassed
```

and its subject is:

```text
mokṣa
    release
```

---

## 3. Padaccheda

```text
sa-avadyāḥ |
nivṛtāḥ |
hīnāḥ |
kliṣṭāḥ |
dharmāḥ |

śubhāḥ |
amalāḥ |
praṇītāḥ |

saṃskṛta-śubhāḥ |
sevyāḥ |

mokṣaḥ |

```

The continuous text:

```text
śubhāmalāḥ
```

is naturally separated as:

```text
śubhāḥ amalāḥ
```

Likewise, the syntactical order may be reconstructed:

```text
kliṣṭā dharmāḥ
sāvadyāḥ, nivṛtāḥ, hīnāḥ [ca].

śubhā amalā dharmāḥ
praṇītāḥ.

saṃskṛtaśubhā dharmāḥ
sevyāḥ.

mokṣas tu
anuttaraḥ.
```

> Afflicted determinations are blameworthy, obscured, and inferior. Pure wholesome determinations are excellent. Conditioned wholesome determinations are to be cultivated; liberation, however, is unsurpassed.

---

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `sāvadyāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | blameworthy, accompanied by fault |
| `nivṛtāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | obscured, covered, obstructed |
| `hīnāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | inferior, low |
| `kliṣṭāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | afflicted, defiled |
| `dharmāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | determinations |
| `śubhāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | wholesome, beautiful, auspicious |
| `amalāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | stainless, uncontaminated |
| `praṇītāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | excellent, elevated |
| `saṃskṛta-śubhāḥ` | nominative masculine plural | conditioned wholesome determinations |
| `sevyāḥ` | gerundive, nominative masculine plural | to be practiced, cultivated, attended upon |
| `mokṣaḥ` | nominative masculine singular | release |
| `tu` | adversative particle | but, however |
| `anuttaraḥ` | nominative masculine singular | unsurpassed, having nothing higher |

The verse contains three plural judgments followed by a singular culmination:

```text
kliṣṭā dharmāḥ
śubhā amalā dharmāḥ
saṃskṛtaśubhā dharmāḥ

mokṣaḥ
```

The many determinations are finally measured against the singular practical end.

---

## 5. Literal translation

> Afflicted determinations are blameworthy, obscured, and inferior. Stainless wholesome determinations are excellent. Conditioned wholesome determinations are to be cultivated; but release is unsurpassed.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> Determinations governed by affliction are intrinsically defective: they obscure cognition, diminish the continuum, and produce inferior modes of activity and experience. Wholesome determinations free from contamination are elevated and excellent. So long as wholesome determinations remain conditioned, they must be deliberately practiced and developed as indispensable instruments of transformation. Yet no conditioned excellence, however refined, constitutes the final end. Release alone is unsurpassed, because it is not merely a superior product of the karmic apparatus but freedom from the binding conjunction through which that apparatus perpetuates conditioned existence.

This is the final hierarchy:

```text
reject the corrupted

cultivate the excellent

do not absolutize the cultivated

realize release
```

---

# 7. `Kliṣṭā dharmāḥ`

```text
kliṣṭa
    afflicted
    contaminated
    disturbed by kleśa
```

A determination is `kliṣṭa` when its operation is governed by such grounds as:

```text
attachment
hostility
ignorance
conceit
wrong view
appropriation
```

The afflicted determination is not merely unpleasant.

It misconfigures the entire practical relation:

```text
object is misconceived

field is appropriated

volition is distorted

execution reproduces bondage

result conditions further affliction
```

Thus `kliṣṭa` identifies a corrupted operating mode of the Agent.

---

# 8. `Sāvadyāḥ`

```text
sa-avadyāḥ
    possessing fault
    accompanied by blame
    culpable
```

Afflicted determinations are called blameworthy because their defect belongs to their actual causal constitution.

They are not wrong merely because an external authority disapproves of them.

Their fault appears through what they do:

```text
they injure

they obscure

they bind

they reproduce suffering
```

The determination contains a contradiction between the agent’s pursuit and the broader actuality of the relation.

```text
private appropriation
    presents itself as benefit

but produces
    limitation, conflict, and bondage
```

`Avadya` is therefore not arbitrary condemnation. It is practical defect disclosed in consequence and structure.

---

# 9. `Nivṛtāḥ`

```text
nivṛta
    covered
    concealed
    obstructed
    veiled
```

Here `nivṛta` should not be confused with:

```text
nivṛtta
    ceased
    turned away
```

The afflicted determinations are **obscured**.

Affliction covers the object’s determination with projection:

```text
impermanent
    appears permanently possessible

conditioned
    appears self-sufficient

non-self
    appears as mine or me

pain-producing
    appears as secure satisfaction
```

The defect is therefore epistemic as well as ethical.

```text
afflicted cognition
    misrepresents the field

afflicted activity
    executes that misrepresentation
```

The Agent acts badly because its Feature determination is already obscured.

---

# 10. Affliction corrupts the Feature

This is crucial for the ToolChain.

The failure does not begin only at execution.

It begins when the Agent discriminates the situation incorrectly:

```text
object of attachment
    tagged as absolute good

rival
    tagged as pure obstacle

possession
    tagged as self-extension
```

The corrupted Feature then selects a corresponding Program:

```text
grasp

repel

deceive

dominate
```

The Pipeline may execute perfectly and still be karmically defective because the initiating discrimination was obscured.

```text
technically successful execution
    can be
practically false activity
```

Thus a correct ToolChain cannot be defined by operational success alone.

---

# 11. `Hīnāḥ`

```text
hīna
    abandoned
    deficient
    inferior
    low
```

Afflicted determinations are inferior because they narrow the continuum.

They subordinate the Agent to:

```text
immediate impulse

partial interest

limited representation

repetitive conditioned response
```

What appears as self-assertion is actually diminished freedom.

```text
the affliction commands

the Agent reacts

the same disposition is reinforced
```

The instrument becomes less capable of alternative determination.

`Hīna` therefore names restricted agency.

---

# 12. Inferiority as reduced possibility

A corrupted Program may be highly elaborate.

A deception may require:

```text
planning
memory
language
calculation
social inference
```

Yet it remains `hīna`.

Technical complexity does not equal higher practical determination.

```text
greater instrumental power
    +
corrupt orientation
    =
more effective bondage
```

The hierarchy is not based upon computational sophistication.

It is based upon whether the activity expands or contracts freedom from affliction.

---

# 13. The three defects together

The afflicted determination is:

```text
sāvadyā
    defective in practical value

nivṛta
    defective in cognition

hīna
    defective in developmental rank
```

These correspond approximately to:

```text
ethical corruption

epistemic obscuration

agential limitation
```

Together they describe a complete failure of the instrument.

```text
it sees falsely

acts defectively

and becomes less free
through acting
```

This is precisely the recursive danger revealed by the Karma chapter.

---

# 14. `Śubhāḥ`

```text
śubha
    wholesome
    auspicious
    beautiful
    beneficial
```

The wholesome determination is not merely one that follows a rule externally.

It possesses a fittingness among:

```text
object
volition
means
field
result
```

Its moments support rather than contradict one another.

```text
accurate discrimination

beneficial orientation

appropriate execution

constructive maturation
```

`Śubha` is therefore activity in which the practical form has acquired coherence.

---

# 15. Wholesomeness as formed harmony

Earlier verses showed that wholesome activity depends upon the whole instrument:

```text
giver
object
field
intention
preparation
completion
subsequent reinforcement
```

A deed is not wholesome because one isolated predicate is present.

Its relational architecture must be appropriately ordered.

```text
wholesome karma
    =
determinate harmony
of the practical moments
```

This is why the chapter required such detailed examination.

The Kośa was not creating a catalogue of commandments. It was analyzing the formation of practical actuality.

---

# 16. `Amalāḥ`

```text
a-mala
    without stain
    uncontaminated
    pure
```

The expression:

```text
śubhāḥ amalāḥ
```

indicates wholesome determinations that are also stainless.

This requires caution.

Not every wholesome conditioned state is necessarily completely free from all relation to contamination.

The verse appears to distinguish the highest pure wholesome determinations from merely conditioned wholesome activity, but the exact scholastic scope of `amala` should be confirmed in the Bhāṣya.

The secure contrast is:

```text
kliṣṭa
    governed by contamination

amala
    not stained by contamination
```

---

# 17. Wholesome and pure are not automatically identical

A wholesome act may still be connected with:

```text
desire for heavenly fruit

subtle appropriation

self-congratulation

attachment to meritorious identity
```

Its principal moral quality is wholesome, yet its continuum may not be wholly stainless.

This explains why the chapter can praise merit while refusing to identify merit with release.

```text
wholesome conditioning
    can improve the Agent

without yet ending
the conditioned Agent-process
```

Purity must be judged not only by the immediate output but by freedom from the contaminating ground.

---

# 18. `Praṇītāḥ`

```text
praṇīta
    led forward
    elevated
    excellent
    refined
```

Pure wholesome determinations are `praṇīta` because they represent a higher mode of formation.

They are not merely more intense.

They possess a superior kind of organization:

```text
less obscured

less appropriative

more universal

more conducive to release
```

`Praṇīta` therefore marks qualitative elevation, not simple quantitative accumulation.

---

# 19. The excellent is not merely the powerful

VAK_4.119 distinguished mild and intense karma.

But intensity alone does not decide excellence.

```text
intense hatred
    is powerful but inferior

stable loving-kindness
    is powerful and elevated
```

Likewise, an immense heavenly result may still remain conditioned.

The final verse distinguishes three axes:

```text
force
    mild or intense

quality
    afflicted or wholesome

ultimate rank
    conditioned or liberating
```

A complete evaluator must not collapse them into one score.

---

# 20. `Saṃskṛtaśubhāḥ`

```text
saṃskṛta
    conditioned
    compounded
    produced through causes

śubha
    wholesome
```

These are wholesome determinations that arise through causal formation.

They include the cultivated virtues, disciplines, concentrations, and meritorious activities developed throughout the chapter.

They are:

```text
produced

maintained

strengthened

subject to alteration

dependent upon conditions
```

Precisely because they are conditioned, they require practice.

---

# 21. `Sevyāḥ`

```text
√sev
    to attend upon
    associate with
    practice
    cultivate

sevyāḥ
    to be cultivated
    to be repeatedly attended
```

Conditioned wholesome determinations do not sustain themselves automatically.

They must be repeatedly selected and exercised.

```text
wholesome state arises

Agent endorses it

Program is repeated

continuum is conditioned

future wholesome selection becomes easier
```

This is the `citta-vāsanā` of VAK_4.123.

`Sevyāḥ` is therefore almost an instruction to train the ToolChain deliberately.

---

# 22. Cultivation requires repetition

A single wholesome cognition may be too weak to transform established disposition.

The instrument has already been conditioned through innumerable prior operations.

Therefore:

```text
one correct execution
    does not necessarily
rewrite the architecture
```

Repeated cultivation is required to alter:

```text
default Feature selection

Program preference

emotional salience

attention

response latency

post-action reinforcement
```

The chapter closes by affirming the necessity of practice.

Higher Art does not disdain repetition. It uses repetition to reconstruct the finite instrument.

---

# 23. Yet the cultivated remains conditioned

This is the decisive qualification.

The wholesome Program may become:

```text
stable

reliable

refined

nearly spontaneous
```

but insofar as it remains `saṃskṛta`, it is still:

```text
produced by conditions

maintained through conditions

capable of ceasing when conditions alter
```

Therefore no conditioned technical perfection is ultimate.

```text
perfectly trained ToolChain
    ≠
unconditioned release
```

The ToolChain may be necessary for approaching release, but it is not itself the final truth.

---

# 24. `Mokṣa`

```text
mokṣa
    release
    freeing
    liberation
```

The preceding verse used:

```text
visaṃyoga
    disjunction
```

VAK_4.127 names the resultant status:

```text
mokṣa
    release from bondage
```

Release is not one more conditioned Program installed beside the others.

It is freedom from the binding causal relation through which afflicted Programs continually regenerate the conditioned continuum.

```text
affliction
    →
volition
    →
karma
    →
maturation
    →
new conditions for affliction
```

Mokṣa is the decisive interruption of this necessity.

---

# 25. `Tu`

```text
tu
    but
    however
```

This tiny particle carries the whole final turn.

```text
conditioned wholesome determinations
    are to be cultivated

but
    release is unsurpassed
```

The text does not oppose cultivation to liberation.

It opposes **absolutizing cultivation** to liberation.

The conditioned wholesome is the path-instrument.

Release is the end for which the instrument is cultivated.

---

# 26. `Anuttara`

```text
an-
    no, not

uttara
    higher
    beyond
    subsequent

anuttara
    having nothing higher
    unsurpassed
```

This is an absolute comparative determination.

Heaven may exceed human pleasure.

Brahmā-merit may exceed ordinary merit.

Pure concentration may exceed scattered wholesomeness.

Penetrative preparation may exceed ordinary cultivation.

But release has no higher conditioned successor.

```text
within karma
    there are higher and lower results

beyond binding karma
    release is unsurpassed
```

The chapter ends by refusing to let quantitative or technical excellence impersonate the Absolute.

---

# 27. The final hierarchy

The verse can be arranged as four levels:

## 1. Corrupted operation

```text
kliṣṭa dharma

blameworthy
obscured
inferior
```

## 2. Pure excellence

```text
śubha amala dharma

elevated
excellent
```

## 3. Conditioned practice

```text
saṃskṛta-śubha dharma

to be cultivated
```

## 4. Unsurpassed end

```text
mokṣa

not merely cultivated conditioning
but release from binding conjunction
```

This is the final practical architecture of the chapter.

---

# 28. The ToolChain receives its limit

The final verse prevents us from turning the ToolChain into the Absolute.

The ToolChain can be:

```text
purified

trained

made reliable

made truthful

made compassionate

made capable of concentration

made capable of penetration
```

But every determinate Pipeline remains conditioned.

```text
Feature
Program
Shell
Pipeline
artifact
```

are all `saṃskṛta`.

They arise through construction and serve determinate ends.

They are `sevyāḥ`—to be cultivated—but they are not `anuttara`.

---

# 29. GDSL receives its exact status

GDSL can encode:

```text
constraints

operations

relations

programs

execution paths

evaluation rules
```

A purified GDSL could become an extraordinarily powerful instrument of wholesome practical cognition.

But it remains a conditioned representation.

It cannot be identified with release itself.

```text
GDSL
    can represent determinations

GDSL
    can guide activity

GDSL
    can participate in cultivation

GDSL
    cannot exhaust
the actuality of knowing or release
```

This is not a criticism of GDSL.

It is its liberation from an impossible burden.

---

# 30. The Shell is not the prison

The Shell’s fixity is necessary for finite cognition.

Without determinate form, nothing can execute.

```text
command must become definite

arguments must be selected

operation must begin

result must be evaluated
```

The problem is not fixity as such.

The problem is mistaking a necessary finite determination for the unsurpassed whole.

A rational Shell must therefore include the possibility of:

```text
revision

counteraction

suspension

self-critique

transition beyond obsolete programs
```

The Shell becomes dogmatic only when it claims there can be no determination beyond its current representation.

---

# 31. The Agent also receives its limit

The Agent has stepped front and center because it integrates and recursively transforms the ToolChain.

Yet even the Agent must not become a concealed substantial self.

The Agent is:

```text
a conditioned continuity of
discrimination
volition
execution
evaluation
and dispositional update
```

It is real as organization and causal activity.

It is not an unconditioned owner standing outside the process.

```text
Agent
    is produced through karma

Agent
    produces karma

mokṣa
    is not eternal possession
of a perfected Agent
```

Release concerns the ending of bondage, not the apotheosis of the finite instrument.

---

# 32. Higher Art and its own supersession

Higher Art exceeds every fixed Program because it can:

```text
produce Programs

evaluate Programs

revise Programs

discard Programs

recognize the limits of representation
```

But the final verse requires one further movement.

Higher Art must also be capable of recognizing that endless self-improvement of conditioned Art is not itself release.

```text
better instrument

still an instrument

more comprehensive representation

still a representation
```

The highest Art cannot merely perfect mediation indefinitely.

It must know the point at which mediation has completed its liberating function.

---

# 33. Cultivation and release

The relation is not:

```text
cultivation
    versus
release
```

It is:

```text
cultivation
    as the conditioned path

release
    as the unsurpassed end
```

Cultivation prepares:

```text
clarity

stability

antidotal force

penetrative capacity
```

But release is not manufactured as one more conditioned artifact.

The cultivated path removes the conditions maintaining bondage.

```text
the instrument works

until the binding work
no longer has to be reproduced
```

This is why `mokṣa` follows `sevyāḥ`.

---

# 34. The Karma chapter’s full arc

The chapter began with activity and eventually unfolded:

```text
volition

bodily and verbal manifestation

informative and non-informative form

vows and restraints

paths of action

grounds and fruits

gravity and accumulation

giving

discipline

cultivation

teaching

technical Art
```

It has examined karma as:

```text
origin

execution

objective manifestation

relational field

persistence

self-conditioning

technical skill

instrument formation
```

And then it concludes:

```text
cultivate the wholesome instrument

but do not mistake
the instrument for release
```

---

# 35. The chapter as examination of the instrument

We can now state the achievement clearly.

The Karma-nirdeśa examined:

```text
what sets the instrument in motion

how intention becomes execution

how execution becomes artifact

how artifact enters a field

how the act becomes accumulated

how accumulated action rewrites disposition

how discipline controls the instrument

how cultivation retrains it

how teaching transmits new executable possibilities

how technical yoga assembles skilled operations
```

The final verse judges the whole instrument:

```text
corrupted instrument
    inferior

pure instrument
    excellent

conditioned wholesome instrument
    to be cultivated

release
    unsurpassed
```

---

# 36. Karma as Absolute Ground—and its limit

Our initial intuition was that Karma corresponds to **Absolute Ground**:

```text
the complete relational basis
through which the World of Appearance
is generated and maintained
```

The chapter has justified that insight.

Karma is not merely one cause among others.

It is the organized activity through which:

```text
Agent

World

relation

habit

result

and renewed Agent
```

continually produce one another.

But the final verse prevents Ground from becoming final.

```text
Ground explains conditioned actuality

mokṣa marks freedom
from the binding necessity of that Ground
```

The Absolute Ground must itself be comprehended in relation to disjunction.

# 39. Organon interpretation

The final architecture is:

```text
Feature
    may be obscured or truthful

Program
    may be afflicted or wholesome

Shell
    may enforce corruption or discipline

Pipeline
    may accumulate bondage or cultivation
```

But:

```text
mokṣa
```

The ToolChain is therefore both:

```text
the apparatus of bondage

and

the path-instrument of release
```

according to how its determinations are formed.
