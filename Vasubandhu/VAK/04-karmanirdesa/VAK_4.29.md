# VAK_4.29

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
शीलाङ्गान्यप्रमादाङ्गं व्रताङ्गानि यथाक्रमम् ।
चत्वार्येकं तथा त्रीणि स्मृतिनाशो मदश्च तैः ॥
```

## Literal translation

> Respectively, four are limbs of discipline, one is a limb of non-negligence, and three are limbs of observance. Through these, the destruction of recollection and intoxication are prevented.

## Doctrinal determination

The eight upavāsa restraints are functionally classified:

```text
first four:
    śīlāṅga
    non-killing, non-stealing, celibacy, truthful speech

fifth:
    apramādāṅga
    abstention from intoxicants

last three:
    vratāṅga
    improper-time eating, adornment/entertainment,
    high or luxurious beds and seats
```

The first four directly regulate conduct. The fifth preserves recollection and vigilance. The final three reshape the surrounding mode of life and counter indulgent excitation.

## Philosophical translation

> Of the eight limbs of the observance, the first four constitute ethical discipline; abstention from intoxicants safeguards non-negligence; and the final three constitute the special ascetic form of the vow. The safeguarding rule prevents the destruction of recollection, while the ascetic limbs counter intoxication, self-inflation, and indulgent excitation.

## Logical determination

```text
śīla
    determines conduct

apramāda
    preserves the active presence of discipline

vrata
    constructs a supporting form of life
```

Thus the vow is not a flat list of prohibitions. It is a functional whole composed of direct restraint, preservation, and environmental support.

## Relation to kleśa and Path

```text
ordinary vow:
    restrains manifestation

meditative restraint:
    suppresses kleśa

noble Path:
    abandons kleśa
```

The upavāsa vow begins to reorganize not only overt action but also the conditions in which kleśa gains strength.
