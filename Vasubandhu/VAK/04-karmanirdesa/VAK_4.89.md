## 5. Literal Translation

> For unwholesome karma, the results beginning with the wholesome are respectively two, three, and four. For indeterminate karma, these results beginning with the wholesome are two, three, and three.

A fuller rendering:

> Unwholesome karma stands in two wholesome result-relations, three unwholesome result-relations, and four indeterminate result-relations. Indeterminate karma stands in two wholesome, three unwholesome, and three indeterminate result-relations.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The quality of an activity and the quality of the determinations functioning as its results do not coincide through simple identity. Unwholesome activity may enter into two wholesome, three unwholesome, and four indeterminate kinds of result-relation. Indeterminate activity may enter into two wholesome, three unwholesome, and three indeterminate kinds. The causal system is therefore mediated through a matrix: each source-quality must be related separately to each possible result-quality.

The completed structure is not:

```text
wholesome produces wholesome
unwholesome produces unwholesome
indeterminate produces indeterminate
```

Rather:

```text
each source class
    stands in several distinct relations
to each result class
```

The ethical determination of the source is one axis. The ethical determination of the result is another.

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

### aśubha

```text
aśubha
    unwholesome
```

It identifies the ethical quality of the source karma, not automatically the quality of every dharma that may function as a result in one of the five technical senses.

### avyākṛta

```text
avyākṛta
    ethically indeterminate
```

An indeterminate dharma is neither wholesome nor unwholesome, but it remains fully classifiable by cause, condition, result, domain, time, function, and relation. Ethical indeterminacy is therefore a restricted determination.

### anukrama

```text
anukrama
    ordered succession
    corresponding sequence
```

The numbers have no independent meaning without the ordered columns. The verse therefore encodes a relation table in compressed grammatical form.

### śubhādi

```text

    beginning with the wholesome
```

It operates as a reusable heading across verses 4.88–4.89.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

### The unwholesome row

For unwholesome karma:

```text
wholesome results: 2
unwholesome results: 3
indeterminate results: 4
```

The largest number lies in the indeterminate column. This must not immediately be interpreted psychologically or morally. It means that four of the technical result-relations can have indeterminate dharmas functioning as results of unwholesome karma. The exact four must await the Bhāṣya.

### The indeterminate row

For indeterminate karma:

```text
wholesome results: 2
unwholesome results: 3
indeterminate results: 3
```

Again, ethical indeterminacy of the source does not restrict its result-relations to indeterminate dharmas alone.

### Completion of the table

```text
                        Result quality
Source karma        Wholesome   Unwholesome   Indeterminate
Wholesome               4            2              3
Unwholesome             2            3              4
Indeterminate           2            3              3
```

This is a classification of permissible relations between two determinations: quality of source karma and quality of result dharma.

### No immediate moral paradox

The statement that unwholesome karma has wholesome results does not by itself mean that wrongdoing produces morally good recompense. `Phala` here ranges over five technical causal relations: maturation, outflow, disconnection, activity-produced result, and dominant result. Some of these relations may connect a source karma with dharmas whose own ethical classification differs from that source.

### Mediation is the system

The table demonstrates that karma is not related to result by simple qualitative resemblance alone. The result relation is mediated by result-type, ethical quality of source, ethical quality of result, contamination status, and path relation.

## 9. Logical Determination

Let:

```text
Qₛ = source-karma quality
Qᵣ = result quality
T  = technical result-type
```

Then the table defines a cardinality function:

```text
C(Qₛ, Qᵣ)
    = number of result-types T
      admissible for that ordered pair
```

The values are:

```text
C(Wholesome, Wholesome) = 4
C(Wholesome, Unwholesome) = 2
C(Wholesome, Indeterminate) = 3
C(Unwholesome, Wholesome) = 2
C(Unwholesome, Unwholesome) = 3
C(Unwholesome, Indeterminate) = 4
C(Indeterminate, Wholesome) = 2
C(Indeterminate, Unwholesome) = 3
C(Indeterminate, Indeterminate) = 3
```

The relation is ordered and not necessarily symmetric. Equal cardinality also does not imply identical result-type membership.

The five result-types form the mediating axis. The fuller structure is ternary:

```text
SourceQuality × ResultType × ResultQuality
```

The two-dimensional table is a projection of that deeper three-place relation.
