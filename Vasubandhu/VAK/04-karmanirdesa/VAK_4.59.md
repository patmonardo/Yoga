# VAkK 4.59

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```text
वङ्कदोषकषायोक्तिः शाठ्यद्वेषजरागजे ।
कृष्णशुक्लादिभेदेन पुनः कर्म चतुर्विधम् ॥ ४.५९ ॥
```

## Sanskrit — IAST

```text
vaṅka-doṣa-kaṣāyoktiḥ śāṭhya-dveṣaja-rāgaje /
kṛṣṇa-śuklādi-bhedena punaḥ karma caturvidham // VAkK_4.59 //
```

## Padaccheda

```text
vaṅka-doṣa-kaṣāya-uktiḥ
śāṭhya-dveṣaja-rāgaje
kṛṣṇa-śukla-ādi-bhedena
punaḥ karma caturvidham
```

## Literal translation

“Crookedness,” “fault,” and “stain” are expressions for what is born respectively from deceit, hatred, and attachment. Again, karma is fourfold according to the division beginning with black and white.

## Doctrinal determination

The first half correlates three afflictive deformations:

```text
śāṭhya → vaṅka
    deceitfulness → crookedness

dveṣa → doṣa
    hatred → fault or corruption

rāga → kaṣāya
    attachment → stain or defilement
```

The second half introduces a new fourfold classification of karma. Its members are completed in VAkK 4.60:

```text
black
white
black-and-white
neither black nor white
```

## Technical vocabulary

- **vaṅka** — crookedness
- **śāṭhya** — deceitfulness or duplicity
- **doṣa** — fault, corruption
- **dveṣa** — hatred
- **kaṣāya** — stain, defilement
- **rāga** — attachment
- **kṛṣṇa** — black, karmically dark
- **śukla** — white, karmically bright

## Determinate result

VAkK 4.59 establishes:

```text
deceit produces crookedness
hatred produces fault
attachment produces stain
```

It then announces the fourfold classification of karma beginning with black and white.
