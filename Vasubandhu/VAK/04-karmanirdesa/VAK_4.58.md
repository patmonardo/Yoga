# VAkK 4.58

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```text
चित्तक्षेपो मनश्चित्ते स च कर्मविपाकजः ।
भयोपघातवैषम्यशोकैश्चाकुरुकामिनाम् ॥ ४.५८ ॥
```

## Grammar

| Form | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **cittakṣepaḥ** | mental derangement, disturbance of mind |
| **manaścitte** | in mental cognition |
| **karmavipākajaḥ** | born from karmic maturation |
| **bhaya** | fear |
| **upaghāta** | injury, violent disturbance |
| **vaiṣamya** | disorder or imbalance |
| **śoka** | grief |
| **akurukāminām** | of desire-domain beings other than the Kurus |

## Literal translation

> Mental derangement occurs in mental cognition. It is born from karmic maturation, and also from fear, injury, imbalance, and grief, among desire-domain beings other than the Kurus.

## Doctrinal determination

The verse distinguishes two causal levels:

```text
underlying cause
    karmic maturation

immediate occasions
    fear
    injury
    bodily imbalance
    grief
```

Mental derangement itself belongs to **mental cognition**. The karma does not necessarily produce the deranged mental state directly; it may mature in a bodily or constitutional disorder that then occasions disturbance of cognition.

### Akurukāminām

```text
a-kuru-kāminām
    desire-domain beings excluding Uttarakuru
```

The inhabitants of Uttarakuru are excluded because the conditions producing such derangement are absent there according to the Kośa cosmology.

## Important distinction

```text
karma
    may provide the matured basis

present conditions
    trigger the manifest derangement
```

Thus the same event can have both:

```text
a remote karmic ground
and
an immediate present condition
```

## Technical vocabulary

- **cittakṣepa** — mental derangement or disruption
- **manaścitta** — mental cognition
- **karmavipākaja** — produced from karmic maturation
- **upaghāta** — injury or damaging disturbance
- **vaiṣamya** — imbalance, especially bodily humoral disorder

## Determinate result

VAkK 4.58 establishes:

```text
mental derangement
    occurs in mental cognition

its underlying basis
    may arise from karmic maturation

its immediate causes include
    fear, injury, imbalance, and grief
```
