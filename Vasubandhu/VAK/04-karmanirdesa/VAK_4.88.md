# VAK_4.88

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

अनास्रवं पुनः शेषं त्रिभिरव्याकृतं च यत् ।  
चत्वारि द्वे तथा त्रीणि कुशलस्य शुभादयः ॥ ४.८८ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**anāsravaṃ punaḥ śeṣaṃ tribhir avyākṛtaṃ ca yat |**  
**catvāri dve tathā trīṇi kuśalasya śubhādayaḥ || 4.88 ||**

Local source:

```text
anāsravaṃ punaḥ śeṣaṃ tribhiravyākṛtaṃ ca yat /
catvāri dve tathā trīṇi kuśalasya śubhādayaḥ // VAkK_4.88 //
```

The verse performs two connected tasks:

```text
1. completes the general classification
   of karma by result-count;

2. begins a new table classifying
   the results of karma according
   to wholesome, unwholesome,
   and indeterminate status.
```

Its first half states provisionally:

```text
remaining uncontaminated karma:
    three results

indeterminate karma:
    three results
```

Its second half begins the quality-table:

```text
for wholesome karma:

    wholesome results:
        four

    unwholesome results:
        two

    indeterminate results:
        three
```

VAK_4.89 completes the corresponding rows for unwholesome and indeterminate karma.

## 4. Grammar

### anāsravam

Neuter nominative singular:

```text
an-
    without

āsrava
    outflow
```

Thus:

```text
anāsrava
    without outflows
    uncontaminated
```

It qualifies the understood `karma`.

### punaḥ

Indeclinable:

```text
again
in turn
on the other hand
```

It marks continuation of the classification begun in VAK_4.87.

### śeṣam

Neuter nominative singular:

```text
remaining
what is left over
the residual class
```

It distinguishes this karma from the uncontaminated path-karma already classified in the preceding verse.

Thus:

```text
anāsravaṃ śeṣam
    the remaining uncontaminated karma
```

### tribhiḥ

Instrumental plural:

```text
with three
```

The understood noun is:

```text
phalaiḥ
    results
```

### avyākṛtam

Neuter nominative singular:

```text
a-
    not

vyākṛta
    determined
    declared
    ethically specified
```

Technical translation:

```text
avyākṛta
    indeterminate
```

This does not mean unintelligible or causally undefined. It means that the karma is not determined as wholesome or unwholesome.

### yat

Neuter nominative singular relative pronoun:

```text
whatever

```

It refers to:

```text
whatever karma is indeterminate
```

### catvāri

Neuter nominative or accusative plural:

```text
four
```

It agrees with the understood `phalāni`, results.

### dve

Neuter dual:

```text
two
```

Again, two results.

### trīṇi

Neuter plural:

```text
three
```

### kuśalasya

Genitive singular:

```text
of the wholesome
of wholesome karma
```

The understood noun is:

```text
karmaṇaḥ
```

### śubhādayaḥ

Masculine nominative plural compound:

```text
śubha
    wholesome

ādi
    beginning with
```

Thus:

```text
śubhādayaḥ
    those beginning with the wholesome
```

The ordered three are supplied by the established ethical classification:

```text
wholesome
unwholesome
indeterminate
```

The exact members of each numerical result-set remain to be explained by the Bhāṣya.

## 5. Literal Translation

> The remaining uncontaminated karma possesses three results, as does whatever karma is indeterminate. For wholesome karma, the results beginning with the wholesome are respectively four, two, and three.

A slightly fuller rendering:

> Uncontaminated karma other than the previously mentioned path-karma is connected with three kinds of result; indeterminate karma is likewise connected with three. With respect to wholesome karma, four result-types are wholesome, two are unwholesome, and three are indeterminate.

---

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> The causal range of activity must be classified not only by the total number of result-relations available to it, but also by the ethical determination of those results. Remaining uncontaminated activity and indeterminate activity each stand in three result-relations. Wholesome activity, however, can be related to results of all three ethical classes: four wholesome result-types, two unwholesome result-types, and three indeterminate result-types.

The verse introduces a new distinction:

```text
result-count
    ≠
result-quality
```

VAK_4.87 asked:

```text
How many result-types
belong to this class of karma?
```

VAK_4.88 now asks:

```text
Of what ethical quality
are the results related to
wholesome karma?
```

The classification therefore becomes two-dimensional:

```text
number of results

and

quality of results
```

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

### anāsrava

```text
anāsrava
    uncontaminated
    free from outflows
```

The verse distinguishes two uncontaminated classes:

```text
uncontaminated path-karma
    four results
    from VAK_4.87

remaining uncontaminated karma
    three results
    from VAK_4.88
```

The distinction between these two classes must be retained.

### śeṣa

```text
śeṣa
    remainder
    residual class
```

A residual class is not doctrinally insignificant. It means that the preceding classification did not exhaust the extension of uncontaminated karma.

### avyākṛta

```text
avyākṛta
    indeterminate
    neither wholesome nor unwholesome
```

This is an ethical classification, not the absence of all determination.

An indeterminate karma may still possess:

```text
causal conditions
result-relations
domain
temporal location
and functional classification
```

It is only indeterminate with respect to the wholesome–unwholesome opposition.

### kuśala

The verse uses `kuśala`, while its results are introduced through `śubha`.

For this pass both belong to the wholesome field, but the lexical distinction should not be erased:

```text
kuśala
    skillful or wholesome determination

śubha
    favorable or wholesome result-quality
```

The Bhāṣya may clarify whether the variation carries technical force here.

### śubhādayaḥ

This is a compact table-heading:

```text
śubha-ādi
    wholesome and the classes following it
```

The sequence is:

```text
wholesome
unwholesome
indeterminate
```

The numerals are assigned in that order.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

### Remaining uncontaminated karma: three results

VAK_4.87 assigned four results to karma in the uncontaminated path.

VAK_4.88 now identifies another uncontaminated class:

```text
remaining uncontaminated karma
```

This residual uncontaminated karma possesses three results.

Therefore:

```text
all uncontaminated karma
    does not possess
the same result-count
```

Path relation remains a determining factor.

### Indeterminate karma: three results

Whatever karma is ethically indeterminate also possesses three results.

This does not imply that its three results are identical to the three belonging to remaining uncontaminated karma.

The kārikā gives equal cardinality:

```text
3 and 3
```

but does not yet prove identical membership.

### Wholesome karma and result-quality

For wholesome karma, the result-types are distributed:

```text
wholesome:
    four

unwholesome:
    two

indeterminate:
    three
```

This is initially surprising if one assumes:

```text
wholesome karma
    can have only wholesome results
```

The verse rejects that simple identity.

But this must not immediately be interpreted as saying:

> A wholesome act morally produces an evil recompense.

The table concerns the five technical Abhidharma result-relations and the ethical classification of the dharmas functioning as those results.

The exact relations involved must be identified from the Bhāṣya.

### Karma-quality does not uniquely determine result-quality

The safe determination is:

```text
Quality(Karma)
    does not uniquely determine
Quality(Result)
```

A wholesome karma may stand in result-relations with dharmas classified as:

```text
wholesome
unwholesome
or indeterminate
```

The number of applicable result-types differs for each class.

## 9. Logical Determination

Let:

```text
K
    karma

Q(K)
    ethical quality of karma

R(K)
    result-types related to K

Q(r)
    ethical quality of result r
```

For the first half:

```text
RemainingUncontaminatedKarma(K)
    →
|R(K)| = 3
```

```text
IndeterminateKarma(K)
    →
|R(K)| = 3
```

For wholesome karma:

```text
WholesomeKarma(K)
    →
|{r ∈ R(K) : Wholesome(r)}| = 4
```

```text
WholesomeKarma(K)
    →
|{r ∈ R(K) : Unwholesome(r)}| = 2
```

```text
WholesomeKarma(K)
    →
|{r ∈ R(K) : Indeterminate(r)}| = 3
```

### These numbers are not additive into nine distinct results

The verse does **not** necessarily state that wholesome karma possesses nine mutually distinct results.

The counts are taken across the technical result-types under different quality-relations.

A single result-type may be instantiated by dharmas of different ethical quality under different conditions.

Therefore:

```text
4 + 2 + 3
```

should not be interpreted without the Bhāṣya as a simple nine-element set.

### Cardinality must be indexed by quality

The correct structure is:

```text
ResultCardinality(
    karmaClass,
    resultQuality
)
```

not merely:

```text
ResultCardinality(karmaClass)
```

The table is indexed along two axes:

```text
source-karma quality

result quality
```

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

The Kernel-level lesson remains classificatory.

A result relation requires at least:

```text
ResultRelation {
    sourceKarmaClass
    sourceQuality
    resultType
    resultQuality
}
```

The verse shows that these fields cannot be inferred from one another.

In particular:

```text
sourceQuality = Wholesome
```

does not imply:

```text
resultQuality = Wholesome
```

for every technical result-relation.

The system therefore requires a relation table rather than a single inheritance chain:

```text
KarmaQuality × ResultQuality
    → permitted result-types
```

For VAK_4.88, the wholesome row begins:

```text
                 Result quality

Source karma     Wholesome   Unwholesome   Indeterminate
Wholesome            4            2              3
```

VAK_4.89 supplies the remaining rows.

No stronger interpretation is needed before the Bhāṣya identifies the exact result-types counted in each cell.
