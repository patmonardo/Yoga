# VAK_4.15

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
पञ्चाष्टदशसर्वेभ्यो वर्ज्येभ्यो विरतिग्रहात् ।
उपासकोपवासस्थश्रमणोद्देशभिक्षुता ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
pañcāṣṭadaśasarvebhyo varjyebhyo viratigrahāt |
upāsakopavāsasthaśramaṇoddeśabhikṣutā ||
```

## 3. Source reference and caution

Source: `VAkK_4.15`.

This verse explains the **four substantial kinds** announced in VAK_4.14.

Its governing construction is:

```text
abstention from:
    five
    eight
    ten
    all prohibited acts

produces the status of:
    upāsaka
    one established in the fast
    śramaṇoddeśa
    bhikṣu
```

The masculine status terms represent substantial restraint-types. The corresponding gendered and disciplinary names account for the broader eightfold nominal classification.

## 5. Sandhi and compound analysis

### `pañcāṣṭadaśasarvebhyaḥ`

```iast
pañca + aṣṭa + daśa + sarvebhyaḥ
```

The sequence enumerates four scopes of abstention:

```text
five
eight
ten
all
```

Each is understood with `varjyebhyaḥ`:

```text
from five prohibited acts
from eight prohibited acts
from ten prohibited acts
from all prohibited acts
```

### `viratigrahāt`

```iast
virati + graha
```

- `virati` — abstention, cessation, refraining
- `graha` — taking, undertaking, acceptance

A direct translation is:

> from undertaking abstention

This is important: the verse is not merely counting acts one happens not to perform. It concerns the formal taking up of restraint.

### `upavāsastha`

```iast
upavāsa + stha
```

> one standing or established in the fast

This refers to the person undertaking the eightfold observance.

### `śramaṇoddeśa`

```iast
śramaṇa + uddeśa
```

A technical term for one designated toward ascetic status: a novice.

Working translation:

> novice ascetic

It should not be dissolved into an ordinary descriptive phrase.

## 6. Grammatical structure

The ablative `viratigrahāt` expresses the basis or cause:

> From undertaking abstention...

The ablative plurals `varjyebhyaḥ` and `sarvebhyaḥ` specify what one abstains from.

The second half supplies the corresponding results or statuses:

```text
fivefold abstention
    → upāsaka status

eightfold abstention
    → upavāsa status

tenfold abstention
    → śramaṇoddeśa status

abstention from all
    → bhikṣu status
```

The compressed verse omits repeated verbs and case markers because the correspondences are parallel.

## 7. Literal translation

> Through undertaking abstention from five, eight, ten, or all prohibited acts, there is respectively the state of an upāsaka, one established in the fast, a novice ascetic, or a bhikṣu.

## 8. Philosophical translation

> The four substantial forms of prātimokṣa restraint are determined by the scope of abstention undertaken: restraint regarding five prohibited acts constitutes lay discipleship; restraint regarding eight constitutes the fasting observance; restraint regarding ten constitutes novice discipline; and restraint regarding the complete body of prohibitions constitutes full monastic discipline.

## 9. The four substantial kinds

VAK_4.14 stated:

```text
eightfold by name
fourfold in substance
```

VAK_4.15 identifies the four substantial kinds:

```text
prātimokṣa restraint
├── fivefold restraint
├── eightfold restraint
├── tenfold restraint
└── complete restraint
```

These correspond to:

```text
fivefold
    → upāsaka

eightfold
    → upavāsastha

tenfold
    → śramaṇoddeśa

complete
    → bhikṣu
```

The substantial difference therefore lies primarily in the **extent of the restraint undertaken**.

## 10. Fivefold restraint

```text
pañca varjyāḥ
    five things to be avoided
```

This establishes the restraint associated with an `upāsaka`, a lay disciple.

At the kārikā level, the verse gives the number but does not enumerate the five individual precepts.

That enumeration can wait for the commentary or later verses.

## 11. Eightfold restraint

```text
aṣṭa varjyāḥ
    eight things to be avoided
```

This constitutes the status of the `upavāsastha`:

```text
one established in the upavāsa observance
```

This restraint is distinguished by its scope and likely also by its temporal form, but VAK_4.15 itself states only the numerical correspondence.

## 12. Tenfold restraint

```text
daśa varjyāḥ
    ten things to be avoided
```

This establishes the restraint of the `śramaṇoddeśa`, the novice ascetic.

Again, the verse classifies by scope rather than enumerating the content.

## 13. Complete restraint

```text
sarvebhyo varjyebhyaḥ
    from all things to be avoided
```

This establishes `bhikṣutā`:

> the condition or status of a fully ordained monk

The crucial contrast is:

```text
limited enumerated restraint
    five / eight / ten

complete disciplinary restraint
    all prohibited acts
```

## 14. Why eight names but four substances

VAK_4.15 clarifies VAK_4.14.

The real restraint-types are determined by four scopes:

```text
5
8
10
all
```

But disciplinary names may multiply according to gender and institutional designation.

Thus corresponding male and female categories need not constitute different restraint-dharmas when the scope of abstention is substantially the same.

The governing distinction is:

```text
disciplinary person-name
    nominal classification

scope of undertaken abstention
    substantial classification
```

## 15. `virati` as the decisive factor

The verse does not define the status merely through social recognition.

It grounds the status in:

```iast
virati-graha
```

the undertaking of abstention.

Thus:

```text
name alone
    does not establish the restraint

undertaking abstention
    establishes the restraint-type
```

This is the empirical doctrinal point the verse permits.

We need not yet interpret `graha` as a general theory of production or efficient causation.

## 16. Restraint and omission

The verse reinforces the distinction already visible in VAK_4.13:

```text
mere non-performance
    ≠
undertaken abstention
```

A person may happen not to commit an act without possessing the corresponding prātimokṣa restraint.

The restraint arises through:

```text
a determinate undertaking
directed toward
a specified field of prohibited acts
```

## 17. Logical determination

The verse classifies one genus by quantitative scope:

```text
prātimokṣa restraint
    differentiated by
    extent of prohibited field
```

The progression is:

```text
five
    limited lay restraint

eight
    expanded temporary observance

ten
    novice restraint

all
    complete monastic restraint
```

Thus the substantial distinction is not merely qualitative naming. It is a graded determination of extension.

## 18. Field structure

A restrained first-pass schema is:

```text
field of prohibited acts
    ↓

selected extent of abstention
    ↓

corresponding restraint-type
```

More precisely:

```text
five prohibited objects
    → fivefold restraint

eight prohibited objects
    → eightfold restraint

ten prohibited objects
    → tenfold restraint

complete prohibited field
    → complete restraint
```

This is a technical mapping between the scope of abstention and disciplinary status.

## 19. Relation to the sequence

```text
VAK_4.13
    avijñapti is restraint, non-restraint, or neither

VAK_4.14
    prātimokṣa restraint is eightfold nominally
    but fourfold substantially

VAK_4.15
    the four substantial kinds are determined
    by abstention from five, eight, ten, or all
```

VAK_4.15 therefore completes the immediate classification begun in VAK_4.14.

## 20. Light Organon interpretation

The safe logical result is:

```text
one universal form:
    prātimokṣa restraint

four substantial specifications:
    five
    eight
    ten
    all

multiple personal designations:
    the named disciplinary statuses
```

The determination of the restraint depends upon the extension of its prohibited field.

No stronger speculative reading is required at this stage.
