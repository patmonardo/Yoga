# VAK_4.16

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
शीलं सुचरितं कर्म संवरश्चोच्यते पुनः ।
आद्ये विज्ञप्त्यविज्ञप्ती प्रातिमोक्षक्रियापथः ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```iast
śīlaṃ sucaritaṃ karma saṃvaraś cocyate punaḥ |
ādye vijñaptyavijñaptī prātimokṣakriyāpathaḥ ||
```

## 3. Source reference and textual caution

Source: `VAkK_4.16`.

The supplied electronic source reads:

```iast
ādye vijñaptyavijñapto prātimokṣakriyāpathaḥ
```

The ending `-o` in `avijñapto` is grammatically difficult. The working normalization above reads:

```iast
vijñapty-avijñaptī
```

with a dual ending, “manifest and non-manifest action.” This should be checked against the Pradhan critical edition before the text is treated as fully settled.

The compound division of the final expression is also compressed:

```iast
prātimokṣa-kriyāpathaḥ
```

Its exact syntactic attachment should remain provisional until the Bhāṣya pass.

## 5. First pāda

### `śīlaṃ sucaritaṃ karma saṃvaraś cocyate punaḥ`

The verse presents four terms:

```text
śīla
sucarita
karma
saṃvara
```

A restrained translation is:

> It is also called discipline, good conduct, action, and restraint.

The understood subject continues from the preceding verses: **prātimokṣa restraint**.

Thus the same practical factor is being considered under several technical descriptions.

## 6. The four terms

### `śīla`

```text
discipline
ethical conduct
trained mode of behavior
```

Here `śīla` is not merely a moral quality in the abstract. It names the disciplined bodily and verbal conduct associated with restraint.

### `sucarita`

```text
su-carita
    well-conducted
    good conduct
```

This emphasizes the wholesome character of the conduct.

### `karma`

```text
action
```

The restraint belongs within the chapter’s classification of karma because it is an ethically operative bodily or verbal determination.

### `saṃvara`

```text
restraint
containment
```

This emphasizes the prevention of prohibited bodily and verbal activity.

## 7. `ādye` — “the first two”

The most natural grammatical referents are:

```text
śīla
sucarita
```

Thus:

```text
ādye
    the first two terms
```

The verse then appears to explain that these first two names have a broader extension than prātimokṣa avijñapti alone.

## 8. `vijñapti` and `avijñapti`

A cautious construction is:

```text
śīla and sucarita
    include:

    vijñapti
    avijñapti
```

That is:

```text
manifest bodily and verbal conduct

and

the corresponding non-manifest continuation
```

So “discipline” and “good conduct” are not restricted exclusively to the invisible restraint-factor.

They may cover both:

```text
the manifest performance

and

the non-manifest restraint
```

## 9. Working syntactic reconstruction

The verse’s compressed structure may be expanded provisionally as:

```text
Prātimokṣa restraint is called:

    śīla
    sucarita
    karma
    saṃvara

Of these,
the first two terms—śīla and sucarita—
apply to both vijñapti and avijñapti.

Prātimokṣa is a kriyāpatha,
a course of action.
```

The final sentence remains the least secure part of the first-pass reconstruction.

## 10. Literal translation

> It is further called discipline, good conduct, action, and restraint. The first two concern manifest and non-manifest action; prātimokṣa is a course of action.

## 11. Cautious philosophical translation

> Prātimokṣa restraint may be described as discipline, good conduct, action, and restraint. The designations “discipline” and “good conduct” extend across both manifest conduct and its non-manifest continuation. Prātimokṣa restraint is classified within a course of action.

## 12. What the verse adds

VAK_4.13–15 classified restraint by type and scope.

VAK_4.16 now asks:

```text
Under what broader technical names
is this restraint discussed?
```

Its answer is:

```text
discipline
good conduct
action
restraint
```

It therefore places prātimokṣa restraint simultaneously within:

```text
ethical terminology
    śīla, sucarita

action terminology
    karma, kriyāpatha

restraint terminology
    saṃvara
```

## 13. Manifest and non-manifest ethical conduct

The important empirical distinction is:

```text
ethical conduct is not exhausted
by avijñapti alone
```

Rather, at least the terms `śīla` and `sucarita` can encompass:

```text
vijñapti
    observable bodily or verbal expression

avijñapti
    non-observable continuing determination
```

Thus the Kośa does not simply identify ethical conduct with an invisible restraint.

It preserves both aspects.

## 14. Relation to `karma`

The verse explicitly calls restraint:

```iast
karma
```

That classification is significant within the chapter.

Prātimokṣa restraint is not merely:

```text
a social title
a verbal rule
a subjective intention
```

Within the Sarvāstivāda classification it belongs to action.

But the verse does not yet explain in full how the term `karma` is distributed among vijñapti, avijñapti, and cetanā.

## 15. `kriyāpatha`

```text
kriyā
    doing
    performance
    action

patha
    course
    path
```

Working translation:

```text
course of action
```

We should distinguish this wording from the more familiar technical compound:

```iast
karmapatha
```

“path of action.”

The electronic text specifically gives:

```iast
kriyāpathaḥ
```

The precise doctrinal force of the distinction should be verified from the edition and Bhāṣya.

## 16. Restraint remains action

The verse yields a technically important result:

```text
restraint
    is not outside action

restraint
    is one classified form of action
```

Even though restraint may involve the non-occurrence of prohibited conduct, it is itself included under `karma`.

This is the strongest conclusion allowed directly by the first pāda.

We need not yet interpret the metaphysical status of that efficacy.

## 17. Extension of the terms

A provisional extension table is:

| Term | Apparent extension |
|---|---|
| `śīla` | manifest and non-manifest wholesome conduct |
| `sucarita` | manifest and non-manifest good conduct |
| `karma` | restraint considered as action |
| `saṃvara` | restraint considered as containment |
| `prātimokṣa-kriyāpatha` | restraint considered within a course of action |

The exact boundaries among the last three require the Bhāṣya.

## 18. Logical determination

The same factor is classified under several descriptions:

```text
one restraint

considered as trained conduct
    → śīla

considered as good conduct
    → sucarita

considered as causal activity
    → karma

considered as prevention
    → saṃvara
```

This is not yet a claim that all four terms are universally interchangeable.

It states their convergence in the present context.

## 19. Relation to the sequence

```text

    restraint is one form of avijñapti

VAK_4.14
    prātimokṣa restraint is eightfold by name,
    fourfold substantially

    the four substantial forms depend upon
    abstention from five, eight, ten, or all prohibitions

VAK_4.16
    the restraint is described as
    discipline, good conduct, action, and restraint
```

The chapter is moving from numerical classification toward the technical vocabulary under which restraint is analyzed.

## 20. Light Organon interpretation

The safe logical observation is:

```text
one determinate restraint
    admits several valid predicates
```

These predicates express distinct aspects:

```text
form of conduct
ethical quality
action-status
restrictive function
```

No speculative copular reconstruction is needed at this stage.
