# VAkK 4.62

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

नवमे चेतना या सा कृष्णशुक्लक्षयाय च ।
शुक्लस्य ध्यानवैराग्येष्वन्त्यानन्तर्यमार्गजा ॥ ४.६२ ॥

## Sanskrit — IAST

navame cetanā yā sā kṛṣṇaśuklakṣayāya ca /
śuklasya dhyānavairāgyeṣv antyānantaryamārgajā // VAkK_4.62 //

## Padaccheda

navame / cetanā / yā / sā / kṛṣṇa-śukla-kṣayāya / ca / śuklasya / dhyāna-vairāgyeṣu / antya-ānantarya-mārga-jā

## Literal translation

The volition in the ninth [uninterrupted path] serves for the destruction of black-and-white karma; that arising from the final uninterrupted path in the detachments from the Dhyānas serves for the destruction of white karma.

## Doctrinal determination

VAkK 4.61 assigned the first eight uninterrupted paths of detachment from the desire-domain to the destruction of black karma. The ninth and final uninterrupted path completes detachment from the desire-domain; its volition destroys black-and-white karma, that is, wholesome karma belonging to the desire-domain.

White karma is wholesome karma belonging to the form-domain. Its destruction is assigned to volition arising from the final uninterrupted path in detachment from the Dhyānas.

Thus the three conditioned karmic colors are destroyed according to their domains:

- black karma: first eight uninterrupted paths of desire-domain detachment;
- black-and-white karma: ninth uninterrupted path of desire-domain detachment;
- white karma: final uninterrupted paths of Dhyāna-detachment.

The supramundane volitions that perform this destruction are themselves neither black nor white.

## Technical vocabulary

- **navama** — ninth
- **kṛṣṇaśukla** — black-and-white
- **dhyāna-vairāgya** — detachment from a Dhyāna level
- **antya** — final
- **ānantarya-mārga-ja** — arising from an uninterrupted path

## Determinate result

The ninth uninterrupted path of desire-domain detachment destroys black-and-white karma, while the final uninterrupted paths of detachment from the Dhyānas destroy white karma.
