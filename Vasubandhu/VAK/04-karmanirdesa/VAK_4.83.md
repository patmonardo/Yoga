# VAK_4.83

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

सप्तमः स्वयमप्यत्र कामेऽन्यत्र दशाशुभाः ।  
शुभास्त्रयस्तु सर्वत्र संमुखीभावलाभतः ॥ ४.८३ ॥

The local source reads:

```text
saptamaḥ svayamapyatra kāme 'nyatra daśāśubhāḥ /
śubhāstrayastu sarvatra saṃmukhībhāvalābhataḥ // VAkK_4.83 //
```

Normalized segmentation:

```text
saptamaḥ svayam api atra
kāme anyatra daśa aśubhāḥ |

śubhāḥ trayaḥ tu sarvatra
saṃmukhībhāva-lābhataḥ ||
```

The verse completes the distribution begun in VAK_4.82 and introduces the paired modes:

```text
saṃmukhībhāva
    present manifestation

lābha
    acquisition or endowment
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**saptamaḥ svayam apy atra kāme ’nyatra daśāśubhāḥ |**  
**śubhās trayas tu sarvatra saṃmukhībhāva-lābhataḥ || 4.83 ||**

The structure is:

```text
in Uttarakuru:
    the seventh unwholesome course
    also occurs presently by itself

elsewhere in the desire domain:
    all ten unwholesome courses occur

the three wholesome mental courses:
    occur everywhere through
    present manifestation and endowment
```

The “seventh” is counted in the standard sequence:

```text
1–3
    bodily courses

4–7
    verbal courses

7
    disconnected speech

8–10
    mental courses
```

Thus:

```text
saptamaḥ
    = bhinna-pralāpa
    = disconnected speech
```

## 4. Grammar

### saptamaḥ

Masculine nominative singular:

```text
seventh
```

It refers to the seventh karmic course in the standard enumeration: disconnected speech.

The standard sequence is:

```text
1. taking life
2. taking what is not given
3. sexual misconduct

4. false speech
5. divisive speech
6. harsh speech
7. disconnected speech

8. covetousness
9. ill will
10. false view
```

### svayam

Indeclinable:

```text
itself
by itself
independently
personally or actually
```

Here it contrasts the seventh course’s actual occurrence with the merely endowed status of the three mental unwholesome courses described in VAK_4.82.

Provisional sense:

```text
it itself becomes presently manifest
```

The exact nuance should be sharpened during the Bhāṣya pass.

### api

Indeclinable:

```text
also
even
```

It adds disconnected speech to the Uttarakuru configuration already stated.

### atra

Indeclinable:

```text
here
in this case
```

Its nearest contextual referent is:

```text
kurau
    in Uttarakuru
```

from the preceding verse.

### kāme

Locative singular of `kāma`:

```text
in the desire domain
```

The understood full expression is:

```text
kāma-dhātau
```

Following the project terminology:

```text
dhātu
    domain
```

### anyatra

Indeclinable:

```text
elsewhere
in another place
apart from the cases just stated
```

Thus:

```text
kāme anyatra
    elsewhere in the desire domain
```

This excludes the special environments already discussed:

```text
hell

and

Uttarakuru
```

### daśa

Numeral:

```text
ten
```

### aśubhāḥ

Masculine nominative plural:

```text
unwholesome
practically unfavorable
```

The understood noun is:

```text
karmapathāḥ
    karmic courses
```

### śubhāḥ trayaḥ

```text
the three wholesome ones
```

These are the three wholesome mental courses corresponding to the negation of:

```text
covetousness
ill will
false view
```

That is:

```text
non-covetousness

non-ill-will or benevolence

right view
```

The precise canonical names should be retained cautiously until the Bhāṣya supplies them.

### tu

Adversative or contrastive particle:

```text
but
whereas
now
```

It shifts from the unwholesome distribution to the wholesome mental courses.

### sarvatra

Indeclinable:

```text
everywhere
in every relevant domain
```

The scope will be further qualified by VAK_4.84.

### saṃmukhībhāva

Compound:

```text
saṃmukhī
    immediately present
    face-to-face

bhāva
    occurrence
    condition
```

Working translation:

```text
present manifestation
```

### lābhataḥ

Ablative used adverbially:

```text
through acquisition
through attainment
by way of endowment
```

Here `lābha` is closely related to the prior technical notion of `samanvāgama`.

It means that the continuum has acquired and remains endowed with the relevant course.

## 5. Literal Translation

> Here, the seventh also occurs by itself. Elsewhere in the desire domain, there are the ten unwholesome courses. But the three wholesome courses occur everywhere through present manifestation and acquisition.

A fuller rendering:

> In Uttarakuru, the seventh karmic course—disconnected speech—also becomes presently manifest. Elsewhere in the desire domain, all ten unwholesome karmic courses occur through endowment and manifestation. The three wholesome mental courses occur everywhere both by becoming presently manifest and by being acquired as endowments.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> In Uttarakuru, although the three unwholesome mental courses belong to the continuum only as endowments, disconnected speech can additionally become an actual present occurrence. Outside the exceptional environments of Uttarakuru and hell, the desire domain supports the complete tenfold field of unwholesome action. By contrast, the three wholesome mental orientations possess universal scope: they may belong to a continuum as acquired capacities and may also become presently operative throughout the relevant domains.

The verse continues to distinguish:

```text
the structure possessed by a continuum

from

the structure presently actualized
```

It now adds a second determination:

```text
domains differ
not only in what may be possessed

but in what may pass
from possession into manifestation
```

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

### saptama karmapatha

The seventh karmic course is:

```text
bhinna-pralāpa
    disconnected speech
```

Its manifestation in Uttarakuru indicates that even a highly favorable human environment does not exclude every unwholesome verbal occurrence.

The course is apparently able to manifest independently of the three unwholesome mental courses that remain merely endowed there.

This is what `svayam` appears to emphasize.

### kāma-dhātu

```text
kāma-dhātu
    desire domain
```

The domain includes multiple modes of existence with different manifestation constraints.

It is therefore not homogeneous.

```text
one domain

contains

several distinct local possibility-spaces
```

### aśubha karmapatha

```text
aśubha karmapatha
    unwholesome karmic course
```

Elsewhere in the desire domain, the complete set of ten is available.

This means the ordinary desire-domain environment possesses the object-supports, capacities, and situational conditions necessary for all ten courses.

### śubha

```text
śubha
    wholesome
    favorable
    productive of favorable result
```

The three here are mental wholesome courses, not yet the seven bodily and verbal abstentions considered in the following verse.

### saṃmukhībhāva

```text
saṃmukhībhāva
    present actualization
    event-level manifestation
```

It answers:

> What is operating now?

### lābha

```text
lābha
    acquisition
    attainment
    endowment
```

It answers:

> What has become an operative determination of the continuum?

The distinction is:

```text
lābha
    capability or acquired state

saṃmukhībhāva
    current event
```

### sarvatra

The statement that the three wholesome courses occur “everywhere” points to their broad availability.

The following verse will introduce qualifications concerning formless existence and non-percipient beings, so `sarvatra` should be read within the unfolding multi-verse construction rather than as an isolated absolute.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

### Uttarakuru

From VAK_4.82:

```text
the three mental unwholesome courses
    exist through endowment
```

VAK_4.83 adds:

```text
the seventh course
    disconnected speech

may become presently manifest
```

Thus the Uttarakuru configuration is provisionally:

```text
endowed:
    covetousness
    ill will
    false view

presently manifestable:
    disconnected speech
```

The phrase `svayam api` suggests that disconnected speech may occur without the present activation of those three mental courses.

This is doctrinally delicate and should be checked in the Bhāṣya.

### Elsewhere in the desire domain

Outside hell and Uttarakuru:

```text
all ten unwholesome karmic courses
```

are available.

Given the governing contrast of VAK_4.82–4.83, this means availability in the two modes:

```text
endowment

and

present manifestation
```

The ordinary human and other desire-domain environments therefore support the complete practical field.

### The three wholesome mental courses

The three wholesome mental courses are counterparts to:

```text
covetousness
ill will
false view
```

Their positive forms may be provisionally represented as:

```text
non-covetous orientation

benevolent or non-hostile orientation

right view
```

They occur:

```text
through acquisition/endowment

and

through present manifestation
```

### Universality does not mean constant manifestation

The word `sarvatra` does not entail:

```text
every being
at every moment
actively manifests
all three wholesome courses
```

It means that their acquisition and manifestation are not confined to one narrow local environment in the way that some unwholesome courses are.

Thus:

```text
universal availability
    ≠
continuous actuality
```

### Complete unwholesome availability is local

There is an important asymmetry:

```text
all ten unwholesome courses
    are not supported in every environment

the three wholesome mental courses
    possess a broader distribution
```

The verse therefore maps practical possibility onto existential structure.

## 9. Logical Determination

Let:

```text
D
    existential domain

C
    causal continuum

K
    karmic course
```

The Kernel requires at least three predicates:

```text
EndowedWith(C, K)

ManifestableIn(K, D)

PresentlyManifest(C, K, t)
```

### Uttarakuru rule

```text
ExistsIn(C, Uttarakuru)
and K ∈ {
    Covetousness,
    IllWill,
    FalseView
}
    →
EndowedWith(C, K)
```

For the seventh course:

```text
ExistsIn(C, Uttarakuru)
and K = DisconnectedSpeech
    →
ManifestableIn(K, Uttarakuru)
```

### Other desire-domain environments

```text
ExistsIn(C, DesireDomain)
and not ExistsIn(C, Hell)
and not ExistsIn(C, Uttarakuru)
and UnwholesomeCourse(K)
    →
EndowableWith(C, K)
and ManifestableIn(K, D)
```

### Wholesome mental courses

```text
WholesomeMentalCourse(K)
    →
BroadlyEndowable(K)
and BroadlyManifestable(K)
```

### Three levels must remain distinct

```text
CourseTypeExists(K)

EndowedWith(C, K)

PresentlyManifest(C, K, t)
```

These correspond to:

```text
universal determination

continuum-level possession

event-level actuality
```

The first must not be collapsed into the second, nor the second into the third.

### Domain as modal constraint

The domain does not create the universal course-type.

It determines whether that course can be acquired or manifested under its local conditions.

```text
course-type
    universal schema

domain
    modal restriction

continuum
    bearer of endowment

event
    actual manifestation
```

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

The verse remains within the **Kernel’s rational possibility-space**.

It requires the Kernel to distinguish:

```text
defined course

available course

acquired course

currently active course
```

A suitable representation is:

```text
CourseStatus {
    courseType
    domain
    isSupported
    isAcquired
    isManifestable
    isCurrentlyManifest
}
```

### Domain profiles

Each domain can expose a capability profile:

```text
DomainProfile {
    endowableCourses[]
    manifestableCourses[]
    prohibitedManifestations[]
    requiredObjectSupports[]
    completionConditions[]
}
```

For example:

```text
UttarakuruProfile {
    endowedUnwholesomeMentalCourses:
        [covetousness, illWill, falseView]

    manifestableAdditionalCourse:
        [disconnectedSpeech]
}
```

Elsewhere in the desire domain:

```text
OrdinaryDesireDomainProfile {
    endowableUnwholesomeCourses:
        allTen

    manifestableUnwholesomeCourses:
        allTen
}
```

### Why the seventh matters

The special mention of the seventh demonstrates that capability sets are not necessarily inherited as simple blocks.

The Kernel cannot assume:

```text
if one course in a class is manifestable
then all courses in that class are manifestable
```

Nor can it assume:

```text
if mental unwholesome courses
are not manifest

then no unwholesome verbal course
can manifest
```

Each course must be evaluated according to its own supports and constraints.

### Logic as substrate

This is exactly why Logic belongs below the Agent:

```text
Logic determines:
    admissible state
    domain compatibility
    possible transition
    present activation
```

The Agent may later act freely over this substrate, but the Kernel first determines the space of possible configurations.
