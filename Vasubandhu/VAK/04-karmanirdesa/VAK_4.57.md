# VAkK 4.57

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```text
कुशलस्यावितर्कस्य कर्मणो वेदना मता ।
विपाकश्चैतसिक्येव कायिक्येवाशुभस्य तु ॥ ४.५७ ॥
```

## Literal translation

> The maturation of wholesome karma without discursive thought is held to be exclusively mental feeling; but the maturation of unwholesome karma is exclusively bodily feeling.

## Doctrinal determination

```text
wholesome avitarka karma
    → mental feeling

unwholesome karma
    → bodily feeling
```

**Avitarka** indicates wholesome karma associated with higher meditative states beyond discursive thought. Its maturation-feeling is mental.

Unwholesome karma exists only in the desire-domain. Its proper painful maturation is bodily feeling. Mental distress may accompany bodily pain, but it is not thereby necessarily the direct karmic maturation.

## Technical vocabulary

- **avitarka** — without discursive thought
- **caitasikī vedanā** — mental feeling
- **kāyikī vedanā** — bodily feeling
- **vipāka** — maturation

## Determinate result

VAkK 4.57 distinguishes the locus of matured feeling:

```text
wholesome non-discursive karma
    matures as mental feeling

unwholesome karma
    matures as bodily feeling
```
