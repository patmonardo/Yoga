# VAK_4.119

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

```sanskrit
पृष्ठं क्षेत्रमधिष्ठानं प्रयोगश्चेतनाशयः ।
एषां मृद्वधिमात्रत्वात् कर्म मृद्वधिमात्रता ॥ ४.११९ ॥
```

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

```text
pṛṣṭhaṃ kṣetram adhiṣṭhānaṃ prayogaś cetanāśayaḥ |
eṣāṃ mṛdvadhimātratvāt karma mṛdvadhimātratā || 4.119 ||
```

The local source reads exactly:

```text
pṛṣṭhaṃ kṣetramadhiṣṭhānaṃ prayogaścetanāśayaḥ /
eṣāṃ mṛdvadhimātratvāt karmamṛdvadhimātratā //
```

This verse moves from the special analysis of giving to a general examination of the factors by which **karma itself becomes weak or intense**.

---

```text

eṣām |
```

Prose order:

```text
pṛṣṭham, kṣetram, adhiṣṭhānam,
```

> The subsequent accompaniment, the field, the basis, the preparation, the volition, and the underlying intention: because these are weak or intense, karma is weak or intense.

---

## 4. Grammar

| Term | Form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `pṛṣṭham` | nominative neuter singular | what follows behind; subsequent accompaniment |
| `kṣetram` | nominative neuter singular | field or recipient-field |
| `adhiṣṭhānam` | nominative neuter singular | basis, support, object upon which the deed operates |
| `prayogaḥ` | nominative masculine singular | preparation, undertaking, practical application |
| `cetanā` | nominative feminine singular | volition |
| `āśayaḥ` | nominative masculine singular | underlying intention, disposition, inward orientation |
| `eṣām` | genitive plural | of these |
| `mṛdu` | adjective | weak, mild |
| `adhimātra` | adjective | intense, excessive, powerful |
| `-tvāt` | ablative suffix | because of the condition of |
| `karma` | nominative neuter singular | karma, activity |
| `mṛdvadhimātratā` | compound abstract noun | weakness or intensity |

The compound:

```text
mṛdu-adhimātratā
```

expresses a scale:

```text
mild
    ↔
intense
```

The verse does not merely divide karma into good and bad. It asks how strongly a deed is karmically constituted.

---

## 5. Literal translation

> Subsequent accompaniment, field, basis, preparation, volition, and underlying intention—because these are mild or intense, karma has mildness or intensity.

---

## 6. Philosophical translation

> The force of karma is not fixed by the externally visible deed alone. Its intensity depends upon the entire instrument through which the deed is constituted: what follows and reinforces it, the field toward which it is directed, the object or basis upon which it operates, the practical preparation through which it is carried out, the volition governing the act, and the deeper orientation from which that volition arises. As each of these is weak or powerful, the resulting karma is correspondingly weak or powerful.

This is indeed a direct **examination of the instrument**.

```text
visible deed
    is only one moment

complete karma
    is the organized totality
of its conditions and determinations
```

---

# 7. Six determinants of karmic intensity

The verse identifies six:

```text
1. pṛṣṭha
    subsequent reinforcement

2. kṣetra
    field

3. adhiṣṭhāna
    basis or object

4. prayoga
    preparation and execution

5. cetanā
    operative volition

6. āśaya
    underlying orientation
```

These can be grouped into three larger moments:

```text
objective determination
    field and basis

practical determination
    preparation and subsequent reinforcement

subjective determination
    volition and underlying intention
```

Thus karma is evaluated as a whole practical system.

---

# 8. `Pṛṣṭha`

```text
pṛṣṭha
    back
    what stands behind
    what follows afterward
```

In this context, it most plausibly indicates what follows the principal act and reinforces or weakens it.

Examples might include:

```text
rejoicing afterward
repetition afterward
approval afterward
regret afterward
attempted correction afterward
continued support of the deed
```

Thus the deed does not necessarily end at the instant of bodily or verbal completion.

Its causal configuration can continue through the agent’s subsequent relation to it.

```text
act completed
    →
agent endorses, repeats, regrets,
or counteracts it
```

The exact technical scope of `pṛṣṭha` should be secured in the Bhāṣya pass.

---

# 9. Before and after the deed

`Prayoga` and `pṛṣṭha` appear to frame the principal activity temporally:

```text
prayoga
    what leads into and executes the deed

principal deed

pṛṣṭha
    what follows and consolidates the deed
```

This gives karma a temporal extension.

It is not a dimensionless instant.

```text
preparation
    →
performance
    →
subsequent endorsement or reversal
```

The complete instrument includes the approach to the act and the agent’s relation to it afterward.

---

# 10. `Kṣetra`

```text
kṣetra
    field
    the being or relation toward which activity is directed
```

The preceding verses examined this in detail.

A field may be intensified through:

```text
suffering
prior beneficence
spiritual qualities
Dharma function
assured future significance
```

Therefore the same external act can possess radically different karmic weight according to its field.

```text
same material transfer

to an ordinary field
    one magnitude

to a parent, sick person,
Dharma speaker, or Bodhisattva
    another magnitude
```

The field is one component within a larger instrument, not the sole determinant.

---

# 11. `Adhiṣṭhāna`

```text
adhi-sthā
    to stand upon
    to be founded upon

adhiṣṭhāna
    support
    basis
    objective locus
```

Here it likely means the concrete basis upon which the activity operates.

Depending on the deed, this may include:

```text
the living being harmed or helped
the property transferred
the stūpa damaged
the Saṃgha divided
the bodily or verbal object of action
```

`Kṣetra` and `adhiṣṭhāna` should therefore not be immediately collapsed.

A cautious distinction is:

```text
kṣetra
    the karmically qualified relational field

adhiṣṭhāna
    the concrete objective support
upon which the act is carried out
```

The Bhāṣya will need to determine their exact technical separation.

---

# 12. Field and basis

Consider an act of giving:

```text
recipient
    kṣetra

food, medicine, clothing,
or another transferred support
    adhiṣṭhāna
```

Or an act of injury:

```text
parent or arhat
    exceptional field

body or life-faculty directly attacked
    objective basis
```

Thus the act is determined both by:

```text
who stands in the relation
and
what concrete reality is affected
```

This is another reason external description alone is insufficient.

---

# 13. `Prayoga`

```text
pra-√yuj
    to apply
    to undertake
    to set in operation

prayoga
    preparation
    undertaking
    practical execution
```

`Prayoga` concerns how the deed is approached and carried out.

Its intensity may vary through:

```text
duration of planning
number of preparations
effort expended
persistence against obstacles
use of instruments
repetition
deliberateness
```

An impulsive minor attempt and an elaborately organized undertaking may aim at the same external end but possess different karmic force.

```text
thin preparation
    →
weaker practical determination

sustained organized preparation
    →
stronger practical determination
```

---

# 14. Preparation is already karma-forming

The preparation is not morally neutral until the final act occurs.

As the agent organizes means around an end, the continuum is already being determined.

```text
end conceived
    →
means selected
    →
body and speech organized
    →
object affected
```

The more thoroughly the agent makes reality into an instrument of the intended deed, the more strongly the activity is constituted.

This is why `prayoga` belongs to the measure of karma.

---

# 15. `Cetanā`

```text
cetanā
    volition
    operative intention
    that which directs activity
```

Cetanā is the immediate determining activity.

It does not merely accompany bodily and verbal karma from outside.

It organizes them toward an object and result.

```text
cetanā
    directs body

cetanā
    directs speech

cetanā
    makes the deed this deed
rather than an accidental event
```

An accidental physical result lacks the same karmic status because the operative volition is absent or different.

---

# 16. Degrees of volition

Volition may be mild or intense.

It may be:

```text
hesitant
divided
momentary
reluctant

or

firm
unified
persistent
fully endorsed
```

Thus two agents may perform externally similar acts while the intensity of `cetanā` differs.

```text
same bodily movement
    ≠
same karmic activity
```

The bodily event is interpreted through the volitional organization that produces it.

---

# 17. `Āśaya`

```text
āśaya
    underlying disposition
    inward orientation
    deep intention
    that in which the intention rests
```

`Āśaya` is deeper than the immediate volition of a single moment.

A useful provisional distinction is:

```text
cetanā
    what the agent presently wills

āśaya
    the deeper orientation
from which that willing proceeds
```

For example, one may give with the immediate volition to hand over food, while the underlying orientation is:

```text
compassion
desire for fame
fear
calculation of reward
reverence
wish to dominate
```

The external act and immediate volition must therefore be read through their deeper ground.

---

# 18. Cetanā and āśaya

These form a crucial dyad:

```text
āśaya
    underlying purposive disposition

cetanā
    determinate volitional activation
```

The movement is:

```text
deep orientation
    →
specific volition
    →
organized preparation
    →
bodily or verbal execution
```

But the completed deed can also reinforce the underlying orientation:

```text
activity repeatedly performed
    →
āśaya strengthened
```

Thus disposition produces activity, and activity reproduces disposition.

---

# 19. `Mṛdu`

```text
mṛdu
    soft
    mild
    weak
    of limited intensity
```

A karma is weak when its determining factors are weak.

For example:

```text
field only slightly significant
object minor
preparation minimal
volition hesitant
underlying purpose divided
subsequent endorsement absent
```

Weak does not mean causally nonexistent.

It means the activity has limited power to organize future maturation.

---

# 20. `Adhimātra`

```text
adhi
    exceeding
    intensified

mātra
    measure

adhimātra
    exceeding the ordinary measure
    powerful
    intense
```

A karma becomes intense when its factors converge strongly.

```text
exceptional field
significant basis
extended preparation
firm volition
deep underlying purpose
continued reinforcement
```

The force does not arise from a mysterious addition called “karmic weight.”

It arises from the degree of integration of the deed.

```text
more fully organized activity
    =
more causally powerful activity
```

---

# 21. No single-factor reduction

The verse prevents several reductions.

Karma is not determined solely by:

```text
intention alone
external result alone
recipient alone
object alone
preparation alone
```

Rather:

```text
KarmicIntensity =
    integrated determination
of all relevant factors
```

A powerful field does not automatically make every act maximally intense if the volition is weak or accidental.

A fierce volition may be limited if the objective basis is slight or the attempt never develops.

The act must be examined as a structured whole.

---

# 22. A multidimensional measure

The verse supplies something like a vector rather than one scalar property:

```text
KarmaVector {
    subsequentReinforcement
    fieldIntensity
    basisIntensity
    preparationIntensity
    volitionalIntensity
    dispositionalIntensity
}
```

The resulting karmic force emerges from their conjunction.

This is more sophisticated than assigning a fixed moral value to an externally named act.

```text
“giving”
“killing”
“speaking”
```

are only headings.

Their actual karmic determination requires analysis of the complete instrument.

# 24. Relation to “examining the instrument”

The verse identifies at least three layers of instrumentality.

## Inner instrument

```text
āśaya
cetanā
```

## Operational instrument

```text
prayoga
pṛṣṭha
```

## Objective instrument

```text
adhiṣṭhāna
kṣetra
```

So the instrument is not merely a bodily organ or external tool.

It is the entire mediated structure through which purpose becomes effective.

```text
subjective orientation
    →
operative organization
    →
objective field
```

Karma is the actuality of this whole circuit.

---

# 25. Karma as determinate relation

This verse supports our strongest formulation:

```text
Dharma
    relation in general

Karma
    determinate relation in activity
```

The karmic deed is determinate because every pole is specified:

```text
from what disposition
with what volition
through what preparation
upon what basis
toward what field
with what subsequent reinforcement
```

The more completely these moments are unified, the more intense the karma.

Karmic intensity is therefore the intensity of determination itself.

---

# 26. Doctrinal determination

The verse establishes:

1. karma possesses degrees of weakness and intensity;
2. these degrees cannot be inferred from the visible act alone;
3. the field contributes to karmic intensity;
4. the objective basis contributes;
5. preparation and practical execution contribute;
6. immediate volition contributes;
7. deeper intention or disposition contributes;
8. what follows the deed can reinforce or weaken it;
9. karmic magnitude arises through the integrated configuration of these factors.

# 28. Organon interpretation

Here the Kośa almost explicitly gives us the architecture of an evaluative instrument.

```text
Āśaya
    deep agential orientation

Cetanā
    selected practical determination

Prayoga
    execution plan

Adhiṣṭhāna
    objective support

Kṣetra
    relational environment

Pṛṣṭha
    persistence and post-execution reinforcement
```

This resembles a complete agent pipeline:

```text
Disposition
    →
Evaluation
    →
Plan
    →
Action upon support
    →
Interaction with field
    →
Post-action consolidation
```

The deed’s strength is the degree to which this entire pipeline is coherently organized.

That is the astonishing practical value of the analysis: the Kośa is not only judging deeds. It is decomposing the **instrument that produces, executes, and stabilizes them**.
