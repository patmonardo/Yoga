# VAK_4.76

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

पैशुन्यं क्लिष्टचित्तस्य वचनं परभेदने ।  
पारुष्यमप्रियं सर्वं क्लिष्टं भिन्नप्रलापिता ॥ ४.७६ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**paiśunyaṃ kliṣṭa-cittasya vacanaṃ para-bhedane |**  
**pāruṣyam apriyaṃ sarvaṃ kliṣṭaṃ bhinna-pralāpitā || 4.76 ||**

Source text:

```text
paiśunyaṃ kliṣṭacittasya vacanaṃ parabhedane /
pārūṣyamapriyaṃ sarva kliṣṭaṃ bhinna pralāpitā //
```

The second half is normalized as `pāruṣyam apriyaṃ sarvaṃ kliṣṭaṃ bhinna-pralāpitā`. The compound `bhinna-pralāpitā` represents the course more commonly expressed as `saṃbhinna-pralāpa`; the precise textual form remains for the Bhāṣya pass.

## 3. Padaccheda

```text
paiśunyam
kliṣṭa-cittasya
vacanam
para-bhedane
pāruṣyam
apriyam
sarvam
kliṣṭam
bhinna-pralāpitā
```

Expanded definitions:

```text
kliṣṭa-cittasya para-bhedane vacanam = paiśunyam
apriyam vacanam = pāruṣyam
sarvam kliṣṭam vacanam = bhinna-pralāpitā
```

Thus divisive speech is afflicted speech directed toward separating others; harsh speech is speech functioning as what is unwelcome or injurious; the remaining afflicted speech is disconnected or disordering speech.

## 4. Grammar

| Form | Analysis | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **paiśunyam** | neuter nominative singular | divisive speech |
| **kliṣṭa-cittasya** | genitive singular compound | of one whose knowledge-process is afflicted |
| **vacanam** | neuter nominative singular | utterance; speech |
| **para-bhedane** | locative singular compound | for the division of others |
| **pāruṣyam** | neuter nominative singular | harshness; harsh speech |
| **apriyam** | neuter nominative singular | unwelcome; painful; disagreeable |
| **sarvam** | neuter nominative singular | all; the remainder |
| **kliṣṭam** | neuter nominative singular | afflicted |
| **bhinna-pralāpitā** | feminine nominative singular abstract noun | disconnected or disordering speech |

`Kliṣṭa-citta` is treated functionally: a knowledge-process operating under greed, hatred, or delusion, not an inner psychological substance.

## 5. Literal Translation

> Divisive speech is the utterance of one whose knowledge is afflicted, directed toward separating others. Harsh speech is what is disagreeable. All remaining afflicted speech is disconnected chatter.

A fuller rendering:

> Speech produced from an afflicted determination with the purpose of dividing others is divisive speech. Speech functioning as unwelcome verbal injury is harsh speech. Afflicted speech not otherwise included among the specific verbal courses is disconnected or disordering speech.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> Verbal action becomes divisive when it is practically directed toward dissolving an existing relation among others. It becomes harsh when its determinate form is verbal injury. Speech that remains afflicted but lacks the determinate structures of falsehood, division, or harshness is classified as disconnected discourse: linguistic activity that dissipates rather than sustains meaningful practical relation.

The verse identifies three objective forms of failed semantic activity:

```text
division of relation
injury through expression
dissipation of meaningful discourse
```

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

### paiśunya

Divisive speech: speech ordered toward breaking a relation between others. The decisive factor is practical direction, not merely transmission of negative information.

### kliṣṭa

Afflicted or ethically contaminated: generated under greed, hatred, or delusion.

### citta

Here: a determinate knowledge-process, not a psychological substance.

### bhedana

Division or separation; here, difference produced as rupture of an existing practical unity.

### pāruṣya

Harsh verbal conduct. VAK_4.70 assigned its characteristic completion-root to hatred.

### apriya

Unwelcome, disagreeable, or painful. In context it indicates speech functioning as verbal injury, not merely any statement a hearer dislikes.

### pralāpa

Talk, discourse, or chatter.

### bhinna

Broken, divided, disconnected. It may indicate both internal disconnection of discourse and disconnection from meaningful practical purpose.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

The four verbal unwholesome karmapathas are now complete:

```text
1. false speech
2. divisive speech
3. harsh speech
4. disconnected speech
```

Their formal defects are:

| Course | Practical failure |
|---|---|
| False speech | contradiction between recognition and statement |
| Divisive speech | destruction of relation |
| Harsh speech | verbal injury |
| Disconnected speech | dissolution of meaningful order |

From VAK_4.70–4.71:

```text
harsh speech
    characteristically completed through hatred

false speech
divisive speech
disconnected speech
    may be completed through greed, hatred, or delusion
```

Divisive speech may serve gain, hostility, or deluded faction-making. Disconnected speech may likewise arise under any of the three roots.

## 9. Logical Determination

Speech is analyzed through practical function rather than propositional content alone:

```text
Speaker
    produces Utterance
    toward Recipient
    under Root
    for PracticalEnd
    producing RelationalEffect
```

Divisive speech acts upon a relation:

```text
A ↔ B
  ↓ utterance directed toward division
A | B
```

Harsh speech is hostile expression directed as verbal injury.

Disconnected speech functions as a residual logical class:

```text
AfflictedSpeech(x)
and not FalseSpeech(x)
and not DivisiveSpeech(x)
and not HarshSpeech(x)
    → DisconnectedSpeech(x)
```

Thus the verse partitions the verbal domain by determinate practical function.

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

A Rational Agent should not classify utterances solely by lexical content. It must ask:

```text
Is the statement contrary to represented knowledge?
Is it directed toward separating agents?
Is it designed to injure through expression?
Is it semantically or practically disconnected?
```

A useful structure is:

```text
VerbalAction {
    content
    speakerState
    recipient
    practicalEnd
    relationalTarget
    semanticCoherence
    expectedEffect
    completionRoot
}
```

Divisive speech targets a relation. Harsh speech should not be inferred merely from recipient dislike; the stronger criterion is that the utterance functions as verbal injury. Disconnected speech is linguistic production whose governing semantic-practical relation has broken down.

For AI design, the structural analogy includes hallucinated continuation, irrelevant generation, context-free verbosity, semantic drift, and performative chatter—without simply identifying these modern phenomena with the ancient category.

The practical question remains:

```text
not:
    what personality disorder caused the utterance?

but:
    what relation did the utterance establish,
    destroy, injure, or fail to sustain?
```
