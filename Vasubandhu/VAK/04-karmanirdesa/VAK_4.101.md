# VAK_4.101

## 1. Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

चक्रभेदः स च मतो जम्बूद्वीपे नवादिभिः ।
कर्मभेदस्त्रिषु द्वीपेष्वष्टभिरधिकैश्च सः ॥ ४.१०१ ॥

## 2. Sanskrit — IAST

**cakrabhedaḥ sa ca mato jambūdvīpe navādibhiḥ |**  
**karmabhedas triṣu dvīpeṣv aṣṭabhir adhikaiś ca saḥ || 4.101 ||**

## 3. Padaccheda

`cakra-bhedaḥ | saḥ | ca | mataḥ | jambū-dvīpe | nava-ādibhiḥ | karma-bhedaḥ | triṣu | dvīpeṣu | aṣṭabhiḥ | adhikaiḥ | ca | saḥ`

Expanded:

`sa ca cakrabhedaḥ jambūdvīpe navādibhiḥ mataḥ; sa karmabhedaḥ triṣu dvīpeṣu aṣṭabhir adhikaiś ca.`

## 4. Grammar

- **cakrabhedaḥ** — division of the wheel, i.e. rupture of the Dharma-wheel.
- **jambūdvīpe** — in Jambūdvīpa.
- **navādibhiḥ** — by nine or more.
- **karmabhedaḥ** — division of formal communal action.
- **triṣu dvīpeṣu** — in the three human continents.
- **aṣṭabhir adhikaiś ca** — by eight or more.

## 5. Literal Translation

> That division of the wheel is recognized in Jambūdvīpa through nine or more persons. Division of communal action occurs in the three continents through eight or more.

## 6. Philosophical Translation

> Full division of the Saṃgha as rupture of the Buddha’s teacher and Path occurs only in Jambūdvīpa and requires at least nine participants. A lesser division concerning formal communal action may occur throughout the three human continents and requires at least eight participants.

## 7. Technical Vocabulary

- **cakrabheda** — division of the Dharma-wheel; rupture of normative unity.
- **karmabheda** — division of Saṃgha action; rupture of operative unity.
- **navādibhiḥ** — nine or more: four monks in each faction plus the divider.
- **aṣṭabhiḥ** — eight: two groups of four performing communal actions separately.
- **Jambūdvīpa** — the unique locus of complete division of the Buddha’s dispensation.

## 8. Doctrinal Determination

The verse distinguishes two grades of collective division:

- **cakrabheda** attacks the constitutive principle of the Saṃgha through another teacher and another path;
- **karmabheda** divides the performance of formal communal action without necessarily replacing teacher and Path.

Thus division of principle and division of operation are distinct.

## 9. Logical Determination

The Saṃgha possesses at least two unities:

- normative unity: one teacher and one Path;
- operative unity: one communal action.

Their negations are:

- negation of normative unity → `cakrabheda`;
- negation of operative unity → `karmabheda`.

## 10. Light Organon Interpretation

A collective may preserve the same members while dividing at different structural levels. A semantic or constitutional schism is not identical with a procedural schism. The former concerns what governs the system; the latter concerns how the system acts.
