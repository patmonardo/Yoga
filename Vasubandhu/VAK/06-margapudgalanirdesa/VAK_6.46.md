# VAK 6.46

## Sanskrit

लौकिकेनार्यवैराग्ये विसंयोगाप्तयो द्विधा ।
लोकोत्तरेण चेत्येके त्यक्ते क्लेशासमन्वयात् ॥

*laukikenārya-vairāgye visaṃyogāptayo dvidhā /
lokottareṇa cety eke tyakte kleśāsamanvayāt //*

## Source Caution

This is a first-pass seed analysis of the kārikā alone. The exact doctrinal disagreement marked by *eke*, and the scope of *tyakte*, are deferred to the Bhāṣya pass.

## Padaccheda

- *laukikena* — by the worldly path
- *ārya-vairāgye* — in the dispassion of an Ārya
- *visaṃyoga-āptayaḥ* — attainments of separation
- *dvidhā* — twofold
- *lokottareṇa ca* — and by the supramundane path
- *iti eke* — so some hold
- *tyakte kleśa-asamanvayāt* — because, when the defilement has been abandoned, there is non-possession of it

## Grammar

The main statement is *ārya-vairāgye visaṃyogāptayo dvidhā*: in the dispassion of an Ārya, the attainments of separation are twofold. The instrumental forms *laukikena* and *lokottareṇa* specify the two modes. The phrase *iti eke* marks a qualified doctrinal position.

## Literal Translation

In the dispassion of an Ārya, the attainments of separation are twofold: by the worldly and by the supramundane path—so some hold—because, when the defilement has been abandoned, there is non-possession of it.

## Seed Analysis

The verse distinguishes two determinations of attained separation:

1. worldly attainment of separation;
2. supramundane attainment of separation.

The same abandoned defilement is not destroyed twice. Rather, the separation from it is possessed under two path-determinations. The verse therefore distinguishes the non-possession of a defilement from the positive attainment through which separation from it is possessed.

## Logical Determination

```text
kleśa abandoned
    → non-possession of kleśa
    → possession of separation from kleśa

same absent kleśa
    → worldly attainment of separation
    → supramundane attainment of separation
```

The content is the absence of the defilement; the form is the worldly or supramundane determination under which that separation is attained.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.46 states that, for an Ārya who has attained dispassion, attainments of separation may be twofold: worldly and supramundane. It distinguishes the mere non-possession of an abandoned defilement from the positive attainment through which separation from it is possessed.
