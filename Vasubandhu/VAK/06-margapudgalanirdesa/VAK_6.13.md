# VAK 6.13

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

आनापानौ यतः कायः सत्त्वाख्यावनुपात्तकौ ।  
नैःष्यन्दिकौ नाधरेण लक्ष्येते मनसा च तौ ॥ ६.१३ ॥

### IAST

**ānāpānau yataḥ kāyaḥ sattvākhyāv anupāttakau |**  
**naiḥṣyandikau nādhareṇa lakṣyete manasā ca tau || 6.13 ||**

## Literal Translation

Inhalation and exhalation belong to the same level as the body; they are designated as belonging to a living being, yet are unappropriated. They are homogeneous-flow products, and those two are not observed by a lower-level cognition, but by mind.

## Philosophical Translation

The breaths are bodily processes and belong to the same domain as the body in which they occur. Although included within the continuum conventionally designated as a living being, they are not organically appropriated as faculties. They arise as homogeneous continuations of prior conditions and become objects only for an adequate cognition, not for a lower cognitive standpoint.

## Technical Determinations

- **yataḥ kāyaḥ**: the breath-process belongs to the same level as the body.
- **sattvākhyau**: it is included within the conventionally designated sentient continuum.
- **anupāttakau**: it is not organically appropriated as an indriya-supported constituent.
- **naiḥṣyandikau**: it is a homogeneous-flow product, a conditioned continuation rather than a substance.
- **nādhareṇa lakṣyete**: it is not adequately discriminated by a cognition structurally lower than its own level.
- **manasā ca**: it becomes a mentally articulated object, not merely a sensory occurrence.

## Doctrinal Determination

VAK 6.12 classified ānāpānasmṛti as Prajñā taking vāyu as its range. VAK 6.13 now classifies the vāyu-process itself. Breath is not simply given as “my breath.” It is reconstructed as:

```text
body-level process
sentient-continuum member
unappropriated event
homogeneous causal flow
object of adequate cognition
```

Thus the Path does not eliminate the empirical field. It publishes the real determinations through which the field becomes scientifically knowable.

## Logical Determination

The verse establishes a middle position:

```text
internal enough
    to belong to embodied life

external enough
    not to be appropriated as self or faculty
```

Breath is therefore an ideal hinge-object between the polarities:

```text
Dhātu–Indriya
    elemental and organic determination

Loka–Karma
    manifested process and activity

Mārga
    their discriminated mediation in Prajñā
```

## Field Construction

The scientific object Breath requires at least five axes:

1. domain determination,
2. continuum determination,
3. appropriation determination,
4. causal determination,
5. epistemic determination.

The possessive sentence “my breath” collapses these relations into appropriation. Prajñā unfolds them again into lawful determinations.
