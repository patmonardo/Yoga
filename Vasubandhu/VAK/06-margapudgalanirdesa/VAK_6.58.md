# VAK 6.58

## Sanskrit

गोत्राच्चतुर्णां पञ्चानां फलाद्धानिर्न पूर्वकात् ।
शैक्षानार्याश्च षड्गोत्राः संचारो नास्ति दर्शने ॥

*gotrāc caturṇāṃ pañcānāṃ phalād dhānir na pūrvakāt /
śaikṣānāryāś ca ṣaḍgotrāḥ saṃcāro nāsti darśane //*

## Source Caution

This is an exceptionally compressed classificatory verse. Its general structure is clear, but the exact distribution of the four and five Arhat types across *gotra-parihāṇi* and *phala-parihāṇi* should be checked against the Bhāṣya. The final statement is clear: no transition of spiritual disposition occurs during the Path of Seeing.

## Padaccheda

- *gotrāt* — from the spiritual disposition or lineage
- *caturṇām* — of four
- *pañcānām* — of five
- *phalāt* — from the fruit
- *hāniḥ* — falling away, regression
- *na* — not
- *pūrvakāt* — from the preceding or earlier fruit
- *śaikṣāḥ* — trainees
- *anāryāḥ* — non-Āryas
- *ca* — and
- *ṣaḍ-gotrāḥ* — possessing six dispositions
- *saṃcāraḥ* — transition from one disposition to another
- *na asti* — does not occur
- *darśane* — during the Path of Seeing

## Literal Translation

For four there is falling away from the disposition, and for five there is falling away from the fruit, but not from an earlier fruit. Trainees and non-Āryas possess the six dispositions. There is no transition during the Path of Seeing.

## Philosophical Translation

Four types can regress from their acquired spiritual disposition, and five can regress from the Arhat fruit, though no one falls beneath a previously secured lower fruit. The six dispositions are also applicable to trainees and ordinary persons, but no change of disposition takes place within the immediate Path of Seeing.

## Doctrinal Structure

### Regression from disposition

```text
gotrāc caturṇāṃ hāniḥ
    four may fall away from their spiritual disposition
```

A developed spiritual type is not always absolutely irreversible.

### Regression from fruit

```text
pañcānāṃ phalād hāniḥ
    five may fall away from the fruit
```

The sixth, unshakeable Arhat is excluded by definition.

### No loss of an earlier fruit

```text
na pūrvakāt
    not from the preceding fruit
```

Even where Arhatship is lost, the practitioner does not collapse beneath the lower fruit already secured. Regression is bounded and does not return the person to ordinary worldliness.

## Six Dispositions

The sixfold *gotra* classification applies not only to Arhats but also to trainees and non-Āryas:

```text
ordinary person
    six possible dispositions

Ārya trainee
    six possible dispositions

Arhat
    six completed types
```

The same taxonomy traverses different stages of Path-development, though its concrete significance changes at each level.

## No Transition During Seeing

```text
saṃcāro nāsti darśane
```

Change of disposition may occur through cultivation and intensification, but not within the concentrated sequence of the Path of Seeing. Transition belongs to cultivation rather than to the immediate breakthrough of seeing.

```text
Path of Seeing
    breakthrough
    no transition of gotra

Path of Cultivation
    strengthening
    transformation
    possible advance or regression
```

## Basic Organon Light

Verse 6.57 established mobility: one may arrive at a stronger disposition. Verse 6.58 determines the limits of that mobility:

```text
disposition may be acquired
disposition may be lost
fruit may be lost
earlier fruit is not lost
disposition does not change during seeing
```

The architecture is neither rigid nor fluid without limit. Development has memory: a higher determination may be lost, while the mediated result beneath it remains.

A regressible Arhat does not become a non-Ārya. Arhatship may be lost, but the fundamental Path-breakthrough is not undone.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.58 limits the mobility introduced in the preceding verse. Four Arhat types may regress from an acquired disposition, and five may regress from the Arhat fruit, but no one thereby loses the previously secured lower fruit. The six dispositions extend across ordinary persons, Ārya trainees, and Arhats. Transition between dispositions does not occur during the immediate Path of Seeing; it belongs to the mediated work of cultivation.
