# VAK 6.44

## Sanskrit

आभवाग्राष्टभागक्षिदर्हत्त्वे प्रतिपन्नकः ।
नवमस्याप्यानन्तर्यपथे वज्रोपमश्च सः ॥

*ā-bhavāgrāṣṭa-bhāga-kṣid arhattve pratipannakaḥ /
navamasyāpy ānantarya-pathe vajropamaś ca saḥ //*

## Source Caution

This is a first-pass seed analysis of the kārikā alone. The exact counting of the eight grades through the summit of existence, and the technical force of the diamond-like path, are deferred to the Bhāṣya pass.

## Padaccheda

- *ā-bhavāgra* — up to the summit of existence
- *aṣṭa-bhāga-kṣit* — one who has destroyed eight grades
- *arhattve pratipannakaḥ* — one who has entered upon arhatship; a candidate for arhatship
- *navamasya api* — of the ninth also
- *ānantarya-pathe* — on the uninterrupted path
- *vajra-upamaḥ* — diamond-like
- *ca saḥ* — and he is

## Grammar

The first half identifies the practitioner who has destroyed eight grades up through the summit of existence as one entered upon arhatship. The second half states that, on the uninterrupted path directed against the ninth grade, that same practitioner is called diamond-like.

## Literal Translation

One who has destroyed eight grades up through the summit of existence is entered upon arhatship. On the uninterrupted path for the ninth grade, he is also diamond-like.

## Seed Analysis

The verse marks the final candidate-stage before arhatship:

1. destruction of eight grades through the summit of existence establishes the candidate for arhatship;
2. the uninterrupted path against the ninth grade is called diamond-like.

The verse therefore identifies the final removal-path immediately preceding completion. The detailed explanation of *vajropama* remains for the Bhāṣya pass.
