# VAK 6.27

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

इति षोडशचित्तोऽयं सत्याभिसमयस्त्रिधा ।  
दर्शनालम्बकार्याख्यः सोऽग्रधर्मैकभूमिकः ॥ ६.२७ ॥

### IAST

**iti ṣoḍaśa-citto 'yaṃ satyābhisamayas tridhā |**  
**darśanālamba-kāryākhyaḥ so 'gradharmaika-bhūmikaḥ || 6.27 ||**

## Source Caution

The verse gathers the sequence distributed across VAK 6.25–6.26:

```text
four truths
    ×
Dharma-Acceptance
Dharma-Knowledge
Anvaya-Acceptance
Anvaya-Knowledge
    =
sixteen moments
```

The expression **ṣoḍaśa-citta** does not mean sixteen enduring minds. It designates sixteen successive moments of cognition within one ordered direct realization of the truths.

The second half introduces three ways in which **abhisamaya** is spoken of. The compound **darśanālamba-kārya** must therefore be unpacked as:

```text
darśana-abhisamaya
ālambana-abhisamaya
kārya-abhisamaya
```

## Padaccheda

```text
iti
ṣoḍaśa-cittaḥ
ayam
satya-abhisamayaḥ
tridhā

darśana-ālambana-kārya-ākhyaḥ
saḥ
agra-dharma-eka-bhūmikaḥ
```

## Grammar

- **iti** — indeclinable: “thus,” concluding the sequence just enumerated.
- **ṣoḍaśa-cittaḥ** — nominative singular masculine compound: “consisting of sixteen cognitive moments.”
- **ayam** — nominative singular masculine: “this.”
- **satya-abhisamayaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “the direct realization of the truths.”
- **tridhā** — indeclinable: “threefold” or “in three ways.”
- **darśana-ālambana-kārya-ākhyaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “called seeing, object-support, and function”; a compressed coordination of three designations.
- **saḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “it,” referring to the direct realization.
- **agra-dharma-eka-bhūmikaḥ** — nominative singular masculine compound: “having the same level as the Highest Mundane Dharmas.”

## Literal Translation

Thus this direct realization of the truths consists of sixteen cognitive moments. It is threefold, called seeing, object-support, and function. It belongs to the same level as the Highest Mundane Dharmas.

## Philosophical Translation

The ordered sequence just described constitutes a single direct realization of the four truths articulated through sixteen successive cognitive moments. This realization is designated in three ways: according to the act of seeing, according to the truth that serves as its object-support, and according to the function accomplished through it. The entire supramundane sequence arises on the same concentration level that supported the immediately preceding Highest Mundane Dharmas.

## The Sixteen-Moment Realization

The word **iti** gathers the sequence established in the preceding verses:

```text
Suffering
    1. Dharma-Acceptance
    2. Dharma-Knowledge
    3. Anvaya-Acceptance
    4. Anvaya-Knowledge

Origin
    5. Dharma-Acceptance
    6. Dharma-Knowledge
    7. Anvaya-Acceptance
    8. Anvaya-Knowledge

Cessation
    9. Dharma-Acceptance
    10. Dharma-Knowledge
    11. Anvaya-Acceptance
    12. Anvaya-Knowledge

Path
    13. Dharma-Acceptance
    14. Dharma-Knowledge
    15. Anvaya-Acceptance
    16. Anvaya-Knowledge
```

These are not sixteen disconnected cognitions. They form one fixed realization-sequence:

```text
one abhisamaya
    differentiated into
sixteen successive moments
```

## Satya-Abhisamaya

```text
satya
    truth

abhisamaya
    direct realization
    immediate comprehension
    penetration
```

At the kārikā-pass level, **abhisamaya** should be retained as a technical term and rendered as “direct realization.” It is not merely conceptual understanding of the truths. It is the supramundane sequence in which they are directly seen.

The structure is therefore:

```text
truth as taught
    ↓
truth as prepared for
    ↓
truth directly realized
```

VAK 6.27 marks the third level.

## Threefold Abhisamaya

The verse states:

```text
tridhā
    threefold
```

and gives three designations.

### 1. Darśana-Abhisamaya

```text
darśana
    seeing
```

This names the realization according to the uncontaminated cognitive act that directly sees the truths.

```text
subjective determination:
    the path-moment as seeing
```

### 2. Ālambana-Abhisamaya

```text
ālambana
    object-support
```

This names the realization according to the truths that support and determine the act of seeing.

```text
objective determination:
    the truth as object-support
```

The four truths are therefore called “realized” because they are the objects directly taken up by the sixteen moments.

### 3. Kārya-Abhisamaya

```text
kārya
    function
    what is accomplished
```

This names the realization according to what the path does in relation to the truths and their opposed afflictions.

At the basic structural level:

```text
seeing suffering
abandoning origin
realizing cessation
cultivating path
```

These are the functions associated with the four truths. The detailed distribution of abandonment and realization belongs to the Bhāṣya pass.

## One Process, Three Determinations

The threefold account does not posit three separate paths. It distinguishes one realization according to three aspects:

```text
seeing
    cognitive act

object-support
    truth realized

function
    result accomplished
```

Thus:

```text
one direct realization
    viewed from
        act
        object
        function
```

This is the primary structural determination of the verse.

## Same Level as the Highest Mundane Dharmas

The final compound states:

```text
agra-dharma-eka-bhūmika
```

The first uncontaminated Dharma-Acceptance follows immediately after the Highest Mundane Dharmas. The supporting concentration-level does not change at that boundary.

```text
Highest Mundane Dharmas
    mundane
    final preparation
    ↓ immediate succession
Dharma-Acceptance
    uncontaminated
    first Path-of-Seeing moment
```

The status changes:

```text
mundane
    → uncontaminated
```

but the supporting **bhūmi** remains the same:

```text
one concentration-level
    supports both
        final mundane preparation
        first supramundane realization
```

The verse therefore distinguishes the quality of the path-moment from the level on which it arises.

## Logical Determination

The movement of VAK 6.25–6.27 is:

```text
final mundane preparation
    ↓
first uncontaminated acceptance
    ↓
sixteen ordered moments
    ↓
one direct realization of the truths
```

VAK 6.27 then determines this unity threefold:

```text
Direct Realization
    as seeing
    as object-support
    as function
```

The important first-pass relation is:

```text
multiplicity:
    sixteen moments

unity:
    one satya-abhisamaya

threefold articulation:
    act
    object
    function
```

This is not yet a claim about the complete Logic of the Kośa. It is the local structural form of the Path of Seeing as explicitly stated by the kārikā.

## Interpretive Note

VAK 6.26 presented the path as a determinate succession. VAK 6.27 prevents that succession from being treated as a mere list by naming it one **satya-abhisamaya**.

The verse therefore holds together:

```text
ordered differentiation
    and
unified realization
```

The three designations further show that realization cannot be described only as an inward cognitive event. It includes:

```text
the act of seeing
what is seen
what that seeing accomplishes
```

For the kārikā pass, this is enough. The precise Vaibhāṣika extensions of the three abhisamayas should be handled during the Bhāṣya pass.
