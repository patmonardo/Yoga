# VAK 6.26

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

कामदुःखे ततोऽत्रैव धर्मज्ञानं तथा पुनः ।  
शेषे दुःखेऽन्वयक्षान्तिज्ञाने सत्यत्रये तथा ॥ ६.२६ ॥

### IAST

**kāma-duḥkhe tato 'traiva dharma-jñānaṃ tathā punaḥ |**  
**śeṣe duḥkhe 'nvaya-kṣānti-jñāne satya-traye tathā || 6.26 ||**

## Source Caution

The kārikā is highly compressed. It continues directly from VAK 6.25, where the first uncontaminated moment was announced as:

```text
dharma-kṣāntiḥ anāsravā
    uncontaminated Dharma-Acceptance
```

VAK 6.26 supplies the remaining moments by ellipsis. The complete doctrinal names must be restored from the sequence:

```text
duḥkhe dharma-jñāna-kṣāntiḥ
duḥkhe dharma-jñānam

duḥkhe anvaya-jñāna-kṣāntiḥ
duḥkhe anvaya-jñānam
```

The same fourfold sequence is then repeated for the other three truths.

## Padaccheda

## Grammar

- **kāma-duḥkhe** — locative singular: “with regard to suffering in the desire realm.”
- **tataḥ** — indeclinable: “thereafter,” following the Dharma-Acceptance introduced in VAK 6.25.
- **atra eva** — “here itself,” with respect to this same desire-realm suffering.
- **dharma-jñānam** — nominative singular neuter: “Dharma-Knowledge.”
- **tathā punaḥ** — “and then likewise again,” indicating continuation of the ordered series.
- **śeṣe duḥkhe** — locative singular: “with regard to the remaining suffering,” namely suffering in the form and formless realms.
- **anvaya-kṣānti-jñāne** — nominative dual neuter in compressed compound form: “Anvaya-Acceptance and Anvaya-Knowledge.” More fully these are **anvaya-jñāna-kṣānti** and **anvaya-jñāna**.
- **satya-traye** — locative singular: “with regard to the three truths,” namely origin, cessation, and path.
- **tathā** — “in the same manner,” extending the four moments already stated for suffering to each of the remaining truths.

## Literal Translation

Thereafter, with regard to suffering in the desire realm, there is Dharma-Knowledge here itself. Then, with regard to the remaining suffering, there are Anvaya-Acceptance and Anvaya-Knowledge. It is likewise with regard to the three truths.

## Philosophical Translation

Following the first uncontaminated acceptance of suffering in the desire realm, Dharma-Knowledge of that same suffering arises. The path then turns to suffering in the higher realms, producing Anvaya-Acceptance and Anvaya-Knowledge. This four-moment pattern—acceptance and knowledge for the desire realm, followed by acceptance and knowledge for the higher realms—is repeated for origin, cessation, and path.

## The First Four Moments

VAK 6.25 introduced the first moment:

```text
1. duḥkhe dharma-jñāna-kṣāntiḥ
       Dharma-Knowledge-Acceptance concerning suffering
```

VAK 6.26 completes the first group:

```text
1. Dharma-Acceptance concerning suffering
       desire-realm suffering

2. Dharma-Knowledge concerning suffering
       desire-realm suffering

3. Anvaya-Acceptance concerning suffering
       suffering of the higher realms

4. Anvaya-Knowledge concerning suffering
       suffering of the higher realms
```

The first pair concerns the directly present field of the desire realm. The second pair extends the realization according to correspondence to the form and formless realms.

## Dharma and Anvaya

### Dharma pair

```text
dharma-jñāna-kṣānti
dharma-jñāna
```

These take as their object the truth as it pertains to the desire realm.

Here **dharma** belongs to the technical name of the knowledge and should not be expanded into a general metaphysical interpretation during the first kārikā pass.

### Anvaya pair

```text
anvaya-jñāna-kṣānti
anvaya-jñāna
```

These take as their object the corresponding truth in the higher realms:

```text
rūpa-dhātu
    form realm

ārūpya-dhātu
    formless realm
```

**Anvaya** indicates ordered correspondence or application according to the established sequence. The realization of the truth is extended from the desire-realm field to the remaining domains.

## Acceptance and Knowledge

Each object is traversed through a pair:

```text
kṣānti
    acceptance
    receptivity to the truth
    removal of the obstruction opposed to seeing

jñāna
    knowledge
    possession of the truth as seen
```

At this first-pass level, the essential distinction is functional:

```text
Acceptance
    enters and removes the opposed obstruction

Knowledge
    confirms and possesses the realized truth
```

VAK 6.28 will identify these respectively as uninterrupted and liberation paths.

## Extension to the Three Remaining Truths

The expression:

```text
satya-traye tathā
```

extends the same pattern to:

```text
samudaya
    origin

nirodha
    cessation

mārga
    path
```

For each truth there are four moments:

```text
Dharma-Acceptance
Dharma-Knowledge
Anvaya-Acceptance
Anvaya-Knowledge
```

The complete arrangement is therefore:

```text
Suffering
    4 moments

Origin
    4 moments

Cessation
    4 moments

Path
    4 moments
```

Total:

```text
4 truths × 4 moments = 16 moments
```

VAK 6.27 will name this complete sixteen-moment sequence the direct realization of the truths.

## The Ordered Sequence

The structure is not a simultaneous survey. It is a fixed succession:

```text
Desire-realm truth
    Acceptance
        ↓
    Knowledge
        ↓
Higher-realm truth
    Acceptance
        ↓
    Knowledge
```

This sequence is performed first for suffering and then repeated for origin, cessation, and path.

The kārikā thus constructs a determinate path of seeing rather than an undifferentiated intuition.

The verse explicitly states the four moments for suffering and supplies the other twelve through **satya-traye tathā**—“likewise for the three truths.”

## Logical Determination

The basic movement is:

```text
immediate acceptance
    ↓
knowledge of the same field
    ↓
acceptance of the corresponding higher field
    ↓
knowledge of that corresponding field
```

Applied across the four truths:

```text
one truth
    differentiated into four ordered path-moments

four truths
    integrated into one sixteen-moment realization
```

The path therefore joins:

```text
identity of truth
    with
ordered differentiation of its domains and functions
```

For the kārikā pass, this structural sequence is sufficient. The detailed Vaibhāṣika account of what each acceptance abandons and what each knowledge realizes belongs to the Bhāṣya pass.

## Determinate Summary

```text
For suffering in the desire realm:

    Dharma-Acceptance
        is followed by
    Dharma-Knowledge

For suffering in the higher realms:

    Anvaya-Acceptance
        is followed by
    Anvaya-Knowledge

The same fourfold sequence is repeated for:

    origin
    cessation
    path

Therefore:

    four truths
        × four moments each
        = sixteen moments
```

VAK 6.26 lays out the ordered contents of the Path of Seeing; VAK 6.27 will gather them as the sixteen-moment direct realization of the truths.
