# VAK 6.51

## Sanskrit

श्रामण्यममलो मार्गः संस्कृतासंस्कृतं फलम् ।
एकोननवतिस्तानि मुक्तिमार्गाः सह क्षयैः ॥

*śrāmaṇyam amalo mārgaḥ /
saṃskṛtāsaṃskṛtaṃ phalam /
ekānnavatis tāni muktimārgāḥ saha kṣayaiḥ //*

## Source Caution

The numerical construction requires a fuller Bhāṣya pass. The basic doctrinal statement is clear: the uncontaminated path is *śrāmaṇya*; its fruit is both conditioned and unconditioned; and the fruits are enumerated through liberation paths together with corresponding destructions or cessations. We should not yet overstate precisely how the eighty-nine paired elements are counted.

## Padaccheda

- *śrāmaṇyam* — the state, practice, or efficacy of the śramaṇa
- *amalaḥ* — stainless; uncontaminated
- *mārgaḥ* — path
- *saṃskṛta* — conditioned
- *asaṃskṛtam* — unconditioned
- *phalam* — fruit
- *ekānnavatiḥ* — eighty-nine
- *tāni* — those
- *mukti-mārgāḥ* — paths of liberation
- *saha* — together with
- *kṣayaiḥ* — destructions; cessations

## Grammar

The first pāda is a definition:

```text
śrāmaṇyam
    = amalaḥ mārgaḥ
```

The stainless or uncontaminated path constitutes true *śrāmaṇya*.

The next statement characterizes its fruit:

```text
phalam
    saṃskṛtam
    asaṃskṛtam
```

The fruit is both conditioned and unconditioned.

The final half identifies the relevant plurality:

```text
mukti-mārgāḥ
    saha kṣayaiḥ
```

liberation paths together with their destructions or cessations.

## Literal Translation

The stainless path is *śrāmaṇya*. Its fruit is conditioned and unconditioned. Those are eighty-nine: the paths of liberation together with the destructions.

## Philosophical Translation

Authentic śramaṇa-practice is the uncontaminated path. Its result has two inseparable sides: the conditioned actuality of liberation-path cognition and the unconditioned cessation acquired through that path.

## Śrāmaṇya

The verse does not define *śrāmaṇya* merely as belonging socially to the class of renunciants.

```text
śramaṇa
    not merely a personal designation

śrāmaṇya
    the operative character of the uncontaminated path
```

The real śramaṇa is determined by participation in the stainless path. This is an objective definition through Dharma rather than through personhood.

## The Twofold Fruit

### Conditioned fruit

The *mukti-mārga* is conditioned. It is an arisen path-cognition possessing the separation won by the preceding immediate path.

```text
ānantarya-mārga
    directly abandons the obstruction

mukti-mārga
    arises as possession of that separation
```

Because it arises through causes and conditions, the liberation path is itself conditioned.

### Unconditioned fruit

The corresponding *kṣaya* is unconditioned. Here *kṣaya* is not simply a psychological event that occurs and then passes away. It is the acquired cessation—the separation from the defilement.

```text
conditioned cognition
    possesses

unconditioned cessation
```

Thus liberation is not reduced either to a mental episode or to an abstract absence.

## Immediate and Mediate

```text
ānantarya-mārga
    immediate exclusion of the obstruction

mukti-mārga
    conditioned mediation and possession of freedom

kṣaya
    unconditioned cessation that is possessed
```

The fruit therefore has two aspects:

```text
conditioned fruit
    the path as actual cognition

unconditioned fruit
    the cessation attained by that cognition
```

The mediation does not manufacture the unconditioned as though cessation were a conditioned product. The path produces acquisition of cessation; it does not produce cessation as a conditioned thing. The path makes the unconditioned operative as an attained fruit.

## Basic Organon Light

The verse places a conditioned and an unconditioned determination within one fruit:

```text
conditioned
    mukti-mārga

unconditioned
    kṣaya

unity
    śrāmaṇya-phala
```

The conditioned liberation path is the positive cognitive actuality through which unconditioned cessation belongs to the practitioner as attained separation.

```text
mukti-mārga
    form of possession

kṣaya
    content possessed

fruit
    their determinate unity
```

The conditioned and unconditioned are not collapsed into one ontological class. They are united functionally within the fruit.

## Relation to VAK 6.50

VAK 6.50 distinguished destruction, knowledge of destruction, and knowledge of non-arising. VAK 6.51 now supplies the ontological distinction underlying that cognitive sequence:

```text
unconditioned cessation
    kṣaya

conditioned cognition possessing it
    mukti-mārga
```

Thus the path knows and possesses a cessation that is not itself merely another passing path-event.

## The Eighty-Nine

The number eighty-nine apparently gathers the complete set of liberation paths corresponding to the abandonments accomplished through the path. The exact enumeration should be reserved for the Bhāṣya, since the count likely integrates the paths associated with seeing and cultivation.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.51 defines śrāmaṇya as the stainless path and declares its fruit to be both conditioned and unconditioned. The conditioned side is the liberation path; the unconditioned side is the corresponding destruction or cessation. The path does not create the unconditioned. It makes unconditioned cessation a determinate, cognitively possessed, and operative fruit.
