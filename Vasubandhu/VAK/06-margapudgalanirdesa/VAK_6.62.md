# VAK 6.62

## Sanskrit

सविशेषं फलं त्यक्त्वा फलमाप्नोति वर्धयन् ।
द्वौ बुद्धौ श्रावकाः सप्त नवैते नवधेन्द्रियाः ॥

*saviśeṣaṃ phalaṃ tyaktvā phalam āpnoti vardhayan /
dvau buddhau śrāvakāḥ sapta navaite navadhendriyāḥ //*

## Source Caution

The first half describes what occurs during the strengthening of the faculties: a qualified or differentiated fruit is relinquished and another fruit is attained.

The second half establishes nine principal persons:

```text
two Buddhas
seven Śrāvakas
```

and correlates them with nine grades of faculties. The precise sevenfold Śrāvaka enumeration should be confirmed from the Bhāṣya rather than reconstructed prematurely.

## Padaccheda

- *sa-viśeṣam* — possessing a distinction; qualified by a specific difference
- *phalam* — fruit
- *tyaktvā* — having relinquished
- *phalam* — fruit
- *āpnoti* — attains
- *vardhayan* — one strengthening or developing
- *dvau* — two
- *buddhau* — Buddhas
- *śrāvakāḥ* — Śrāvakas
- *sapta* — seven
- *nava* — nine
- *ete* — these
- *navadhā* — ninefold
- *indriyāḥ* — faculties; persons differentiated through their faculties

## Literal Translation

One strengthening the faculties relinquishes a fruit possessing a specific distinction and attains a fruit. There are two Buddhas and seven Śrāvakas; these nine are differentiated according to the ninefold faculties.

## Philosophical Translation

When the practitioner strengthens the faculties, the previously possessed fruit under its earlier specific determination is relinquished and the corresponding fruit is newly attained in a superior mode. The two kinds of Buddha and seven kinds of Śrāvaka form nine principal persons, distinguished through nine grades of spiritual faculty.

## Strengthening Does Not Mean a New Fruit-Level

```text
saviśeṣaṃ phalaṃ tyaktvā
phalam āpnoti vardhayan
```

The practitioner does not necessarily pass from one of the four fruits to a later fruit.

Rather, the same basic fruit is transformed according to a new distinction of capacity.

```text
earlier fruit
    possessed under a weaker faculty-determination
        ↓
faculty strengthening
        ↓
earlier specification relinquished
        ↓
fruit possessed under a stronger determination
```

Thus:

```text
fruit identity
    remains

mode of possessing the fruit
    changes
```

The person relinquishes the fruit **as specifically qualified by the former faculty**, not liberation altogether.

## `Saviśeṣaṃ Phalam`

`viśeṣa` marks a determinate differentiation.

The fruit is not an abstractly identical possession shared without distinction by every practitioner. It is concretely determined by the operative faculties of the person possessing it.

```text
fruit
    + faculty type
    = specifically determined fruit
```

When the faculty changes, the specific form of the fruit also changes.

This reinforces the architecture seen in the preceding verses:

```text
same Path-result
    differently possessed
    differently stabilized
    differently operationalized
```

## Two Buddhas

```text
dvau buddhau
```

The two are most naturally:

```text
pratyekabuddha
    independently awakened Buddha

samyaksaṃbuddha
    fully and perfectly awakened Buddha
```

Both have completed awakening, but they represent distinct forms of Buddhahood within the Path architecture.

The verse does not yet analyze their full difference. It counts them as two of the nine principal faculty-types.

## Seven Śrāvakas

```text
śrāvakāḥ sapta
```

Seven kinds of Śrāvaka are distinguished.

These likely gather the principal trainee and beyond-training forms discussed throughout the chapter, but the exact seven should be left for Bhāṣya verification.

For the Kārikā pass, the stable result is:

```text
Path-persons
    two Buddhas
    seven Śrāvakas
        =
    nine principal persons
```

## Ninefold Faculties

```text
navaite navadhendriyāḥ
```

The nine persons correspond to nine grades of faculties.

The familiar gradation is:

```text
mild-mild
mild-medium
mild-strong

medium-mild
medium-medium
medium-strong

strong-mild
strong-medium
strong-strong
```

The verse therefore correlates:

```text
pudgala type
    ↔
indriya grade
```

The classification is not merely nominal. Each person-type expresses a determinate power of Path-operation.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse makes two related points.

### Faculty-development transforms possession of fruit

```text
weaker faculty
    fruit possessed weakly or conditionally

stronger faculty
    same fruit repossessed under a superior determination
```

### The Path field contains nine principal personal forms

```text
2 Buddhas
+ 7 Śrāvakas
= 9 persons
```

These correspond to the ninefold differentiation of faculties.

The classification of persons is therefore grounded in operative Path-capacity.

## Logical Determination

A fruit is not simply an inert object that remains externally identical while the subject changes.

The transformation of the subject’s faculty alters the determinate form of the fruit itself:

```text
change of operative capacity
    ↓
change in mode of attainment
    ↓
fruit re-determined
```

This is the subject–field correlation in a very concentrated form.

```text
pudgala
    changes in faculty

phala
    changes in specific determination
```

Neither side changes alone.

The fruit remains universally the same Path-result, but its particular mode of possession is transformed.

## Basic Organon Light

This verse prevents us from treating the Path as a single abstract universal equally instantiated by all persons.

The Path is one, yet its actuality is differentiated through the faculties:

```text
Mega Uni Principle
    one Path

ninefold indriya
    differentiated powers of actualization

nine pudgalas
    personal forms of that actualization
```

The person does not merely look differently upon an independently fixed fruit. The knowable Path-field itself is rearticulated with the operative capacity of the knower.

```text
stronger indriya
    → stronger form of fruit-possession
```

Thus the Kośa again presents:

```text
karmic and spiritual constitution
    ↔
operative capacities
    ↔
determinate knowable field
```

The two Buddhas and seven Śrāvakas are not nine independent ultimate principles. They are nine determinate personal expressions of one Path.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.62 states that strengthening the faculties transforms the specific mode in which a fruit is possessed: the fruit qualified by the former capacity is relinquished, and the fruit is attained under a stronger determination. The Path-persons are then gathered into two Buddhas and seven Śrāvakas, forming nine principal types corresponding to the ninefold grades of spiritual faculty.
