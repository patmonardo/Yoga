# VAK 6.57

## Sanskrit

अतोऽसमयमुक्तोऽसौ दृष्टिप्राप्तान्वयश्च सः ।
तद्गोत्रा आदितः केचित्केचिदुत्तापनागताः ॥

*ato 'samayamukto 'sau dṛṣṭiprāptānvayaś ca saḥ /
tadgotrā āditaḥ kecit kecid uttāpanāgatāḥ //*

## Source Caution

The verse determines the sixth Arhat through two related descriptions: *asamayamukta*, one not dependent upon occasion, and *dṛṣṭiprāptānvaya*, one belonging to the developmental succession of the *dṛṣṭiprāpta*. The precise technical force of *uttāpanāgata* should receive fuller treatment in the Bhāṣya pass.

## Padaccheda

- *ataḥ* — therefore
- *asamaya-muktaḥ* — liberated independently of occasion
- *asau* — this one
- *dṛṣṭi-prāpta-anvayaḥ* — belonging to the succession of the one attained through seeing
- *tad-gotrāḥ* — of that disposition
- *āditaḥ* — from the beginning
- *kecit* — some
- *uttāpana-āgatāḥ* — arrived through intensification or sharpening

## Literal Translation

Therefore, this one is liberated independently of occasion, and he belongs to the succession of the one who has attained through seeing. Some are of that disposition from the beginning; others have arrived at it through intensification.

## Doctrinal Structure

```text
akopyadharman
    = asamayamukta
    = dṛṣṭiprāptānvaya
```

The two developmental lines are now aligned:

```text
śraddhādhimukta lineage
    → five condition-dependent Arhats

dṛṣṭiprāpta lineage
    → unshakeable Arhat
```

The distinction is both genetic and modal: it determines where the Arhat type develops from and how liberation is possessed.

## Original and Acquired Disposition

```text
tadgotrā āditaḥ kecit
    some belong to that disposition from the beginning

kecid uttāpanāgatāḥ
    others reach it through intensification
```

The unshakeable condition may therefore be original or cultivated. The pudgala architecture is not wholly static; determinate spiritual capacity may be strengthened.

The stronger Arhat type may be either given at the outset or achieved through development.
