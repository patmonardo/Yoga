# VAK 6.65

## Sanskrit

अशैक्षपरिपूर्णत्वं द्वाभ्यां मार्गः समासतः ।
विशेषमुक्त्यानन्तर्यप्रयोगाख्यश्चतुर्विधः ॥

*aśaikṣaparipūrṇatvaṃ dvābhyāṃ mārgaḥ samāsataḥ /
viśeṣamuktyānantaryaprayogākhyaś caturvidhaḥ //*

## Source Caution

The first pāda completes the classification begun in VAK 6.64. The completeness of the `aśaikṣa`, the one beyond training, is determined through two dimensions rather than the three dimensions used for the complete trainee.

The second half changes level. It no longer classifies a kind of person, but gives the general operative structure of the Path itself.

The kārikā lists the four names metrically as:

```text
viśeṣa
mukti
ānantarya
prayoga
```

Their operative sequence is:

```text
prayoga
    → ānantarya
        → vimukti
            → viśeṣa
```

## Padaccheda

- *aśaikṣa-paripūrṇatvam* — completeness of the one beyond training
- *dvābhyām* — through two; in two respects
- *mārgaḥ* — Path
- *samāsataḥ* — in summary; comprehensively
- *viśeṣa* — further distinction, intensification, advancement
- *mukti* — release, liberation
- *ānantarya* — immediacy, absence of an interval
- *prayoga* — application, preparatory engagement
- *ākhyaḥ* — called, designated
- *caturvidhaḥ* — fourfold

## Grammar

`aśaikṣaparipūrṇatvam` is a neuter compound naming the completeness proper to the Aśaikṣa.

`dvābhyām` is instrumental dual and indicates the two respects through which that completeness is determined.

`viśeṣa-mukti-ānantarya-prayoga-ākhyaḥ` qualifies `mārgaḥ`: the Path is called by these four names.

`caturvidhaḥ` gives the principal determination of the second half: the Path is fourfold.

## Literal Translation

The completeness of the Aśaikṣa is through two. In summary, the Path is fourfold, called the Path of Further Distinction, Release, Immediacy, and Application.

## Philosophical Translation

The one beyond training is complete through two powers: faculty and contemplative attainment. Considered in its universal operation, the Path is fourfold: preparatory application, the immediate removal of obstruction, the first actuality of release, and the further development of that liberated actuality.

## Aśaikṣa Completeness

VAK 6.64 defined the complete trainee through:

```text
samāpatti
indriya
phala
```

For the Aśaikṣa, fruit is no longer counted as an independent dimension of completeness. Possession of the final fruit is already presupposed by the designation `aśaikṣa`.

The remaining two dimensions are:

```text
indriya
    faculty
    operative spiritual capacity

samāpatti
    contemplative attainment
    mastery of the meditative field
```

Thus:

```text
Aśaikṣa completeness
    ≠ merely the abstract fact of Arhatship

Aśaikṣa completeness
    = determinate fullness
      of faculty and samāpatti
```

The verse preserves the primacy of liberation while allowing degrees or modes of realized fullness within the class of those beyond training.

## The Fourfold Path

### Prayoga-mārga — Path of Application

`prayoga` is directed application, preparation, and putting-into-operation.

The Path of Application configures the conditions from which the decisive Path of Immediacy can arise.

```text
prayoga
    directed engagement
    preparation of the effective configuration
    immediate antecedent of ānantarya
```

Canonical translation:

```text
Path of Application
```

### Ānantarya-mārga — Path of Immediacy

`an-antara` means that there is nothing between.

The Path of Immediacy is the path-moment that directly abandons the determinate obstruction.

```text
ānantarya
    direct counterforce
    immediate abandonment
    no intervening state
```

Canonical translation:

```text
Path of Immediacy
```

This is more exact than treating it merely as an “uninterrupted” or “irresistible” path. Its primary determination is immediate relation to the abandonment it effects.

### Vimukti-mārga — Path of Release

The Path of Release is the first path-moment to arise free from the obstruction abandoned by the Path of Immediacy.

```text
ānantarya
    abandons the obstruction

vimukti
    first arises unobstructed
```

Canonical translation:

```text
Path of Release
```

Release is therefore not identical with the act of abandonment. It is the first positive actuality in which the removed obstruction is no longer operative.

### Viśeṣa-mārga — Path of Further Distinction

`viśeṣa` indicates further determination, intensification, or advancement.

It includes the path-development that follows the first actuality of release:

```text
higher intensity within the same class
progressively higher attainment
further development of liberated capacity
```

Canonical translation:

```text
Path of Further Distinction
```

“Special Path” is too weak. The point is not mere peculiarity but the positive development of achieved release.

## Operative Sequence

The conceptual order is:

```text
prayoga
    application prepares the effective relation

ānantarya
    the obstruction is immediately abandoned

vimukti
    unobstructed actuality first arises

viśeṣa
    that actuality advances and intensifies
```

Or:

```text
preparation
    → decisive negation
        → achieved freedom
            → development of freedom
```

## Why Release Remains Path

The Path does not terminate with the negative removal of an obstruction.

The Path includes:

```text
the preparation for abandonment
the effective abandonment
the actuality of freedom
the further determination of freedom
```

`vimukti` and `viśeṣa` remain Path because liberation is not merely a static result. It is an actuality capable of intensification, higher attainment, and final consummation.

The decisive movement is:

```text
ānantarya
    determinate negation

vimukti
    positive actuality of freedom

viśeṣa
    self-development of liberated actuality
```

## Doctrinal Determination

VAK 6.65 establishes two connected teachings.

First:

```text
Aśaikṣa completeness
    faculty
    samāpatti
```

Second:

```text
Fourfold Path
    prayoga
    ānantarya
    vimukti
    viśeṣa
```

The verse therefore passes from:

```text
completeness of the Path-person
    → universal operation of the Path
```

This is the correct opening of the final Bodhi–Mārga block.

## Relation to Bodhipakṣya

The bodhipakṣya teaching beginning at VAK 6.67 is not an unrelated list appended to the preceding analysis.

It is a further determination of this same Path.

```text
VAK 6.65
    universal fourfold operation of Path

VAK 6.66
    modes in which the Path is traversed

VAK 6.67 onward
    the same Path articulated
    as the dharmas belonging to Bodhi
```

This suggests the block title:

```text
The Bodhi–Mārga:
Fourfold Path, Bodhipakṣya Dharmas,
and the Determination of Liberation
```

## Logical Determination

```text
1. Prayoga
   the relation to the obstruction is prepared

2. Ānantarya
   the obstruction is immediately negated

3. Vimukti
   freedom from the obstruction becomes actual

4. Viśeṣa
   the liberated actuality develops further
```

The fourfold form prevents two reductions:

```text
Path
    ≠ preparation alone

Path
    ≠ negation alone

Liberation
    ≠ static absence of bondage
```

Instead:

```text
Path
    = preparation
      + transformation
      + liberated actuality
      + positive advancement
```

## Basic Organon Light

A restrained Organon correspondence is:

```text
Prayoga
    Form is actively prepared

Ānantarya
    the determinate obstruction is negated

Vimukti
    the unobstructed Form becomes actual

Viśeṣa
    the liberated Form develops
    its higher determination
```

The important point is not to force the four moments into an inherited triad. The fourfold sequence is the concrete operational articulation of a Path that incarnates a higher Idea.

The central determination is:

```text
The Path does not terminate in release.

Release becomes Path
because it advances into viśeṣa.
```

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.65 completes the preceding pudgala analysis by defining Aśaikṣa fullness through faculty and contemplative attainment. It then rises to the general form of the Path itself. The Path is fourfold: Application prepares the effective relation, Immediacy abandons the obstruction, Release first arises free from it, and Further Distinction develops the liberated actuality beyond its initial achievement. This fourfold architecture supplies the foundation for the coming Bodhi and bodhipakṣya teaching.
