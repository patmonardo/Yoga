# VAK 6.10

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

आसमुद्रास्थिविस्तारसंक्षेपादादिकर्मिकः ।
पादास्थ्न आ कपालार्धत्यागात् कृतजयः स्मृतः ॥ ६.१० ॥

### IAST

āsamudrāsthi-vistāra-saṃkṣepād ādikarmikaḥ |
pādāsthna ā kapālārdha-tyāgāt kṛtajayaḥ smṛtaḥ || 6.10 ||

The transmitted Sanskrit is difficult in the second pāda, especially `pādāsthna`, but the doctrinal sequence is clear: the beginner expands and contracts the skeletal representation; the practitioner who has gained mastery progressively removes the bones beginning with the feet until only half the skull remains.

## Literal Translation

The beginner is one who expands the skeleton as far as the sea and then contracts it. One who, through removing the bones beginning with those of the foot up to half the skull, has gained mastery is so designated.

## Philosophical Translation

At the initial stage, the practitioner learns to enlarge the skeletal representation until it encompasses the entire surrounding world and then to draw it back into concentration. Mastery is attained when the represented skeleton can be progressively dissolved, beginning with the feet, until only a final half-skull remains.

## Technical Vocabulary

### ādikarmika

An `ādikarmika` is the beginner in the actual cognitive operation. The beginner learns two opposed capacities:

```text
vistāra
    expansion

saṃkṣepa
    contraction
```

These establish control over the scale and scope of the representation.

### asthi-vistāra

The skeletal representation is expanded from a single body through the surrounding field and as far as the sea. What was formerly apprehended as many attractive and differentiated bodies is reclassified under one determination: skeletal structure.

### saṃkṣepa

After expansion, the representation is contracted again. The practitioner must not become dependent on either scale. The support becomes cognitively governable: expandable and contractible at will.

### kṛtajaya

`Kṛtajaya` is accomplished mastery over the cognitive construction. The representation can be generated, extended, contracted, and partially removed without loss of stability.

### tyāga

Here `tyāga` is progressive removal of represented bones. The antidote is not retained as an absolute object. The skeleton is itself relinquished.

## Analysis

The verse describes three operations:

```text
1. expansion
2. contraction
3. progressive relinquishment
```

Expansion universalizes the skeletal determination. Contraction gathers the field back into a concentrated support. Progressive removal dismantles the representation from within.

The complete movement is:

```text
construction
    ↓
universal application
    ↓
re-concentration
    ↓
dissolution
```

VAK 6.09 assigned the skeleton as the common counter-object for desire. VAK 6.10 explains how that object is trained. The practice is not a static visualization but controlled transformation of the cognitive field:

```text
generate
expand
contract
subtract
retain stability
```

The practitioner must gain freedom relative to the representation itself, preventing the counterpractice from becoming another rigid fixation.

## The Antidote Must Also Be Sublated

Aśubha dismantles the desirable body:

```text
beautiful whole
    ↓
skeleton
```

VAK 6.10 then dismantles the skeleton:

```text
skeleton
    ↓
partial skeleton
    ↓
minimal remainder
```

Thus the structure is not replacement of a false beautiful body by a true skeleton. It is an afflictive representation countered by a strategic representation that is itself relinquished.

## Relation to VAK 6.04

VAK 6.04 showed that pot-cognition disappears when the whole is decomposed. VAK 6.09 applied this to bodily attraction. VAK 6.10 goes further: skeleton-cognition is itself decomposable. Even the therapeutic representation is conventionally constituted.

## Path-of-Knowing Determination

The practitioner develops control over three dimensions of cognition:

```text
extension
concentration
subtraction
```

These correspond to three scientific capacities:

- applying a determination throughout the object-field;
- retaining it in a singular instance;
- removing constituents without confusion concerning what remains.

This is `bhāvanāmayī dhī` in operation.

## Logical Determination

The verse defines two practitioner stages:

```text
Beginner
    controls expansion and contraction

Master
    controls progressive subtraction
```

The transition is from control of scale to control of internal composition.
