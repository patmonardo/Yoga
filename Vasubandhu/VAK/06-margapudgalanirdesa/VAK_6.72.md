# VAK 6.72

## Sanskrit

द्वितीयेऽन्यत्र संकल्पाद् द्वयोस्तद्द्वयवर्जिताः ।
ध्यानान्तरे च शीलाङ्गैस्ताभ्यां च त्रिष्वरूपिषु ॥

*dvitīye ’nyatra saṃkalpād dvayos tad-dvaya-varjitāḥ /
dhyānāntare ca śīlāṅgais tābhyāṃ ca triṣv arūpiṣu //*

## Padaccheda

- *dvitīye* — in the second dhyāna
- *anyatra saṃkalpāt* — except for saṃkalpa
- *dvayoḥ* — in the two, namely the third and fourth dhyānas
- *tad-dvaya-varjitāḥ* — deprived of that pair
- *dhyānāntare ca* — and in the intermediate dhyāna
- *śīlāṅgaiḥ* — by the ethical limbs
- *tābhyām ca* — and by those two
- *triṣu arūpiṣu* — in the three formless grounds

## Literal Translation

In the second dhyāna they occur except for saṃkalpa. In the two higher dhyānas they are deprived of that pair; likewise in the intermediate dhyāna. In the three lower formless grounds they are deprived of those two and of the ethical limbs.

## Philosophical Translation

The bodhipakṣya system is progressively restricted according to contemplative ground. The second dhyāna excludes right resolve; the third and fourth exclude both joy and resolve; and the first three formless grounds exclude those two together with the ethical factors of the Path.

## Ground Distribution

```text
first dhyāna
    complete system

anāgamya
    without prīti

second dhyāna
    without saṃkalpa

third and fourth dhyānas
    without prīti and saṃkalpa

intermediate dhyāna
    similarly restricted

first three formless grounds
    without prīti
    without saṃkalpa
    without the ethical Path-limbs
```

The ethical limbs are right speech, right action, and right livelihood. They are unavailable in the formless grounds because bodily and vocal form are absent there.

## Doctrinal Determination

The thirty-seven factors are not uniformly instantiated in every contemplative domain. Their availability depends upon the structural capacities of the ground:

```text
absence of vitarka
    excludes saṃkalpa

absence of prīti
    excludes the joy factor

absence of material and vocal activity
    excludes the ethical limbs
```

Thus ground-structure determines possible Path-composition.

## Logical Determination

```text
Contemplative ground
    determines admissible factors

Factor
    requires supporting conditions

Loss of supporting condition
    entails exclusion of factor
```

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.72 continues the distribution of the bodhipakṣya factors by ground. The second dhyāna lacks saṃkalpa; the third and fourth lack prīti and saṃkalpa; and the first three formless attainments lack those two together with the ethical Path-limbs.
