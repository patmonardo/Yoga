# VAK 6.55

## Sanskrit

कामे त्रयाप्तिरन्त्यस्य त्रिषु नोर्ध्वं हि दृक्पथः ।
असंवेगादिह विधा तत्र निष्ठेति चागमात् ॥

*kāme trayāptir antyasya triṣu nordhvaṃ hi dṛkpathaḥ /
asaṃvegād iha vidhā tatra niṣṭheti cāgamāt //*

## Source Caution

The second half is compressed and should be checked carefully in the Bhāṣya, especially *iha vidhā* and the scriptural contrast between commencement here and completion there. The doctrinal structure is clear: the first three fruits are attained in the desire domain, the final fruit can be attained in all three domains, and the path of seeing does not arise in the higher domains.

## Padaccheda

- *kāme* — in the desire domain
- *traya-āptiḥ* — attainment of the three
- *antyasya* — of the final one
- *triṣu* — in the three domains
- *na ūrdhvam* — not above
- *hi* — for
- *dṛk-pathaḥ* — path of seeing
- *asaṃvegāt* — because of lack of urgency or spiritual shock
- *iha* — here
- *vidhā* — institution, commencement, or prescribed undertaking
- *tatra* — there
- *niṣṭhā* — completion
- *iti ca āgamāt* — and because scripture says so

## Literal Translation

The attainment of the first three occurs in the desire domain; that of the final one occurs in all three. For the path of seeing does not exist above, because of the absence of spiritual urgency, and because scripture says: the undertaking is here, the completion there.

## Doctrinal Structure

The four fruits are distributed asymmetrically:

```text
first three fruits
    attained in the desire domain

final fruit, Arhatship
    attainable in all three domains
```

The decisive reason is that the path of seeing does not newly arise in the higher domains. One may complete the path there, but its initial breakthrough belongs to the desire domain.

## Saṃvega and Commencement

The verse connects the desire domain with *saṃvega*: the shock, urgency, or existential pressure that impels entry into the path.

```text
desire domain
    suffering encountered with sufficient immediacy
        ↓
saṃvega
        ↓
path of seeing
```

The higher domains may possess greater calm, but precisely for that reason they do not provide the same initiating contradiction. Their relative serenity can support completion without supplying the crisis that begins seeing.

## Here the Beginning, There the Completion

The scriptural formula gives a compact architecture:

```text
iha vidhā
    here: institution or commencement

atra/tatra niṣṭhā
    there: completion
```

Thus Path need not begin and end in the same domain. Its ground of origination and its field of completion are distinct.

This recapitulates the distinction already visible in VAK 6.47–6.49:

```text
local basis of path-entry
    ≠
full range of path-completion
```

## Immediate and Mediate

The path of seeing is an immediate breakthrough, but this verse shows that its immediacy has determinate conditions. It arises where contradiction is sufficiently manifest to compel a reorganization of cognition.

Completion is mediated through the subsequent path and may occur in a higher domain:

```text
urgent contradiction
    ↓
seeing begins
    ↓
cultivation continues
    ↓
final fruit may be completed elsewhere
```

The immediate is therefore not groundless. It is the concentrated result of a determinate worldly and contemplative situation.

## Basic Organon Light

The verse distinguishes origin from fulfillment:

```text
origin
    desire domain
    contradiction and urgency

mediation
    seeing and cultivation

fulfillment
    final fruit across the three domains
```

This is a useful limit on any abstract theory of liberation. The Path is universal in range, but it begins from a determinate contradiction. The higher is not self-originating; it presupposes the work initiated below.

The calm higher ground can complete what the contradictory lower ground compelled into motion.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.55 states that the first three fruits are attained in the desire domain, while the final fruit can be attained in all three domains. The path of seeing does not originate above the desire domain because the initiating urgency is absent there. The Path therefore begins where contradiction becomes compelling, but its completion can extend beyond that original ground.
