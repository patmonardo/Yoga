# VAK 6.12

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

आनापानस्मृतिः प्रज्ञा पञ्चभूर्वायुर्गोचरा ।  
कामाश्रया न बाह्यानां षड्विधा गणनादिभिः ॥ ६.१२ ॥

### IAST

**ānāpānasmṛtiḥ prajñā pañcabhūr vāyur-gocarā |**  
**kāmāśrayā na bāhyānāṃ ṣaḍvidhā gaṇanādibhiḥ || 6.12 ||**

## Literal Translation

Recollection of inhalation and exhalation is Prajñā; it belongs to five levels and has wind as its cognitive range. It has its support in the desire-domain, does not belong to outsiders, and is sixfold through counting and the other methods.

## Philosophical Translation

Breath-recollection is not merely passive attention but a discriminative cognition. It may operate on five meditative levels, takes the moving breath-element as its object, and arises upon the embodied basis of the desire-domain. It is distinctive to the Buddha’s Path and unfolds through six ordered operations beginning with counting.

## Determination

The decisive statement is:

```text
ānāpānasmṛtiḥ prajñā
```

The practice is named recollection, but its intrinsic determination is Prajñā. It is therefore not bare awareness of a sensation. Recollection maintains continuity with the breathing process, while Prajñā discriminates its direction, phase, duration, arising, continuation, and cessation.

The verse classifies the practice across several independent axes:

```text
intrinsic nature:
    Prajñā

levels:
    five

object-range:
    breath as vāyu

support:
    desire-domain embodiment

method:
    sixfold
```

The five levels are the desire-domain and the four principal concentrations. The support remains embodied existence in the desire-domain, while the cognition taking breath as its object may belong to increasingly refined meditative levels.

Breath is classified as vāyu, a moving elemental process. The ordinary interpretation “I breathe” is displaced by discrimination of an arising, continuing, and ceasing wind-process. A personal act becomes an analyzable dharma-process.

The six operations are traditionally ordered as:

```text
1. gaṇanā — counting
2. anugama — following
3. sthāpanā — establishing
4. upalakṣaṇā — discerning
5. vivarta — turning
6. pariśuddhi — purification
```

Their development is:

```text
counting
    establishes order

following
    establishes continuity

establishing
    establishes cognitive location

discerning
    establishes determination

turning
    relates the process to the wider Dharma-field

purification
    establishes adequate knowing
```

Thus a raw physiological event is compiled into a lawful cognitive field:

```text
raw breathing
    ↓
ordered sequence
    ↓
continuous process
    ↓
stable cognitive object
    ↓
discriminated characteristics
    ↓
relation to Dharma
    ↓
purified knowledge
```

This practice stands in the middle between the poles:

```text
Dhātu–Indriya
    elemental process and cognitive capacity

Loka–Karma
    manifested embodied activity

Mārga
    their organization into Prajñā
```

The senses provide material, but they no longer determine the nature of the field. Breath-recollection converts an embodied activity into an internally founded and discriminated field of Prajñā.

## Determinate Summary

```text
Ānāpānasmṛti is Prajñā.

It belongs to five levels,
takes vāyu as its range,
rests upon desire-domain embodiment,
and develops through six operations.
```

The central result is:

```text
Breath-recollection does not merely
concentrate cognition upon a sensation.

It constructs a lawful field
in which an embodied process
becomes an object of Prajñā.
```
