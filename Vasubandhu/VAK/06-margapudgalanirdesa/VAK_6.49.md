# VAK 6.49

## Sanskrit

विमुक्त्यानन्तर्यपथा लौकिकास्तु यथाक्रमम् ।
शान्ताद्युदाराद्याकाराः उत्तराधरगोचराः ॥

*vimuktyānantaryapathā laukikās tu yathākramam /
śāntādyudārādyākārā uttarādharagocarāḥ //*

## Source Caution

The transmitted compound *śāntādyudārādyākārāḥ* should be checked against the Bhāṣya. The doctrinal structure is nevertheless clear: the worldly liberation path and immediate path operate through two contrasting sets of aspects directed respectively toward higher and lower grounds.

## Padaccheda

- *vimukti-pathāḥ* — paths of liberation
- *ānantarya-pathāḥ* — immediate paths
- *laukikāḥ* — worldly
- *tu* — but
- *yathākramam* — respectively
- *śānta-ādi-ākārāḥ* — having aspects beginning with calmness
- *udāra-ādi-ākārāḥ* — having aspects beginning with coarseness or defectiveness
- *uttara-gocarāḥ* — having the higher ground as object-field
- *adhara-gocarāḥ* — having the lower ground as object-field

## Grammar

The two coordinated subjects are the worldly liberation paths and immediate paths. They are characterized respectively by two sets of aspects and two object-fields: the liberation path takes the higher ground under aspects beginning with calmness, while the immediate path takes the lower ground under aspects beginning with coarseness or defectiveness.

## Literal Translation

But the worldly paths of liberation and immediate paths are, respectively, of aspects beginning with calmness and aspects beginning with coarseness, having the higher and lower grounds as their respective object-fields.

## Philosophical Translation

In worldly dispassion, the immediate path determines the lower ground through its deficient aspects, while the liberation path determines the higher ground through peaceful and superior aspects.

## Seed Analysis

The worldly path proceeds through a comparative judgment between two conditioned levels. The lower ground is negatively determined as coarse, defective, or obstructive; the higher ground is positively determined as calm, superior, and conducive to escape. Dispassion is therefore produced by revaluing the field rather than by directly determining the conditioned hierarchy from an uncontaminated principle.

## Logical Determination

```text
lower ground
    determined as inadequate
        → immediate path

higher ground
    determined as relatively peaceful
        → liberation path
```

The two paths display an immediate–mediate structure. The immediate path directly removes the obstruction tied to the lower ground, while the liberation path is the mediated possession of the higher determination made possible by that removal.

## Light Organon Interpretation

The lower ground founders through its contradictions and the higher appears as its relative resolution. Yet the higher is still only another conditioned ground. Worldly dispassion therefore resolves contradiction within the hierarchy rather than from beyond it.

```text
worldly path
    lower contradiction
        → higher conditioned resolution

supramundane path
    conditioned hierarchy
        → determination from uncontaminated principle
```

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.49 explains worldly dispassion as a comparative determination of conditioned grounds. The immediate path negatively determines the lower ground through aspects beginning with coarseness, while the liberation path positively determines the higher ground through aspects beginning with calmness. The verse therefore distinguishes immediate removal from mediated possession while keeping both within the conditioned hierarchy.
