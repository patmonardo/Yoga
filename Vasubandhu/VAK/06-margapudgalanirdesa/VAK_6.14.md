# VAK 6.14

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

निष्पन्नशमथः कुर्यात् स्मृत्युपस्थानभावनाम् ।
कायविच्चित्तधर्माणां द्विलक्षणपरीक्षणात् ॥ ६.१४ ॥

### IAST

niṣpanna-śamathaḥ kuryāt smṛtyupasthāna-bhāvanām |
kāya-vic-citta-dharmāṇāṃ dvi-lakṣaṇa-parīkṣaṇāt || 6.14 ||

## Padaccheda

- niṣpanna-śamathaḥ
- kuryāt
- smṛti-upasthāna-bhāvanām
- kāya-vit-citta-dharmāṇām
- dvi-lakṣaṇa-parīkṣaṇāt

## Literal Translation

One whose śamatha has been accomplished should cultivate the Foundations of Recollection through examining the two determinations of body, feeling, cognition, and dharmas.

## Philosophical Translation

Having completed the stabilization of the cognitive field, the practitioner should cultivate the four Foundations of Recollection by scientifically examining body, feeling, cognition, and dharmas according to both their intrinsic and universal determinations.

## Doctrinal Determination

The two preceding entrance practices, aśubha and ānāpānasmṛti, establish śamatha. Śamatha is not the terminus. It creates the stable cognitive laboratory in which vipaśyanā can proceed.

The four object-fields are:

- kāya — body
- vedanā — feeling
- citta — cognition
- dharma — determinations

Each is examined according to two lakṣaṇas:

1. svalakṣaṇa — intrinsic determination
2. sāmānyalakṣaṇa — universal determination

The universal determinations are scoped as follows:

- conditioned dharmas: impermanent
- contaminated dharmas: suffering or unstable
- all dharmas: empty and not-self

Thus the Path unites particular determination and universal law. Svalakṣaṇa without sāmānyalakṣaṇa yields fragmented empiricism; sāmānyalakṣaṇa without svalakṣaṇa yields empty abstraction.

## Scientific and Logical Determination

Śamatha establishes field-stability. Smṛti preserves the attained determination. Parīkṣaṇa performs scientific examination. Vipaśyanā sees the field adequately.

The possessive field is rewritten:

- my body → body as body
- my feeling → feeling as feeling
- my cognition → cognition as cognition
- my experience → dharmas as determinations

Smṛti guards the sentence-form and prevents the object from collapsing back into appropriation.

## Seer–Seen Structure

This is the lower seer–seen discipline: cognition first learns to separate, classify, and examine its field. The knower does not yet stand as a metaphysical Puruṣa over against Prakṛti. Rather, the empirical-cognitive field is made transparent through disciplined discrimination.

## Determinate Summary

Having accomplished śamatha, the practitioner does not eliminate mind. The practitioner begins Science. Body, feeling, cognition, and dharmas are each examined according to intrinsic determination and universal law.

Śamatha establishes the laboratory. Smṛti preserves the sentence. Parīkṣaṇa performs the Science. Vipaśyanā liberates the Field Knower.
