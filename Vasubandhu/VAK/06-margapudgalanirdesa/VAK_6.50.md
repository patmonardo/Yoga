# VAK 6.50

## Sanskrit

यद्यकोप्यः क्षयज्ञानादनुत्पादमतिः न चेत् ।
क्षयज्ञानमशैक्षो वा दृष्टिः सर्वस्य सार्हतः ॥

*yady akopyaḥ kṣayajñānād anutpādamatiḥ na cet /
kṣayajñānam aśaikṣo vā dṛṣṭiḥ sarvasya sārhataḥ //*

## Source Caution

The transmitted text is compressed. The first word should be divided as *yadi akopyaḥ*, “if he is immovable.” The final expression *sarvasya sārhataḥ* means, in effect, “belonging to every Arhat,” but its exact grammatical expansion should be revisited with the Bhāṣya.

The doctrinal structure is clear: the verse distinguishes the sequence of cognition in an immovable Arhat from that in an Arhat still capable of falling away.

## Padaccheda

- *yadi* — if
- *akopyaḥ* — immovable; incapable of being shaken or falling away
- *kṣaya-jñānāt* — after the knowledge of destruction
- *anutpāda-matiḥ* — cognition or knowledge of non-arising
- *na cet* — if not; otherwise
- *kṣaya-jñānam* — knowledge of destruction
- *aśaikṣaḥ dṛṣṭiḥ* — the right view of one beyond training
- *vā* — or
- *sarvasya arhataḥ* — belonging to every Arhat

## Literal Translation

If he is immovable, after the knowledge of destruction there is the cognition of non-arising. Otherwise, there is either the knowledge of destruction or the view of one beyond training. The latter view belongs to every Arhat.

## Technical Distinction

The verse distinguishes three cognitions:

```text
kṣaya-jñāna
    knowledge that the task has been accomplished

anutpāda-jñāna
    knowledge that the abandoned defilements
    will not arise again

aśaikṣa-dṛṣṭi
    right view belonging to the state
    in which no further training remains
```

For the immovable Arhat:

```text
kṣaya-jñāna
    ↓
anutpāda-jñāna
```

The destruction is not merely accomplished. Its irreversibility is known.

For an Arhat who is not immovable:

```text
kṣaya-jñāna
    ↓
kṣaya-jñāna again
        or
aśaikṣa right view
```

This marks an important distinction between completion and known irrevocability of completion.

## Immediate–Mediate Structure

The destruction of the final defilement is immediate in the sense that the obstruction is actually terminated:

```text
final obstruction
    ↓
kṣaya
```

But the knowledge of destruction is the cognition that grasps this accomplished fact:

```text
destruction
    ↓
kṣaya-jñāna
```

Then, in the immovable Arhat, the result is carried further:

```text
knowledge of accomplished destruction
    ↓
knowledge of its non-recurrence
```

Thus:

```text
immediate event
    destruction of the defilements

mediated cognition
    knowledge that destruction has occurred

completed mediation
    knowledge that the destroyed condition
    cannot reappear
```

The path does not merely remove. It converts removal into self-knowing and stable liberation.

## Philosophical Determination

We can distinguish three stages:

```text
Negation
    the defilement is destroyed

Reflection
    the destruction is known

Completed reflection
    the destruction is known as irreversible
```

The last is not merely an empirical prediction that the defilement will probably not return. It is cognition of the attained state as possessing its own necessity.

## Basic Organon Light

```text
Immediate
    destruction occurs

Mediate
    destruction is known through kṣaya-jñāna

Unity of Immediate and Mediate
    destruction is known as non-arising
```

Or, provisionally:

```text
Being of liberation
    the obstruction is absent

Essence of liberation
    its absence is reflected and known

Concept of liberation
    the result knows itself as self-maintaining
```

We should not force the full Hegelian architecture into one verse, but the sequence is clearly more than chronological succession. The knowledge of non-arising makes the result self-related: liberation relates to its own ground and knows that ground as excluding renewed bondage.

## Relation to the Path

The immediate cessation does not exhaust the notion of liberation. The path contains:

```text
removal
possession
knowledge
stabilization
```

Liberation is fully actual only when the negation of bondage becomes a determinate possession and, in its highest form, knows itself as irreversible.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.50 distinguishes accomplished liberation from cognition of its irreversibility. Every Arhat possesses knowledge of destruction and the right view beyond training, but only the immovable Arhat proceeds from knowledge of destruction to knowledge of non-arising.

```text
destruction
    → knowledge of destruction
    → knowledge of non-recurrence
```

Liberation is mediated not because freedom is deferred by the path, but because immediate cessation must become known, possessed, and self-stabilizing actuality.
