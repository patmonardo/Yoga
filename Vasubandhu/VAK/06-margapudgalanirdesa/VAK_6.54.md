# VAK 6.54

## Sanskrit

ब्राह्मण्यं ब्रह्मचक्रं च तदेव ब्रह्मवर्तनात् ।
धर्मचक्रं तु दृङ्मार्ग आशुगत्वादरादिभिः ॥

*brāhmaṇyaṃ brahmacakraṃ ca tadeva brahmavartanāt /
dharmacakraṃ tu dṛṅmārga āśugatvād arādibhiḥ //*

## Source Caution

The exact force of *brāhmaṇya* and *brahmavartana* should be checked in the Bhāṣya. The second half is clearer: the path of seeing is called the Dharma-wheel because of its swift movement and because it is comparable to a wheel with spokes and related parts.

## Padaccheda

- *brāhmaṇyam* — brahminhood; the brahmic or noble state
- *brahma-cakram* — Brahma-wheel
- *tat eva* — that very same thing
- *brahma-vartanāt* — because it is turned or set in motion by Brahmā/the Buddha as Brahmā
- *dharma-cakram* — Dharma-wheel
- *tu* — but
- *dṛk-mārgaḥ* — path of seeing
- *āśu-gatvāt* — because of moving swiftly
- *ara-ādibhiḥ* — because of spokes and the other wheel-components

## Literal Translation

That very brahminhood is also the Brahma-wheel because it is set in motion by Brahmā. But the path of seeing is the Dharma-wheel because of its swift movement and because of its spokes and other wheel-parts.

## Philosophical Translation

The stainless Path, insofar as it is publicly set in motion by the Buddha, is the Brahma-wheel. More specifically, the path of seeing is the Dharma-wheel: it moves rapidly through the truths and possesses an articulated internal structure comparable to hub, spokes, and rim.

## From Fruit to Wheel

VAK 6.51–6.53 analyzed the fruit as an achieved totality. VAK 6.54 now presents the Path in objective, transmissible form:

```text
attained Path
    ↓
articulated teaching and operation
    ↓
wheel set in motion
```

A wheel is not merely a static symbol. It is a structure whose differentiated parts operate together in motion.

## Brahma-wheel and Dharma-wheel

The verse places two determinations side by side:

```text
Brahma-wheel
    Path as set in motion by the supreme teacher

Dharma-wheel
    Path of seeing as a structured and rapid operation
```

The first emphasizes authoritative inauguration. The second emphasizes the Path's own articulated movement.

## Path of Seeing as Wheel

The path of seeing traverses the truth-structure without interruption. Its rapidity matters because it is not a leisurely comparison among optional objects; once entered, the sequence proceeds as one determinate operation.

The wheel analogy adds internal organization:

```text
hub
    governing center

spokes
    differentiated path-factors

rim
    unity holding the factors together

rotation
    operative traversal of the truths
```

The exact assignment of wheel-parts should be left to the Bhāṣya pass, but the logical point is secure: the Path is a moving whole whose moments have validity through their place in the total operation.

## Basic Organon Light

This verse marks a transition from subjective possession to objective operation.

```text
fruit
    Path possessed by the practitioner

wheel
    Path externalized as a self-connected movement
```

The Dharma-wheel is therefore not a mere image attached to the Path. It expresses that the Path has become:

- internally articulated,
- continuously operative,
- transmissible,
- capable of carrying cognition through a determinate sequence.

The path of seeing is immediate in its continuous movement, yet mediated through its differentiated moments. Its immediacy is the achieved unity of its mediation.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.54 converts the accomplished Path into the image of a wheel. The Brahma-wheel emphasizes its authoritative setting in motion; the Dharma-wheel identifies the path of seeing as a rapid, internally articulated whole. The Path is no longer only possessed as fruit: it operates objectively as a connected movement through the truths.
