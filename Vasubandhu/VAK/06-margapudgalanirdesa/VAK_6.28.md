# VAK 6.28

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

क्षान्तिज्ञानान्यनन्तर्यमुक्तिमार्गा यथाक्रमम् ।  
अदृष्टदृष्टेर्दृङ्मार्गस्तत्र पञ्चदश क्षणाः ॥ ६.२८ ॥

### IAST

**kṣānti-jñānāny ānantarya-mukti-mārgā yathākramam |**  
**adṛṣṭa-dṛṣṭer dṛṅ-mārgas tatra pañcadaśa kṣaṇāḥ || 6.28 ||**

## Source Caution

VAK 6.28 interprets the acceptances and knowledges enumerated in VAK 6.25–6.27 according to their path-functions.

The first half must be distributed in order:

```text
kṣāntayaḥ
    ānantarya-mārgāḥ

jñānāni
    mukti-mārgāḥ
```

Thus the acceptances are uninterrupted paths, while the knowledges are liberation paths.

The second half distinguishes the Path of Seeing from the sixteenth moment. Only the first fifteen moments belong to the Path of Seeing in the strict sense, because they still perform the seeing of what had not previously been seen. The sixteenth moment follows after the four truths have already been completely seen and therefore belongs to the Path of Cultivation.

## Padaccheda

```text
kṣānti-jñānāni
ānantarya-mukti-mārgāḥ
yathā-kramam

adṛṣṭa-dṛṣṭeḥ
dṛk-mārgaḥ
tatra
pañcadaśa
kṣaṇāḥ
```

## Grammar

- **kṣānti-jñānāni** — nominative plural neuter: “the acceptances and knowledges.” The compound coordinates the eight acceptances and eight knowledges of the sixteen-moment realization.
- **ānantarya-mukti-mārgāḥ** — nominative plural masculine, predicative: “uninterrupted paths and liberation paths.” The two terms are distributed respectively across the acceptances and knowledges.
- **yathā-kramam** — indeclinable: “in the stated order,” “respectively.”
- **adṛṣṭa-dṛṣṭeḥ** — genitive singular compound: “because of seeing what had not been seen,” or “of the seeing of the unseen.” It gives the defining reason for the name Path of Seeing.
- **dṛk-mārgaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “Path of Seeing”; equivalent here to **darśana-mārga**.
- **tatra** — indeclinable: “there,” within the sixteen-moment direct realization.
- **pañcadaśa kṣaṇāḥ** — nominative plural masculine: “fifteen moments.”

## Literal Translation

The acceptances and knowledges are respectively uninterrupted paths and liberation paths. Because it sees what had not been seen, it is the Path of Seeing; within it there are fifteen moments.

## Philosophical Translation

Each Acceptance is an uninterrupted path that directly removes the obstruction opposed to seeing its truth; each corresponding Knowledge is a liberation path in which release from that obstruction is possessed. This sequence is called the Path of Seeing because it brings into view what had never before been directly seen. Strictly speaking, only the first fifteen moments have this power of first seeing. By the sixteenth moment, the four truths have already been completely seen, so that final moment begins the Path of Cultivation.

## Acceptance and Knowledge as Path-Functions

The verse gives a precise functional distinction:

```text
kṣānti
    Acceptance
    ↓
    ānantarya-mārga
    uninterrupted path

jñāna
    Knowledge
    ↓
    mukti-mārga
    liberation path
```

### Acceptance as uninterrupted path

```text
ānantarya
    without interval
    immediately effective
```

Acceptance is not merely an attitude of tolerance. It is the path-moment that directly confronts the obstruction opposed to seeing a truth.

Its function is eliminative:

```text
obstruction opposed to seeing
    ↓ directly struck by Acceptance
abandonment
```

It is called an uninterrupted path because the corresponding liberation follows immediately, without another path-moment intervening.

### Knowledge as liberation path

```text
mukti
    release
    liberation
```

Knowledge follows the Acceptance directed toward the same truth and domain. It is the moment in which freedom from the abandoned obstruction is established and possessed.

Its function is appropriative:

```text
obstruction abandoned
    ↓
release possessed as Knowledge
```

The pair is therefore not repetition:

```text
Acceptance
    removes

Knowledge
    possesses the release
```

## The Special Power of Seeing

The defining phrase is:

```text
adṛṣṭa-dṛṣṭi
    seeing what has not been seen
```

This gives **darśana** a strict path-meaning. Seeing is not ordinary perception, conceptual recognition, or repeated contemplation. It is the first direct disclosure of a truth that had never previously been taken as an uncontaminated object.

The power of Seeing consists in three linked determinations.

### 1. Seeing is originary

```text
before the Path of Seeing:
    truth heard
    truth reflected upon
    truth prepared for

in the Path of Seeing:
    truth directly seen for the first time
```

The truths are not newly produced. What is new is their direct supramundane manifestation within the path.

### 2. Seeing is transformative

The act of seeing is inseparable from abandonment:

```text
truth directly seen
    =
obstruction opposed to that seeing directly removed
```

Seeing therefore does not leave the knower unchanged. Its cognitive act is simultaneously a path-function.

This confirms the threefold abhisamaya of VAK 6.27:

```text
Seeing
    act

Object-support
    truth seen

Function
    obstruction abandoned
```

In VAK 6.28 these are no longer merely three descriptions. They are joined in the operative power of the path.

### 3. Seeing establishes a threshold

The distinction between unseen and seen generates a determinate boundary:

```text
adṛṣṭa
    not yet directly seen
        ↓ Path of Seeing

dṛṣṭa
    directly seen
```

Once a truth has been directly seen, a later moment concerning that completed field cannot again be a first seeing. It belongs instead to cultivation of what has already been disclosed.

The Path of Seeing is therefore intrinsically self-limiting:

```text
it exists only
    while there remains
    something not yet seen
```

This is its special power and its exact limit.

## Why Only Fifteen Moments?

The sixteen moments are:

```text
1–4
    suffering

5–8
    origin

9–12
    cessation

13–16
    path
```

The first fifteen moments extend through:

```text
15. Anvaya-Acceptance concerning Path
```

At that fifteenth moment, the final obstruction opposed to seeing the four truths is directly removed.

The sixteenth moment is:

```text
16. Anvaya-Knowledge concerning Path
```

By then:

```text
all four truths
    in the desire realm
    and in the higher realms
have been directly seen
```

Therefore:

```text
moments 1–15
    Path of Seeing

moment 16
    Path of Cultivation
```

The sixteenth moment remains part of the sixteen-moment **satya-abhisamaya**, but it is not part of the Path of Seeing in the strict classification.

## The Fifteenth Moment as Completion of First Seeing

The fifteenth moment has a unique boundary-position:

```text
Anvaya-Acceptance concerning Path
    final uninterrupted path
    final first-seeing moment
    final abandonment belonging to Seeing
```

It completes the conversion:

```text
not-yet-seen field
    ↓
completely seen field
```

The sixteenth moment then confirms and possesses the liberation produced by this final Acceptance.

Thus the transition is:

```text
fifteenth moment:
    completion of Seeing

sixteenth moment:
    first cultivation of the seen
```

## Darśana and Bhāvanā

The verse gives a clean functional distinction between two paths.

### Path of Seeing

```text
darśana-mārga
    sees what was not previously seen
    abandons afflictions removable by seeing
    consists of fifteen moments
```

### Path of Cultivation

```text
bhāvanā-mārga
    cultivates what has already been seen
    repeatedly develops the realized truth
    begins with the sixteenth moment
```

The transition is therefore not from ignorance to mere information. It is from first disclosure to sustained formation by what has been disclosed.

```text
Seeing
    opens the truth

Cultivation
    stabilizes and completes its power
```

Acceptance

The Path of Seeing includes every moment required for first direct seeing, but no moment whose object has already been completely seen.

## Logical Determination

The fundamental dyad is:

```text
adṛṣṭa
    not seen

dṛṣṭa
    seen
```

The Path of Seeing is the determinate mediation between them:

```text
not-seen
    ↓ through Acceptance and Knowledge
seen
```

Within each pair:

```text
Acceptance
    negative function
    removal of obstruction

Knowledge
    positive function
    possession of liberation
```

The path does not merely alternate between two mental states. It advances through a sequence in which each Acceptance removes a determinate limit and each Knowledge establishes the achieved release.

The special power of Seeing can therefore be expressed as:

```text
Seeing {
    object:
        PreviouslyUnseenTruth

    act:
        DirectUncontaminatedDisclosure

    function:
        AbandonmentOfOpposedObstruction

    result:
        TruthAsSeen
}
```

Its completion necessarily transforms Seeing into Cultivation:

```text
when no truth remains unseen
    PathOfSeeing terminates
    PathOfCultivation begins
```

This is a particularly exact example of a path being defined by the transformation it accomplishes and thereby abolishing the condition of its own continuation.

## Interpretive Note

VAK 6.27 unified the sixteen moments as one direct realization. VAK 6.28 reveals the internal power of that realization.

```text
Acceptance
    cuts through the obstruction

Knowledge
    stands in the liberation
```

Seeing is powerful because it is simultaneously:

```text
cognitive
    truth is directly disclosed

negative
    the opposed obstruction is abandoned

positive
    liberation is immediately possessed
```

Yet its greatest precision lies in its finitude. Seeing does not continue indefinitely as Seeing. It brings the unseen into sight, and by succeeding it creates a new path-condition:

```text
what was unseen
    has become seen

therefore:
    seeing gives way to cultivation
```

At the kārikā-pass level, this is the decisive determination. The detailed enumeration of the specific afflictions abandoned by each Acceptance belongs to the Bhāṣya pass.
