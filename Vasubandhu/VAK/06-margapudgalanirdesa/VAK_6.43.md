# VAK 6.43

## Sanskrit

तत्पाञ्चविध्यात् पञ्चैव शुद्धावासोपपत्तयः ।
निरोधलाभ्यनागामी कायसाक्षी पुनर्मतः ॥

*tad-pāñcavidhyāt pañcaiva śuddhāvāsopapattayaḥ /
nirodha-lābhy anāgāmī kāya-sākṣī punar mataḥ //*

## Source Caution

This is a first-pass seed analysis of the kārikā alone. The technical grounds for the five Pure Abode rebirths and the precise definition of the bodily witness are deferred to the Bhāṣya pass.

## Padaccheda

- *tat-pāñca-vidhyāt* — because of its fivefold division
- *pañca eva* — exactly five
- *śuddhāvāsa-upapattayaḥ* — rebirths in the Pure Abodes
- *nirodha-lābhī* — one who has attained cessation
- *anāgāmī* — a non-returner
- *kāya-sākṣī* — bodily witness
- *punar mataḥ* — is further regarded as

## Grammar

The ablative compound *tat-pāñcavidhyāt* gives the ground for the fivefold enumeration of Pure Abode rebirths. The second half defines the designation *kāyasākṣin* by apposition: a non-returner who has attained cessation is regarded as a bodily witness.

## Literal Translation

Because of its fivefold division, there are exactly five rebirths in the Pure Abodes. A non-returner who has attained cessation is further regarded as a bodily witness.

## Seed Analysis

The verse establishes two linked classifications:

1. a fivefold differentiation corresponding to five Pure Abode rebirths;
2. the designation **bodily witness** for a non-returner who has attained cessation.

The verse does not yet explain the experiential or doctrinal force of *kāyasākṣin*. That belongs to the Bhāṣya pass.
