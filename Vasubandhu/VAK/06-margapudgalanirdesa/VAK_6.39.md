# VAK 6.39

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

तद्विशेषः पुनः कर्मक्लेशेन्द्रियविशेषतः ॥ ६.३९ ॥

### IAST

**tad-viśeṣaḥ punaḥ karma-kleśendriya-viśeṣataḥ || 6.39 ||**

## Source Caution

The verse gives the general basis for the distinctions among the practitioner-types just enumerated.

It does not yet allocate each subtype in detail. It states only that their differentiation arises from differences in:

```text
karma
kleśa
indriya
```

The detailed correspondence belongs to the Bhāṣya pass.

## Padaccheda

```text
tat-viśeṣaḥ
punaḥ
karma-kleśa-indriya-viśeṣataḥ
```

## Grammar

- **tat-viśeṣaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “their distinction” or “the distinction among them.”
- **punaḥ** — indeclinable: “again,” “further,” or “in turn.”
- **karma-kleśa-indriya-viśeṣataḥ** — ablative expression: “because of differences in karma, affliction, and faculty.”

## Literal Translation

Their distinction, in turn, is due to differences in karma, affliction, and faculty.

## Philosophical Translation

The several practitioner-types described above are differentiated according to variations in their karmic formation, their afflictive condition, and the strength or quality of their faculties.

## Seed Analysis

The verse supplies the schema underlying the preceding classification:

```text
variation in practitioner-type
    arises from
        variation in karma
        variation in affliction
        variation in faculty
```

It therefore links person-classification to three determinate conditions rather than treating the classes as arbitrary names.

## Logical Determination

VAK 6.37–6.38 enumerate several kinds of non-returner and upward-streamer.

VAK 6.39 provides their common differentiating principle:

```text
same general path-position
    differentiated by
        karma
        kleśa
        indriya
```

The classification is therefore relational and conditional.
