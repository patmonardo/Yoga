# VAK 6.31

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

षोडशे तु फलस्थौ तौ यत्र यः प्रतिपन्नकः ।  
श्रद्धाधिमुक्तदृष्ट्याप्तौ मृदुतीक्ष्णेन्द्रियौ तदा ॥ ६.३१ ॥

### IAST

**ṣoḍaśe tu phalasthau tau yatra yaḥ pratipannakaḥ |**  
**śraddhādhimukta-dṛṣṭyāptau mṛdu-tīkṣṇendriyau tadā || 6.31 ||**

## Source Caution

The verse completes the transition begun in VAK 6.29–6.30. During the first fifteen moments, practitioners are candidates progressing toward a fruit. At the sixteenth moment, they are established in the corresponding fruit.

The two faculty-types also receive new names:

```text
gentle faculty
    Faith-Follower
        → Faith-Liberated

sharp faculty
    Dharma-Follower
        → One Attained through View
```

The verse does not introduce a new truth-object. It changes the status of the practitioner because the seeing-sequence has reached its sixteenth moment.

## Grammar

- **ṣoḍaśe** — locative singular: “at the sixteenth [moment].”
- **tu** — adversative or transitional particle: “but,” “however,” marking the change from candidate-status to fruit-status.
- **phala-sthau** — nominative dual masculine: “the two established in the fruit.”
- **tau** — nominative dual masculine: “those two.”
- **yatra yaḥ pratipannakaḥ** — distributive expression: “toward whichever [fruit] each was progressing as a candidate.”
- **śraddhā-adhimukta-dṛṣṭi-āptau** — nominative dual masculine: “the Faith-Liberated and the one Attained through View.”
- **mṛdu-tīkṣṇa-indriyau** — nominative dual masculine: “the one of gentle faculties and the one of sharp faculties.”
- **tadā** — indeclinable: “then,” namely at the sixteenth moment.

## Literal Translation

But at the sixteenth moment those two are established in the fruit toward which each had been progressing. Then the one of gentle faculties is Faith-Liberated, and the one of sharp faculties is one Attained through View.

## Philosophical Translation

At the sixteenth cognitive moment, the status of the two practitioners is transformed. Each becomes established in the very fruit toward which that practitioner had been advancing during the preceding fifteen moments. The gentle-faculty Faith-Follower is now called Faith-Liberated, while the sharp-faculty Dharma-Follower is now called one Attained through View.

## The Sixteenth Moment

VAK 6.28 distinguished:

```text
moments 1–15
    Path of Seeing

moment 16
    beginning of the Path of Cultivation
```

VAK 6.31 states the corresponding change in the practitioner:

```text
moments 1–15
    candidate toward a fruit

moment 16
    established in that fruit
```

The sixteenth moment is therefore not merely another cognition added to the sequence. It is the moment in which candidacy becomes possession.

## From Pratipannaka to Phalastha

### Candidate

```text
pratipannaka
    one who has entered upon the path toward a fruit
    but is not yet established in that fruit
```

### Fruit-established

```text
phala-stha
    one who stands in
    or is established in
    the attained fruit
```

The transition is:

```text
progressing toward
    → established in
```

This is the primary determination of the verse.

## “Whichever Fruit Each Was Approaching”

The expression **yatra yaḥ pratipannakaḥ** preserves the distinctions established in VAK 6.29–6.30.

Depending on prior abandonment, a practitioner may have been progressing toward:

```text
stream-entry
once-return
non-return
```

At the sixteenth moment, each becomes established in that corresponding fruit.

Thus the sixteenth moment does not flatten previous differences. It actualizes the determinate trajectory already established.

## Transformation of the Two Followers

### Gentle faculty

```text
mṛdu-indriya
    during Path of Seeing:
        śraddhā-anusārin
        Faith-Follower

    at the sixteenth moment:
        śraddhā-adhimukta
        Faith-Liberated
```

The practitioner’s relation to faith is no longer merely that of following. Faith has become a mode of release or decisive commitment within attained fruit.

### Sharp faculty

```text
tīkṣṇa-indriya
    during Path of Seeing:
        dharma-anusārin
        Dharma-Follower

    at the sixteenth moment:
        dṛṣṭi-āpta
        One Attained through View
```

The sharp-faculty practitioner is now named according to attainment through direct view or insight.

## One Sequence, Two Renamings

The verse gives a systematic change of designation:

```text
same practitioner-continuum
same faculty
same corresponding fruit

but:
    path-position changes
    therefore name changes
```

This means that the names are not fixed personal identities. They are path-functional classifications.

```text
name
    follows
        faculty
        path-stage
        attained status
```

## Logical Determination

VAK 6.29–6.30 gave the practitioner as a determinate candidate:

```text
faculty
    +
prior abandonment
    +
Path of Seeing
        =
fruit-candidacy
```

VAK 6.31 gives the result:

```text
completed Seeing
    +
sixteenth moment
        =
fruit-possession
```

The movement is therefore:

```text
possibility determined by path-position
    → actuality as fruit-established status
```

The sixteenth moment is the conversion of a directed process into an attained result.

## Interpretive Note

The verse shows that full Seeing changes not only what is known but the formal status of the knower-continuum.

Before the sixteenth moment:

```text
the fruit is the end approached
```

At the sixteenth moment:

```text
the fruit is the position occupied
```

Thus the completed seeing-sequence closes an objective traversal and produces a new stable path-position from which cultivation proceeds.

For the kārikā pass, this should remain a local structural determination. The Bhāṣya pass can later clarify the exact force of **śraddhādhimukta** and **dṛṣṭyāpta**, as well as the detailed relation between the sixteenth moment and the several fruits.

## Determinate Summary

```text
At the sixteenth moment:

    each practitioner becomes established
    in the fruit toward which
    that practitioner had been progressing

    the gentle-faculty Faith-Follower
        becomes Faith-Liberated

    the sharp-faculty Dharma-Follower
        becomes One Attained through View

The decisive transition is:

    candidate
        → fruit-established

    following
        → attained possession
```

VAK 6.31 determines the sixteenth moment as the point at which the directed movement of the Path of Seeing becomes an attained fruit and the two followers receive names appropriate to realized possession.
