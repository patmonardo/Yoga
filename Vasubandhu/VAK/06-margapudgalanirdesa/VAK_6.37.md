# VAK 6.37

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

सोऽन्तरोत्पन्नसंस्कारासंस्कारपरिनिर्वृतिः ।  
ऊर्ध्वस्रोताश्च स ध्याने व्यवकीर्णोऽकनिष्ठगः ॥ ६.३७ ॥

### IAST

**so 'ntarotpanna-saṃskārāsaṃskāra-parinirvṛtiḥ |**  
**ūrdhvasrotāś ca sa dhyāne vyavakīrṇo 'kaniṣṭhagaḥ || 6.37 ||**

## Source Caution

The mounted kārikā source reads **antarotpanna**. The verse is a compressed list of types of non-returner and begins a further subdivision of the **ūrdhvasrotas**, the upward-streamer. The exact expansion of the compounds and the finer relation between mixed meditation and the Akaniṣṭha destination should be checked during the Bhāṣya pass.

## Padaccheda

```text
saḥ
antarotpanna-saṃskāra-asaṃskāra-parinirvṛtiḥ
ūrdhvasrotāḥ
ca
saḥ
dhyāne
vyavakīrṇaḥ
akaniṣṭhagaḥ
```

## Grammar

- **saḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “he,” referring to the non-returner of VAK 6.36.
- **antarotpanna-saṃskāra-asaṃskāra-parinirvṛtiḥ** — compressed nominative list naming several modes of final nirvāṇa.
- **ūrdhvasrotāḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “the upward-streamer.”
- **ca** — “and.”
- **dhyāne** — locative singular: “in meditation” or “with respect to meditation.”
- **vyavakīrṇaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “mixed” or “intermingled.”
- **akaniṣṭhagaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “one who goes to Akaniṣṭha.”

## Literal Translation

He is one who attains final nirvāṇa in the intermediate state, upon arising, with exertion, or without exertion; and he is an upward-streamer. When his meditation is mixed, he goes to Akaniṣṭha.

## Philosophical Translation

The non-returner is further classified according to the manner and stage at which final nirvāṇa is reached: in the intermediate state, after rebirth, through effort, or without effort. A further type is the upward-streamer, whose ascent through higher existences is here connected with mixed meditation and culminates in Akaniṣṭha.

## Principal Classification

The verse lists five principal forms:

```text
1. intermediate-state final nirvāṇa
2. final nirvāṇa after arising
3. final nirvāṇa with exertion
4. final nirvāṇa without exertion
5. upward-streamer
```

These are classifications within the third fruit, non-returning.

## The Upward-Streamer

```text
ūrdhvasrotas
    one whose stream proceeds upward
```

The verse begins subdividing this type according to meditative condition and destination:

```text
mixed meditation
    → Akaniṣṭha destination
```

The following verse continues the classification of the upward-streamer.

## Logical Determination

VAK 6.36 established the third fruit through destruction of all nine desire-realm grades. VAK 6.37 now differentiates persons already established in that fruit according to:

```text
time or manner of final nirvāṇa
    and
pattern of upward rebirth
```

The verse is therefore classificatory rather than transitional.
