# VAK 6.70

## Sanskrit

आदिकर्मिकनिर्वेधभागीयेषु प्रभाविताः ।
भावने दर्शने चैव सप्त वर्गा यथाक्रमम् ॥

*ādikarmika-nirvedhabhāgīyeṣu prabhāvitāḥ /
bhāvane darśane caiva sapta vargā yathākramam //*

## Padaccheda

- *ādi-karmike* — in the beginner
- *nirvedha-bhāgīyeṣu* — in the factors conducive to penetration
- *prabhāvitāḥ* — especially developed, predominant
- *bhāvane* — in cultivation
- *darśane* — in seeing
- *sapta vargāḥ* — the seven groups
- *yathā-kramam* — in corresponding sequence

## Literal Translation

The seven groups are predominant, in sequence, in the beginner, in the factors conducive to penetration, in cultivation, and in seeing.

## Philosophical Translation

The seven Bodhi-aligned groups become especially prominent in a determinate developmental order: first in the beginner’s practice, then through the four stages conducive to penetration, afterward in the Path of Cultivation, and finally in the Path of Seeing.

## The Seven Groups

1. four smṛtyupasthānas
2. four samyakprahāṇas
3. four ṛddhipādas
4. five indriyas
5. five balas
6. seven bodhyaṅgas
7. eight mārgāṅgas

## Sequential Distribution

- beginner — four establishments of recollection
- uṣmagata, heat — four correct abandonments
- mūrdhan, summits — four bases of accomplishment
- kṣānti, receptivity — five faculties
- laukikāgradharma, highest mundane dharmas — five powers
- Path of Cultivation — seven factors of Bodhi
- Path of Seeing — eight factors of the Path

The four nirvedhabhāgīya stages provide four positions, so the sequence contains seven positions in all.

## Prabhāvita

The verse does not claim that only one group exists at each stage. *Prabhāvita* means especially developed or predominant. Other Bodhi-aligned dharmas may be present, but one group gives the stage its characteristic form.

Predominance does not mean isolation.

## Developmental Structure

The thirty-seven factors are not a simultaneous checklist. They form a developmental system:

- establish the examined field — smṛtyupasthāna
- apply exertion — samyakprahāṇa
- stabilize accomplishment — ṛddhipāda
- develop governing faculties — indriya
- make them unopposable powers — bala
- cultivate the factors of Bodhi — bodhyaṅga
- enter the structure of seeing — mārgāṅga

The groups overlap substantially but differ in developmental predominance.

## Logical Determination

The same underlying dharmas recur across the Path, while their governing function changes with developmental position. The sevenfold structure is therefore both a classification of functions and a sequence of maturation.

VAK 6.67–6.69 reduced thirty-seven names to ten operative dharmas. VAK 6.70 supplies the reverse movement: ten operative dharmas form seven organized groups distributed across seven developmental positions.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.70 distributes the seven bodhipakṣya groups across seven developmental positions. The thirty-seven factors become predominant in a determinate order as the Path develops.
