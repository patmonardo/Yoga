# VAK 6.60

## Sanskrit

म्रियते न फलभ्रष्टस्तदकार्यं करोति न ।
विमुक्त्यानन्तर्यपथा नवाकोप्येऽतिसेवनात् ॥

*mriyate na phalabhraṣṭas tad-akāryaṃ karoti na /
vimukty-ānantarya-pathā navākopye 'tisevanāt //*

## Source Caution

The first half gives two protections governing an Arhat who has fallen from the fruit:

```text
he does not die while fallen from the fruit
he does not perform what is improper to that fruit
```

The second half concerns the transition into the unshakeable type through repeated cultivation. The exact relation of the nine liberation paths and nine uninterrupted paths should be checked carefully in the Bhāṣya.

## Padaccheda

- *mriyate* — dies
- *na* — not
- *phala-bhraṣṭaḥ* — one fallen from the fruit
- *tad-akāryam* — an act improper to that state or fruit
- *karoti* — performs
- *na* — not
- *vimukti-ānantarya-pathāḥ* — liberation paths and uninterrupted paths
- *nava* — nine
- *akopye* — with regard to the unshakeable condition; toward becoming unshakeable
- *atisevanāt* — through intensive or repeated cultivation

## Literal Translation

One who has fallen from the fruit does not die in that fallen condition, nor does he perform an act improper to that fruit. Through intensive cultivation, there are nine liberation paths and uninterrupted paths toward the unshakeable condition.

## Philosophical Translation

Even when a regressible Arhat falls from the fruit, the regression is strictly bounded: he does not die before recovering it, and he does not act in a manner fundamentally incompatible with the fruit previously attained. By repeatedly strengthening the Path, he can pass through nine uninterrupted and liberation paths and reach the unshakeable condition.

## Bounded Regression

The first half clarifies that `phala-parihāṇi` is not an unrestricted collapse.

```text
phala-bhraṣṭa
    fallen from the fruit
```

Yet:

```text
mriyate na
    he does not die in that condition
```

The loss must therefore be repaired before death.

This gives regression an internal limit:

```text
fruit lost
    ↓
fruit necessarily recovered
    ↓
only then can death occur
```

The person does not carry the fallen condition into the next existence.

## No Incompatible Action

```text
tad-akāryaṃ karoti na
```

The regressible Arhat does not perform an action fundamentally contrary to the fruit.

This is important because loss of fruit does not erase the whole transformation already accomplished.

```text
fruit temporarily lost

but

Path-structure not destroyed
ethical constitution not reversed
prior Ārya determination not annulled
```

The person cannot simply become indistinguishable from an ordinary person.

The earlier verse stated:

```text
na pūrvakāt
    there is no falling from the preceding fruit
```

Verse 6.60 now adds:

```text
no death while fallen
no action incompatible with the prior attainment
```

Regression is therefore tightly enclosed by the Path.

## Nine Uninterrupted and Liberation Paths

The second half turns from regression to transformation:

```text
vimukty-ānantarya-pathā nava
```

The familiar structure is:

```text
ānantarya-mārga
    uninterrupted path
    directly removes an obstruction

vimukti-mārga
    liberation path
    possesses the release produced by that removal
```

The ninefold sequence corresponds to the nine grades of what is to be overcome:

```text
mild-mild
mild-medium
mild-strong

medium-mild
medium-medium
medium-strong

strong-mild
strong-medium
strong-strong
```

Each grade requires:

```text
uninterrupted removal
    ↓
liberated possession
```

## Becoming Unshakeable

```text
akopye 'tisevanāt
```

`atisevanā` indicates intensive, repeated, or exceptional cultivation.

The unshakeable condition may therefore be produced through sustained practice:

```text
regressible disposition
    ↓
repeated strengthening
    ↓
nine removals and nine liberations
    ↓
akopyadharman
```

This confirms that the six Arhat types are not completely sealed classes.

A person may transform the mode in which Arhatship is possessed.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse establishes two movements:

### Recovery

```text
falling from fruit
    does not become final
```

The person necessarily regains the fruit before death.

### Strengthening

```text
regressible Arhat
    can cultivate toward unshakeability
```

The Path can therefore repair loss and transform disposition.

This is more than a static taxonomy. It is an architecture of controlled mobility.

## Logical Determination

The verse distinguishes regression from destruction.

```text
regression
    interruption of a higher determination

destruction
    annulment of the underlying Path
```

Only the first occurs.

The prior rational determination continues to govern what is possible:

```text
the fallen Arhat cannot die as fallen
the fallen Arhat cannot enact the contrary
the fallen Arhat can regenerate the fruit
```

The Path remains operative beneath the loss of its highest present form.

The second half makes that operative remainder productive:

```text
retained Path-determination
    ↓
repeated cultivation
    ↓
stronger form of liberation
```

## Basic Organon Light

This verse gives the Path a kind of **structural memory**.

A higher determination may cease to be presently possessed, but the subject–field relation generated by the Path has already been altered too deeply for a complete reversal.

```text
appearance of regression
    ≠
return to the former constitution
```

The karmic structure and operative capacities of the person have been transformed.

That is why certain actions are no longer possible, even when the highest fruit has temporarily been lost.

The Path does not merely add a property to an unchanged subject. It reorganizes the field of possible action:

```text
what can be known
what can be done
what can appear as a genuine possibility
```

The move to `akopya` then intensifies this reorganization until regression itself is excluded.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.60 shows that regression from Arhatship is bounded by the enduring force of the Path. The fallen Arhat neither dies before recovering the fruit nor performs an action fundamentally incompatible with it. Through intensive cultivation of nine uninterrupted and nine liberation paths, a regressible Arhat can transform into the unshakeable type.
