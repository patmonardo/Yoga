# VAK 6.47

## Sanskrit

भवाग्राधविमुक्तोर्ध्वजातवत्त्वसमन्वयः ।
अनास्रवेण वैराग्यमनागम्येन सर्वतः ॥

*bhavāgrādhavimuktordhvajātavattvasamanvayaḥ /
anāsraveṇa vairāgyam anāgāmyena sarvataḥ //*

## Source Caution

The opening compound is compressed and should be checked carefully in the Bhāṣya pass. This first-pass analysis preserves the likely doctrinal movement without pretending to settle every syntactic detail.

## Padaccheda

- *bhavāgra* — summit of becoming
- *adhaḥ* — below
- *vimukta* — liberated from
- *ūrdhva-jāta-vat* — like one born above
- *asamanvayaḥ* — non-possession
- *anāsraveṇa* — by the uncontaminated path
- *vairāgyam* — dispassion
- *anāgāmyena* — by the preliminary concentration called anāgamya
- *sarvataḥ* — with respect to all levels

## Grammar

The first half most likely states that one liberated from a lower level no longer possesses the defilements proper to that level, as with one born above it. The second half states more clearly that uncontaminated dispassion can extend to every level when based upon the anāgamya preliminary concentration.

## Literal Translation

For one liberated from what lies below the summit of becoming, there is non-possession, as with one born above. Through the uncontaminated path, dispassion by means of the anāgamya extends to all levels.

## Seed Analysis

The verse distinguishes the contemplative basis from the range of the liberating path. A practitioner need not ascend through every fundamental dhyāna as though liberation were merely a cosmological climb. The anāgamya can support an uncontaminated path whose dispassion ranges over every conditioned level, including bhavāgra.

## Logical Determination

```text
local contemplative ground
    anāgamya
        → supports
universal liberating operation
    dispassion from all levels
```

The ground from which the path operates is not identical with the scope of what the path can overcome.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.47 states that liberation from a lower domain entails non-possession of its defilements and that the uncontaminated path based in the anāgamya preliminary concentration can produce dispassion from every conditioned level. It therefore distinguishes contemplative ground from liberating range.
