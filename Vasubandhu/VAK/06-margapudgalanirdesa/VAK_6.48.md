# VAK 6.48

## Sanskrit

ध्यानात्सामन्तकाद्वान्त्यो मुक्तिमार्गस्त्रिभूजये ।
नोर्ध्वं सामन्तकादार्यैरष्टाभिः स्वोर्ध्वभूजयः ॥

*dhyānāt sāmantakād vāntyo muktimārgas tribhūjaye /
nordhvaṃ sāmantakād āryair aṣṭābhiḥ svordhvabhūjayaḥ //*

## Source Caution

The compounds *vāntyaḥ*, *tribhūjaye*, and *svordhvabhūjayaḥ* are highly compressed. The exact distribution of the eight paths and the three grounds should be checked in the Bhāṣya pass. This first analysis preserves the doctrinal movement without claiming to settle every syntactic detail.

## Padaccheda

- *dhyānāt* — from a fundamental concentration
- *sāmantakāt vā* — or from a preliminary concentration
- *antyaḥ mukti-mārgaḥ* — the final path of liberation
- *tri-bhū-jaye* — in the conquest of three grounds
- *na ūrdhvam sāmantakāt* — not above from a preliminary concentration
- *āryaiḥ* — by the noble ones
- *aṣṭābhiḥ* — by eight
- *sva-ūrdhva-bhū-jayaḥ* — conquest of one’s own and higher grounds

## Grammar

The first half most likely states that, in the conquest of three grounds, the final liberation path may arise from either a fundamental dhyāna or its preliminary concentration. The second half restricts the use of a preliminary concentration for higher conquests and introduces a distribution involving eight noble paths. The precise numerical assignment remains to be confirmed from the Bhāṣya.

## Literal Translation

In the conquest of three grounds, the final path of liberation is from a dhyāna or from a preliminary concentration. Above that, conquest of one’s own and higher grounds is not from a preliminary concentration; it is by the eight noble paths.

## Seed Analysis

The verse determines the relation between contemplative ground and liberating path. A dhyāna or preliminary concentration can serve as the basis from which the final liberation path operates, but the ground itself is not the liberating principle. Nor can every higher conquest be performed from any preliminary concentration whatsoever.

The *mukti-mārga* is the positive possession of liberation following the immediate path. Thus the verse concerns not merely the removal of a defilement but the path-moment in which separation is acquired and stabilized.

## Logical Determination

The range of a path is determined by its principled function, while the contemplative ground supplies the local basis of operation. This continues the distinction introduced in VAK 6.47 between the path’s basis and its range.

## Light Organon Interpretation

The verse is compatible with the distinction between a local operative sphere and a universally determining cognition. The path does not conquer higher grounds merely by increasing contemplative intensity. Its range depends upon cognition from principle.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.48 distributes the final liberation path across fundamental and preliminary concentrations while limiting the use of preliminary concentrations in higher conquests. It thereby distinguishes the contemplative ground that conditions a path from the liberating principle and range of the path itself.
