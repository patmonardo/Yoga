# VAK_6.77

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

> विमुच्यते जायमानमशैक्षं चित्तमावृतेः ।  
> निरुध्यमानो मार्गस्तु प्रजहाति तदावृतिम् ॥

## IAST

> **vimucyate jāyamānam aśaikṣaṃ cittam āvṛteḥ |**  
> **nirudhyamāno mārgas tu prajahāti tad-āvṛtim ||**

## Literal translation

> **The Aśaikṣa mind, as it arises, is liberated from the obstruction; but the Path, as it ceases, abandons that obstruction.**

## Analysis

The verse distinguishes two contiguous but non-identical moments:

- the **ceasing Path-moment** performs abandonment;
- the **arising Aśaikṣa mind** is liberated from the obstruction.

Thus **prahāṇa** and **vimukti** are not simple synonyms. The Path has the operative function of removing the obstruction, while the subsequent mind bears the achieved determination of release.

The temporal language is exact:

```text
nirudhyamāna mārga
    the Path while ceasing
    abandons the obstruction

jāyamāna aśaikṣa-citta
    the mind beyond training while arising
    is liberated from it
```

This gives the transition from Śaikṣa to Aśaikṣa a precise Abhidharma form. The Path does not persist as a permanent subject that later enjoys its own result. Rather, one conditioned moment performs abandonment and ceases; another arises as liberated.

## Relation to VAK_6.76

VAK_6.76 distinguished conditioned and unconditioned liberation. VAK_6.77 shows their dynamic conjunction:

```text
Path ceasing
    abandons the obstruction

Aśaikṣa mind arising
    is free from the obstruction
```

The unconditioned side is the accomplished cessation; the conditioned side is the liberated mental actuality.

## Determinate summary

VAK_6.77 establishes that the Path and liberated mind have different functions. The Path, while ceasing, abandons the obstruction; the Aśaikṣa mind, while arising, is liberated from it. The transition beyond training is therefore temporally and functionally determined.
