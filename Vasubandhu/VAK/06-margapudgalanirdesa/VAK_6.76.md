# VAK_6.76

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

> असंस्कृता क्लेशहानमधिमुक्तस्तु संस्कृता ।  
> साङ्गः सैव विमुक्ती द्वे ज्ञानं बोधिर्यथोदिता ॥

## IAST

> **asaṃskṛtā kleśahānam adhimuktas tu saṃskṛtā |**  
> **sāṅgaḥ saiva vimuktī dve jñānaṃ bodhir yathoditā ||**

## Translation

> The unconditioned liberation is the abandonment of the afflictions; conditioned liberation is resolute release. Liberation is twofold, and the conditioned form occurs together with its associated factors. Bodhi is knowledge, as previously stated.

The transmitted wording is compressed, especially around **adhimuktas tu saṃskṛtā** and **sāṅgaḥ saiva**; the exact grammar should be checked against the Bhāṣya and critical apparatus in the deeper pass.

## Twofold liberation

VAK_6.75 announced that liberation is twofold. VAK_6.76 specifies:

```text
asaṃskṛta-vimukti
    unconditioned liberation

saṃskṛta-vimukti
    conditioned liberation
```

### Unconditioned liberation

```text
asaṃskṛtā
    kleśa-hānam
```

The unconditioned liberation is the abandonment and cessation of the afflictions. It is not a momentary mental event, but the unconditioned cessation established through discriminative abandonment.

```text
knowledge of the truths
    → abandonment of kleśa
    → pratisaṃkhyānirodha
```

Thus:

```text
unconditioned liberation
    freedom as accomplished cessation
```

### Conditioned liberation

```text
adhimuktiḥ tu saṃskṛtā
```

The conditioned liberation is identified with **adhimukti**: decisive release, mastery, or liberated orientation of mind. Because it is a present mental dharma, it arises and ceases and is therefore conditioned.

```text
conditioned liberation
    freedom as operative liberated mind
```

The two sides can be provisionally distinguished as:

```text
asaṃskṛta vimukti
    the real absence of bondage

saṃskṛta vimukti
    the positive actuality of liberated functioning
```

## Sāṅga — with associated factors

The liberated mental state does not occur in isolation. It is accompanied by the appropriate associated Aśaikṣa factors.

```text
principal factor
    does not mean solitary factor
```

The exact syntactic reference of **sāṅgaḥ saiva** remains to be settled during the Bhāṣya pass.

## Bodhi is knowledge

```text
jñānaṃ bodhir yathoditā
```

Bodhi is knowledge, as already stated in VAK_6.67:

```text
kṣaya-jñāna
anutpāda-jñāna
    = Bodhi
```

The verse therefore distinguishes without separating:

```text
jñāna
    discriminates

mārga
    abandons

asaṃskṛta vimukti
    is achieved cessation

saṃskṛta vimukti
    is liberated mental actuality
```

Bodhi is not merely a state, place, feeling, or title. It has a determinate cognitive constitution.

## Doctrinal determination

```text
unconditioned liberation
    abandonment of afflictions

conditioned liberation
    liberated mental orientation

liberated actuality
    accompanied by associated factors

Bodhi
    knowledge
```

The Path culminates in a unity of knowing, abandonment, cessation, and liberated actuality.

## Determinate summary

1. Liberation is twofold.
2. Unconditioned liberation is the abandonment and cessation of the afflictions.
3. Conditioned liberation is the operative liberated orientation of mind.
4. Liberated mind occurs together with associated factors.
5. Bodhi is knowledge, specifically the two knowledges identified in VAK_6.67.
6. Liberating abandonment is specifically aligned with **pratisaṃkhyānirodha**.
