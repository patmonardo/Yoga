# VAK 6.45

## Sanskrit

तत्क्षयाप्त्या क्षयज्ञानमशैक्षोऽर्हन्नसौ तदा ।
लोकोत्तरेण वैराग्यं भवाग्रादन्यतो द्विधा ॥

*tat-kṣayāptyā kṣaya-jñānam aśaikṣo 'rhann asau tadā /
lokottareṇa vairāgyaṃ bhavāgrād anyato dvidhā //*

## Source Caution

This is a first-pass seed analysis of the kārikā alone. The precise twofold classification of supramundane dispassion is continued in the following verses and should be tested against the Bhāṣya.

## Padaccheda

- *tat-kṣaya-āptyā* — through attainment of the destruction of that
- *kṣaya-jñānam* — knowledge of exhaustion
- *aśaikṣaḥ* — beyond training; one for whom no further training remains
- *arhan* — arhat
- *asau tadā* — that one then
- *lokottareṇa* — by the supramundane
- *vairāgyam* — dispassion; separation from attachment
- *bhavāgrāt* — from the summit of existence
- *anyataḥ* — from another level
- *dvidhā* — twofold

## Grammar

The instrumental *tat-kṣayāptyā* gives the condition for the arising of *kṣaya-jñāna*: through attaining the destruction of the final grade described in VAK 6.44. The same practitioner, *asau*, is then identified as *aśaikṣa* and *arhat*. The second half opens a new classification: supramundane dispassion is twofold according to whether it concerns the summit of existence or another level.

## Literal Translation

Through attainment of its destruction, there is knowledge of exhaustion. That one is then an aśaikṣa, an arhat. By the supramundane, dispassion from the summit of existence and from another level is twofold.

## Seed Analysis

The verse completes the movement begun in VAK 6.44:

1. the diamond-like uninterrupted path destroys the final grade;
2. attainment of that destruction yields knowledge of exhaustion;
3. the practitioner is thereby an aśaikṣa and an arhat.

The verse therefore distinguishes the destruction itself from the knowledge that the destruction has been accomplished. Arhatship is not presented as mere absence of defilement but as possession of the completed result in knowledge.

The second half begins the analysis of *vairāgya*. The summit of existence is still the highest conditioned level, not liberation itself. Supramundane dispassion therefore concerns release from becoming, including its highest summit.

## Organon Contact

The local structural distinction is:

```text
kṣaya
    accomplished negation

kṣaya-jñāna
    accomplished negation known as accomplished

aśaikṣa / arhat
    the path-subject possessing that completed result
```

A broader comparative note is reserved for later reconstruction: Yoga's *dhāraṇā → dhyāna → samādhi → renewed dhāraṇā* cycle concerns distinct Powers of Judgment, while the Kośa's *śaikṣa / aśaikṣa* distinction classifies path-status as work being accomplished and work accomplished. The present kārikā pass records this contact without expanding it into a general Path synthesis.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.45 states that attainment of the final destruction yields knowledge of exhaustion, at which point the practitioner is an aśaikṣa and an arhat. It then opens the twofold analysis of supramundane dispassion from the summit of existence and from other levels.
