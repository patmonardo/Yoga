# VAK 6.05

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

वृत्तस्थः श्रुतचिन्तावान् भावनायां प्रयुज्यते ।
नामोभयार्थविषयाः श्रुतमय्यादिका धियः ॥ ६.५ ॥

### IAST

vṛttasthaḥ śrutacintāvān bhāvanāyāṃ prayujyate |
nāmobhayārthaviṣayāḥ śrutamayyādikā dhiyaḥ || 6.5 ||

## Padaccheda

- vṛtta-sthaḥ
- śruta-cintā-vān
- bhāvanāyām
- prayujyate
- nāma-ubhaya-artha-viṣayāḥ
- śruta-mayī-ādikāḥ
- dhiyaḥ

## Literal Translation

Established in conduct and possessing learning and reflection, one applies oneself to cultivation. The cognitions beginning with that formed from learning have respectively name, both name and meaning, and meaning as their objects.

## Philosophical Translation

Once firmly established in conduct and equipped with learned and reflective knowing, the practitioner enters cultivation. The cognition born of learning initially takes verbal designation as its object; reflective cognition joins designation with meaning; cultivated cognition directly takes the meaning itself as its object.

## Technical Determination

The verse distributes three object-structures across three cognitions:

```text
śrutamayī dhī
    object: nāma

cintāmayī dhī
    object: nāma + artha

bhāvanāmayī dhī
    object: artha
```

Bhāvanā is therefore not merely repetition of received language. It is the transformation by which the meaning communicated through language becomes the direct object of knowing.

The practitioner enters this sequence only when established in conduct and already possessing learning and reflection:

```text
vṛtta
    disciplined conduct

śruta
    reception of teaching

cintā
    mediation of name and meaning

bhāvanā
    direct cultivation of meaning
```

## Logical Determination

The movement is:

```text
nāma
    ↓
nāma + artha
    ↓
artha
```

Śruta is cognition bound initially to name. Cintā mediates name and meaning. Bhāvanā takes meaning itself as object. The result is not mute immediacy but meaning that has passed through linguistic and reflective mediation.

## Path-of-Knowing Note

A doctrine can remain at the level of name. Philosophical reflection reaches name and meaning. A Path requires meaning as directly actualized cognition.

```text
information
    ≠
reflection
    ≠
realization
```

The immediacy of direct knowing is produced through mediation:

```text
directness
    is not absence of formation

directness
    is completed formation
```
