# VAK 6.24

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

आबोधेः सर्वमेकत्र ध्यानान्त्ये शास्तृखड्गयोः ।  
प्राक्तेभ्यो मोक्षभागीयं क्षिप्रं मोक्षस्त्रिभिर्भवैः ॥ ६.२४ ॥

### IAST

**ābodheḥ sarvam ekatra dhyānāntye śāstṛ-khaḍgayoḥ |**  
**prāktebhyo mokṣabhāgīyaṃ kṣipraṃ mokṣas tribhir bhavaiḥ || 6.24 ||**

## Grammar

- **ā-bodheḥ** — adverbial construction with **ā**, “up to Awakening.”
- **sarvam** — accusative singular neuter: “the whole” or “all of it”; the complete sequence is understood.
- **ekatra** — indeclinable: “in one place,” here “in one sitting.”
- **dhyāna-antye** — locative singular: “in the final dhyāna,” the fourth principal dhyāna.
- **śāstṛ-khaḍgayoḥ** — genitive dual: “of the Teacher and the Rhinoceros”; the Buddha and the rhinoceros-like Pratyekabuddha.
- **prāk tebhyaḥ** — “prior to those,” referring to the preparations conducive to penetration.
- **mokṣa-bhāgīyam** — nominative or accusative singular neuter: “conducive to liberation.”
- **kṣipram** — adverb: “quickly,” “in the most rapid case.”
- **mokṣaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “liberation.”
- **tribhiḥ bhavaiḥ** — instrumental plural: “through three existences” or “within three lives.”

## Literal Translation

For the Teacher and the Rhinoceros, the entire sequence up to Awakening occurs in one sitting, in the final dhyāna. Prior to those is what is conducive to liberation. In the rapid case, liberation occurs through three existences.

## Philosophical Translation

The fully awakened Buddha and the rhinoceros-like Pratyekabuddha produce the entire decisive sequence—from the preparations for penetration through Awakening—in one uninterrupted sitting, relying upon the fourth dhyāna. Before the preparations for penetration, however, a wholesome root conducive to liberation must first be produced. In the most rapid ordinary sequence, liberation is attained across three lives.

## The Teacher and the Rhinoceros

The two are:

```text
śāstṛ
    the Teacher
    the fully awakened Buddha

khaḍga
    the rhinoceros-like solitary Buddha
    khaḍgaviṣāṇakalpa Pratyekabuddha
```

Both produce the sequence from the **nirvedhabhāgīyas** up to Awakening in a single sitting, relying upon the fourth dhyāna.

## Ekatrāsana — one sitting

The kārikā has **ekatra**, while the Bhāṣya makes the intended sense explicit:

```text
ekatra eva āsane
    in one and the same seat
    in one uninterrupted sitting
```

The sequence is not distributed across separate lives or interrupted periods:

```text
Preparations for Penetration
    ↓
Path of Seeing
    ↓
Path of Cultivation
    ↓
final Knowledges
    ↓
Awakening
```

For these two forms of awakening, the complete decisive ascent occurs continuously.

## Dhyānāntya — the final dhyāna

The supporting concentration is the fourth dhyāna:

```text
dhyāna-antya
    final dhyāna
    fourth principal dhyāna
```

Its stability and power enable it to sustain the uninterrupted progression.

The verse thus distinguishes:

```text
ordinary preparatory development:
    extended across lives

Buddha and rhinoceros-like Pratyekabuddha:
    complete decisive sequence
    in one sitting
```

## Prior to the preparations: mokṣabhāgīya

```text
prāk tebhyo mokṣabhāgīyam
```

Before the four **nirvedhabhāgīyas** there must arise a wholesome root **conducive to liberation**:

```text
mokṣa-bhāgīya
    tending toward liberation
    contributing to liberation
```

This is distinct from:

```text
nirvedha-bhāgīya
    conducive to penetration
```

The sequence is therefore:

```text
mokṣabhāgīya
    orientation toward liberation

nirvedhabhāgīya
    preparation for penetration

āryamārga
    Noble Path
```

The liberation-conducive root establishes the direction of the continuum before the technical preparations for direct penetration arise.

## Liberation in three lives

The verse states:

```text
kṣipraṃ mokṣas tribhir bhavaiḥ
```

The most rapid ordinary sequence is given as three lives:

```text
first life:
    production of the liberation-conducive root

second life:
    production of the penetration-conducive preparations

third life:
    arising of the Noble Path and liberation
```

The structure may be represented as:

```text
seed:
    mokṣabhāgīya

growth:
    nirvedhabhāgīya

fruit:
    Noble Path and liberation
```

The three-life structure expresses ordered maturation.

## Two preparatory classes

VAK 6.24 distinguishes two classes of wholesome roots.

### Liberation-conducive

```text
mokṣabhāgīya
    establishes the orientation
    toward release
```

### Penetration-conducive

```text
nirvedhabhāgīya
    Heat
    Summits
    Acceptance
    Highest Mundane Dharmas
```

The first precedes the second:

```text
orientation toward liberation
    ↓
cultivated preparation for penetration
    ↓
direct Noble knowledge
```
