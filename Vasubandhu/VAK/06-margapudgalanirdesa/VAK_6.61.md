# VAK 6.61

## Sanskrit

दृष्ट्याप्ततायामेकैकः अनास्रवाः नृषु वर्धनम् ।
अशैक्षो नव निश्रित्य भूमीः शैक्षस्तु षट् यतः ॥

*dṛṣṭyāptatāyām ekaikaḥ anāsravāḥ nṛṣu vardhanam /
aśaikṣo nava niśritya bhūmīḥ śaikṣas tu ṣaṭ yataḥ //*

## Source Caution

The transmitted text is unusually compressed, and the first pāda is not fully secure in the working source. The doctrinal movement appears to concern:

```text
development into the dṛṣṭiprāpta condition
development by uncontaminated paths
the human basis of that development
the meditation grounds available to śaikṣa and aśaikṣa
```

The exact reading and syntax of `dṛṣṭyāptatāyām ekaikaḥ` should be checked during the Bhāṣya pass.

## Padaccheda

- *dṛṣṭi-āptatāyām* — in attaining the condition of one who has attained through seeing
- *eka-ekaḥ* — one by one; each individually
- *anāsravāḥ* — uncontaminated, free from influx
- *nṛṣu* — among human beings; on a human basis
- *vardhanam* — development, increase, strengthening
- *aśaikṣaḥ* — one beyond training
- *nava* — nine
- *niśritya* — relying upon, based upon
- *bhūmīḥ* — grounds
- *śaikṣaḥ* — trainee
- *tu* — but
- *ṣaṭ* — six
- *yataḥ* — because; from which fact

## Literal Translation

In the attainment of the seeing-attained condition, development occurs one by one through uncontaminated paths and among humans. The one beyond training develops relying upon nine grounds, but the trainee upon six.

## Philosophical Translation

The transformation into the seeing-attained type proceeds through distinct uncontaminated developments and occurs on the human basis. An Arhat can undertake this strengthening through nine contemplative grounds, whereas an Ārya who remains under training can employ only six.

## Development Through Pure Means

The verse continues the theme of `indriya-vardhana`, the strengthening of spiritual faculties or disposition.

The operative means are:

```text
anāsrava
    uncontaminated
    belonging to the Path rather than to conditioned impurity
```

Thus the transition is not achieved through an ordinary worldly practice merely producing a more favorable empirical condition.

```text
change of Path-capacity
    requires
uncontaminated Path-development
```

This aligns with the distinction already emerging:

```text
worldly cultivation
    can prepare, calm, and organize

uncontaminated cultivation
    transforms the operative Path-capacity itself
```

## `Ekaikaḥ` — One by One

The development occurs:

```text
ekaikaḥ
    individually
    one determination at a time
```

The practitioner does not leap indiscriminately across the entire taxonomy.

The strengthening is determinate:

```text
one disposition
    ↓
a specifically stronger disposition
    ↓
further development where applicable
```

This is controlled succession rather than an abstract conversion into the strongest class.

## Human Basis

```text
nṛṣu vardhanam
```

The development occurs among human beings.

This recalls the special role of the human basis throughout the Path:

```text
sufficient suffering and contradiction
    to generate urgency

sufficient rational and contemplative capacity
    to transform the faculties
```

Higher domains may support attained states, but this particular active strengthening belongs to human existence.

The human field is therefore not merely inferior to the higher domains. It is the field of productive Path-development.

## Nine Grounds of the Aśaikṣa

```text
aśaikṣo nava niśritya bhūmīḥ
```

The `aśaikṣa`, here the Arhat, can rely upon nine grounds.

These are presumably:

```text
anāgamya
    preparatory concentration

dhyānāntara
    intermediate concentration

four dhyānas

three lower ārūpya attainments
```

giving:

```text
1 + 1 + 4 + 3 = 9
```

Bhavāgra is excluded as an effective basis for the uncontaminated transformation under discussion.

The Arhat therefore has the broadest available contemplative range beneath the summit of becoming.

## Six Grounds of the Śaikṣa

```text
śaikṣas tu ṣaṭ
```

The trainee relies upon six grounds:

```text
anāgamya
dhyānāntara
four dhyānas
```

giving:

```text
1 + 1 + 4 = 6
```

The śaikṣa does not use the three formless grounds for this development.

This creates a concrete asymmetry:

```text
śaikṣa
    six grounds

aśaikṣa
    nine grounds
```

The Arhat possesses a wider range of pure contemplative bases than the still-training Ārya.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse appears to establish four conditions of faculty-development:

```text
means
    uncontaminated Path

sequence
    one by one

basis of existence
    human

contemplative range
    six grounds for the trainee
    nine grounds for the Arhat
```

This is not merely a psychological increase in confidence or intelligence. It is a transformation of the practitioner’s operative Path-constitution.

## Logical Determination

The distinction between Ārya and Arhat is here expressed not simply by what each knows, but by the range of grounds from which each can operate.

```text
Ārya
    possesses Path-knowledge
    remains bounded to six operative grounds

Arhat
    completes the Path
    operates through nine grounds
```

The difference is thus architectural:

```text
same pure principle
    differently extensive field of operation
```

Development concerns the unity of:

```text
Path-principle
operative capacity
available contemplative field
```

The knowing person and the knowable field are altered together.

## Basic Organon Light

This verse helps clarify the relation between the Path as Mega Uni Principle and the differentiated capacities of the pudgala.

The universal Path is one, but it does not operate abstractly or identically through every person.

```text
one Path
    ↓
different operative capacities
    ↓
different ranges of available grounds
```

The śaikṣa and aśaikṣa do not merely hold different beliefs about the same fixed contemplative world. They possess differently articulated fields of possible Path-operation.

```text
śaikṣa field
    six pure grounds

aśaikṣa field
    nine pure grounds
```

Thus the capacity of the knowing subject and the structure of the knowable field are reciprocally determinate.

The Arhat’s broader field is not supplied by spacetime sensibility. It is generated through completed Path-capacity.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.61 presents spiritual strengthening as a sequential development through uncontaminated means on the human basis. The trainee can undertake this development using six contemplative grounds, while the Arhat can rely upon nine. The difference between śaikṣa and aśaikṣa therefore includes not only degree of attainment but the extent of the pure field through which Path-capacity can operate.
