# VAK 6.59

## Sanskrit

परिहाणिस्त्रिधा ज्ञेया प्राप्ताप्राप्तोपभोगतः ।
अन्त्या शास्तुरकोप्यस्य मध्या चान्यस्य तु त्रिधा ॥

*parihāṇis tridhā jñeyā prāptāprāptopabhogataḥ /
antyā śāstur akopyasya madhyā cānyasya tu tridhā //*

## Source Caution

This verse classifies *parihāṇi*, regression or falling away, into three distinct senses. The exact referents of *antyā*, *madhyā*, and *anya* should be checked carefully in the Bhāṣya, but the broad distinction is clear:

```text
loss of attainment
loss through non-attainment
loss of enjoyment or actualization
```

## Padaccheda

- *parihāṇiḥ* — falling away, regression, decline
- *tridhā* — threefold
- *jñeyā* — should be known
- *prāpta* — attained
- *aprāpta* — not attained
- *upabhogataḥ* — with respect to enjoyment, use, or actual experience
- *antyā* — the last
- *śāstuḥ* — of the Teacher
- *akopyasya* — of the unshakeable one
- *madhyā* — the middle
- *ca* — and
- *anyasya* — of another
- *tu* — but
- *tridhā* — in all three ways

## Literal Translation

Falling away should be known as threefold: with respect to attainment, non-attainment, and enjoyment. The last pertains to the Teacher and the unshakeable one; the middle also pertains to another, while for another it is threefold.

## Philosophical Translation

Regression is distinguished in three senses: actual loss of an attainment, failure to attain what might otherwise be reached, and loss of the active enjoyment or exercise of an attainment. The Buddha and the unshakeable Arhat are subject only to the last; other types may be subject to the middle, while still others may undergo all three.

## Threefold Regression

### 1. Regression from attainment

```text
prāpta-parihāṇi
    something already attained is lost
```

This is the strongest form. It concerns the actual possession of a Path-result or spiritual determination.

### 2. Regression through non-attainment

```text
aprāpta-parihāṇi
    a higher determination is not attained
```

Nothing already possessed is necessarily lost. Rather, advance fails to occur. This is regression relative to a possible development.

### 3. Regression from enjoyment

```text
upabhoga-parihāṇi
    the attainment remains
    but is not actively enjoyed, exercised, or made manifest
```

The underlying determination may remain secure while its present actualization is absent. This is the weakest form of regression.

## A Three-Level Structure

The three senses can be arranged as:

```text
possession
    prāpti

development
    further attainment

actualization
    upabhoga
```

Correspondingly:

```text
loss of possession
failure of development
loss of actual exercise
```

The verse is therefore much more precise than simply asking whether someone “falls back.”

## The Teacher and the Unshakeable One

```text
antyā śāstur akopyasya
```

The Buddha and the *akopyadharman* are associated only with the last kind:

```text
upabhoga-parihāṇi
```

Their attainment itself cannot be lost. Nor is their disposition vulnerable to failure of advance in the ordinary sense. What can cease is only the active manifestation or enjoyment of a particular attainment.

```text
attainment
    secure

capacity
    secure

present exercise
    may be absent
```

This is not doctrinal regression in the strong sense.

## Other Persons

The second half distributes the remaining kinds of regression among other types.

The probable structure is:

```text
Teacher and unshakeable Arhat
    only loss of enjoyment

another type
    loss through non-attainment
    and loss of enjoyment

other regressible types
    all three
```

The Bhāṣya will need to identify the exact classes covered by each phrase. For the first pass, the main point is that vulnerability varies by pudgala type.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse distinguishes:

```text
what one possesses
what one can still attain
what one presently actualizes
```

A practitioner may therefore fail in three very different ways:

```text
lose an established result

fail to advance beyond the present result

retain the result but cease to exercise it
```

These must not be conflated. The strongest Arhat and the Buddha are immune to loss of possession and failure of spiritual constitution. Only actual exercise can be interrupted.

## Logical Determination

The verse differentiates three moments of a determination:

```text
in-itself possession
    prāpti

development toward further determination
    aprāpti as failed advance

for-itself actualization
    upabhoga
```

Regression can affect one moment without destroying the others. This blocks an abstract binary of attained versus not attained.

Instead, the Kośa distinguishes:

```text
having
becoming capable of more
actively exercising
```

The spiritual determination is internally articulated.

## Basic Organon Light

This verse gives a subtle model of spiritual stability. An attainment is not exhausted by its visible exercise.

```text
not presently manifest
    does not imply
not possessed
```

Likewise, failure to advance does not necessarily imply loss of what has already been secured.

The Path therefore has at least three dimensions:

```text
acquisition
development
actualization
```

The Buddha and the unshakeable Arhat have completed the first two in such a way that only the third can vary.

The disappearance of an empirical display does not imply the disappearance of the rational ground that generates it.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.59 distinguishes three kinds of regression: loss of an attained determination, failure to attain a further determination, and loss of the active enjoyment or exercise of an attainment. The Buddha and the unshakeable Arhat are vulnerable only in the last and weakest sense. The verse therefore separates possession, development, and manifestation, showing that absence of present exercise does not entail loss of the underlying Path-determination.
