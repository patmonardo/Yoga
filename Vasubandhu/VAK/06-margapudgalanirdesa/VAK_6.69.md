# VAK 6.69

## Sanskrit

वीर्यं सम्यक्प्रहाणाख्यमृद्धिपादाः समाधयः ।
प्रधानग्रहणं सर्वे गुणाः प्रायोगिकास्तु ते ॥

*vīryaṃ samyakprahāṇākhyam ṛddhipādāḥ samādhayaḥ /
pradhānagrahaṇaṃ sarve guṇāḥ prāyogikās tu te //*

## Padaccheda

- *vīryam* — energy, exertion
- *samyak-prahāṇa-ākhyam* — called the correct abandonments
- *ṛddhi-pādāḥ* — bases of accomplishment
- *samādhayaḥ* — samādhis
- *pradhāna-grahaṇam* — designation through the principal factor
- *sarve guṇāḥ* — all associated qualities
- *prāyogikāḥ* — belonging to application or cultivation

## Literal Translation

Vīrya is called the correct abandonments, and the bases of accomplishment are samādhis. This is the taking of the principal factor; all the associated qualities belong to the application.

## Philosophical Translation

The four correct abandonments are principally vīrya, while the four bases of accomplishment are principally samādhi. These identifications name the predominant dharma in each practice-complex, without excluding the associated wholesome qualities that operate together in cultivation.

## Principal Factors

```text
four samyakprahāṇas
    principally vīrya

four ṛddhipādas
    principally samādhi
```

The four correct abandonments are differentiated forms of exertion:

```text
prevent unarisen unwholesome dharmas
abandon arisen unwholesome dharmas
produce unarisen wholesome dharmas
develop arisen wholesome dharmas
```

Their governing dharma is `vīrya`.

The four bases of accomplishment are organized through aspiration, energy, mind, and investigation, but each is an accomplishment-base through a corresponding samādhi. Their governing dharma is therefore `samādhi`.

## Pradhānagrahaṇa

`pradhānagrahaṇa` means taking or naming the predominant factor.

```text
practice-complex
    compositionally many

classification
    by one governing dharma
```

Thus:

```text
smṛtyupasthāna
    principally prajñā

samyakprahāṇa
    principally vīrya

ṛddhipāda
    principally samādhi
```

The principal factor does not exclude associated factors such as śraddhā, smṛti, prajñā, or samādhi. The verse identifies predominance, not isolation.

## Doctrinal Determination

The bodhipakṣya groups are coordinated practice-complexes. One dharma governs each form, while the associated wholesome qualities make its full operation possible.

```text
principal
    not solitary

associated
    not incidental
```

## Logical Determination

```text
many associated powers
    organized under
one predominant power
    producing
one determinate Path-function
```

This distinguishes composition from classification.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.69 identifies the four correct abandonments principally with vīrya and the four bases of accomplishment principally with samādhi. The reduction to ten dharmas names the predominant factor within each coordinated practice-complex, while retaining the participation of all associated wholesome qualities.
