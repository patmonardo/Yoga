# VAK 6.38

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

स प्लुतोऽर्धप्लुतः सर्वच्युतश्चान्यो भवाग्रगः ।  
आरूप्यगश्चतुर्धान्य इह निर्वापकोऽपरः ॥ ६.३८ ॥

### IAST

**sa pluto 'rdha-plutaḥ sarva-cyutaś cānyo bhavāgragaḥ |**  
**ārūpyagaś caturdhānya iha nirvāpako 'paraḥ || 6.38 ||**

## Source Caution

The verse continues the classification of the upward-streamer introduced in VAK 6.37 and then adds further destinations and modes of final nirvāṇa.

The compact compounds are taxonomic. Their detailed doctrinal force should be checked against the Bhāṣya in the second pass.

## Grammar

- **saḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “he,” referring to the upward-streamer.
- **plutaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “the leaper.”
- **ardha-plutaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “the half-leaper.”
- **sarva-cyutaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “the one who falls from all,” or “the all-descending one”; exact technical force deferred.
- **anyaḥ bhavāgragaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “another goes to the summit of existence.”
- **ārūpya-gaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “one who goes to the formless realm.”
- **caturdhā** — indeclinable: “fourfold.”
- **anyaḥ iha nirvāpakaḥ** — “another attains nirvāṇa here.”
- **aparaḥ** — nominative singular masculine: “another,” marking an additional type.

## Literal Translation

He is the leaper, the half-leaper, and the all-descending one; another goes to the summit of existence. The one who goes to the formless realm is fourfold. Another attains nirvāṇa here.

## Philosophical Translation

The upward-streamer is further divided according to the pattern and destination of ascent: one is called the leaper, one the half-leaper, and one the all-descending type; another proceeds to the summit of existence. The one whose course is into the formless realm is itself fourfold, while another type attains nirvāṇa in the present existence.

## Technical Classification

The verse extends the schema begun in VAK 6.37:

```text
Upward-streamer
    leaper
    half-leaper
    all-descending type
    one who reaches the summit of existence
```

It then adds:

```text
one who goes to the formless realm
    fourfold

one who attains nirvāṇa here
    another type
```

The verse is therefore concerned with destination, route, and locus of final nirvāṇa.

## Logical Determination

The local structure is classificatory:

```text
one principal type
    differentiated by route
    differentiated by destination
    differentiated by locus of nirvāṇa
```

No further interpretive synthesis is required at this first-pass stage.
