# VAK 6.68

## Sanskrit

श्रद्धा वीर्यं स्मृतिः प्रज्ञा समाधिः प्रीत्युपेक्षणे ।
प्रश्रब्धिशीलसंकल्पाः प्रज्ञा हि स्मृत्युपस्थितिः ॥

*śraddhā vīryaṃ smṛtiḥ prajñā samādhiḥ prītyupekṣaṇe /
praśrabdhiśīlasaṃkalpāḥ prajñā hi smṛtyupasthitiḥ //*

## Padaccheda

- *śraddhā* — confidence
- *vīryam* — energy, exertion
- *smṛtiḥ* — recollection
- *prajñā* — discriminative principle
- *samādhiḥ* — collectedness
- *prīti* — joy
- *upekṣaṇā* — equanimous observation
- *praśrabdhi* — calm pliancy
- *śīla* — ethical restraint
- *saṃkalpa* — resolve, conceptual orientation
- *prajñā hi smṛtyupasthitiḥ* — the establishment of recollection is, in its principal reality, prajñā

## Literal Translation

Confidence, energy, recollection, prajñā, samādhi, joy, equanimity, pliancy, ethical restraint, and resolve are the ten. For the establishments of recollection are indeed prajñā.

## Philosophical Translation

The thirty-seven Bodhi-aligned functions reduce substantially to ten operative determinations: confidence, exertion, recollection, discriminative principle, collectedness, joy, equanimity, calm pliancy, ethical discipline, and right resolve. The establishments of recollection are, in their defining operative reality, prajñā.

## Ten Underlying Dharmas

```text
1. śraddhā
2. vīrya
3. smṛti
4. prajñā
5. samādhi
6. prīti
7. upekṣā
8. praśrabdhi
9. śīla
10. saṃkalpa
```

VAK 6.67 distinguished:

```text
thirty-seven by functional name

ten by underlying dharma
```

The present verse supplies those ten operative realities.

## Smṛtyupasthāna and Prajñā

The key determination is:

```text
prajñā hi smṛtyupasthitiḥ
```

Although the practice is named through `smṛti`, its principal activity is examination and discrimination. Thus:

```text
smṛti
    retains and sustains the object

prajñā
    examines and determines the object
```

The functional name and the principal operative dharma need not be identical.

## Doctrinal Determination

The thirty-seven factors are not thirty-seven independent substances. They are differentiated Path-functions of ten operative dharmas.

```text
named Path-function
    ≠ necessarily the name
      of its principal dharma
```

`smṛtyupasthāna` is the first explicit example:

```text
functional designation
    smṛtyupasthāna

principal operative reality
    prajñā

supporting condition
    smṛti
```

## Basic Organon Light

A named Path-form may contain:

```text
principal dharma
supporting dharmas
object-domain
Path-function
```

The richer modeling belongs to the later Organon pass. For the kārikā pass, the central distinction is between functional designation and operative dharma.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.68 identifies the ten dharmas underlying the thirty-seven bodhipakṣya names and establishes that the establishments of recollection are principally prajñā. The verse therefore distinguishes the name and organization of a practice from the dharma that primarily performs its liberating function.
