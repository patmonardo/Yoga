# VAK_6.79

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

> निर्विद्यते दुःखहेतुक्षान्तिज्ञानैर्विरज्यते ।  
> सर्वैर्जहाति यैरेवं चतुष्कोटिकसम्भवः ॥

## IAST

> **nirvidyate duḥkha-hetu-kṣānti-jñānair virajyate |**  
> **sarvair jahāti yair evaṃ catuṣkoṭika-saṃbhavaḥ ||**

## Padaccheda


## Literal translation

> **One becomes disenchanted through the receptivities and knowledges concerning suffering and its ground; one becomes dispassionate through all the relevant receptivities and knowledges, by which one also abandons. Thus a fourfold alternative is possible.**

The exact distribution of **sarvaiḥ** and **yaiḥ** should be confirmed from the Bhāṣya. The kārikā nevertheless clearly distinguishes three Path-functions: **nirveda**, **virāga**, and **prahāṇa**.

## Nirveda

**Nirvidyate** expresses becoming disenchanted with or radically turned away from something through comprehension.

Here it is associated with the receptivities and knowledges directed toward:

```text
duḥkha
    suffering

hetu
    its origin or ground
```

Thus disenchantment follows from seeing both conditioned suffering and the ground that produces it. It is not mere emotional disgust, but a discriminatively grounded reversal of orientation.

## Virāga

**Virajyate** indicates freedom from adhesion or passion.

Virāga is broader than the initial disenchantment produced through seeing suffering and its origin. It belongs to the complete realization of the truth-structure, including cessation and the Path.

```text
nirveda
    turning away through comprehension

virāga
    release from attachment
    through the complete truth-structure
```

## Prahāṇa

**Jahāti** means that one actually abandons.

The relevant Path-cognitions therefore do not merely alter attitude. They perform the determinate work of removing obstruction.

```text
nirveda
    discriminative turning-away

virāga
    exhaustion of adhesion

prahāṇa
    actual abandonment of obstruction
```

These functions may coincide in a Path-event, but they are not synonymous.

## Catuṣkoṭi

The verse concludes with:

```text
catuṣkoṭika-saṃbhavaḥ
    the possibility of four alternatives
```

The precise Bhāṣya allocation must be revisited, but the kārikā prevents simple identification of the functions. A four-cornered analysis can distinguish cases such as:

```text
1. nirveda without virāga
2. virāga without nirveda
3. both nirveda and virāga
4. neither nirveda nor virāga
```

The classification depends upon the truth contemplated, the Path-position, the status of the relevant affliction, and the function performed by the cognition.

## Doctrinal determination

```text
knowledge of suffering and its ground
    especially produces nirveda

complete realization of the truths
    establishes virāga

efficacious Path-cognition
    performs prahāṇa

relations among these functions
    admit catuṣkoṭi analysis
```

Chapter 6 therefore ends not with an undifferentiated proclamation of liberation, but with exact discrimination of the functions through which liberation occurs.

## Relation to Aśaikṣa-citta

The Aśaikṣa mind is not a featureless absolute state. It emerges through a highly articulated sequence:

```text
truths discriminated
functions distinguished
obstructions abandoned
dispassion achieved
cessation established
liberated cognition arises
```

Thus:

> **The Aśaikṣa mind emerges not by abandoning discrimination, but by bringing discriminative knowledge to completion.**
