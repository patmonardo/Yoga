# VAK_6.75

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

> मार्गश्च द्रव्यतस्तु द्वे श्रद्धा शीलं च निर्मलाः ।  
> नोक्ता विमुक्तिः शैक्षाङ्गं बद्धत्वात् सा पुनर्द्विधा ॥

## IAST

> **mārgaś ca dravyatas tu dve śraddhā śīlaṃ ca nirmalāḥ |**  
> **noktā vimuktiḥ śaikṣāṅgaṃ baddhatvāt sā punar dvidhā ||**

## Padaccheda

- mārgaḥ ca
- dravyataḥ tu dve
- śraddhā
- śīlam ca nirmalam
- na uktā vimuktiḥ śaikṣa-aṅgam
- baddhatvāt
- sā punaḥ dvidhā

## Literal translation

> And the Path [is included]. But substantially they are two: confidence and pure ethical discipline. Liberation is not stated as a factor of the trainee because of being bound. It, moreover, is twofold.

## Doctrinal determination

The four verified confidences—confidence in Buddha, Dharma, Saṅgha, and śīla—reduce substantially to two operative dharmas:

- **śraddhā**, underlying the first three;
- **nirmala śīla**, pure ethical discipline.

Thus the Kośa again distinguishes classification by object and designation from enumeration by real dharma.

Liberation is not counted as a completed factor of the Śaikṣa because the trainee remains bound by residual defilements. Noble seeing is therefore not yet identical with complete liberation.

The verse then introduces a twofold division of liberation, specified in VAK_6.76.

## Logical determination

One underlying dharma may receive several names according to its object. Difference of intentional object does not necessarily imply difference of dharma-substance.

## Determinate summary

- Four verified confidences reduce to two real dharmas.
- Objectual distinction differs from substantial enumeration.
- The Śaikṣa remains bound and does not yet possess complete liberation.
- Vimukti is introduced as twofold.
