# VAK 6.30

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

यावत्पञ्चप्रकारघ्नौ द्वितीयेऽर्वाङ्नवक्षयात् ।  
कामाद्विरक्तावूर्ध्वं वा तृतीयप्रतिपन्नकौ ॥ ६.३० ॥

### IAST

**yāvat pañca-prakāra-ghnau dvitīye 'rvāṅ nava-kṣayāt |**  
**kāmād viraktāv ūrdhvaṃ vā tṛtīya-pratipannakau || 6.30 ||**

## Source Caution

VAK 6.30 continues the classification begun in VAK 6.29. The same two practitioners remain in view:

```text
śraddhā-anusārin
    Faith-Follower

dharma-anusārin
    Dharma-Follower
```

Their designation now depends upon the degree of prior abandonment of the defilements removed through cultivation.

The verse is compressed and supplies several expressions elliptically:

```text
up to five grades destroyed
    candidate for the second fruit

nine grades destroyed
    or detachment from the desire realm and above
    candidate for the third fruit
```

The kārikā does not describe abandonment accomplished by the fifteen moments of Seeing themselves. It classifies practitioners according to cultivation-abandonable defilements that had already been weakened or destroyed before entry into the Path of Seeing.

## Grammar

- **yāvat** — indeclinable: “up to,” establishing the upper limit of the first range.
- **pañca-prakāra-ghnau** — nominative dual masculine: “the two who have destroyed up to five grades.” The dual refers to the Faith-Follower and Dharma-Follower.
- **dvitīye** — locative singular used with an understood **phale**: “with respect to the second fruit.”
- **arvāk nava-kṣayāt** — “short of the destruction of nine,” indicating a range below complete destruction of the nine desire-realm grades.
- **kāmāt viraktau** — nominative dual masculine construction: “the two detached from the desire realm.”
- **ūrdhvam vā** — “or higher,” extending the prior detachment beyond the desire realm.
- **tṛtīya-pratipannakau** — nominative dual masculine: “the two candidates progressing toward the third fruit.”

## Literal Translation

When they have destroyed up to five grades, the two are candidates for the second fruit, so long as the destruction of nine has not been reached. When they are detached from the desire realm—or from higher levels—the two are candidates progressing toward the third fruit.

## Philosophical Translation

The Faith-Follower and Dharma-Follower are classified further according to the cultivation-abandonable defilements already removed before the Path of Seeing. If no more than five of the nine grades belonging to the desire realm have been destroyed, they proceed as candidates toward the second fruit, once-return. If all nine desire-realm grades have been destroyed—so that detachment from the desire realm has been attained—or if detachment extends still higher, they proceed as candidates toward the third fruit, non-return.

## Prior Abandonment Determines the Candidate

VAK 6.29 established the simplest case:

```text
no cultivation-abandonable defilements previously destroyed
    ↓
candidate toward the first fruit
```

VAK 6.30 adds two further ranges:

```text
partial prior abandonment
    ↓
candidate toward the second fruit

complete desire-realm detachment
    ↓
candidate toward the third fruit
```

The Path of Seeing is the same in all three cases. What changes is the condition of the practitioner entering it.

## The Nine Grades

The defilements of a given level are divided into nine grades:

```text
strong-strong
strong-medium
strong-weak

medium-strong
medium-medium
medium-weak

weak-strong
weak-medium
weak-weak
```

At this stage the verse presupposes that scheme without yet explaining it. VAK 6.33 will state the ninefold division explicitly.

For the desire realm:

```text
0 grades previously destroyed
    candidate toward first fruit

1–5 grades previously destroyed
    candidate toward second fruit

9 grades previously destroyed
    candidate toward third fruit
```

The intervening sixth through eighth grades are handled by the fruit-classifications developed in the following verses.

## Candidate for the Second Fruit

```text
dvitīya-phala-pratipannaka
    candidate progressing toward the second fruit
```

The second fruit is:

```text
sakṛdāgāmi-phala
    fruit of once-return
```

The verse states:

```text
yāvat pañca-prakāra-ghnau
    having destroyed up to five grades
```

Thus partial prior weakening of desire-realm defilement changes the fruit toward which the practitioner progresses during the Path of Seeing.

This is not yet establishment in once-return. The practitioner remains a **pratipannaka**, one progressing toward the fruit.

## Candidate for the Third Fruit

```text
tṛtīya-pratipannaka
    candidate progressing toward the third fruit
```

The third fruit is:

```text
anāgāmi-phala
    fruit of non-return
```

The determining condition is:

```text
kāmāt virakta
    detached from the desire realm
```

To be detached from the desire realm is to have destroyed all nine grades of its cultivation-abandonable defilements.

The expression **ūrdhvaṃ vā** indicates that the same classification applies when prior detachment extends beyond the desire realm into higher levels.

## One Path, Different Entry Conditions

The verse distinguishes path-position from prior attainment.

```text
path-position:
    fifteen moments of Seeing

prior condition:
    degree of cultivation-abandonment

resulting designation:
    candidate toward first,
    second, or third fruit
```

The Path of Seeing itself remains invariant:

```text
same truths
same fifteen moments
same uncontaminated sequence
```

But the practitioner enters that path with a differently prepared continuum.

VAK 6.30 therefore classifies the same two followers according to the degree of liberation already achieved through prior mundane detachment.

## Logical Determination

VAK 6.29 differentiated the practitioner according to faculty:

```text
gentle faculty
    → Faith-Follower

sharp faculty
    → Dharma-Follower
```

VAK 6.30 adds a second axis:

```text
faculty
    determines mode of following

prior abandonment
    determines fruit-candidacy
```

The resulting structure is:

```text
one universal Path of Seeing
    ↓
subjectively differentiated by faculty
    ↓
positionally differentiated by prior abandonment
```

The noble path does not begin from an abstractly identical practitioner. It takes effect in a continuum already bearing a determinate history of cultivation.

## Interpretive Note

The verse shows why the Buddhist Path can appear unusually classificatory. It refuses to collapse the entry into Seeing into a single undifferentiated event.

At the moment of direct realization, the practitioner is simultaneously determined by:

```text
what is being seen
how it is being followed
what has already been abandoned
which fruit is now approached
```

This does not multiply the Path itself. It makes explicit the determinate conditions under which the one Path becomes effective.

For the kārikā pass, the central point is:

```text
Seeing is universal,
but fruit-candidacy is conditioned
by the practitioner's prior degree of detachment.
```

## Determinate Summary

```text
The Faith-Follower and Dharma-Follower
remain within the same Path of Seeing.

Their fruit-candidacy depends upon
prior cultivation-abandonment:

    up to five desire-realm grades destroyed
        → candidates toward the second fruit
        → once-return

    all nine desire-realm grades destroyed
        → detachment from the desire realm
        → candidates toward the third fruit
        → non-return

    detachment extending higher
        → likewise candidates toward
          the third fruit
```

VAK 6.30 joins the universal sequence of Seeing to the practitioner's prior history of abandonment, determining which noble fruit that Seeing is now approaching.
