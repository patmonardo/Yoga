# VAK 6.73

## Sanskrit

कामधातौ भवाग्रे च बोधिमार्गाङ्गवर्जिताः ।
त्रिसत्यदर्शने शीलधर्मावेत्यप्रसादयोः ॥

*kāmadhātau bhavāgre ca bodhimārgāṅgavarjitāḥ /
trisatyadarśane śīladharmāvetyaprasādayoḥ //*

## Padaccheda

- *kāma-dhātau* — in the desire domain
- *bhava-agre* — at the summit of existence
- *bodhi-aṅga* — factor of Bodhi
- *mārga-aṅga* — factor of the Path
- *varjitāḥ* — deprived of, without
- *tri-satya-darśane* — at the seeing of the three truths
- *śīla* — ethical discipline
- *dharma* — Dharma
- *avetya-prasāda* — verified confidence, confidence grounded in direct comprehension

## Literal Translation

In the desire domain and at the summit of existence, they are devoid of the factors of Bodhi and the factors of the Path. At the seeing of the three truths, there is acquisition of verified confidence in ethical discipline and Dharma.

## Philosophical Translation

Within the desire domain and at the highest conditioned ground, the specifically pure Bodhi factors and Path factors are unavailable. When the first three truths are directly seen, verified confidence arises in Dharma and in the pure ethical discipline of the noble Path.

## Ground Limits

The verse completes the distribution of the Bodhi-aligned factors by contemplative ground.

```text
kāmadhātu
    lacks bodhyaṅgas and mārgāṅgas

bhavāgra
    lacks bodhyaṅgas and mārgāṅgas
```

This does not exclude all Bodhi-aligned cultivation from the desire domain. The restriction concerns the two groups classified in VAK 6.71 as exclusively anāsrava.

Bhavāgra is the summit of conditioned existence, not its transcendence.

```text
highest conditioned attainment
    ≠
liberating ground
```

## Avetyaprasāda

*Avetyaprasāda* is confidence grounded in direct comprehension.

```text
avetya
    having understood directly

prasāda
    clarity, confidence, serene trust
```

It is not blind belief. It is confidence produced by seeing.

## Seeing the Three Truths

The three truths are:

```text
duḥkha
samudaya
nirodha
```

Seeing them produces verified confidence in:

```text
Dharma
śīla
```

The Dharma is verified because the truths are directly known. Pure ethical discipline is verified as belonging to the noble Path that has become actual.

## Logical Determination

```text
darśana
    direct seeing

avetya

prasāda
    settled confidence
```

Thus:

```text
direct realization
    → verified comprehension
        → stable confidence
```

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.73 excludes the pure Bodhi and Path factors from the desire domain and bhavāgra, introduces *avetyaprasāda* as confidence grounded in direct seeing, and connects the realization of the first three truths with verified confidence in Dharma and pure ethical discipline.
