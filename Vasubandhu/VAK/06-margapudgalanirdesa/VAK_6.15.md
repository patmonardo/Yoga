# VAK 6.15

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

प्रज्ञा श्रुतादिमयी अन्ये संसर्गालम्बनाः क्रमः ।  
यथोत्पत्ति चतुष्कं तु विपर्यासविपक्षतः ॥ ६.१५ ॥

### IAST

**prajñā śrutādimayī anye saṃsargālambanāḥ kramaḥ |**  
**yathotpatti catuṣkaṃ tu viparyāsavipakṣataḥ || 6.15 ||**

## Literal Translation

The intrinsic Foundation of Recollection is Prajñā produced through hearing and the succeeding modes; the others consist of its associated factors and its object-support. Their sequence follows the order of arising, while the fourfold division exists because it opposes the inversions.

## Philosophical Translation

The actual scientific principle of the Foundations of Recollection is Prajñā, developed through hearing, reflection, and cultivation. The wider practice-field also includes the factors associated with Prajñā and the object-supports upon which it operates. Body, feeling, cognition, and dharmas are taught in sequence because they emerge in an ordered field and because each directly counters a corresponding fundamental inversion.

## Doctrinal Determination

VAK 6.15 gives three dimensions of smṛtyupasthāna:

1. **Intrinsic nature** — Prajñā.
2. **Association** — the conjoined cognitive factors that enable its operation.
3. **Object-support** — body, feeling, cognition, and dharmas as the seen-field.

Thus the Foundation is not merely an object attended to. It is a complete epistemic structure:

- Prajñā: the Seer-function.
- Associated factors: the instruments and operating context of seeing.
- Ālambana: the seen-field.

The intrinsic Prajñā is **śrutādimayī**:

- śrutamayī prajñā — born from hearing or study;
- cintāmayī prajñā — born from rational reflection;
- bhāvanāmayī prajñā — born from cultivation.

The educational sentence is first received, then reflected, then made operative as the form through which the field is seen.

## Lower Seer–Seen Science

This is the lower discipline that must precede any higher Buddhi–Puruṣa/Prakṛti determination. Cognition must first distinguish:

- the cognitive act,
- the factors conditioning that act,
- the object given to that act.

Without this training, ahaṃkāra can simply appropriate the language of transcendence while remaining governed by body, feeling, thought, and possession.

The four fields form an ascent in reflexivity:

- body — immediate seen;
- feeling — evaluative reaction to the seen;
- cognition — the conditioned seer becomes seen;
- dharma — the law of seer and seen.

## Four Counter-Inversions

The four Foundations function as counterforces:

- body counters taking the impure as pure;
- feeling counters taking suffering as pleasure;
- cognition counters taking the impermanent as permanent;
- dharmas counter taking the not-self as self.

The Foundation is therefore not neutral observation. It is a deliberately constructed scientific operation that replaces false predication with adequate predication.

## Science of Prajñā

The verse does not merely identify one cognition called Prajñā. It reveals the scientific form of Prajñā.

Cognition appears in two moments:

- intuition: the field is given;
- concept or Dharma-thinking: the field is reflected into lawful determination.

Prajñā is the unity of these two. It is intuition capable of determination and thinking that remains grounded in the presented field.

The prior verse's two lakṣaṇas express the same form:

- svalakṣaṇa — distinctive determination, the discriminated given;
- sāmānyalakṣaṇa — universal determination, reflective law.

Thus:

> Intuition gives the Field. Dharma thinks the Field. Prajñā is the Science of their unity.

Dharma is not merely a subjective concept. It is the reflected intelligibility through which the object becomes scientifically knowable: object, law, determination, relation, and thinkability in one.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.15 teaches that the Foundation of Recollection is the Science of Prajñā:

- Prajñā is the intrinsic discriminative principle;
- associated factors provide the operating context;
- body, feeling, cognition, and dharmas form the object-field;
- hearing, reflection, and cultivation transform sentence into vision;
- the four Foundations correct the four basic inversions.

> Before Buddhi can know Prakṛti as a whole, Prajñā must learn the elementary grammar of Seer, instrument, object, and inversion.
