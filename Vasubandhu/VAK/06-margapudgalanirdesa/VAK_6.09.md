# VAK 6.09

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

तत्रावतारोऽशुभया चानापानस्मृतेन च ।
अधिरागवितर्काणां शंकला सर्वरागिणाम् ॥ ६.९ ॥

### IAST

**tatrāvatāro ’śubhayā cānāpānasmṛtena ca |**  
**adhirāgavitarkāṇāṃ śaṃkalā sarvarāgiṇām || 6.9 ||**

## Literal Translation

Entry into cultivation is through the unattractive and through recollection of inhalation and exhalation, for those dominated respectively by excessive desire and discursive thought. The skeleton is the object for all those affected by desire.

## Philosophical Translation

The practitioner enters the cultivated Path through one of two diagnostic practices: contemplation of the unattractive counteracts dominant desire, while recollection of breathing gathers a mind dominated by discursive proliferation. For every form of desire, the skeletal framework provides the common object through which bodily attractiveness is analytically dismantled.

## Analysis

The verse marks the transition from preparation to empirical path technology. The basic diagnostic rule is:

```text
rāga-dominance
    → aśubha

vitarka-dominance
    → ānāpānasmṛti
```

Aśubha transforms the represented object. Ānāpānasmṛti stabilizes the sequence of cognition. The first corrects object-constitution; the second corrects cognitive dispersion.

The skeleton functions as a counter-object because desire depends upon synthetic completion. Surface, color, gesture, and animation are analytically removed, leaving a skeletal framework that breaks the cognition of bodily attractiveness.

The practice extends the method of VAK 6.04:

```text
pot analyzed into constituents
    → pot-cognition ceases

body analyzed into constituents
    → desire-cognition weakens
```

Thus Abhidharma analysis becomes Path when decomposition of conventional appearance is applied directly to the cognition through which affliction arises.
