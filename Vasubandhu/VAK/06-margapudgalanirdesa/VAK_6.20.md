# VAK 6.20

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

इति निर्वेधभागीयं चतुर्धा भावनामयम् ।  
अनागम्यान्तरध्यानभूमिकं द्वे त्वधोऽपि वा ॥ ६.२० ॥

### IAST

**iti nirvedhabhāgīyaṃ caturdhā bhāvanāmayam |**  
**anāgamyāntaradhyānabhūmikaṃ dve tv adho ’pi vā || 6.20 ||**

## Padaccheda

```text
iti
nirvedha-bhāgīyam
caturdhā
bhāvanā-mayam

anāgamya-antara-dhyāna-bhūmikam
dve
tu
adhaḥ
api
vā
```

## Grammar

- **iti** — concluding particle: “thus”; it closes the preceding exposition.
- **nirvedha-bhāgīyam** — nominative singular neuter: “conducive to penetration.”
- **caturdhā** — indeclinable adverb: “fourfold.”
- **bhāvanā-mayam** — nominative singular neuter: “produced through cultivation” or “consisting in cultivated Prajñā.”
- **anāgamya-antara-dhyāna-bhūmikam** — nominative singular neuter compound: “belonging to the levels of the anāgamya, the intermediate concentration, and the dhyānas.”
- **dve** — nominative or accusative dual neuter: “the two,” referring to Heat and the Summits.
- **tu** — “but,” marking an alternative qualification.
- **adhaḥ api** — “also below,” namely in the desire-domain according to the alternative position.
- **vā** — “or”; signals that this final statement is an alternative doctrinal opinion.

## Literal Translation

Thus the factors conducive to penetration are fourfold and produced through cultivation. They belong to the levels of the preliminary concentration, the intermediate concentration, and the dhyānas; according to another position, the first two may also belong to a lower level.

## Philosophical Translation

The four stages—Heat, Summits, Acceptance, and the Highest Mundane Dharmas—constitute the fourfold preparation for penetration. They arise from cultivated Prajñā and ordinarily belong to six concentration-levels: the preliminary concentration, the intermediate concentration, and the four principal dhyānas. An alternative position also allows Heat and the Summits to arise on the lower, desire-domain level.

## The Fourfold Nirvedhabhāgīya

The word **iti** closes the sequence developed in VAK 6.17–6.19:

```text
1. uṣmagata
   Heat

2. mūrdhan
   Summits

3. kṣānti
   Acceptance

4. laukikāgradharma
   Highest Mundane Dharmas
```

These are collectively called **nirvedhabhāgīya**:

```text
nirvedha
    penetration,
    decisive breakthrough

bhāgīya
    contributing toward,
    conducive to
```

They are not yet the supramundane Path of Seeing. They are the cultivated stages that prepare cognition for direct penetration of the Truths.

## Bhāvanāmayam

The four are **bhāvanāmayam**: produced through cultivation.

This places them within the threefold Prajñā sequence established earlier:

```text
śrutamayī Prajñā
    determination received through teaching

cintāmayī Prajñā
    determination examined through reflection

bhāvanāmayī Prajñā
    determination made operative through cultivation
```

The nirvedhabhāgīyas belong to the third. Their Truth-structure is no longer merely heard or rationally considered. It has become the form of a cultivated cognitive operation.

```text
Four Truths learned
    → śruta

Four Truths examined
    → cintā

Four Truths established
as cultivated object-field
    → bhāvanā
```

## Their Six Principal Levels

The compound **anāgamyāntaradhyānabhūmikam** assigns the four stages to six concentration-levels.

### 1. Anāgamya

**Anāgamya** is the preliminary or access concentration immediately before the first principal dhyāna.

```text
desire-domain cognition
    ↓
anāgamya
    ↓
first dhyāna
```

It is sufficiently concentrated to support the nirvedhabhāgīyas without yet being the first principal dhyāna.

### 2. Dhyānāntara

**Dhyānāntara**, the intermediate concentration, lies between the first and second principal dhyānas.

### 3. The Four Principal Dhyānas

```text
first dhyāna
second dhyāna
third dhyāna
fourth dhyāna
```

Thus the normal enumeration is:

```text
anāgamya
    1

dhyānāntara
    1

four dhyānas
    4

total
    6 levels
```

The four nirvedhabhāgīyas can be obtained on these six concentration-levels. They are not assigned to the formless attainments because their examination includes the suffering and origin belonging to the desire-domain.

## Dve tv adho ’pi vā

The final pāda introduces an alternative:

```text
dve
    the first two stages

adhaḥ api
    may also occur below

vā
    according to another position
```

“The two” are:

```text
Heat
Summits
```

According to the alternative position, these first two may also arise in the desire-domain, giving them seven possible levels rather than six. Acceptance and the Highest Mundane Dharmas remain restricted to the concentration-levels.

The verse therefore preserves two classifications:

```text
standard position:
    all four stages belong to six levels

alternative position:
    Heat and Summits may additionally
    belong to the desire-domain
```

## Why Heat and Summits May Be Treated Differently

Heat and Summits are the two earlier, still-retrogressible stages.

```text
Heat
    initial indication

Summits
    highest point of the unstable roots
```

Acceptance represents a stronger establishment in the Truths, and the Highest Mundane Dharmas immediately precede the Path of Seeing.

The alternative therefore permits the earlier two stages a broader lower basis while preserving the more advanced stages within cultivated concentration.

## Doctrinal Determination

VAK 6.20 concludes the definition of the four preparations according to three axes:

```text
number:
    fourfold

source:
    cultivation-produced Prajñā

valid levels:
    six concentration-levels
```

With an alternative addition:

```text
Heat and Summits:
    possibly also desire-domain
```

This distinguishes:

```text
intrinsic nature
    cultivated Prajñā

path-function
    conducive to penetration

level of occurrence
    concentration-ground
```
