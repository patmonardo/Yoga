# VAK 6.64

## Sanskrit

निरोधलाभ्युभयतोविमुक्तः प्रज्ञयेतरः ।
समापत्तीन्द्रियफलैः पूर्णः शैक्षोऽभिधीयते ॥

*nirodhalābhy ubhayatovimuktaḥ prajñayetaraḥ /
samāpattīndriyaphalaiḥ pūrṇaḥ śaikṣo ’bhidhīyate //*

## Source Caution

The first half distinguishes two kinds of liberated Arhat:

- `ubhayatovimukta` — liberated in both respects
- `prajñāvimukta` — liberated through prajñā

The distinction turns on attainment of cessation, `nirodhasamāpatti`.

The second half changes focus and defines the complete trainee, `pūrṇa śaikṣa`, through three dimensions:

- `samāpatti`
- `indriya`
- `phala`

The precise scope of “complete in fruits” should be checked during the Bhāṣya pass.

## Padaccheda

- *nirodha-lābhī* — one who has attained cessation
- *ubhayataḥ-vimuktaḥ* — liberated in both respects
- *prajñayā* — through prajñā
- *itaraḥ* — the other
- *samāpatti* — contemplative attainment
- *indriya* — faculty
- *phalaiḥ* — through fruits; with respect to fruits
- *pūrṇaḥ* — complete, fulfilled
- *śaikṣaḥ* — trainee
- *abhidhīyate* — is designated, is called

## Literal Translation

One who has attained cessation is liberated in both respects; the other is liberated through prajñā. A trainee complete in contemplative attainments, faculties, and fruits is called complete.

## Philosophical Translation

The Arhat who possesses the attainment of cessation is doubly liberated, through both discriminative knowledge and contemplative mastery. The Arhat without that attainment is liberated through prajñā. A trainee is designated complete when the relevant contemplative attainments, spiritual faculties, and Path-fruits are fully developed.

## Two Modes of Arhat Liberation

The first pāda identifies the doubly liberated Arhat:

```text
nirodhalābhī
    one possessing cessation attainment

ubhayatovimukta
    liberated in both respects
```

The two respects are broadly:

```text
prajñā
    liberation from the afflictive obstacles

samāpatti
    mastery of contemplative release
```

The person possesses both the knowledge that destroys bondage and the meditative attainment in which mental activity is suspended.

## The Prajñā-Liberated Arhat

```text
prajñayā itaraḥ
```

The other Arhat is liberated through `prajñā`.

This is the:

```text
prajñāvimukta
    one liberated through discriminative principle
```

The absence of cessation attainment does not make liberation incomplete in the essential sense.

```text
prajñāvimukta
    afflictions exhausted
    liberation complete

but

not endowed with
    the full contemplative attainment
    of cessation
```

Thus the distinction is not:

```text
liberated
versus
not fully liberated
```

It is:

```text
liberated through prajñā

versus

liberated through prajñā
and endowed with cessation attainment
```

## Why “Liberated in Both Respects”?

The doubly liberated person integrates two determinations:

```text
discriminative liberation
    prajñā

contemplative mastery
    samāpatti
```

This matches VAK 6.63, where `ubhaya` functioned as an explicit principle for generating a pudgala class.

The present verse supplies the doctrinal definition:

```text
attainment of cessation
    +
liberation through prajñā
    =
ubhayatovimukta
```

The class is therefore not merely a superior degree of insight. It joins two distinct Path-capacities.

## The Complete Trainee

The second half returns from Arhats to the `śaikṣa`:

```text
pūrṇaḥ śaikṣaḥ
    complete trainee
```

This is not an `aśaikṣa`, because training remains unfinished. Yet the trainee can be complete relative to the determinations proper to training.

Three dimensions are named.

### Completeness in Samāpatti

```text
samāpatti-pūrṇa
    complete in contemplative attainments
```

The trainee possesses the relevant meditative attainments available at that level.

### Completeness in Indriya

```text
indriya-pūrṇa
    complete in spiritual faculty
```

The operative capacity has reached its strongest trainee form.

This concerns not merely intellectual ability, but the faculty through which the Path becomes active.

### Completeness in Phala

```text
phala-pūrṇa
    complete in fruits
```

The trainee possesses the full fruit-structure available before Arhatship.

Most naturally, this points toward the non-returner who has completed the trainee fruits but has not yet attained the final fruit of Arhatship.

## Relative Completeness

The phrase `pūrṇa śaikṣa` is important because completeness is internally differentiated.

```text
complete as trainee
    ≠
complete beyond training
```

A person can fully actualize one Path-position while the Path as a whole remains unfinished.

```text
śaikṣa completeness
    all relevant trainee determinations fulfilled

aśaikṣa completion
    training itself brought to an end
```

This gives us a determinate rather than abstract notion of completeness.

## Doctrinal Structure

The verse presents two classifications.

### Arhat classification

```text
prajñāvimukta
    liberation through prajñā

ubhayatovimukta
    liberation through prajñā
    plus cessation attainment
```

### Complete trainee

```text
complete in samāpatti
complete in indriya
complete in phala
```

The first classification concerns mode of completed liberation.

The second concerns completeness within unfinished training.

## Logical Determination

The verse shows that liberation and contemplative attainment are related but non-identical.

```text
prajñā
    supplies the decisive liberating determination

samāpatti
    supplies an additional contemplative determination
```

Therefore:

```text
liberation does not logically require
every possible contemplative attainment
```

Yet the conjunction of both generates a richer personal determination:

```text
one liberation
    two modes of realized actuality
```

The complete trainee exhibits the same structural logic from the other side:

```text
training remains

but

its internal determinations
can form a completed totality
```

Completion is thus always completion of a determinate form.

## Basic Organon Light

This verse closes the present block by showing that the Path differentiates persons according to several independent but coordinated axes.

```text
knowledge
    prajñā

contemplative realization
    samāpatti

operative power
    indriya

attained result
    phala
```

No single axis exhausts the Path-person.

The `prajñāvimukta` demonstrates the primacy of Principle:

```text
prajñā alone
    is sufficient for essential liberation
```

The `ubhayatovimukta` adds a second fullness:

```text
Principle fully liberates

and

samāpatti fully actualizes
a contemplative field
```

The complete trainee is similarly not an abstractly unfinished person. The trainee can form a complete configuration within the boundary of training:

```text
complete operative capacity
complete contemplative field
complete trainee fruit

yet

final Path completion remains
```

This is a good boundary for VAK_6d because it gathers the entire pudgala architecture into four coordinated determinations:

```text
samāpatti
indriya
phala
vimukti
```

## Bhavāgra Note

The cessation attainment is not simply the highest refinement of formless existence.

```text
Bhavāgra
    summit of conditioned formless existence

nirodhasamāpatti
    cessation of perception and feeling
```

Bhavāgra is still a `bhava`, however subtle. It remains within the formless domain.

The attainment of cessation marks a different relation to that entire field. It is not the unconditioned condition itself, but it can appear as the contemplative boundary at which conditioned mental display is suspended.

That is precisely why it contributes to `ubhayatovimukti` without itself replacing prajñā as the liberating principle.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.64 distinguishes the Arhat liberated through prajñā from the Arhat liberated in both respects through prajñā and attainment of cessation. It then defines the complete trainee through fullness of contemplative attainment, spiritual faculty, and Path-fruit. The verse closes VAK_6d by showing that a Path-person is a coordinated configuration of knowledge, capacity, attainment, fruit, and liberation.
