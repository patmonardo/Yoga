# VAK 6.52

## Sanskrit

चतुष्फलव्यवस्था तु पञ्चकारणसम्भवात् ।
पूर्वत्यागोऽन्यमार्गाप्तिः क्षयसंकलनं फले ॥

*catuṣphalavyavasthā tu pañcakāraṇasaṃbhavāt /
pūrvatyāgo ’nyamārgāptiḥ kṣayasaṃkalanaṃ phale //*

## Source Caution

The verse announces five reasons for establishing four fruits, but states only three here: abandonment of the preceding, attainment of another path, and gathering cessations into the fruit. VAK 6.53 supplies the remaining two.

## Padaccheda

- *catuḥ-phala-vyavasthā* — establishment of four fruits
- *pañca-kāraṇa-saṃbhavāt* — because of five reasons
- *pūrva-tyāgaḥ* — abandonment of the preceding
- *anya-mārga-āptiḥ* — attainment of another path
- *kṣaya-saṃkalanam* — gathering of cessations
- *phale* — in the fruit

## Literal Translation

The establishment of four fruits occurs because of five reasons: abandonment of the preceding, attainment of another path, and the gathering of cessations into the fruit.

## Seed Analysis

A principal fruit is not a bare event or one more item in a series. It is a qualitative reorganization of the attained path: the previous path-status is relinquished, a distinct path is attained, and already acquired cessations are integrated into a new whole.

## Immediate–Mediate Structure

```text
immediate abandonment
    ↓
liberation-path possession
    ↓
integration of cessations
    ↓
fruit
```

The immediate path performs determinate negation; the liberation path possesses the resulting separation; the fruit integrates a plurality of such possessions into one path-position.

## Logical Determination

```text
previous whole
    relinquished

new mediation
    attained

plural results
    collected into unity
        ↓
new whole
```

The earlier fruit is preserved as accomplished content but relinquished as the present governing form. The later fruit reorganizes that content under a higher path-determination.

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.52 begins the explanation of why the Path is organized into four fruits. A fruit is established where the preceding status is relinquished, another path is attained, and acquired cessations are gathered into one whole. Fruit is therefore a qualitative reorganization of the entire attained Path, not an isolated reward.
