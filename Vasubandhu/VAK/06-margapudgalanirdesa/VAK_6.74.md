# VAK_6.74

## Sanskrit — Devanāgarī

> लाभो मार्गाभिसमये बुद्धतत्संघयोरपि ।  
> धर्मः सत्यत्रयं बोधिसत्त्वप्रत्येकबुद्धयोः ॥

## IAST

> **lābho mārgābhisamaye buddha-tat-saṃghayor api |**  
> **dharmaḥ satya-trayaṃ bodhisattva-pratyekabuddhayoḥ ||**

## Literal translation

> At the direct realization of the Path there is also acquisition of verified confidence in the Buddha and in his Saṅgha. Dharma is the three truths; the final Bodhisattva–Pratyekabuddha clause remains compressed and should be reopened in the Bhāṣya pass.

## Analysis

VAK_6.74 completes the four **avetyaprasādas** begun in VAK_6.73.

- Seeing the first three truths grounds verified confidence in **Dharma** and **śīla**.
- Direct realization of the Path grounds verified confidence in **Buddha** and **Saṅgha**.

Here **mārga-abhisamaya** means direct realization of the Path rather than merely doctrinal understanding. The Buddha is verified through the realized efficacy of the Path; the Saṅgha is the noble community constituted through that realization.

The phrase **dharmaḥ satya-trayam** identifies the first three truths as the Dharma-object within this specific four-confidence distribution. It is not a universal reduction of Dharma to only three truths.

The final compound **bodhisattva-pratyekabuddhayoḥ** indicates that the account must also consider the Bodhisattva and Pratyekabuddha trajectories, but the kārikā alone is too compressed to settle the exact application.

## Doctrinal determination

The four verified confidences are:

1. Buddha
2. Dharma
3. Saṅgha
4. śīla

They arise from direct realization rather than replacing it. Confidence is therefore knowledge-grounded clarity.
