# VAK 6.08

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

कर्मान्तेन त्रिभिर्वृत्तिस्तृष्णोत्पादविपक्षतः ।
ममाहंकारवस्त्विच्छा तत्कालात्यन्तशान्तये ॥ ६.८ ॥

### IAST

karmāntena tribhir vṛttis tṛṣṇotpāda-vipakṣataḥ |
mamāhaṃkāra-vastv-icchā tat-kālātyanta-śāntaye || 6.8 ||

## Padaccheda

| Pada | Form | Determination |
|---|---|---|
| karmāntena | instrumental singular | by the activity or occupation |
| tribhiḥ | instrumental plural | by the three |
| vṛttiḥ | nominative singular feminine | maintenance; mode of livelihood |
| tṛṣṇā-utpāda-vipakṣataḥ | adverbial compound | because they oppose the arising of thirst |
| mama-ahaṃkāra-vastu-icchā | compound | desire concerning objects of “mine” and “I-making” |
| tat-kāla-atyanta-śāntaye | dative singular compound | for temporary and final pacification |

## Literal Translation

Livelihood is maintained by the three and by the activity, because they oppose the arising of thirst. Desire concerning the objects of “mine” and “I-making” is thereby pacified temporarily and ultimately.

## Philosophical Translation

The noble practitioner’s life is sustained through the first three lineages and through the fourth, the activity of abandonment and cultivation. Together they counteract the arising of thirst: possessive appropriation is quieted for the present, while the deeper production of “I” and “mine” is brought toward final cessation.

## Analysis

VAK 6.07 introduced the noble lineages. VAK 6.08 completes them:

```text
1. contentment with robes
2. contentment with alms-food
3. contentment with lodging
4. delight in abandonment and cultivation
```

The first three regulate bodily maintenance. The fourth gives that life its purpose.

```text
minimal support
    +
maximal Path-activity
```

The first three oppose the material expansion of craving. The fourth directs the simplified life toward prahāṇa and bhāvanā.

## Technical Vocabulary

### karmānta

Karmānta refers here to the fourth noble lineage: the activity of abandonment and cultivation.

```text
prahāṇa
    abandonment

bhāvanā
    cultivation
```

Renunciation is therefore not inactivity.

### vṛtti

Vṛtti is maintenance or mode of living. Life is sustained within a limit through robes, food, and lodging, without allowing necessity to become expanding appropriation.

### tṛṣṇā

Tṛṣṇā is thirst or craving: a self-renewing lack that is not permanently satisfied through acquisition.

### mama-kāra and ahaṃkāra

```text
mama-kāra
    mine-making
    appropriation of objects

ahaṃkāra
    I-making
    appropriation of the subject-position
```

The two sustain one another:

```text
I
    establishes
mine

mine
    confirms
I
```

### tat-kāla and atyanta

```text
tat-kāla-śānti
    temporary pacification

atyanta-śānti
    final pacification
```

Practical restraint may quiet possessive craving for the present. Path-activity aims at the final cessation of appropriation itself.

## Doctrinal Determination

The simplified life is not yet the goal. It is the empirical condition for the Path.

```text
material supports
    maintain embodied life

abandonment and cultivation
    determine why that life is maintained
```

Without the fourth lineage, simplicity could become mere austerity or personal preference. The fourth gives the first three their telos.

## Logical Determination

The four lineages form a three-plus-one structure:

```text
Three:
    regulate subsistence

One:
    determines actualization
```

The central relation is:

```text
life is maintained
not for indefinite continuation
but for completion of the Path
```

## Interpretive Note

The practical economy is subordinate to cognition and liberation.

```text
contentment
    releases energy

fewness of desires
    restricts dispersion

withdrawal
    stabilizes attention

abandonment and cultivation
    accomplish the mission
```

## Determinate Summary

```text
VAK 6.08

The first three noble lineages:
    maintain bodily life
    through limited material support

The fourth:
    consists in abandonment and cultivation
    and determines the purpose of that life
```

Together they oppose:

```text
tṛṣṇā
    thirst

mama-kāra
    mine-making

ahaṃkāra
    I-making
```

The Path restricts maintenance to what is necessary and redirects the entire life-process toward final release from appropriation.
