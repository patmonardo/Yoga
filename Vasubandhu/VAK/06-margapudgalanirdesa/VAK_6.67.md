# VAK 6.67

## Sanskrit

अनुत्पादक्षयज्ञाने बोधिस्तदनुलोम्यतः ।
सप्तत्रिंशत्तु तत्पक्ष्याः नामतो द्रव्यतो दश ॥

*anutpādakṣayajñāne bodhis tad-anulomyataḥ /
saptatriṃśat tu tat-pakṣyā nāmato dravyato daśa //*

## Padaccheda

- *anutpāda-jñāne* — in knowledge of non-arising
- *kṣaya-jñāne* — in knowledge of exhaustion
- *bodhiḥ* — Bodhi, awakening
- *tad-anulomyataḥ* — because of conformity with that
- *sapta-triṃśat* — thirty-seven
- *tat-pakṣyāḥ* — belonging to its side
- *nāmataḥ* — by designation
- *dravyataḥ* — substantially
- *daśa* — ten

## Literal Translation

Bodhi consists in knowledge of non-arising and knowledge of exhaustion. Because they conform to that, thirty-seven are said to belong to its side. By name they are thirty-seven, but substantially they are ten.

## Philosophical Translation

Awakening is the twofold consummate knowledge of completed exhaustion and irreversible non-arising. The thirty-seven determinations are called Bodhi-aligned because they accord with this awakened knowing. They are thirty-seven according to functional designation, but ten according to underlying dharma-reality.

## Doctrinal Determination

```text
kṣaya-jñāna
    knowledge that the obstruction is exhausted

anutpāda-jñāna
    knowledge that it will not arise again
```

Thus Bodhi knows liberation as both accomplished and irreversible.

The term *bodhipakṣya* means not merely an external aid, but a determination belonging to the side of Bodhi and moving in conformity with it.

The seven groups total thirty-seven by name:

```text
4 smṛtyupasthānas
4 samyakprahāṇas
4 ṛddhipādas
5 indriyas
5 balas
7 bodhyaṅgas
8 mārgāṅgas
```

Yet these reduce to ten underlying dharmas, identified in VAK 6.68.

## Logical Determination

```text
Bodhi
    governing principle

thirty-seven names
    functional Path-positions

ten dharmas
    operative realities
```

The same dharma can occupy several Path-functions without becoming several substances.

## Basic Organon Light

Bodhi is not an additional item beside the thirty-seven. It is the principle that unifies them as one Path-system.

```text
Bodhi
    supplies direction

Dravya
    supplies operative capacity

Nāman
    supplies determinate function
```

## Determinate Summary

VAK 6.67 defines Bodhi as knowledge of exhaustion and non-arising. The thirty-seven bodhipakṣya determinations belong to the side of Bodhi because they conform to it. They are thirty-seven by functional designation but ten according to underlying dharma-reality.
