# VAK 6.29

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

मृदुतीक्ष्णेन्द्रियौ तेषु श्रद्धाधर्मानुसारिणौ ।  
अहीनभावनाहेयौ फलाद्युप्रतिपन्नकौ ॥ ६.२९ ॥

### IAST

**mṛdu-tīkṣṇendriyau teṣu śraddhā-dharmānusāriṇau |**  
**ahīna-bhāvanā-heyau phalādy-upapratipannakau || 6.29 ||**

## Source Caution

The kārikā classifies the two practitioners who traverse the fifteen moments of the Path of Seeing described in VAK 6.28.

The first compound distinguishes them by faculty:

```text
mṛdu-indriya
    weak or gentle faculty

tīkṣṇa-indriya
    sharp faculty
```

The second compound names them according to what they follow:

```text
śraddhā-anusārin
    one who follows through faith

dharma-anusārin
    one who follows through Dharma
```

The final half is compressed. It states that, so long as the defilements abandoned by cultivation have not been abandoned, both are candidates progressing toward the first fruit.

## Padaccheda

```text
mṛdu-tīkṣṇa-indriyau
teṣu
śraddhā-dharma-anusāriṇau

ahīna-bhāvanā-heyau
phala-ādya-upapratipannakau
```

## Grammar

- **mṛdu-tīkṣṇa-indriyau** — nominative dual masculine: “the one of gentle faculties and the one of sharp faculties.”
- **teṣu** — locative plural: “among those,” referring to the fifteen moments of the Path of Seeing.
- **śraddhā-dharma-anusāriṇau** — nominative dual masculine: “the Faith-Follower and the Dharma-Follower.”
- **ahīna-bhāvanā-heyau** — nominative dual masculine: “for whom the defilements abandonable through cultivation have not been abandoned.”
- **phala-ādya-upapratipannakau** — nominative dual masculine: “candidates progressing toward the first fruit.” The compact expression points to entry upon the fruit-sequence, beginning with stream-entry.

## Literal Translation

Among those moments, the one of gentle faculties and the one of sharp faculties are respectively the Faith-Follower and the Dharma-Follower. When the defilements abandonable through cultivation have not been abandoned, both are candidates progressing toward the first fruit.

## Philosophical Translation

While traversing the fifteen moments of the Path of Seeing, practitioners are differentiated according to the power of their faculties. The one whose faculty is gentler advances as a Faith-Follower; the one whose faculty is sharp advances as a Dharma-Follower. If neither has previously abandoned the defilements removed by cultivation, both are candidates approaching the first noble fruit, stream-entry.

## The Two Followers

VAK 6.29 introduces two path-persons:

```text
Faith-Follower
    śraddhā-anusārin

Dharma-Follower
    dharma-anusārin
```

They do not represent two different truths or two different Paths of Seeing. They represent two determinations of the practitioner who undergoes the same fifteen-moment seeing-sequence.

```text
one Path of Seeing
    traversed by
        two faculty-types
```

## Gentle and Sharp Faculties

### Gentle faculty

```text
mṛdu-indriya
    gentler cognitive power
    realization supported more prominently by confidence
```

This practitioner is called the **Faith-Follower**.

Faith here is not blind assent. It is the confidence by which the practitioner enters and sustains the uncontaminated sequence before the truth is possessed through the sharper mode of discriminative penetration.

### Sharp faculty

```text
tīkṣṇa-indriya
    acute cognitive power
    realization supported more prominently by direct discernment
```

This practitioner is called the **Dharma-Follower**.

The term **dharma** here names the determination or teaching directly followed and discerned. The practitioner advances through the path by a sharper capacity to penetrate the Dharma itself.

## Śraddhā and Dharma

The distinction is not:

```text
faith
    versus
knowledge
```

Both are already within the uncontaminated Path of Seeing. The distinction concerns the dominant faculty by which the same truth-sequence is traversed.

```text
Faith-Follower
    seeing led by confidence

Dharma-Follower
    seeing led by sharper discernment
```

Thus the difference belongs to the practitioner, not to the objective content of the four truths.

## Within the Fifteen Moments

The phrase **teṣu** refers back to the fifteen moments that VAK 6.28 identified as the Path of Seeing.

```text
moments 1–15
    Path of Seeing

within these:
    gentle-faculty practitioner
        Faith-Follower

    sharp-faculty practitioner
        Dharma-Follower
```

At the sixteenth moment, the truths have all been seen. VAK 6.31 will accordingly give these practitioners new names as persons established in fruit.

## Defilements Abandoned through Cultivation

The verse states:

```text
ahīna-bhāvanā-heya
```

The relevant defilements are not those abandoned by Seeing. Those are being abandoned in the fifteen-moment sequence itself.

The phrase concerns defilements abandoned by the Path of Cultivation:

```text
bhāvanā-heya
    abandonable through cultivation
```

If these have not previously been weakened or abandoned through mundane detachment, the practitioner proceeds toward the first fruit.

## Candidate for the First Fruit

```text
phala-ādya-upapratipannaka
    candidate progressing toward the first fruit
```

The first fruit is:

```text
srota-āpatti-phala
    fruit of stream-entry
```

During the first fifteen moments, the practitioner has not yet become established in that fruit. The practitioner is therefore a **pratipannaka**—one who has entered upon and is progressing toward it.

The structure is:

```text
Path of Seeing, moments 1–15
    candidate toward stream-entry

sixteenth moment
    established in stream-entry fruit
```

## Logical Determination

VAK 6.28 determined the objective power of Seeing:

```text
unseen truth
    → seen truth
```

VAK 6.29 now determines the subjective differentiation of the one who sees:

```text
same truth
same Path of Seeing
same fifteen moments

but:
    gentle faculty
        → Faith-Follower

    sharp faculty
        → Dharma-Follower
```

The verse therefore shifts from:

```text
structure of the path
    to
structure of the path-person
```

This is the beginning of the **pudgala** dimension of the chapter: not a substantial self, but a determinate classification of the continuum according to path, faculty, and attainment.

## Interpretive Note

The special significance of the verse is that the Path of Seeing does not erase difference among practitioners. Its object and sequence are invariant, but its subjective mode varies.

```text
objective invariance:
    four truths
    fifteen seeing-moments

subjective variation:
    faith-led faculty
    Dharma-led faculty
```

This preserves both universality and concrete differentiation. The noble path is one, yet the practitioner is determinately qualified by the manner in which that path becomes effective.

At the kārikā-pass level, the key point is simple: faculty determines the name of the practitioner, while prior abandonment determines the fruit toward which that practitioner is progressing.

## Determinate Summary

```text
Within the fifteen moments
of the Path of Seeing:

    the gentle-faculty practitioner
        is the Faith-Follower

    the sharp-faculty practitioner
        is the Dharma-Follower

Both see the same truths
through the same path-sequence.

    strength and dominant mode of faculty

    both are candidates
    progressing toward the first fruit,
    stream-entry
```

VAK 6.29 begins the explicit classification of noble path-persons by joining the universal structure of Seeing to the determinate faculties of the practitioner.
