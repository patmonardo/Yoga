# VAK_7.19 — The Progressive Possession of Knowledge in the Path of Seeing

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

```text
एकज्ञानान्वितो रागी प्रथमेऽनास्रवक्षणे ।
द्वितीये त्रिभिरूर्ध्वस्तु चतुर्ष्वेकैकवृद्धिमान् ॥ ७.१९ ॥
```

### IAST

```text
ekajñānānvito rāgī prathame 'nāsravakṣaṇe /
dvitīye tribhir ūrdhvas tu caturṣv ekaikavṛddhimān // VAK_7.19 //
```

## Source Caution

VAK 7.19 begins a new block concerning the possession and cultivation of the ten knowledges along the path.

The present verse concerns:

```text
possession of knowledge
    jñānānvaya / jñānasamanvāgama

not yet

cultivation of knowledge
    jñānabhāvanā
```

The subject is the noble practitioner who has not already attained detachment from the desire realm through a worldly path before entering the Path of Seeing.

The compressed sequence is expanded by the bhāṣya as follows:

```text
first pure moment
    one knowledge

second moment
    three knowledges

four later knowledge-moments
    one additional knowledge at each
```

The four additions occur at moments:

```text
4
6
10
14
```

They add, respectively:

```text
inferential knowledge
knowledge of origin
knowledge of extinction
knowledge of the path
```

Thus the practitioner possesses seven knowledges upon reaching the fourteenth moment.

## Grammar

The first pāda is construed:

```text
rāgī
    the practitioner not previously detached

eka-jñāna-anvitaḥ
    is endowed with one knowledge

prathame anāsrava-kṣaṇe
    in the first uncontaminated moment
```

`rāgī` literally means one who possesses attachment. In this context it is doctrinally restricted to the noble practitioner who has not already become detached from the desire realm by a worldly path.

The second half is elliptical:

```text
dvitīye
    in the second moment

tribhiḥ
    [he is endowed] with three knowledges

ūrdhvaḥ tu caturṣu
    subsequently, in four [further moments]

eka-eka-vṛddhimān
    he possesses an increase of one at each
```

The four unspecified moments are supplied by the structure of the sixteen moments of direct realization.

## Literal Translation

The one still possessing attachment is endowed with one knowledge in the first uncontaminated moment; in the second, with three. Subsequently, in four moments, he possesses an increase of one at each.

## Philosophical Translation

For the noble practitioner who enters the uncontaminated Path without prior worldly detachment, knowledge is acquired in a determinate sequence. At the first moment only conventional knowledge is possessed. At the second moment the field becomes threefold. Thereafter four decisive moments each introduce one new determination of knowledge, until seven knowledges are possessed.

## Technical Vocabulary

### jñānānvita

```text
jñāna-anvita
    accompanied by knowledge
    endowed with knowledge
    possessing knowledge
```

The verse is counting the classes of knowledge presently possessed by the practitioner.

It is not merely counting the single cognition occurring in that instant.

### rāgī

```text
rāgī
    literally:
        possessing attachment

    technically here:
        not previously detached
        from the desire realm
        through the worldly path
```

This qualification matters because the previously detached practitioner also possesses knowledge of another's mind, increasing the count by one.

### anāsravakṣaṇa

```text
anāsrava-kṣaṇa
    uncontaminated moment
    pure moment of the noble path
```

The first uncontaminated moment is:

```text
duḥkhe dharmajñānakṣānti
    receptivity to the knowledge of dharmas
    concerning suffering
```

This receptivity is not itself knowledge according to VAK 7.1, because the doubt opposed by it is only then being abandoned.

### ekaikavṛddhimat

```text
eka-eka-vṛddhimat
    increasing one by one
    possessing one additional class at each stage
```

The increase is cumulative. Previously acquired classes remain possessed while a new class is added.

## Doctrinal Determination

### First uncontaminated moment: one knowledge

At the first moment of the Path of Seeing:

```text
duḥkhe dharmajñānakṣānti
```

The practitioner possesses:

```text
1. conventional knowledge
```

The present receptivity is not counted as knowledge.

This directly applies the opening determination of Chapter 7:

```text
pure receptivities
    are seeing
    but are not knowledge
```

### Second moment: three knowledges

At the second moment:

```text
duḥkhe dharmajñāna
    knowledge of dharmas concerning suffering
```

The practitioner possesses:

```text
1. conventional knowledge
2. knowledge of dharmas
3. knowledge of suffering
```

One cognition is therefore classified under two newly acquired determinations:

```text
by opposition-domain
    knowledge of dharmas

by truth-object and aspect
    knowledge of suffering
```

The numerical rise from one to three does not mean that three separate cognitions arise simultaneously. It means that the practitioner's possessed knowledge-system now includes three classes.

### Four later increases

One knowledge is added at each of four subsequent moments:

| Moment | Present cognition | Newly possessed knowledge | Total possessed |
|---:|---|---|---:|
| 1 | receptivity to dharma-knowledge of suffering | none; receptivity is not knowledge | 1 |
| 2 | dharma-knowledge of suffering | dharma-knowledge and suffering-knowledge | 3 |
| 4 | inferential knowledge of suffering | inferential knowledge | 4 |
| 6 | dharma-knowledge of origin | knowledge of origin | 5 |
| 10 | dharma-knowledge of extinction | knowledge of extinction | 6 |
| 14 | dharma-knowledge of the path | knowledge of the path | 7 |

The resulting seven are:

```text
1. conventional knowledge
2. knowledge of dharmas
3. inferential knowledge
4. knowledge of suffering
5. knowledge of origin
6. knowledge of extinction
7. knowledge of the path
```

Knowledge of another's mind is absent because the subject specified by `rāgī` has not previously attained worldly detachment.

The two terminal knowledges are also absent:

```text
knowledge of destruction
knowledge of non-arising
```

They belong to the completion of the path in the Arhat.

## Logical Determination

VAK 7.19 presents knowledge as a progressively articulated system rather than a collection given all at once.

The sequence is:

```text
first pure receptivity
    one inherited worldly knowledge

first pure knowledge
    differentiation into
        domain-determination
        truth-determination

subsequent realization
    cumulative addition of determinate classes
```

### Receptivity and knowledge

The first moment is already uncontaminated, yet it does not add a new knowledge.

This is decisive:

```text
purity
    does not by itself imply
    completed knowledge
```

The receptivity performs the negative work of overcoming doubt. Knowledge arises only when that opposition has been completed.

Thus:

```text
receptivity
    transition / removal of obstruction

knowledge
    determinate possession after removal
```

### One cognition, multiple logical determinations

At the second moment, one occurring cognition is simultaneously:

```text
dharma-knowledge
    according to its domain of opposition

suffering-knowledge
    according to its truth-object and aspect
```

This confirms the architecture established in VAK 7.8:

```text
one cognitive event
    can be classified through
        distinct principles of division
```

The increase from one to three is therefore not a multiplication of mental events. It is an enrichment of the system of determinate predicates under which knowledge is possessed.

### Cumulative conceptual articulation

The Path of Seeing advances by determinate additions:

```text
conventional field
    ↓
knowledge of dharmas and suffering
    ↓
inferential extension
    ↓
origin
    ↓
extinction
    ↓
path
```

The progression is neither arbitrary nor merely quantitative. Each addition marks a new logical reach of the knowing system.

## Organon Light

This verse gives a strong model of the genesis of a science.

A science does not begin with its completed taxonomy already actualized. Its determinations become possessed through a sequence of acts in which each new standpoint preserves the preceding ones.

```text
possession
    is cumulative

classification
    is intensional

progression
    is genetically ordered
```

The first uncontaminated moment is particularly important for the Organon:

```text
Reason has entered the true path
    but

its first act is still receptivity
    not completed knowledge
```

This resembles the distinction between:

```text
negating the obstruction to knowing
    and

positively determining what is known
```

The second moment then displays how one act may acquire multiple systematic meanings according to different principles of classification.

The verse therefore supplies a compact architecture for knowledge accumulation:

```text
state of the knower
    practitioner not previously detached

path moment
    exact genetic position

present cognition
    what occurs now

possessed knowledge classes
    cumulative systematic achievement
```

This distinction will become essential in the following verses, where Vasubandhu turns from which knowledges are possessed to which knowledges are cultivated in present and future modes.
