# VAK_7.02 — The Primary Division of Knowledge

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

```text
सास्रवानास्रवं ज्ञानम् आद्यं संवृतिज्ञानकम् ।
अनास्रवं द्विधा धर्मज्ञानम् अन्वयमेव च ॥ ७.२ ॥
```

### IAST

```text
sāsravānāsravaṃ jñānam ādyaṃ saṃvṛtijñānakam /
anāsravaṃ dvidhā dharmajñānam anvayameva ca // VAK_7.02 //
```

## Source Caution

The source transmits:

```text
ādyaṃ saṃvṛtijñāpakam
```

The working text normalizes this to:

```text
ādyaṃ saṃvṛtijñānakam
```

The context requires the name of a kind of knowledge: the first member, contaminated knowledge, is called conventional knowledge. The transmitted `jñāpakam` is therefore treated as a textual or input error in this first-pass file.

## Grammar

The opening compound is resolved distributively:

```text
sāsravaṃ jñānam
anāsravaṃ jñānam
```

Knowledge is first divided into two:

```text
contaminated knowledge
uncontaminated knowledge
```

The adjective `ādyam`, “the first,” refers to contaminated knowledge. That first member is named `saṃvṛtijñāna`, conventional knowledge.

The second member, uncontaminated knowledge, is then divided again by `dvidhā`:

```text
dharmajñāna
anvayajñāna
```

The verse therefore contains a division within a division. It does not initially place three knowledges side by side as coordinate members.

## Literal Translation

Knowledge is contaminated and uncontaminated.
The first is conventional knowledge.
The uncontaminated is twofold:
dharma-knowledge and subsequent knowledge.

## Philosophical Translation

Knowledge is first distinguished according to whether it remains joined to the contaminants or is free from them. The contaminated form is conventional knowledge. Uncontaminated knowledge then divides into dharma-knowledge and subsequent knowledge.

## Technical Vocabulary

### sāsrava

```text
sa-āsrava
    = with contaminants
    = contaminated
```

The determination does not mean merely false or defective knowledge. It means knowledge belonging to a continuum and field in which the contaminants remain operative.

### anāsrava

```text
an-āsrava
    = without contaminants
    = uncontaminated
```

Uncontaminated knowledge belongs to the liberating path. Its distinction from contaminated knowledge is not merely a difference of object but a difference in the cognitive condition and path-function of the knowing itself.

### saṃvṛtijñāna

```text
saṃvṛtijñāna
    = conventional knowledge
```

This is the broad worldly form of knowledge. It operates within conventional designation and can take the ordinary field as its object.

The present verse only names it. Its range will be specified in the next verse.

### dharmajñāna

```text
dharmajñāna
    = dharma-knowledge
```

For this chapter, `dharmajñāna` is a technical path-knowledge. Its object-range is not inferred from the English word “dharma” alone. The following verse will specify its relation to the truths in the desire domain.

### anvayajñāna

```text
anvayajñāna
    = subsequent knowledge
    = homologous knowledge
```

`Anvaya` carries the sense of sequence, correspondence, or following according to a common order. Here it names the uncontaminated knowledge that follows and extends the determination established by dharma-knowledge to the higher domains.

The exact canonical English rendering remains provisional until the chapter has displayed its full operation.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse establishes a nested classificatory structure.

### First division

```text
jñāna
    ├── sāsrava
    └── anāsrava
```

Knowledge is divided according to contamination.

### Naming the first member

```text
sāsrava-jñāna
    = saṃvṛtijñāna
```

Contaminated knowledge is here gathered under conventional knowledge.

### Division of the second member

```text
anāsrava-jñāna
    ├── dharmajñāna
    └── anvayajñāna
```

Uncontaminated knowledge is not left as an undifferentiated unity. It possesses two distinct path-forms.

### Resulting field

```text
jñāna
    ├── saṃvṛtijñāna
    └── anāsrava-jñāna
            ├── dharmajñāna
            └── anvayajñāna
```

The three named knowledges are therefore not three members of one flat enumeration. Conventional knowledge stands opposite uncontaminated knowledge, while dharma-knowledge and subsequent knowledge are the internal division of the uncontaminated side.

## Logical Determination

The primary opposition is:

```text
sāsrava
    /
anāsrava
```

This is a complete division with respect to contamination:

```text
with contaminants
without contaminants
```

The second division occurs only within the uncontaminated member:

```text
anāsrava
    → dharmajñāna / anvayajñāna
```

The logical architecture is therefore:

```text
Universal field
    jñāna

First difference
    contaminated / uncontaminated

Internal difference of the uncontaminated
    dharma / anvaya
```

The verse begins constructing the formal system of the ten knowledges through successive determinate divisions rather than through an arbitrary list.

## Organon Light

VAK 7.01 distinguished knowledge from view and acceptance according to cognitive function. VAK 7.02 now divides knowledge according to its relation to contamination.

```text
VAK 7.01
    What counts as knowledge?

VAK 7.02
    What are the primary kinds of knowledge?
```

A light Organon reading is:

```text
jñāna
    divides itself first as

world-involved knowledge
    /
path-pure knowledge
```

But path-pure knowledge is itself not simple:

```text
immediate dharma-determination
    /
its subsequent extension
```

The verse therefore gives the opening architecture of JÑĀNA as determinate division:

```text
JÑĀNA
    ├── conventional determination
    └── uncontaminated determination
            ├── dharma-knowledge
            └── subsequent knowledge
```

This remains a first-pass structural determination. The object-domains of these knowledges belong to VAK 7.03.

## Interpretive Note

The verse should not yet be read as opposing ordinary knowledge to an abstract transcendence. The uncontaminated side immediately divides into two operative knowledges. Liberation requires differentiated cognition, not merely withdrawal from the conventional field.

This is important for the chapter's method:

```text
purity
    does not abolish determination

purity
    makes a more exact division possible
```
