# VAK_7.21 — Conventional Knowledge at the End of Direct Comprehension

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

```text
अतोऽभिसमयान्त्याख्यं तदनुत्पत्तिधर्मकम् ।
स्वाधोभूमि निरोधेऽन्त्यं स्वसत्याकारं यत्निकम् ॥ ७.२१ ॥
```

### IAST

```text
ato 'bhisamayāntyākhyaṃ tad anutpattidharmakam /
svādhobhūmi nirodhe 'ntyaṃ svasatyākāraṃ yātnikam // VAK_7.21 //
```

## Source Caution

The verse explains the special conventional knowledge cultivated at the three inferential knowledges named in VAK 7.20.

Its determinations are:

```text
name
    knowledge at the end of direct comprehension

mode of being
    not destined actually to arise

sphere
    its own level or a lower level

foundation of mindfulness
    at suffering and origin: all four
    at cessation: only the last, dharmas

object and aspect
    the truth just comprehended
    under that truth's own aspects

mode of acquisition
    through effort
    not merely through detachment
```

The bhāṣya also preserves a major dispute.

```text
Vaibhāṣika
    this is a future, unarisen conventional knowledge
    genuinely acquired but never produced

Former Masters / Sautrāntika
    what is acquired is a transformed support
    capable of producing a superior conventional knowledge
    after emergence from the noble contemplation
```

The first kārikā pass should register this dispute without resolving it prematurely.

## Grammar

The verse continues the subject from VAK 7.20:

```text
tat
    that conventional knowledge
```

The first half is construed:

```text
ataḥ
    therefore
    because it is cultivated
    at the completion of comprehension
    of each of the three truths

abhisamayāntya-ākhyam
    it is called
    knowledge at the end of direct comprehension

tat anutpatti-dharmakam
    that knowledge has non-arising as its character
    it is not destined actually to arise
```

The second half gathers several determinations:

```text
sva-adho-bhūmi
    it belongs to its own meditative level
    or to a lower level

nirodhe antyam
    at cessation it is the last
    foundation of mindfulness
    namely mindfulness of dharmas

sva-satya-ākāram
    it bears the aspects
    of the truth just comprehended

yātnikam
    it is acquired through effort
```

`antyam` requires expansion from the bhāṣya. It refers to the last of the four foundations of mindfulness, not simply to the final temporal moment.

## Literal Translation

Therefore that is called the knowledge at the end of direct comprehension. It has non-arising as its character. It belongs to its own or a lower level; at cessation it is the last foundation. It has the aspects of its own truth and proceeds from effort.

## Philosophical Translation

At the completion of direct comprehension of suffering, origin, or cessation, the practitioner acquires a corresponding conventional cognition. It is defined by the truth just realized and by that truth's own aspects. Yet, according to the Vaibhāṣika account, this acquired cognition remains an unarisen future determination: it is possessed as a capacity without ever becoming an actual cognitive event. Its acquisition depends upon the exertion of the Path of Seeing.

## Technical Vocabulary

### abhisamaya

```text
abhisamaya
    direct comprehension
    immediate realization
    determinate penetration of a truth
```

It is not merely discursive understanding. It is the ordered realization carried by the receptivities and knowledges of the Path of Seeing.

### abhisamayāntika

```text
abhisamaya
    direct comprehension

anta
    end
    completion

abhisamayāntika-jñāna
    conventional knowledge acquired
    at the completion of comprehending a truth
```

This knowledge is acquired at the inferential knowledge of:

```text
suffering
origin
cessation
```

It is not acquired at inferential knowledge of the Path.

### Why the Path is excluded

The bhāṣya gives several related controls:

```text
suffering
    can be fully known

origin
    can be fully abandoned

cessation
    can be fully realized

path
    cannot be practiced in its entirety
    at one terminal moment
```

The Path is not merely an object whose comprehension is completed. It is the continuing activity of realization.

The diversity of the paths of:

```text
Śrāvaka
Pratyekabuddha
Buddha
```

also prevents a single conventional terminal cognition from exhausting the Path as such.

### anutpattidharmaka

```text
anutpatti-dharmaka
    characterized by non-arising
    not destined to be produced
```

This does not mean unconditioned cessation. It describes the peculiar status of this acquired conventional knowledge in the Vaibhāṣika system.

```text
it is future
it is acquired
it remains unarisen
it never becomes present
```

This generates the controversy recorded by Vasubandhu.

### bhūmi

```text
bhūmi
    level
    meditative plane
    supporting domain of cognition
```

When the Path of Seeing is realized on a certain level, the conventional knowledge acquired belongs to:

```text
that same level
or
one of the lower levels
```

It does not exceed the supporting level of the realization.

### antya

Here `antya`, “last,” refers to the fourth foundation of mindfulness:

```text
kāya
vedanā
citta
dharma
```

At the completion of suffering and origin, the corresponding conventional knowledge may have the nature of all four foundations.

At the completion of cessation:

```text
only dharma-smṛtyupasthāna
    mindfulness of dharmas
```

is applicable, because cessation is not body, feeling, or mind.

### svasatyākāra

```text
sva-satya-ākāra
    possessing the aspects
    of its own truth
```

The knowledge cultivated at the end of a truth's comprehension:

```text
has that truth as object
and
takes on that truth's determinate aspects
```

Thus it is not an indeterminate conventional awareness.

### yātnika

```text
yātnika
    proceeding from effort
    acquired through exertion
    application-produced
```

The knowledge is acquired through the force of the Path of Seeing. It is not obtained merely as an automatic consequence of detachment.

## Doctrinal Determination

### Why it is called the end of direct comprehension

At the inferential knowledge of each of the first three truths, that truth has been comprehended across both:

```text
desire-domain
and
higher domains
```

The conventional knowledge is therefore acquired when the truth has reached the completion of its immediate comprehension.

```text
knowledge of dharmas
    comprehends the truth in the desire-domain

inferential knowledge
    comprehends the corresponding truth in the higher domains

completion of both
    end of direct comprehension
```

### The paradox of acquisition without arising

The Vaibhāṣika position is structurally striking:

```text
before the Path of Seeing
    the knowledge is not acquired

after the relevant inferential knowledge
    the knowledge is acquired

but

at no time
    does it actually arise
```

It cannot arise during the concentrated noble realization because conventional cognition is incompatible with that immediate pure contemplation.

It cannot arise immediately outside that contemplation because the ordinary mind is too coarse to instantiate precisely that cognition.

Thus:

```text
acquisition
    ≠ present production
```

The system distinguishes possession of a future dharma from the dharma's actual occurrence.

### Vasubandhu's critical pressure

The bhāṣya does not let the Vaibhāṣika formula pass without challenge.

The objection is effectively:

```text
How can a knowledge be cultivated
if it is never produced?
```

To say that it is acquired simply because it is acquired does not explain the mechanism.

The Former Masters supply a genetic interpretation:

```text
noble realization transforms the support
    ↓
that transformed support becomes capable
of producing a superior conventional cognition
    ↓
after emergence from contemplation
that conventional knowledge can arise
```

On this account, acquisition concerns the transformed `āśraya`, the cognitive support, rather than possession of a permanently unarisen entity.

The bhāṣya's analogy is:

```text
acquiring ore containing gold
    is called
acquiring gold
```

What is immediately obtained is the ground capable of yielding the later cognition.

### Truth-specific determination

The conventional cognition is not merely caused by realization in general.

It is internally specified:

```text
end of suffering-comprehension
    suffering as object
    aspects of suffering

end of origin-comprehension
    origin as object
    aspects of origin

end of cessation-comprehension
    cessation as object
    aspects of cessation
```

Thus pure realization reorganizes conventional cognition according to the truth realized.

## Logical Determination

VAK 7.21 exposes a distinction between three moments of knowledge:

```text
actual realization
    the pure cognition now occurring

acquisition of capacity
    the continuum becomes capable
    of a new conventional cognition

later articulation
    the realization becomes expressible
    in conventional form
```

The doctrinal dispute concerns whether the middle term should be treated as:

```text
Vaibhāṣika
    possession of a real future dharma
    which remains forever unarisen

Sautrāntika
    transformation of the present support
    capable of generating a later cognition
```

The second model is more explicitly genetic:

```text
realization
    does not deposit a hidden cognitive object

realization
    transforms the power of the knower
```

### The completion of an object and the incompletion of the Path

The exclusion of Path knowledge is logically important.

```text
suffering
origin
cessation
    can be comprehended as completed truth-fields

path
    is the act of comprehension itself
    continuing beyond any one cognition
```

The Path cannot be enclosed in the same way as its objects.

This suggests:

```text
truth as object
    admits a terminal comprehension

path as method
    remains internally productive
```

The method does not become exhausted by being known.

## Organon Light

The light Organon determination is:

```text
True realization
    transforms the capacity
    for subsequent ordinary knowledge.
```

VAK 7.20 stated that present cognition establishes future cognitive power. VAK 7.21 now asks what kind of futurity this is.

Two models appear:

```text
stored-future model
    a determinate future cognition is acquired
    even though it never becomes actual

transformed-capacity model
    the support is altered
    so that a superior conventional cognition
    can later be generated
```

For Organon purposes, the second is especially suggestive:

```text
knowledge
    is not merely retained content

knowledge
    is a transformation
    of the system's generative capacity
```

The verse also supplies an important limit:

```text
an object can reach
an end of comprehension

but

the Path cannot be exhausted
as a finished object
because it is the continuing method
of actualization
```

No larger decision between Vaibhāṣika and Sautrāntika ontology is imposed in this first pass.
