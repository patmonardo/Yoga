# VAK_7.09 — The Counteractive Range of Dharma- and Anvaya-Knowledge

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

```text
धर्मज्ञानं निरोधे यन्मार्गे वा भावनापथे ।
त्रिधातुप्रतिपक्षस्तत् कामधातोस्तु नान्वयम् ॥ ७.९ ॥
```

### IAST

```text
dharmajñānaṃ nirodhe yan mārge vā bhāvanāpathe /
tridhātupratipakṣas tat kāmadhātos tu nānvayam // VAK_7.09 //
```

## Source Caution

The verse assigns different counteractive ranges to `dharmajñāna` and `anvayajñāna` within the Path of Cultivation. Its syntax is compressed, and the precise doctrinal distribution should later be checked against the bhāṣya and major commentaries. The first-pass structure is clear:

```text
dharmajñāna concerning cessation or path
    in the Path of Cultivation
    can oppose all three realms

anvayajñāna
    does not oppose the desire realm
```

The asymmetry is therefore the central determination.

## Grammar

The relative-correlative construction is:

```text
yat ... tat
    that which ... that
```

Thus:

```text
that dharma-knowledge
    which concerns cessation or path
    in the Path of Cultivation

is a counteragent to the three realms
```

The closing phrase is elliptical:

```text
kāmadhātos tu na anvayam
    but anvaya-knowledge is not [a counteragent] to the desire realm
```

The omitted predicate is supplied from `tridhātupratipakṣaḥ`.

## Literal Translation

That dharma-knowledge which, in the Path of Cultivation, concerns cessation or the path is a counteragent to the three realms; but anvaya-knowledge is not [a counteragent] to the desire realm.

## Philosophical Translation

Within the Path of Cultivation, dharma-knowledge of cessation or path has a universal counteractive reach across all three realms. Anvaya-knowledge, by contrast, does not function against the desire realm.

## Technical Vocabulary

### bhāvanāpatha

```text
bhāvanāpatha
    = Path of Cultivation
    = the post-seeing development of what has been directly discerned
```

The verse no longer concerns the initial sixteen moments of the Path of Seeing alone. It specifies how knowledge functions once the truths are cultivated repeatedly and made effective against remaining defilements.

### pratipakṣa

```text
pratipakṣa
    = counteragent
    = that which stands against and removes an opposed condition
```

VAK 7.08 named `pratipakṣa` as one ground of division. VAK 7.09 now begins applying that ground concretely.

The question is not only:

```text
what does this knowledge know?
```

It is also:

```text
what can this knowledge counteract?
```

### tridhātu

```text
tridhātu
    = the three realms
    = desire, form, and formlessness
```

Here dharma-knowledge of cessation or path, when active in cultivation, is not confined to the desire realm merely because dharma-knowledge was first defined through truths belonging to that realm.

### anvayajñāna

```text
anvayajñāna
    = knowledge according to or following the principle
    = knowledge of the corresponding truths in the higher realms
```

Its object-domain gives it a different counteractive range. Since it belongs to the higher-realm articulation, it does not function as the counteragent to desire-realm defilements.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse applies one of the grounding laws announced in VAK 7.08:

```text
species of knowledge
    differ by
counteractive function
```

The division is not justified merely by object-domain. Two knowledges are further distinguished by the range of opposition they exercise.

| Knowledge | Truth-basis here | Counteractive range |
|---|---|---|
| dharmajñāna | cessation or path | all three realms |
| anvayajñāna | corresponding higher-realm truths | not the desire realm |

The important point is that origin does not mechanically imprison function. Dharma-knowledge originates through the desire-realm truth structure, yet in cultivation its cessation- and path-forms can function universally across the three realms.

By contrast, anvaya-knowledge retains a restricted counteractive relation determined by its higher-realm scope.

## Logical Determination

VAK 7.09 is the first concrete execution of the grounding program stated in VAK 7.08.

```text
Ground announced in 7.08:
    pratipakṣa

Ground applied in 7.09:
    differential counteractive range
```

This yields a genuine scientific division:

```text
dharmajñāna
    universally counteractive in cultivation

anvayajñāna
    not counteractive with respect to kāmadhātu
```

The distinction is therefore functional, not nominal.

```text
same general genus:
    knowledge of the truths

specific difference:
    range of effective opposition
```

This also shows why all divisions must be grounded. Without `pratipakṣa`, the difference between dharma- and anvaya-knowledge would remain a mere difference of labels or object-fields. With it, their distinct places in the Path become necessary.

## Organon Light

The verse confirms the rule that emerged in VAK 7.08:

> Every scientific division must state the ground that makes its members differ.

Here the ground is operative power.

```text
knowledge is not defined only by representation
knowledge is defined also by efficacy
```

That is crucial for the Organon Dataset. A knowledge-node cannot be represented adequately by:

```text
name
object
translation
```

It must also include:

```text
counteragent
range of application
path-stage
realm-scope
```

The result is a more exact account of Reason:

```text
Pure Reason
    determines the principle

Determinate Reason
    specifies where the principle is effective

Science
    grounds the division through that specified efficacy
```

The striking point is the expansion of dharma-knowledge. Although first introduced through the truths of the desire realm, its cultivated cessation- and path-forms become counteragents throughout the three realms.

Provisional Organon formula:

> A determination is not confined to the empirical field in which it was first discovered once its principle has acquired universal counteractive force.

No stronger Kant–Fichte–Hegel identification is imposed in this first pass.
