Chapter 7 of the *Abhidharmakośa* is the **Jñānanirdeśa**, the Determination of Knowledge.

The chapter begins by distinguishing knowledge, view, and acceptance; develops the ten knowledges and their objects, aspects, supports, acquisition, and cultivation; and then passes into the distinctive powers and qualities of a Buddha and advanced practitioners.

Its broad movement is:

```text
Knowledge classified
    ↓
Knowledge cultivated
    ↓
Knowledge perfected as power
```

The proposed division into three working blocks is provisional. Each block is a structural unit for the first kārikā pass and may later be subdivided or reorganized during the bhāṣya and Organon passes.

# Provisional Chapter Division

## VAK_7a — Classification and Domain of Knowledge

```text
VAkK_7.1–VAkK_7.18
```

### Principal movement

```text
jñāna / dṛṣṭi / kṣānti
    ↓
ten knowledges
    ↓
aspects, domains, supports, and objects
```

### Topic outline

#### VAkK_7.1 — Knowledge, View, and Acceptance

Initial discrimination of:

```text
kṣānti
jñāna
kṣayajñāna
anutpādajñāna
dṛṣṭi
```

This verse establishes the primary relation between knowledge and view and determines which forms of discriminative cognition count as one, the other, or both.

#### VAkK_7.2–7.4 — The Ten Knowledges

Division of knowledge into:

```text
sāsrava / anāsrava
saṃvṛtijñāna
dharmajñāna
anvayajñāna
four knowledges of the truths
kṣayajñāna
anutpādajñāna
```

The ten knowledges form the principal classificatory architecture of the chapter.

#### VAkK_7.5–7.7 — Knowledge of Another Mind and the Final Knowledges

Determination of:

```text
paracittajñāna
its range and limits
kṣayajñāna
anutpādajñāna
```

These verses distinguish direct knowledge of another continuum from the knowledges that determine exhaustion and non-arising.

#### VAkK_7.8–7.9 — Why the Knowledges Are Ten

The tenfold division is grounded through differences of:

```text
svabhāva
pratipakṣa
ākāra
ākāragocara
prayoga
kṛtakṛtyatva
hetu-upacaya
```

The relation of dharma-knowledge and anvaya-knowledge to the three domains is also specified.

#### VAkK_7.10–7.13 — Aspects of Knowledge

Analysis of the sixteen aspects of the truths and their relation to:

```text
dharmajñāna
anvayajñāna
saṃvṛtijñāna
paracittajñāna
prajñā
ālambana
```

This section is especially important for the relation between the form of knowing and the object-support that is formed or determined by it.

#### VAkK_7.14–7.18 — Levels, Supports, Foundations, and Objects

Classification of the knowledges according to:

```text
bhūmi
āśraya
smṛtyupasthāna
gocara
saṃprayoga
known dharmas
```

VAkK_7.18 closes the first block by distinguishing the object-range of conventional knowledge and the knowledge of selflessness.

### Working determination

VAK_7a establishes the **formal field of knowledge**. Its central problem is not merely what is known, but how forms of knowledge differ through their nature, opposition, aspect, support, level, and object-domain.

## VAK_7b — Cultivation and the Distinctive Qualities of a Buddha

```text
VAkK_7.19–VAkK_7.34
```

### Principal movement

```text
acquisition of knowledge
    ↓
cultivation of knowledge
    ↓
Buddha's powers, fearlessnesses, and great compassion
```

### Topic outline

#### VAkK_7.19–7.21 — Acquisition in the Path of Seeing

Determination of how many knowledges are possessed at successive moments of the path of seeing, together with the cultivation of:

```text
kṣānti
jñāna
saṃvṛtijñāna
```

The section distinguishes what arises presently from what is cultivated as future capacity.

#### VAkK_7.22–7.26 — Cultivation in the Path of Cultivation

Enumeration of the knowledges cultivated in:

```text
muktimārga
ānantaryamārga
successive conquests of the levels
abhijñā acquisition
akopya attainment
kṣayajñāna
```

These verses provide a detailed map of the increase and acquisition of knowledge across the path.

#### VAkK_7.27 — Four Senses of Cultivation

Cultivation is differentiated as:

```text
pratilambha-bhāvanā
niṣevā-bhāvanā
pratipakṣa-bhāvanā
vinirdhāvana-bhāvanā
```

This verse is a major conceptual hinge between possession, exercise, opposition, and purification.

#### VAkK_7.28–7.31 — The Ten Powers

Introduction of the eighteen distinctive qualities of a Buddha, beginning with the ten powers:

```text
sthānāsthānajñānabala
karmaphalajñānabala
knowledge of meditations, faculties, dispositions, elements, and paths
knowledge of previous lives
knowledge of death and rebirth
knowledge of the exhaustion of the contaminants
```

The section also examines the unrestricted range of these powers and the bodily power of a Buddha.

#### VAkK_7.32 — Four Fearlessnesses

The four forms of fearlessness are correlated with the Buddha's powers and analyzed through knowledge, recollection, and principle.

#### VAkK_7.33 — Great Compassion

The great compassion is distinguished through:

```text
saṃbhāra
ākāra
gocara
equality
intensity
```

The text determines how great compassion differs from ordinary compassion.

#### VAkK_7.34 — Equality and Difference among Buddhas

All Buddhas are equal through:

```text
saṃbhāra
dharmakāya
benefit to the world
```

They may differ in lifespan, birth, and bodily measure.

### Working determination

VAK_7b studies **knowledge becoming an acquired and cultivated power of the path**. The block culminates in the distinctively Buddhist form of universal capacity: powers and fearlessness are inseparable from great compassion and benefit to the world.

## VAK_7c — Shared Qualities, Superknowledges, and Manifest Power

```text
VAkK_7.35–VAkK_7.56
```

### Principal movement

```text
shared and uncommon qualities
    ↓
araṇā / praṇidhijñāna / pratisaṃvid
    ↓
abhijñā / vidyā / prātihārya
    ↓
ṛddhi / nirmāṇa / divine faculties
```

### Topic outline

#### VAkK_7.35 — Shared and Unshared Qualities

Distinction between qualities shared with disciples or ordinary persons and qualities of advanced practitioners, including:

```text
araṇā
praṇidhijñāna
pratisaṃvid
```

#### VAkK_7.36 — Araṇā

Determination of the knowledge and meditative basis of freedom from conflict, together with the beings and afflictions that form its object-range.

#### VAkK_7.37–7.40 — Resolute Knowledge and the Four Analytical Knowledges

Analysis of:

```text
praṇidhijñāna
pratisaṃvid of dharma
pratisaṃvid of meaning
pratisaṃvid of expression
pratisaṃvid of inspired discourse
```

These verses distinguish knowledge of names, meanings, speech, expression, and mastery of communicative paths.

#### VAkK_7.41 — The Limit-Development of the Six Qualities

The six qualities reach their culminating limit through progressive cultivation. In a Buddha they arise without preparatory effort; in others they are produced through practice.

#### VAkK_7.42–7.46 — The Six Superknowledges and Three Sciences

The six superknowledges are:

```text
ṛddhi
hearing
knowledge of another mind
recollection of former lives
knowledge of death and rebirth
knowledge of the exhaustion of the contaminants
```

Their relation to conventional and non-contaminated knowledge, meditative levels, foundations of recollection, and the three sciences is determined.

#### VAkK_7.47 — The Three Miracles

The three miracles are:

```text
miracle of supernormal power
miracle of mind-reading
miracle of instruction
```

Instruction is supreme because it is reliable and joins beings to the desired beneficial result.

#### VAkK_7.48–7.53 — Supernormal Power and Magical Creation

Analysis of:

```text
ṛddhi
samādhi
gamana
nirmāṇa
mind-speed movement
movement through resolve
constructed forms
construction-consciousness
speech of constructed beings
adhiṣṭhāna
sources of supernormal power
```

The five sources of supernormal power are identified as cultivation, birth, mantra, medicine, and karma.

#### VAkK_7.54–7.55 — Divine Hearing and Divine Vision

Determination of the refined material faculties underlying divine hearing and vision, their meditative basis, range, limitations, and differences among an Arhat, a Pratyekabuddha, and a Buddha.

#### VAkK_7.56 — Knowledge of Another Mind

The chapter closes by distinguishing forms of knowledge of another mind, including inferential, acquired, and birth-produced forms, and specifying which beings possess them.

### Working determination

VAK_7c studies **knowledge made manifest as exceptional capacity**. The decisive distinction is between mere display and power ordered toward instruction. The miracle of teaching is placed above spectacle because the culmination of knowledge is not domination of appearances but the communication of a liberating path.

# Chapter-Level Logical Orientation

The Jñānanirdeśa may provisionally be read as a threefold development:

```text
VAK_7a
Knowledge as classified determination

VAK_7b
Knowledge as cultivated power

VAK_7c
Knowledge as communicable and manifest efficacy
```

Or more compactly:

```text
Form of Knowledge
    ↓
Development of Knowledge
    ↓
Actuality of Knowledge
```

This remains an Organon hypothesis and must not replace Vasubandhu's own sequence. It will be tested verse by verse.

A central chapter question is:

> How does discriminative knowing become path-capacity without collapsing knowledge into mere power?

The chapter's answer appears to culminate in the superiority of instruction: knowledge becomes complete not when it merely sees or performs, but when it can guide another continuum toward liberation.
