# VAK_7.08 — Why the Knowledges Are Ten

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

```text
स्वभावप्रतिपक्षाभ्यामाकाराकारगोचरात् ।
प्रयोगकृतकृत्यत्वहेतूपचयतो दश ॥ ७.८ ॥
```

### IAST

```text
svabhāva-pratipakṣābhyām ākārākāra-gocarāt /
prayoga-kṛta-kṛtyatva-hetūpacayato daśa // VAK_7.08 //
```

## Source Caution

The kārikā is exceptionally compressed. Its sequence names the grounds by which the previously introduced knowledges are distinguished as ten. The exact assignment of every ground to every member belongs to the following verses and the bhāṣya; the present first pass therefore preserves the structure without forcing a complete one-to-one allocation prematurely.

A useful syntactic reading of `ākārākāragocarāt` is:

```text
ākārāt
    from aspect

ākāra-gocarāt
    from aspect together with object-domain
```

This accounts for the apparently repeated `ākāra`.

## Padaccheda

| Pada | Analysis | Working sense |
|---|---|---|
| svabhāva-pratipakṣābhyām | instrumental dual | by intrinsic nature and by opposition/counteraction |
| ākārāt | ablative singular | by aspect or mode of apprehension |
| ākāra-gocarāt | ablative singular compound | by aspect together with its object-domain |
| prayoga | ablative relation within compound | by preparatory application or exercise |
| kṛta-kṛtyatva | ablative relation within compound | by the state of having accomplished what had to be done |
| hetu-upacayataḥ | ablative expression | by the extension or accumulation of causes |
| daśa | nominative plural neuter | ten |

## Grammar

The governing predicate is compressed:

```text
the knowledges are ten
```

The intervening ablatives state the bases of differentiation:

```text
because of intrinsic nature
because of opposition
because of aspect
because of aspect together with object
because of preparatory exercise
because of accomplished task
because of extension of cause
```

`svabhāva-pratipakṣābhyām` is an instrumental dual. The remaining grounds are expressed through ablative formations, indicating the reasons or standpoints from which the tenfold distinction is established.

`kṛta-kṛtyatva` literally indicates the condition of one for whom what had to be done has been done.

## Literal Translation

By intrinsic nature and opposition, by aspect, by aspect together with object-domain, by preparatory exercise, by the state of having accomplished what was to be done, and by the extension of cause, the knowledges are ten.

## Philosophical Translation

Knowledge is articulated into ten forms because cognition differs not merely by what it knows, but by what kind of cognition it is, what it counteracts, how it apprehends, which object-field it apprehends in that manner, how it is prepared, whether its task has been completed, and how fully its causal basis has been developed.

## Technical Vocabulary

### svabhāva

```text
svabhāva
    = intrinsic nature
    = what the cognition is in itself
```

This is the most immediate ground of distinction. Two cognitions may bear on related material while differing in their own cognitive constitution.

### pratipakṣa

```text
pratipakṣa
    = counteragent
    = opposition
    = that against which a cognition functions
```

A knowledge is also determined by the obstruction, defilement, or domain it is capable of counteracting.

### ākāra

```text
ākāra
    = aspect
    = determinate mode under which an object is known
```

The object alone does not exhaust cognition. The same object can be apprehended under different truth-aspects or determinate forms.

### gocara

```text
gocara
    = object-domain
    = sphere within which cognition ranges
```

The compound `ākāra-gocara` prevents us from isolating object from mode. A cognition is distinguished by the articulated unity of:

```text
how it knows
    /
what it knows
```

### prayoga

```text
prayoga
    = preparatory application
    = deliberate exercise leading to cognition
```

Some knowledges arise through cultivated preparation; the Buddha’s unprepared knowledge in VAK 7.06 already showed that preparation is itself a differentiating condition.

### kṛta-kṛtyatva

```text
kṛta-kṛtyatva
    = having done what was to be done
    = accomplished path-task
```

This directly recalls VAK 7.07. Knowledge of exhaustion and knowledge of non-arising are distinguished by cognition’s certainty regarding completed work and its non-repetition.

### hetūpacaya

```text
hetūpacaya
    = extension, increase, or accumulation of cause
    = development of the causal basis of cognition
```

This indicates that a knowledge can be differentiated by the degree or range to which its causes have been developed.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse does not simply repeat a list of ten names. It explains why ten are scientifically warranted.

```text
TEN KNOWLEDGES
    are not ten by convention alone

TEN KNOWLEDGES
    arise from distinct grounds of determination
```

The grounds are:

| Ground | Question answered |
|---|---|
| intrinsic nature | What kind of cognition is it? |
| opposition | What does it counteract? |
| aspect | Under what determination does it know? |
| aspect-object domain | How and what does it know together? |
| preparation | Through what exercise is it produced? |
| accomplished task | Has its path-work been completed? |
| causal extension | How fully has its causal basis been developed? |

The next verses begin distributing these grounds more exactly across the ten knowledges. VAK 7.08 therefore functions as a methodological heading for the analysis that follows.

## Logical Determination

This verse is a striking statement of scientific division.

The tenfold result is not presented as a flat empirical enumeration. It is generated from determinate criteria.

```text
number of members
    follows from
principles of distinction
```

The classification is therefore reflective but disciplined by reasons:

```text
Reflection
    gathers the known forms

Reason
    states the grounds by which they differ

Science
    validates the tenfold articulation
```

The verse also shows that identity of object does not imply identity of knowledge. Cognition is determined by an Essential Relation among:

```text
cognitive nature
counteragent
aspect
object-domain
preparation
achievement
cause
```

Thus knowledge is not a bare relation between an isolated subject and an isolated object. It is a structured function within the Path.

## Organon Light

VAK 7.08 may be the clearest methodological verse yet in Chapter 7.

It says, in effect:

> A science of knowledge must justify its species by the grounds that generate their differences.

This fits the Organon method exactly. Reflection supplies the articulated forms of the Buddha’s mastered cognition, but those forms become Science only when restored to their determining principles.

```text
Kośa reflection
    preserves ten forms of knowledge

Jñānanirdeśa
    states the grounds of their distinction

Organon reconstruction
    restores each form to its proper principle
```

The verse therefore stands at the transition from the initial enumeration of the ten knowledges to their determinate scientific specification.

A provisional Organon schema is:

```text
KNOWLEDGE
    nature
    function of opposition
    mode of apprehension
    object-field
    genesis through practice
    status of completion
    causal development
```

This is already more than an “NLP dataset” description of terms. It is the architecture required by the Organon Dataset: each knowledge must be represented not merely by a label, but by the complete structure that makes it the knowledge it is.

No final Kant–Fichte–Hegel mapping is imposed in this first pass.
