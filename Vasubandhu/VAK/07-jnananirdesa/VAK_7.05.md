# VAK_7.05 — Knowledge of Another's Mind and Its Limits

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

```text
दुःखहेत्वन्वयज्ञाने चतुर्भ्यः परचित्तवित् ।
भूम्यक्षपुद्गलोत्क्रान्तं नष्टाजातं न वेत्ति तत् ॥ ७.५ ॥
```

## Grammar

The first half is compressed. The four knowledges are understood as:

```text
duḥkha-dharmajñāna
hetu-dharmajñāna
duḥkha-anvayajñāna
hetu-anvayajñāna
```

From these four, `paracittajñāna`, knowledge of another's mind, is derived.

The second half states the limits of that knowledge. The pronoun `tat` refers back to knowledge of another's mind.

The compound:

```text
bhūmi-akṣa-pudgala-utkrāntam
```

means a mind surpassing the knower with respect to:

```text
level
faculty
person
```

The pair:

```text
naṣṭa / ajāta
```

indicates what has passed away and what has not yet arisen: past and future mind.

## Literal Translation

Knowledge of another's mind arises from four knowledges of suffering and origin, as dharma-knowledge and subsequent knowledge.

That knowledge does not know a mind surpassing it in level, faculty, or person, nor a mind that is past or future.

## Philosophical Translation

Knowledge of another's mind is not an unrestricted intuition. It is generated from four determinate path-knowledges: the dharma- and subsequent knowledges of suffering and its origin. Its range remains bounded. It does not comprehend a cognition superior to it in plane, faculty, or spiritual person, and it does not know a cognition that has already ceased or has not yet arisen.

## Technical Vocabulary

### paracittajñāna

```text
para-citta-jñāna
    = knowledge of another's mind
```

The verse abbreviates the expression as `paracittavit`, literally one who knows another's mind or the cognition that knows another's mind.

This is not omniscience. It is a determinate knowledge with a specified basis and a delimited object-range.

### duḥkhajñāna and hetujñāna

```text
duḥkha-jñāna
    = knowledge of suffering

hetu-jñāna
    = knowledge of its origin or cause
```

The cognition of another's mind is associated only with the truth-pair:

```text
suffering
origin
```

It is not here derived from the knowledges of cessation and path.

### bhūmi

```text
bhūmi
    = level
    = plane
    = stage of attainment
```

Knowledge of another's mind does not cross upward beyond its own attained level.

### akṣa

```text
akṣa
    = faculty
```

Here the term indicates a difference in the strength or grade of cognitive faculties.

### pudgala

```text
pudgala
    = person
    = type or rank of practitioner
```

The verse recognizes distinctions among persons as limits on what one practitioner can know of another.

### naṣṭa / ajāta

```text
naṣṭa
    = vanished
    = past

ajāta
    = not yet arisen
    = future
```

Knowledge of another's mind is directed to presently occurring mind, not to a past cognition that has ceased or a future cognition that has not arisen.

## Doctrinal Determination

The verse gives both the derivation and limitation of `paracittajñāna`.

### Derivation from four knowledges

```text
paracittajñāna
    derives from

    dharmajñāna of suffering
    dharmajñāna of origin
    anvayajñāna of suffering
    anvayajñāna of origin
```

Thus knowledge of another's mind is not introduced as an isolated supernatural faculty. It is structurally grounded in previously established knowledges.

### Limitation by superiority

It cannot know a mind that exceeds it with respect to:

```text
bhūmi
akṣa
pudgala
```

The higher cannot simply be made an object by the lower.

### Limitation by time

It does not know:

```text
past mind
future mind
```

Its direct field is present cognition.

## Logical Determination

The verse introduces a new kind of knowledge by a twofold determination:

```text
ground of derivation
    /
limit of application
```

Its positive determination is:

```text
from four knowledges
```

Its negative determination is:

```text
not beyond level, faculty, or person
not past
not future
```

The knowledge is therefore defined neither by an empirical description alone nor by an unlimited claim. It is positioned within the system through exact relations to prior knowledges and exact exclusions from what it cannot know.

The temporal field remains dichotomously bounded around the present:

```text
past
    excluded

future
    excluded

present
    operative field
```

The verse does not yet construct an explicit threefold doctrine of time. It defines the present field by excluding the two non-present extremes.

## Organon Light

VAK 7.02 divided knowledge into contaminated and uncontaminated forms. VAK 7.03 assigned their object-domains. VAK 7.04 articulated the uncontaminated knowledges through the truths. VAK 7.05 now shows how a further knowledge is generated from that articulated structure.

```text
truth-knowledge
    ↓
knowledge of another's mind
```

But the derivative knowledge remains finite:

```text
it knows another
but not without measure
```

This is important for the chapter's method. A higher cognitive power is admitted, but immediately determined through its ground and limits.

The light structural result is:

```text
paracittajñāna
    = relational knowledge
    = knowledge grounded in four prior determinations
    = knowledge restricted to an equal or lower present cognition
```

No broader identification with Yoga siddhi is required in the first-pass analysis.

## Interpretive Note

This verse will eventually invite comparison with Yoga's knowledge of another's mind. That comparison should be deferred until the Kośa has completed its own account.

For now the important result is:

> Knowledge of another's mind is systematic, derivative, and bounded—not an indeterminate clairvoyance.
