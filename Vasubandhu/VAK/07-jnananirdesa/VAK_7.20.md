# VAK_7.20 — Cultivation in the Path of Seeing

## Sanskrit

### Devanāgarī

```text
यथोत्पन्नानि भाव्यन्ते क्षान्तिज्ञानानि दर्शने ।
अनागतानि तत्रैव सांवृतं चान्वयत्रये ॥ ७.२० ॥
```

### IAST

```text
yathotpannāni bhāvyante kṣāntijñānāni darśane /
anāgatāni tatraiva sāṃvṛtaṃ cānvayatraye // VAK_7.20 //
```

## Source Caution

The verse moves from the **possession** of knowledges in VAK 7.19 to their **cultivation** (*bhāvanā*).

The first half is compressed and must be read under the control of the bhāṣya:

```text
in the Path of Seeing
    the future counterparts
    of the receptivities and knowledges
    are cultivated
    according to the kinds already produced
```

The second half adds a special acquisition:

```text
at the three inferential knowledges
    conventional knowledge is also cultivated
```

The three moments are the inferential knowledges concerning:

```text
suffering
origin
cessation
```

The inferential knowledge of the Path is excluded. VAK 7.21 explains the special conventional knowledge acquired here.

## Grammar

The first half is construed:

```text
kṣānti-jñānāni
    receptivities and knowledges

darśane
    in the Path of Seeing

yathā utpannāni
    according as they have arisen

anāgatāni bhāvyante
    their future forms are cultivated
```

The verse does not say that all possible receptivities and knowledges are cultivated indiscriminately from the beginning. Cultivation follows the determinate order of production.

The second half is construed:

```text
anvaya-traye
    at the three inferential knowledges

sāṃvṛtam ca
    conventional knowledge also

tatra eva bhāvyate
    is cultivated there in the Path of Seeing
```

The verb *bhāvyate* is supplied from the first half.

## Literal Translation

In the Path of Seeing, future receptivities and knowledges are cultivated according as they have arisen; and precisely there, at the three inferential knowledges, conventional knowledge is also cultivated.

## Philosophical Translation

The Path of Seeing does not merely produce isolated pure cognitions. Each determinate receptivity and knowledge, once actualized, establishes the capacity for corresponding future cognition. Moreover, at the inferential knowledges of suffering, origin, and cessation, a corresponding conventional knowledge is acquired as part of the path's developing cognitive system.

## Technical Vocabulary

### bhāvanā

```text
bhāvanā
    cultivation
    development
    making-become
    establishment as an available capacity
```

Here cultivation must not be reduced to repetition or meditation in a loose psychological sense.

It marks a technical distinction:

```text
utpāda
    present arising of a cognition

bhāvanā
    establishment of a corresponding capacity
    extending beyond that single arising
```

Thus the path does not merely contain events. It generates a structured power of future cognition.

### kṣānti

```text
kṣānti
    receptivity
    acceptance
    pure cognitive bearing toward a truth
    which removes doubt but is not yet jñāna
```

VAK 7.1 established that the pure receptivities are not classified as knowledge. They are nevertheless cultivated together with the knowledges because they belong to the genetic sequence of Seeing.

### jñāna

```text
jñāna
    knowledge
    positive determination following receptivity
```

In the Path of Seeing:

```text
receptivity
    removes the obstruction

knowledge
    positively determines the truth
```

### anāgata

```text
anāgata
    future
    not yet arisen
```

The future dharmas discussed here are not vague possibilities. They are future members of a determinate cognitive type whose causal capacity is established through present cultivation.

### anvaya-jñāna

```text
anvaya-jñāna
    inferential knowledge
    knowledge concerning the corresponding truths
    in the higher domains
```

The three relevant moments concern:

```text
higher-domain suffering
higher-domain origin
higher-domain cessation
```

### sāṃvṛta-jñāna

```text
sāṃvṛta-jñāna
    conventional knowledge
```

This is striking because conventional knowledge is cultivated within the pure Path of Seeing. It does not thereby become identical with the pure cognition. Rather, the pure realization establishes a corresponding conventional capacity that can later articulate what was realized.

## Doctrinal Determination

### From possession to cultivation

VAK 7.19 asked:

```text
How many knowledges does the practitioner possess
at each decisive moment?
```

VAK 7.20 now asks:

```text
Which cognitive capacities are cultivated
as those moments arise?
```

The distinction is:

```text
possession
    what belongs to the cognitive continuum
    at a given moment

cultivation
    what is generated or strengthened
    as an available present or future capacity
```

### Cultivation follows arising

```text
yathā utpannāni
    according as they have arisen
```

This establishes a strict genetic order.

The Path of Seeing is not a simultaneous revelation of an undifferentiated whole. Its determinations arise in sequence, and cultivation follows that sequence.

```text
new determinate cognition arises
    ↓
its corresponding future capacity is established
    ↓
the cognitive continuum becomes more articulated
```

### The three conventional knowledges

At the inferential knowledges of:

```text
suffering
origin
cessation
```

one also cultivates a conventional knowledge corresponding to that truth.

The special case points toward a relation between:

```text
pure direct realization
    and
subsequent conventional articulation
```

The conventional cognition is not the source of the realization. It is made possible by the realization.

### Why not at inferential knowledge of the Path?

The verse says *anvaya-traye*, “at three inferential knowledges,” rather than all four.

VAK 7.21 will explain that the acquired conventional knowledge is called the “end of direct comprehension” and is especially determined by the preceding realization of the three truths. The Path itself is the ongoing realizing activity and does not yield the same conventional terminal cognition at its inferential moment.

This point remains provisional until VAK 7.21 is analyzed.

## Logical Determination

VAK 7.20 introduces a decisive difference between:

```text
actual cognition
    and
cognitive capacity
```

A cognition arises as a determinate event. But its cultivation means that the event modifies the structured possibility of future knowing.

```text
present determination
    produces
future availability of the determination
```

The path therefore has memory in a strong, nonmerely representational sense.

It accumulates determinate powers.

### Knowledge becomes a system

The Path of Seeing is not a sequence in which each cognition disappears without remainder.

```text
moment 1
    produces its determination
    and establishes a future capacity

moment 2
    adds a new determination
    without erasing the first

later moments
    progressively articulate
    the complete cognitive system
```

VAK 7.19 gave the quantitative increase in possessed knowledges. VAK 7.20 supplies its genetic ground: each arising is cultivated as a reproducible or available power.

### Pure realization and conventional expression

The cultivation of conventional knowledge at three inferential moments gives the relation:

```text
pure cognition
    realizes the truth

conventional cognition
    becomes capable of expressing
    classifying
    teaching
    or recollecting that realization
```

The conventional does not stand outside the Path. It is reorganized by the Path.

This is important for a Dharma Science:

> realization that could never become conventionally articulable would not yet form a communicable science.

The verse therefore points toward the conversion of pure insight into available scientific knowledge.

## Organon Light

The lightest Organon determination is:

```text
Knowing is not exhausted
by the occurrence of a true cognition.

A true cognition changes
what the system is thereafter capable of knowing.
```

This is a genetic account of knowledge.

```text
actuality
    the cognition now arising

possibility
    the future cognition made available

cultivation
    the mediation converting actuality
    into determinate future possibility
```

The three conventional knowledges add another movement:

```text
pure realization
    ↓
conventional availability
    ↓
possible recollection, exposition, and transmission
```

Thus Dharma Science requires both:

```text
truth becoming actual
and
truth becoming available as knowledge
```

No larger identification with Absolute Knowing is imposed in this first kārikā pass.
