# VAK_3a.01 — VAkK_3.1

## 1. Sanskrit

```sanskrit
नरकप्रेततिर्यञ्चो मनुष्याः षड् दिवौकसः ।
कामधातुः स नरकद्वीपभेदेन विंशतिः ॥
```

## 2. IAST

```iast
narakapretatiryañco manuṣyāḥ ṣaḍ divaukasaḥ /
kāmadhātuḥ sa narakadvīpabhedena viṃśatiḥ //
```

This is the opening verse of **tṛtīyaṃ kośasthānam**, the **Lokanirdeśa**.

---

## 3. Padaccheda

```iast
naraka-preta-tiryañcaḥ
manuṣyāḥ
ṣaṭ
divaukasaḥ

kāmadhātuḥ
saḥ
naraka-dvīpa-bhedena
viṃśatiḥ
```

A more syntactically normalized padaccheda:

```iast
narakāḥ pretāḥ tiryañcaḥ manuṣyāḥ ṣaṭ divaukasaḥ /
saḥ kāmadhātuḥ naraka-dvīpa-bhedena viṃśatiḥ //
```

The transmitted verse compresses the opening list into compounds and sandhi:

```iast
naraka + preta + tiryañcaḥ → narakapretatiryañcaḥ
ṣaṭ + divaukasaḥ → ṣaḍ divaukasaḥ
saḥ → sa
```

## 4. Grammar Table

| Form | Lemma | Grammar | Function |
|---|---|---:|---|
| **naraka-** | naraka | nominal stem, “hell-being / hell realm” | first member of compound |
| **preta-** | preta | nominal stem, “preta” | second member of compound |
| **tiryañcaḥ** | tiryañc | nom. masc. pl. | “animals,” literally “going horizontally / across” |
| **manuṣyāḥ** | manuṣya | nom. masc. pl. | “humans” |
| **ṣaḍ / ṣaṭ** | ṣaṣ | numeral | “six” |
| **divaukasaḥ** | divaukas | nom. masc. pl. | “heaven-dwellers,” devas |
| **kāmadhātuḥ** | kāma-dhātu | nom. masc. sg. | “the desire-domain” |
| **saḥ / sa** | tad | nom. masc. sg. pronoun | “that” / “this” referring to the domain just listed |
| **naraka-dvīpa-bhedena** | naraka-dvīpa-bheda | instr. masc./neut. sg. | “by distinction of hells and continents/islands” |
| **viṃśatiḥ** | viṃśati | nom. fem. sg. numeral noun | “twenty” / “a twentyfold set” |

## 5. Syntax / Construction

The verse has two movements.

First:

```iast
narakapretatiryañco manuṣyāḥ ṣaḍ divaukasaḥ
```

This is a nominative classificatory list:

```text
hell-beings,
pretas,
animals,
humans,
six heaven-dwellers
```

Second:

```iast
kāmadhātuḥ sa narakadvīpabhedena viṃśatiḥ
```

This identifies the list as the **kāmadhātu**, then gives its numerical determination:

```text
That is the desire-domain;
by the distinction of hells and continents, it is twentyfold.
```

The core syntax is:

```text
X, Y, Z ... = saḥ kāmadhātuḥ
saḥ kāmadhātuḥ = viṃśatiḥ naraka-dvīpa-bhedena
```

So the verse is not merely naming types of beings. It is performing a **domain count**.

---

## 6. Literal Translation

```text
Hell-beings, pretas, animals, humans, and six heaven-dwellers:
that is the desire-domain, twentyfold by the distinction of hells and continents.
```

---

## 7. Philosophical Translation

```text
The desire-domain consists of the hell beings, pretas, animals, humans, and the six classes of devas. As a structured world-domain, it is counted as twentyfold through the differentiation of hells and inhabited continents.
```

Or more Organon-clean:

```text
The kāma-domain is the first appearing field of worldling existence: hells, pretas, animals, humans, and six deva-classes. It is not an abstract space, but a differentiated domain of birth, destiny, embodiment, and karmic placement.
```

---

## 8. Technical Vocabulary

### dhātu

Here **dhātu** must remain **domain**, according to the project rule.

```text
kāmadhātu
    kāma-domain
    desire-domain
    domain structured by desire, affect, embodiment, and karmic result
```

Not:

```text
desire element
```

The latter is too flat and misses the world-structuring force of **dhātu**.

### kāma

Here **kāma** is not merely “sexual desire.” It names the affective-desiring field of embodied existence: craving, enjoyment, pain, hunger, fear, need, pleasure, possession, and relational entanglement.

```text
kāma
    desire
    affective appetite
    embodied want
    enjoyment-seeking orientation
```

### naraka

Can mean both “hell” as realm and “hell-being” by metonymic usage. In this verse, since the list is parallel to pretas, animals, humans, and devas, we may render:

```text
naraka
    hell-being
    or hell-destiny
```

### preta

Leave as **preta** initially. “Hungry ghost” is useful, but it imports a later interpretive idiom. For technical work:

```text
preta
    preta-being
    hungry/deprived post-mortem being
```

### tiryañc

Literally “going across / horizontal.” Conventionally “animal.”

```text
tiryañc
    animal
    laterally-going being
    non-human sentient embodiment within kāmadhātu
```

### divaukas

Compound:

```text
div + okas
heaven + dwelling
```

So:

```text
divaukasaḥ
    heaven-dwellers
    devas
```

This verse mentions **six** deva-classes of the kāma-domain. It does not yet enumerate them in the verse itself.

### bheda

```text
bheda
    distinction
    differentiation
    division
```

Here it is crucial: the world is not just listed; it is **differentiated**.

### viṃśatiḥ

```text
viṃśatiḥ
    twenty
    twentyfold totality
```

The kāma-domain is not an indefinite plurality. It is a counted field.

---

## 9. Logical Determination

The first logical determination of Lokanirdeśa is:

```text
World appears as counted domain.
```

Not as:

```text
world = object
```

But as:

```text
world = classified domain of possible birth and placement
```

So VAkK_3.1 gives us:

```text
Loka
    ↓
dhātu
    ↓
kāmadhātu
    ↓
gati / birth-class / inhabitant-class
    ↓
counted differentiation
```

The verse performs a transition from **being-list** to **domain-structure**.

```text
hell-beings
pretas
animals
humans
devas
    ↓
not merely separate beings
    ↓
one kāmadhātu
    ↓
twentyfold by internal distinction
```

So the logical act is **classification under an immediately appearing domain**.

## 10. Organon Interpretation

This is the first appearance of the **World-pole**.

```text
VAK 3.1
    does not begin with metaphysical creation.
    does not begin with abstract space.
    does not begin with substance.

It begins with:
    Where beings are born.
    How beings are placed.
    How the desire-domain is differentiated.
```

This is exactly the **I of worldling** beginning to see its enclosure.

When waking consciousness becomes worldling-consciousness, it does not first encounter “the universe” abstractly. It encounters a situated field:

```text
I am here.
There are others.
There are higher and lower conditions.
There is suffering, desire, embodiment, danger, pleasure, aspiration.
```

The kāma-domain is therefore the first **outer enclosure** of the worldling-I.

```text
I of consciousness
    immediate awareness-field

I of worldling
    placed within kāmadhātu
    caught in birth, desire, class, destiny, and world-order
```

So this opening verse is already existential. It says:

```text
The world into which the worldling awakens
is a differentiated desire-domain.
```

Within the wheel schema:

```text
Bhava Chakra
    appears first as kāmadhātu

Dharma Chakra
    is implicit as the law of differentiation

Karma
    is not yet stated
    but is already demanded by the plurality of placements
```

That forward reference will become explicit in Chapter 4:

```iast
karmajaṃ lokavaicitryam
```

The variety of world is karma-born.

So VAK_3.1 is the immediate world-variety. VAK_4.1 will give the ground of that variety.
