# Kośa Refutation, Organic Unity, and Brahman

## Status

Working source note arising from the VAK Chapter 7 study.

This note records a decisive reconstruction of the relation between:

- the *Abhidharmakośa*;
- Yoga Samāpatti;
- Fichte’s Science of Knowing;
- Hegel’s Organon;
- Buddhi, Māyā, Ātman, Puruṣa, and Brahman.

The result is not a dismissal of the Kośa. It is its immanent refutation, preservation, elevation, and practical appropriation.

---

## 1. The Kośa Raised from Dry Bones

The Kośa initially appears as an immense analytical treasury:

- dharmas;
- aggregates, spheres, and domains;
- causes, conditions, and results;
- kleśa and karma;
- knowledges and objects;
- cultivation, path, cessation, and liberation.

Taken merely as an inventory, it remains a collection of dry bones. Its classifications are powerful, but the Principle that unifies them as one science is not yet fully discriminated.

The Organon does not refute the Kośa by declaring it false. It refutes the Kośa’s isolated claim to self-sufficiency. It discovers the Principle already active within its determinations, raises that Principle into explicit form, and returns it to the Kośa as its ground.

Thus the Kośa becomes:

> **The Science of Duḥkha within the Doctrine of Pure Practical Reason.**

Its subject is Knowing under the conditions of bondage:

- Knowing afflicted;
- Knowing conditioned;
- Knowing acting;
- Knowing reproducing its contradiction;
- Knowing cultivated;
- Knowing released.

The Kośa is therefore not the complete Science of Citta. It is the special practical science of Citta under contradiction.

---

## 2. Refutation as Capture and Digestion

The refutation is Hegel-clean.

The truth of the Kośa is preserved, but its independent form is negated. Yoga grants the Kośa its Principle and thereby puts the treasury to work.

```text
Kośa as received
    analysis of conditioned dharmas
    path, cultivation, knowledge, cessation
    Principle of Knowing presupposed

Immanent refutation
    the classifications do not ground their own unity
    practical cultivation does not derive
    the Being whose liberation it serves

Yoga–Fichte Samāpatti
    makes explicit the Being of Knowing

Kośa raised
    becomes the Science of Duḥkha
    within Pure Practical Reason
```

The Kośa is not discarded. It is captured, digested, and reactivated as an operational subsystem of the Organon.

Its determinations become practical functions:

```text
kleśa
    contradiction within conditioned cognition

karma
    cognition externalized as effective determination

mārga
    practical reconstruction of conditioned cognition

jñāna
    discriminated instruments of correction

bhāvanā
    transformation of the dharma-series

nirodha
    Nothing brought to completion as One
```

---

## 3. Continuity without a Substantial Self

The Organon does not require a hidden bearer beneath the conditioned dharma-series.

Continuity is explained through the conditioned series itself.

The bearer is not a concealed substance. The bearer is **Nothing**.

Nothing here does not mean blank nonexistence. It is active negativity:

- the passing away of each determination;
- the interval through which one determination conditions another;
- the non-substantial continuity of becoming;
- the power by which the series proceeds without a permanent owner.

```text
dharma
    arises

dharma
    ceases

Nothing
    remains as no thing
    and carries the transition
```

Hence:

> **The conditioned dharma-series is borne by Nothing because Nothing is the continuity of its becoming.**

And:

> **Nirodha is Nothing brought to completion as One.**

This One is not yet Brahman, nor an abstract Absolute standing over the process. It is the accomplished closure of negativity.

---

## 4. Samāpatti as the Dharma of Being

Samāpatti is the Dharma of Being, but its One must not be understood as an abstract or featureless Absolute One.

Samāpatti is an **Organic One**.

Its articulation is given through:

- **grahītṛ** — grasper, knower;
- **grahaṇa** — grasping, cognition, mediation;
- **grāhya** — grasped, known, object.

These are not three independent substances. Nor are they accidental attributes of a prior One. They are the internally necessary moments through which the Organic One exists.

```text
grahītṛ
    the whole under the determination of subjectivity

grahaṇa
    the whole under the determination of mediation

grāhya
    the whole under the determination of objectivity
```

Each is a Relative Absolute: each presents the whole from one determinate standpoint, yet none is self-standing in isolation.

The Organic One is not first complete and then divided. It exists only through this differentiation.

> **Samāpatti is the Organic Being of Knowing.**

This is the positive power of Knowing:

> Knowing can exist as knower, knowing, and known while remaining one living cognition.

The unity is therefore immanent in the triad. It is not a fourth term behind it.

---

## 5. Samāpatti as a Theory of Māyā qua Vidyā

The Organon is a System of Māyā.

Māyā is not merely illusion or error. In its positive determination, Māyā is the power by which Organic Unity asserts and articulates itself.

> **Māyā is the self-assertion of the Organic I as organic.**

The distinction is:

```text
Māyā qua avidyā
    the moments are mistaken for independently real substances

Māyā qua vidyā
    the moments are comprehended
    as the organic articulation of one living cognition
```

Thus the Being of Knowing is Māyā qua Vidyā.

Samāpatti does not escape Māyā. It presents Māyā truthfully as the organic structure of Knowing.

The Organic I is not a substance trapped in appearance. It is the living unity of appearance, relation, cognition, and return.

> **I, as Organic I, am Māyā.**

This does not mean that the I is unreal. It means that the I is not a self-subsistent thing. It is organic self-articulation.

---

## 6. Buddhi as Organic Living Unity

The Organic Living Unity is **Buddhi**.

Buddhi is not merely one faculty among others. It is the intelligible organic whole in which the poles of Knowing are internally related.

```text
Buddhi
    Organic Living Unity
    Māyā qua Vidyā
    grahītṛ–grahaṇa–grāhya
    as one articulated cognition
```

Buddhi then sees its Ātman as organic.

Ātman is not a second substance behind Buddhi. It is Buddhi recognizing its own organic unity as Self.

```text
Buddhi
    the Organic I in actuality

Ātman
    Buddhi reflected into itself
    and recognizing its living unity as Self
```

The Organic I has a teleological pole: **Puruṣa**.

Puruṣa is therefore not introduced as an external witness-substance. It is a posit of the Organic I precisely insofar as the Organic I is purposive, directed, and capable of completion.

```text
Māyā
    organic self-articulation

Ātman
    organic self-recognition

Puruṣa
    the teleological pole of the Organic I
```

---

## 7. Organic Unity and Brahman

This distinction must be maintained without compromise:

> **Brahman is not Organic Unity.**

Brahman is **Unity without a Second**.

Organic Unity is Unity **with a Second**.

```text
Organic Unity
    unity through internal otherness
    identity through difference
    one whole articulated through a second

Brahman
    Unity without a Second
    no second standing over against it
```

Therefore Buddhi is not Brahman.

Buddhi is the Organic One. It lives through polarity:

- knower and known;
- internal and external;
- Puruṣa and Prakṛti;
- grahītṛ and grāhya;
- mediated through grahaṇa.

Brahman is not the synthesis or organic totality of these poles. Brahman is the subtle nonduality disclosed through their difference: the truth that no independently ultimate second stands against the One.

The levels are therefore:

```text
Buddhi
    Organic Living Unity
    Unity with a Second

Ātman
    Buddhi knowing its Organic Unity as Self

Puruṣa
    the teleological pole of that Organic I

Brahman
    Unity without a Second
    the nondual Truth disclosed through the organic polarity
```

The Absolute One must not replace the Organic One.

The Organic One is the actuality of articulated knowing. The Absolute One is the subtle truth that the opposition within that organic articulation does not constitute ultimate duality.

> **The Organic One lives through difference; Brahman is the truth that its difference does not establish a Second.**

---

## 8. Freedom Requires More than the Being of Knowing

Freedom is not centered merely on the Being of Knowing.

The Being of Knowing, as Samāpatti, is the Organic One of Māyā qua Vidyā. But Freedom requires the Organic One to be united with its Essence and to determine itself through that mediation.

```text
Being
    Organic Unity of Knowing

Essence
    reflection, difference, contradiction,
    ground, conditioning, appearance

Concept
    Organic Unity comprehending
    and determining its own mediation
```

Therefore:

```text
Samāpatti
    Organic Being of Knowing
    Theory of Māyā qua Vidyā

Kośa
    Science of Duḥkha
    determinate Essence of conditioned cognition

Pure Practical Reason
    practical reconstruction of conditioned cognition
    under the Concept of Freedom
```

Freedom cannot be defined as mere escape from Māyā.

It is Māyā becoming adequate to its own Concept.

The Kośa can therefore be wrapped in the Concept of Freedom only after its Science of Duḥkha is grounded in the Organic Being of Knowing and raised through Essence into Concept.

---

## 9. Final Determination

The result of the present discussion is:

```text
Samāpatti
    the Organic Being of Knowing

Buddhi
    the Organic Living Unity

Māyā
    the self-assertion and articulation
    of the Organic I as organic

Ātman
    Buddhi recognizing its Organic Unity as Self

Puruṣa
    the teleological posit of the Organic I

Kośa
    the Science of Duḥkha
    within the Doctrine of Pure Practical Reason

Nirodha
    Nothing brought to completion as One

Brahman
    Unity without a Second
    not Organic Unity
```

The central speculative distinction is:

> **Organic Unity is Unity with a Second. Brahman is Unity without a Second.**

The Organon is therefore a System of Māyā because it develops the Organic One through its determinations rather than fleeing manifestation into abstract identity.

The Kośa is refuted because its Principle is made explicit beyond its received form. It is preserved because its analytical treasury is indispensable. It is elevated because it becomes a special science of Pure Practical Reason. It is put to work because its dharmas become determinations within the living Organic architecture of Yoga.

The Kośa has been captured and eaten—but its truth has become stronger in digestion.
